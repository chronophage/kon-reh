\chapter{Core Rules (Condensed)}

\noindent
\textit{Fate's Edge} is a narrative-first TTRPG where every action has a cost, the fiction leads the rules, and tension is managed with \textbf{Position}, \textbf{Effect}, and \textbf{Story Beats (SB)}. This chapter gives you the complete table loop.

\subsection*{X-Card}
Any player (including the GM) can draw an “X” on a card (or simply say “X”) when a moment makes them uncomfortable. 
When the X‑Card is shown, the described content is \textbf{removed from the fiction} – no questions, no explanations, no judgments. 
You do not need to justify using the X‑Card. The scene is retconned or redirected immediately.

\subsection*{Lines}
A \textbf{Line} is a hard boundary: content that will not appear in the game at all. 
Examples: “No sexual violence,” “No harm to children,” “No body horror.” 
Before the campaign or a session, players name their Lines. The GM and group honor them completely.

\subsection*{Veils}
A \textbf{Veil} is a soft boundary: content that can happen “off screen” or fade to black. 
The event is acknowledged but not described in detail. 
Example: “A romantic scene can happen, but fade to black before it becomes explicit.” 
Veils allow the story to acknowledge mature themes without forcing players to roleplay through them.

\subsection*{How to Use These Tools at Your Table}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Establish Lines and Veils during \textbf{Session Zero} (or before your first game). Write them down where everyone can see them.
  \item Keep an X‑Card visible on the table (a printed card, a drawn X, or a digital symbol).
  \item Remind players they can use the X‑Card at any time, even mid‑sentence.
  \item When the X‑Card is used, \textbf{pause}, rewind if needed, and move on. Do not interrogate the player who used it.
  \item The GM is also a player. The GM may use the X‑Card for their own comfort.
  \item Respect the tools. If someone calls an X‑Card, trust that they had a good reason and support them.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Why These Tools Matter}
These tools are not about censorship – they are about \textbf{consent}. 
They allow the group to tell darker, more intense stories together because everyone knows they can tap out at any time. 
A game that feels safe is a game that can go to the edge.

\smallskip
\noindent\textit{For more detail, see the X‑Card by John Stavropoulos and the “Lines and Veils” RPG safety toolkit. Many free resources are available online.}

\section{The Core Resolution Loop}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Declare intent \& approach} – What do you do? How?
  \item \textbf{GM sets Position \& DV} – Risk (Dominant/Controlled/Desperate) and Difficulty Value (2–6).
  \item \textbf{Roll dice} = Attribute + Skill (d10s). Each 6–10 = 1 success. Each 1 = 1 \textbf{Story Beat (SB)} for the GM.
  \item \textbf{Compare successes to DV} – Use the Outcome Matrix.
  \item \textbf{GM spends SB} to introduce complications, worsen Position, or tick clocks.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Difficulty Values (DV)}
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Situation} \\
\midrule
2 & Routine, low stakes \\
3 & Default – pressured or minor opposition \\
4 & Hard – hostile conditions \\
5+ & Extreme – multiple constraints, dramatic failure \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\textit{When in doubt, start at DV 3.}

\subsection{Position \& Effect}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Position} (risk): \textit{Dominant} (safe), \textit{Controlled} (balanced), \textit{Desperate} (high stakes).
  \item \textbf{Effect} (reward): \textit{Limited} (small), \textit{Standard} (expected), \textit{Great} (big).
  \item The GM sets both based on your approach and the fiction.
\end{itemize}
\noindent
\textit{Improve Position:} advantage, tools, or spend 1 Boon. \textit{Worsen Position:} GM spends SB, alarms, exposure.

\subsection{Outcome Matrix}
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{Roll Result} & \textbf{What Happens} \\
\midrule
Successes $\ge$ DV, SB=0 & Clean Success – intent achieved \\
Successes $\ge$ DV, SB$\ge$1 & Success \& Cost – intent + complications \\
$0 <$ Successes $<$ DV & Partial – progress + complication; gain \textbf{1 Boon} \\
Successes = 0 & Miss – no progress; gain \textbf{2 Boons} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Boons}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Earned on Miss (2) or Partial (1). Hold up to 5 (trim to 2 at scene end).
  \item \textbf{Spend 1 Boon to:} re-roll one die, activate an Asset, improve Position by one step.
  \item \textbf{Spend 2 Boons $\rightarrow$ 1 XP} (max 2 XP/session).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Story Beats (SB)}
GM resource. Spend SB to:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{1 SB} – Minor: noise, trace, tick a 4/6 clock +1.
  \item \textbf{2 SB} – Moderate: lose Position, raise alarm, worse terms.
  \item \textbf{3 SB} – Serious: reinforcements, break an asset, split party.
  \item \textbf{4+ SB} – Major: authority arrives, environment shifts, Patron intervenes.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Description Ladder}
How you describe your action adds optional re-rolls (all 1s still generate SB):
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Basic}: roll as-is.
  \item \textbf{Detailed}: re-roll one die showing 1.
  \item \textbf{Intricate}: re-roll all 1s; add a flourish on success.
\end{itemize}

\section{Attributes \& Skills}
\subsection{Attributes (1–5)}
\textbf{Body} (strength, endurance), \textbf{Wits} (perception, speed), \textbf{Spirit} (will, focus), \textbf{Presence} (charisma, command).

\subsection{Skills (0–5)}
Common skills: Melee, Ranged, Athletics, Sway, Subterfuge, Insight, Stealth, Survival, Command, Arcana, Lore, Craft/Tinker.  
\textit{Roll Attribute + Skill dice.}

% -----------------------------------------------------
\section{Action Economy}
Each turn: \textbf{1 Action} + \textbf{1 Move}.  
\textbf{Move} changes range band (Close $\leftrightarrow$ Near $\leftrightarrow$ Far) or repositions within same band.  
\textbf{Dash (Action)}: move two bands; your next defense is Desperate.  
\textbf{Disengage (Action)}: then your Move does not provoke opportunity attacks; you cannot Attack this turn.

\subsection{Opportunity Attacks}
If you move out of an enemy’s \textbf{Close} range without using Disengage, that enemy may make one free melee attack against you (no action cost, Position Controlled).

\subsection{Charge \& Skirmish (Full Turn Actions)}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Charge}: move two bands toward an enemy and make one melee attack. Costs 1 Fatigue. Next defense Desperate. Requires open ground.
  \item \textbf{Skirmish}: move two bands away from an enemy and make one ranged attack. Costs 1 Fatigue. Next defense Desperate. Requires open ground.
\end{itemize}
\textit{Talents like Effortless Charge, Effortless Skirmish, and Relentless modify these actions.}

% -----------------------------------------------------
\section{Combat Basics}
Initiative is narrative: GM spotlights based on fiction. Ambushers act first.

\subsection{Range Bands}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Close}: melee reach.
  \item \textbf{Near}: thrown weapons, short bows.
  \item \textbf{Far}: long range.
  \item \textbf{Absent}: off-screen (requires travel).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Hiding \& Cover}
To hide, be behind full cover (wall, large tree) and take the \textbf{Interact} action (Stealth vs Perception). Once hidden, you are not a valid target unless an enemy uses an action to \textbf{Search} (Perception vs your Stealth). Reveal yourself by attacking or moving into the open.

\subsection{Fatigue \& Harm}
\textbf{Fatigue track} = Body. Mark Fatigue for exertion, backlash, or Charge/Skirmish.  
Each Fatigue worsens Position one step (Dominant $\to$ Controlled $\to$ Desperate). If already Desperate, each Fatigue gives –1 die instead.  
When Fatigue fills, increase Harm by 1 step and clear Fatigue.  
\textbf{Harm levels:} 1 (–1 die), 2 (–2 dice), 3 (incapacitated/dying).  
\textbf{Recovery}: Short rest clears 1–2 Fatigue; full night clears all; medical care reduces Harm.

% -----------------------------------------------------
\section{Magic (Brief)}
Three paths:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Caster (Freeform)}: Requires \textit{Spellcraft} talent. Weave elements + Channel power. DV = 1 + number of TAGS (dangerous TAGS +2 DV). On mixed result, Backlash relates to TAGS used.
  \item \textbf{Runekeeper}: Requires \textit{Familiar} + \textit{Codex}. Invoke Rites (1 action) and mark Obligation.
  \item \textbf{Invoker}: Requires \textit{Patron's Symbol}. Rituals take time; \textit{Crack the Seal} for instant cast (mark extra Obligation).
\end{itemize}

% -----------------------------------------------------
\section{Travel Framework}
Break journey into legs. Each leg has a \textbf{Travel Clock [4]}. Assign roles:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Guide}: reduces DV for movement checks.
  \item \textbf{Scout}: ignores next 1 SB spend.
  \item \textbf{Quartermaster}: reduces Supplies Clock by 1.
  \item \textbf{Watch}: starts next scene at Dominant.
\end{itemize}
Role actions require a roll (GM sets DV). Success advances clock or grants benefit. Complications may add segments.

% -----------------------------------------------------
\section{Character Advancement}
\subsection{Starting Build}
30 XP. Attributes 1–3, Skills 0–2. Bonds (+2 XP) and Complications (+4 XP) optional.

\subsection{XP Awards (per session)}
Standard 6–10; major objectives +2–4; discoveries +1–2; hard choices +1–2; arc milestones +8–12.

\subsection{Spending XP}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item New Attribute rating $\times$ 3 XP.
  \item New Skill level $\times$ 2 XP.
  \item Talents: see talent costs (2–12+ XP).
  \item Followers: Cap$^2$ XP.
\end{itemize}

% -----------------------------------------------------
\section{GM Quick Reference}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Default DV:} 3.
  \item \textbf{Position:} Controlled unless fiction says otherwise.
  \item \textbf{SB menu:} 1=minor, 2=moderate, 3=serious, 4+=major.
  \item \textbf{Clocks:} Keep at most three active (scene, journey, campaign).
\end{itemize}

\noindent
\textit{Remember: Fail forward, let the fiction lead, spend SB to create momentum, and let players' choices reshape the world.}
