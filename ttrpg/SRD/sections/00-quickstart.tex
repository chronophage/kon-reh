\section*{Quickstart}

\subsection*{The Core Loop in Brief}

\begin{enumerate}
  ℹtem \textbf{Declare intent and approach.} What do you do and how?
  ℹtem \textbf{GM sets Position and Difficulty (DV).}
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Position: \textbf{Dominant} (advantage), \textbf{Controlled} (balanced), \textbf{Desperate} (high risk).
    ℹtem DV: 2 (routine) to 5+ (extreme). Default DV = 3.
  \end{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Roll dice.} Pool = Attribute + Skill (d10s). Each 6−−9 = 1 success; each 10 = 2 successes.
  ℹtem \textbf{Count Story Beats (SB).} Each die showing a 1 gives the GM 1 SB.
  ℹtem \textbf{Compare successes to DV.} Use the outcome matrix.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Outcome Matrix}

\begin{tabular}{@{}llp{6cm}@{}}
⊤rule
\textbf{Roll Result} & \textbf{Outcome} & \textbf{What Happens} \\
∣rule
Successes $≥$ DV, SB = 0 & Clean Success & Intent achieved; no complication. \\
Successes $≥$ DV, SB $>$ 0 & Success & SB & Intent achieved; GM spends SB to add a complication. \\
$0 <$ Successes $<$ DV & Partial & Make progress, but with a cost; player gains 1 Boon. \\
Successes = 0 & Miss & No progress; GM escalates with SB; player gains 2 Boons. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection*{Boons (Player Resource)}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Earn 1 Boon on a \textbf{Partial}, 2 Boons on a \textbf{Miss}.
  ℹtem Maximum 5 Boons; trim to 2 at scene end.
  ℹtem Spend 1 Boon to: re−roll a single die, activate an Asset, or improve Position by one step.
  ℹtem During downtime, convert 2 Boons $→$ 1 XP (max 2 XP per session).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Story Beats (GM Resource)}

Each 1 on a player's die gives the GM 1 SB. Spend SB to introduce complications:

\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
⊤rule
\textbf{SB SB} & \textbf{Effect} \\
∣rule
1 & Minor pressure: noise, trace, tick a 4−−6 segment timer +1. \\
2 & Moderate setback: alarm raised, lose Position, a lesser foe appears. \\
3 & Serious trouble: reinforcements, key gear breaks, advance a campaign timer. \\
4+ & Major turn: trap springs, authority arrives, environment shifts dramatically. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection*{Position and Effect}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Position} (risk): Dominant (safe), Controlled (normal), Desperate (high stakes).
  ℹtem \textbf{Effect} (reward): Limited, Standard, Great.
  ℹtem Spending 1 Boon before a roll improves Position by one step.
  ℹtem The GM may spend SB to worsen Position.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Fatigue and Harm}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Fatigue} track = Body. Each Fatigue point worsens Position by one step. At Desperate, each Fatigue gives −1 die.
  ℹtem \textbf{Harm} levels: 1 (−1 die on related actions), 2 (−1 die on most actions), 3 (incapacitated).
  ℹtem \textbf{Armor converts Harm to Fatigue.} Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit.
  ℹtem Taking Harm clears all existing Fatigue (the shock of injury resets exhaustion).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Armor Conversion Table}

\begin{tabular}{@{}lccc@{}}
⊤rule
\textbf{Armor Type} & \textbf{Harm 1} & \textbf{Harm 2} & \textbf{Harm 3} \\
∣rule
Light  & Fatigue 1 & Harm 1 + Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Medium & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Heavy  & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 2 \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection*{Quick Reference: Defaults}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Default DV = 3.
  ℹtem Default Position = Controlled.
  ℹtem Default Effect = Standard.
  ℹtem Default Range = Near (unless otherwise stated).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Example of Play (One Minute)}

\textit{Lyra (Wits 3 + Tinker 2 = 5 dice) tries to pick a lock while a guard patrol approaches.}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem GM: "Controlled, DV 3. On a Miss, the guard hears you."
  ℹtem Roll: 9, 6, 4, 2, 1 $→$ 2 successes vs DV 3 $→$ \textit{Partial}. Lyra gains 1 Boon. The 1 gives the GM 1 SB.
  ℹtem GM: "The lock clicks open, but the last pin squeals. I spend the SB: the guard stops and turns his head. You have one beat to hide."
\end{itemize}
