\section{Tags & Effects Library}

Tags are keywords that define the mechanical and narrative behavior of spells, rites, talents, and assets. They do nothing on their own; they only function when granted by a rule (Spell, Rite, Talent, Ability). Unless noted otherwise, the Duration of a tag effect is \textbf{Scene}, and the DV for contests or resistances is set by the fiction (typical range 2–5).

Every spell or magical effect is built from one or more of the eight fundamental elements. The first four are physical; the second four are metaphysical.

\subsection{Building a Spell}

A spell is a combination of one or more element tags and one or more effect tags. The base Difficulty Value is:

\[
\text{DV} = 1 + \text{total number of tags (element + effect)}
\]

⫕section{Dangerous Tags}
Some effects are so powerful that each dangerous tag adds +2 DV instead of +1. Dangerous tags include: \texttt{[GATE]}, \texttt{[FOLD]}, \texttt{[LEAP]}, \texttt{[CREATE]}, \texttt{[SUMMON]}, \texttt{[TRANSMUTE]}, \texttt{[ANIMATE]}, \texttt{[TRANSFORM]}, \texttt{[DOMINATE]}, \texttt{[REWIND]}.

⫕section{Stacking Identical Effect Tags}
You may include the same effect tag multiple times to increase its potency. Each additional copy adds its normal DV cost. Example:
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \texttt{[AIR][DEFEND]} – 2 tags $→$ DV 3. Grants +1 die to one defense roll.
  ℹtem \texttt{[AIR][DEFEND][DEFEND]} – 3 tags $→$ DV 4. Grants +2 dice to one defense roll.
\end{itemize}
Stacking is limited by the caster's Tier (max 3 copies per effect at Tier III, 4 at Tier V).

\subsection{Repair & Restoration}

Two tags handle mending objects: \texttt{[MEND]} for quick, temporary fixes in the field, and \texttt{[REPAIR]} for thorough, lasting restoration.

⫕section{[MEND] (Quick Fix)}
Restore a damaged object to functioning order for the duration of the scene.

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (severity of damage, size, material). Typical DV 2–4.
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Remove one level of \textit{Neglected} or \textit{Compromised} from a single item for the rest of the scene. The item works normally.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} The item functions for the scene but becomes \textit{Neglected} (or worsens one step) when the scene ends.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No repair; the item may become \textit{Compromised} or break (GM discretion).
  ℹtem \textbf{Duration:} Scene. After the scene, the damage returns (or worsens) unless followed by a proper [REPAIR] during downtime.
\end{itemize}

\textit{Example:} A snapped bowstring is magically re−knotted for the remainder of the fight; the next day it frays again.

⫕section{[REPAIR] (Permanent Fix)}
Fully restore a damaged object, tool, or piece of equipment.

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (severity, material, magical nature). Typical DV 3–5. Magical items add +1 DV.
  ℹtem \textbf{Time:} One downtime action (a few hours of focused work). Can be used during a scene only if the caster has Significant Time and appropriate tools.
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Remove all levels of \textit{Neglected} and \textit{Compromised} from a single item. The item is restored to full function.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Remove one level of damage (e.g., \textit{Compromised} $→$ \textit{Neglected}), or the item becomes \textit{Neglected} but usable.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No repair; the item may become \textit{Compromised} or gain a new flaw (GM discretion).
\end{itemize}

\textit{Example:} A shattered shield is reforged overnight; a rusted lock is cleaned and oiled to working order.

\subsection{Influence Over Mortals}

These tags affect the minds and loyalties of living beings. Using them to alter free will carries narrative and ethical consequences.

⫕section{[BOND]}
Strengthen or create a positive emotional tie between a target and a person, place, or ideal.

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (target's current loyalty, resistance to magic). Typical DV 3–5.
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Increase a follower's \textit{Loyalty} state by one step (e.g., \textit{Strained} $→$ \textit{Faithful}). The target genuinely feels the bond.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} The bond forms but is fragile; any subsequent external pressure may break it (GM may downgrade loyalty on a future setback).
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No effect; the target may become suspicious or resistant to further magical influence.
  ℹtem \textbf{Ethical note:} Forcing loyalty removes agency. The GM may rule that such magic is taboo, attracts Patron attention, or creates hidden complications (e.g., the follower secretly resents the caster).
\end{itemize}

\textit{Example:} A Runekeeper of Inaea weaves a [BOND] rite to mend a hireling's shaken trust after a failed mission.

⫕section{[SUBVERT]}
Coerce obedience or twist existing loyalties.

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Dangerous tag:} adds +2 DV.
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (target's will, opposing magic). Typical DV 5–7.
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} The target follows one simple command or shifts loyalty one step in the caster's favor (e.g., \textit{Faithful} $→$ \textit{Strained} toward their original master). The effect lasts one scene or until the command is fulfilled.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} The target hesitates, reveals information, or acts with clear reluctance; the compulsion is visible to observers.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} The target resists; the caster marks 1 Fatigue and the target's original loyalty increases by one step (they become more devoted to their original cause).
  ℹtem \textbf{Backlash:} On any roll showing two or more 1s, the GM may impose a \textit{Patron's Disfavor} or a \textit{Scandal} timer (the act becomes known).
\end{itemize}

\textit{Example:} A desperate Invoker of Maelstraeus uses [SUBVERT] to force a gate guard to look away. The guard later testifies under oath, and a bounty appears.

\subsection{Control & Countermagic}

⫕section{[DISPEL]}
End an ongoing magical effect or construct.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (usually equal to the effect's Tier or casting DV).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Effect ends or is suppressed per source text.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Effect suppressed briefly (one exchange) or reduced in scope.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No effect; GM may gain SB.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[COUNTER]}
Interrupt a spell, rite, or ritual in progress.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Timing:} During the casting window (declared action or ritual phase).
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (complexity of the interrupted effect).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Cancel the action; resources used are lost.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Worsen the caster's Position or impose Fatigue.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No interruption; GM may spend SB to escalate.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[BARRIER]}
Create cover, obstruction, or a physical wall.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (size, material, duration).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Place a barrier with Integrity Timer [2/4/6]; blocks movement/attacks until cracked.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Barrier is fragile or has a gap.
  ℹtem \textbf{Countered by:} [DISPEL], brute force (Athletics), or clever route.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[SEAL] / [UNSEAL]}
Magically lock or unlock a container, door, or portal (not spirits).
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (quality of lock, magical binding).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Locked/unlocked as intended; specify any bypass conditions.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Seal is temporary or leaks (can be forced open with +1 DV).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Concealment & Revelation}

⫕section{[VEIL]}
Obscure a person, object, or zone.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (scale, conditions).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Impose –1 die to Notice or perception rolls against the veiled subject; specify limits (angle, distance, scent).
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Works only at a limited range or in dim light.
  ℹtem \textbf{Countered by:} [REVEAL], strong light, physical proof.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[REVEAL]}
Expose illusions, disguises, or hidden clauses.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (complexity of the concealment).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Surface the truth; describe how the revelation manifests (brief glow, visible flaw, etc.).
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} A tell or clue, not the full picture.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[MARK]}
Tag a target for tracking or leverage.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (resistance, visibility).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Place a visible or invisible mark; once per scene, you or an ally gain +1 die when acting directly against the Marked target.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Mark is noisy or short−lived.
  ℹtem \textbf{Countered by:} [CLEANSE], [DISPEL], or listed materials (salt, iron).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Boons & Burdens}

⫕section{[CURSE]}
Inflict a sticky hindrance with a clear release condition.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (severity, target's resilience).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Apply a named Condition (e.g., Shaken, Weakened) that persists until the release condition is met.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Condition is milder or intermittent.
  ℹtem \textbf{Countered by:} [CLEANSE] or the listed key (apology, theft return, pilgrimage).
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[CLEANSE]}
Remove or suppress a Condition (poison, disease, curse, fear).
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (potency of the affliction).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Remove one named Condition.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Suppress or reduce severity for the scene.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[FORTIFY]}
Harden a person, place, or object against a specific vector (fire, blades, fear, persuasion).
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (scale, strength).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Raise Position by one step or reduce consequence severity for attacks of that vector this scene.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Limited scope (one ally, one doorway).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Influence & Oaths}

⫕section{[COMMAND]}
Issue a clear order to a sapient target.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (authority, leverage, fear, ritual standing).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Target complies now or suffers an immediate cost stated by the GM.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Target hesitates, bargains, or partially complies.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} Refusal or blowback (GM may spend SB).
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[OATH]}
Bind parties to terms; breaking has teeth.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (witnesses, sanctity, stakes).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Enforceable pact with stated boon for keeping and consequence for breaking.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} A loophole exists in the oath.
  ℹtem \textbf{Countered by:} Release clause, [CLEANSE] if text allows.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[SANCTIFY]}
Consecrate a zone to a code or Patron.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (size, opposition).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Specify allowed/prohibited acts; violations start at Controlled Position or impose a soft consequence.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Patchy coverage (e.g., only the altar, not the whole room).
  ℹtem \textbf{Countered by:} Opposing rite, profanation (bloodshed, lies), time.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Movement & Making}

⫕section{[PASSAGE]}
Declare a route as permitted or easy.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (length, danger).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Allies on that path gain improved flow (+1 Position or ignore one level of difficult terrain).
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Only one ally or one segment benefits.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[TRANSPORT]}
Move a target across an obstacle (blink, lift, pull).
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (mass, range, warding).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Relocate within listed range; state any tells or costs (SB, Fatigue).
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Arrive off−balance, drop gear, or suffer worse Position.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[CONJURE]}
Create a useful object, cover, or hazard.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (size, complexity).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Conjure an item or zone with Integrity Timer [2/4/6] (if destructible) or a ticking hazard (burn, freeze, haze).
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Fragile or short−lived (lasts one exchange).
  ℹtem \textbf{Countered by:} [DISPEL], force, time.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Knowledge, Movement & Transformation}

⫕section{[TRUTH]}
Ask the GM one yes/no or short−answer question about the present situation. The GM answers truthfully.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Full, clear answer.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Cryptic or incomplete answer (still true).
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No answer; GM gains 1 SB.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[SECRET]}
Reveal a deeply hidden fact about a person, place, or object – something the target actively conceals.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Contest:} Spirit+Lore vs. target's Spirit+Resolve (DV 4 for passive secrets).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} GM reveals the secret.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} A clue or hint toward the secret.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No information; the secret becomes harder to find (+1 DV next time).
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[READ]}
Sense the target's current dominant emotion or a single surface thought. No roll required; narrative permission only. Cannot read deep secrets or memories.

⫕section{[OMEN]}
Glimpse a likely future tied to a specific action or decision.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} GM gives a cryptic warning (one sentence). You may reroll one die later this session if it relates to the omen.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Omen is vague; no reroll.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} False omen; GM may spend 1 SB to make you act on misinformation.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[LIBERATE]}
Automatically succeed on one attempt to escape mundane physical restraints (ropes, manacles, grapple). Magical restraints require a Spirit+Resolve test (DV 4) instead.

⫕section{[BOOST]}
Gain +1 die on a single related roll, or clear 1 Fatigue. This tag does nothing on its own; the spell description must state which benefit applies.

⫕section{[TRANSFORM]}
Take on a new form for the scene. Choose one benefit:
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem +1 Armor
  ℹtem +1 die to a specific skill (e.g., Stealth, Athletics)
  ℹtem A minor supernatural ability (e.g., flight for one exchange, water breathing, claws that deal +1 Harm)
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Partial:} You gain the benefit but mark 1 Fatigue when the scene ends. \textbf{Miss:} No transformation; you mark 1 Fatigue.

⫕section{[ERASURE] (Multi−Mode)}
Choose one of the following when you cast:
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textit{Annihilate:} Destroy a single mundane object (size ≤ chest). Magical objects resist with a [DISPEL] test (DV 4).
  ℹtem \textit{Oblivion:} Erase a specific memory from a living target. Contest Spirit+Resolve (DV 4). On a Miss, you forget instead.
  ℹtem \textit{Nullify:} Void a formal debt, oath, or contract. Requires the original document or a witnessed recitation.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[PRESSURE]}
All enemies within the affected zone suffer –1 die to physical actions (attacks, Athletics, etc.) for the duration.

⫕section{[DREAM]}
Enter a trance or dream state. Ask the GM one question about a past event or a possible future; the answer is always cryptic (one sentence). On a Hit, you may also ignore the first Fatigue from sleep deprivation this scene.

⫕section{[TIME]}
Alter the flow of time in a small zone (Near range). Choose one:
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textit{Slow:} Enemies act at Desperate Position for one exchange.
  ℹtem \textit{Haste:} Allies gain +1 band of movement this turn.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Dangerous tag:} `[TIME]` adds +2 DV when used to rewind or stop time completely (GM discretion).

⫕section{[THRESHOLD]}
Interact with a boundary – door, gate, portal, or conceptual threshold (e.g., between life and death).
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} You may open, close, or pass through a mundane threshold effortlessly. A magical threshold requires a Spirit+Resolve test (DV 4).
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} The threshold resists; you cross but mark 1 Fatigue.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[BURDEN]}
Take a Condition (e.g., Poisoned, Cursed, Fear) or 1 Fatigue from an ally onto yourself. The ally is freed from that affliction. Cannot transfer Harm directly.

\subsection{Outsider−Specific Tags}

⫕section{[WARD]}
Challenge Outsiders crossing a warded edge or zone.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV = target Cap.} (Cap is the Outsider's tier: 1 for Lesser, 3 for Greater.)
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Crosses; add +DV segments to its Leash.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Crosses; add +1 segment.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} Cannot cross this beat.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[BANISH]}
Drive a visible Outsider toward departure.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV = target Cap.}
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Add +DV to its Leash.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Add +1.
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No effect.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{[UNWARD]}
Unmake or suppress a [WARD] created by a Talent, Ability, or Spell.
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{DV:} by fiction (strength of the ward).
  ℹtem \textbf{Hit:} Dismiss or suppress per source text.
  ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Weaken or suppress briefly (one exchange).
  ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} No effect.
\end{itemize}

When a magical roll generates SB (1s) or when a Partial/Miss occurs, the GM may apply a thematic backlash based on the dominant element(s).

\subsection{Stacking & Duration Rules (Global)}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{No Same−Source Stacking:} Multiple scene−long effects from the same source do not stack; take the best instance.
  ℹtem \textbf{Identical Tag Collision:} If two abilities would grant the same Tag in the same window, use the stronger; the weaker is suppressed while it lasts.
  ℹtem \textbf{Default Duration:} Scene, unless a source states otherwise (e.g., ``until the end of your next turn'').
  ℹtem \textbf{DV by Fiction:} Potency, preparation, place, materials, and opposition set DV (typical 2–4; 5+ for set−pieces).
\end{itemize}
