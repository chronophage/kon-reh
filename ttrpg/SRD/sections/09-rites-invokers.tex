\section{Rituals, Rites, and Invocations}
\label{sec:rites}

Magic in Fate’s Edge is not limited to the gifted. Anyone with knowledge, time, and the right components can perform a **ritual** – a slow, careful working that calls upon a Patron, a spirit, or the hidden grammar of the world.

\subsection{Rituals (Universal)}
\label{subsec:universal-rituals}

A ritual is a magical working that takes **Significant Time** (hours) or **Days**, requires specific **components** (rare herbs, a consecrated circle, a true name), and always carries risk.

\paragraph{Who Can Attempt a Ritual?}
Any character, regardless of magical talent. They need:
\begin{itemize}
  \item A clear **intent** (what they want to achieve).
  \item A **method** (e.g., summoning circle, prayer, sympathetic object).
  \item **Components** (consumed or used up).
  \item **Time** (at least one scene of uninterrupted work).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Ritual Resolution}
The GM sets a **DV** (typically 4–6) based on the scope of the effect, and the player rolls **Spirit + Lore** (or another appropriate skill). On a success, the ritual works as intended. On a **Partial**, it works but with a cost (backlash, extra time, compromised component). On a **Miss**, it fails, and the GM may introduce a complication (e.g., the summoned entity is hostile, the ward collapses).

\paragraph{Ritual Costs}
\begin{itemize}
  \item **Obligation:** If the ritual invokes a Patron, mark +1 Obligation (or more for greater powers).
  \item **Components:** Rare or precious components may be consumed.
  \item **SB:** The GM gains SB equal to the number of 1s rolled, as usual.
  \item **Backlash:** On a Partial or Miss, the GM may impose a thematic backlash (e.g., the wrong spirit answers, a ward backfires).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Example Rituals}
See the “Universal Rituals” table in Chapter 5 (Magic System) for examples: \textit{Wayfinder’s Thread}, \textit{Oath-Ward}, \textit{Bargain-Bead}, etc.

\subsection{Learned Rites (Runekeepers)}
\label{subsec:runekeepers}

Characters who bind themselves to a **single Patron** and study that Patron’s **Codex** become **Runekeepers**. Their magic is structured, immediate, and tied to service.

\begin{itemize}
  \item **One-Patron Rule.** A Runekeeper may be bound to only one Patron at a time.
  \item **Thiasos (Familiar).** A requiring a Thiasos (Familiar talent) to access the Patron’s Gift (see below).
  \item **Codex (4 XP).** Grants access to the Patron’s Rites.
  \item **Invoke Rites.** Invoke a known Rite as a **1 action** effect. Mark **+1 Obligation** (or as listed). \emph{Push It:} +1 additional Obligation to amplify the effect.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Rites Difficulty Value (DV)}
\[
\text{DV} = \max\!\big(\text{Obligation Cost} - \text{Spirit},\; \text{Tier}\big)
\]
Obligation Cost is the Rite’s listed toll; Spirit reduces it; Tier sets the minimum.

\subsection{Invokers and Symbols}
\label{subsec:invokers}

Invokers relate to Patrons through consecrated **Symbols** – physical tokens (4 XP each). They may own Symbols of different Patrons (one per Patron).

\begin{itemize}
  \item **Ritual Invocation.** Display the Symbol and perform the Rite as a ritual (DV+1 rounds). Completion marks **+1 Obligation**.
  \item **Crack the Seal.** Resolve instantly by marking **+2 Obligation** (+3 for High Rites) and setting the Symbol to \textsc{Compromised}. The Symbol is inert until restored (Downtime action + test DV 3, or spend 1 XP).
  \item **Display Requirement.** Symbols must be openly visible to function in a ritual.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Obligation Capacity and Clearing}
\label{subsec:obligation}

A character’s **Obligation Capacity** = Spirit + Presence. Track total Obligation across all Patrons (or Symbols).

\begin{itemize}
  \item **Exceeding Capacity:** Mark 1 Fatigue per segment over capacity. Cannot invoke Rites or perform rituals until Obligation is reduced below Capacity.
  \item **Clearing Obligation (Off‑Screen).** Perform an act of service aligned with the Patron and provide an Intricate Description. Clear 1 segment per act.
  \item **Clearing Obligation (On‑Screen).** With the \emph{Focused Devotion} talent (3–5 XP), once per scene spend 1 Boon to clear 1 segment (cannot clear segments accrued in the same scene).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Borrowed Grace (Invoker Talent)}
\label{talent:borrowed-grace}

Once per scene, spend 1 Boon or 1 Fatigue (1 action) to gain **+1 die** to a single combat action, or **+1 Effect** to a social/stealth action. Requires visibly displaying your Patron’s Symbol. If the Symbol is Compromised, Borrowed Grace works at –1 die.

\subsection{Mixing Paths}
Characters may combine Rituals, Rites, Invocations, and Summoning. However, specialization is usually stronger; mixing increases upkeep (Obligation, Symbol state, Leash, component tracking) without guaranteed power gains. Let fiction guide choices.
