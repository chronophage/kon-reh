\section{The Magic System}

Magic in Fate’s Edge is expressed through four interconnected paths. Each uses the same dice engine and SB/Obligation/Corruption economies.

\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.5em, style=nextline]
  \item[Caster (Freeform):] Requires \textbf{Spellcraft (6 XP)}. Flexible, improvisational magic using elements and \texttt{[TAGS]}. Risk of Backlash.
  \item[Runekeeper (Rites):] Requires \textbf{Familiar (2 XP)} + \textbf{Codex (4 XP)}. Channel a Patron’s Rites. Structured, powerful, debt-driven through \textbf{Obligation}.
  \item[Invoker (Symbols):] Requires one or more \textbf{Patron’s Symbols (4 XP each)}. Ritual access to Patron Rites. Slow but safe; can \emph{Crack the Seal} for instant cast at high cost.
  \item[Cantor (Songs):] Requires \textbf{Cantor’s Path (8 XP)} + \textbf{Performance 2+, Lore 1+}. Sing Low Rites as Songs, trading Obligation for \textbf{Corruption}. Performance with transformative risk; corruption clock tied to \textbf{Spirit}.
\end{description}

\subsection{Caster (Freeform / Weave \& Cast)}

\textbf{Core Concept:} Improvisational magic shaping raw elemental forces. Power brings instability.

\paragraph{Mechanics}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Prerequisite:} Spellcraft (6 XP).
  \item \textbf{Process:} Two actions – \textbf{Weave} (shape) then \textbf{Cast} (release). 
  \item \textbf{Roll:} Attribute (usually Wits or Spirit) + Arcana (or appropriate skill).
  \item \textbf{DV:} 1 + number of \texttt{[TAGS]}. Dangerous \texttt{[TAGS]} (Teleport, Transform, Dominate) add +2 each.
  \item \textbf{Backlash:} Every 1 on either roll generates SB. Total 1s determine severity (Minor/Moderate/Severe). The GM applies consequences tied to the elements used (see Elemental Backlash table).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Cantrips}
A spell with a single \texttt{[TAG]} can be cast as a Cantrip: one roll (standard action), no Weave phase. Cannot bypass wards or create large-scale effects.

\subsection{Runekeeper (Rites)}

Runekeepers bind structured magic through a Codex and a Thiasos (familiar spirit). They excel at reliable, thematic magic.

\paragraph{Requirements}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Patron bond}
  \item \textbf{Thiasos (Familiar talent)}
  \item \textbf{Codex (4 XP)}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Invocation}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{1 Action} to invoke a Rite.
  \item Mark \textbf{Obligation} as listed.
  \item \textbf{Push It:} Mark +1 Obligation to increase duration or potency.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Patron’s Gift (Free, requires Thiasos)}
Once per scene, imbue an item with +1 Melee and +1 Thematic Skill (matching Patron) for the scene. Push It to extend one more scene (+1 Obligation).

\subsection{Invoker (Symbols)}

Invokers channel power through consecrated Symbols. Rituals are slow but safe; \emph{Crack the Seal} offers desperate speed.

\paragraph{Requirements}
\begin{itemize}
  \item A \textbf{Patron’s Symbol} (4 XP each)
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Ritual Invocation}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Takes DV+1 rounds.
  \item Always marks +1 Obligation.
  \item If Symbol is displayed (visible), DV –1 and Obligation –1 (min 0).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Crack the Seal}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Instant invocation (1 action).
  \item Mark +2 Obligation (+3 for High Rites).
  \item Symbol becomes \textsc{Compromised} (repair in downtime or pay 1 XP).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Borrowed Grace (Invoker Talent)}
Once per scene, spend 1 Boon or 1 Fatigue to gain +1 die on a combat action or +1 Effect on social/stealth action. Must display Symbol.

\subsection{Cantor (Songs)}

Cantors perform Low Rites as Songs, channelling power through voice and performance. They do not incur Obligation; instead they track a \textbf{Corruption Clock} based on their \textbf{Spirit} – their willpower and connection to the intangible. A higher Spirit lets them resist the transformative pull of their songs longer, but when the clock fills, the change is more profound.

\paragraph{Requirements}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Cantor’s Path (8 XP)}.
  \item Performance 2+, Lore 1+, Presence 2+.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Singing a Song}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{1 action} to begin; resolves at the start of your next turn.
  \item Roll \textbf{Lore + Performance} vs DV (typically 2–3).
  \item On success, the Low Rite effect occurs; no Obligation.
  \item Mark \textbf{Corruption accumulation} (see below).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Push It}
Resolve the Song immediately (instead of next turn). Mark \textbf{1 Fatigue} and advance your Corruption Clock by 1 segment.

\paragraph{Corruption Clock}
Your Corruption Clock has segments equal to your \textbf{Spirit} attribute. Mark a segment when you:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Push a Song.
  \item Perform a \textbf{Resonant Rite} (a powerful thematic working designated by your Patron).
  \item The GM spends a Story Beat on your occult activities.
\end{itemize}
When the clock fills, you immediately gain a thematic \textbf{boon and burden} from the last Patron whose Rite you performed (see Patron corruption tables). Then reset the clock to zero (cannot go below your current Tier).

\subsubsection*{Chorus Cults (Optional)}
\label{sec:chorus-cults}
If you perform a Resonant Rite before 10+ witnesses, you may found a \textbf{Chorus}. This grants a minor follower benefit (see \ref{ch:assets-followers}), but you must mark Obligation to the cult's patron (the patron who's Resonant Rite was sung to form the cult) each session you use it. \textbf{If neglected, the cult may fracture} – your followers could splinter into a rival sect, spread false rumours, or ally with a Patron’s enemy. Keep an eye on your followers; a neglected Chorus can become a serious problem.

\paragraph{Inspire Chorus}
Once per scene, while actively singing a Song, all allies within Near range shift \textbf{Position +1} for one exchange (cannot exceed Controlled). Additional uses in the same scene mark toward Corruption accumulation.

\paragraph{Repertoire (Optional)}
Track a \textbf{Repertoire Clock [6]}. Mark a segment each time you learn a new unique Song. At 2 segments, reduce the base DV of all your Songs by 1 (min 2). At 4 segments, gain +1 die to Performance rolls. At 6 segments, you may learn one Standard Rite as a Song (temporary; requires practice to retain).

\subsection{Elemental Backlash (Condensed)}
\label{sec:backlash-condensed}

When a free caster rolls 1s (or accepts SB to escalate), apply backlash themed to the dominant element. Severity: Minor (1 one), Moderate (2‑3 ones), Severe (4+ ones). Players may accept +1 SB to escalate to the next tier.

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l X X}
\toprule
\textbf{Element} & \textbf{Minor Backlash} & \textbf{Major Backlash} \\
\midrule
Earth / Fate & Slip, bind, encumbrance (–1 Position) & Fissure, entrapment (Clock +1 Collapse) \\
Fire / Life & Smoke, sparks, heat (–1 Effect) & Blaze, fever (Clock +1 Fire or 1 Harm) \\
Air / Luck & Scatter, misheard words (–1 Position) & Unlikely mishap (lose a tool or +1 SB) \\
Water / Dreams & Slippery, slow (–1 Effect) & Undertow, veering path (Clock +1 Flood) \\
Death / Obishaal & Whispers, chill (Shaken or Clock +1 Haunting) & Threshold opens (Clock +1 Crossing Due) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

\subsection{TAGS System for Free Casting}

Free casters build spells using \texttt{[TAGS]}. Each spell has a DV = 1 + number of TAGS (dangerous TAGS +2). Example: \texttt{[Burning] [Area] [Force]} = DV 4.

\paragraph{Common TAGS}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Elemental:} Burning, Freezing, Storm, Stone, Wave, Wind.
  \item \textbf{Force:} Force, Area, Strike, Wall, Bind, Dispel.
  \item \textbf{Mind/Veil:} Veil, Scry, Memory, Command, Fear.
  \item \textbf{Life/Body:} Mend, Purify, Strengthen, Waken, Beast.
  \item \textbf{Space/Motion (dangerous):} Leap, Fold, Gate, Gravity.
  \item \textbf{Creation (dangerous):} Create, Summon, Transmute, Animate.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{DV Examples}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{[Light]} (Cantrip) – DV 2, one roll.
  \item \texttt{[Burning] [Strike]} – DV 3, two actions.
  \item \texttt{[Fold] [Self]} – DV 3 (Fold is dangerous +2, plus 1 tag = 3).
\end{itemize}
