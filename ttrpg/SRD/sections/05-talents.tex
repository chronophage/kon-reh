\section{Talents}

Talents are the building blocks of character specialization. Each costs XP. Passive talents are always on; active talents require an action or reaction and cannot be used simultaneously unless noted.

\subsection{Talent Costs}

\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Cost} & \textbf{Category} \\
\midrule
2--3 XP & Minor edge (small bonus, once per scene). \\
4--6 XP & Major edge (permanent +1 die in a niche, access to magic). \\
7--10 XP & Prestige (campaign-defining). \\
11+ XP & Epic (legendary, story-shaping). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Acquiring New Talents}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Talents are purchased with XP during downtime.
  \item Training time: minor talents require 3--10 days; major talents require 1--2 weeks.
  \item Prestige and epic talents require narrative justification (finding a master, completing a quest, Patron bargain).
  \item A character may retrain one talent per downtime (replace with another of equal or lesser cost). The replaced talent is lost.
\end{itemize}
