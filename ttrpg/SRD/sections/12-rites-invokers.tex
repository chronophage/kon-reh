\section{Rites and Invokers}

This chapter covers universal rituals (available to any character with time and components) and the Invoker path (characters who channel Patrons through consecrated Symbols).

\subsection{Universal Rituals}

Any character, regardless of magical talent, may attempt a universal ritual. Requirements:

\begin{itemize}
  \item A clear intent (what you want to achieve).
  \item A method (e.g., summoning circle, prayer, sympathetic object).
  \item Components (consumed or used up).
  \item Time (at least one scene of uninterrupted work).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Ritual Resolution}
The GM sets a DV (typically 4–6) based on scope. The player rolls \textbf{Spirit + Lore} (or another appropriate skill).

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Clean Success:} The ritual works as intended.
  \item \textbf{Success \& Cost:} Works, but with a cost (backlash, extra time, compromised component). GM spends SB.
  \item \textbf{Partial:} Partial effect; player gains 1 Boon.
  \item \textbf{Miss:} Fails; GM may impose a complication (wrong spirit answers, ward backfires). Player gains 2 Boons.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Invokers (Symbol Path)}

Invokers channel power through consecrated \textbf{Symbols} — physical tokens tied to a specific Patron.

\subsubsection{Requirements}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Patron's Symbol (4 XP each):} Minor Asset. Grants ritual access to that Patron's Rites.
  \item One Symbol per Patron; Invokers may carry Symbols of multiple Patrons.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Ritual Invocation}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Takes \textbf{DV + 1 rounds} to complete.
  \item Display the Symbol openly (visible).
  \item Always marks \textbf{+1 Obligation} to that Patron.
  \item If the Symbol is displayed, DV –1 and Obligation –1 (minimum 0 Obligation).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Crack the Seal (Instant Cast)}
Resolve the Rite immediately (1 action) by:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Marking \textbf{+2 Obligation} (+3 for High Rites).
  \item Setting the Symbol to \textbf{Compromised} (inert until restored).
  \item The GM may immediately spend 1 SB on a thematic complication.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Restoring a Symbol}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Requires a Downtime action.
  \item Roll \textbf{Wits + Arcana or Lore} (DV 3).
  \item \textbf{Clean Success:} Symbol returns to Maintained.
  \item \textbf{Partial:} Symbol becomes Neglected (future rituals +1 Obligation).
  \item \textbf{Miss:} Symbol remains Compromised; requires a quest or Patron service to restore.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Multiple Symbols}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Carrying 4 or more different Symbols causes the first Invoker ritual each scene to mark +1 additional Obligation.
  \item Displaying a rival Patron's Symbol while invoking another worsens Position by 1 and marks +1 Obligation to the current Patron. On any rolled 1, the GM may trigger a Patron omen or glitch.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Borrowed Grace (Invoker Talent)}
\textbf{Cost:} 3–5 XP (minor talent). Once per scene, spend 1 Boon or 1 Fatigue (1 action) to gain:
\begin{itemize}
  \item +1 die to a single combat action, \textbf{or}
  \item +1 Effect to a social or stealth action.
\end{itemize}
Requires visibly displaying your Patron's Symbol. If the Symbol is Compromised, Borrowed Grace works at –1 die.

\subsection{Sample Invoker Rites by Patron}

These Low and Standard Rites are examples. See the full Player's Guide for complete lists.

\subsubsection{Oath of Flame \& Light (Dawn, Vows)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Rite of Kindle Vow (Low):} Bind a scene-long vow; bearer gains +1 die when fulfilling it.
  \item \textbf{Rite of Lay on Hands (Low):} Cleanse affliction, reduce Harm by 1, or remove 1 Fatigue.
  \item \textbf{Rite of Radiant Smite (Standard):} Next melee attack flares with light; +1 Harm, ignores undead armor.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{The Sealed Gate (Thresholds, Wards)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Rite of Sealed Threshold (Low):} Mark a doorway; unauthorized crossing requires Resolve test (DV 3).
  \item \textbf{Rite of Circle of Denial (Standard):} Protective circle; spirits/outsiders must resist (Resolve vs DV 4) to enter.
  \item \textbf{Rite of Banishment Knot (High):} Cast out a supernatural entity; test vs its Cap.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Ra\'eyn, Mistress of the Sea (Tides, Storms)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Rite of Tidemark's Blessing (Low):} Steady footing on wet surfaces; +1 die to balance/climb.
  \item \textbf{Rite of Whispering Currents (Low):} Learn safest route on water; +1 die to navigation.
  \item \textbf{Rite of Wave-Road Blessing (Standard):} Consecrate a sea lane; +2 dice to travel or evasion.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Ikasha, She Who Sleeps (Shadow, Secrets)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Touch the Umbral Veil (Low):} +1 die to one Stealth roll; soften a noise.
  \item \textbf{Rite of Crossroads Raven (Low):} Omen-raven gives +1 die to navigation or forces enemy hesitation.
  \item \textbf{Become the Shadow at the Crossroads (High):} Intangible to mundane harm; pass through gaps; +2 dice Stealth.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Obligation and Invokers}

Invokers track Obligation per Patron (see Section~\ref{sec:backlash-obligation}). Each ritual invocation marks +1 Obligation (reduced by displaying the Symbol). Crack the Seal marks +2 Obligation (+3 for High Rites). Obligation Capacity = \textbf{Spirit + Presence}. Exceeding capacity causes Fatigue.

\subsection{Ritual Interruption and Counterplay}
\begin{itemize}
  \item A ritual can be interrupted by damage, loud noise, or direct countermagic before completion.
  \item A character may use \texttt{[COUNTER]} (see Section~\ref{sec:tags-effects}) to disrupt a ritual in progress.
  \item If interrupted, the ritual fails; components may be lost. The GM gains 1 SB.
\end{itemize}
