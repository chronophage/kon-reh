\section{Core Mechanic}

\subsection{The Resolution Loop}

Every action that carries risk and uncertainty follows the same five steps.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Declare intent and approach.} The player states what they want to accomplish and how.
  \item \textbf{GM sets Position and Difficulty (DV).}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Position: Dominant (advantage), Controlled (normal), Desperate (high risk).
    \item DV: 2 (routine) to 5+ (extreme). Default DV = 3.
  \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Roll dice.} Pool = Attribute + Skill (d10s). Count successes:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item 6--9 = 1 success.
    \item 10 = 2 successes.
  \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Count Story Beats (SB).} Each die showing a 1 gives the GM 1 SB.
  \item \textbf{Apply the Outcome Matrix.}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Critical Success}
Rolling a \textbf{10} (counting as 2 successes) creates narrative opportunities beyond raw math:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{One 10}: Strong success—grant a \textit{free Boon}, improve Position by one step, or add descriptive flourish (e.g., ``Your arrow splits the bullseye, impressing the archery master'').
  \item \textbf{Two 10s}: Exceptional success—choose \textit{two benefits} (e.g., +2 effect + free Boon) or one powerful effect (e.g., ignore armor \textit{and} gain tactical advantage).
  \item \textbf{Three+ 10s}: Legendary success—resolve the conflict dramatically \textit{and} advance a secondary timer (e.g., clear 1 segment on a Rival's Trust Timer ) or create significant story development (e.g., uncover a hidden faction motive).
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Important:} \textbf{10s are never re-rolled} by Position effects (even in Desperate). Critical success effects apply \textit{only if the roll succeeds} (Successes $\geq$ DV).

\subsection{Difficulty Values (DV)}

\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Situation} \\
\midrule
2 & Routine, low stakes, nearly guaranteed success. \\
3 & Default — pressured or minor opposition. \\
4 & Hard — hostile conditions, active resistance. \\
5 & Extreme — multiple constraints, dramatic gambles. \\
6+ & Nearly impossible — requires exceptional setup or mythic aid. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

When in doubt, start at DV 3.

\subsection{Position \& Effect}

Position and Effect are set by the GM based on the fiction and the character's approach.

\subsubsection{Position (Risk)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Dominant:} You act from advantage. \textit{Re-roll one failure (die showing 5 or lower).}
  \item \textbf{Controlled:} Balanced, normal risk. \textit{No re-rolls.}
  \item \textbf{Desperate:} You act under duress. \textit{Re-roll one success (die showing 6 or higher).}
\end{itemize}

A die showing 10 is never re-rolled, even by Desperate position.

\subsubsection{Effect (Reward)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Limited:} Partial or weak outcome; the intent is achieved minimally.
  \item \textbf{Standard:} Expected outcome; the intent is achieved fully.
  \item \textbf{Great:} Strong or sweeping outcome; the intent is achieved with additional benefit.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Modifying Position and Effect}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The player may spend \textbf{1 Boon} before rolling to improve Position by one step.
  \item The GM may spend \textbf{Story Beats (SB)} to worsen Position or reduce Effect.
  \item If bonuses would raise Position above Dominant, each additional step becomes +1 die instead.
  \item If penalties would drop Position below Desperate, each additional step becomes –1 die instead.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Outcome Matrix}

\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{2.2cm}p{7cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Roll Result} & \textbf{Outcome} & \textbf{What Happens} \\
\midrule
Successes $\ge$ DV, SB = 0 & Clean Success & Intent achieved; no complication. \\
Successes $\ge$ DV, SB $>$ 0 & Success with SB & Intent achieved; GM spends SB for a complication. \\
$0 <$ Successes $<$ DV & Partial & Progress made; player gains 1 Boon. \\
Successes = 0 & Miss & No progress; player gains 2 Boons; GM escalates with SB. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Boons (Player Resource)}

Boons represent momentum, luck, or a second wind.

\subsubsection{Earning Boons}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Partial:} gain 1 Boon.
  \item \textbf{Miss:} gain 2 Boons (if the roll was meaningful and generated at least one SB).
  \item \textbf{Bond-driven action:} Once per bond per session, act on a Mutual Bond with an Intricate Description, gain 1 Boon.
  \item \textbf{GM award:} For creative solutions, sacrifices, or outstanding roleplay.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Spending Boons}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Re-roll a single die} after seeing the pool (keep the second result; new 1s generate additional SB).
  \item \textbf{Activate an Asset} (on-screen).
  \item \textbf{Improve Position} by one step before a roll.
  \item \textbf{Power certain Rites or abilities} as described.
  \item \textbf{Convert 2 Boons $\rightarrow$ 1 XP} (during downtime, once per session; max 2 XP per session).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Limits}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Maximum 5 Boons held at any time.
  \item At the end of each scene, reduce held Boons to a maximum of 2 (excess are lost).
  \item At most 2 Boons from failures per character per scene (repetition rule: same action + same stakes cannot award another Boon).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Story Beats (GM Resource)}

Every die showing a 1 generates 1 Story Beat for the GM.

\subsubsection{Spending SB}
Use SB to introduce complications, escalate pressure, or shift the fiction. See Section~\ref{sec:complications} for the full SB menu.

\textbf{Guidelines:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item 1 SB: minor pressure (noise, trace, tick a 4--6 segment timer +1).
  \item 2 SB: moderate setback (alarm raised, lose Position, lesser foe appears).
  \item 3 SB: serious trouble (reinforcements, key gear breaks, advance a campaign timer).
  \item 4+ SB: major turn (trap springs, authority arrives, environment shifts dramatically).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Assistance and Description}

\subsubsection{Assistance}
\begin{itemize}
  \item One helper per action.
  \item Total assist dice across all sources is capped at +3 (unless a talent states otherwise).
  \item The assistant accepts any risk named by the GM.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Description Ladder}
How you describe your action adds optional re-rolls (all 1s still generate SB):
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Basic:} roll as-is.
  \item \textbf{Detailed:} re-roll one die showing a 1.
  \item \textbf{Intricate:} re-roll all 1s; add a narrative flourish on success (no extra mechanical benefit).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Fatigue and Harm}

\subsubsection{Fatigue}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Fatigue track size = Body (minimum 1).
  \item Mark Fatigue from exertion (Charge/Skirmish, forced marches, magic costs, Obligation overflow).
  \item Each point of Fatigue worsens Position by one step (Dominant $\rightarrow$ Controlled $\rightarrow$ Desperate).
  \item If already Desperate, each additional Fatigue instead imposes a –1 die penalty on all rolls.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Harm}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Harm 1:} –1 die on actions directly related to the injury (GM discretion).
  \item \textbf{Harm 2:} –1 die on most actions until treated.
  \item \textbf{Harm 3:} incapacitated or dying.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Taking Harm Clears Fatigue (The Roller-Coaster Effect)}
When you take Harm, follow this order:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Convert incoming Harm to Fatigue using the \textbf{Armor Conversion Table}.
  \item Apply any remaining Harm. If Harm remains, clear all existing Fatigue, then increase your Harm level by the amount of Harm taken.
  \item Add the Fatigue from step 1 to your Fatigue track. If this fills or exceeds your track, increase Harm by 1 and clear all Fatigue.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Armor Conversion Table}

\begin{tabular}{@{}lccc@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Armor Type} & \textbf{Harm 1} & \textbf{Harm 2} & \textbf{Harm 3} \\
\midrule
Light  & Fatigue 1 & Harm 1 + Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Medium & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Heavy  & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 2 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit, even if conversion would give 0.

\subsubsection{Recovery}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Fatigue:} Short rest (1 hour) removes 2 Fatigue; a full night's rest removes all.
  \item \textbf{Harm:} Requires medical care (Medicine roll) or magical healing; natural recovery takes one week per level.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Maximum Dice Pool}
An individual character's maximum die pool is 10d10. Any additional dice beyond 10 are converted to automatic successes (1 auto-success per extra die).

\subsection{Fail Forward: Every Roll Matters}
\begin{itemize}
  \item No roll should result in ``nothing happens.''
  \item A Miss does not stop the story — it changes direction.
  \item Use SB to escalate, not to punish.
  \item Success with SB is not failure — it is success with interesting complications.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Over‑Stack Rule}

The Over‑Stack rule prevents trivializing scenes through excessive pre‑scene preparation.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Trigger:} The party enters a scene with \textbf{3 or more structural advantages} (see below). Check once, at scene framing.
  \item \textbf{Effect:} The GM chooses one:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Increase the DV of one obstacle or challenge by +1.
      \item Gain +1 Story Beat (SB) to spend during the scene.
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Limit:} Applies at most once per scene, regardless of how many advantages beyond the third.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{What Counts as a Structural Advantage?}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Two or more active scene‑long buffs on the party.
  \item An unused Follower Initiative from a previous scene.
  \item An Asset activation that grants a bonus at scene start.
  \item The opponent is Surprised, Trapped, or otherwise disadvantaged by prior setup.
  \item A ritual completed before the scene that directly affects the scene's conditions.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{What Does NOT Count?}
\begin{itemize}
  \item A single buff or spell.
  \item Advantages created \textbf{during} the scene (e.g., Setup actions, in‑scene Asset uses).
  \item Flavor or purely narrative preparation.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Design Intent:} Over‑Stack discourages pre‑scene buff stacking without punishing improvisation. Advantages gained during play are always free.
