\section{Core Mechanic}

\subsection{The Resolution Loop}

Every action that carries risk and uncertainty follows these five steps.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Declare intent and approach.} The player states what they want to accomplish and how (Attribute + Skill).
  \item \textbf{GM sets Position and Difficulty (DV).}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Position: Dominant (advantage), Controlled (normal), Desperate (high risk).
      \item DV: 2 (routine) to 5+ (extreme). Default DV = 3.
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Roll dice.} Pool = Attribute + Skill (d10s). Count successes:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item 6--9 = 1 success.
      \item 10 = 2 successes.
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Count Story Beats (SB).} Each die showing a 1 gives the GM 1 SB.
  \item \textbf{Apply the Outcome Matrix.}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Critical Success}

Rolling a \textbf{10} (counting as 2 successes) creates additional benefits:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{One 10:} Choose one -- gain a free Boon, improve Position by one step, or add a narrative flourish.
  \item \textbf{Two 10s:} Choose two benefits or one powerful effect (e.g., ignore armor + gain tactical advantage).
  \item \textbf{Three+ 10s:} Legendary success -- resolve the conflict dramatically and advance a secondary timer or create a significant story development.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Note:} 10s are never re-rolled (even in Desperate position). Critical effects apply only if the roll succeeds (Successes $\ge$ DV).

\subsection{Difficulty Values (DV)}

\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Situation} \\
\midrule
2 & Routine, low stakes, nearly guaranteed. \\
3 & Default -- pressured or minor opposition. \\
4 & Hard -- hostile conditions, active resistance. \\
5 & Extreme -- multiple constraints, dramatic gambles. \\
6+ & Nearly impossible -- requires exceptional setup or mythic aid. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Position \& Effect}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Position (Risk)}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Dominant:} Re-roll one failure (die $\le$ 5).
      \item \textbf{Controlled:} No re-rolls.
      \item \textbf{Desperate:} Re-roll one success (die $\ge$ 6). 10s are never re-rolled.
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Effect (Reward)}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Limited:} Minimal outcome.
      \item \textbf{Standard:} Expected outcome.
      \item \textbf{Great:} Strong outcome with additional benefit.
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Modifiers:}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Player may spend 1 Boon before rolling to improve Position by one step.
      \item GM may spend SB to worsen Position or reduce Effect.
      \item If bonuses would raise Position above Dominant, each extra step becomes +1 die.
      \item If penalties would drop Position below Desperate, each extra step becomes --1 die.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Outcome Matrix}

\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{2.2cm}p{7cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Roll Result} & \textbf{Outcome} & \textbf{What Happens} \\
\midrule
Successes $\ge$ DV, SB = 0 & Clean Success & Intent achieved; no complication. \\
Successes $\ge$ DV, SB $>$ 0 & Success with SB & Intent achieved; GM spends SB for a complication. \\
$0 <$ Successes $<$ DV & Partial & Progress made; player gains 1 Boon. \\
Successes = 0 & Miss & No progress; player gains 2 Boons; GM escalates with SB. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Boons (Player Resource)}

\subsubsection{Earning Boons}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Partial: gain 1 Boon.
  \item Miss: gain 2 Boons (if the roll was meaningful and generated at least one SB).
  \item Bond-driven action (once per bond per session): gain 1 Boon.
  \item GM award (creative solutions, sacrifices, outstanding roleplay).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Spending Boons}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Re-roll a single die after seeing the pool (keep second result; new 1s generate additional SB).
  \item Activate an Asset (on-screen).
  \item Improve Position by one step before a roll.
  \item Power certain Rites or abilities.
  \item Convert 2 Boons $\rightarrow$ 1 XP (downtime, once per session, max 2 XP).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Limits}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Max 5 Boons held.
  \item At end of each scene, reduce to max 2 (excess lost).
  \item At most 2 Boons from failures per character per scene (repetition of same action + stakes does not award another Boon).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Story Beats (GM Resource)}

Every die showing a 1 gives the GM 1 Story Beat (SB). Spend SB to introduce complications or escalate pressure.

\textbf{Guidelines:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item 1 SB: minor -- noise, trace, tick a 4--6 segment timer +1.
  \item 2 SB: moderate -- alarm raised, lose Position, lesser foe appears.
  \item 3 SB: serious -- reinforcements, key gear breaks, advance a campaign timer.
  \item 4+ SB: major -- trap springs, authority arrives, environment shifts dramatically.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{NPCs and Resources}

Non-player characters do \textbf{not} track Fatigue or Boons. If an ability would cause an NPC to lose these resources, convert it into a narrative consequence:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Instead of ``loses 1 Boon'' $\rightarrow$ ``hesitates -- loses next action''.
  \item Instead of ``marks 1 Fatigue'' $\rightarrow$ ``staggers -- next attack at Desperate Position''.
  \item Instead of ``loses 1 Boon and marks 1 Fatigue'' $\rightarrow$ ``drops weapon (disarmed) and stumbles back one range band''.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{NPC Special Abilities as SB Spends}

Instead of rolling for NPC abilities, the GM may spend Story Beats to trigger narrative events.

\textbf{Example SB Spends:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{1 SB:} ``The cultist's eyes burn -- mark 1 Fatigue (near miss).''
  \item \textbf{2 SB:} ``The spider drops on you -- you are Grappled (escape DV 3).''
  \item \textbf{3 SB:} ``The drake breathes frost -- everyone in Near, test Body+Athletics (DV 3) or take Harm 2.''
  \item \textbf{4+ SB:} ``The lich raises its hand -- test Spirit+Resolve (DV 5) or be Paralyzed for 1 exchange.''
\end{itemize}
The GM narrates the effect; players roll to resist.

\subsection{Assistance}

\begin{itemize}
  \item One helper per action.
  \item Total assist dice capped at +3 (unless a talent says otherwise).
  \item Assistant accepts any risk named by the GM.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Fatigue and Harm}

\subsubsection{Fatigue}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Track size = Body (minimum 1).
  \item Each point worsens Position by one step (Dominant $\rightarrow$ Controlled $\rightarrow$ Desperate).
  \item If already Desperate, each additional Fatigue imposes --1 die on all rolls.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Harm}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Harm Level} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
1 & --1 die on related actions (GM discretion). \\
2 & --1 die on most actions until treated. \\
3 & Incapacitated or dying. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsubsection{Taking Harm Clears Fatigue (Roller-Coaster)}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Convert incoming Harm to Fatigue using \textbf{Armor Conversion Table}.
  \item Apply any remaining Harm. If Harm remains, clear all existing Fatigue, then increase Harm level by amount taken.
  \item Add Fatigue from step 1 to Fatigue track. If track fills, increase Harm by 1 and clear all Fatigue.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Armor Conversion Table}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lccc@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Armor} & \textbf{Harm 1} & \textbf{Harm 2} & \textbf{Harm 3} \\
\midrule
Light  & Fatigue 1 & Harm 1 + Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Medium & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Heavy  & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 2 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit, even if conversion would give 0.

\subsubsection{Recovery}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Fatigue: Short rest (1 hour) removes 2; full night's rest removes all.
  \item Harm: Requires medical care (Medicine roll) or magical healing; natural recovery takes one week per level.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Wards and Traps}

Wards are magical barriers that block passage, teleportation, and shadow-walking. Traps are mundane or magical hazards that trigger on intrusion. Both use the same base resolution structure.

\subsubsection{Wards (Magical Barriers)}

A ward is defined by its \textbf{Base DV} (2--5), chosen by the GM when the ward is placed. The ward's other properties derive from this DV:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Harm:} A creature that triggers the ward suffers Harm equal to \texttt{max(0, Base DV - 2)}.
    \item \textbf{Spirit Leash:} A spirit (including summoned creatures) attempting to cross the ward suffers Leash ticks equal to the ward's Base DV.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Crossing a Ward}
A creature cannot teleport, shadow-walk, or use any effect that bypasses intervening space to cross a ward without first overcoming it. To cross, the creature must succeed on a test of the appropriate attribute (usually Wits or Presence) \textbf{+ Spirit} against the ward's Base DV. On a failure, the crossing fails and the creature suffers the ward's Harm (if any). On a success, the creature crosses but may still suffer other consequences (e.g., alerting the ward's creator).

\paragraph{Disabling a Ward}
A character may attempt to disable a ward with a \textbf{Wits + Arcana} test against the ward's Base DV. Success renders the ward inert for one scene (or permanently, at the GM's discretion for non-recurring wards). Alternatively, a spell with the \textbf{[DISPEL]} or \textbf{[FATE]} tags may suppress or destroy the ward as described in the spell's effect.

\subsubsection{Traps (Mundane or Magical)}

Traps function similarly to wards but do \textbf{not} impede teleportation or shadow-walking, nor do they inflict Leash ticks on spirits. A trap has a Base DV (2--5) and deals Harm equal to \texttt{max(0, Base DV - 2)} when triggered.

\paragraph{Disarming a Trap}
A trap can be disarmed with a \textbf{Wits + Subterfuge} test against its Base DV. On a success, the trap is neutralised. On a failure, the trap may trigger (GM's discretion).

\paragraph{Example}
\begin{quote}
    A \textbf{Lesser Wardstone} (DV 3) bars a doorway. A character attempting to shadow-walk past must roll \textbf{Wits + Spirit} (DV 3). Failure inflicts \textbf{Harm 1} (since 3--2 = 1). A spirit crossing the same ward gains \textbf{3 Leash ticks}. A \textbf{Wits + Arcana} (DV 3) success disables the ward.
\end{quote}

\subsection{Maximum Dice Pool}

Maximum die pool = 10d10. Any additional dice beyond 10 become automatic successes (1 auto-success per extra die).
