\section{Core Mechanic}

\subsection{The Resolution Loop}

Every action that carries risk and uncertainty follows these five steps.

\begin{enumerate}
  ℹtem \textbf{Declare intent and approach.} The player states what they want to accomplish and how (Attribute + Skill).
  ℹtem \textbf{GM sets Position and Difficulty (DV).}
    \begin{itemize}
      ℹtem Position: Dominant (advantage), Controlled (normal), Desperate (high risk).
      ℹtem DV: 2 (routine) to 5+ (extreme). Default DV = 3.
    \end{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Roll dice.} Pool = Attribute + Skill (d10s). Count successes:
    \begin{itemize}
      ℹtem 6–9 = 1 success.
      ℹtem 10 = 2 successes.
    \end{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Count Story Beats (SB).} Each die showing a 1 gives the GM 1 SB.
  ℹtem \textbf{Apply the Outcome Matrix.}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Critical Success}

Rolling a \textbf{10} (counting as 2 successes) creates additional benefits:
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{One 10:} Choose one – gain a free Boon, improve Position by one step, or add a narrative flourish.
  ℹtem \textbf{Two 10s:} Choose two benefits or one powerful effect (e.g., ignore armor + gain tactical advantage).
  ℹtem \textbf{Three+ 10s:} Legendary success – resolve the conflict dramatically and advance a secondary timer or create a significant story development.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Note:} 10s are never re−rolled (even in Desperate position). Critical effects apply only if the roll succeeds (Successes $≥$ DV).

\subsection{Difficulty Values (DV)}

\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
⊤rule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Situation} \\
∣rule
2 & Routine, low stakes, nearly guaranteed. \\
3 & Default – pressured or minor opposition. \\
4 & Hard – hostile conditions, active resistance. \\
5 & Extreme – multiple constraints, dramatic gambles. \\
6+ & Nearly impossible – requires exceptional setup or mythic aid. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Position & Effect}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Position (Risk)}
    \begin{itemize}
      ℹtem \textbf{Dominant:} Re−roll one failure (die $≤$ 5).
      ℹtem \textbf{Controlled:} No re−rolls.
      ℹtem \textbf{Desperate:} Re−roll one success (die $≥$ 6). 10s are never re−rolled.
    \end{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Effect (Reward)}
    \begin{itemize}
      ℹtem \textbf{Limited:} Minimal outcome.
      ℹtem \textbf{Standard:} Expected outcome.
      ℹtem \textbf{Great:} Strong outcome with additional benefit.
    \end{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Modifiers:}
    \begin{itemize}
      ℹtem Player may spend 1 Boon before rolling to improve Position by one step.
      ℹtem GM may spend SB to worsen Position or reduce Effect.
      ℹtem If bonuses would raise Position above Dominant, each extra step becomes +1 die.
      ℹtem If penalties would drop Position below Desperate, each extra step becomes –1 die.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Outcome Matrix}

\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{2.2cm}p{7cm}@{}}
⊤rule
\textbf{Roll Result} & \textbf{Outcome} & \textbf{What Happens} \\
∣rule
Successes $≥$ DV, SB = 0 & Clean Success & Intent achieved; no complication. \\
Successes $≥$ DV, SB $>$ 0 & Success with SB & Intent achieved; GM spends SB for a complication. \\
$0 <$ Successes $<$ DV & Partial & Progress made; player gains 1 Boon. \\
Successes = 0 & Miss & No progress; player gains 2 Boons; GM escalates with SB. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Boons (Player Resource)}

⫕section{Earning Boons}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Partial: gain 1 Boon.
  ℹtem Miss: gain 2 Boons (if the roll was meaningful and generated at least one SB).
  ℹtem Bond−driven action (once per bond per session): gain 1 Boon.
  ℹtem GM award (creative solutions, sacrifices, outstanding roleplay).
\end{itemize}

⫕section{Spending Boons}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Re−roll a single die after seeing the pool (keep second result; new 1s generate additional SB).
  ℹtem Activate an Asset (on−screen).
  ℹtem Improve Position by one step before a roll.
  ℹtem Power certain Rites or abilities.
  ℹtem Convert 2 Boons $→$ 1 XP (downtime, once per session, max 2 XP).
\end{itemize}

⫕section{Limits}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Max 5 Boons held.
  ℹtem At end of each scene, reduce to max 2 (excess lost).
  ℹtem At most 2 Boons from failures per character per scene (repetition of same action + stakes does not award another Boon).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Story Beats (GM Resource)}

Every die showing a 1 gives the GM 1 Story Beat (SB). Spend SB to introduce complications or escalate pressure.

\textbf{Guidelines:}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem 1 SB: minor – noise, trace, tick a 4–6 segment timer +1.
  ℹtem 2 SB: moderate – alarm raised, lose Position, lesser foe appears.
  ℹtem 3 SB: serious – reinforcements, key gear breaks, advance a campaign timer.
  ℹtem 4+ SB: major – trap springs, authority arrives, environment shifts dramatically.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{NPCs and Resources}

Non‑player characters do \textbf{not} track Fatigue or Boons. If an ability would cause an NPC to lose these resources, convert it into a narrative consequence:

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Instead of ``loses 1 Boon'' → ``hesitates – loses next action''.
  ℹtem Instead of ``marks 1 Fatigue'' → ``staggers – next attack at Desperate Position''.
  ℹtem Instead of ``loses 1 Boon and marks 1 Fatigue'' → ``drops weapon (disarmed) and stumbles back one range band''.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{NPC Special Abilities as SB Spends}

Instead of rolling for NPC abilities, the GM may spend Story Beats to trigger narrative events.

\textbf{Example SB Spends:}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{1 SB:} ``The cultist's eyes burn – mark 1 Fatigue (near miss).''
  ℹtem \textbf{2 SB:} ``The spider drops on you – you are Grappled (escape DV 3).''
  ℹtem \textbf{3 SB:} ``The drake breathes frost – everyone in Near, test Body+Athletics (DV 3) or take Harm 2.''
  ℹtem \textbf{4+ SB:} ``The lich raises its hand – test Spirit+Resolve (DV 5) or be Paralyzed for 1 exchange.''
\end{itemize}
The GM narrates the effect; players roll to resist.

\subsection{Assistance}

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem One helper per action.
  ℹtem Total assist dice capped at +3 (unless a talent says otherwise).
  ℹtem Assistant accepts any risk named by the GM.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Fatigue and Harm}

⫕section{Fatigue}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Track size = Body (minimum 1).
  ℹtem Each point worsens Position by one step (Dominant $→$ Controlled $→$ Desperate).
  ℹtem If already Desperate, each additional Fatigue imposes –1 die on all rolls.
\end{itemize}

⫕section{Harm}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
⊤rule
\textbf{Harm Level} & \textbf{Effect} \\
∣rule
1 & –1 die on related actions (GM discretion). \\
2 & –1 die on most actions until treated. \\
3 & Incapacitated or dying. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}

⫕section{Taking Harm Clears Fatigue (Roller−Coaster)}
\begin{enumerate}
  ℹtem Convert incoming Harm to Fatigue using \textbf{Armor Conversion Table}.
  ℹtem Apply any remaining Harm. If Harm remains, clear all existing Fatigue, then increase Harm level by amount taken.
  ℹtem Add Fatigue from step 1 to Fatigue track. If track fills, increase Harm by 1 and clear all Fatigue.
\end{enumerate}

⫕section{Armor Conversion Table}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lccc@{}}
⊤rule
\textbf{Armor} & \textbf{Harm 1} & \textbf{Harm 2} & \textbf{Harm 3} \\
∣rule
Light  & Fatigue 1 & Harm 1 + Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Medium & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Heavy  & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 2 \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}
Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit, even if conversion would give 0.

⫕section{Recovery}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Fatigue: Short rest (1 hour) removes 2; full night's rest removes all.
  ℹtem Harm: Requires medical care (Medicine roll) or magical healing; natural recovery takes one week per level.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Maximum Dice Pool}

Maximum die pool = 10d10. Any additional dice beyond 10 become automatic successes (1 auto−success per extra die).

\subsection{Over‑Stack Rule}

To prevent trivializing scenes through excessive pre‑scene preparation:

\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Trigger:} Party enters a scene with 3 or more \textbf{structural advantages} (checked once, at scene framing).
  ℹtem \textbf{Effect:} GM chooses one – increase DV of one obstacle by +1, or gain +1 SB.
  ℹtem \textbf{Limit:} Applies at most once per scene.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Structural Advantages (examples):}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Two or more scene‑long buffs on the party.
  ℹtem Unused Follower Initiative from previous scene.
  ℹtem Asset activation granting a bonus at scene start.
  ℹtem Opponent is Surprised, Trapped, or disadvantaged by prior setup.
  ℹtem Ritual completed before the scene that affects the scene's conditions.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Not structural:}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Single buff or spell.
  ℹtem Advantages created during the scene (e.g., Setup action).
  ℹtem Flavor or purely narrative preparation.
\end{itemize}
