\subsection{Scene Example: The Bridge at Dusk}

This example shows the core resolution loop in practice across multiple player actions.

\subsubsection{Setup}

The party---Lyra (rogue), Thane (fighter), and Sera (caster)---is on a narrow stone bridge overlooking a ravine. Cultists are chanting on the far side, completing a ritual that will summon a demon. The GM has already established a \textbf{Ritual Completion [6]} timer with 3 segments filled.

\textbf{GM:} ``You're on the bridge. The cultists are 40 feet away, their chant rising. You have 3 segments left on the Ritual Completion timer. What do you do?''

\subsubsection{Player Action 1: Lyra Scouts Ahead}

\textbf{Lyra:} ``I creep forward, keeping to the shadow of the bridge's railing, trying to see how many cultists there are and what they're doing.''

\textbf{GM:} ``Controlled, DV 3. On a Miss, you dislodge a stone and they notice you.''

\textbf{Lyra rolls} (Wits 4 + Stealth 2 = 6 dice): 9, 7, 4, 3, 2, 1.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Successes:} 2 (9, 7) vs DV 3 $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Partial}.
  \item \textbf{SB:} The 1 gives the GM 1 Story Beat.
  \item \textbf{Boons:} Lyra gains 1 Boon.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{GM:} ``You get a good look. There are six cultists---four chanting around a stone altar, two watching the bridge. You count the segments: the ritual is at 4 of 6. They're close. But your foot scrapes a loose stone. The watch cultists are looking your way.''

\textbf{Lyra:} ``I freeze.''

\textbf{GM:} ``They don't see you. But they're alert now. What does everyone else do?''

\subsubsection{Player Action 2: Thane Charges}

\textbf{Thane:} ``I charge the watch cultists while they're distracted. I want to take them out before they raise the alarm.''

\textbf{GM:} ``Charge is a Full Turn Action. Move two bands and attack. Costs 1 Fatigue. Your next defense is Desperate. The bridge is narrow---open ground, so no penalty.''

\textbf{GM:} ``Controlled, DV 3. On a Miss, you stumble and the cultists get a free shot.''

\textbf{Thane rolls} (Body 3 + Melee 3 = 6 dice): 10, 8, 6, 5, 4, 2.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Successes:} 4 (10 counts as 2, 8, 6) vs DV 3 $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Clean Success}.
  \item \textbf{SB:} None.
  \item \textbf{Boons:} None.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{GM:} ``You barrel into the first cultist---he goes down hard (Harm 2 from your medium weapon). The second cultist stumbles back. The bridge shakes. I'll tick \textbf{Collapse [4]} +1---you're at 1 of 4. The ritual's chant falters, but doesn't stop.''

\textbf{Thane:} ``I'm in the middle of them now. Desperate defense?''

\textbf{GM:} ``Yes. Your next defense is Desperate.''

\subsubsection{Player Action 3: Sera Casts a Spell}

\textbf{Sera:} ``I cast a [FIRE][AREA] burst to scatter the remaining cultists and disrupt the ritual. I want to hit the altar.''

\textbf{GM:} ``That's two tags---DV 4 (1 + 2 tags). It's a two-action spell (Weave then Cast). You're not in melee, so Controlled Position. On a Miss, backlash.''

\textbf{Sera:} ``I weave.'' (Action 1)

\textbf{GM:} ``The fire gathers in your palm. The cultists see you. The chant quickens. Roll to cast.''

\textbf{Sera rolls} (Wits 4 + Arcana 2 = 6 dice): 1, 1, 8, 7, 6, 3.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Successes:} 3 (8, 7, 6) vs DV 4 $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Partial}.
  \item \textbf{SB:} Two 1s $\rightarrow$ GM gains 2 Story Beats.
  \item \textbf{Boons:} Sera gains 1 Boon.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{GM:} ``The fire bursts outward. Two cultists are thrown back (Harm 1 each). The altar cracks---the ritual chant stutters. But the backlash: fire licks back at you. Mark 1 Fatigue, and the bridge timbers catch fire. I'll tick \textbf{Collapse [4]} +1---you're at 2 of 4. Also, I'll spend 1 SB: the lead cultist survives and screams, 'The Ninth will rise!'''

\subsubsection{Spending Story Beats}

The GM now has 3 SB total (1 from Lyra, 2 from Sera). The GM spends 2 SB:

\textbf{GM:} ``I spend 2 SB. The lead cultist's scream echoes---and the bridge groans. You feel the stone shift. Tick \textbf{Collapse [4]} +1 (now 3 of 4). And a new timer: \textbf{Reinforcements [6]} starts ticking---something is coming from the far side of the ravine.''

\subsubsection{Resolution}

The party has 1 segment left on the Ritual Completion timer, 1 segment before the bridge collapses, and a new threat incoming. The scene escalates.

\textbf{GM:} ``The bridge groans. The fire is spreading. The lead cultist is reaching for a bell. What do you do?''

\subsubsection{What This Example Teaches}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Position and DV} are set based on fiction---not arbitrary numbers.
  \item \textbf{Partials} give Boons and progress, but with costs.
  \item \textbf{1s give SB} even on successful rolls.
  \item \textbf{SB are spent} to escalate tension, not punish.
  \item \textbf{Timers} are visible and ticked openly.
  \item \textbf{Fail forward:} Even Partials and Misses change the situation---the story never stalls.
\end{itemize}
