\section{Encounters}

All encounters – combat, social, environmental, magical – use the same core engine: 
\textbf{Position}, \textbf{Effect}, \textbf{DV}, \textbf{Boons}, and \textbf{Story Beats (SB)}. 
Clocks track progress toward resolution.

\subsection{Common Framework}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{State Intent \& Approach.} What do you want to achieve, and how?
  \item \textbf{Set Position \& DV.} GM determines risk (Dominant/Controlled/Desperate) and difficulty (2–7+).
  \item \textbf{Roll Attribute + Skill.} Each 6+ = success; each 1 = SB for the GM.
  \item \textbf{Apply Outcome.} Use the Outcome Matrix (Clean Success, Success \& Cost, Partial, Miss).
  \item \textbf{Spend SB.} GM introduces complications, shifts Position, or advances clocks.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Clocks in Encounters}
Clocks track cumulative progress or increasing pressure:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Progress Clock (4–8 segments):} Fill to succeed (e.g., Persuasion, Escaping a maze).
  \item \textbf{Threat Clock (4–8 segments):} Fill to trigger a disaster (e.g., reinforcements, structural collapse).
  \item \textbf{Contest Clocks:} Two clocks – one for each side; first to fill wins.
\end{itemize}
Tick clocks on Partial (1 tick) or Clean Success (2 ticks). Misses may tick opposition clocks.

% ============================================================
\section{Combat Encounters}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Rhythm of Battle}
No fixed initiative. GM spotlights based on fiction. Each turn: \textbf{1 Action + 1 Move}.

\subsubsection*{Movement Basics}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Move:} Shift one range band (Close$\leftrightarrow$Near$\leftrightarrow$Far) or reposition within same band.
  \item \textbf{Dash (Action):} Move two bands; your next defense is Desperate.
  \item \textbf{Disengage (Action):} Then your Move does not provoke opportunity attacks. You cannot Attack this turn.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Opportunity Attacks}
Leaving an enemy’s \textbf{Close} range without Disengage provokes one free melee attack (Controlled Position, no action cost).

\subsubsection*{Charge \& Skirmish (Full Turn Actions)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Charge:} Move two bands toward an enemy and make one melee attack. Costs 1 Fatigue. Next defense Desperate. Requires open ground.
  \item \textbf{Skirmish:} Move two bands away from an enemy and make one ranged attack. Costs 1 Fatigue. Next defense Desperate. Requires open ground.
\end{itemize}
Talents (e.g., \textit{Effortless Charge}, \textit{Relentless}) modify these.

\subsection{Health, Fatigue, and Harm}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Fatigue Track} = Body. Mark from exertion, Charge/Skirmish, magic.
  \item \textbf{Effect of Fatigue:} Each Fatigue worsens Position one step (Dominant$\to$Controlled$\to$Desperate). If already Desperate, apply –1 die per Fatigue.
  \item \textbf{Overflow:} When Fatigue fills, increase Harm by 1 level and clear Fatigue.
  \item \textbf{Harm Levels:} Harm 1 (–1 die), Harm 2 (–2 dice), Harm 3 (incapacitated/dying).
  \item \textbf{Recovery:} Short rest removes 1–2 Fatigue; full night removes all. Harm requires medical/magical care.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Weapons \& Armor (Condensed)}
See the full Equipment section for details. Quick reference:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Light Weapon:} +2d at Close, +1d at Near (Fast tempo).
  \item \textbf{Medium Weapon:} +1d at Close, +2d at Near (Standard tempo).
  \item \textbf{Heavy Weapon:} –1d at Close, +3d at Near (Slow tempo).
  \item \textbf{Armor Conversion:} Light (1 Harm→1 Fatigue), Medium (2→1), Heavy (3→2).
\end{itemize}
Shields and tags (Accurate, Brutal, etc.) are optional.

\section{Social Encounters}
% ============================================================

Use \textbf{Persuasion Clocks} for extended negotiations, debates, or intimidation.

\subsection{Setting Up}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Target’s Resolve:} Easy (4-segment), Moderate (6-segment), Hard (8-segment).
  \item \textbf{Initial Position:} Based on relationship, leverage, and setting (Dominant = mutual trust, Desperate = hostile audience).
  \item \textbf{DV:} Use the Standard Ladder (3–7+) with modifiers for cultural gaps, time pressure, etc.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Non-Face Characters Can Help}
Even without high social skills, non‑face characters can contribute:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Setup:} Create an advantage – produce evidence, cause a distraction, or perform a display of force. Roll an appropriate skill (e.g., Stealth to slip a note, Intimidation to glare). On success, grant the primary speaker +1 Position or +1 die.
  \item \textbf{Assist:} Spend 1 Boon to give +1 die (narrated as a timely interjection or meaningful glance).
  \item \textbf{Interrupt:} On a Miss, a non‑face character can take the fallout (e.g., absorb a social rebuke) by marking 1 Fatigue.
\end{itemize}
This ensures everyone can engage, not just the “face” character.

\subsection{Persuasion Rolls}
Each distinct argument or lever advances the clock:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Clean Success:} +2 segments.
  \item \textbf{Partial:} +1 segment, but GM may spend SB for a complication (e.g., “They agree, but you’ll owe a favor”).
  \item \textbf{Miss:} No progress; GM may tick an \textbf{Opposition Clock} (4–6 segments). When the Opposition Clock fills, the target hardens – Position worsens or the ask becomes impossible.
\end{itemize}
When the Persuasion Clock fills, the target agrees as framed.

\section{Environmental Encounters}
% ============================================================

Hazards, terrain, and exploration use the same mechanics.

\subsection{Terrain and Positioning}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Height Advantage:} +1 die or +1 Effect for attacks from above.
  \item \textbf{Cover:} Partial cover (+1 DV to target you), Full cover (cannot be targeted directly). You can use an action to \textbf{Hide} behind full cover (Stealth vs Perception). Once hidden, you are not a valid target until you reveal yourself or an enemy uses an action to \textbf{Search}.
  \item \textbf{Difficult Terrain:} Costs 2 Move actions to traverse one band (or use a full action to Dash). Charge/Skirmish require open ground unless you have the \textit{Relentless} talent.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Hazards and Clocks}
Active hazards (fire, flooding, toxic gas) are represented by \textbf{Threat Clocks}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Tick the clock} on missed rolls, GM SB spends, or at scene intervals.
  \item When the clock fills, the hazard triggers (e.g., collapse, wave of fire).
  \item Characters may attempt to \textbf{slow} or \textbf{disable} the hazard (e.g., Tinker to seal a gas leak, Magic to freeze spreading flames). Treat as a Progress Clock.
\end{itemize}
Example: \textit{Collapsing Bridge [6]} – ticks on each Partial or Miss. At 3 segments, footing becomes Desperate; at 6, the bridge falls.

\subsection{Example: Chase Through Ruins}
Use a \textbf{Contest Clock} – flee clock vs. pursuit clock. Characters can roll Athletics to sprint, Stealth to hide, or Subterfuge to mislead. Successes tick their side’s clock. SB can introduce obstacles (loose rubble, dead ends).

\section{Magical \& Miscellaneous Encounters}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Magic in Combat}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Caster (Freeform):} Requires \textit{Spellcraft}. Two actions: \emph{Weave} then \emph{Cast}. May \emph{Push} (mark Fatigue) to compress into one action.
  \item \textbf{Runekeeper:} Invoke Rites as 1 action; mark Obligation. May \emph{Push It} (+1 Obligation) for extra effect.
  \item \textbf{Invoker:} Ritual invocation takes DV+1 rounds. \textit{Crack the Seal} (instant) marks +2 Obligation and sets Symbol to Compromised.
  \item \textbf{Cantor:} Sing a Song (1 action, resolves next turn). Push to resolve immediately (mark Corruption).
\end{itemize}
Casting while Engaged worsens Position by one step unless the spell is [TOUCH] or [WARD] (self).

\subsection{Traps \& Wards}
Treat traps as a \textbf{Threat Clock} that triggers on a Miss or when SB is spent. Disabling a trap uses Tinker (mechanical) or Arcana (magical). 
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Discover:} Notice vs DV (4–6).
  \item \textbf{Disable:} Contested roll against trap’s “tick” – on Clean Success, trap neutralized; on Partial, disable but generate SB (e.g., noise, partial trigger); on Miss, trap activates.
  \item \textbf{Activation:} GM spends SB to trigger the trap (damage, condition, clock advance).
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Wards} are magical barriers or alarms. They have a \textbf{DV} set at creation (typically 3–6). A character may attempt to dispel a ward with Arcana + Lore vs. its DV. If the ward is \emph{crossed} without dispelling (e.g., a summoned spirit passing through, or a character forcing through), the crossing triggers the ward’s effect and ticks any relevant \textbf{Leash} (see below) by an amount equal to the ward’s DV.

\subsection{Summoning and Spirits}
Summoned spirits have a \textbf{Leash} (Cap + Spirit segments). The Leash ticks when:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The spirit takes harm (1 segment per Harm level).
  \item The spirit acts against its nature (1 segment).
  \item The spirit \textbf{crosses a [WARD]} without dispelling it – tick segments equal to the ward’s DV.
\end{itemize}
When the Leash fills, the spirit acts once to its nature (potentially hostile) and departs. The summoner can spend Boons to clear Leash ticks (once per round, 1 Boon = 1 tick). Dispelling or bypassing a ward (e.g., with a successful Arcana roll) prevents the tick. See the full Summoning rules for details.

\subsection{Extended Rituals and Downtime Encounters}
For activities that take hours or days (researching a ward, brewing a potion, interrogating a prisoner), use a \textbf{Progress Clock} (4–12 segments). Each segment represents a scene or a block of time. Players describe their approach and roll an appropriate skill; success advances the clock. Complications (GM SB) may add segments, require rare components, or draw unwanted attention.

\subsubsection*{GM Reminders}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Let non‑specialists contribute} – use Setup and Assist actions, and allow creative skill use.
  \item \textbf{Spend SB to escalate, not punish.} A well‑spent SB changes the situation, creating new choices.
  \item \textbf{Clocks make progress visible.} When a clock ticks, describe what changes in the fiction.
\end{itemize}
