# Fate's Edge — System Summary

## Introduction

Fate's Edge is a narrative‑first tabletop RPG where every action carries weight, every choice has consequence, and every spell risks backlash. This document summarises the core rules and major systems. For complete rules, see the *Player's Guide* and *GM Guide*.

## Core Resolution

### The 90‑Second Loop
1. **Declare** intent and approach (Attribute + Skill).
2. **GM sets** Difficulty Value (DV) and Position.
3. **Roll** dice pool (Attribute + Skill d10s).
4. **Count successes** (6–9 = 1 success; 10 = 2 successes) and **Story Beats** (each 1 = 1 SB for the GM).
5. **Compare** successes to DV → Outcome Matrix.
6. **Apply** outcome; GM spends SB for complications.

### Difficulty Value (DV)
| DV | Situation |
|----|-----------|
| 2  | Routine, almost guaranteed |
| 3  | Pressured (default), mild opposition |
| 4  | Hard, serious resistance |
| 5+ | Extreme, dramatic gamble |

### Position (Risk)
| Position | Mechanical Effect |
|----------|------------------|
| Dominant | Re‑roll one failure |
| Controlled | No re‑rolls |
| Desperate | Re‑roll one success |

Spend 1 Boon to improve Position by one step before rolling.

### Outcome Matrix
| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Successes ≥ DV, SB = 0 | **Clean Success** – intent achieved, no complication |
| Successes ≥ DV, SB > 0 | **Success with SB** – intent achieved; GM spends SB for twist |
| 0 < Successes < DV | **Partial** – progress made; you gain 1 Boon |
| Successes = 0 | **Miss** – no progress; you gain 2 Boons; GM escalates |

### Critical Success (10s)
A 10 counts as two successes. If the roll succeeds:
- **One 10**: Strong success – improve Position or gain minor advantage.
- **Two 10s**: Exceptional – choose two benefits.
- **Three 10s**: Legendary – clear 1 segment from a relevant timer.
A 10 is never re‑rolled.

## Resources

### Boons (Player Currency)
- **Hold**: up to 5. At end of scene, reduce to 2 (excess lost).
- **Earn**: Partial (1), Miss (2), bond‑driven action, GM award.
- **Spend 1 Boon to**:
  - Re‑roll a single die.
  - Improve Position by one step.
  - Activate an on‑screen Asset.
  - Convert 2 Boons → 1 XP (once per session, max 2 XP).

### Story Beats (GM Currency)
Each 1 on a player’s die gives the GM 1 SB. Spend SB to complicate the scene:
- **1 SB**: Minor – noise, tick a timer, small setback.
- **2 SB**: Moderate – alarm raised, lose advantage, worsen Position.
- **3+ SB**: Major – reinforcements, scene shift, break an asset.

## Fatigue & Harm

### Fatigue
- Each point worsens Position one step (Dominant → Controlled → Desperate).
- If already Desperate, each Fatigue imposes –1 die.
- Track size = Body. When track fills: increase Harm by 1, clear all Fatigue.
- **Recovery**: Short rest (1 hour) removes 2 Fatigue; full night’s rest removes all.

### Harm
| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Harm 1 | –1 die on related actions |
| Harm 2 | –1 die on most actions |
| Harm 3 | Incapacitated or dying |

Taking Harm clears all existing Fatigue (the “roller‑coaster” effect).

## Attributes & Skills

### Attributes (1–5)
- **Body** – strength, agility, endurance
- **Wits** – perception, quick thinking
- **Spirit** – willpower, intuition
- **Presence** – charisma, leadership

### Skills (0–5)
- **Combat**: Melee, Ranged, Brawl, Tactics
- **Physical**: Athletics, Stealth, Endurance, Craft, Survival
- **Social**: Sway, Command, Deception, Performance, Insight
- **Knowledge**: Lore, Investigation, Medicine
- **Magic**: Arcana, Ritual

Dice pool = Attribute + Skill.

## Combat Quick Reference
- **Turn**: 1 Action + 1 Move. Move shifts one range band (Close ↔ Near ↔ Far).
- **Opportunity Attack**: Leaving Close without Disengage provokes one free melee attack.
- **Sidestrike**: Move one band as part of a melee attack; worsen Position by one step.
- **Shove**: Opposed Body+Athletics; push target one band.
- **Grapple**: Opposed Body+Melee; both Engaged; target suffers –1 die.

## Magic Systems

### Paths of Magic
| Path | Access | Core Mechanic | Primary Risk |
|------|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Free Caster** | Spellcraft (6 XP) | Weave & Cast (TAGS) | Backlash (Harm/Fatigue) |
| **Runekeeper** | Familiar (2 XP) + Codex (4 XP) | Invoke Rites (1 action) | Obligation |
| **Invoker** | Patron’s Symbol (4 XP each) | Ritual Invocation (slow) | Symbol compromise |
| **Cantor** | Cantor’s Path (8 XP) | Songs (resolve next turn) | Corruption |
| **Summoner** | Pact‑Whisperer (2 XP) + Lesser Pactwright (2 XP) | Call + Bind spirits | Leash (spirit breaks free) |

### TAGS System (Free Casting)
- DV = 1 + number of TAGS (dangerous tags add +2 instead of +1).
- Casting roll: Wits + Arcana.
- **Backlash** (on Miss or GM spending SB): Harm 2 (Arcane), +2 Fatigue, Corruption +1, or catastrophic side effect.

### Dangerous TAGS (examples)
Leap, Fold, Gate, Gravity, Create, Summon, Transmute, Animate, Transform, Dominate, Rewind, Resurrect.

### Healing
- **[HEAL]** cantrip (DV 2): remove 1 Fatigue.
- Healing Harm requires [HEAL] + another tag (Purify, Strengthen, Cleanse) and a +2 DV surcharge (minimum DV 4).
- Complications from SB: scars, spirit attention, Patron demands.

## Character Creation (Starting at Tier I)
- **32 XP** base, up to +4 XP from Bonds and Complications (max 36 XP).
- **Attributes**: new rating × 3 XP. Recommended: primary 3 (9 XP), secondaries 2 each (6 XP).
- **Skills**: new level × 2 XP. Spend 8–12 XP on key skills (levels 2–3).
- **Talents**: 1–3 talents (2–6 XP each).
- **Bonds**: +2 XP each (max 2). Once per session, act on bond with intricate description → both gain 1 Boon.
- **Complications**: +2 XP each (max 2). Each unresolved adds +1 banked SB to early scenes.
- **Starting gear**: free (clothing, light/medium weapon, light armor, backpack, etc.).

## Advancement

### Earning XP (per session)
- **Attendance**: +2 XP
- **Major objectives**: +2–4 XP
- **Discoveries**: +1–2 XP
- **Hard choices**: +1–2 XP
- **Story engagement**: +1–3 XP
- **Personal goals**: +1–2 XP
- **Arc completion bonus**: +8–12 XP

### Spending XP
| Improvement | Cost | Downtime |
|-------------|------|----------|
| Attribute increase | new rating × 3 XP | new rating days |
| Skill increase | new level × 2 XP | new level days |
| Minor Talent | 2–3 XP | 3–10 days |
| Major Talent | 4–6 XP | 1–2 weeks |
| Prestige Talent | 7–10 XP | 1 month |
| Epic Talent | 11+ XP | varies |
| Minor Asset | 4 XP | 1 day |
| Standard Asset | 8 XP | 1 week |
| Major Asset | 12 XP | 1 month |
| Follower | Cap² XP | 1–3 days |

### Rush Rule
Skip downtime by accepting a Risk Timer [4]. If it fills, the new ability has a flaw (GM’s choice).

## Assets & Followers

### Assets (off‑screen resources)
- **Minor** (4 XP), **Standard** (8 XP), **Major** (12 XP).
- Free off‑screen use: once per session.
- Boon activation (1 Boon): improve Position, reduce DV, advance timer, etc.
- Status: Flourishing (+1 die) → Stable → Strained (–1 die) → Collapsed.

### Followers (on‑screen allies)
- Cost = Cap² XP (Cap 1–5).
- Assist: add min(Cap, relevant skill) dice (max +3 from all sources).
- Independent action (once per scene, party‑wide): worsen Fitness or Loyalty.
- **Loyalty**: Faithful → Strained → Broken.
- **Fitness**: Ready → Hurt → Down.
- **Upkeep** (each downtime): Efficient (XP ≈ acquisition/3) or Intensive (1 XP + downtime scene).

## Terrestrial Patrons & Trusts
- **Terrestrial Patrons** (noble houses, guilds, syndicates): offer Assets/Favours in exchange for Obligation (Influence). Capacity = Spirit + Presence.
- **Trusts** (player‑owned factions): pool XP (10 XP to found). Members can call on the Trust, borrow Assets, request Followers, all costing Obligation to the Trust.

## Travel Framework
- Break journey into **legs**, each with a Travel Timer (4–10 segments).
- **Roles**: Guide (navigation), Scout (avoid surprise), Quartermaster (supplies), Watch (position on ambush).
- Each role requires a roll; success advances timer, failure may tick backward or cause complications.
- **Supply Timer** [4]: when empty, each character gains Fatigue 1.

## Deck of Consequences (Optional)
Draw up to 3 cards when GM spends SB:
- **Hearts**: social/emotional complications.
- **Spades**: physical/violent setbacks.
- **Clubs**: resource/wealth problems.
- **Diamonds**: mystical/supernatural events.
Rank determines severity (Ace–3 minor, 10–King major).

## GM Quick Reference

### Defaults
- DV = 3, Position = Controlled, one short timer.

### SB Spend Menu
- **1 SB**: minor pressure (noise, timer +1).
- **2 SB**: moderate setback (alarm, lose advantage).
- **3+ SB**: major trouble (reinforcements, scene shift).

### Three‑Timer Rule
Maintain at most three active timers per scene:
1. Situation Timer (immediate goal)
2. Counter‑Clock (opposition pressure)
3. Campaign Timer (long‑term front)

## Core Principles
- **Narrative First**: mechanics serve the story.
- **Risk as Drama**: every roll carries potential for triumph and complication.
- **Fail Forward**: a Miss is never “nothing happens” – introduce a complication.
- **Boons are dramatic fuel**, not XP tokens – spend them freely.
- **The Golden Rule**: when in doubt, make the choice that serves the story. Default to DV 3, Controlled Position, and let the dice fall.
