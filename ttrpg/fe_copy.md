**You want power? Fine. Don't come crying when the debt comes due.**

**Fate's Edge** is a tabletop roleplaying game about obligation, memory, and the spaces between what is owed and what is paid.

You are not a hero. You are a debtor. Everyone is.

The world runs on three ledgers: the coins you spend, the promises you keep, and the names the fog remembers. Every spell costs a breath you may not have. Every alliance is a thread that can be pulled taut. Every death leaves a weight that someone must carry.

**What you'll find inside:**

- **Six Roads to power** – Runekeeper, Invoker, Cantor, Witch, Psion, Summoner. Each path asks a different price. Each price is paid in flesh, memory, or obligation.

- **A world that remembers** – From the fog-choked levees of the Mistlands to the broken courts of Vhasia, from the counting-houses of Silkstrand to the frost-bound cairns of Linn. Every culture counts in eights. The ninth breath belongs to the Hollow.

- **Patrons who collect** – The Witness records every truth you try to bury. Mykkiel judges the covenant you swore. Malachai offers you everything you desire—and everything you fear. Ikasha sleeps, but she dreams of your secrets.

- **Debt as currency** – No piles of gold. Your resources are favors, secrets, promises, and the stories you're willing to spend. Power is a negotiation. The negotiation never ends.

- **Generators that breathe** – Draw a card. ♠ is the place. ♥ is the actor. ♣ is the pressure. ♦ is the leverage. Build a scene in seconds. Build a campaign in an hour.

- **Horror that watches** – The Dread Timer tracks your unraveling. The Reality Fracture Timer tracks the world's. The Hollow is patient. The Hollow is always counting.

**You are not the first to walk this road.** The Grey Wanderer has worn a dozen faces. Saikou Ira has exorcised things you wouldn't name. Dusana of the Raven Road pays her debts in stories. This is their compendium. This is their warning.

**The soup is always free.**  
**The price never is.**

*Fate's Edge – Risk, consequence, and sharp choices at the table.*
