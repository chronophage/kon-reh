\section*{1. Five−Second Position & Effect}

\begin{quickbox}{Risk Assessment}
Ask: What is the Risk?
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Controlled:} Safe, prepared, low threat
    ℹtem \textbf{Controlled (default):} Pressure, danger, uncertainty  
    ℹtem \textbf{Desperate:} Immediate danger, overmatched, exposed
\end{itemize}
\end{quickbox}

\begin{quickbox}{Impact Assessment}
Ask: What is the Impact?
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Limited:} Partial progress or minor effect
    ℹtem \textbf{Standard (default):} Normal success
    ℹtem \textbf{Great:} Powerful, overwhelming, high−impact
\end{itemize}
\end{quickbox}

\textbf{Example:} A player charges a cultist across open ground. Risk = standard danger (⇒ Controlled). Impact = standard attack (⇒ Standard). Controlled / Standard. Roll.

\section*{3. No−Thinking SB Spending}

When players roll 1s, react instantly. Pick one option and move on:

\begin{quickbox}{1 SB − Minor Complication}
Noise, track left behind, small loss of gear, or tick a timer +1
\end{quickbox}

\begin{quickbox}{2 SB − Moderate Complication}
Alarm raised, lose Position, broken tool, tougher foe, or tick a timer +2
\end{quickbox}

\begin{quickbox}{3+ SB − Major Complication}
Reinforcements, collapsing terrain, ally endangered, or fill 3+ timer segments
\end{quickbox}

\textbf{Never overthink it. One spend is enough.}

\sectionrule

\section*{4. The Three−Timer Rule}

Only three active timers at once:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Scene Timer :} e.g., Guards Alerted [4]
    ℹtem \textbf{Journey Timer :} e.g., Mist Encroaches [6]  
    ℹtem \textbf{Campaign Timer :} e.g., Baron's Suspicion [8]
\end{itemize}

If a new complication appears, fold it into an existing timer. This keeps the table focused and the fiction tight.

\section*{5. Magic Adjudication Shortcut}

If a player invents a spell on the fly:
\begin{enumerate}
    ℹtem Hear the intent
    ℹtem Assign a fitting TAG (Veil, Ward, Barrier, Glamour, etc.)
    ℹtem Pick DV based on scale:
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Personal / Subtle = DV 2
        ℹtem Scene−wide / Strong = DV 3  
        ℹtem Area / Devastating = DV 4+
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem Choose Position based on danger while casting
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Example:} Creating a force wall under fire? TAG = Barrier, scale = Scene ⇒ DV 3. Casting in melee ⇒ Controlled. Controlled / Standard, DV 3. Roll.

    ℹtem Apply Lazy DV Table to key challenges

    ℹtem Bank 2−3 SB spends per major scene

    ℹtem Reassess Position every major beat
\end{itemize}
