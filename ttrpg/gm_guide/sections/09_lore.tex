\chapter{The Known World: A Lore Compendium}
\label{ch:lore-compendium}

This chapter presents a practical overview of the major regions of Fate’s Edge. For each area, you will find its dominant **fantasy subgenre**, the **primary conflicts** that drive adventure, and the **key stakeholders** (factions, leaders, and powers) that shape events. Use this as a quick reference when planning sessions or improvising.

\section{A Note on Subgenres and Travel}

Each region is designed as a distinct fantasy subgenre (see \S\ref{ch:world-interaction}). When the party travels from one region to another, the genre shifts – a journey from the folk horror of the Mistlands to the political intrigue of Ecktoria should feel like turning the page to a different kind of story. Use the travel rules to blend these genres organically.

\section{Acasia — “The Broken Province”}
\index{Acasia}
\textbf{Genre:} Low fantasy / mercenary realism / border feud.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Feuding petty kingdoms and warlords.
  \item Mercenary companies (the Black Banners) that shift loyalties with coin.
  \item The lingering “Curse” – a supernatural blight that twists roads and ruins.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Margravine of the Broken March} – The highest authority, half‑saint, half‑tax‑roll.
  \item \textbf{The Lame King} – A traveling pretender who makes law by pointing.
  \item \textbf{Blackwood Matriarchs} – Hedge‑witches and feud‑keepers.
  \item \textbf{Free Company Captains} – Pike and shot mercenaries between contracts.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“In Acasia, every bargain is an echo of a promise once broken, and every drink tastes faintly of debt.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Aelaerem (Halflings) — “Hearth \& Hollow”}
\index{Aelaerem}
\textbf{Genre:} Folk horror / pastoral quiet with dark undercurrents.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The “Neighbors” – fae or otherworldly powers that demand observance.
  \item Feuds between clan matriarchs that can last generations.
  \item Harvest tithes and famine pressure.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Apple-Matron} – Hostess of harvest feasts; power sits where she pours.
  \item \textbf{The Pale Shepherd} – Comes when lambs are born and when people go missing.
  \item \textbf{Mummers’ Captain} – Keeps a chest of masks and stricter rules than church.
  \item \textbf{Lantern-Warden} – Trims lamps and knows which shadow is wrong.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“Don't cross the Pale Causeway at dusk, especially not if you've got a silver tooth.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Aeler (Dwarves) — “Crowns \& Under‑Vaults”}
\index{Aeler}
\textbf{Genre:} Industrial / political / survival horror (underground).

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Resource scarcity (air, water, light, food) in sealed holds.
  \item Jurisdictional feuds – bell‑code conflicts between crown and basilica.
  \item The “White Flood” – mountain thaw that becomes a river through routes.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Vault-Queen} – Sovereign above and below.
  \item \textbf{High King Beneath the Peaks} – First among crowns by ancient concord.
  \item \textbf{True Masons} – Wanderers repairing ancient stonework.
  \item \textbf{Edgewalkers} – Prospectors and border scanners.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“When the stone starts singing a different tune, you best listen.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Aelinnel (Gnomes) — “Stone, Bough, and Bright Things”}
\index{Aelinnel}
\textbf{Genre:} Fae / mathematical / courtesy horror.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Geasa and spoken taboos that bind without warning.
  \item Thorn Court vs. human law – jurisdiction debates.
  \item The Green Gate – a threshold that demands exact change in truths.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Stone Prince of Aelinnel} – Mortal crown with fae debts properly indexed.
  \item \textbf{Lady of Thorns} – Hawthorn court sovereign; smiles like a snare.
  \item \textbf{Green Knight} – Duelist of paths and proofs.
  \item \textbf{Oath-Carver} – Sets promises in quartz so they can be seen.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The law here flows like water over stone – it finds its own path, and woe to those who try to dam it with paper.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Black Banners — “Condotta \& Crowns”}
\index{Black Banners}
\textbf{Genre:} Military / mercenary / low fantasy warfare.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Payday delays and supply shortages.
  \item Condotta units flipping sides mid‑battle.
  \item Winter campaigns and frozen battlefields.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Banner-Captain} – Griffon standard and a ledger of noble grudges.
  \item \textbf{The Black Colonel} – Legendary commander not seen in months, yet his banner still rides.
  \item \textbf{Clan-Mother} (Ykrul) – Wears war‑braids from oath‑breakers’ hair.
  \item \textbf{Veteran Sergeant} – Counts scars like coins.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“A contract is only as good as the next payday, and a grudge is only as sharp as the sword backing it.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Ecktoria — “Marble \& Fire”}
\index{Ecktoria}
\textbf{Genre:} Political intrigue / legal drama / fallen empire.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Rival dukes, abbesses, and parlements claiming legitimacy.
  \item Bread riots, grain shortages, and currency devaluation.
  \item The Everflame orthodoxy vs. reformist sects.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Grand Magistrate} – Guardian of forms, breaker of them when needed.
  \item \textbf{High Priest of the Everflame} – Smiles like law; flame judges truth and debt.
  \item \textbf{Duchess-Regent} – Speaks through cousins, rules through silence.
  \item \textbf{Censor’s Clerk} – “Misfiles” careers; knows which names to forget.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The law here is written not in books but in marble and flame.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Linn — “Skerries \& Storm‑Oaths”}
\index{Linn}
\textbf{Genre:} Viking / maritime / oath‑bound raiding.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Feuds that turn weddings into funerals.
  \item Mistslands fog and misdirection – channels “move” without warning.
  \item Thing injunctions that forbid sailing until a grievance is heard.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Sea-Queen} – Crown of hammered silver, eyes for weather and law.
  \item \textbf{High Jarl} – First among raids, last to boast.
  \item \textbf{Volva of the Mist} – Oath‑reader whose nod stills surf.
  \item \textbf{Skald} – Verses buy silence, start fights, end them.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The sea keeps better counsel than any scribe, and the storm remembers every oath sworn upon its winds.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Mistlands — “Bells, Salt, and Breath”}
\index{Mistlands}
\textbf{Genre:} Folk horror / gothic / supernatural dread.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The Direwood’s encroachment – undead and wraiths testing the bell‑lines.
  \item Ward‑salt shortages and bell‑line failures.
  \item Aeler protectorate authority vs. local independence.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Legate of the Mists} – Aeler governor; equal parts mercy and decree.
  \item \textbf{Bell-Warden} – Ringbook decides which notes keep which ghosts at bay.
  \item \textbf{Salt-Monk} – Blesses pans and keeps ward‑measure honest.
  \item \textbf{Mist-Seer} – Bargains breath for answers; candles shorten when she speaks.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The bells here do more than mark time – they weave the very fabric of reality.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Silkstrand — “City of Bridges \& Dyewater”}
\index{Silkstrand}
\textbf{Genre:} Urban / mercantile / noir.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Guild rivalries and Exchange cornering.
  \item The “Curse” – dyewater omens and awakened curses in the canals.
  \item Condottieri flipping colors mid‑contract.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Matron} – Rules from her palazzo stairs with velvet and iron.
  \item \textbf{Guildmistress of Dyers} – Stained hands, permits tighter than purse strings.
  \item \textbf{Bridge Bailiff} – Rents stalls, sells gossip by the breath.
  \item \textbf{Night-Boat Smuggler (“Ravel”)} – Silent oar, louder favors.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“In this city, every bridge is a ledger and every current carries coin.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Theona — “Three Greens, No Ninth”}
\index{Theona}
\textbf{Genre:} Celtic / folk horror / taboo‑driven.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The “No Ninth” taboo – omissions, unspoken names, missing steps.
  \item Bride‑peace feasts that can end or restart feuds.
  \item The Green Host – spectral riders that appear when customs are broken.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Matron of Wells} – Queen‑mother in truth if not name.
  \item \textbf{Three-Isles King} – Elected by moots; whale‑bone throne.
  \item \textbf{Lady Beneath} – Unseen queen who bargains in dreams.
  \item \textbf{Taboo-Witness} – Geas‑keeper who knows which words ruin deals.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The wells here hold more than water – they hold the dreams and prayers of generations.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Thepyrgos — “City of a Thousand Stairs”}
\index{Thepyrgos}
\textbf{Genre:} Byzantine / academic / arcane mystery.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Synod edicts that proscribe or permit chants and rites.
  \item Iconoclast riots between competing faiths.
  \item Witch‑hunters and arcane talent conscription.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Archon} – Elected, entrenched, counting bells.
  \item \textbf{Lighthouse-Patriarch} – Reclusive seer who steers beacons in storms.
  \item \textbf{Nomophylax} – Law‑scholar whose citations close mouths.
  \item \textbf{Palkar Captain} – Tower guards; ladder‑quick and letter‑shy.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“Every stair climbed is a word in the great conversation between earth and sky.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Ubral — “The Stone Between Spears”}
\index{Ubral}
\textbf{Genre:} Highland clan / reiver / honour culture.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Feuds that last generations, settled by wergild in cattle or blood.
  \item Guest‑right – breaking hospitality stains name worse than breaking a sword.
  \item Dwarf toll roads and clan independence.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Lady of Tor} – Clan‑chief in cloak and mail.
  \item \textbf{Council of Cairns} – Greybeards and granite wills; they do not hurry.
  \item \textbf{Feud-Broker} – Knows the weight of life in cattle and coin.
  \item \textbf{Wergild Counter} – Keeps tallies, ends grudges, starts others.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The stone here remembers every oath sworn upon it and every blood spilled in its shadow.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Valewood — “Empire Under Leaves”}
\index{Valewood}
\textbf{Genre:} Fairy tale / dark forest / fey remnant.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Phasing ruins and star‑roads that shift between worlds.
  \item Fae courts (Hazel vs. Alder) competing for influence.
  \item Imperial echoes – ancient shades still marching patrols.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Hazel Queen} – Lethai‑ar sovereign in leaf and law.
  \item \textbf{Alder King} – Twilit Court sovereign in shadow and debt.
  \item \textbf{The Huntsman} – Antlers of moonlight; shortcuts owe him.
  \item \textbf{Pathweaver} – Knots safe routes into cord.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The forest here remembers empires that crumbled to dust, and its laws are older than your kingdoms.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Vhasia — “The Fractured Sun”}
\index{Vhasia}
\textbf{Genre:} Courtly romance / chivalric / fractured kingdom.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Rival dukes claiming the Sun‑crown.
  \item Chevauchée – raiding parties that burn loyalty oaths as well as barns.
  \item Parlement divided – war by paperwork.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Two Crowns} – Rival dukes, each crowned in rival cathedrals.
  \item \textbf{Queen-Mother} – Weaves alliances as if they were dowries.
  \item \textbf{Last Dauphin} – Rumor with a birthmark; armies march for whispers.
  \item \textbf{Marshal-Exile} – Sun‑court’s hammer, now wielded by whoever pays.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The sun may be fractured, but our authority is absolute within our domains.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Viterra — “The Hedge‑Law Realm”}
\index{Viterra}
\textbf{Genre:} Legal drama / low fantasy / frontier justice.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Border disputes over hay‑carts and toll rights.
  \item Legal duels – formal combat to settle property claims.
  \item The Queen’s progress – royal authority moving through semi‑autonomous duchies.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Warrior Queen} – Crown on helm, sword at side, patient sums behind gaze.
  \item \textbf{Queen’s Justiciar} – Law on road, polite as a noose.
  \item \textbf{Fen Reeve} – Tally‑rod; speaks for dike guilds.
  \item \textbf{Master Advocate Halric} – Silver‑tongued legal warrior.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“Every hedge here marks not just a boundary but a principle – the careful balance between order and freedom.”}
\end{quote}

\section{Ykrul (Orcs) — “Wolf Standards, Winter Camps”}
\index{Ykrul}
\textbf{Genre:} Steppe nomad / saga / honour culture.

\paragraph{Primary Conflicts.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Feuds and blood‑price (wergild) in cattle or horses.
  \item Hostage strings – token kin left to secure treaties.
  \item White squalls and rasputitsa (spring melt) that seal passes.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Key Stakeholders.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Khatun of Ring} – Camp‑law and hostage strings.
  \item \textbf{Khagan’s Kin} – Sent to bind foedus with a look.
  \item \textbf{Sky-Speaker} – Shaman of storms; shortcuts owe them price.
  \item \textbf{Kon'reh Masters} – Geometry arbiters and teachers.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Quote.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{“The steppe remembers every oath sworn beneath its endless sky, and the wolf‑standards carry the honor of clans that have outlasted empires.”}
\end{quote}

\paragraph{Quote.}

\end{quote}

\section{Other Regions (Brief)}
\index{other regions}
For completeness, these areas appear in the comprehensive guide but are less central to the core setting:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Dungeon – “Living Infrastructure”} – A mythic underworld that breathes; genre: dungeon crawl as survival horror.
  \item \textbf{The Ways Between – “Spiritways \& Veilways”} – Liminal paths between death and dreams; genre: metaphysical / dream‑logic.
  \item \textbf{The Wilds – “Roads, Ruins, and Weather”} – A reskin palette for any terrain – forest, desert, tundra, coast.
  \item \textbf{Vilikari – “Laurels \& Longhouses”} – A federated frontier mixing Utaran law with barbarian custom.
\end{itemize}

\section{Using This Compendium at the Table}
\index{lore compendium!use}

When preparing a session or improvising, consult the region’s entry for:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Genre} – to set the right mood.
  \item \textbf{Primary conflicts} – to generate immediate stakes.
  \item \textbf{Key stakeholders} – to populate scenes with recognizable NPCs.
  \item \textbf{Quote} – to read aloud as a flavour text.
\end{itemize}

Remember that travel rules (see \S\ref{ch:world-interaction}) allow you to blend these genres as the party moves from one region to another.
