## Revision Plan for Fate's Edge GM Guide (Preserving File Structure)

This plan keeps the existing 11 sections (`01_intro.tex` through `11_appendix.tex`) and focuses on **clarity, usability, and philosophical alignment** based on the simulation insights. No structural reorganization—just targeted improvements within each file.

---

### Section 01: Introduction (`01_intro.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** Dense philosophy dump, no explicit Session 0 guidance, safety tools buried.

**Revisions:**

1. **Add a prominent “Session Zero” subsection** (after safety tools) that covers:
   - The game’s core philosophy: *“Optimal play = most fun. Spending Boons, embracing failure, and courting Patron attention leads to better stories.”*
   - Player-GM contract: “We are co-creators, not adversaries.”
   - Resource mindset: “Boons are not XP tokens. Spend them.”
   - Patron expectation: “They are allies who demand payment—embrace it.”
   - A sample Session Zero checklist (topics to discuss, questions to ask).

2. **Clarify the “Flavor is Free” box:** Add a note that this applies to **all** descriptive actions, not just magic – encourage players to narrate even mundane successes.

3. **Safety tools:** Keep as is, but add a reminder that the GM can use the X-card too.

4. **Add a “How to Use This Guide” quick reference** – a one‑page summary of where to find core rules, magic, travel, etc. (mirroring the SRD structure).

---

### Section 02: Core Procedures (`02_core_procedures.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** Core loop buried after initiative options and player‑managed modules; DV ladder on p. 15 but explanation on p. 13; optional initiative overshadows narrative initiative.

**Revisions:**

1. **Re‑order subsections** (without changing file structure, just move paragraphs):
   - 2.1 How to Think in Fate’s Edge (keep as is)
   - **2.2 The Core Resolution Cycle** (move from current 2.5 up)
   - 2.3 Position & DV (consolidate DV ladder and Position descriptions)
   - 2.4 Outcome Matrix & Story Beats
   - **2.5 Initiative** – move optional systems (popcorn, side‑based, speed slots) to end, and first state: *“Default is narrative initiative. Use these optional structures only if your table prefers them.”*
   - 2.6 Player‑Managed Modules (good as is)
   - 2.7 Timers & Fronts (move from later)
   - 2.8 First Game Scenario (The Sunstone Tower) – keep as practical example.

2. **Add a “Golden Rule” call‑out box:** *“When in doubt, make a ruling that keeps the story moving.”*

3. **Clarify DV 3 as default** and warn against DV inflation (mention section 08).

4. **Add a short example** of a GM using SB to escalate a scene (like the bridge example) directly after the SB spend menu.

---

### Section 03: Managing Resources (`03_managing_resources.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** Boon economy explanation is functional but lacks the “optimal play” framing; Oversupply of subsystems (Supply, Fatigue, Boons, Followers, Assets, Patrons, XP, Mandate/Crisis).

**Revisions:**

1. **Add a “Philosophy of Resources” intro box:** *“Resources are not there to hoard – they are tools to create drama. Spend Boons freely. Let Fatigue build. Obligation is plot, not punishment.”*

2. **Boon section (3.3):** Add a sidebar: *“Why you should spend Boons – the math of fun.”* Include the insight that hoarding Boons for XP conversion leads to less engagement and lower fun scores.

3. **Fatigue section (3.2):** Add a note on the “roller‑coaster” effect: taking Harm clears Fatigue temporarily (adrenaline surge). Include a small example.

4. **Supply Timer (3.1):** No major changes, but cross‑reference travel rules.

5. **Followers and Assets (3.4):** Clarify that followers are on‑screen allies and can be **successors** (cross‑reference Legacy Engine). Add a short “when to invest” table.

6. **Terrestrial Patrons (3.5):** Add a note: “Use debt timers aggressively – they are story fuel.” Provide 2‑3 more example patron demands.

7. **Mandate/Crisis (3.7):** Good as is.

---

### Section 04: Magic (`04_magic.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** Dense, assumes GM knows all patron rites; lack of guidance on how to make Patrons *active* vs. *passive* based on campaign length.

**Revisions:**

1. **Add a “Patron as Co‑Star” subsection** (after 4.1 Role of Magic):
   - Passive Patrons (long campaigns): *“Let the players accrue Obligation slowly; the GM narrates consequences only when thresholds are breached.”*
   - Active Patrons (short campaigns / one‑shots): *“The GM looks for hooks, offers bargains, and pushes the Obligation spiral aggressively.”*
   - Use the simulation insight: players enjoy being tempted, not forced.

2. **Adjudicating Magic (4.2):** Include a **quick reference table** for DV by spell scope (cantrip, combat, ritual, epic) and another for backlash severity by Position.

3. **Story Beats and Backlash (4.3):** Add an example of a GM spending SB to create a *choice* (e.g., “You can succeed, but your Patron will demand a task – accept or let the spell fizzle”).

4. **Managing Obligation and Corruption (4.4):** Add a note: *“Let players track their own Obligation (player‑managed modules). Your job is to enforce thresholds and frame Patron intrusions as story hooks, not punishments.”*

5. **Summoning (4.5):** Clarify that the spirit’s independent action on a Miss should be *interesting* – not just damage. Provide d6 table of spirit whims.

6. **Cantors and Corruption (4.6):** Add a note that chorus cults can become **rival factions** if neglected (cross‑reference section 06).

7. **Psionics (4.8):** Keep optional, but add a warning: “Psionics generates SB quickly – be prepared.”

---

### Section 05: Campaign Design (`05_campaign_design.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** The chapter is a mix of timers, faction dynamics, Crown Spread, and tier‑appropriate design. The “Position as Narrative Leverage” section is excellent but buried. No guidance on **rival parties**.

**Revisions:**

1. **Add “Rival Parties” subsection** (after 5.1 Scaling Threats). Include:
   - Why rivals work: they multiply Patron presence, create shifting alliances, and generate SB from both sides.
   - Template for creating a rival party (name, composition, Patrons, goals, relationship to PCs).
   - Guidance on rival advancement (they can succeed off‑screen, tick timers, and appear later).
   - Example: The Ashen Cord from simulations.

2. **Timers and Fronts (5.0):** Clarify the “Three‑Timer Guideline” with a concrete example.

3. **Position as Narrative Leverage (5.0.5):** Move this earlier (right after timers) – it’s critical. Emphasize that Position scales with tier (life/death → regional → world‑shaping).

4. **Crown Spread (5.0.5):** Good as is – keep.

5. **Character Arc Planning (5.3):** Add a note: *“Arcs should generate Obligation and Patron interest – use them as a source of complications.”*

6. **Tier‑Appropriate Design (5.0.7):** Add a cross‑reference to Section 08 (Epic Tier) for deeper guidance.

### Section 06: Advanced GM Techniques (`06_advanced_gm_tools.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** Good content but lacks connection to the “optimal play” philosophy; the Deck of Consequences could use more examples; no mention of rivals.

**Revisions:**

1. **Advanced Story Beat Management (6.1):** Add a sidebar: *“You are not limited by SB – they are a pacing tool, not a budget. If the story needs a complication, introduce it. SB just tells you when players *want* escalation.”*

2. **Mastering the Deck of Consequences (6.2):** Provide a **shortcut table** for interpreting draws (suit + rank → typical complication). Add an example of combining draws (e.g., Hearts+Spades = betrayal leads to physical harm).

3. **Advanced Travel and Genre Blending (6.3):** Keep, but cross‑reference the Travel Guide (separate document). Add a note that travel legs are great places to introduce rivals.

4. **Faction Grand Strategy (6.4):** Add a short example of a “faction turn” using rival parties as active agents.

5. **Designing High‑Tier Custom Content (6.5):** Good as is.

6. **Running Mythic Scenarios (6.6):** Add a note: *“At Tier V, every choice may erase an alternative timeline – embrace this. Let players narrate what they sacrifice.”*

### Section 07: Enhanced GM Play (`07_enhanced_gm_play.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** This chapter is excellent (GM as player, Threat Pool optional). Only minor clarifications needed.

**Revisions:**

### Section 08: Epic Tier (`08_epic_tier.tex`)

**Current weaknesses:** Excellent content (Boon scarcity, no DV inflation). Needs stronger link to the “brittleness” insight (high‑tier characters are *supposed* to be more brittle).

**Revisions:**

1. **Add a “Why Brittleness is Good” sidebar** (after 8.2 Boon Economy):
   - *“At high tiers, your power comes from skill, not luck. Fewer Boons mean every roll matters. This is intentional – embrace the tension.”*
   - Reference the simulation: chaos players thrived at high tier because they understood this.

2. **Do Not Inflate Difficulty Values (8.3):** Add a **warning box**: *“Raising DV starves players of Boons. Use timers, Position, and rivals instead.”*

3. **Using Bonds as Primary Boon Engine (8.5):** Expand with an example of bonded allies transferring Boons at high tier (from Player’s Guide).

4. **Mythic Failure Table (8.9):** Add 6 more entries (d12) for variety.

5. **Sample High‑Tier Scenario (8.10):** Keep – it’s good.

## Cross‑Sectional Consistency Fixes

- **Indexing:** Ensure every term in the index appears in the final printed index (imakeidx). Run `makeindex` after compilation.
- **Cross‑references:** Use `\ref` and `\pageref` liberally. For example, in Section 03 when discussing Obligation, cross‑reference Section 04.4.
- **Box styling:** Use `\begin{infobox}{Title}` for examples, `\begin{warningbox}` for common pitfalls, `\begin{fatebox}{Fate's Edge}` for philosophical asides.
- **Numbering:** Keep subsection numbers as they are (i.e., 2.1, 2.2, etc.) but reorder content within subsections as described.
