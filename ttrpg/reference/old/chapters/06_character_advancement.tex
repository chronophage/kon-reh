\chapter{Character Advancement}

\section{Player Archetypes}\index{Character Build!Archetypes}

Not every group plays the same way. These archetypes describe how players spend XP and seek spotlight.

\subsection{The Solo}\index{Character Build!Solo}

\begin{description}
\item[Definition] Invests XP primarily in Attributes and Skills. Minimal followers, minimal holdings. All power is on the sheet.\index{Character Build!Solo!Definition}
\item[Typical XP Spread] 70--90\% Self; 0--10\% On-screen help; 0--20\% Off-screen.\index{Character Build!Solo!XP Spread}
\item[Strengths] Consistent scene impact; few moving parts; resilient to follower loss.\index{Character Build!Solo!Strengths}
\item[Risks] Limited fiction reach between sessions; can stall when problems demand logistics or networks.\index{Character Build!Solo!Risks}
\end{description}

\subsection{The Mixed Player}\index{Character Build!Mixed Player}

\begin{description}
\item[Definition] Splits XP between self-growth and one or two meaningful assets (a small follower or a reliable holding).\index{Character Build!Mixed Player!Definition}
\item[Typical XP Spread] 50--65\% Self; 15--25\% On-screen help; 15--25\% Off-screen.\index{Character Build!Mixed Player!XP Spread}
\item[Strengths] Versatile: credible in scenes and has a lever for special problems.\index{Character Build!Mixed Player!Strengths}
\item[Risks] Upkeep pressure; helper can be targeted when the GM spends 2+ Story Beats.\index{Character Build!Mixed Player!Risks}
\end{description}

\subsection{The Mastermind}\index{Character Build!Mastermind}

\begin{description}
\item[Definition] Prioritizes followers/cadres/familiars and off-screen networks. The sheet is the hub of a larger apparatus.\index{Character Build!Mastermind!Definition}
\item[Typical XP Spread] 25--40\% Self; 35--55\% On-screen help; 20--40\% Off-screen.\index{Character Build!Mastermind!XP Spread}
\item[Strengths] Scene control via assistance; strategic reach between sessions; strong heist/social-planning play.\index{Character Build!Mastermind!Strengths}
\item[Risks] Dependency on assist lanes; followers can be endangered on 2+ Story Beat spends; upkeep pressure.\index{Character Build!Mastermind!Risks}
\end{description}

\section{XP Costs}\index{Experience Points!Costs}

\subsection{Attributes and Skills}\index{Experience Points!Attributes and Skills}

\begin{description}
\item[Attributes] New rating $\times$ 3 XP\index{Experience Points!Attributes}
\item[Skills] New level $\times$ 2 XP\index{Experience Points!Skills}
\item[Followers (On-Screen)] Cap$^2$ XP\index{Experience Points!Followers}
\end{description}

\subsection{Off-Screen Assets}\index{Experience Points!Assets}

\begin{description}
\item[Minor] 4 XP\index{Experience Points!Assets!Minor}
\item[Standard] 8 XP\index{Experience Points!Assets!Standard}
\item[Major] 12 XP\index{Experience Points!Assets!Major}
\end{description}

\section{Tiers of Reputation}\index{Reputation Tiers}

Though there are no levels, XP spent creates soft "tiers" that shape world response:

\begin{description}
\item[Tier I -- Rookie] (0--40 XP): Local reputation; prestige locked.\index{Reputation Tiers!Rookie}
\item[Tier II -- Seasoned] (41--90): Regional notice; prestige abilities may be unlocked.\index{Reputation Tiers!Seasoned}
\item[Tier III -- Veteran] (91--150): National influence; second follower slot suggested.\index{Reputation Tiers!Veteran}
\item[Tier IV -- Paragon] (151--220): Movers and shakers; rivals emerge to challenge.\index{Reputation Tiers!Paragon}
\item[Tier V -- Mythic] (221+): Legendary status; kingdoms and cults respond.\index{Reputation Tiers!Mythic}
\end{description}

    \item \textbf{Battle Instincts} (Cost: 6 XP): Once per scene, re-roll a failed defense roll.\index{Prestige Abilities!Battle Instincts}
    \item \textbf{Silver Tongue} (Cost: 4 XP): Gain +1 die when persuading or deceiving through speech.\index{Prestige Abilities!Silver Tongue}
    \item \textbf{Iron Stomach} (Cost: 3 XP): Immune to mundane poisons and spoiled food; halve Complications from toxic sources.\index{Prestige Abilities!Iron Stomach}

\section{Cultural Talent Examples}\index{Prestige Abilities!Cultural Examples}

\subsection{Humans}\index{Humans!Abilities}

\begin{description}
\item[Versatile] (3 XP) Once per session, swap one Skill die for another you lack.\index{Humans!Versatile}
\item[Guild Ties] (7 XP, Presence 2+) Call in favors from a guild or order once per session.\index{Humans!Guild Ties}
\item[Banner-Bearer] (15 XP, Presence 4+, Leadership 3) Inspire an allied unit: once per battle, erase 2 Complications for the group.\index{Humans!Banner-Bearer}
\end{description}

\subsection{Dwarves (Aeler)}\index{Dwarves!Abilities}

\begin{description}
\item[Stone-Sense] (5 XP, Dwarf only) Detect flaws in stone; +1 die to underground Craft/Engineering rolls.\index{Dwarves!Stone-Sense}
\item[Spirit Shield] (8 XP, Spirit 3+) Commune with ancestors; once per session, block 1 Complication for an ally.\index{Dwarves!Spirit Shield}
\item[Forge-Patriarch] (18 XP, Body 4+, Craft 4) Found a forge-citadel; gain loyal smiths and engineers who count as On-Screen specialists (Cap 5).\index{Dwarves!Forge-Patriarch}
\end{description}

\subsection{Wood Elves}\index{Wood Elves!Abilities}

\begin{description}

\item[Wild Speaker] (18 XP, Spirit 5+, Nature 3) Speak to beasts and trees; once per session, command local wildlife as allies.\index{Wood Elves!Wild Speaker}
