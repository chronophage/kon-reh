#### **Echoing Strike (3 XP)**
*Requirements: Melee 2+*
**Effect:** When you strike an enemy with a melee attack, the sound of the impact resonates unnaturally. Until the start of your next turn, that enemy is considered **Marked**. The next attack against the Marked enemy by you or an ally gains **+1d**.

#### **Patron's Glare (3 XP)**
*Requirements: Spirit 2+, Bonded to a Patron*
**Effect:** You can channel a sliver of your Patron's authority. Once per scene, as an action, force a single mortal target in Near range to test Spirit vs. DV 3. On a failure, they are **Shaken** and act with **-1d** on their next action. This has no effect on otherworldly beings like Outsiders.
*Narrative: Your eyes briefly flash with the sigil of your Patron, striking fear into the hearts of mortals.*

#### **Ritual Anchor (4 XP)**
*Requirements: Arcana 2+ or Runekeeper*
**Effect:** You are adept at stabilizing magical energies. When you Assist an ally who is Casting a spell or performing a Ritual, you may spend 1 Boon when you declare the assist. If you do, the first 1 rolled on the casting roll does not generate a Story Beat.
*Narrative: You provide a steady hand or a calming chant, helping to contain volatile magic.*

#### **Savage Fury (4 XP)**
*Requirements: Body 3+*
**Effect:** When you take Harm from a physical attack, you may immediately mark 1 Fatigue to gain **+1 Effect** on your next melee attack against the creature that harmed you. This bonus must be used before the end of the scene.
*Narrative: Pain doesn't slow you down; it only makes you more dangerous.*

### üí° Design Notes for Creating New Talents

When you create more Talents, keep these *Fate's Edge* principles in mind:

1.  **Cost = Impact + Flexibility:** A 2-4 XP Talent should provide a situational bonus or a new minor option. An 8+ XP Talent should be a scene-defining power.
2.  **Interact with Core Systems:** The best Talents play with SB, Boons, Position, Effect, and Clocks. They should feel like part of the game's engine.
3.  **Narrative Weight:** A Talent's name and description should inspire roleplaying. "Savage Fury" tells you more about a character than "+1 to damage."
4.  **Create Interesting Choices:** Talents like *Dual Patronage* are powerful but come with a built-in, escalating cost that drives story.
5.  **Avoid Pure Number Inflation:** Talents that just give +1d to everything are boring. Instead, give +2d in a specific, flavorful situation (like *Keen-Eyed Scout*).

These new Talents provide more avenues for character specialization, from the mystical (*Word of Unmaking*) to the tactical (*Volley of a Thousand Arrows*) and the high-risk (*Dual Patronage*). They should give players more ways to engage with the game's core themes of risk, consequence, and supernatural bargains.
