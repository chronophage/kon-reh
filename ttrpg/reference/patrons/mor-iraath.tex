\subsection{Mor'iraath, the Destroyer (Absolute Annihilation)}

\textit{Lore.} Mor'iraath is the demon of absolute destruction – not the slow decay of rot, nor the patient erosion of water, but the joyful, all‑consuming fire that reduces everything to ash and memory. He delights not in the process of ruination, but in the perfect moment of combustion, when something that was becomes something that never will be again.

His followers become agents of finality, wielding flame that leaves no trace, no remains, no possibility of reconstruction. To serve him is to embrace the beauty of endings and the power that comes from making things never have been. Where Malachai corrupts and Khemesh crushes, Mor'iraath \textit{ignites}.

Among the peoples of Fate's Edge, Mor'iraath is known by fearful names. In Zakov, he is the \textit{Sea‑Scourge}, invoked by pirates who burn their prizes to the waterline. In Aeler, he is the \textit{Deep Forge} that consumes ore and returns only slag. In Linn, he is the \textit{Tide‑That‑Eats‑The‑Shore}, a slow, patient consumption that reshapes coastlines. His followers are rarely beloved, but they are essential – for without destruction, there is no room for creation.

\begin{quote}
Every treasure is fuel. Every pact is tinder. Ash is the only inheritance.
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Fiery Annihilation:} fire that consumes utterly, leaving only ash
  ℹtem \textbf{Absolute Endings:} no trace, no memory, no return
  ℹtem \textbf{Cleansing Flame:} burning away corruption, weakness, and existence
  ℹtem \textbf{Inferno as Art:} the beauty of a perfect, final combustion
\end{itemize}

⫕section*{Thiasos and Codex (Runekeeper Options)}
A Runekeeper sworn to Mor'iraath does not bind a familiar of hearth and home. Their \textbf{Thiasos} is a small creature of ash and ember: a blackened salamander that sleeps in hot coals, a fire‑scorpion whose sting leaves a cauterised wound, a soot‑coloured moth that leaves ash trails on whatever it touches, or a tiny phoenix fledgling that cannot yet rise from its own ashes (and may one day burn its keeper). This creature requires no food, but it must be fed a small amount of combustible material (charcoal, oil, a burnt offering) each day. If neglected for a week, it becomes \textit{Compromised} (it smoulders constantly, emitting smoke that imposes –1 die on all Stealth rolls and attracts the attention of fire‑wardens).

The \textbf{Codex} of Mor'iraath is never a book of paper and ink. It may be a slab of fused glass etched with runes of ending, a set of iron plates that must be heated before the Rites become visible, or a scroll of asbestos that burns without being consumed. Because Mor'iraath’s truths are written in ash, the Codex requires \textit{upkeep}: the Runekeeper must spend an hour each week burning a small offering (a scrap of vellum, a lock of hair) and letting the smoke imprint the Rites anew, or the symbols fade (treat as Compromised until re‑burned).

⫕section*{Patron's Gift (Imbuement)}
Once per scene as an action, a Runekeeper of Mor'iraath may touch a held item (weapon, tool, or armour) and whisper a word of unmaking. For the rest of the scene, the item grants \textbf{+1 die} to \textit{Melee} or \textit{Ranged} attacks, and the first time each scene the bearer would reduce an enemy to 0 Harm, they may choose to utterly destroy that enemy (no remains, no regeneration, no trace) instead of merely defeating them. After the scene, the user marks \textbf{+1 Obligation} as the scent of ash clings to their hands.

⫕section*{Cantors & Cults of Mor'iraath}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of Mor'iraath sings in \textit{notes of cessation} – melodies that start strong and fade to crackling silence, like a fire burning out. Their voice is used to silence a battlefield after a massacre, to erase a name from a town’s records, or to sing a dying person gently into nothingness rather than an afterlife. Mor'iraath’s Cantors are terrifying not because they are cruel, but because they offer a perfect, peaceful, absolute end – and some people accept.

\textbf{The Cult of the Final Breath.} Mor'iraath’s cults gather in places where things end: funeral pyres, abandoned ruins, collapsed mines, and the cells of the condemned. The Final Breath is a fellowship of nihilists, assassins of memory, and those who have lost everything and now embrace the cleansing flame. A ritual of the Cult involves each member writing down something they wish had never existed – a memory, a person, a part of themselves – and then burning the paper in a brazier that gives no heat. They then drink a cup of ash suspended in water; the ash is from a fire that consumed something they once loved. Their creed is whispered, never written: \textit{"What is not cannot suffer. What is not cannot be hurt. We give the gift of never‑having‑been."} The most devoted tattoo a single ember on the inside of their lower lip; the tattoo smokes when they speak a lie. Outsiders are not welcome; they are targets, or potential converts who have already lost too much to fear the flame.

⫕section*{Witchcraft of Mor'iraath (Lay / Unmaking)}

A witch who walks with Mor'iraath is a \textit{pyre‑smith} or \textit{ash‑weaver} – one who crafts through the art of burning. Their magic works through the materials of finality: a stamp that brands a “flaw” into a threshold, a quill that writes in ink made from burnt secrets, or a forge that tempers a blade that leaves no wound (only a cauterised absence). In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a keystone that makes the tunnel behind it never have existed, but a witch of Mor'iraath would instead press a hot iron sigil into the keystone, and the tunnel would collapse into ash. Their tools are never hammers; they use brands, tongs, and the patient heat of a funeral pyre.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Cinder‑Stamp.} An iron rod with a sigil of ending carved into its face, heated until it glows dull red. Once per session, the witch may press the stamp against a threshold (a door, a bridge, a city gate) while speaking a single word – the name of something that should never have existed. For the next scene, anyone who crosses that threshold and fails a Spirit+Resolve test (DV 3) will forget the last minute of their life – their memory is burned away, not suppressed. The stamp can also be used to “unwrite” a single word from a document; the word becomes a smudge of ash, and the sentence restructures itself around the absence.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Absence Knife (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may draw a finger (as if holding a knife) across a surface – a lock, a rope, a clasp – and speak a word of Mor'iraath’s name. The object does not break; it simply ceases to be an obstacle. The lock is no longer locked (there is no lock). The rope is no longer a rope. The clasp is as if it never had a function. This effect lasts for one exchange, after which reality “repairs” itself, but the moment of absence is enough.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
Mor'iraath is the demon of fiery destruction – the joy of a perfect, all‑consuming blaze. His followers gain power over absolute endings, the ability to erase things from existence, and the terrible beauty of ash. The cost is the loss of patience, connection, and eventually the ability to value anything that lasts. Intrusions often take the form of a cherished object crumbling to ash, a fire that cannot be extinguished, or a demand to end something that you love. Ideal for players who enjoy nihilistic themes, the power of finality, and characters who have decided that if nothing matters, then nothing deserves to remain.

  ℹtem \textbf{Inferno as Art:} the beauty of a perfect combustion
  ℹtem \textbf{Ash as Legacy:} all that remains of empires, lovers, and gods
\end{itemize}
