\subsection{Mab, Queen of Courts (Glamour \& Bargain)}

\textit{Lore.} Mab rules not from throne or blade, but from dance and debt. She is the smile that binds, the jest that ensnares, the hostess who makes guests complicit in her game. To speak in her Court is to pay; to receive her token is to owe.

Where others rule by force, Mab rules by etiquette, glamour, and the hidden hook in every gift. Her followers thrive on charm, wit, and story, spreading webs of bargains too subtle to escape. The Cantor's Path sings her name most sweetly, for every verse carries a price.

\begin{quote}
``Every laugh is a promise. Every promise is a debt. Every debt belongs to Mab.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Fae Glamour:} Illusions, enchantments, faerie magic
  \item \textbf{Social Bargains:} Oaths, promises, debt economy
  \item \textbf{Courtly Intrigue:} Etiquette, wordplay, social games
  \item \textbf{Trickster Arts:} Mischief, cleverness, ironic twists
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Mab}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Mab sings in \textit{harmonies of the half‑truth} -- melodies that shift key without warning, leaving the listener uncertain of what was heard. Their voice is used to seal a bargain with a sung clause, to make a crowd forget a social faux pas, or to weave a glamour that lasts as long as the music plays. Mab's Cantors are prized at every court where masks are worn, and feared by those who know that a beautiful tune can hide a poisoned promise.

\textbf{The Cult of the Gilded Jest.} Mab's cults meet in places where masks are natural: masquerade balls, carnival tents, and the back rooms of theatres. The Gilded Jest is less a religion and more a conspiracy of wits -- actors, gamblers, and lawyers who have learned that every exchange is a performance. A ritual of the Jest involves each member telling a truth disguised as a lie, then a lie disguised as a truth. Their creed is printed on a card that is passed, never kept: \textit{"The best chains are made of laughter. The strongest oaths are sworn in jest."} Outsiders are welcome as "audience," but those who laugh too loudly may find themselves on stage.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Mab (Craft / Glamour)}

A witch who walks with Mab is a \textit{toy‑maker} or \textit{glamour‑weaver} -- one who crafts objects that are never quite what they seem. Their magic works through misdirection: a doll that laughs when a promise is broken, a mirror that shows a flattering reflection (and nothing else), or a mask that lets the wearer speak any lie as truth. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a cornerstone that makes everyone forget the building's weak point. In Viterra, a hedge‑wright might sew a cloak that changes colour to match the wearer's mood, charming all who see it.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The False Needle.} A needle of polished bone, warm to the touch, that sews thread into seams that do not exist. Once per session, the witch may sew a "glamour seam" into a garment or threshold. For the next scene, anyone wearing or crossing that item will be seen as they wish to be seen -- more beautiful, more authoritative, or simply forgettable. The effect grants +1 die to social rolls, but a single pointed question (Resolve DV 3) can pierce the illusion.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Mask of the Moment (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may place a hand over their own face and draw it away, revealing a different expression (or a different face entirely, briefly). This grants \textbf{+1 die} to a single Deception or Performance roll, as the target's expectations are momentarily overturned. The illusion lasts only for that exchange.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
Mab excels in social manipulation and fae‑style bargaining. Followers become masters of subtle magic, binding others through oaths and glamour. The corruption progression leads toward becoming fae‑touched, gaining power over truth and perception at the cost of mortal constraints. Ideal for players who enjoy social intrigue, wordplay, and characters who prefer cleverness over brute force.
