\subsection{Khemesh, the Abyssal Maw (Depths, Inexorability, Eldritch Terror)}

\textit{Lore.} Khemesh is not merely a lord of the depths but the hunger beneath them, a pressure older than seas. Those who bargain with him are marked by the abyss—seen in the way shadows cling, in the whispers heard when no voice speaks, in the certainty that all things will sink.

\begin{quote}
In the trench without light, the Maw waits. Even silence drowns.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Rites of Khemesh}

\paragraph{Whisper of the Trench (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} None. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Target hears impossible echoes and suffers \textbf{–1 die} on their next action. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Echoes coil in your own skull – take \textbf{Fatigue 1}, but the target also loses their next minor action. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of Crushing Silence (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A broken shell filled with ink‑dark water. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Establish an oppressive silence; sound carries only as distorted whispers. Enemies in the zone gain \textbf{–1 die} to coordination or morale‑driven actions. \\
\textbf{Push It:} A single enemy’s voice is stolen entirely for the scene. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Pressure of the Maw (Standard, 7 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A length of rusted chain submerged in water. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Target is pinned by invisible crushing force: treat as \texttt{[ENTANGLE]} with \textbf{Great Effect} if underwater or confined. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Inflict \textbf{Fatigue 1} on the target in addition to the restraint. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Abyssal Vision (Standard, 9 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} None. \\
\textbf{Effect:} You perceive the world as Khemesh does – fractured, alien, crushing. Gain \textbf{+2 dice} to Notice and Arcana, and may ask one “true nature” question about a foe or structure. \\
\textbf{Cost:} When the scene ends, you suffer \textbf{Exposure +1} as your perception warps. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend the vision to one ally, but both take \textbf{Fatigue 1}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{The Maw Opens (High, 12 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A sealed vessel of abyssal water, broken open. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Reality in the zone folds inward like the crushing deep:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Enemies act at \textbf{Desperate Position} by default.
  \item Each beat, the GM may force \textbf{1 SB} (Spades/Clubs favoured).
  \item Structures, vessels, or wards fracture as if under immense weight.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Push It:} For one beat, declare a single enemy “crushed” (severe harm/effect). You immediately suffer \textbf{Fatigue 2} and \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} \textbf{2 Boons}).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 8 segments.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Khemesh}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of Khemesh sings in \textit{pressures} – low, resonant drones that feel like the weight of water on the chest. Their voice is used to invoke the crushing depths, to rupture a ship’s hull with a single sustained note, or to fill a room with the sensation of drowning. Khemesh’s Cantors are rare, feared, and often found in coastal cults where they sing the tides into madness.

\textbf{The Cult of the Drowned Chorus.} Khemesh’s cults are the stuff of eldritch horror – sunken temples beneath storm‑tossed seas, congregations of shore‑dwellers with wet eyes and too‑pale skin, and the occasional deep one or kraken‑spawn acolyte. A ritual of the Drowned Chorus involves each member drinking a cup of brine and salt water while whispering a secret hope into the cup – hope that the depths will grant, or consume. Their creed is whispered in wet, gurgling tones: \textit{“All things sink. The only question is what you carry down with you.”} Outsiders are bait, or converts. Sometimes both.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Khemesh (Craft / Pressure)}

A witch who walks with Khemesh is a \textit{deep‑salvager} or \textit{pressure‑smith} – one who works with the spoils of the abyss. Their craft is the art of enduring pressure: forging a diving bell that can reach the trench, tempering steel in salt water to make it unbreakable, or carving a ward that holds back the sea. In Linn, a shipwright might season a keel with a prayer to Khemesh – the ship will never sink, but its crew will dream of drowning. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might seal a flooded tunnel with pressure‑set stone, cursed to collapse on any who speak the wrong name.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Abyssal Hammer.} A heavy hammer of black iron, always cold and wet to the touch, even in a forge. Once per session, when used to seal a threshold (a door, a hatch, a bulkhead), the hammer drives the seal with crushing finality. For the next scene, any attempt to force that threshold suffers \textbf{+1 DV} and a successful breach inflicts 1 Fatigue on the breaker (the pressure “pushes back”).

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Carried Depth (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may touch a container (a bottle, a chest, a room) and fill it with the “pressure of the deep.” For the next hour, any living creature inside that container must test Endurance (DV 3) or suffer 1 Fatigue from the crushing weight. The container may also be used to preserve food or seal a wound (the pressure stops bleeding), but the flesh grows pale and cold.
