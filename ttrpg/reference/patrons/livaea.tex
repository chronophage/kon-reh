\subsection{Livaea, the Crimson Courtier (Seduction \& Social Binding)}

\textit{Lore.} In salons where laughter cuts deeper than daggers and promises bind tighter than chains, the Crimson Courtier reigns. She is the whisper behind the curtain, the smile that sells secrets, the velvet hand that closes a cage. Her servants are not conquerors but consorts, binding others through song, wit, and the perilous glamour of intimacy. Where Mab entangles through fae trickery, Livaea rules through allure, performance, and the hunger for connection.

\begin{quote}
``A promise sealed with desire is harder to break than any oath sworn in iron.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Seductive Arts:} Charm, persuasion, social manipulation
  \item \textbf{Social Binding:} Oaths of affection, courtly promises, emotional debts
  \item \textbf{Performance Magic:} Dance, music, appearance enhancement
  \item \textbf{Court Intrigue:} Gossip, reputation, social standing
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Livaea}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of Livaea sings in \textit{harmonies of longing} – melodies that linger in the ear like a half‑remembered lover’s sigh. Their voice is used to sway a court’s opinion, to bind a marriage with a ballad, or to seduce a secret from a guarded heart. Livaea’s Cantors are prized in every noble house, and feared by those who know that a beautiful song can be a leash.

\textbf{The Cult of the Velvet Circle.} Livaea’s cults are not hidden in basements; they meet in salons, tea houses, and private galleries. The Velvet Circle appears to be a social club – but its members trade in secrets, favours, and carefully curated introductions. A ritual of the Velvet Circle involves each member drinking from a shared cup (the \textit{Crimson Chalice}) while naming a desire they wish to weave into reality. The Circle’s creed is embroidered on its seal: \textit{“Every bond is a thread. Every thread can be tied – or cut.”} Outsiders are welcome as guests, but they will leave with a new debt, a new ally, or a new enemy. Rarely all three.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Livaea (Craft / Social Weaving)}

A witch who walks with Livaea is a \textit{gossip‑scribe} or \textit{perfume‑smith} – one who crafts social reality from threads of rumour, scent, and gesture. Their magic works with the tools of influence: a letter that cannot be forgotten, a perfume that makes the wearer irresistibly charming, or a needle that sews a promise into a dress’s hem. In Ecktoria, a Livaean witch might be a letter‑writer for the powerful, sealing each envelope with a kiss that binds the reader’s loyalty. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a keystone that makes every handshake struck beneath it unbreakable.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Gossip Quill.} A silver‑tipped quill that writes in ink that darkens when a secret is true. Once per session, the witch may write a single sentence about a person they have met. That sentence becomes “known” to the local gossip network within a day, affecting the target’s reputation (GM determines effect: –1 die to social rolls, a new ally, an enemy, etc.). The witch cannot control how the rumour spreads, only that it spreads.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Velvet Glove (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may touch a person’s hand (or a letter they have written) and speak a single word of command (“trust,” “forget,” “desire”). For the next hour, the target feels a mild but genuine inclination toward that emotion or action – not enough to override self‑preservation, but enough to grant the wielder \textbf{+1 die} on social rolls against them.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
Livaea excels in social manipulation and courtly intrigue. Followers become masters of persuasion, binding others through desire and obligation. The corruption progression leads toward becoming a social virtuoso who cannot function without admiration and connection. Ideal for players who enjoy social combat, political maneuvering, and characters who wield influence as their primary weapon.
