\subsection{Maelstraeus, The Infernal Bargainer (Commerce \& Exchange)}

\textit{Lore.} Maelstraeus is the Infernal Bargainer, the Prince of Ledgers, the shadow at every crossroads of exchange. He was born from the first inequity, when one gave and another took, and debt was chained into existence.

He does not trade fairly—he trades \textit{inevitably}. Every pact carries a hidden surcharge, every kindness is collateral, every smile hides an interest rate. His realm is an endless market where stalls never close, contracts burn with invisible ink, and every bargain tilts toward him.

To serve Maelstraeus is to admit the world itself is transactional—that all things, from love to breath to soul, are bound in his ledger. He collects. He always collects.

\begin{quote}
A deal is never even. The scales tilt; the Merchant smiles. All debts come due.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Maelstraeus}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of Maelstraeus sings in \textit{recitations of accounting} – melodies that sound like the rustle of parchment and the chink of coins. Their voice is used to seal a contract with a sung term, to recite a debtor’s tally until the weight of it crushes, or to make a market stall appear where none stood before. Maelstraeus’s Cantors are employed by every major guild, and feared by those who know that a song of profit always hides a verse of loss.

\textbf{The Cult of the Gilded Ledger.} Maelstraeus’s cults meet in counting‑houses, bank vaults, and the back rooms of commodity exchanges. The Gilded Ledger is not a secret society – it is a fraternity of merchants, moneylenders, and tax collectors who have learned that every transaction is a prayer. A ritual of the Ledger involves each member presenting a profit (real or anticipated) and a loss (hidden or deferred), then striking a balance aloud. Their creed is stamped on every bond they issue: \textit{“The soul is currency. Spend it wisely – someone is always counting.”} Outsiders are welcome as “investors,” but those who fail to pay their dues find their names struck from the book.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Maelstraeus (Craft / Exchange)}

A witch who walks with Maelstraeus is a \textit{ledger‑smith} or \textit{coin‑turner} – one who crafts the instruments of exchange: contracts that cannot be broken, scales that never lie, and inks that write in terms that bind the reader’s soul. Their magic works through the materials of commerce: a quill that writes in debt, a press that stamps obligations into parchment, or a forge that tempers coins that always return to the spender. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a toll‑gate that remembers every traveller’s debt to the mountain. In Zakov, a salvager might stitch a charter that grants ownership of a wreck – and all who died on it.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Debtor’s Quill.} A steel‑nibbed quill that writes in ink that darkens as the writer’s obligation grows. Once per session, the witch may write a one‑sentence contract on a scrap of parchment. Anyone who signs (or accepts the parchment) is bound to a minor task or payment. Breach inflicts \textbf{1 SB (Spades)} and marks the signer with a faint, glowing sigil for one day.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Fair Weight (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may touch a scale, a till, or a handshake and speak a single word (“balance”). For the next exchange, all appraisals and negotiations involving that transaction are treated as if they were perfectly fair – no hidden advantage, no secret interest. The witch may choose to be the one who benefits from that fairness, or they may grant it to another as a genuine (but binding) gift.
