\subsection{Mor'iraath, the Destroyer (Absolute Annihilation)}

\textit{Lore.} Mor'iraath is the principle of absolute annihilation—the void that leaves no trace, no memory, no possibility of return. Where others corrupt or decay, Mor'iraath eliminates. His followers become agents of finality, wielding the power to unmake what others merely damage. He delights not in the process of destruction, but in the perfect moment of ceasing‑to‑be. To serve him is to embrace the beauty of endings and the power that comes from making things never have been.

\begin{quote}
Every treasure is fuel. Every pact is tinder. Ash is the only inheritance.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Rites of Mor'iraath}

\paragraph{Rite of the Perfect Ending (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Touch; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} The final component of something (last brick, final word, closing signature). \\
\textbf{Effect:} Cause one small thing to cease existing entirely – no remains, no trace, no possibility of reconstruction. Gain \textbf{+1 die} to rolls involving finality or absolute conclusions. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The ending creates a beneficial void – gain 1 Boon as space opens for something else; mark \textbf{1 SB (Spades)} as reality adjusts to the absence. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Unmaking Gaze (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A lens of obsidian or void‑glass. \\
\textbf{Effect:} \textbf{+2 dice} to identify the precise weakness that will cause complete collapse. See the “breaking point” in any structure, plan, or construct. Your next destructive action against the identified target gains \textbf{+1 Effect}. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The gaze becomes prophetic – see three possible endings for one target; choose which occurs, but mark \textbf{1 SB (Diamonds)} as fate resists your vision. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Cleansing Flame (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Fuel that was once part of what you intend to destroy. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Create a fire that burns not just substance but essence – what is consumed by this flame never existed. The zone becomes \texttt{[VOID]}; things that enter may cease to exist. Enemies suffer \textbf{–2 dice} to preservation/continuation actions. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The flame becomes selective – choose what is unmade and what remains; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation} as you assume judgment over existence itself. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Absolute Negation (Standard, 9 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} The complete destruction of something that represents the target (a lock for a door, a name for a person, a fragment for a whole). \\
\textbf{Effect:} The target suffers \textbf{–2 dice} to resist any effect that would cause their non‑existence. If they are reduced to Harm 0 by your actions, they are \texttt{[BANISH]}ed from all realities – they never were, never could be. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The negation becomes retroactive – the target never existed in your presence; mark \textbf{2 SB (Hearts)} as the timeline rewrites around their absence. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Unraveling (High, 13 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Touch; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} The foundational element of what you wish to unmake (keystone, cornerstone, source code). \\
\textbf{Effect:} Your touch causes cascading dissolution – the target and all things fundamentally connected to it begin immediate unmaking. Test DV = Complexity.
\begin{itemize}
  \item Success: Target and connections cease to exist.
  \item Partial: Significant damage, accelerated decay.
  \item Miss: Backlash; mark 2 SB.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Push It:} The unraveling becomes contagious – similar things in Near range also begin unmaking; mark \textbf{+2 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} 2 Boons).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 7 segments.

\paragraph{Rite of the Final Void (High, 14 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Extended; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Complete dedication of the space to Mor'iraath’s principle of non‑existence. \\
\textbf{Effect:} The zone becomes a pocket of absolute negation. Anything that enters ceases to exist after 1d4 rounds unless they make a Spirit+Resolve test (DV 5) each round. The space itself becomes \texttt{[VOID]} – it cannot be entered without risk of unmaking. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The void becomes permanent and expands at 1 segment per scene; mark Harm 2 and \textbf{+3 Obligation} as you become partially unmade yourself. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} 2 Boons).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 8 segments.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Mor'iraath}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of Mor'iraath sings in \textit{notes of cessation} – melodies that start strong and fade to nothing, leaving only the memory of a sound that once was. Their voice is used to silence a battlefield after a massacre, to erase a name from a town’s records, or to sing a dying person gently into non‑existence rather than an afterlife. Mor'iraath’s Cantors are terrifying not because they are cruel, but because they offer a perfect, peaceful, absolute end – and some people accept.

\textbf{The Cult of the Final Breath.} Mor'iraath’s cults gather in places where things end: funeral pyres, abandoned ruins, collapsed mines, and the cells of the condemned. The Final Breath is a fellowship of nihilists, assassins of memory, and those who have lost everything and now embrace the void. A ritual of the Cult involves each member writing down something they wish had never existed – a memory, a person, a part of themselves – and then burning the paper in a brazier that gives no heat. The ashes are scattered into a pit that leads nowhere. Their creed is whispered, never written: \textit{“What is not cannot suffer. What is not cannot be hurt. We give the gift of never‑having‑been.”} Outsiders are not welcome; they are targets, or potential converts who have already lost too much to fear the void.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Mor'iraath (Craft / Unmaking)}

A witch who walks with Mor'iraath is a \textit{void‑smith} or \textit{unmaker} – one who crafts tools of absolute erasure. Their magic works through the materials of finality: a forge that tempers a blade that leaves no wound (only an absence), a chisel that carves a hollow that cannot be filled, or a quill that writes words that delete themselves after being read. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a keystone that makes the tunnel behind it never have existed. In Zakov, a salvager might forge an anchor that drags a ship into a pocket of non‑time.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Void Chisel.} A chisel of black iron that leaves a grey, crumbling dust wherever it strikes. Once per session, the witch may carve a “void mark” into a threshold (a door, a bridge, a city gate). For the next scene, anyone who crosses that threshold and fails a Spirit+Resolve test (DV 3) will forget the last minute of their life – their memory is unmade, not suppressed. The witch may also use the chisel to permanently erase a single, small object (a key, a letter, a coin) from existence.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Absence Knife (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may draw a knife (real or imagined) across a surface – a lock, a rope, a clasp – and speak a word of Mor'iraath’s name. The object does not break; it simply ceases to be an obstacle. The lock is no longer locked (there is no lock). The rope is no longer a rope. The clasp is as if it never had a function. This effect lasts for one exchange, after which reality “repairs” itself, but the moment of absence is enough.
