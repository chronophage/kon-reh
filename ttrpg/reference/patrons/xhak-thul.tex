\subsection{Xhak'Thul, the Thunder-Speaker (Storm, Shock \& Awe)}

\textit{Lore.} Before words, there was thunder. Before scripture, lightning carved its commandments into the bones of the world. Xhak'Thul is the Voice That Splits Mountains, the Drum That Shakes Foundations, a power born from the first storm that ever raged across the primal earth. His faithful are whirlwinds of action---they do not study; they dance, they shout, they move with the sky's fury. His gifts are not gentle miracles but raw terror, awe, and vitality ripped from nature's heart.

Among the peoples of Fate's Edge, Xhak'Thul is the patron of the pre‑literate, the neolithic, those who have not yet built walls against the wild. In the deep Vhasian highlands, he is the \textit{Storm‑Father}, invoked by herders who watch lightning split the sky. In Ykrul, he is the \textit{Thunder‑Hoof}, the sound of a thousand horses stampeding. In Linn, he is the \textit{Raging Tide}, the storm that comes without warning. His followers are not scholars; they are dancers, drummers, shamans, and warriors who feel the storm in their bones before they hear it in the sky.

\begin{quote}
``Feel the drum in your bones before you hear it in the sky. When the storm speaks, you will already be dancing to its rhythm.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Primal Power:} storm, thunder, lightning, earthquake, wildfire
  \item \textbf{Shock and Awe:} overwhelming sensory impact, terror as a weapon
  \item \textbf{Fear‑Eater:} absorbing panic to fuel strength
  \item \textbf{Immediate Magic:} no rituals, no books -- only emotion, movement, and the moment
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Xhak'Thul}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Xhak'Thul does not sing melodies -- they drum. Their voice is a percussive roar, a heartbeat rhythm that shakes the ground. Their "song" is the pattern of a storm: the slow build of pressure, the sudden crack of lightning, the fading rumble of retreating thunder. Xhak'Thul's Cantors are often found in places without writing, where memory is carried in dance and the beat of a skin drum.

\textbf{The Cult of the First Drum.} Xhak'Thul's cults gather in clearing, on hilltops, or in caves that echo with thunder. The First Drum is a fellowship of dancers, shamans, and warriors who believe that civilisation is a cage and that the storm is the only honest law. A ritual of the Cult involves each member drumming on a skin drum (never bought, always made from an animal they killed themselves) until the first person collapses from exhaustion. The collapsed dancer is declared "storm‑taken" and must lead the next ritual. Their creed is not written -- it is beaten into the earth: \textit{"The sky does not ask permission. Neither do we."} Outsiders are welcome to dance, but they must bring a drum they made with their own hands.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Xhak'Thul (Craft / Storm)}

A witch who walks with Xhak'Thul is a \textit{storm‑smith} or \textit{drum‑maker} -- one who crafts the tools of primal power: a drum that can be heard for a mile, a spear of lightning‑struck wood that never misses its target, or a charm of meteoric iron that calls a storm when struck. Their magic works through raw, pre‑literate materials: a chisel that sparks when it hits stone, a needle that sews with thread made from hair singed by lightning, or a forge that tempers steel with the breath of a thunderstorm. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a "storm keystone" that channels lightning away from a hold. In Ykrul, a bone‑carver might shape a whistle that mimics the sound of a thunderclap, terrifying horses.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Thunder Chisel.} A chisel of meteoric iron, its edge rough as a lightning scar. Once per session, the witch may strike the chisel against any surface (stone, metal, wood). The impact sounds like a thunderclap, startling all within Near range (they must test Resolve DV 2 or lose their next minor action). The chisel can also be used to "split" a single, small object (a lock, a knot, a chain) as if struck by lightning -- it breaks cleanly, but the break smokes slightly.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Drum of the First Beat (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may tap a rhythm on any surface (a table, a shield, a tree trunk). For the next minute, all allies within Near range who hear the rhythm gain \textbf{+1 die} to Athletics and combat rolls, as their heartbeat synchronises with the storm. The rhythm cannot be sustained if the witch is silenced or takes a wound.
