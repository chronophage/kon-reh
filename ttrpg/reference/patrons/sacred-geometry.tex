\subsection{The Sacred Geometry, Architect of Perfect Forms (Harmony \& Immutable Law)}

\textit{Lore.} The Sacred Geometry teaches that perfect forms underlie all seeming chaos: the divine ratios that steer the spiral of galaxies, the symmetry that lattices frost, the measures that turn sound into song. Not merely a calculus but the hidden architecture of the world, it makes artists of order from those who serve it. Devotees do not count to chill the heart; they weigh and tune until beauty and structure converge, revealing that disorder is often only unrecognised design waiting to be drawn true.

This philosophy finds its purest expression in the game of **Kon'reh** – the living geometry played on an eight‑pointed diamond board, where Apexes, Sanctums, and the Cross teach the dance of form and consequence. Yet the Geometry itself is not the game; the game is a *mirror* of the Geometry. The Ykrul favour Kon'reh as a tool for oath‑geometry and battle‑field prediction, reading the angles of feuds and the arcs of raids. The Aelinnel, by contrast, use it to navigate thresholds, courtesy, and the hidden patterns of fae bargains – they count steps not to win, but to avoid stepping where the Green Gate listens. To master Kon'reh is to glimpse the Sacred Geometry in motion; to serve the Geometry is to play the game with the world itself, whether by the steppe fire or the hawthorn arch.

\begin{quote}
``In the drift of atoms, the sweep of planets, and the curve of a lover's smile, the same ratios ring. Learn them, and you may conduct the world's quiet music.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Perfect Proportion:} building and reading ideal ratios and harmonious relationships
  \item \textbf{Pattern Recognition:} discerning the structures that govern complex phenomena
  \item \textbf{Harmonic Resonance:} working with natural frequencies and number‑made consonance
  \item \textbf{Form Architecture:} imposing clear structure on tangled situations
  \item \textbf{Kon'reh Discipline:} the game as meditation, ritual, and tactical mirror
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of the Sacred Geometry}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of the Sacred Geometry sings in \textit{canons of proportion} -- melodies that follow strict mathematical progressions, harmonies that lock into resonant frequencies. Their voice is used to measure a room's acoustic perfection, to chart a course through a labyrinth by singing the ratios of its walls, or to lull a chaos‑spirit into stillness by singing the geometry of order. The Geometry's Cantors are often found in surveyor's tents, courtrooms, and the halls of mathematicians, humming the laws of form.

\textbf{The Cult of the Unbroken Compass.} The Geometry's cults gather in places where measure is sacred: ateliers, observatories, courtrooms of appeal, and the quiet rooms where Kon'reh is played. The Unbroken Compass is a fellowship of architects, cartographers, logicians, and Kon'reh masters who believe that the universe is a puzzle that can be solved, and that solving it is the highest form of worship. A ritual of the Cult involves each member laying out a perfect geometric figure (a circle, a square, a golden spiral) and then finding the flaw in another's figure. The one with the fewest flaws leads the next session. Their creed is inscribed on a brass protractor that is never used for mundane measurement: \textit{"Form follows number. Number follows truth. Truth follows no one."} Outsiders are welcome to bring a proof or a problem. If they cannot, they may learn by watching.

Among the Ykrul, the same cult takes a different flavour: Kon'reh boards are cut from hide, and the “pieces” are oath‑tokens. A Ykrul geomancer reads the angles of a feud as if they were lines of play, and a victory in Kon'reh can settle a blood‑debt without steel. Among the Aelinnel, the cult is quieter, more courteous: they use Kon'reh to map the thresholds between fae and mortal, and a solved puzzle can open a hedge‑gate that would otherwise remain sealed.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of the Sacred Geometry (Lay / Measure)}

A witch who walks with the Sacred Geometry is a \textit{compass‑scribe} or \textit{grid‑weaver} – one who crafts the tools of perfect form: a square that never warps, a level that finds true horizontal even on a ship at sea, or a set of Kon'reh pieces carved from the same block of stone so that they resonate together. Their magic works through the materials of measurement: a protractor that reveals hidden angles, a string that stretches to an exact ratio, or a lens that shows the underlying grid of a forest floor. They do not use hammers; they use scribes, rules, and the patient turning of a brass dial.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Protractor of Unseen Measures.} A brass semicircle etched with glyphs of ratio and proportion, its hinge oiled with a drop of mercury. Once per session, the witch may place the protractor against any surface (a doorframe, a contract, a creature’s brow) and read the “true angle” of its nature. For the next scene, the witch gains \textbf{+2 dice} to any roll that exploits a hidden weakness, structural flaw, or imbalance revealed by the measurement. The protractor can also be used to “true” a distorted measurement: touching it to a warped scale or a crooked ruler resets it to perfect accuracy for one use.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Compass of True Bearing (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may draw a circle with a compass (a real one, or just their finger) around a small area (a room, a crossroads). Those within the circle gain a perfect sense of direction and distance for the next hour; they are immune to mundane spatial confusion (mazes, fog, unfamiliar streets). However, the compass point leaves a faint, glowing mark on the ground that can be seen by anyone with supernatural senses.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
The Sacred Geometry suits makers and marshals who tune chaos into form. Its rites prize clean plans, crisp teamwork, and seeing the load paths hidden in trouble. The cost is rigidity: the more you perfect, the harder rough edges are to bear. For Ykrul players, the Geometry is a tool of honour and battle‑field geometry; for Aelinnel players, a tool of courtesy and threshold‑walking. Both are valid, and both lead to the same cold, beautiful truth.
