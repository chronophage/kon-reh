\subsection{The Savage Heart (Red Maw)}

\textit{Lore.} The Savage Heart is the truth behind the green veil: nature not as garden, but as gullet. It is the claw that tears, the fang that crushes, the hunger that stalks without malice and kills without mercy. Its followers are the druids you do not meet by firelight --- those who have learned that the forest feeds on its own, that the strong devour the weak, and that the only law is the one written in blood on snow.

Across the known world, the Savage Heart wears the face of the apex predator. In the Amaranthine core, it is the \textit{Great Bear}, patient and unstoppable, a wall of fur and fury. Among the Ykrul and Kuvani, it is the \textit{Wolf of the Steppe}, a pack hunter with tireless stamina. In Oshiira, it is the \textit{Golden Lion}, whose roar shatters the hunt's silence. In Sekogo, it is the \textit{Leopard of the Tall Grass}, a shadow that strikes once and is gone. In Dhahara, it is the \textit{Tiger of the Mangroves}, whose amber gaze promises a death that runs faster than you can flee. Wherever prey fears the dark, the Savage Heart is already there, waiting.

\begin{quote}
``The deer does not weep for the wolf. The sapling does not beg the axe. In the red maw, there is no cruelty---only truth. The truth that all flesh feeds the earth, and the earth feeds the strong.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Apex Predation:} hunting, stalking, dominating the food chain
  \item \textbf{Red Tooth:} killing as nature's sacrament, strength proven through violence
  \item \textbf{Scavenger's Wisdom:} using death for growth, nothing wasted
  \item \textbf{Territory and Pack:} dominance over a domain, leadership of the hunt
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Rites of the Savage Heart}

\paragraph{Rite of the Predator's Gaze (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A tooth from a predator that killed its own prey. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Mark a single target as your prey. Gain \textbf{+1 die} on all rolls to track, pursue, or intimidate that target. The effect lasts until the target escapes or is killed. \textbf{Tags:} FEAR, TRACK. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The prey feels your gaze; they suffer --1 die to resist social pressure from you. Mark \textbf{1 SB (Spades)}. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Blood Scent (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; Yes.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A fresh kill's blood smeared on your face. \\
\textbf{Effect:} You scent the air like a beast. Gain \textbf{+2 dice} to \textit{Investigation} or \textit{Survival} when tracking by smell. You may also detect fear -- you sense if a creature within Near is Frightened. \textbf{Tags:} SENSE, FEAR. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Your sense expands to Far, but you are overwhelmed by the smell of blood; mark \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} and suffer --1 die to social rolls until the end of the scene. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Savage Will (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A roar or howl that echoes. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Shrug off fear and hesitation. You gain \textbf{+1 die} to resist Fear or intimidation effects, and your next attack this scene gains \textbf{+1 Harm}. \textbf{Tags:} FEROCITY, RESIST. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The effect lasts the whole scene, but you mark \textbf{1 SB (Diamonds)} as your rage becomes difficult to control. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Guardian's Charge (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A claw or fang from a protective parent animal. \\
\textbf{Effect:} When an ally within Near range is attacked, you may immediately move up to your full movement toward the attacker and interpose yourself. The attack is redirected to you, and you gain \textbf{+1 Armor} against that single attack. If you are already in bear form (see \textit{Rite of the Shifting Shape}), you also gain \textbf{+1 die} to your next melee attack against that enemy. \\
\textbf{Tags:} PROTECT, SPEED, BEAST. \\
\textbf{Push It:} After redirecting the attack, you may make a free melee attack against the attacker. Mark \textbf{1 Fatigue}. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action (reaction); mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Shifting Shape (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; Yes.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A pelt or mask from the beast whose form you take. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Assume the form of a predator for the scene. Choose one:

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Bear (Tank Form)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Passive:} While in bear form, you gain \textbf{+1 Armor} and \textbf{+2 dice to Body rolls}.
  \item \textbf{Guardian's Pounce:} Once per scene, when you use \textit{Rite of the Guardian's Charge}, you may also knock the attacker prone (they must test Body+Resolve DV 3 or fall).
  \item \textbf{Limitation:} Your movement is reduced by one band (cannot Dash).
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Feline or Canine (Striker Form)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Passive:} While in striker form, you gain \textbf{+2 dice to Agility and Stealth} and \textbf{+1 band to movement} (can Dash as a free action once per scene).
  \item \textbf{Ambush Predator:} When you attack from stealth or while unseen, your attack gains \textbf{+1 Effect} and ignores 1 point of the target's Armor.
  \item \textbf{Limitation:} You suffer --1 Armor (or +1 Harm taken) while in this form; you are a glass cannon.
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textit{Amaranthine / Aeler:} Bear (tank), Wolf (striker)

  \item \textit{Dhahara:} Tiger (tank), Panther (striker)

You may speak but cannot use tools or cast other Rites while transformed. \textbf{Tags:} TRANSFORM, BEAST. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The transformation becomes instinctive -- you may shift as a free action once this scene, but mark \textbf{1 SB (Clubs)} as the beast presses against your mind. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of the Savage Heart}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of the Savage Heart sings in \textit{hunting calls} -- short, sharp cries that mimic the bark of a bear or the cough of a leopard. Their voice is used to coordinate a pack, to scare prey from hiding, or to summon a predator from the deep woods. They are rarely heard near villages; their songs echo in the places between game trails.

\textbf{The Cult of the Bloody Hand.} The Savage Heart's cults do not meet in groves -- they meet at kills. The Bloody Hand is a fellowship of hunters, trappers, and those who have tasted raw meat in the dark. A ritual involves each member bringing a fresh kill (any animal, any size) and laying it on a stone. The cult eats the kill raw, speaking the names of the animals they have taken. Their creed is spoken with full mouths: \textit{"The forest is a stomach. We are its teeth."} Outsiders are not welcome; they are either prey or future members.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of the Savage Heart (Craft / Hunter)}

A witch who walks with the Savage Heart is a \textit{skin‑stitcher} or \textit{tooth‑carver} -- one who crafts from the bodies of the slain. Their tools are made from bone, claw, fur, and sinew. A knife of wolf fang, a cloak of bear hide that traps warmth, a drum of leopard skin that calls the hunt. Their magic works through the materials of the kill: a needle that sews wounds shut with sinew that never rots, a chisel carved from a tiger's femur that splits wood as if it were soft meat, or a net woven from gut that tightens when prey struggles.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Flesh Chisel.} A chisel made from the limb bone of a great predator, its edge sharpened on stone. Once per session, the witch may strike the chisel against a fresh kill. The chisel "honours" the kill, allowing the witch to extract one perfect trophy (a tooth, a claw, a patch of fur) without damaging the rest. The trophy can be used as a material component for any Rite of the Savage Heart, granting +1 die to the roll.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Hunter's Snuff (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may blow a pinch of powdered bone into the air. For the next minute, the witch can smell the fear of any creature within Near range -- they know if a target is Frightened, and gain +1 die to track it.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
The Savage Heart rewards those who hunt, kill, and dominate. Its followers are not gentle guardians of nature -- they are its teeth and claws. Power comes from taking, from proving strength, from the raw act of predation. The cost is the slow loss of the civilised self, the creeping realisation that you are becoming the thing you stalk.

\noindent\textbf{Emphasises}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Predation as Sacrament:} killing is holy when done with purpose
  \item \textbf{Territory and Dominance:} claim, defend, expand
  \item \textbf{Pack Tactics:} hunting is a team sport
  \item \textbf{Red Tooth Wisdom:} death feeds life, cruelty is not malice but nature
  \item \textbf{Transformation:} becoming the beast to master the beast
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Rivalries}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Grimmir (Seasonal Wisdom):} Grimmir preserves the balance; the Savage Heart devours it. They are two faces of the same coin -- but the coin is always flipped.
  \item \textbf{The Sealed Gate (Containment):} The Gate wards things out; the Savage Heart tears down walls.
  \item \textbf{The Sacred Geometry (Harmony):} Order and perfect form are prey to the red maw.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Obligation Triggers}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{8+ Segments:} The Savage Heart demands you take a worthy trophy -- hunt a creature of equal or greater strength than yourself, alone.
  \item \textbf{10+ Segments:} You hear the call of the hunt always. Refusing to answer when prey is weak will cost you the use of your Rites until you kill again.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Signs of Favor}
Your eyes reflect light in darkness, your shadow seems to move independently, smaller animals flee your presence, and the scent of blood often follows you (real or imagined).
