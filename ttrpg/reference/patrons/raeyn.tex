\subsection{Raéyn, Mistress of the Sea (Tides \& Change)}

\textit{Lore.} Raéyn is the tempestuous goddess of the sea, the restless tide that carries news between shores and the promise of change between lives. She is mother to all who sail, her voice the wind that fills sails and her moods the storms that test every mariner's resolve.

But Raéyn's heart is torn by her greatest tragedy: her son Khemesh, the Kraken of the Depths, who embodies the crushing inevitability of the ocean's dark heart. Where Raéyn brings change and opportunity, Khemesh brings the final, inescapable pressure that grinds all things to nothing. Sailors pray to Raéyn for safe passage and favourable winds, but whisper Khemesh's name when seeking to lay the dead to rest beneath the waves.

Raéyn is passionate, mercurial, and fiercely protective of those who respect her domain. She favours those who read currents, bargain with weather, and carry news between shores. But cross her, and the sea itself becomes your enemy: fair weather turns to fury, and every wave a judgement.

\begin{quote}
Mark the tide, name your course, and trust the wave‑road. But speak ill of Khemesh, and even I may let the deep take you.
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Tides and Currents:} change, opportunity, the rhythm of the sea
  \item \textbf{Maritime Trade and Travel:} safe passage, navigation, weather
  \item \textbf{Storm and Calm:} the duality of protection and wrath
  \item \textbf{Motherhood and Loss:} fierce love, grief for Khemesh, the weight of a divided heart
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Raéyn}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Raéyn sings in \textit{shanties of the turning tide} -- melodies that rise and fall with the rhythm of waves, harmonies that mimic the creak of rigging and the cry of gulls. Their voice is used to calm a restless sea, to call a favourable wind, or to sing a drowned sailor's soul back to shore. Raéyn's Cantors are often found aboard ships, their songs becoming part of the vessel's luck.

\textbf{The Cult of the Salt‑Washed Circle.} Raéyn's cults gather on beaches at low tide, in sea‑caves, and aboard ships during storms. The Salt‑Washed Circle is a fellowship of sailors, pilots, fisherfolk, and coastal dwellers who honour the sea's gifts and respect its rages. A ritual of the Circle involves each member pouring a libation of fresh water into the sea (to honour Raéyn's tears for Khemesh) while speaking a wish for safe passage or a warning of danger ahead. Their creed is carved into driftwood and left on the tide: \textit{"The sea gives, the sea takes. We who love her learn to swim."} Outsiders are welcome to join a libation, but they must bring salt from their home shore.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Raéyn (Craft / Maritime)}

A witch who walks with Raéyn is a \textit{shipwright} or \textit{net‑mender} -- one who crafts with the materials of the tide: a hull carved from wood seasoned by sea‑salt, a sail woven with a prayer for fair wind, or a net tied with knots that remember every fish they have caught. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a "tide stone" at a harbour entrance, so that the current always favours outgoing vessels. In Linn, a ship's carpenter might seal a keel with a drop of their own blood, binding the ship to Raéyn's protection.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Tide Chisel.} A chisel of bronze, etched with wave patterns. Once per session, the witch may strike the chisel against a wooden threshold (a ship's prow, a dock post, a doorframe facing the sea). For the next scene, anyone who crosses that threshold is under Raéyn's eye: they gain \textbf{+1 die} to navigation and balance rolls, but the GM may spend 1 SB to make a sudden wave or gust complicate their action (as Raéyn tests their worth). The chisel can also be used to carve a small wave symbol into a tool, giving it +1 die on maritime tasks for the session.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Salt‑Kissed Rope (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may tie a simple knot in a rope, cord, or strand of hair while speaking a short prayer to Raéyn. The knot becomes a \textit{tide‑knot} -- when the witch unties it (as a free action), the nearest body of water (a puddle, a river, the sea) briefly rises or falls by a few inches, as if a small tide has turned. This can unstick a boat, wet a fire, or startle a guard -- but never causes direct harm.
