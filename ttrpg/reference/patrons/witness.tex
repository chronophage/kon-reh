\subsection{The Witness (Truth \& Revelation)}

\textit{Lore.} The Witness remembers what others bury. Every shadow cast and oath broken is a line in her unending ledger. She is the keeper of inconvenient truths, the patron of those who seek to expose lies or recover forgotten knowledge. Her followers learn that knowledge comes with a price—the weight of remembering what others would forget.

Among the peoples of Fate’s Edge, the Witness is known by many names. In Ecktoria, she is the \textit{Ink‑Eye}, the Censor’s clerk who copies every redacted line onto a private scroll. In Vhasia, she is the \textit{Fractured Mirror}, prayed to by spies who seek to reveal a rival’s true loyalty. In the Mistlands, she is the \textit{Fog‑Scribe}, whose name is whispered when a bell‑line fails and the dead must be identified by their secrets. And in the shadows of every great city, there are those who follow the \textit{Apocalyptic Witness} – a doomsaying cult that believes the world will end when the last hidden truth is finally spoken aloud, and who work to hasten that revelation by exposing every lie, no matter the cost.

\begin{quote}
I will show you what you would rather forget. But first, you must forget what you think you know.
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Truth Revelation:} exposing lies, uncovering secrets, forcing confessions
  \item \textbf{Memory Keeping:} preserving knowledge, recalling forgotten facts
  \item \textbf{Inconvenient Knowledge:} learning what others wish to hide
  \item \textbf{Prophetic Warning:} seeing the consequences of hidden truths
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Rites of the Witness}

\paragraph{Rite of the Lingering Glimpse (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; Yes (Investigation/Notice only).} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A trace of the thing to be remembered (hair, dust, a spoken name). \\
\textbf{Effect:} Gain \textbf{+1 die} to your roll to investigate or notice something directly related to the trace within the current scene. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Gain \textbf{+2 dice} instead, but mark 1 segment on a \textbf{Memory Strain Clock [4]}. If the clock fills, you gain Fatigue 1 and suffer –1 die on Investigation/Notice rolls until the end of the next scene. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of Piercing Scrutiny (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A circle drawn with chalk or string while focusing on the truth to be sought. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Within the zone, gain \textbf{+1 die} to rolls to detect deception (Insight vs. Deceit, spotting social tells) or to recall hidden knowledge (Lore/Investigate for memory). Social interactions within the zone begin one Position step worse for those attempting to deceive. \\
\textbf{Push It:} One target within the zone must make a Wits test (DV 3) or involuntarily reveal one pertinent lie or hidden fact they are currently concealing (GM determines relevance). Regardless of the test result, mark Exposure +1 for the target(s) in the zone. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Echoing Truth (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A reflective surface (mirror, still water, polished metal) used to focus on the target. \\
\textbf{Effect:} The target must make a Resolve test (DV 3) or suffer –1 die to rolls involving memory, deception, or resisting interrogation for the scene. If they fail, you may ask one specific, factual question about something they know, and they must answer truthfully or suffer \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} as the memory is forcibly drawn forth. \\
\textbf{Push It:} If the target fails their Resolve test, you may ask a second question, but the mental intrusion causes them Harm 1 (Stress/Mental). \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Immutable Record (Standard, 7 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A document signed by all parties within the zone, or a spoken pact witnessed by the caster. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Bind the agreement. Any party who knowingly breaches it suffers \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} immediately and gains a persistent \textbf{Oathbreaker’s Mark} Condition (–1 die on social rolls involving honour, trust, or oaths until amends are made or a significant act redeems them). \\
\textbf{Push It:} The bond becomes magically enforced for one specific, crucial clause: violation automatically inflicts Harm 1 (Stress) on the breaker in addition to the SB and Mark. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Unveiled Heart (High, 12 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A private setting where the target feels safe or is speaking freely. \\
\textbf{Effect:} The target suffers –2 dice to all attempts to conceal true emotions, intentions, or lies for the scene. Any successful social roll (Sway, Command, Deceit) made by the target generates \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} as the effort to maintain falsehoods under the Witness’s gaze creates internal discord. \\
\textbf{Push It:} You may designate one specific, complex question about the target’s motivations, fears, or hidden loyalties. If you successfully use Sway or Insight against them this scene, you automatically learn the answer to that question. The intense scrutiny marks \textbf{1 SB (Spades)} for you as the Witness’s attention lingers. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+2 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} 2 Boons).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 6 segments.

\paragraph{Rite of the Final Reckoning (High, 13 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A formally called gathering (court, council, family meeting) within the consecrated zone. \\
\textbf{Effect:} All present must speak their greatest debt, wrongdoing, or hidden truth related to the gathering’s purpose. Those who lie or withhold suffer Harm 2 (Stress/Reputation). Truth‑tellers gain +2 dice to social actions for the remainder of the scene within the zone. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The truth becomes inescapable – even indirect lies or evasions related to the core topic suffer the Harm 2 penalty. The absolute nature of the revelation creates \textbf{2 SB (Diamonds)} as the disruption to fates and secrets resonates. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} Extended rite; mark \textbf{+2 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} 2 Boons).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 7 segments.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of the Witness}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of the Witness sings in \textit{truth‑tolling} – clear, piercing melodies that cut through evasion, harmonies that make the hidden surface. Their voice is used to compel a confession, to sound an alarm that a secret is about to be spoken, or to sing a historical record into being, each note a word in a ledger. The Witness’s Cantors are often found in courtrooms, in archive vaults, and at the bedsides of the dying, singing the last truth.

\textbf{The Congregation of the Unmasking (Apocalyptic Cult).} The most feared of the Witness’s cults, the Congregation of the Unmasking believes that the world will end when the last hidden truth is finally revealed – and that this ending is not a catastrophe but a liberation. They seek to expose every lie, every buried crime, every forgotten promise, no matter the cost. A ritual of the Congregation involves each member speaking aloud a truth they have kept secret, then burning a representation of the lie that concealed it. Their creed is painted on a scroll that is never fully unrolled: \textit{“When all that is hidden is seen, the world will either break or be free. We will be there to witness which.”} Outsiders are not welcome; they are potential sources of truth – or obstacles to be unmasked. The Congregation has been known to destroy families, topple governments, and start wars in their pursuit of total revelation. They are loved by none and feared by all, but even their enemies cannot deny that they are right: some secrets truly are worth dying to expose.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of the Witness (Craft / Record)}

A witch who walks with the Witness is a \textit{ledger‑scribe} or \textit{seal‑breaker} – one who crafts the tools of permanent record: a book that cannot be altered once written, a quill that writes in ink that fades only when the truth it records is fulfilled, or a press that stamps each page with a sigil of authenticity. Their magic works through the materials of documentation: a chisel that carves a truth into stone so that it cannot be erased, a needle that sews a confession into a garment so that the wearer cannot deny it, or a forge that tempers a door that can only be opened by someone who has never told a lie. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a “witness stone” at a courthouse entrance, so that all testimony given within is remembered by the stone for a century. In Thepyrgos, a bookbinder might stitch a volume that screams if its pages are cut.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Truth Chisel.} A chisel of white stone, its edge sharp as a razor. Once per session, the witch may carve a single word into a surface (stone, wood, leather) while speaking a truth aloud. The word glows faintly for a day. Anyone who touches the carved word and speaks a lie in its presence will feel a sharp sting (1 Fatigue) as the truth chisel protests. The chisel can also be used to “unseal” a locked record – a sealed envelope will open by itself if the witch carves the recipient’s true name into the wax.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Record of a Breath (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may hold a blank piece of parchment up to a person’s lips as they speak. The parchment absorbs the spoken words, recording them exactly as they were said – including tone, hesitation, and any unconscious meaning. The parchment can be read by anyone (the words appear in the speaker’s own handwriting), but it crumbles to dust after being read aloud. The witch may also use this ability to record the final words of the dying, preserving them for one day.
