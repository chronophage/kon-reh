\subsection{Isoka, Angel of Serpents (Transformation \& Renewal)}

\textit{Lore.} Isoka, sister to Ikasha (Shadow) and Inaea (Mercy), completes the Triad of Transformation. Where thresholds and compassion mark her sisters" domains, Isoka teaches that every self is temporary---identity is a skin to be shed so growth can continue. Her serpents are omens and teachers: each cast skin a lesson in release; each venom a catalyst for necessary change. Those who walk her path become alchemists of the self, embracing dissolution as the first motion of rebirth.

\begin{quote}
Do not mourn the skin you shed. It was never meant to last. The venom that burns away the old self is the same that grants the strength to become new.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Rites of Isoka}

\paragraph{Rite of the Venomous Benediction (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Touch; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A drop of serpent's venom or shed snakeskin. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Bless an ally's strike with serpentine malice. Their next successful attack this scene inflicts \textbf{+1 Harm} and the target must roll Resolve (DV 3) or become Poisoned (loses 1 die on physical rolls until cured). \\
\textbf{Push It:} The venom suffuses the caster too -- gain \textbf{+1 die} to melee attacks this scene, but also suffer --1 die to Resolve tests against fear or corruption. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Loosening Skin (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Discarded snakeskin or loose thread. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Gain \textbf{+1 die} to resist an ongoing Condition this scene, or reroll one \textbf{1} on an escape/evasion. On success, you may declare the Condition \textit{shed} and create a 2‑segment \textit{Transformation Residue} clock to ignore a similar effect later. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Also ignore one minor movement penalty; leave a token of the old self that can be traced (mark \textbf{1 SB (Diamonds)}). \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Subtle Shift (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Palmed trinket passed hand‑to‑hand. \\

\textbf{Push It:} Bypass one minor identity check; you must maintain the false role to scene end (generate \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} if challenged). \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Shedding (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Full change of clothing and an adopted mannerism. \\
\textbf{Effect:} \textbf{+2 dice} to resist one named ongoing Condition; once/session declare a minor physical contingency retroactively ("I packed the tool," "I took that step earlier"). Create a 6‑segment \textit{Shed Identity} clock. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Clear a temporary identity‑based Minor Condition; your former identity stirs in the fiction (an ally, rival, or witness appears). \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Forked Tongue (Standard, 7 XP)} \hfill \\

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Isoka}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Isoka sings in \textit{whispers of shedding} -- melodies that rise and fall like a snake's undulating motion, with harmonies that slither rather than resolve. Their voice is used to ease a difficult transformation (ritual scarring, a change of identity, the acceptance of loss) or to accelerate a foe's destructive change (madness, mutation, despair). Isoka's Cantors are often found in snake cults, their songs weaving venom and mercy in equal measure.

\textbf{The Cult of the Coiled Heart.} Isoka's cults are the classic Sword & Sorcery snake cults -- secretive, dangerous, and obsessed with metamorphosis. They gather in sunken temples, caverns beneath city sewers, or groves where adders nest. A ritual of the Coiled Heart involves each member holding a live serpent while speaking a name they wish to shed: a debt, a crime, a former allegiance. The serpent is then released into a central pit. Their creed is carved in ouroboros rings: \textit{"What devours itself is never consumed by another."} Outsiders are rarely invited; those who stumble into a rite are offered a choice -- join, drink venom and be remade, or be forgotten as the cult sheds their memory.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Isoka (Craft / Shedding)}

A witch who walks with Isoka is a \textit{scale‑smith} or \textit{venomer} -- one who understands that transformation is a craft. Their magic works with the materials of change: a forge that tempers steel by "shedding" impurities, a alembic that distills venom into medicine, or a loom that weaves a new identity from discarded threads. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might "shed" an old tunnel by sealing it and carving a new one; in Zakov, a poisoner might shed a target's will with a single drop.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Shedding Knife.} A small, curved blade of obsidian or serpentine, never used for combat -- only for cutting away what is no longer needed. Once per session, the witch may cut a physical token of an old identity (a lock of hair, a uniform patch, a letter) and burn it. For the remainder of the session, they gain +1 die on all rolls to deceive, hide, or escape detection, as the old self's traces are literally erased.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Venom's Mercy (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may apply a drop of harmless (or carefully dosed) venom to a wound, a drink, or a lock. Choose one effect: remove 1 Fatigue from an ally (as a mild stimulant), impose --1 die on a target's next physical roll (a mild paralytic), or force a target to speak a single truthful sentence (a truth serum). The victim may resist with a Resolve test (DV 3) if they are aware of the venom.
