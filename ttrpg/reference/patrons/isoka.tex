\subsection{Isoka, Angel of Serpents (Transformation \& Renewal)}

\textit{Lore.} Isoka, sister to Ikasha (Shadow) and Inaea (Mercy), completes the Triad of Transformation. Where thresholds and compassion mark her sisters’ domains, Isoka teaches that every self is temporary—identity is a skin to be shed so growth can continue. Her serpents are omens and teachers: each cast skin a lesson in release; each venom a catalyst for necessary change. Those who walk her path become alchemists of the self, embracing dissolution as the first motion of rebirth.

\begin{quote}
Do not mourn the skin you shed. It was never meant to last. The venom that burns away the old self is the same that grants the strength to become new.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Rites of Isoka}

\paragraph{Rite of the Loosening Skin (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A discarded snakeskin or a strand of your own hair. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Shrug off a temporary burden. Remove one Condition (Fear, Poison, Fatigue, or a minor social stain) that you have acquired this scene. Alternatively, reroll one failed roll to escape restraint or pursuit. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Also ignore one environmental penalty (difficult terrain, smoke, etc.) for the scene, but leave a visible trace of your passage (mark \textbf{1 SB (Diamonds)}). \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Forked Tongue (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; Yes.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A harmless lie whispered to a mirror. \\
\textbf{Effect:} For one scene, you may speak ambiguously without penalty. When you use \textit{Deception} or \textit{Sway} to conceal your true intent or pass as someone else, you may treat a Partial Success as a Clean Success (the lie holds). On a Miss, however, the GM gains \textbf{2 SB} as your deceit unravels in an embarrassing way. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend the effect to one willing ally; they may also lie freely for the scene. Mark \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} as both of you become tangled in the same falsehood. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Shedding (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A complete change of clothing and a token from your former role (a badge, a lock of hair, a written name). \\
\textbf{Effect:} You shed one persistent identity – a title, a bond, a debt, or a reputation. For the remainder of the scene, you are treated as a stranger to those who knew you in that role. You gain \textbf{+2 dice} on any roll to avoid detection, escape pursuit, or convince others that you are not yourself. Create a 6‑segment \textit{Shed Identity} clock; when it fills, the old identity returns as a separate NPC (a rival, a witness, or a ghost). \\
\textbf{Push It:} Immediately erase one minor obligation (a Debt Clock segment or 1 Obligation to a Patron) as you slough it off. Mark \textbf{1 SB (Clubs)} as the discarded debt finds a new debtor. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of Venomous Rebirth (Standard, 9 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Touch; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A drop of venom from a serpent that has shed its skin within the last day. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Inflict or cure through poison’s paradox. Choose one:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Curse:} Touch a creature and inject venom. They suffer \textbf{+1 Harm} (poison) and must test Resolve (DV 4) or gain the \textit{Poisoned} Condition (–1 die to physical actions until cured).
\item \textbf{Cleanse:} Touch a poisoned or diseased creature. The venom neutralises the malady; remove one \textit{Poisoned} or \textit{Diseased} Condition, and the target clears 1 Fatigue.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Push It:} Apply both effects to the same target (curse and cleanse in a painful purge), or affect two different targets. Mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Unremembered Name (Standard, 7 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; Yes.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A written name on a scrap of paper, burned in a copper dish. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Erase a single person’s memory of you – or your memory of them. Choose one:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Unperson:} One creature within Near who knows you must test Resolve (DV 4). On a failure, they forget you exist for one scene (cannot recognise you, recall your name, or target you deliberately).
\item \textbf{Unburden:} You forget a specific guilt, a failed oath, or a traumatic memory. Clear 1 Fatigue and downgrade one psychological Condition (e.g., \textit{Haunted, Obsessed}) by one step.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Push It:} The effect lasts a full day, but the GM gains \textbf{2 SB} as reality strains to remember what was lost. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of Complete Metamorphosis (High, 13 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Extended; Self; Yes.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A full identity kit (garb, voice, mannerisms), a serpent’s shed skin, and a personal item from the life you are leaving. \\
\textbf{Effect:} After an hour of ritual, you emerge with a new face, voice, and documented identity (if you have prepared papers). For one week, you are immune to recognition by anyone who knew your previous self, and you gain \textbf{+2 dice} on all \textit{Deception} and \textit{Stealth} rolls related to maintaining that new identity. Create an 8‑segment \textit{New Identity} clock; when it fills, the old self may attempt to reassert itself (GM’s discretion). \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend the metamorphosis to a willing ally, or make the change permanent (but you lose access to one of your Bonds or a Minor Asset forever). Mark \textbf{+2 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} \textbf{2 Boons}).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 7 segments.

\paragraph{Rite of the Cast-Off History (High, 14 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Extended; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Burning or defacing all mundane records of your old life (letters, portraits, legal documents). \\
\textbf{Effect:} After a full night’s ritual, your previous identity becomes \textit{unreliable}. Common records and casual memories of you become vague or contradictory; those who track you via that identity suffer –2 dice on their rolls. You gain \textbf{+2 dice} on all rolls that rely on your new identity. Create a 6‑segment \textit{Erased Past} clock; when it fills, one intimate from your old life senses the deception and may begin a quiet investigation. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Arrange a plausible “death” for your old self (a funeral, a witness). Mark \textbf{2 SB (Clubs/Spades)}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} \textbf{2 Boons}).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 7 segments.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Isoka}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of Isoka sings in \textit{whispers of shedding} – melodies that rise and fall like a serpent’s undulation, with harmonies that slither rather than resolve. Their voice is used to ease a difficult transformation (ritual scarring, a change of identity, the acceptance of loss) or to accelerate a foe’s destructive change (madness, mutation, despair). Isoka’s Cantors are often found in snake cults, their songs weaving venom and mercy in equal measure.

\textbf{The Cult of the Coiled Heart.} Isoka’s cults are secretive, dangerous, and obsessed with metamorphosis. They gather in sunken temples, caverns beneath city sewers, or groves where adders nest. A ritual of the Coiled Heart involves each member holding a live serpent while speaking a name they wish to shed: a debt, a crime, a former allegiance. The serpent is then released into a central pit. Their creed is carved in ouroboros rings: \textit{“What devours itself is never consumed by another.”} Outsiders are rarely invited; those who stumble into a rite are offered a choice – join, drink venom and be remade, or be forgotten as the cult sheds their memory.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Isoka (Craft / Shedding)}

A witch who walks with Isoka is a \textit{scale‑smith} or \textit{venomer} – one who understands that transformation is a craft. Their magic works with the materials of change: a forge that tempers steel by “shedding” impurities, an alembic that distills venom into medicine, or a loom that weaves a new identity from discarded threads. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might “shed” an old tunnel by sealing it and carving a new one; in Zakov, a poisoner might shed a target’s will with a single drop.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Shedding Knife.} A small, curved blade of obsidian or serpentine, never used for combat – only for cutting away what is no longer needed. Once per session, the witch may cut a physical token of an old identity (a lock of hair, a uniform patch, a letter) and burn it. For the remainder of the session, they gain \textbf{+1 die} on all rolls to deceive, hide, or escape detection, as the old self’s traces are literally erased.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Venom’s Mercy (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may apply a drop of harmless (or carefully dosed) venom to a wound, a drink, or a lock. Choose one effect: remove 1 Fatigue from an ally (as a mild stimulant), impose –1 die on a target’s next physical roll (a mild paralytic), or force a target to speak a single truthful sentence (a truth serum). The victim may resist with a Resolve test (DV 3) if they are aware of the venom.
