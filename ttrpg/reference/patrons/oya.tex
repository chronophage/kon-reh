\subsection{Oya --- The Wind of the Sahel (Liberation \& Dust)}

\subsubsection*{Lore}
Oya is the harmattan --- the dry wind that sweeps the grasslands, carrying red dust from the Ashaani desert. She is neither cruel nor kind. She is the breath that clears the old, the storm that scatters what has grown stagnant, the wind that opens a locked door by rattling it until the latch gives way. Her followers are iconoclasts, slave-liberators, and those who believe that the only way to defeat a corrupt system is to let it collapse under its own weight --- and then scatter the ashes.

In Ashaan, Oya is whispered as the \textit{Dust-Rider}, a faceless figure who appears during sandstorms to cut the chains of the bound. In the Sahel, she is the \textit{Cleansing Wind}, invoked before burning a corrupt contract. In Ngomebe, her name is carved on keystones that have cracked --- because Oya taught the stone to break. She does not build. She makes room for those who will.

\begin{quote}
``Oya does not ask permission. She does not wait for the right season. She blows, and the dead leaves fall, and the dust settles on new ground. That is not destruction. That is preparation.''
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Dust and Wind:} the harmattan, sandstorms, the dry breath of the Sahel
  \item \textbf{Change:} destroying the old to make way for the new
  \item \textbf{Liberation:} breaking chains, opening locked doors, scattering what binds
  \item \textbf{Cleansing:} burning corrupt contracts, erasing false ledgers, the red dust that covers old tracks
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Thiasos and Codex (Runekeeper Options)}

A Runekeeper sworn to Oya does not bind a creature of settled earth or still water --- they bind a creature of dust and dry wind. Their \textbf{Thiasos} is a small beast or mythological being that embodies the harmattan: a sand-colored gecko that sheds its skin twice as fast as any other, a small horned viper that leaves no tracks in loose sand, a dust-bathed finch that appears only after a windstorm, a scarab beetle that rolls dry dung across the hottest plains, or a pale mouse that nests in abandoned grain silos and vanishes when the wind shifts. This creature requires no food, but it must be allowed to feel the dry wind each day --- a sealed room suffocates it. If neglected for a week, it becomes \textit{Compromised} (its color fades, and its bearer suffers --1 die on all rolls involving escape or evasion until it next tastes a dust storm).

The \textbf{Codex} of Oya is never a book kept indoors. It may be a collection of cracked clay tablets that must be read in the wind, a scroll of desert leather that crumbles if not exposed to dry air, a bundle of dried reeds tied with red thread, or a single shard of green glass that holds the memory of a storm. Because the harmattan erases what is written, the Codex requires upkeep: the Runekeeper must spend an hour each week in a dry, windy place, allowing the dust to resettle on the pages, or the Rites stored there become faint (treat as Compromised until re-swept).

\subsubsection*{Patron's Gift (Imbuement)}

Once per scene as an action, a Runekeeper of Oya may touch a held item (weapon, tool, or garment) and whisper a word of the harmattan. For the rest of the scene, the item grants \textbf{+1 die} to \textit{Stealth} or \textit{Escape} rolls in dry or dusty environments, and the first time each scene the bearer would be restrained (by chains, ropes, or a grapple), they may reroll the escape check (the wind loosens what binds). After the scene, the user marks \textbf{+1 Obligation} to Oya.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Oya}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Oya sings in \textit{dry tones} --- melodies that crackle like dust on the tongue, harmonies that feel like a parched throat. Their voice is used to call the harmattan, to make a guard's eyes sting so they cannot see, or to sing a locked door open by vibrating the latch. Oya's Cantors are often found in deserts, on the edge of mines, or in the company of those who are about to burn a bridge.

\textbf{The Cult of the Red Dust.} Oya's cults meet in places where the wind never stops: dry riverbeds, abandoned quarries, the lee side of tall buildings during a sandstorm. The \textit{Red Dust} is a fellowship of iconoclasts, ex-slaves, and those who have destroyed a contract that bound them. A ritual involves each member writing a binding obligation on a piece of parchment, then throwing it into the wind. If the parchment is carried away, the obligation is considered voided --- by Oya, not by law. If it falls back to the ground, the debtor must perform a service before the next storm. Their creed is written in dust on a single stone: \textit{``What the wind scatters, no chain can gather.''} Outsiders are welcome if they bring something they wish to see destroyed --- a letter, a collar, a memory.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Oya (Lay / Dust)}

A witch who walks with Oya is a \textit{dust-weaver} or \textit{wind-caller} --- one who crafts erasure. Their magic works through the tools of dispersal: a small leather pouch of red dust from a slave market, a fan made of dried palm fronds, a whistle that mimics the sound of the harmattan. Their tools are never permanent; a fan is burned after its seventh use, a whistle cracks after the ninth. In the Brass Coast, a Sidhi witch might scatter red dust at a customs post to make the officers forget a smuggler's face; in Ashaan, a Breath-less agent uses Oya's rites to erase a name from the Black Hand's ledger.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Dust Pouch.} A small leather pouch filled with red dust collected from a place of oppression --- a slave market, a debtors' prison, a burnt courthouse. Once per session, the witch may scatter the dust in a line across a threshold. For the next hour, any who cross that threshold forget why they entered (Resolve DV 4 to resist). The pouch can also be used to ``clean'' a document: sprinkling dust on a contract erases one clause permanently. The dust is consumed on use.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Scatter the Tracks (Basic, 4 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may touch a set of tracks (footprints, wheel ruts, blood trails) and whisper a word of the harmattan. The tracks are erased as if a wind had passed --- no mundane tracker can follow them. The Hollow, however, notices the absence; the GM gains 1 SB to spend on a pursuit complication later in the session.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
Oya rewards those who act decisively, who destroy what is corrupt, and who trust that the wind will scatter the evidence. Followers become masters of erasure, escape, and the art of making things vanish. The corruption progression leads toward a being that is itself becoming dust --- forgetful, light, and difficult to hold --- but one that can pass through any locked door. Ideal for iconoclasts, ex-slaves, thieves, and anyone who has ever wanted to burn a contract and walk away whistling.
