\subsection{Grimmir, the Wild Speaker (Primal Wisdom \& Seasonal Cycles)}

\textit{Lore.} Grimmir walks the threshold between cultivated lands and untamed wilderness, embodying the ancient pact between humanity and the natural world. Neither wholly beast nor entirely human, Grimmir speaks in root and stone, seasons and sap. Those who walk his path gain insight into nature's patterns, the tongues of leaf and fang, and mastery over the primal forces that shape the wild places—yet must choose which voice to trust: the cultivated mind or the feral heart.

\begin{quote}
``In the space between planted row and forest edge, where the first grain met the first acorn, Grimmir waits. He speaks in the rustle of leaves, the migration of birds, and the patient growth of ancient oaks. Listen, and learn that true power comes not from dominion, but from harmony.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Seasonal Wisdom:} cyclical patterns, timing, and natural rhythms
  \item \textbf{Verdant Speech:} communion with plant and animal life
  \item \textbf{Growth \& Decay:} life‑cycles, healing, and transformation
  \item \textbf{Boundary Keeping:} guarding wild spaces and the old order
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Grimmir}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Grimmir sings the \textit{drone of green things} – a low, humming chant that mimics the vibration of leaves in wind or the deep thrum of growing roots. Their voice is used to coax reluctant seeds, to calm wounded animals, and to mark the turning of the seasons in rituals that entire villages attend. A Grimmir Cantor does not seek an audience; they sing to the land itself, and the land answers in fruit, rain, or silence.

\textbf{The Cult of the Green Accord.} Grimmir’s cults are not secret, but they are often solitary. A “cult” may be a single hermit who tends a sacred grove, or a scattered network of rangers who meet only at the solstices. Their rituals are practical: the spring planting, the autumn cull, the winter waiting. Their creed is carved into the oldest oak in each grove: \textit{“Take only what the season gives. Give back what the season will need.”} Outsiders are welcome at the solstice fires, but expected to work.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Grimmir (Craft / Threshold)}

A witch who walks with Grimmir is a \textit{hedgewright} or \textit{path‑keeper} – a crafter of living boundaries. Their work may be invisible: weaving a branch to guide deer away from a village, carving a marker that tells the frost when to end, or building a root bridge that only becomes passable at low tide. This craft can look like “infrastructure” – an Aeler True‑Mason might carve a stone that remembers the boundary between a mine and a sacred spring, or a Linn shipwright might season a keel with a prayer for the right wind.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Green Chisel.} A chisel of petrified wood, cold to the touch but warm when it cuts living matter. Once per session, when used to carve a boundary marker (a post, a stone, a tree) it inscribes a minor \texttt{[SEASONAL]} tag into the threshold. The marker will “remember” the season of its carving for one year, granting +1 die to rolls that honour that season’s work.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Knot of the Turning Year (2 XP).} Once per scene, you may tie a simple knot (in a cord, a strand of hair, or a blade of grass) and speak a phrase appropriate to the current season. The knot subtly influences the weather or local plant growth for the next hour – a gust of wind, a sudden rain, or a patch of ivy that grows just enough to provide cover. The GM adjudicates effect.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
Grimmir rewards harmony over conquest, cyclical thinking, and fluent communication with the living world. Expect environmental problem‑solving, seasonal planning, and hard choices at the boundary of field and forest. Power grows with attunement; the price is comfort, certainty, and the ease of polite society.
