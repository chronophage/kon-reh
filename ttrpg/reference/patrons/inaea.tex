\subsection{Inaea, Angel of the Spider (Webs, Fate \& Patient Predation)}

\textit{Lore.} Inaea sits in patient silence at the centre of the unseen lattice. Where others see isolation, she sees threads—debt, affection, rivalry, loyalty—all binding mortals to one another. She is comforter and conspirator alike: the hearth‑mother who eases grief with a warm hand, and the shadow‑spinner who knots fates so they cannot slip free. Those who serve her learn to pull strands, tying allies closer and ensnaring foes until mercy or trap is inevitable.

The spider does not rush. It weaves, waits, and feels the tremor of every caught fly. So too does Inaea. Her mercy is never without cost; her kindness is a thread that, once accepted, can be pulled taut at a moment of her follower’s choosing. To serve her is to understand that every bond is a leash, every promise a snare, and every act of compassion a line cast into the dark.

\begin{quote}
“The thread that comforts is the same that binds. The knot that saves is also the snare. What you accept from my hand, you give me leave to tighten.”
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Webs of Obligation:} debts, promises, favours, and the ties that bind
  \item \textbf{Patient Predation:} setting traps, waiting for the moment to strike
  \item \textbf{Unseen Connections:} sensing hidden relationships, leverage, and weakness
  \item \textbf{Mercy as Control:} healing and aid that leave lasting threads
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Rites of Inaea}

\paragraph{Hearth-Thread Knot (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; Yes (link once).} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A thread tied around a wrist or shared cup. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Bind yourself and one willing target (ally or neutral) with a sympathetic thread. Both gain \textbf{+1 die} to Aid each other this scene. Additionally, if either takes Harm, the other may reduce it by 1 (to a minimum of 0) by suffering that same Harm instead. This transfer is voluntary for an ally; for an unwilling target, you must succeed on a Resolve test (DV 3) to force the link. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend to two allies; mark \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} as your own lifeline strains. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Snaring Filament (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A knotted cord or woven scrap. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Lay an invisible snare in a lane, doorway, or narrow passage. The first hostile creature to cross suffers \textbf{–2 dice} on their next action and must succeed on a Resolve test (DV 2) or lose their movement for that turn (tangled). The snare lasts for the scene or until triggered. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Lay a second snare elsewhere, but allies risk entanglement if they forget its location; mark \textbf{1 SB (Spades)} as the web spreads thin. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Strand of Inevitability (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Three coloured threads (white, black, red) braided together. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Link two actors (which can include yourself) with a visible or invisible strand. For the rest of the scene, whenever one acts, the other is dragged into consequence. You may trigger this linkage once to either:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Force \textbf{1 SB (Hearts)} on the linked target after they perform a significant action.
  \item Gain \textbf{+2 dice} on a \textit{Setup}, \textit{Predict}, or \textit{Manipulate} roll that exploits the link (e.g., “you cannot betray me without hurting your ally”).
\end{itemize}
The link is one‑way or two‑way as you choose. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Invert the direction of the link once after it is set (e.g., make the pursuer stumble instead of the pursued). Mark \textbf{1 SB (Diamonds)} as the weave resists. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Weaver's Glance (Standard, 7 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Observe a spider finishing a web’s radial line (or a convincing imitation). \\
\textbf{Effect:} Ask the GM one question about a hidden tie, debt, or leverage between two people, or between a person and a faction. The GM must answer truthfully. Your next \textit{Manipulate}, \textit{Pressure}, or \textit{Intimidate} action exploiting that knowledge gains \textbf{+1 Effect}. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Reveal a secret tie that was not previously declared in the fiction (the GM must invent a plausible connection). Mark \textbf{Exposure +1} as your own entanglements surface. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Spinner’s Debt (Standard, 9 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A silver coin and a strand of your own hair, knotted together. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Offer a willing creature a boon: they may immediately clear \textbf{1 Fatigue} or gain \textbf{+1 die} on their next roll. In return, they accept a \textbf{Promise Clock [4]} named “Debt to the Web.” You may advance this clock by 1 segment at any time as a free action (once per scene). When the clock fills, you may demand a single service from them – not impossible, but significant (e.g., “speak for me at court,” “reveal a secret,” “take a blow meant for me”). Refusal causes them to suffer \textbf{Harm 1 (Stress)} and the clock resets. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend the offer to a group (up to three targets). Each who accepts gives you a separate Promise Clock. Mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Binding Knot (High, 11 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A silk loop tied between two hands, then cut or knotted with a spoken vow. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Bind two parties (individuals or small groups) to a mutual oath. Breaking the vow forces \textbf{2 SB} on the violator and marks them with a subtle tell (a visible thread, a nervous twitch, a lingering scent) that persists for one week. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Widen the binding to up to four parties, each of whom may define one loophole. Exploiting a loophole causes \textbf{1 SB (Clubs)} as the web tightens around the exploiter. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} \textbf{2 Boons}).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 6 segments.

\paragraph{Merciful Severing (High, 13 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Touch; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A white thread burned to ash, spoken with a name. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Sever one harmful tie – a curse, a toxic obsession, a coercive bond, or a supernatural debt. Remove one Condition or \textbf{1 SB} tied to it. This is an act of genuine mercy; the target may be unwilling, but the thread can still be cut (Resolve test DV 4 to resist). \\
\textbf{Push It:} Sever a tie without the target’s consent or knowledge; they will not know who cut them free, but you mark \textbf{+2 Obligation} as the web recoils against you, and the severed strand may attach to you instead (GM’s discretion). \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} \textbf{2 Boons}).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 7 segments.

\paragraph{Web of Slumber (High, 12 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Extended; Zone; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A complete spider’s web, stolen at dawn, and a lullaby hummed into it. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Weave an area of drowsy stillness. For the next hour, any creature entering the zone must succeed on a Resolve test (DV 4) or fall into a light trance, losing their next action and suffering \textbf{–1 die} to all perception rolls while inside. Allies you touch beforehand are immune. The web is invisible until triggered. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The trance becomes deeper – sleeping creatures cannot be woken by non‑magical means for the duration. Mark \textbf{2 SB (Spades/Hearts)} as the web draws hungry attention. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (\textit{Invoke:} \textbf{2 Boons}).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 6 segments.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Inaea}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of Inaea sings in \textit{knots} – melodies that loop back on themselves, harmonies that tie two voices into one. Their voice is used to HEAL quarrels, to bind a community together after a tragedy, or to weave a subtle compulsion into a seemingly innocent lullaby. Inaea’s Cantors are beloved at weddings, feared at treaty signings, and always watched by those who understand that a pretty tune can be a leash.

\textbf{The Cult of the Woven Circle.} Inaea’s cults gather in domestic spaces – kitchens, weaving sheds, longhouses – and resemble extended families more than secret societies. A ritual of the Woven Circle involves each member tying a coloured thread around their wrist, then passing the ends to their neighbours. The resulting web is then tightened, and a communal promise spoken. The cult’s creed is embroidered onto a single cushion that passes from host to host: \textit{“No thread stands alone. The weakest knot still holds the strongest weight.”} Outsiders are welcome if they bring a thread and a truth. Betrayal of the circle is repaid by being unravelled – socially, financially, or worse.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Inaea (Craft / Threshold)}

A witch who walks with Inaea is a \textit{web‑weaver} or \textit{knot‑smith} – one who understands that every threshold (a door, a marriage, a treaty) is held together by invisible threads. Their magic works with domestic tools: a spinning wheel that twists fate, a loom that weaves obligations into cloth, or a pair of shears that cuts a single strand to end a feud. This craft can be as humble as tying a red thread around a child’s wrist for protection, or as grand as weaving a tapestry that records an entire bloodline’s debts.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The True Loom.} A small, portable loom – no larger than a book – woven from hawthorn wood and strung with thread dyed in three colours (red for blood, white for truth, black for secret). Once per session, the witch may weave a single knot into a new or existing thread; that knot becomes a \emph{promise clock} [4]. When the clock fills, the promise is either fulfilled or broken, as agreed at the time of weaving.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Bind the Threshold (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may tie a simple knot in a cord, ribbon, or strand of hair and place it on a threshold (a doorstep, a gate, a bridge). For the next hour, anyone crossing that threshold must succeed on a Resolve test (DV 2) if they intend to break an oath, tell a lie, or harm someone inside. The knot visibly tightens if a violation is attempted.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
Inaea rewards those who see relationships as both comfort and leverage. Her followers are not merely kind; they are patient, observant, and willing to let a debt ripen before collecting. Intrusions often take the form of a sudden obligation – a person you helped now demands help in return, a promise you thought forgotten tightens, or a web you spun catches someone you did not intend. Refusing an intrusion does not break the thread, but it frays it: the GM may mark a \textit{Tangled Web} clock that, when filled, causes all your active promises and bonds to tighten at once, often at the worst moment.

  \item \textbf{Mercy as Control:} healing and aid that leave lasting threads
\end{itemize}
