\subsection{Inaea, Angel of the Spider (Webs \& Fate)}

\textit{Lore.} Inaea sits in patient silence at the center of the unseen lattice. Where others see isolation, she sees threads—debt, affection, rivalry, loyalty—all binding mortals to each other. She is comforter and conspirator alike: the hearth‑mother who eases grief with a warm hand, and the shadow‑spinner who knots fates so they cannot slip free. Those who serve her learn to pull strands, tying allies closer and ensnaring foes until mercy or trap is inevitable.

\begin{quote}
“The thread that comforts is the same that binds. The knot that saves is also the snare.”
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Inaea}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Inaea sings in \textit{knots} – melodies that loop back on themselves, harmonies that tie two voices into one. Their voice is used to mend quarrels, to bind a community together after a tragedy, or to weave a subtle compulsion into a seemingly innocent lullaby. Inaea’s Cantors are beloved at weddings, feared at treaty signings, and always watched by those who understand that a pretty tune can be a leash.

\textbf{The Cult of the Woven Circle.} Inaea’s cults gather in domestic spaces – kitchens, weaving sheds, longhouses – and resemble extended families more than secret societies. A ritual of the Woven Circle involves each member tying a coloured thread around their wrist, then passing the ends to their neighbours. The resulting web is then tightened, and a communal promise spoken. The cult’s creed is embroidered onto a single cushion that passes from host to host: \textit{“No thread stands alone. The weakest knot still holds the strongest weight.”} Outsiders are welcome if they bring a thread and a truth. Betrayal of the circle is repaid by being unravelled – socially, financially, or worse.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Inaea (Craft / Threshold)}

A witch who walks with Inaea is a \textit{web‑weaver} or \textit{knot‑smith} – one who understands that every threshold (a door, a marriage, a treaty) is held together by invisible threads. Their magic works with domestic tools: a spinning wheel that twists fate, a loom that weaves obligations into cloth, or a pair of shears that cuts a single strand to end a feud. This craft can be as humble as tying a red thread around a child’s wrist for protection, or as grand as weaving a tapestry that records an entire bloodline’s debts.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The True Loom.} A small, portable loom – no larger than a book – woven from hawthorn wood and strung with thread dyed in three colours (red for blood, white for truth, black for secret). Once per session, the witch may weave a single knot into a new or existing thread; that knot becomes a \emph{promise clock} [4]. When the clock fills, the promise is either fulfilled or broken, as agreed at the time of weaving.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Bind the Threshold (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may tie a simple knot in a cord, ribbon, or strand of hair and place it on a threshold (a doorstep, a gate, a bridge). For the next hour, anyone crossing that threshold must succeed on a Resolve test (DV 2) if they intend to break an oath, tell a lie, or harm someone inside. The knot visibly tightens if a violation is attempted.
