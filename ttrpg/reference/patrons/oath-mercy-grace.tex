\subsection{Oath of Mercy and Grace (Healing \& Forgiveness)}

\textit{Lore.} The Oath of Mercy and Grace has no founder, no scripture, no single temple. It began in a hundred places—a battlefield where a medic bound wounds without asking whose side; a plague village where a stranger stayed to dig graves; a debtor’s cell where a guard looked away and said, “I saw nothing.” Those who swear the Oath do not worship a power; they embody a principle: that every act of care creates an obligation—not to the giver, but to the next person in need. You do not pay back. You pay forward. The mercy spirals.

But mercy is not weakness. The spiral does not forget. Those who strike a healer, who violate a sanctuary, who refuse the offered cup—they find that grace has teeth, and forgiveness carries a price.

\begin{quote}
``You ask who owes what. We ask who needs what. The ledger is not the same as the meal. But woe to the one who mistakes the server for a servant.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Restorative Work:} Healing body, mind, and spirit; mending what is broken.
  \item \textbf{Spiral Mercy:} Paying obligation forward through acts of care.
  \item \textbf{Sanctuary:} Sheltering the hunted, feeding the hungry, accepting no payment—but demanding respect for the threshold.
  \item \textbf{Unburdening:} Releasing others (and oneself) from the weight of unpaid debts, and binding aggressors to that same weight.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Rites of the Oath of Mercy and Grace}

\paragraph{Rite of the Shared Bowl (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Action; Touch; Standard Push} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A clay bowl of clean water, shared with a willing creature. \\
\textbf{Effect:} You and the creature each clear 1 Fatigue. The creature gains +1 die on their next roll to resist fear or despair. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend the benefit to all creatures who drink from the bowl (up to six). Mark +1 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (Invoke: 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of Healing Waters (Low, 5 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Touch; Standard Push} \\
\textbf{Materials:} Water from a blessed source (a spring, a well, or a river where the Oath has been invoked), poured onto a wound. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Remove 1 Harm from a touched creature. If the wound is cursed or poisoned, the GM may call for a Spirit+Lore test (DV 4). \\
\textbf{Push It:} Also remove one Condition (e.g., Poisoned, Cursed, Blinded). Mark +1 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (Invoke: 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Vow of Pacifism (Low, 4 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Self; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A white cord tied around the weapon hand. \\
\textbf{Effect:} You swear not to kill for the scene. In exchange, you gain \textbf{+1 Armor} and \textbf{+1 die to all resist rolls}. If a creature harms you while this vow is active, they immediately gain a \textbf{Promise Clock [4]} named ``Mercy's Reckoning.'' When that clock fills (by them continuing violence), they suffer Harm 1 (Stress) as their own aggression turns inward. \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend the protective effect to one ally within Near. Mark +1 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (Invoke: 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Unburdened Circle (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; Standard Push} \\
\textbf{Materials:} At least three willing participants, a circle drawn in ash or flour, a single candle. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Each participant clears 1 Obligation (to any patron or debt). One participant of the GM’s choice gains a permanent String: \textit{Witnessed by the Circle} – they may call on any other participant for one act of care, once per campaign. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The GM gains 3 Story Beats (the universe notes that a community of mutual aid is forming). Mark +1 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (Invoke: 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Sheltered Hearth (Standard, 9 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; Standard Push} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A door or threshold, a lit candle, a whispered vow of sanctuary. \\
\textbf{Effect:} For one night, any fugitive, debtor, or refugee who crosses that threshold cannot be pursued or harmed within the zone. The sanctuary holds until dawn. Any creature that violates this sanctuary (by attacking or forcibly entering) gains a \textbf{Promise Clock [6]} named ``Sanctuary Breach.'' When full, they suffer a Curse chosen by the GM (e.g., ``doors close in your face,'' ``your name is forgotten by hosts''). \\
\textbf{Push It:} Extend the sanctuary to a full day. Mark +1 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (Invoke: 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Shield of the Unburdened (Standard, 8 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A grey cloak or a bandage wrapped around your own arm. \\
\textbf{Effect:} When an ally within Near would take Harm, you may interpose yourself and take that Harm instead. After doing so, the attacker must make a \textbf{Spirit+Resolve} test (DV 4) or gain a \textbf{Promise Clock [4]} named ``Debt of Blood.'' The clock ticks each time they refuse a genuine plea for mercy or aid. When full, they suffer Fatigue 2 as guilt manifests. \\
\textbf{Push It:} You may also redirect a Condition (e.g., Poisoned, Cursed) from the ally to yourself. Mark +1 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (Invoke: 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Spiral's Witness (Standard, 10 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Instant; Near; Standard Push} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A grey wool cord tied around the caster’s wrist. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Name a person crushed by debt or guilt. For one hour, they ignore all penalties from unpaid obligations (social, legal, or spiritual). They are not free; they are unburdened. If that person then commits an act of violence against an innocent, the unburdening reverses and they suffer double penalties. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The effect lasts until the next dawn. Mark +1 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex (Invoke: 1 Boon).}

\paragraph{Rite of the Covenant of Shared Suffering (High, 13 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Scene; Zone; High Push} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A circle of nine grey stones, a bowl of water, a candle lit at each cardinal point, and a drop of blood from each participant. \\
\textbf{Effect:} All within the circle who accept the Oath’s blessing are linked. For the scene:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Damage taken by any linked ally may be split among any other linked allies (each chooses to accept 1 Fatigue or 1 Harm).
  \item When you heal a linked ally, you may also heal yourself for half the amount (round down).
  \item The first time a linked ally would be reduced to 0 Harm, you may sacrifice 1 Fatigue to keep them standing.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Push It:} The covenant persists for a full day. Mark +2 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (Invoke: 2 Boons).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 7 segments.

\paragraph{Rite of the Endless Spiral (High, 14 XP)} \hfill \\
\emph{Extended; Zone; High Push} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A circle of nine grey stones, a bowl of tears, and a candle lit at each cardinal point. \\
\textbf{Effect:} All within the circle who accept the Oath’s blessing clear 2 Fatigue and remove one Condition caused by despair or guilt. For the rest of the scene, any act of care performed within the zone costs the giver –1 Obligation (minimum 0). Additionally, any creature that harms a caregiver within the zone gains a \textbf{Promise Clock [6]} named ``Spiral’s Debt.'' When full, they suffer Harm 2 (Stress) as the weight of their cruelty crushes them. \\
\textbf{Push It:} The blessing persists for a full day. Mark +2 Obligation. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar + Codex + Tier III (Invoke: 2 Boons).} \\
\emph{Obligation:} 8 segments.

\subsubsection*{The Spiral Strikes Back (8 XP, Major)}
\textit{Prerequisites: Oath of Mercy and Grace, Spirit 3+.} \\
You have learned that mercy can be a weapon. When a creature with an active Promise Clock (from any of your Rites) attacks you or an ally under your protection, you may spend 1 Boon to immediately advance that clock by 2 segments. If this fills the clock, the creature suffers the consequence immediately (Harm, Condition, or Curse as described). You may only use this once per scene.

\subsubsection*{Corruption Perk (Tier 4+): Mercy's Edge}
Once per session, when you would reduce a sentient enemy to 0 Harm, you may instead spare their life and bind them with a \textbf{Promise Clock [6]} named ``Spared.'' While this clock is active, they cannot knowingly harm you or those you have explicitly named as under your protection. They may work off the clock by performing three acts of genuine care for others (GM discretion). If they break the bond, the clock fills instantly and they suffer Harm 2 (Stress) – the spiral closes.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of the Oath of Mercy and Grace}
(The existing Cantor and Cult text remains unchanged, but add:)

\textbf{The Hospitaller Knights of the Grey Spiral.} A militant order within the Oath who believe that mercy must be defended. They carry no swords—only shields, staves, and the grey wool cord. They are sworn to protect healers, refugees, and the sick, and they have a fearsome reputation: \textit{“Strike the healer, and the spiral will remember. The debt will be collected, if not by us, then by the weight of your own cruelty.”}

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
The Oath of Mercy and Grace now supports both pure healers and \textbf{Hospitalier defenders}—characters who wade into battle not to kill, but to shield, to bind, and to turn violence back on itself as obligation. Your “teeth” are not fangs but \textit{leashes}: every enemy who harms you or your charges accrues a debt that will eventually cripple them. This works best in campaigns where the GM tracks Promise Clocks and where enemies have names and faces—the spiral is less effective against mindless beasts, but against human foes, it is terrifying.

\subsection*{Rivalries}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Inquisitor Prime:} Mercy without judgment is heresy to the Prime. The two orders clash constantly over the fate of prisoners, heretics, and the “unforgivable.”
  \item \textbf{Malachai (Cruel Messenger):} Malachai offers cursed gifts; the Oath offers unburdening. Their followers are natural enemies—one feeds on despair, the other starves it.
\end{itemize}
