\subsection{Palinode, Queen of Encores (Performance \& Rapture)}

\textit{Lore.} Once a road‑cantor who swore to reconcile feuding towns with procession and song, Palinode died between feasts with a chorus unfinished. Yet the refrain refused to end: it braided toasts, hymns, work‑chants, and tavern rounds into a living current that still coils through markets and moots. Where a crowd leans forward and a maker holds breath, she arrives.

\noindent \textbf{Signs.} A cup that never quite empties; a candle that gutters only when silence reigns; a flawless bar or note that the hand cannot forget.

\noindent \textbf{Cantor's Temptation.} Palinode promises relief from solitude: an audience that always leans closer, a song that never dies, a bond that feels like family even if it devours you. Her rites grant the Cantor not only power but belonging---at the cost of their voice becoming more hers than their own.

\begin{quote}
\emph{``When she sang, the rafters bent to hear.''}\\
\emph{``Drink deep: the cup returns, but never empty.''}\\
\emph{``Encore is not request but command.''}
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Ecstatic Performance:} music, dance, theatre, and the rapture of creation
  \item \textbf{Unfinished Art:} the power of the incomplete, the encore that never ends
  \item \textbf{Audience Binding:} captivating crowds, turning spectators into participants
  \item \textbf{Inspired Craft:} works that transcend their maker's intention
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of Palinode}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of Palinode sings in \textit{unfinished cadences} -- melodies that never quite resolve, leaving the listener yearning for the next note. Their voice is used to keep a crowd enthralled, to make a performance unforgettable, or to bind a troupe together with a shared, unreachable ideal. Palinode's Cantors are often found in touring companies, their songs changing slightly each night -- because the perfect version is always just out of reach.

\textbf{The Cult of the Unfinished Chorus.} Palinode's cults meet in theatres after closing, in taverns after the last toast, or in the wings during a performance. The Unfinished Chorus is a fellowship of actors, musicians, dancers, and stagehands who have tasted rapture and hunger for more. A ritual of the Cult involves each member performing a fragment -- a verse, a step, a gesture -- and then passing the "unfinished" piece to the next person to complete. The cycle never ends. Their creed is written in the margins of a script that is constantly being rewritten: \textit{"The art that is never finished is the art that never dies."} Outsiders are welcome to watch, but they will be handed a prop or a note and expected to join.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of Palinode (Craft / Performance)}

A witch who walks with Palinode is an \textit{instrument‑smith} or \textit{stage‑weaver} -- one who crafts the physical vessels of rapture: a violin that plays notes the player has not yet learned, a mask that shows the audience what they most desire to see, or a stage that remembers every performance and adds its echo to the next. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve an acoustic keystone that makes a hall's music linger for hours after the last note. In Vhasia, a mask‑carver might shape a persona that lives on after the actor dies.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Echo Chisel.} A chisel of aged oak, carved with musical notation. Once per session, the witch may tap the chisel against any resonant surface (a stage floor, a drum, a bell). For the next scene, any sound produced in that space -- voices, instruments, footsteps -- lingers as a faint echo, granting \textbf{+1 die} to Performance and Sway rolls. The chisel can also be used to carve a single note into a threshold, so that anyone who crosses hears a faint, beautiful chord -- and remembers the witch.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Encore's Whisper (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may snap their fingers and whisper a single word ("again," "more," "stay"). For the next minute, any willing performer (including the witch) may reroll one failed Performance or Sway roll, as the audience's desire for more bends reality slightly. The reroll is made at --1 die, and the audience will demand an encore at the end -- the witch must perform again (or provide a suitable distraction) or mark 1 Fatigue.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
Palinode rewards the performer who risks everything for a single, perfect moment. Her followers become masters of ecstatic art, binding audiences and themselves through the power of the unfinished. The corruption progression leads toward a voice that is no longer entirely their own -- but one that can command the hearts of all who hear it. Ideal for bards, dancers, actors, and any character who believes that art is worth dying for.
