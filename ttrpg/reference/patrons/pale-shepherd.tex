\subsection{The Pale Shepherd, Guide of Transitions (Passage \& Melancholic Wisdom)}

\textit{Lore.} The Pale Shepherd walks the seam between all things – life and death, joy and sorrow, memory and forgetting, the known and the unmarked. Neither wholly kind nor cruel, the Shepherd guides those who must pass through the hardest doors. Cloaked, with shadow‑sheep that bear the shed wool of old possibilities, the Shepherd offers safe conduct through liminal space for a price paid in memory, feeling, or fate. Those who walk this road learn the hidden routes between states of being and can lead others through their hardest crossings – but each passage leaves them more attuned to endings than beginnings.

Across the known world, the Shepherd is known by many names and faces:
\begin{itemize}
  \item In \textbf{Aeler}, the Shepherd is the \textit{Breath‑Accountant}, who weighs the last exhalation of the dying and guides it to the Deep Vaults. Dwarven death‑priests invoke the Shepherd to ensure the dead do not linger in the stone.
  \item In \textbf{Aelaerem}, the Shepherd is the \textit{Pale Gardener}, who walks the hedgerows at twilight, collecting the souls of those who die far from home. Halflings leave a cup of milk on the doorstep for the Shepherd to drink – and for the Hollow to pass by.
  \item In \textbf{Linn}, the Shepherd is the \textit{Fog‑Walker}, a grey‑cloaked figure seen on the horizon before a storm. Sailors who drown are said to join the Shepherd’s flock, guiding their kin to safe harbour.
  \item In \textbf{Viterra}, the Shepherd is the \textit{Silent Notary}, who witnesses the last will and testament of the dying. Hedge‑priests invoke the Shepherd to make a deathbed promise binding.
\end{itemize}
Wherever mortals face the hardest thresholds, the Pale Shepherd is there – not to judge, but to accompany.

\begin{quote}
``The Shepherd goes where feet fail, speaks where voices thin, and points where paths run dim. Each journey loosens the knot that tied you to the life you left behind.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Liminal Navigation:} walking and reading thresholds, dusk‑roads, and in‑betweens
  \item \textbf{Passage Guidance:} steadying hands through grief, change, and transformation
  \item \textbf{Melancholic Wisdom:} understanding born of farewells and last looks
  \item \textbf{Memory Shepherding:} gathering, carrying, and setting down weighted moments
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Rites of the Pale Shepherd}

\emph{Scene; Near; No.} \\
\textbf{Materials:} A small stone from a grave or threshold, warmed in the palm. \\
\textbf{Effect:} Ease a hard crossing. One target gains \textbf{+1 die} to rolls involving change, decision, or emotional steadiness this scene. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\

\emph{Scene; Self; Yes.} \\

\textbf{Push It:} The way runs through a true between‑place; gain the route but mark \textbf{1 SB (Spades)} as boundary powers take notice. \\
\textbf{Invoke:} 1 action; mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \\
\emph{Requires: Familiar (\textit{Invoke:} 1 Boon).}

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of the Pale Shepherd}

\textbf{The Cantor’s Song.} A Cantor of the Pale Shepherd sings in \textit{passing tones} – melodies that begin in a major key and end in a minor one, or that fade to silence rather than resolve. Their voice is used to ease a dying person’s passage, to comfort the grieving at a funeral, or to lull a restless spirit back to the threshold. The Shepherd’s Cantors are often found at hospices, battlefields, and the bedsides of the old, singing the softest of farewells.

\textbf{The Cult of the Grey Flock.} The Shepherd’s cults meet in liminal places: cemeteries at dusk, crossroads at midnight, the rooms above funeral parlours. The Grey Flock is a fellowship of death‑doulas, grief counsellors, and those who have lost so much that they have become experts in loss. A ritual of the Flock involves each member speaking the name of someone they have guided – and then a moment of silence for those they could not save. Their creed is embroidered on a woollen banner that is never washed: \textit{“The door is never locked. We only hold it open.”} Outsiders are welcome to sit quietly, but they must bring a memory to share.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of the Pale Shepherd (Craft / Threshold)}

A witch who walks with the Shepherd is a \textit{shroud‑weaver} or \textit{threshold‑carver} – one who crafts the tools of safe passage: a woollen shroud that keeps the dead from wandering, a stone carved with a doorway that leads to the next room (or the next life), or a bell that rings only when a soul is ready to cross. In Aeler, a True‑Mason might carve a “threshold stone” at a hospice’s entrance, so that the dying feel no pain crossing it. In Viterra, a hedgewitch might weave a shroud that shows the deceased’s favourite flower in the pattern.

\textbf{The Witch’s Tool: The Wool‑Winder.} A wooden spindle carved from a branch that grew over a grave. Once per session, the witch may wind a length of undyed wool around their hand while speaking a name. The wool becomes a \textit{memory cord} – a physical thread that holds one specific memory of the named person. The cord can be given to another to grant them a single vivid memory of the deceased (e.g., “grandmother’s hands knitting”), or it can be burned to release the memory into the threshold, easing grief.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Shepherd’s Stone (2 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may touch a small, smooth stone – preferably from a grave or a threshold – and place it on a dying person’s chest (or on a doorframe before a difficult journey). The stone glows faintly, and for the next hour, the person feels no fear of the passage ahead. They gain \textbf{+1 die} to any roll involving courage, acceptance, or farewells, and may ignore one point of Fatigue from emotional distress.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
The Pale Shepherd favours those who steady others at the edge of change. Its gifts open hidden roads, soften endings, and lend courage to cross. Its prices lean toward memory, ease, and the simple warmth of belonging – spent to buy safe passage when it matters most.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Liminal Mastery:} reading and walking the in‑between
  \item \textbf{Passage Guidance:} anchoring companions through turns of fate
  \item \textbf{Melancholic Wisdom:} counsel drawn from endings
  \item \textbf{Memory Shepherding:} carrying and setting down what weighs
  \item \textbf{Threshold Navigation:} ways through walls the world insists upon
\end{itemize}
