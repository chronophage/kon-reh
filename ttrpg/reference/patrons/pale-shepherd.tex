\subsection{The Pale Shepherd --- Guide of Transitions (Passage \& Melancholic Wisdom)}

\subsubsection*{Lore}
The Pale Shepherd walks the seam between all things -- life and death, joy and sorrow, memory and forgetting, the known and the unmarked. Neither wholly kind nor cruel, the Shepherd guides those who must pass through the hardest doors. Cloaked, with shadow-sheep that bear the shed wool of old possibilities, the Shepherd offers safe conduct through liminal space for a price paid in memory, feeling, or fate. Those who walk this road learn the hidden routes between states of being and can lead others through their hardest crossings -- but each passage leaves them more attuned to endings than beginnings.

The Shepherd has no will, only a path; no demand, only a presence. It does not seek followers, but those who stand at thresholds -- of death, of grief, of irreversible change -- may find the Shepherd already there, waiting to accompany them. Its ``demands'' are not commands but the natural toll of passage: a memory left behind, a name forgotten, a tear shed for no reason. The Shepherd does not punish refusal; it simply withdraws, and the crossing becomes harder.

Among the Aelaerem, the Shepherd is called the \textit{Pale Gardener}, who walks the hedgerows at twilight, collecting the souls of those who die far from home. In Aeler, the \textit{Breath-Watcher} weighs the last exhalation of the dying. In Linn, the \textit{Fog-Walker} appears on the horizon before a storm. In Viterra, the \textit{Silent Notary} witnesses deathbed testaments. Wherever mortals face the hardest thresholds, the Pale Shepherd is there -- not to judge, not to command, but to accompany.

\begin{quote}
``The Shepherd goes where feet fail, speaks where voices thin, and points where paths run dim. Each journey loosens the knot that tied you to the life you left behind.''
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{Domain Focus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Liminal Navigation:} walking and reading thresholds, dusk-roads, and in-betweens
  \item \textbf{Passage Guidance:} steadying hands through grief, change, and transformation
  \item \textbf{Melancholic Wisdom:} understanding born of farewells and last looks
  \item \textbf{Memory Shepherding:} gathering, carrying, and setting down weighted moments
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Thiasos and Codex (Runekeeper Options)}

A Runekeeper sworn to the Pale Shepherd does not bind a creature of fierce guardianship --- they bind a companion of gentle thresholds. Their \textbf{Thiasos} is a small beast or being that embodies passage and memory: a gray mouse that nests in graveyards and knows every hidden crack, a moth with dusty wings that lands on the hands of the dying, a small lamb with wool the color of ash that appears only at crossroads, a pale fox that leaves no footprints in fresh snow, or a white-breasted raven that calls once when a soul is ready to cross. This creature requires no food, but it must witness a farewell or a threshold crossing each day (a death, a departure, or a significant goodbye). If neglected for a week, it becomes \textit{Compromised} (it grows silent, refusing to guide; its bearer suffers --1 die to all rolls involving thresholds or transitions until the creature is shown a proper farewell).

The \textbf{Codex} of the Pale Shepherd is never a book of spells alone. It may be a ledger of names written in disappearing ink that reappears only at twilight, a bundle of woolen cords each tied for a soul guided, a series of river stones stacked in a cairn that shifts with each passing, or a smooth slate etched with the outline of a door that never quite closes. Because the Shepherd's truths are written in passage, the Codex requires upkeep: the Runekeeper must spend an hour each week whispering the names of those they have guided, or the Rites stored there become faint (treat as Compromised until re-dedicated at a threshold).

\subsubsection*{Patron's Gift (Imbuement)}

Once per scene as an action, a Runekeeper of the Pale Shepherd may touch a held item (weapon, tool, or garment) and whisper a word of crossing. For the rest of the scene, the item grants \textbf{+1 die} to \textit{Insight} or \textit{Medicine} rolls when dealing with the dying, the grieving, or those undergoing great change, and the first time each scene the bearer would suffer a Condition from fear or despair, they may ignore it (the Shepherd steadies their hand). After the scene, the user marks \textbf{+1 Obligation} to the Pale Shepherd.

\subsubsection*{Cantors \& Cults of the Pale Shepherd}

\textbf{The Cantor's Song.} A Cantor of the Pale Shepherd sings in \textit{passing tones} -- melodies that begin in a major key and end in a minor one, or that fade to silence rather than resolve. Their voice is used to ease a dying person's passage, to comfort the grieving at a funeral, or to lull a restless spirit back to the threshold. The Shepherd's Cantors are often found at hospices, battlefields, and the bedsides of the old, singing the softest of farewells.

\textbf{The Cult of the Gray Flock.} The Shepherd's cults meet in liminal places: cemeteries at dusk, crossroads at midnight, the rooms above funeral parlors. The Gray Flock is a fellowship of death-doulas, grief counselors, and those who have lost so much that they have become experts in loss. A ritual of the Flock involves each member speaking the name of someone they have guided -- and then a moment of silence for those they could not save. Their creed is embroidered on a woolen banner that is never washed: \textit{``The door is never locked. We only hold it open.''} Outsiders are welcome to sit quietly, but they must bring a memory to share.

\subsubsection*{Witchcraft of the Pale Shepherd (Lay / Threshold)}

A witch who walks with the Shepherd is a \textit{shroud-weaver} or \textit{threshold-carver} -- one who crafts the tools of safe passage: a woolen shroud that keeps the dead from wandering, a stone carved with a doorway that leads to the next room (or the next life), or a bell that rings only when a soul is ready to cross. Their tools are not hammers; they use spindles, wool, and the gentle pressure of a hand on a doorframe. In Aeler, a True-Mason might carve a ``threshold stone'' at a hospice's entrance, but a witch of the Shepherd would instead wind a memory cord around the lintel.

\textbf{The Witch's Tool: The Wool-Winder.} A wooden spindle carved from a branch that grew over a grave. Once per session, the witch may wind a length of undyed wool around their hand while speaking a name. The wool becomes a \textit{memory cord} -- a physical thread that holds one specific memory of the named person. The cord can be given to another to grant them a single vivid memory of the deceased (e.g., ``grandmother's hands knitting''), or it can be burned to release the memory into the threshold, easing grief.

\textbf{A Hedge Gift: Shepherd's Stone (Basic, 4 XP).} Once per scene, the witch may touch a small, smooth stone -- preferably from a grave or a threshold -- and place it on a dying person's chest (or on a doorframe before a difficult journey). The stone glows faintly, and for the next hour, the person feels no fear of the passage ahead. They gain \textbf{+1 die} to any roll involving courage, acceptance, or farewells, and may ignore one point of Fatigue from emotional distress.

\subsection*{Playstyle Notes}
The Pale Shepherd favors those who steady others at the edge of change. Its gifts open hidden roads, soften endings, and lend courage to cross. Its prices lean toward memory, ease, and the simple warmth of belonging -- spent to buy safe passage when it matters most. The Shepherd never demands; it only offers. When a follower accumulates enough Obligation, an intrusion might be a sudden need to guide a dying stranger, a forgotten threshold that now calls to them, or a dream of a road that must be walked before dawn. Refusing the intrusion does not incur wrath -- merely the quiet withdrawal of the Shepherd's favor until the follower once again chooses to accompany someone through their hardest door. Ideal for death-doulas, grief counselors, and anyone who understands that the kindest act is sometimes just to walk beside.

\noindent\textbf{Emphasizes}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Liminal Mastery:} reading and walking the in-between
  \item \textbf{Passage Guidance:} anchoring companions through turns of fate
  \item \textbf{Melancholic Wisdom:} counsel drawn from endings
  \item \textbf{Memory Shepherding:} carrying and setting down what weighs
  \item \textbf{Threshold Navigation:} ways through walls the world insists upon
\end{itemize}
