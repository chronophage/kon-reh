\subsection*{What This Is}
A complete, table−ready arc set across the trade−bridge city of \textbf{Silkstrand}, a night of high etiquette at \textbf{Ravenswood Manor}, and the stair−ruled metropolis of \textbf{Thepyrgos}. Themes: contracts and crowds; masks and glamour; law as performance. The adventure emphasizes ¶osition{} play, interlocking \Term{timers}, and a flavorful §B{} economy.

\subsection*{At a Glance}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{Part I −−− A Song of Poison and Plague} (Tier I−−II): Urban investigation as the Gemcutters and Alchemists edge toward riots while a bard of Desire plays both sides.
  ℹtem \textbf{Part II −−− The Gilded Cage} (Tier II−−III): Survive a night of velvet hospitality; each room is a social encounter with taboos and tells.
  ℹtem \textbf{Part III −−− The Crimson Masque} (Tier III): A public ritual threatens to swap the souls of the city's elite with the troupe; stop it on stage before the final bell.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}
\textbf{System Notes.} Use the game's Position ladder and \DV{} to make leverage matter. City−specific ◇{}s represent codified outcomes (permits, seats, escorts) that shift ¶osition{} without a roll. Timers pace consequences. The GM spends §B{} when 1s appear to twist crowds, shift law, or advance rails.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Adventure Background}
Silkstrand sits over dyed waterways and legal bridges; permits and seals are visible power. Two guilds−−−\textbf{Gemcutters} and \textbf{Alchemists}−−−blame each other for ``poisoned'' water. \textbf{Sel Virelle}, a charismatic devotee of Desire, orchestrates paranoia to ripen the city for a prestige heist by the \textbf{Crimson Masque} troupe, agents of Glamour. Their endgame plays out in Thepyrgos, where bell−law and stair−rights can crown or crush reputations overnight.

\subsection*{Session Count & Tier Flow}
Intended for 6−−10 sessions. Start at Tier I, crest at Tier III. Each part includes typical \DV{} and ¶osition{} guidance to ease scaling.

\section*{Part I −−− A Song of Poison and Plague (Tier I−−II)}

\subsection*{Strong Hooks (Choose 1−−2 to Open)}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Dyewater Omen:} The river runs a prophetic hue; the crowd demands action from anyone wearing steel or sigils.
ℹtem \textbf{Bridge Duel Gone Wrong:} A fatal misstep during a public duel implicates a guild; the watch seeks neutral witnesses (the PCs).
ℹtem \textbf{Missing Ledgers:} Archivolt records vanish; without them, permits are void and riots loom.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Core Timers}
\Clock{City Unrest}{6}, \Clock{Poisonings}{4}, \Clock{Sel's Glamour Net}{4}, plus one Guild Favor timer.

\subsection*{Key NPCs}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Guildmistress Kavra (Gemcutters)} −−− \emph{Desires:} stability, monopoly on luxury. \emph{Leverage:} bravos, patronage. \emph{Tell:} rubs thumb over a signet when lying.
ℹtem \textbf{Master Orun (Alchemists)} −−− \emph{Desires:} legitimacy, funding. \emph{Leverage:} inspectors, antidotes. \emph{Tell:} recites measurements under stress.
ℹtem \textbf{Sel Virelle (Bard of Desire)} −−− \emph{Desires:} acclaim, volatility. \emph{Leverage:} charmed witnesses. \emph{Tell:} mirrors phrasing to draw agreement.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Scenes}
\paragraph{Redwater Audit.} Enter the dyeworks with forged or earned permits. ¶osition{} starts Dominant; DV 3−−4. On success with sb, allow entry but spend §B{} to tick \Clock{Poisonings}{+1} or call an Alchemist observer. \emph{Magic:} Alchemical detection, illusions to mimic stamps, or subtle glamours to steer crowds shine here.

\paragraph{Alleyway Serenade.} Sel ``rescues'' a bystander from a mob to win PC trust. Reading her tells can downshift DV. Failure−forward: the PCs earn a ◇{} (Escort Writ) but tick \Clock{Sel's Glamour Net}{+1}.

\paragraph{Three−Queens Duel.} A formal bridge duel settles a permit dispute. Stakes: purse, permit, or pride. Victory can clear 1 from a Guild Favor timer; loss still reveals a planted second's testimony.

\paragraph{Archivolt Ledgers.} Track falsified entries to a clerks' circle. Options: bribe (Diamonds), debate in public (drop DV by 1 with a crowd), or stealth (risk Desperate). On a miss, the ledgers you \emph{do} find implicate an ally−−−use as leverage later.

\subsection*{Climax Options}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Public Debate at Lion Bridge:} Expose Sel. If you sway the crowd, cool \Clock{City Unrest}{−2}. If you fail, you still win a legal foothold but \Clock{Glamour Net}{+2}.
ℹtem \textbf{Quiet Sabotage:} Collapse distribution of forged evidence; gain a permanent ◇{} (Matron's Audience) but sour one guild permanently.
ℹtem \textbf{Arrest & Bargain:} Trade Sel's testimony for guild peace; start Part II with social capital at Ravenswood.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Part II −−− The Gilded Cage (Tier II−−III)}

\subsection*{Structure}
Track \Clock{Midnight Duel}{8} (the rival's plot) and \Clock{Etiquette Breach}{4} (your faux pas). Every room lists an \emph{Intent}, a \emph{Taboo}, and a \emph{Tell}. Violating taboos increases DV or ticks \Clock{Etiquette Breach}{+1}. Reading tells can reduce DV by 1.

\subsection*{Rooms}
\paragraph{Conservatory of Masks.} \emph{Intent:} choose alliances. \emph{Taboo:} wearing the same mask twice. \emph{Tell:} a rival never mirrors your stance. \emph{Magic:} Disguises and glamours gain a clear advantage; clever illusions can ``prove'' etiquette creatively.

\paragraph{Library of Additions.} \emph{Intent:} alter contracts with lawful flourishes. \emph{Taboo:} citing a text you cannot produce. \emph{Tell:} a steward coughs before lies. \emph{Magic:} Divination or memory magic can surface the missing clause; ritual ink resists forgery unless properly countered.

\paragraph{Ballroom of Vows.} \emph{Intent:} demonstrate poise. \emph{Taboo:} cutting a trio dance to two. \emph{Tell:} a partner flinches at touch. \emph{Magic:} Subtle enchantments risk taboos; protective magic can prevent social harm without force.

\subsection*{Outcomes}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Favor the Host:} Gain introductions−−−begin Part III with a Bellmark.
ℹtem \textbf{Favor the Challenger (Glamour−touched):} Receive access to the Masque's rehearsal; start Act I behind the curtain.
ℹtem \textbf{Seize the Venue:} Control the guest list that matters later; start with ¶osition{} at Dominant for Act I scene one.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}
\textbf{Fail−Forward Examples (Part II).}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \emph{Mask Faux Pas:} \Clock{Etiquette Breach}{+1}, but you notice a hidden side−door used by rivals.
ℹtem \emph{Contract Gaffe:} Your addition is rejected publicly; the steward, impressed by your boldness, offers a private reading later (lower DV once).
ℹtem \emph{Dance Misstep:} You stumble, but hear a whispered cue that reveals the rival's schedule.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Part III −−− The Crimson Masque (Tier III)}

\subsection*{Act Structure}
Run as a three−day countdown to the final performance.
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Act I (Day −−3):} Infiltrate rehearsals, court patrons, secure stair access.
ℹtem \textbf{Act II (Day −−2/−−1):} Decode the rite; seed counters in law, masks, or music.
ℹtem \textbf{Act III (Performance Night):} On−stage skill challenge; illusions are real; dismantle ritual pillars.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Ritual Pillars (Break One to Stop the Rite)}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Bell Cadence:} The final peal synchronizes souls. Counter with bell−law, a forged bellmark, or a timed silence.
ℹtem \textbf{Mask Index:} Masks hold targeted identities. Swap labels, smudge index entries, or sanctify a mask to render it immune.
ℹtem \textbf{Applause Escrow:} The ovation stores consent. Split the crowd, mute applause, or redirect acclaim to the troupe alone.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{On−Stage Challenge (Guidance)}
Start at DV 6 (Desperate). Each broken pillar lowers DV by 1 and shifts ¶osition{} up. Failure−forward should always change the stage: a mask cracks, a bell peals off−time, an actor falters.

\subsection*{Endings}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Expose & Redirect:} Law halts the rite; only the willing swap. Reputation boons carry forward.
ℹtem \textbf{Strike in Glamour:} Duel within unfolding illusions while allies sever a pillar.
ℹtem \textbf{Take the Price:} Bargain with Glamour or Desire; end the rite, owe a favor.
\end{itemize}
