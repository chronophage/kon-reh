\section{Premise}

Ecktoria, heir to the shattered Utaran Empire, stands on the knife−edge of civil conflict. A charismatic pretender has seized the loyalty of the capital militia, silenced dissenting censores, and begun invoking ancient rites to claim the Mandate of Everflame. Meanwhile, the recognized heir −−− King Aldric the Lame −−− clings to legitimacy through law, tradition, and the blessing of the High Temple.

The players navigate a city where every marble colonnade hides a conspiracy, every mosaic conveys a political message, and every oath is engraved in red−lacquered tablets guarded by the Censors. They must decide: bolster one claimant, forge a third path, or engineer a peaceful transfer of power in a system designed to resist change violently.

\end{itemize}

\section{Adventure Structure}

\subsection{Act I −−− Cracks in Marble}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Players witness public demonstration of shifting power through one of the hook scenarios.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Investigate signs of Pretender's growing influence
ℹtem Determine if Duke Logan's claim has substance
ℹtem Identify key supporters and opponents of each faction
ℹtem Begin building relationships with influential NPCs
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Statue Alteration Investigation:} Notice and investigate changes to imperial statues. (Investigation + Notice vs DV 4, Controlled/Limited)
ℹtem \textbf{Militia Patrol Encounter:} Deal with new militia wearing unfamiliar colors. (Social/Combat encounter, DV 4)
ℹtem \textbf{Political Rumor Gathering:} Collect information from Forum conversations. (Sway + Insight vs DV 3, Dominant/Limited)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Act End:} Players discover evidence of systematic effort to legitimize Pretender's claim. Advance Legitimacy Timer +2.

\subsection{Act II −−− Shadows in the Censorium}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Grand Magistrate Valeria Thorne is attacked; players must respond.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Protect or avenge Grand Magistrate Thorne
ℹtem Navigate Censor's Hall to uncover truth about succession
ℹtem Find or protect the Pact of Three Flames
ℹtem Expose forged genealogies or hidden legitimate claimants
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Censor's Hall Infiltration:} Sneak through document labyrinth. (Stealth + Investigation vs DV 5, Desperate/Standard)
ℹtem \textbf{Genealogical Puzzle:} Verify authenticity of claimant lineages. (Lore + Insight vs DV 6, Controlled/Great)
ℹtem \textbf{Archivist Interrogation:} Extract information from knowledgeable but secretive clerks. (Sway + Insight vs DV 4, Controlled/Standard)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Act End:} Players gain crucial legal knowledge about succession. Advance either Lame King's or Pretender's Legal Standing Timer +2.

\subsection{Act III −−− Coin, Steel, and Fire}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Pretender's forces blockade the Basilica; crisis point reached.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Mediate between factions or choose sides
ℹtem Sabotage opposing faction's efforts
ℹtem Escalate conflict through strategic actions
ℹtem Prepare for final confrontation at coronation
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Coin−House Sabotage:} Disrupt financial support for one faction. (Tinker + Subterfuge vs DV 5, Desperate/Standard)
ℹtem \textbf{Mercantile Leverage:} Negotiate with New Merchants for support. (Sway + Command vs DV 4, Controlled/Great)
ℹtem \textbf{Basilica Siege:} Break through Pretender's blockade. (Combat/Strategy challenge, DV 6)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Act End:} Players position themselves for final act. Advance Coronation Readiness Timer +2.

\subsection{Act IV −−− The Coronation of Ash or Glory}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Dawn at the Basilica; final confrontation begins.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Decide which claimant to support or oppose
ℹtem Participate in coronation ritual and Trial of the Everflame
ℹtem Deal with consequences of previous choices
ℹtem Determine Ecktoria's future political structure
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Rhetorical Duel:} Participate in public debate before the people. (Presence + Sway vs DV 5, Desperate/Great)
ℹtem \textbf{Ritual Combat:} Engage in ceremonial combat under priestly supervision. (Melee + Spirit vs DV 5, Desperate/Great)
ℹtem \textbf{Miraculous Display:} Create or expose fake divine signs. (Arcana + Subterfuge vs DV 6, Controlled/Great)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Act End:} Resolution of succession crisis. All timers culminate in final outcome.

\section{GM Notes}

\subsection{Political Complexity}
Emphasize the Byzantine nature of Ecktorian politics:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Every action has multiple interpretations and consequences
ℹtem Legal precedents can be interpreted in conflicting ways
ℹtem Public opinion shifts based on ceremonial displays and rhetoric
ℹtem Economic power is as important as military force
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Ceremonial Importance}
Make ritual and ceremony central to the adventure:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Proper titles and protocols matter for social interactions
ℹtem Ceremonial objects and locations have real power
ℹtem Public performances can shift political momentum
ℹtem Religious rites determine ultimate legitimacy
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Bureaucratic Atmosphere}
Create a sense of the weight of imperial tradition:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Describe marble halls, gold decorations, and ceremonial regalia
ℹtem Emphasize the importance of proper documentation and seals
ℹtem Show the slow but powerful movement of imperial institutions
ℹtem Illustrate the contrast between public grandeur and hidden manipulation
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Player Agency}
Provide multiple paths to influence the outcome:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Legal approach: Master imperial law and precedent
ℹtem Social approach: Manipulate public opinion and personal relationships
ℹtem Military approach: Use force to support chosen faction
ℹtem Economic approach: Control financial resources and trade
ℹtem Religious approach: Influence spiritual legitimacy
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Story Beats}
Use the political environment for complications:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem 1 SB: Minor bureaucratic delay, small legal complication
ℹtem 2 SB: Important document goes missing, key NPC becomes suspicious
ℹtem 3 SB: Public scandal damages faction, military unit defects
ℹtem 4+ SB: Major political upheaval, assassination attempt, ceremonial disaster
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Session−Specific Guidance}

\textbf{Session 1 Focus:} Establish political landscape and hook players into the conflict. Players should understand both claimants have strengths and weaknesses.

\textbf{Session 2 Focus:} Deep dive into legal and archival aspects. Players learn that legitimacy is complex and can be influenced.

\textbf{Session 3 Focus:} Crisis point with Basilica blockade. Players must make strategic choices that affect final outcome.

\textbf{Session 4 Focus:} Climactic resolution and consequences. All previous elements converge in final choices about Ecktoria's future.

\subsection{Alternative Endings}

If players attempt unconventional solutions:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Popular Uprising:} Players incite general revolt against both claimants
ℹtem \textbf{Foreign Intervention:} Players invite external power to resolve succession
ℹtem \textbf{Religious Solution:} Players convince High Priest to reject both claimants
ℹtem \textbf{Economic Coup:} Players use financial power to force political resolution
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scaling for Different Tiers}

\textbf{Tier III (Veteran):} Standard complexity with significant political and military resources.

\textbf{Tier IV (Paragon):} Add foreign power complications, multiple simultaneous succession crises, and empire−wide implications.

\textbf{Tier V (Mythic):} Introduce divine intervention, reality−altering political magic, and consequences that reshape the entire region.
