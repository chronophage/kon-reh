\section*{Campaign Overview}
A three-part military campaign emphasizing tactical combat, war magic, and mercenary company politics. Players command a mercenary unit through escalating conflicts where every battle has strategic consequences.

\subsection*{The Three Crucibles}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Part I: The Granite Gate} (Tier II) - Fortress siege with positional warfare
\item \textbf{Part II: The River of Blades} (Tier III) - Mobile river campaign with naval combat  
\item \textbf{Part III: The Citadel of Ash} (Tier IV) - City-wide battle with magical WMDs
\end{itemize}

\section*{Core Combat Mechanics}

\subsection*{Warfare Position System}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l l X@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Position} & \textbf{Combat Bonus} & \textbf{Tactical State}\\
\midrule
Dominant & +1 dice, +1 Effect & Flanking, high ground, prepared ambush\\
Controlled & no bonus & Standard engagement, cover available\\
Desperate & -1 dice, +1 DV & Pinned, surrounded, disadvantage\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\subsection*{Squad Command Rules}
Players control their character + a squad of followers (Scale = Tier + 1):
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Squad Actions}: Move as group, attack as group, take hits as group
\item \textbf{Squad Fatigue}: Shared track equal to highest Body in squad
\item \textbf{Morale Checks}: When squad takes first casualty or suffers >50% losses
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Battlefield SB Spend Menu}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{1 SB}: Reinforcements arrive for enemy, terrain hazard appears
\item \textbf{2 SB}: Enemy war mage intervenes, supply line disrupted  
\item \textbf{3 SB}: Command betrayal, environmental catastrophe (fire, collapse)
\item \textbf{4+ SB}: Strategic reversal (allies retreat, objective compromised)
\end{itemize}

\section*{Part I: The Granite Gate (Tier II)}

\subsection*{Strategic Situation}
The mercenary company \textbf{Iron Phoenix} must breach the impregnable Granite Gate fortress. Three approaches available, each with different combat challenges.

\subsection*{Approach Clocks}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Main Gate Assault [6]} - Direct but heavily defended
\item \textbf{Cliffside Scalers [4]} - Stealth approach with climbing hazards  
\item \textbf{Underminers [4]} - Tunnel warfare with collapse risks
\item \textbf{Gatehouse Control [8]} - Overall objective clock
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Combat Set-Pieces}

\paragraph{The Killing Ground} 
Open courtyard with murder holes and boiling oil. \textbf{DV 4} to cross without casualties. War magic can create cover (\textbf{DV 3} to summon earthworks).

\paragraph{Arrow Storm Passage}
200-yard corridor under constant arrow fire. \textbf{DV 5} athletics check or take squad casualties. Shields provide +1 Position.

\paragraph{Gatehouse Final Assault}
Heavy door with rotating defender squads. \textbf{Tactical Clock [6]} - each success rotates defenders, each failure adds enemy reinforcements.

\subsection*{Special Combat Rules}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Siege Equipment}: Ballistae (+2 Effect vs structures), battering rams (auto-success vs gates with protection)
\item \textbf{Defender Advantages}: Murder holes (attack at Dominant), boiling oil (area denial)
\item \textbf{Assault Disadvantages}: Limited cover, constant pressure on supplies
\end{itemize}

\section*{Part II: The River of Blades (Tier III)}

\subsection*{Naval Combat Framework}
River warfare with unique positional challenges based on current, wind, and vessel type.

\subsection*{Vessel Types \& Bonuses}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l l X@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Vessel} & \textbf{Combat Bonus} & \textbf{Movement}\\
\midrule
War Galley & +2 dice boarding actions & Slow upstream, fast down\\
River Sloop & +1 dice ranged attacks & Maneuverable, current advantage\\
Fire Barge & Area denial weapon & Drifting hazard, one-time use\\
Troop Transport & Carry 3 squads & Vulnerable, requires escort\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\subsection*{River Battle Clocks}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Enemy Fleet Strength [6]} - Destroy or capture enemy vessels
\item \textbf{Bridgehead Establishment [4]} - Secure landing zone for assault
\item \textbf{Supply Line Security [4]} - Protect logistics from raiders
\item \textbf{Final Naval Battle [8]} - Decisive engagement
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Naval Combat Actions}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Ramming}: Risk vessel damage for automatic boarding advantage
\item \textbf{Boarding Actions}: Close combat between vessels (use squad rules)
\item \textbf{River Current}: Each round, vessels drift 1 position downstream
\item \textbf{Fire Ships}: Drifting weapons that create area denial zones
\end{itemize}

\section*{Part III: The Citadel of Ash (Tier IV)}

\subsection*{City-Wide Warfare}
Urban combat with magical WMDs, shifting front lines, and civilian complications.

\subsection*{District Control System}
Seven districts each with unique combat challenges and strategic value:

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l l X@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{District} & \textbf{Control Value} & \textbf{Combat Special Rules}\\
\midrule
Wizard's Quarter & 3 points & Magical hazards, teleportation risks\\
Market Square & 2 points & Ambush opportunities, civilian complicatons\\
Noble Heights & 2 points & Defensible positions, political stakes\\
Docks & 1 point & Naval support access, smuggling routes\\
Temple District & 1 point & Healing sanctuary, divine intervention\\
Slums & 1 point & Guerrilla warfare, mobility advantage\\
Citadel & 4 points & Final objective, heavy fortifications\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\subsection*{Magical WMD Rules}
Three city-scale weapons that can be captured and used:

\paragraph{The Sun-Thrower}
Siege laser that automatically destroys one district's defenses. \textbf{DV 6} to capture, requires magical specialists to operate.

\paragraph{Quake Engines}
Cause building collapses across multiple districts. Create area denial zones and positional hazards.

\paragraph{Plague Cauldrons}
Biological warfare that affects both sides. Control provides strategic leverage but moral complications.

\subsection*{Urban Combat Challenges}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Rubble Navigation}: Difficult terrain that slows movement
\item \textbf{Ambush Alleyways}: Risk of sudden enemy reinforcements  
\item \textbf{Civilian Complications}: Non-combatants create moral and tactical dilemmas
\item \textbf{Shifting Front Lines}: Control changes create new positional challenges
\end{itemize}

\section*{Session Planning Guide}

\subsection*{Sample Combat Session Structure}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Pre-Battle Planning (30 min)}: Intelligence, squad deployment, magic preparation
\item \textbf{Initial Engagement (45 min)}: Position establishment, first clashes
\item \textbf{Mid-Battle Crisis (60 min)}: SB spends, reinforcements, tactical dilemmas
\item \textbf{Climax Resolution (30 min)}: Objective completion, aftermath
\item \textbf{Between-Mission (15 min)}: Resource management, XP awards
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Combat Balance Guidelines}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Tier II}: 2-3 combat encounters per session, simple tactical options
\item \textbf{Tier III}: 3-4 encounters with complex positioning challenges  
\item \textbf{Tier IV}: 4-5 encounters with strategic resource management
\end{itemize}

\appendix \section*{Appendix A: Miniatures & Tactical Warfare Rules}

This appendix expands the Fate's Edge SRD miniatures rules for large-scale military engagements, integrating seamlessly with The Iron Crucible's warfare system.

\subsection*{A1. Grid System & Scale}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l X@{}} \toprule \textbf{Scale} & \textbf{Measurement & Purpose}\ \midrule \textbf{Tactical (1" = 5 feet)} & Individual character movement, room-scale combat\ \textbf{Operational (1" = 30 feet)} & Squad movements, building-scale engagements\ \textbf{Strategic (1" = 100 feet)} & Company maneuvers, district control warfare\ \bottomrule \end{tabularx}

\subsection*{A2. Unit Bases & Sizes}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l l X@{}} \toprule \textbf{Unit Type} & \textbf{Base Size} & \textbf{Composition & Rules}\ \midrule \textbf{Character} & 1" square & Single PC or named NPC\ \textbf{Squad} & 2"×3" rectangle & 5-10 troops (Scale = Tier + 1)\ \textbf{Platoon} & 3"×4" rectangle & 20-40 troops (Multiple squads)\ \textbf{Vehicles} & Varies by type & War galleys, siege engines, etc.\ \textbf{Structures} & Actual footprint & Gates, towers, defensive works\ \bottomrule \end{tabularx}

\subsection*{A3. Movement & Positioning}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l X X@{}} \toprule \textbf{Action} & \textbf{Tactical Scale} & \textbf{Operational Scale}\ \midrule \textbf{Move} & 6" (30 feet) & 4" (120 feet)\ \textbf{Dash} & 10" (50 feet) & 6" (180 feet)\ \textbf{Charge} & Move + Melee attack & Not applicable\ \textbf{Withdraw} & Half move backwards & Full move backwards\ \bottomrule \end{tabularx}

\subsection*{A4. Zones of Control (ZOC) Warfare}

\textbf{Squad ZOC Rules:} \begin{itemize} \item Infantry: 1" radius around base \item Cavalry: 2" radius around base
\item Archers: ZOC only in threatened squares \item Vehicles: ZOC extends from facing edge \end{itemize}

\textbf{ZOC Interactions:} \begin{itemize} \item Entering enemy ZOC ends movement \item Leaving ZOC requires Disengage action (DV 4) \item Multiple ZOC: +1 DV per additional unit threatening \item Flanking: Attack from outside target's facing arc gains +1 die \end{itemize}

\subsection*{A5. Warfare-Specific Actions}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l X X@{}} \toprule \textbf{Action} & \textbf{Cost} & \textbf{Effect}\ \midrule \textbf{Form Shield Wall} & Full round & +2 armor, cannot move, ZOC extends 1"\ \textbf{Set Spears} & Move action & Charge attacks against unit suffer +1 DV\ \textbf{Ranged Volley} & Standard action & Attack all units in 3" cone at -1 die\ \textbf{Tactical Withdrawal} & Standard action & Move full distance without disengage\ \textbf{Coordinate Attack} & Standard action & Allies gain +1 die vs designated target\ \bottomrule \end{tabularx}

\subsection*{A6. Cover & Terrain Effects}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l l X@{}} \toprule \textbf{Terrain} & \textbf{DV Modifier} & \textbf{Movement Cost}\ \midrule \textbf{Open Ground} & +0 & 1" per movement point\ \textbf{Light Cover} & +1 DV to hit & 1.5× movement\ \textbf{Heavy Cover} & +2 DV to hit & 2× movement\ \textbf{Difficult Terrain} & +0 & 3× movement\ \textbf{Impassable} & — & Cannot enter\ \bottomrule \end{tabularx}

\subsection*{A7. Squad Condition Tracking}

Use colored rings or tokens to mark squad conditions:

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l X X@{}} \toprule \textbf{Condition} & \textbf{Token Color} & \textbf{Mechanical Effect}\ \midrule \textbf{Fresh} & Green & No penalties\ \textbf{Bloodied} & Yellow & -1 die (below 50% strength)\ \textbf{Shaken} & Orange & Morale check required\ \textbf{Routed} & Red & Cannot attack, move away from enemy\ \textbf{Pinned} & Purple & Cannot move, -1 Position\ \bottomrule \end{tabularx}

\subsection*{A8. Warfare Miniatures Procedure}

\paragraph{1. Setup Phase} \begin{itemize} \item Deploy units according to scenario brief \item Mark starting positions and facing \item Place terrain and objective markers \item Determine initial Position for all units \end{itemize}

\paragraph{2. Initiative Sequence} \begin{itemize} \item Each side rolls Command + Wits \item Winner chooses to act first or second \item Alternate unit activations within side \end{itemize}

\paragraph{3. Activation Sequence} \begin{enumerate} \item \textbf{Start Phase}: Resolve ongoing effects \item \textbf{Movement}: Up to full move distance \item \textbf{Action}: Attack, special action, or second move \item \textbf{End Phase}: Check conditions, resolve reactions \end{enumerate}

\paragraph{4. Combat Resolution} \begin{itemize} \item Declare target and approach \item GM sets Position and DV based on tactical situation \item Roll appropriate skill + attribute \item Apply hits and conditions \item Spend SB for complications \end{itemize}

\subsection*{A9. Miniatures Warfare SB Spend Menu}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l X X@{}} \toprule \textbf{SB Cost} & \textbf{Tactical Effect} & \textbf{Strategic Effect}\ \midrule \textbf{1 SB} & Enemy gains cover advantage & Supply line disruption\ \textbf{2 SB} & Reinforcements arrive locally & Morale check for friendly units\ \textbf{3 SB} & Terrain changes (collapse/fire) & Command confusion (skip activation)\ \textbf{4+ SB} & Unit routing or betrayal & Major strategic reversal\ \bottomrule \end{tabularx}

\subsection*{A10. Siege Warfare Miniatures Rules}

\paragraph{Siege Equipment} \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{Battering Ram}: 4"×1" base, moves 2", automatic success vs gates \item \textbf{Siege Tower}: 3"×3" base, moves 3", provides Dominant position to occupants \item \textbf{Trebuchet}: Stationary, attacks at 24" range, +3 Effect vs structures \item \textbf{Mantlet}: 2"×4" mobile cover, provides heavy cover to infantry behind \end{itemize}

\paragraph{Defensive Structures} \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{Walls}: Provide heavy cover, require climbing or siege equipment \item \textbf{Gates}: Heavy armor, must be breached before entry \item \textbf{Murder Holes}: Attack into squares below at Dominant position \item \textbf{Barbican}: Complex gatehouse with multiple defensive layers \end{itemize}

\subsection*{A11. Naval Miniatures Rules}

\paragraph{Vessel Movement} \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{War Galley}: 6"×2" base, moves 4", rams at +2 Effect \item \textbf{River Sloop}: 4"×1" base, moves 6", highly maneuverable \item \textbf{Fire Ship}: 3"×1" base, moves with current, area denial weapon \end{itemize}

\paragraph{Naval Actions} \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{Ramming}: Move into enemy vessel, both take damage \item \textbf{Boarding}: Adjacent vessels can engage in melee \item \textbf{Grappling}: Special action to link vessels for boarding \item \textbf{Current}: All vessels drift 1" downstream each round \end{itemize}

\subsection*{A12. Urban Warfare Miniatures}

\paragraph{District Control Markers} Use colored tokens to track control of city districts: \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{Blue}: Friendly controlled \item \textbf{Red}: Enemy controlled
\item \textbf{Green}: Contested \item \textbf{Yellow}: Neutral/abandoned \end{itemize}

\paragraph{Urban Terrain Effects} \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{Buildings}: Provide heavy cover, may be entered \item \textbf{Rubble}: Difficult terrain, light cover \item \textbf{Alleyways}: Narrow movement, ambush risk \item \textbf{Sewers}: Underground movement routes \end{itemize}

\subsection*{A13. Miniatures Setup Examples}

\paragraph{Example 1: Granite Gate Assault} \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{Board}: 3'×3' area with gatehouse center \item \textbf{Defenders}: 3 squads on walls, 1 at gate \item \textbf{Attackers}: Player squads + battering ram \item \textbf{Terrain}: Killing ground (open), wall (impassable), gate (heavy armor) \end{itemize}

\paragraph{Example 3: Citadel District Control} \begin{itemize} \item \textbf{Board}: 4'×4' city map with 7 districts \item \textbf{Control}: Track with colored tokens \item \textbf{WMD}: Sun-Thrower marker in Wizard's Quarter \item \textbf{Victory}: Control 4+ districts including Citadel \end{itemize}
