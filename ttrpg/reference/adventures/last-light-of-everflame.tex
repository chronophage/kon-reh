\section{Logline}
As the sacred flame that has burned in Ecktoria for centuries begins to die, the ancient rivalry between the Lampers and the Temple of Light erupts into open conflict, forcing the player characters to choose sides in a battle that will determine the future of faith itself.

\section{Core Theme}
The conflict between tradition and innovation − when a sacred truth begins to fail, is it better to die with the old ways or risk everything to create something new?

\section{The Rivalry: Lampers vs. Temple of Light}

\begin{description}
    ℹtem[Lampers (Traditionalists)] Guardians of the flame, who believe the flame must be protected at all costs
    
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Philosophy}: The flame is a gift from the gods that must be preserved exactly as it is. To change it is to profane it.
        ℹtem \textbf{Methods}: Rituals passed down for centuries, strict adherence to tradition, protective wards
        ℹtem \textbf{Leaders}: High Lamper Aris Thorne (a stern traditionalist) and the Circle of Elders
        ℹtem \textbf{Grievance}: The Temple of Light seeks to ``improve'' the flame, which they see as a heresy
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[Temple of Light (Reformers)] Believers that the flame's decline is a sign that it must evolve or be replaced
    
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Philosophy}: The flame is not static but should evolve with the times. Its decline is a call for renewal.
        ℹtem \textbf{Methods}: New rituals, experimental techniques, attempts to ``renew'' the flame through science and faith
        ℹtem \textbf{Leaders}: High Luminary Seraphina Vale (charismatic reformer) and the Council of Illumination
        ℹtem \textbf{Grievance}: The Lampers are allowing the flame to die rather than embracing necessary change
    \end{itemize}
\end{description}

\subsection{The History of the Rivalry}
For 200 years, these two institutions have been locked in a cold war over the flame:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{The Schism of 1278}: The Temple split from the Lampers after a theological dispute
    ℹtem \textbf{The War of Light (1322)}: A brief but violent conflict that was eventually contained
    ℹtem \textbf{The Current Tension}: As the flame falters, both sides see this as proof their approach is correct
\end{itemize}

\section{Core Mechanics}

\begin{description}
    ℹtem[Flame Timer (7 segments)] The strength of the sacred flame
    
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Lamper Action}: Traditional rituals that stabilize the flame but slow its decline
        ℹtem \textbf{Temple Action}: Experimental techniques that may restore the flame but risk disaster
        ℹtem \textbf{Third Path Action}: Unique solutions that could create a new path but require trust
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[Faith Timer (6 segments)] The city's faith in the flame
    
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Lamper Influence}: +1 segment per successful traditional ritual
        ℹtem \textbf{Temple Influence}: +1 segment per successful experimental ritual
        ℹtem \textbf{Third Path Influence}: +1 segment when they mediate successfully
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[Rivalry Timer (6 segments)] Tension between the factions
    
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Lamper Action}: +1 segment when they deny Temple requests
        ℹtem \textbf{Temple Action}: +1 segment when they attempt to seize control
        ℹtem \textbf{Third Path Action}: −1 segment when they mediate successfully
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[Obligation Timers] Personal ties to the conflict
    
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Lamper Obligation}: Ties to tradition, oaths to the Circle
        ℹtem \textbf{Temple Obligation}: Ties to innovation, promises to the Council
        ℹtem \textbf{Third Path Obligation}: Ties to both, creating a precarious balance
    \end{itemize}
\end{description}

\section{Sample Scenes}

\begin{description}
    ℹtem[The Divided Court]
    The high officials of both factions are holding competing ceremonies as the flame flickers. The PCs must:
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Prevent the ritual from becoming violent
        ℹtem Determine who has the right to lead the ceremony
        ℹtem Deal with the fact that both rituals are accelerating the flame's decline
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[The Archives Under Siege]
    The Temple researchers have gained access to the archives, and the Lampers are trying to stop them. The PCs must:
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Navigate between the two groups without taking sides
        ℹtem Discover if the archives hold the key to saving the flame
        ℹtem Prevent the destruction of irreplaceable knowledge
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[The Shadowed District]
    The Unlit have discovered something about the flame's true nature. The PCs must:
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Gain the trust of those who have abandoned faith
        ℹtem Determine if their knowledge is real or dangerous delusion
        ℹtem Decide whether to share this knowledge with either faction
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[The Bridge−Bearers' Dilemma]
    The Third Path is in danger as both sides see them as traitors. The PCs must:
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Protect the bridge−builders from attack
        ℹtem Negotiate with both factions to buy time
        ℹtem Decide if the Third Path is the solution or just another path to destruction
    \end{itemize}
\end{description}

\section{Consequences}

\begin{description}
    ℹtem[Success (Flame Restored)]
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem The flame is restored, but the city is divided between those who support the Lampers and those who support the Temple
        ℹtem The Third Path becomes a recognized institution, but their role is limited
        ℹtem The city's faith is stronger but now fractured between old and new interpretations
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[Failure (Flame Extinguished)]
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem The city falls into chaos as both institutions collapse
        ℹtem The Temple of Light blames the Lampers for the flame's death
        ℹtem The Lampers blame the Temple for ``corrupting'' the flame
        ℹtem A dark age begins as faith in any institution fades
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[Compromise (New Light)]
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem A new understanding of the flame emerges, but it requires both traditions to adapt
        ℹtem The Third Path becomes the new religious authority, but faces constant challenges
        ℹtem The city moves forward, but the wounds of the conflict will take generations to heal
    \end{itemize}
    
    ℹtem[Radical Choice (The Third Path Wins)]
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem The Third Path is proven right, and both factions are forced to accept a new path
        ℹtem The High Lamper and High Luminary are both deposed
        ℹtem A new era begins where faith and reason coexist, but the old ways are gone forever
    \end{itemize}
\end{description}
