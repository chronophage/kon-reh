\section{Premise}

A merchant caravan has been cursed while crossing the Pale Causeway, causing supplies to rot, animals to die, and strange omens to appear. The players must escort the caravan to safety while dealing with the curse's escalating effects. However, they soon discover that the curse is tied to a larger conspiracy involving a powerful entity that feeds on suffering and despair, and that the caravan's troubles may be just the beginning of something far worse.

\section{Hook}

The adventure begins when the players encounter one of the following scenarios (Investigation + Notice vs DV 3 to determine truth):
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Desperate Merchant:} They find the caravan stalled on the road, with Merchant Aldric Thorne desperately seeking help for his dying animals and rotting supplies. He offers them a substantial reward to help get the caravan to the nearest settlement. (Social encounter, Presence + Sway vs DV 3)
ℹtem \textbf{False Accusation:} A Margravine's bounty poster shows the players' faces with the accusation that they're responsible for the curse, forcing them to investigate to clear their names. (Investigation + Subterfuge vs DV 4)
ℹtem \textbf{Witch's Warning:} Hedge−Witch Mira Blackwood approaches them in a tavern, warning that something dark is moving along the trade routes and that they may be the only ones who can stop it. (Social encounter, Presence + Insight vs DV 3)
\end{itemize}

\section{Plot Structure}

\subsection{Session 1: The Cursed Caravan}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Players encounter one of the hook scenarios. Establish the immediate supernatural threat and desperate situation.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Experience immediate effects of the curse firsthand
ℹtem Meet key caravan members and understand their motivations
ℹtem Begin suspecting supernatural involvement
ℹtem Make initial decisions about approach and commitment
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Dying Animals:} Witness caravan horses succumbing to curse. (Notice + Medicine vs DV 4, Desperate/Limited)
ℹtem \textbf{Rotting Supplies:} Deal with spoiled cargo threatening caravan's viability. (Survival + Craft vs DV 3, Controlled/Standard)
ℹtem \textbf{Supernatural Omen:} Experience first clear supernatural manifestation. (Spirit + Notice vs DV 4, Desperate/Standard)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Session End:} Players committed to helping caravan. Advance Curse Escalation Timer +2, Caravan Supplies −1.

\subsection{Session 2: Into the Blackwood}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Players seek help from Hedge−Witch Mira. Supernatural elements become clearer.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Learn about local folklore and supernatural protections
ℹtem Discover curse's connection to sealed chest
ℹtem Face growing influence of Hungering Dark
ℹtem Make moral choices about dealing with curse
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Hedge−Witch Consultation:} Gain supernatural knowledge and protection advice. (Presence + Lore vs DV 4, Controlled/Standard)
ℹtem \textbf{Chest Investigation:} Examine mysterious sealed cargo item. (Investigation + Arcana vs DV 5, Desperate/Standard)
ℹtem \textbf{Protective Ritual:} Perform local charm to resist curse effects. (Craft + Arcana vs DV 4, Controlled/Great)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Session End:} Players understand curse's supernatural nature. Advance Hungering Dark's Strength +1, gain Folk Protection Tokens.

\subsection{Session 3: The Margravine's Justice}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Political/legal complications arise as curse affects wider community.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Navigate Margravine's court and harsh justice system
ℹtem Deal with curse's effects on local community
ℹtem Confront entity behind curse directly
ℹtem Make crucial decisions about curse's ultimate fate
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Court Proceedings:} Present supernatural evidence to skeptical authorities. (Command + Lore vs DV 5, Controlled/Great)
ℹtem \textbf{Community Crisis:} Deal with curse spreading to local settlement. (Leadership challenge, DV 5)
ℹtem \textbf{Direct Confrontation:} Face Hungering Dark's influence at cursed crossroads. (Extended combat/ritual, DV 6)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Session End:} Major confrontation with entity. Advance all timers +1, determine path to resolution.

\subsection{Session 4: The Pale Child}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Curse reaches climax as Cursed Child appears.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Face ultimate consequences of previous choices
ℹtem Deal with Cursed Child's terrible bargain
ℹtem Resolve immediate threat to caravan
ℹtem Determine long−term effects of actions
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Terrible Bargain:} Negotiate with Cursed Child for resolution. (Sway + Spirit vs DV 6, Desperate/Great)
ℹtem \textbf{Final Ritual:} Perform complex containment or destruction ritual. (Extended Arcana/Craft challenge, DV 6)
ℹtem \textbf{Moral Choice:} Choose between different resolution paths with consequences. (Command + Insight vs DV 5)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Session End:} Resolution of curse crisis. Final timer states determine ending.

\section{GM Notes}

\subsection{Atmosphere and Tone}
Emphasize the bleak, desperate atmosphere of Acasia:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Use descriptions of withered crops, gray skies, and desperate people
ℹtem Make supernatural elements feel genuinely threatening and alien
ℹtem Create sense of isolation and hopelessness that makes supernatural solutions tempting
ℹtem Balance horror with folk wisdom and community resilience
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Folk Horror Elements}
Use local customs and superstitions authentically:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Specific folk practices should have mechanical effects
ℹtem Local knowledge should be valuable and hard to find elsewhere
ℹtem Supernatural threats should be tied to regional folklore
ℹtem Community responses should reflect genuine cultural practices
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Moral Ambiguity}
Present difficult choices without clear right answers:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Each resolution path should have significant costs and benefits
ℹtem NPCs should have complex motivations rather than simple good/evil
ℹtem Supernatural forces should be neither entirely benevolent nor malevolent
ℹtem Player choices should have lasting consequences that affect character development
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Escalating Tension}
Gradually increase supernatural threat level:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Session 1: Minor disturbances and growing unease
ℹtem Session 2: Clear supernatural manifestations and local knowledge
ℹtem Session 3: Direct confrontation with entity and community crisis
ℹtem Session 4: Climactic encounter with legendary figure and final choice
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Player Agency}
Provide multiple paths to resolution:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Investigation approach: Master curse mechanics and find proper solution
ℹtem Social approach: Negotiate with factions and supernatural entities
ℹtem Direct action approach: Confront threats through force or ritual
ℹtem Moral approach: Make difficult choices that affect curse's ultimate fate
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Story Beats}
Use curse's influence to generate complications:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem 1 SB: Minor supernatural manifestation, small equipment failure
ℹtem 2 SB: NPC becomes temporarily possessed, important item goes missing
ℹtem 3 SB: Major curse escalation, community member suffers serious harm
ℹtem 4+ SB: Entity direct intervention, climactic supernatural event
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Session−Specific Guidance}

\textbf{Session 1 Focus:} Establish supernatural threat and desperate situation. Players should feel immediate need to help while sensing something deeper is wrong.

\textbf{Session 2 Focus:} Deepen supernatural elements and introduce local knowledge. Players learn curse has ancient, complex origins requiring specialized solutions.

\textbf{Session 3 Focus:} Escalate to community−wide crisis and direct supernatural confrontation. Players must make difficult choices under pressure.

\textbf{Session 4 Focus:} Climactic resolution with moral complexity. All previous elements converge in final choices with lasting consequences.

\subsection{Alternative Endings}

If players attempt unconventional solutions:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Community Solution:} Rally local population to perform massive protective ritual
ℹtem \textbf{Entity Alliance:} Negotiate with Hungering Dark to become its agent
ℹtem \textbf{Folk Hero Path:} Become legendary figures who regularly deal with supernatural threats
ℹtem \textbf{Cursed Path:} Become cursed themselves but gain supernatural powers
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scaling for Different Tiers}

\textbf{Tier II (Seasoned):} Standard supernatural threats, manageable curse effects, local community scope.

\textbf{Tier III (Veteran):} More powerful entities, curse spreading to multiple communities, regional political implications.

\textbf{Tier IV (Paragon):} Ancient conspiracy involving multiple cursed objects, entity gains patron backing, threat to entire region.
