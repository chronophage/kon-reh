\section{Core Premise}

\subsection{What is happening?}
A traveling carnival that grants wishes is stealing memories and identities from visitors, leaving hollow shells behind.

\subsection{Why now?}
The carnival arrives in town tonight for a three−day festival, and its malevolent attraction grows stronger with each victim−−−it plans to consume the entire town's collective memories by the final night.

\subsection{Why the PCs?}
Each PC received a mysterious invitation to the carnival, and one of them has been having prophetic dreams about it. Local authorities are mysteriously absent or unresponsive.

\section{Setting & Tone}

\subsection{Location: Millhaven}
A riverside town during its annual Harvest Festival:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Smell:} Cinnamon and roasting chestnuts mixed with something metallic
ℹtem \textbf{Sound:} Calliope music that sounds slightly off−key, distant carnival barker's voice
ℹtem \textbf{Architecture:} Victorian−era buildings with paper lanterns, black−and−gold tents on outskirts
ℹtem \textbf{Culture:} Close−knit farming community with superstitions about "traveling folk"
ℹtem \textbf{Sight:} Strange shadows that don't match their owners in evening light
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Tone and Theme}
Psychological horror with elements of wonder and melancholy. Themes of identity, memory, and the price of desire.

\subsection{Environmental Pressures}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem The carnival only operates at night
ℹtem Town curfew enforced by mysterious carnival "security"
ℹtem Increasing difficulty distinguishing real memories from false ones
ℹtem Growing paranoia as townspeople become "hollowed"
\end{itemize}

\section{Villain's Plan}

\subsection{Ultimate Goal}
To collect 1,000 souls' worth of memories to ascend to godhood, becoming the eternal keeper of forgotten dreams.

\subsection{Why Not Easily Stopped}
The carnival exists partially outside normal reality and feeds on the desires that draw people to it. Destroying it requires breaking the collective belief in its magic.

\subsection{Plan Phases}

\textbf{Setup (Night 1):} Carnival arrives, begins subtle memory collection through "free" wishes. Victims seem happier but lose small memories.
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Advance Hollowing Timer +1
ℹtem Gain 2−3 Memory Fragments
ℹtem Establish carnival presence in town
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Escalation (Night 2):} More aggressive collection, "hollowed" townspeople begin to act strangely. The carnival expands, offering more tempting wishes.
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Advance Hollowing Timer +2
ℹtem Hollow 3−5 key townspeople
ℹtem Introduce Midpoint Twist (PC memory compromise)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Crisis (Night 3):} Final ritual to consume all remaining memories in town. Hollowed townsfolk become actively hostile to interference.
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Advance Hollowing Timer to Full
ℹtem Final confrontation with Carnival Master
ℹtem Resolution choice determines ending
\end{itemize}

\section{Player Choices}

\subsection{First Three Meaningful Choices}
\begin{enumerate}
ℹtem Investigate the carnival's arrival and strange effects (Investigation + Notice vs DV 3)
ℹtem Confront a hollowed friend/family member for information (Social challenge, DV 4)
ℹtem Accept a tempting wish from the carnival to gain an advantage (Command + Spirit vs DV 4)
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Secondary Choices}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Rescue specific townspeople before they're fully hollowed (Extended challenge, DV 5)
ℹtem Choose which memories to sacrifice to gain carnival knowledge (Lore + Insight vs DV 4)
ℹtem Decide whether to destroy the carnival or try to reform it (Moral choice with consequences)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Choice Consequences}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Accepting wishes makes PCs more vulnerable to memory manipulation (+1 SB on memory−related rolls)
ℹtem Rescuing townspeople gains allies but alerts the carnival (Advance Hollowing Timer +1)
ℹtem Destroying without understanding may leave fragments (New adventure seed)
\end{itemize}

\section{Core Mystery}

\subsection{Central Unknown}
How the carnival steals memories and what happens to them, plus the Carnival Master's true identity and weakness.

\subsection{Investigation Clues}
\textbf{Three Clue Rule − The Carnival's True Nature:}
\begin{enumerate}
ℹtem \textbf{Investigation:} Research reveals pattern of memory−theft across different towns (Lore + Investigation vs DV 5)
ℹtem \textbf{Social:} Partially hollowed NPC reveals carnival feeds on forgotten dreams (Sway + Insight vs DV 4)
ℹtem \textbf{Occult:} Magical investigation shows partial existence in dream realm (Arcana vs DV 5)
\end{enumerate}

\section{Key Mechanics}

\subsection{Hollowing Timer [6]}
\begin{mechanics}
ℹtem[Advance +1:] Carnival operates normally, minor memory theft
ℹtem[Advance +1:] Townspeople show first signs of hollowing
ℹtem[Advance +2:] Hollowed townsfolk become active threats
ℹtem[Advance +1:] PCs accept wishes or fail to stop memory theft
ℹtem[Advance +1:] Carnival Master grows stronger
ℹtem[When Full:] Town's collective memories consumed, carnival ascends
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Memory Fragments Track}
\begin{mechanics}
ℹtem[Collection:] PCs can collect stolen memories as assets
ℹtem[Benefits:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Gain insights into carnival operations
    ℹtem Temporary skill boosts related to memory content
    ℹtem Leverage against hollowed townspeople
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Costs:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Emotional baggage (−1 die to related social rolls)
    ℹtem Identity confusion (difficulty distinguishing real memories)
    ℹtem Vulnerability to memory manipulation
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Maximum:] 5 fragments per PC (reduce to 2 at scene end)
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Wish Corruption}
\begin{mechanics}
ℹtem[Gains:] Each wish grants power but creates weakness
ℹtem[Effects:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem +1 die to one skill for scene
    ℹtem Gain specific supernatural ability temporarily
    ℹtem Improved Position for related actions
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Corruption:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Corresponding vulnerability or moral compromise
    ℹtem Mark 1 segment on Corruption Timer 
    ℹtem Increased susceptibility to carnival influence
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Limit:] Maximum 3 wishes per PC per adventure
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Dream Echoes}
\begin{mechanics}
ℹtem[Manifestation:] PCs experience dreams of other victims' memories
ℹtem[Benefits:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Gain insight into carnival's methods
    ℹtem Forewarning of dangers
    ℹtem Understanding of victims' motivations
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Risks:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Risk to own identity (confusion between real and borrowed memories)
    ℹtem Emotional trauma from victims' experiences
    ℹtem Vulnerability to carnival influence during dreams
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Control:] Requires Spirit + Resolve vs DV 4 to maintain self during echoes
\end{mechanics}

\section{Midpoint Twist}

\subsection{The Revelation}
Halfway through Night 2, PCs discover that one of their own has been making wishes at the carnival, and their memories are already compromised.

\subsection{Immediate Consequences}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Compromised PC gains Wish Corruption segment
ℹtem Other PCs must decide whether to trust this PC
ℹtem Carnival now has inside knowledge of party dynamics
ℹtem Hollowing Timer advances +1 due to betrayal
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Resolution Options}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Confront and potentially exclude compromised PC
ℹtem Work together to overcome the compromise
ℹtem Use compromise as tactical advantage against carnival
ℹtem Allow PC to make redemption choice
\end{itemize}

\section{Final Confrontation}

\subsection{Set−Piece: Dream Consumption Ritual}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Location: Central carnival tent with swirling vortex of stolen memories
ℹtem Effect: Carnival Master grows more powerful as townspeople drawn in
ℹtem Challenge: Multiple simultaneous threats (Master, hollowed townsfolk, environmental hazards)
ℹtem Time Pressure: Hollowing Timer fills during encounter
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Defining Choice}
To destroy the carnival completely (saving town but losing power) or make one final wish to become carnival's new masters (gaining power but unknown cost to humanity).

\subsection{Resolution Paths}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Destruction:} Complex ritual requiring sacrifice, memory restoration, breaking collective belief
ℹtem \textbf{Mastery:} Temptation requiring moral compromise, potential for future corruption
ℹtem \textbf{Reformation:} Transform carnival into force for good, ongoing responsibility
ℹtem \textbf{Escape:} Save some townspeople but leave threat unresolved
\end{itemize}

\section{GM Notes}

\subsection{Atmosphere Guidance}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Emphasize sensory details: off−key music, metallic smells, wrong shadows
ℹtem Create cognitive dissonance: beautiful carnival masking horror
ℹtem Use lighting effects: increasing darkness, flickering lanterns
ℹtem Make time feel distorted: nights that seem too long
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Memory Mechanics}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Players may forget real details about their characters
ℹtem Distinguish between real and false memories becomes challenging
ℹtem Use confusion to create tension without frustrating players
ℹtem Provide clear mechanical indicators of memory compromise
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Horror Elements}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Psychological horror over gore
ℹtem Loss of identity more frightening than death
ℹtem Familiar becoming threatening
ℹtem Moral ambiguity in choices
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scaling for Tiers}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Tier I:} Reduce entity Caps, simplify ritual requirements, provide more NPC assistance
ℹtem \textbf{Tier II:} Standard challenges, full complexity, moderate supernatural threats
ℹtem \textbf{Tier III+:} Add political complications, increase entity power, complicate resolution
\end{itemize}
