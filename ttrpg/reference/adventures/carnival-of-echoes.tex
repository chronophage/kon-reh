\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}lX@{}}

\textbf{Length:} & 3-4 Sessions \\
\textbf{Theme:} & Psychological horror, identity, memory, the price of desire \\
\end{tabularx}

\section{Core Premise}

\subsection{What is happening?}
A traveling carnival that grants wishes is stealing memories and identities from visitors, leaving hollow shells behind.

\subsection{Why now?}
The carnival arrives in town tonight for a three-day festival, and its malevolent attraction grows stronger with each victimâ€”it plans to consume the entire town's collective memories by the final night.

\subsection{Why the PCs?}
Each PC received a mysterious invitation to the carnival, and one of them has been having prophetic dreams about it. Local authorities are mysteriously absent or unresponsive.

\section{Setting \& Tone}

\subsection{Location: Millhaven}
A riverside town during its annual Harvest Festival:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Smell:} Cinnamon and roasting chestnuts mixed with something metallic
\item \textbf{Sound:} Calliope music that sounds slightly off-key, distant carnival barker's voice
\item \textbf{Architecture:} Victorian-era buildings with paper lanterns, black-and-gold tents on outskirts
\item \textbf{Culture:} Close-knit farming community with superstitions about "traveling folk"
\item \textbf{Sight:} Strange shadows that don't match their owners in evening light
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Tone and Theme}
Psychological horror with elements of wonder and melancholy. Themes of identity, memory, and the price of desire.

\subsection{Environmental Pressures}
\begin{itemize}
\item The carnival only operates at night
\item Town curfew enforced by mysterious carnival "security"
\item Increasing difficulty distinguishing real memories from false ones
\item Growing paranoia as townspeople become "hollowed"
\end{itemize}

\section{Villain's Plan}

\subsection{Ultimate Goal}
To collect 1,000 souls' worth of memories to ascend to godhood, becoming the eternal keeper of forgotten dreams.

\subsection{Why Not Easily Stopped}
The carnival exists partially outside normal reality and feeds on the desires that draw people to it. Destroying it requires breaking the collective belief in its magic.

\subsection{Plan Phases}

\textbf{Setup (Night 1):} Carnival arrives, begins subtle memory collection through "free" wishes. Victims seem happier but lose small memories.
\begin{itemize}
\item Advance Hollowing Clock +1
\item Gain 2-3 Memory Fragments
\item Establish carnival presence in town
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Escalation (Night 2):} More aggressive collection, "hollowed" townspeople begin to act strangely. The carnival expands, offering more tempting wishes.
\begin{itemize}
\item Advance Hollowing Clock +2
\item Hollow 3-5 key townspeople
\item Introduce Midpoint Twist (PC memory compromise)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Crisis (Night 3):} Final ritual to consume all remaining memories in town. Hollowed townsfolk become actively hostile to interference.
\begin{itemize}
\item Advance Hollowing Clock to Full
\item Final confrontation with Carnival Master
\item Resolution choice determines ending
\end{itemize}

\section{Player Choices}

\subsection{First Three Meaningful Choices}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Investigate the carnival's arrival and strange effects (Investigation + Notice vs DV 3)
\item Confront a hollowed friend/family member for information (Social challenge, DV 4)
\item Accept a tempting wish from the carnival to gain an advantage (Command + Spirit vs DV 4)
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Secondary Choices}
\begin{itemize}
\item Rescue specific townspeople before they're fully hollowed (Extended challenge, DV 5)
\item Choose which memories to sacrifice to gain carnival knowledge (Lore + Insight vs DV 4)
\item Decide whether to destroy the carnival or try to reform it (Moral choice with consequences)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Choice Consequences}
\begin{itemize}
\item Accepting wishes makes PCs more vulnerable to memory manipulation (+1 SB on memory-related rolls)
\item Rescuing townspeople gains allies but alerts the carnival (Advance Hollowing Clock +1)
\item Destroying without understanding may leave fragments (New adventure seed)
\end{itemize}

\section{Core Mystery}

\subsection{Central Unknown}
How the carnival steals memories and what happens to them, plus the Carnival Master's true identity and weakness.

\subsection{Investigation Clues}
\textbf{Three Clue Rule - The Carnival's True Nature:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Investigation:} Research reveals pattern of memory-theft across different towns (Lore + Investigation vs DV 5)
\item \textbf{Social:} Partially hollowed NPC reveals carnival feeds on forgotten dreams (Sway + Insight vs DV 4)
\item \textbf{Occult:} Magical investigation shows partial existence in dream realm (Arcana vs DV 5)
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Social Negotiation}

\section{Key Mechanics}

\subsection{Hollowing Clock [6]}
\begin{mechanics}
\item[Advance +1:] Carnival operates normally, minor memory theft
\item[Advance +1:] Townspeople show first signs of hollowing
\item[Advance +2:] Hollowed townsfolk become active threats
\item[Advance +1:] PCs accept wishes or fail to stop memory theft
\item[Advance +1:] Carnival Master grows stronger
\item[When Full:] Town's collective memories consumed, carnival ascends
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Memory Fragments Track}
\begin{mechanics}
\item[Collection:] PCs can collect stolen memories as assets
\item[Benefits:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Gain insights into carnival operations
    \item Temporary skill boosts related to memory content
    \item Leverage against hollowed townspeople
  \end{itemize}
\item[Costs:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Emotional baggage (-1 die to related social rolls)
    \item Identity confusion (difficulty distinguishing real memories)
    \item Vulnerability to memory manipulation
  \end{itemize}
\item[Maximum:] 5 fragments per PC (reduce to 2 at scene end)
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Wish Corruption}
\begin{mechanics}
\item[Gains:] Each wish grants power but creates weakness
\item[Effects:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    \item +1 die to one skill for scene
    \item Gain specific supernatural ability temporarily
    \item Improved Position for related actions
  \end{itemize}
\item[Corruption:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Corresponding vulnerability or moral compromise
    \item Mark 1 segment on Corruption Clock
    \item Increased susceptibility to carnival influence
  \end{itemize}
\item[Limit:] Maximum 3 wishes per PC per adventure
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Dream Echoes}
\begin{mechanics}
\item[Manifestation:] PCs experience dreams of other victims' memories
\item[Benefits:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Gain insight into carnival's methods
    \item Forewarning of dangers
    \item Understanding of victims' motivations
  \end{itemize}
\item[Risks:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Risk to own identity (confusion between real and borrowed memories)
    \item Emotional trauma from victims' experiences
    \item Vulnerability to carnival influence during dreams
  \end{itemize}
\item[Control:] Requires Spirit + Resolve vs DV 4 to maintain self during echoes
\end{mechanics}

\section{Stakes and Consequences}

\subsection{Success Outcomes}
\begin{itemize}
\item Carnival destroyed or reformed
\item Memories restored to willing victims
\item Town left with trauma but intact
\item PCs gain reputation as protectors
\item Potential allies in Granny Moira and Mayor Vasquez
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Failure Outcomes}
\begin{itemize}
\item Carnival consumes town's memories
\item Everyone becomes hollow shells
\item Carnival moves on with increased power
\item PCs lose hometown connections
\item New recurring supernatural threat
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Walk Away Outcomes}
\begin{itemize}
\item Carnival succeeds in Millhaven
\item Grows stronger for future encounters
\item PCs' hometown connections lost
\item Moral weight of abandonment
\item Adventure becomes ongoing campaign thread
\end{itemize}

\section{Midpoint Twist}

\subsection{The Revelation}
Halfway through Night 2, PCs discover that one of their own has been making wishes at the carnival, and their memories are already compromised.

\subsection{Immediate Consequences}
\begin{itemize}
\item Compromised PC gains Wish Corruption segment
\item Other PCs must decide whether to trust this PC
\item Carnival now has inside knowledge of party dynamics
\item Hollowing Clock advances +1 due to betrayal
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Resolution Options}
\begin{itemize}
\item Confront and potentially exclude compromised PC
\item Work together to overcome the compromise
\item Use compromise as tactical advantage against carnival
\item Allow PC to make redemption choice
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Stealth/Skill PC}
\begin{itemize}

\section{Final Confrontation}

\subsection{Set-Piece: Dream Consumption Ritual}
\begin{itemize}
\item Location: Central carnival tent with swirling vortex of stolen memories
\item Effect: Carnival Master grows more powerful as townspeople drawn in
\item Challenge: Multiple simultaneous threats (Master, hollowed townsfolk, environmental hazards)
\item Time Pressure: Hollowing Clock fills during encounter
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Defining Choice}
To destroy the carnival completely (saving town but losing power) or make one final wish to become carnival's new masters (gaining power but unknown cost to humanity).

\subsection{Resolution Paths}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Destruction:} Complex ritual requiring sacrifice, memory restoration, breaking collective belief
\item \textbf{Mastery:} Temptation requiring moral compromise, potential for future corruption
\item \textbf{Reformation:} Transform carnival into force for good, ongoing responsibility
\item \textbf{Escape:} Save some townspeople but leave threat unresolved
\end{itemize}

\section{Aftermath and Ripple Effects}

\subsection{Character Changes}
\begin{itemize}
\item PCs gain or lose memories based on choices
\item Hollowed townspeople slowly recover (or don't, becoming NPCs)
\item Granny Moira reveals more of her true nature and age
\item Mayor Vasquez deals with political fallout
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Alliance Shifts}
\begin{itemize}
\item Strong bond with Granny Moira if she survives
\item Tension with townspeople who made regretted wishes
\item Potential rivalry with other monster hunters arriving late
\item New understanding of supernatural threats
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Adventure Seeds}
\begin{itemize}
\item Other carnival fragments survived elsewhere
\item Granny Moira's past with similar threats
\item Carnival Master's origins in realm of forgotten dreams
\item PCs' compromised memories resurface later
\item New carnival appears in different location
\end{itemize}

\item Advance Hollowing Clock +2
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Session 3: Crisis Point (Night 3)}
\begin{itemize}
\item Final preparations for carnival's climax
\item Rescue remaining townspeople
\item Confront lieutenants and disable key machinery
\item Advance Hollowing Clock toward full
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Session 4: Final Confrontation}
\begin{itemize}
\item Dream Consumption Ritual in central tent
\item Defining choice about carnival's fate
\item Resolution of all major plot threads
\item Determine long-term consequences
\item Set up future adventure seeds
\end{itemize}

\section{GM Notes}

\subsection{Atmosphere Guidance}
\begin{itemize}
\item Emphasize sensory details: off-key music, metallic smells, wrong shadows
\item Create cognitive dissonance: beautiful carnival masking horror
\item Use lighting effects: increasing darkness, flickering lanterns
\item Make time feel distorted: nights that seem too long
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Memory Mechanics}
\begin{itemize}
\item Players may forget real details about their characters
\item Distinguish between real and false memories becomes challenging
\item Use confusion to create tension without frustrating players
\item Provide clear mechanical indicators of memory compromise
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Horror Elements}
\begin{itemize}
\item Psychological horror over gore
\item Loss of identity more frightening than death
\item Familiar becoming threatening
\item Moral ambiguity in choices
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scaling for Tiers}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Tier I:} Reduce entity Caps, simplify ritual requirements, provide more NPC assistance
\item \textbf{Tier II:} Standard challenges, full complexity, moderate supernatural threats
\item \textbf{Tier III+:} Add political complications, increase entity power, complicate resolution
\end{itemize}
