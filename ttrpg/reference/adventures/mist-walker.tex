\section*{Premise}
A powerful entity from the Ways Between is using the Mistlands as a conduit to enter the mortal world, corrupting the bell−lines and threatening to break the barriers between realms. The players must investigate the growing supernatural disturbances, uncover the source of the corruption, and either stop the entity or find a way to send it back where it came from.

\section*{Hook}
The adventure begins when the players encounter one of the following scenarios:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem A ferryman refuses to take them across a normally safe waterway, claiming "the mist walks where it shouldn't" and that bells have been heard tolling underwater.
    ℹtem A Bell−Warden approaches them in a tavern, desperate for help as bell−towers across the region are ringing without being touched, and some bells are ringing notes that don't exist.
    ℹtem Their patron sends them with a letter that should have arrived three days ago, but the courier claims they delivered it − yet the recipient has no knowledge of receiving it.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Plot Structure}

\subsection*{Session 1: Signs of Disturbance}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Opening Scene:} Players encounter one of the hook scenarios. Establish the supernatural nature of the threat through minor but unsettling phenomena.
    ℹtem \textbf{Key Objectives:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Interview Maren Thorne or Ferryman Eddis Crow
        ℹtem Investigate disturbed bell−towers (Bell−Line Levee)
        ℹtem Experience first supernatural phenomena
        ℹtem Discover evidence of inter−realm movement
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Sample Encounters:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{False Bell Warning:} Players must navigate levee path while bells ring false warnings. (Athletics + Notice vs DV 4, Controlled/Standard)
        ℹtem \textbf{Possessed Tower Keeper:} NPC behaves normally but gives subtly wrong information. (Insight vs DV 4, Controlled/Limited)
        ℹtem \textbf{Temporal Echo:} At Ghost−Ferry Slip, players see themselves performing different actions. (Wits + Notice vs DV 3, Desperate/Limited)
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Session End:} Players discover the corruption is supernatural and originates from the Ways Between. Advance Mist Corruption Timer +2.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Session 2: Into the Mist}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Opening Scene:} Players venture deeper into affected areas. Supernatural phenomena intensify.
    ℹtem \textbf{Key Objectives:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Navigate through corrupted bell−line territory
        ℹtem Confront direct manifestations of Mist Walker's influence
        ℹtem Discover the entity's anchoring strategy
        ℹtem Make moral choices about possessed NPCs
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Sample Encounters:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Pall Watch−Tower Investigation:} Players must distinguish real observations from temporal echoes. (Insight + Lore vs DV 5, Desperate/Standard)
        ℹtem \textbf{Possessed Ferry Passenger:} NPC attempts to lead ferry into dangerous mist. (Command + Skirmish vs DV 4, Controlled/Great)
        ℹtem \textbf{Bell Correlation Puzzle:} Decode corrupted bell patterns to find safe path. (Lore + Wits vs DV 5, Controlled/Standard)
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Session End:} Players identify Weeping Gate as primary anchor point. Advance Mist Corruption Timer +2.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Session 3: The Weeping Gate}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Opening Scene:} Players approach the primary anchor point. Full supernatural manifestation.
    ℹtem \textbf{Key Objectives:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Deal with complete supernatural corruption
        ℹtem Confront the Mist Walker directly or through proxies
        ℹtem Execute chosen resolution strategy
        ℹtem Resolve immediate threat to Mistlands
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Sample Encounters:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem \textbf{Bell−Warden Possession Wave:} Multiple NPCs turn hostile simultaneously. (Combat encounter, Desperate position for players)
        ℹtem \textbf{Sanctification Ritual:} Complex ritual to purify anchor point. (Extended challenge, Craft + Arcana vs DV 6)
        ℹtem \textbf{Mist Walker Confrontation:} Direct encounter with entity. (Variable based on player approach)
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Session End:} Resolution of main conflict. Advance or reduce Mist Corruption Timer based on outcome.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Key Mechanics}

\begin{mechanicsbox}{Mist Corruption Timer [8]}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Advance +1:} Bell−lines fail to function properly
    ℹtem \textbf{Advance +1:} NPCs become possessed or influenced
    ℹtem \textbf{Advance +2:} Players fail to properly sanctify corrupted areas
    ℹtem \textbf{Advance +1:} The Mist Walker successfully tempts a PC with forbidden knowledge
    ℹtem \textbf{Advance +2:} Direct confrontation with entity results in player retreat
    ℹtem \textbf{When Full:} Barriers between realms begin to collapse, permanent supernatural changes to region
\end{itemize}
\end{mechanicsbox}

\begin{mechanicsbox}{Bell−Line Functionality}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Normal:} +1 Position for travelers, clear warnings
    ℹtem \textbf{Partially Corrupted:} Controlled position, misleading warnings (DV 4 to navigate)
    ℹtem \textbf{Fully Corrupted:} Desperate position, attracting rather than warning (DV 5+ to navigate)
    ℹtem \textbf{Restored:} Return to normal function, may grant +1 Boon for safe passage
\end{itemize}
\end{mechanicsbox}

\begin{mechanicsbox}{Possession and Influence}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Detection:} Insight + Notice vs DV 4 to identify possessed NPC
    ℹtem \textbf{Resistance:} Spirit + Resolve vs DV 3 to resist temptation
    ℹtem \textbf{Temporary Boost:} Possessed characters gain +1 die to one skill for scene, but generate 1 SB
    ℹtem \textbf{Permanent Corruption:} If not cleansed, mark 1 segment on personal Corruption Timer 
    ℹtem \textbf{Cleansing:} Lore + Arcana vs DV 5, requires sanctified iron and salt
\end{itemize}
\end{mechanicsbox}

\section*{Possible Resolutions}

\begin{mechanicsbox}{Banishment}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Requirements:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Sanctify all 4 major corrupted bell−towers (Extended challenge, each DV 5)
        ℹtem Gather 3 bells that still ring true (Investigation challenge, DV 4 each)
        ℹtem Perform willing sacrifice (Social/Command challenge, DV 5)
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Procedure:} Combined ritual requiring all party members
    ℹtem \textbf{Costs:} 2 segments marked on each participant's Corruption Timer 
    ℹtem \textbf{Outcome:} Mist Walker banished, bell−lines restored, but some supernatural knowledge lost
\end{itemize}
\end{mechanicsbox}

\begin{mechanicsbox}{Negotiation}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Discovery:} Entity was drawn by ancient barrier damage from 200 years ago
    ℹtem \textbf{Options:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Help repair original damage (Extended Craft/Arcana challenge, DV 6)
        ℹtem Offer different anchoring point (Investigation/Survival challenge, DV 5)
        ℹtem Trade knowledge for departure (Lore vs DV 4, but mark Corruption)
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Costs:} Ongoing obligation to monitor new anchor point, 1 segment Corruption per party member
    ℹtem \textbf{Outcome:} Entity departs but may return with new demands, players gain some forbidden knowledge
\end{itemize}
\end{mechanicsbox}

\begin{mechanicsbox}{Containment}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Approach:} Strengthen existing wards rather than eliminate entity
    ℹtem \textbf{Requirements:}
    \begin{itemize}
        ℹtem Establish new protective measures (Craft + Arcana, DV 5)
        ℹtem Create monitoring protocol (Command + Lore, DV 4)
        ℹtem Accept entity's continued presence (Mark ongoing Complication)
    \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Costs:} Ongoing responsibility, periodic Corruption exposure
    ℹtem \textbf{Outcome:} Entity contained but players become wardens, gain access to some supernatural knowledge
\end{itemize}
\end{mechanicsbox}

\section*{GM Notes}

\subsection*{Pacing}
Maintain tension by gradually increasing supernatural elements:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Session 1: Minor disturbances, unsettling but manageable
    ℹtem Session 2: Direct supernatural encounters, clear otherworldly threat
    ℹtem Session 3: Full manifestation, existential threat to region
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Player Agency}
Provide multiple paths to resolution:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Combat approach: Direct confrontation with possessed NPCs and entity
    ℹtem Investigation approach: Decoding supernatural phenomena to find weaknesses
    ℹtem Social approach: Negotiating with entity or managing possessed NPCs
    ℹtem Ritual approach: Complex sanctification and restoration procedures
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Atmosphere}
Emphasize isolation and otherworldly nature:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Use fog, bells, and shifting paths to create unsettling atmosphere
    ℹtem Sound effects: Distant bell tones, whispers in mist, temporal echoes
    ℹtem Visual elements: Moving reflections, false memories, phasing objects
    ℹtem Temporal distortion: Events that don't follow logical sequence
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Story Beats}
Use Mist Walker's influence to generate complications:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem 1 SB: Minor temporal displacement, false memory fragment
    ℹtem 2 SB: NPC becomes temporarily possessed, misleading information
    ℹtem 3 SB: Environmental hazard from bell resonance, path becomes unreliable
    ℹtem 4+ SB: Direct manifestation of Mist Walker's influence, major NPC possession
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Session−Specific Guidance}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Session 1 Focus:} Establish supernatural threat without overwhelming players. Use unsettling but manageable phenomena to build tension.
    ℹtem \textbf{Session 2 Focus:} Escalate to direct supernatural encounters. Players should feel the threat is real and growing.
    ℹtem \textbf{Session 3 Focus:} Climactic confrontation. All previous elements converge. Players' choices have clear, significant consequences.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Alternative Endings}
If players fail to resolve the main conflict:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Partial Success:} Reduce corruption but don't eliminate threat. Sets up sequel adventure.
    ℹtem \textbf{Compromise:} Players become possessed but use influence to contain entity from within.
    ℹtem \textbf{Pyrrhic Victory:} Banish entity but at great cost (major NPC death, permanent regional damage).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Scaling for Different Tiers}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Tier I (Rookie):} Reduce entity Cap to 3, simplify ritual requirements, provide more NPC assistance.
    ℹtem \textbf{Tier III (Veteran):} Increase entity Cap to 5, add secondary entities, complicate ritual with additional requirements.
    ℹtem \textbf{Tier IV (Paragon):} Add political complications, multiple simultaneous anchor points, entity gains patron backing.
\end{itemize}
