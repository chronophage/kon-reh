\section{Premise}

Two powerful merchant houses in Silkstrand are engaged in an escalating economic war that's disrupting trade throughout the region. What began as competitive business practices has evolved into sabotage, assassination, and manipulation of the city's political structure. The players must navigate this conflict while pursuing their own objectives, choosing sides or finding a way to end the war before it destroys the city's economy entirely.

\section{Hook}

The adventure begins when the players encounter one of the following scenarios (Investigation + Notice vs DV 3 to determine truth):
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Cargo Seizure:} Their cargo is seized at the docks due to a "regulatory dispute" between the two merchant houses, requiring them to navigate the political maze to recover their goods. (Legal/Social challenge, Sway + Command vs DV 4)
ℹtem \textbf{Botched Job:} A contact offers them a lucrative job that involves retrieving sensitive information from the rival merchant house, but the job goes wrong and they become targets. (Infiltration/Combat encounter, DV 5)
ℹtem \textbf{Witness to Violence:} They witness an assassination attempt on a prominent merchant, making them both witnesses and potential targets in the escalating conflict. (Combat/Skill challenge, DV 4)
\end{itemize}

\section{Plot Structure}

\subsection{Session 1: Entanglement}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Players encounter one of the hook scenarios. Establish the immediate effects of merchant warfare.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Experience direct effects of economic warfare
ℹtem Meet representatives from both factions
ℹtem Learn conflict's origins and current state
ℹtem Make initial choices about faction support
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Regulatory Dispute:} Navigate legal maze to recover seized cargo. (Sway + Lore vs DV 4, Controlled/Standard)
ℹtem \textbf{Faction Recruitment:} Both houses attempt to recruit players. (Social encounter, DV 3−5 depending on approach)
ℹtem \textbf{Marketplace Tension:} Experience faction conflict at Three−Queens Bridge. (Social/Combat encounter, DV 4)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Session End:} Players committed to one side or maintaining neutrality. Advance Merchant War Timer +1.

\subsection{Session 2: Deepening Involvement}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Players take on more significant faction roles. Conflict escalates visibly.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Complete missions for chosen faction (or both)
ℹtem Experience escalating consequences of war
ℹtem Discover secrets about both houses
ℹtem Face moral dilemmas about involvement
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Sabotage Mission:} Infiltrate rival facility to gather intelligence or cause disruption. (Stealth + Tinker vs DV 5, Desperate/Standard)
ℹtem \textbf{Legal Battle:} Represent faction in Archivolt proceedings. (Command + Lore vs DV 5, Controlled/Great)
ℹtem \textbf{Assassination Attempt:} Defend or attack key faction figure. (Combat encounter, DV 5)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Session End:} Players gain significant faction influence. Advance Merchant War Timer +2, shift Reputation Tracks.

\subsection{Session 3: The Breaking Point}

\textbf{Opening Scene:} Conflict reaches crisis point threatening city stability.

\textbf{Key Objectives:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Deal with consequences of previous actions
ℹtem Make crucial decisions about conflict resolution
ℹtem Potentially face secret manipulator (The Broker)
ℹtem Determine future of Silkstrand's merchant community
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Sample Encounters:}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{City−Wide Crisis:} Deal with economic disruption affecting civilians. (Leadership challenge, DV 5)
ℹtem \textbf{The Broker's Revelation:} Confront secret manipulator. (Social/Combat encounter, DV 6)
ℹtem \textbf{Final Negotiation:} Broker peace or decisive victory. (Sway + Command vs DV 6, Desperate/Great)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Session End:} Major conflict resolution. Advance Merchant War Timer to culmination.

ℹtem[Crimson Sails Track:] Corrupt ←→ Progressive
ℹtem[Movement:] Player actions shift tracks by ±1 per significant interaction
ℹtem[Effects:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem High Traditionalist/Progressive: +1 die to faction interactions
    ℹtem High Rebel/Corrupt: −1 die to opposing faction interactions
    ℹtem Extreme positions: Access to special missions or complete rejection
  \end{itemize}
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Bridge Duel Protocol}
\begin{mechanics}
ℹtem[Trigger:] Business disputes escalate to violence on Three−Queens Bridge
ℹtem[Stakes Declaration:] Choose Purse (money), Permit (legal right), or Pride (reputation)
ℹtem[Seconds:] May spend 1 Boon to "Call the Colors" (improve Position by 1 step)
ℹtem[Refusal Consequences:] Automatic reputation damage (−1 die to social rolls with faction)
ℹtem[Resolution:] Standard combat rules with declared stakes determining consequences
\end{mechanics}

\subsection{Exchange Mini−Game}
\begin{mechanics}
ℹtem[Setup:] Track scene−long Spread (starts at 1)
ℹtem[Actions:] Each good call on trading floor moves Spread by ±1
ℹtem[Cash Out:] Gain Price Edge tag (+1 die) when buying/selling during scene
ℹtem[Skills Used:] Sway, Notice, Insight for reading market
ℹtem[DV:] 3 (basic trading), 4 (complex deals), 5+ (high−stakes negotiations)
\end{mechanics}

\section{Possible Resolutions}

\subsection{Faction Victory}
\begin{resolution}{Faction Victory}
ℹtem[Requirements:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Complete major sabotage mission against rival (Tinker + Subterfuge vs DV 6)
    ℹtem Win crucial legal case in Archivolt (Command + Lore vs DV 6)
    ℹtem Eliminate key leadership of opposing faction (Combat/Skill vs DV 5)
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Procedure:] Decisive action giving one faction overwhelming advantage
ℹtem[Costs:] City's balance of power shifts dangerously, potential for future rebellion
ℹtem[Outcome:] Winner gains control, but creates new tensions and dependencies
\end{resolution}

\subsection{Negotiated Settlement}
\begin{resolution}{Negotiated Settlement}
ℹtem[Discovery:] Expose The Broker's manipulation and secret influence
ℹtem[Requirements:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Gather evidence of manipulation (Investigation vs DV 5)
    ℹtem Convince both factions of mutual benefit (Sway + Command vs DV 5)
    ℹtem Gain Matron's support for intervention (Social challenge, DV 4)
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Procedure:] Complex three−way negotiation with city authority backing
ℹtem[Costs:] Both houses must compromise, ongoing oversight required
ℹtem[Outcome:] Stable business environment, mutual obligations to players
\end{resolution}

\subsection{Revolutionary Change}
\begin{resolution}{Revolutionary Change}
ℹtem[Approach:] Overthrow old system entirely, create new governance
ℹtem[Requirements:] 
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Expose corruption in both houses (Investigation + Subterfuge vs DV 5)
    ℹtem Rally common merchants and workers (Command + Sway vs DV 5)
    ℹtem Install new form of governance (Political challenge, DV 6)
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem[Procedure:] Popular uprising with player leadership
ℹtem[Costs:] Complete upheaval with uncertain future, potential for chaos
ℹtem[Outcome:] New order with player influence, but ongoing instability
\end{resolution}

\section{GM Notes}

\subsection{Social Dynamics}
Emphasize social aspects of conflict:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Every interaction has social stakes and long−term consequences
ℹtem Reputation affects access to resources and opportunities
ℹtem Social manipulation as important as economic warfare
ℹtem Class tensions between old nobility and new merchants
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Economic Realities}
Make economic warfare tangible:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Rising prices affecting ordinary citizens
ℹtem Job losses and business closures throughout city
ℹtem Disrupted supply chains creating shortages
ℹtem Economic pressure driving people to extreme measures
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Moral Ambiguity}
Present complex moral landscape:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Neither faction entirely good or evil
ℹtem House Valdris: Stability vs. resistance to progress
ℹtem Crimson Sails: Innovation vs. disruption of communities
ℹtem Players face genuine moral choices with consequences
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Player Agency}
Provide multiple paths to influence conflict:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Work for one side exclusively
ℹtem Play both sides against each other
ℹtem Work to end war entirely
ℹtem Create third option through revolutionary change
ℹtem Focus on personal objectives while navigating conflict
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Urban Environment}
Use Silkstrand's unique features:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Bridges create vertical complexity and strategic points
ℹtem Dyeworks provide industrial hazards and valuable resources
ℹtem Waterways offer transportation and smuggling routes
ℹtem Archivolt represents bureaucratic maze and legal complexity
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Story Beats}
Use economic conflict for complications:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem 1 SB: Minor business disruption, small price fluctuation
ℹtem 2 SB: Key contact becomes suspicious, shipment delayed
ℹtem 3 SB: Major sabotage incident, faction leader threatened
ℹtem 4+ SB: City−wide economic crisis, faction headquarters attacked
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Session−Specific Guidance}

\textbf{Session 1 Focus:} Establish conflict dynamics and player involvement. Players should understand both factions have strengths and weaknesses.

\textbf{Session 2 Focus:} Deepen faction involvement and escalate consequences. Players gain significant influence but face moral complexity.

\textbf{Session 3 Focus:} Climactic resolution with city−wide implications. All previous elements converge in final choices about Silkstrand's future.

\subsection{Alternative Endings}

If players attempt unconventional solutions:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Information Broker Path:} Become neutral facilitator profiting from both sides
ℹtem \textbf{City Guardian Role:} Work directly for Matron to maintain balance
ℹtem \textbf{Foreign Intervention:} Invite external power to resolve conflict
ℹtem \textbf{Economic Innovation:} Create new business model transcending faction divide
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scaling for Different Tiers}

\textbf{Tier II (Seasoned):} Standard economic and social challenges, local city scope.

\textbf{Tier III (Veteran):} Add regional political implications, foreign trade complications, and more powerful faction resources.

\textbf{Tier IV (Paragon):} Introduce international trade war consequences, faction gains patron backing, threat to entire trade network.
