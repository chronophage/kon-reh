\section*{Design Goals}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{City as character.} Districts and institutions carry tags, Strings, and timers that push back.
    ℹtem \textbf{Plug into core.} Use Position/DV, SB, and timers; no new dice math.
    ℹtem \textbf{Street to state.} Blend casework, capers, and civic settlements.
    ℹtem \textbf{Low bookkeeping.} Track names and tags; let timers and Strings carry weight.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Quickstart (2 minutes)}
\begin{enumerate}
    ℹtem Pick a City Kit (§7). Set Mandate 2 / Crisis 2 unless fiction says otherwise.
    ℹtem Choose 4 Factions from the kit; copy their Strings; start each faction's Influence or Agenda timer at 0.
    ℹtem Mark 2 volatile stakes on the relationship map (e.g., “Harbor Master owes Corsairs a favor”).
    ℹtem Draw the Urban Deck (§6.2): one Incident (Spade), one Person (Heart), one Complication (Club) to spark the opening dilemma.
    ℹtem Frame the first Situation Timer [4−−8] and at least one Counter−Timer [4−−6].
    ℹtem Play scenes. All 1s generate SB; spend city−flavored Social SB (optional, see §3.4).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Pro Tip:} Start with the Three−Timer Rule – only track the Situation Timer , one Counter−Clock, and one Campaign Timer at a time.

\subsection*{District Effect (per scene)}
Acting with a district's tags → Position +1 or DV –1 once/scene.  
Acting against a district's tags → Position –1 or GM banks +1 SB.  
Whenever a player rolls a 1 in a district, the GM may tick that district's Complication timer (if any) by 1.

\section*{3) City Procedures}
\subsection*{3.1 Scene Framing}
State the question; pick a Situation Timer ; set Position & DV from fiction + district effect; start a Counter−Timer if opposition is active; tag the venue (Audience/Institution).

\subsection*{3.2 Strings (Urban)}
Common Strings: Bridge Tolls, Aqueduct Permits, Ferry Passbooks, Oath−Tablet Registry, Harbor Pilots, Festival Licenses, Censor's Seal, Ship Papers, Privateer Charters, Contraband Manifests.  

Acting with/through a relevant String: Position +1 or DV –1 once/scene.  
To cut or seize a String, start a 4–6 segment timer. On completion, you may:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Seize:} Gain control of the String for your own use.
    ℹtem \textbf{Destroy:} Prevent anyone from using it.
    ℹtem \textbf{Redirect:} Change who controls it (e.g., assign to a different faction).
\end{itemize}
\textit{Example:} Cutting the String \textit{Harbor Warrants} [4] – on success, you either obtain legitimate papers or prevent the harbor from issuing warrants against you.

\subsection*{3.3 City Dials (Mandate & Crisis)}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Mandate (0–6)} – public legitimacy. At 4–5, cancel one Social SB in public venues once/session; public offices DV –1. At 6, start public scenes Dominant unless fiction forbids.
    ℹtem \textbf{Crisis (0–6)} – ambient pressure. At 4–5, first failed public scene ticks party Exposure +1; at 6, start with a crowd Counter−Timer [4].
\end{itemize}
At the end of each session, if total SB spent by the GM exceeds 10, increase Crisis by 1. If the party successfully resolved 2+ major timers, increase Mandate by 1.

\subsection*{3.4 Social SB Menu (optional, city−flavored)}
Instead of the generic SB menu (noise, alarm, etc.), GMs may choose from these urban complications:

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Faux pas title/ritual
    ℹtem Lose 1 Favor in venue
    ℹtem Leverage leak → Exposure +1
    ℹtem Crowd turns (Warm→Skeptical→Hostile)
    ℹtem Permit pulled (lose String access)
    ℹtem Patrol arrives biased
    ℹtem Festival crush
    ℹtem Toll chain drops
    ℹtem Ship quarantine
    ℹtem Cargo inspection
    ℹtem Guild audit
    ℹtem Noble scandal
    ℹtem Temple censure
    ℹtem Watch curfew
    ℹtem Harbor closure
    ℹtem Price fixing
    ℹtem Counterfeit goods
    ℹtem Debt collector
    ℹtem Bounty hunter
    ℹtem Informer betrayal
    ℹtem Rival sabotage
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsection*{3.5 Investigation Timer Management}
For long−term mysteries, use an \textbf{Investigation Timer [6]}.  
Each significant clue advances the timer by:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Clue Grade D or C → +1
    ℹtem Clue Grade B or A → +2
    ℹtem Clue Grade A+ → +3
\end{itemize}
When the timer fills, the GM describes the complete truth – but also introduces a new complication (e.g., the culprit escapes, evidence is destroyed, or a powerful figure is implicated).

\textit{Example progression:} Clue D (1) → Clue C (1) → Clue B (2) → Clue A+ (3) = 7 segments → truth revealed, but the villain has already fled.

\subsection*{3.6 Between‑Session Cycle}
Update world state → draw events (§6.3) → resolve off‑screen orders → recompute Mandate/Crisis → prep next Situation.

\section*{4) Urban Actions (additions)}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Invoke Rite (Wits/Presence + Rite):} Claim hospitality, sanctuary, or festival privilege. Hit: protective tag; miss: Exposure +1.
    ℹtem \textbf{Grease Palms (Wits + Streetwise):} Secure permits/tolls. Hit: tag \textit{Greased Palms}; miss: rumor of bribery (Exposure +1).
    ℹtem \textbf{Audit Ledger (Wits + Investigation):} Catch graft/tithe anomalies. Hit: DV –1 on later Expose.
    ℹtem \textbf{Call the Watch (Presence + Command):} Leverage order. Hit: \textit{Watchful Patrols} tag; miss: they arrive biased.
    ℹtem \textbf{Smuggle (Wits + Subterfuge):} Move goods/people across wards. Hit: gain Leverage (1) or fill; 1s start \textit{Contraband Trail} timer.
    ℹtem \textbf{Carouse for Rumors (Presence + Performance):} Trade coin and stories. Hit: bank 1 Clue (see §6.1); miss: SB → Hangover Debts.
    ℹtem \textbf{Forge Documents (Wits + Tinker):} Create false papers/permits. Hit: tag \textit{Forged Papers}; miss: Exposure +1.
    ℹtem \textbf{Navigate Politics (Presence + Command):} Maneuver through civic structures. Hit: DV –1 on next Petition; miss: political rival gains Influence.
    ℹtem \textbf{Bribe Official (Wits + Sway):} Pay for favors/permissions. Hit: tag \textit{Bribe Accepted}; miss: Exposure +1.
    ℹtem \textbf{Commission Contract (Presence + Broker):} Hire services/mercenaries. Hit: tag \textit{Contract Secured}; miss: Exposure +1.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Default Ripples (Success / Failure)}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Success:} ally Influence +1 (or +2 if on Aim); enemy Exposure +1; clean win reduces ally Exposure –1.
    ℹtem \textbf{Failure:} enemy Influence +1 (or +2 on Aim); ally Exposure +1; internal failure also Stability +1.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{DV Cheatsheet}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Petition: 2–4 (temple/court)
    ℹtem Broker: 3–4 (neutral ground helps)
    ℹtem Expose: 3–5 (proof lowers)
    ℹtem Infiltrate: 3–5 (wards raise)
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Three−Timer Rule}
Only maintain three active timers at once:  
1. Situation Timer (the current scene’s main tension)  
2. Counter−Timer (opposition or environment)  
3. Campaign Timer (faction agenda or long−term threat)  
Merge or retire others.
