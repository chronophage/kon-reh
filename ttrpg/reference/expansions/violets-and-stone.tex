\section*{Design Goals}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{City as character.} Districts and institutions carry tags, Strings, and timers that push back.
    \item \textbf{Plug into core.} Use Position/DV, SB, and timers; no new dice math.
    \item \textbf{Street to state.} Blend casework, capers, and civic settlements.
    \item \textbf{Low bookkeeping.} Track names and tags; let timers and Strings carry weight.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Quickstart (2 minutes)}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Pick a City Kit (\S7). Set Mandate 2 / Crisis 2 unless fiction says otherwise.
    \item Choose 4 Factions from the kit; copy their Strings; start each faction's Influence or Agenda timer at 0.
    \item Mark 2 volatile stakes on the relationship map (e.g., “Harbor Master owes Corsairs a favor”).
    \item Draw the Urban Deck (\S6.2): one Incident (Spade), one Person (Heart), one Complication (Club) to spark the opening dilemma.
    \item Frame the first Situation Clock [4--8] and at least one Counter-Clock [4--6].
    \item Play scenes. All 1s generate SB; spend city-flavored Social SB (optional, see \S3.4).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Pro Tip:} Start with the Three-Clock Rule – only track the Situation Clock, one Counter-Clock, and one Campaign Clock at a time.

\section*{1) City Sheet (Template)}
\textbf{[CITY NAME]} \\
Mandate (0--6): public legitimacy of the company in this city \\
Crisis (0--6): ambient pressure (riots, audits, plague, war drums) \\

Chase Modes: foot, canal, rooftops, alleyways (see \S5) \\

\subsection*{District Effect (per scene)}
Acting with a district's tags → Position +1 or DV –1 once/scene.  
Acting against a district's tags → Position –1 or GM banks +1 SB.  
Whenever a player rolls a 1 in a district, the GM may tick that district's Complication timer (if any) by 1.

\section*{3) City Procedures}
\subsection*{3.1 Scene Framing}
State the question; pick a Situation Clock; set Position \& DV from fiction + district effect; start a Counter-Clock if opposition is active; tag the venue (Audience/Institution).

\subsection*{3.2 Strings (Urban)}
Common Strings: Bridge Tolls, Aqueduct Permits, Ferry Passbooks, Oath-Tablet Registry, Harbor Pilots, Festival Licenses, Censor's Seal, Ship Papers, Privateer Charters, Contraband Manifests.  

Acting with/through a relevant String: Position +1 or DV –1 once/scene.  
To cut or seize a String, start a 4–6 segment timer. On completion, you may:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Seize:} Gain control of the String for your own use.
    \item \textbf{Destroy:} Prevent anyone from using it.
    \item \textbf{Redirect:} Change who controls it (e.g., assign to a different faction).
\end{itemize}
\textit{Example:} Cutting the String \textit{Harbor Warrants} [4] – on success, you either obtain legitimate papers or prevent the harbor from issuing warrants against you.

\subsection*{3.3 City Dials (Mandate \& Crisis)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Mandate (0–6)} – public legitimacy. At 4–5, cancel one Social SB in public venues once/session; public offices DV –1. At 6, start public scenes Dominant unless fiction forbids.
    \item \textbf{Crisis (0–6)} – ambient pressure. At 4–5, first failed public scene ticks party Exposure +1; at 6, start with a crowd Counter-Clock [4].
\end{itemize}
At the end of each session, if total SB spent by the GM exceeds 10, increase Crisis by 1. If the party successfully resolved 2+ major timers, increase Mandate by 1.

\subsection*{3.4 Social SB Menu (optional, city-flavored)}
Instead of the generic SB menu (noise, alarm, etc.), GMs may choose from these urban complications:

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Faux pas title/ritual
    \item Lose 1 Favor in venue
    \item Leverage leak → Exposure +1
    \item Crowd turns (Warm→Skeptical→Hostile)
    \item Permit pulled (lose String access)
    \item Patrol arrives biased
    \item Festival crush
    \item Toll chain drops
    \item Ship quarantine
    \item Cargo inspection
    \item Guild audit
    \item Noble scandal
    \item Temple censure
    \item Watch curfew
    \item Harbor closure
    \item Price fixing
    \item Counterfeit goods
    \item Debt collector
    \item Bounty hunter
    \item Informer betrayal
    \item Rival sabotage
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\subsection*{3.5 Investigation Clock Management}
For long-term mysteries, use an \textbf{Investigation Clock [6]}.  
Each significant clue advances the timer by:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Clue Grade D or C → +1
    \item Clue Grade B or A → +2
    \item Clue Grade A+ → +3
\end{itemize}
When the timer fills, the GM describes the complete truth – but also introduces a new complication (e.g., the culprit escapes, evidence is destroyed, or a powerful figure is implicated).

\textit{Example progression:} Clue D (1) → Clue C (1) → Clue B (2) → Clue A+ (3) = 7 segments → truth revealed, but the villain has already fled.

\subsection*{3.6 Between‑Session Cycle}
Update world state → draw events (\S6.3) → resolve off‑screen orders → recompute Mandate/Crisis → prep next Situation.

\section*{4) Urban Actions (additions)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Invoke Rite (Wits/Presence + Rite):} Claim hospitality, sanctuary, or festival privilege. Hit: protective tag; miss: Exposure +1.
    \item \textbf{Grease Palms (Wits + Streetwise):} Secure permits/tolls. Hit: tag \textit{Greased Palms}; miss: rumor of bribery (Exposure +1).
    \item \textbf{Audit Ledger (Wits + Investigation):} Catch graft/tithe anomalies. Hit: DV –1 on later Expose.
    \item \textbf{Call the Watch (Presence + Command):} Leverage order. Hit: \textit{Watchful Patrols} tag; miss: they arrive biased.
    \item \textbf{Smuggle (Wits + Subterfuge):} Move goods/people across wards. Hit: gain Leverage (1) or fill; 1s start \textit{Contraband Trail} timer.
    \item \textbf{Carouse for Rumors (Presence + Performance):} Trade coin and stories. Hit: bank 1 Clue (see \S6.1); miss: SB → Hangover Debts.
    \item \textbf{Forge Documents (Wits + Tinker):} Create false papers/permits. Hit: tag \textit{Forged Papers}; miss: Exposure +1.
    \item \textbf{Navigate Politics (Presence + Command):} Maneuver through civic structures. Hit: DV –1 on next Petition; miss: political rival gains Influence.
    \item \textbf{Bribe Official (Wits + Sway):} Pay for favors/permissions. Hit: tag \textit{Bribe Accepted}; miss: Exposure +1.
    \item \textbf{Commission Contract (Presence + Broker):} Hire services/mercenaries. Hit: tag \textit{Contract Secured}; miss: Exposure +1.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{6.1 Clues \& Casework}
Grades:  
A+ (witnessed oath; sealed writ)  
A (signed testimony; verified mark)  
B (two-source rumor; ledger trail)  
C (hearsay; graffiti)

A day without follow-up degrades one step if contested.  
Spend: 1 Clue → DV –1 on a related Expose/Petition once.

\subsection*{6.2 Urban Deck (52-card)}

\textbf{Clock size} by highest rank: 2–5 → [4], 6–10 → [6], J/Q/K → [8], A → [10].

\section*{9) GM Toolkit}
\subsection*{5-Minute City Prep}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Pick 1 City Kit
    \item Circle 4 Factions
    \item Highlight 2 Districts for the scene
    \item Note the Special Rule
    \item Draw 1 Urban Deck card
    \item Ready for first scene
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Default Ripples (Success / Failure)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Success:} ally Influence +1 (or +2 if on Aim); enemy Exposure +1; clean win reduces ally Exposure –1.
    \item \textbf{Failure:} enemy Influence +1 (or +2 on Aim); ally Exposure +1; internal failure also Stability +1.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{DV Cheatsheet}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Petition: 2–4 (temple/court)
    \item Broker: 3–4 (neutral ground helps)
    \item Expose: 3–5 (proof lowers)
    \item Infiltrate: 3–5 (wards raise)
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Three-Clock Rule}
Only maintain three active timers at once:  
1. Situation Clock (the current scene’s main tension)  
2. Counter-Clock (opposition or environment)  
3. Campaign Clock (faction agenda or long-term threat)  
Merge or retire others.
