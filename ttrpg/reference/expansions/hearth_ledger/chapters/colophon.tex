\chapter*{Colophon: Margins and Dust}

The paper is pressed flax -- soft, cheap, good for ledgers. Ink is lamp‑black and oak gall, mixed with a drop of my own blood. Not for binding. For memory.

\smallskip

Ikasha asks for a secret with every telling. I gave her the corners of these pages, where the words thin and the eye looks away.

\smallskip

If you find this book in a chest, on a ferry, or lying open in a shrine -- leave a copper coin on the cover. The toll is for the road, not for me.

\smallskip

The fires are cold. I have another leg to walk.

\smallskip

\noindent --- \textit{Dusana}  

\noindent \textit{Crossroads east of Silkstrand, dawn of the autumn equinox.}  

\noindent \textit{I poured water first, then drank. The bell's shadow fell across my page. I left before it moved.}
