\chapter{The Peddler's Swerve}

\section*{Told by}
Rusti the Factor, a Vilikari trader with a missing finger and a smile that never reaches her eyes. She’s sold the same horse three times and still owns it.

\section*{The Tale}

A peddler came to a border ford. The toll was high. She had no coin. She had a cart full of empty barrels.

“Air,” she told the toll‑keeper. “Fresh mountain air. The valley pays handsomely.”

He laughed. “Air? I’ll let you pass for a taste.”

She uncorked a barrel, held it to his nose. Pine and snow.

He waved her through.

The next day, a rival told him the barrels were empty. Furious, he rode after her.

She had already sold the air – as “breathing lessons for the rich.” Copper per lungful. The barrels were now full of gold.

He cornered her. “You cheated me.”

She shrugged. “Air is free. You chose not to sell it. I did.”

She paid double the original toll – from the gold – and asked, “Do you want a lesson in breathing?”

He did not. But the next year, he sold fog to the lowlands. Made a fortune.

\section*{The Witch's Closing}
“Sell the hour, not the horse. Value is not in the thing – it is in the need.”

\section*{What Lurks in the Shadow of the Tale}

\subsection*{The Hollow Barrel}

A barrel that appears empty but can be “filled” with anything people believe is inside. Works by suggestion – open it, describe the contents, it emits faint scent, sound, warmth. Cannot create matter, only perception.

\paragraph{Tags / Mechanics:}
\begin{itemize}
\item [ILLUSION] [TRICK]
\item Once per scene, a character can declare the barrel contains a plausible small object. It produces sensory evidence. Perception DV 3 reveals illusion.
\item Used to fool guards, start rumours, create false trails.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Adventure Hook:}
A con artist is selling “dragon’s breath” and “ghost tears” using hollow barrels. Catch her – or join her.
