\chapter*{Preface: The Road That Listens}

A ledger of stories is not a ledger of coin. It balances nothing.

\smallskip

You travel. You sit by fires. A woman speaks. You write. The ash on your cuff becomes ink. That is all.

\smallskip

My people are the Tulkani. We build no temples. Our wagons carry the hearth. Kuva rides the storm -- some say. I have lit a candle for him when the road was ice. Kuva counts the stars; I count the thresholds.

\smallskip

Ikasha does not command. She sleeps. Between her breaths, there is room for secrets. I have borrowed that space.

\smallskip

These tales were told by midwives, bell‑wardens, hedge‑mothers, cord‑weavers. They did not call themselves witches. The women who speak to the dead never do.

\smallskip

I wrote as the fire guttered. Where a teller coughed, I left a space. Where a bell rang, I marked the hour. Where the ninth word was missing -- I did not add it.

\smallskip

Some tales are comfort. Some are warnings. A few are keys.

\smallskip

Read them. Or don't. The road turns regardless.

\smallskip

\noindent --- \textit{Dusana of the Raven Road}  

\noindent \textit{Under the lamp, bread and salt. The ink is ash and rain.}
