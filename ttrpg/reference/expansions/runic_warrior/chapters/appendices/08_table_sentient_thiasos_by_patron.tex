\subsection*{Sentient Thiasos Traits}

All sentient Thiasos share the following traits:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Witness:} Counts as a witness for all Rites. It sees, it remembers, it can testify.
\item \textbf{Codex:} Rites can be engraved, etched, or woven into its surface. If destroyed, all Rites are lost.
\item \textbf{Communication:} Speaks aloud (not just in the Runekeeper's mind). It has its own personality, opinions, and occasionally inappropriate commentary.
\item \textbf{Vulnerability:} Cannot act independently. Cannot accept Shared Burden (Fatigue transfer). Can be damaged or destroyed.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Sentient Thiasos Talents}

Characters with a sentient Thiasos may take the following talents:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Talking Weapon (2 XP):} Your sentient Thiasos can speak aloud. The GM may use it to deliver hints, warnings, or comic relief.
\item \textbf{The Blade Remembers (4 XP):} Once per session, your Thiasos can recall a past enemy's fighting style, granting you +1 die to defend against a similar foe.
\item \textbf{The Hilt Listens (6 XP):} Your Thiasos can hear conversations within Near range even when you are not present (if you leave it in a room). At the end of the scene, it reports what it heard.
\item \textbf{The Edge Speaks (8 XP):} Once per session, your Thiasos can deliver a single sentence in the voice of anyone whose blood it has tasted. Use this to confuse, frighten, or compel an enemy.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Creating a Sentient Thiasos}

When building a sentient Thiasos, work with your GM to determine:

\begin{itemize}
\item Its \textbf{voice} (cracked, smooth, whispering, booming, multiple)
\item Its \textbf{personality} (honorable, cynical, mournful, eager, reluctant)
\item Its \textbf{memory} (what it remembers, what it has forgotten)
\item Its \textbf{demand} (what it asks for in return for service—a story, a promise, a memory)
\end{itemize}

A sentient Thiasos is not a pet. It is a partner—and sometimes a critic.

The thread held. The water carried. That is not a Rite. That is grace.
