\subsection*{Codex Forms by Category}

\subsubsection*{Worn (Armor, Clothing, Jewelry)}
The Codex is worn on the body. It cannot be stolen without being noticed. It is always visible. Examples: gorget, gauntlet, grey cloak, locket, scar-plate, silk mask.

\subsubsection*{Carried (Weapons, Tools, Instruments)}
The Codex is held or wielded. It can be lost or stolen. It can serve as a weapon or tool. Examples: shield, gavel, staff, axe, waystone, compass, drum, spear.

\subsubsection*{Marked (Tattoos, Brands, Scars)}
The Codex is inscribed on the Runekeeper's skin. It cannot be stolen. It cannot be hidden without clothing. Examples: flesh-tattoos, sealed lips tattoo, scar-plate.

\subsubsection*{Kept (Tokens, Relics, Trinkets)}
The Codex is a small object carried in a pocket or pouch. It is portable and concealable. It can be lost or stolen. Examples: silver coin, thorn, black pearl, bone-chime, shed snakeskin.

\subsection*{Choosing a Codex}

When building a Runic Warrior, choose a Codex form appropriate to your Patron and cultural origin. The Codex should reflect your covenant—a Dawn Warrior's engraved gorget, a Bloodsworn's flesh-tattoos, a Shadow Warrior's raven's quill.

A Codex may be damaged or destroyed. Repairing it requires a Craft test (DV 3-5) and appropriate materials. A destroyed Codex loses all Rites engraved on it.

The thread held. The water carried. That is not a Rite. That is grace.
