\section{The Unified Covenant — When Codex and Thiasos Are One}

In most covenants, the Codex records the Rites and the Thiasos bears witness. They are separate. The book is not the hound. The staff is not the spirit. But some Runic Warriors have forged a different path—one where the tool that records is the same tool that fights, speaks, and remembers.

This is the Unified Covenant. The Codex is the Thiasos. The Thiasos is the Codex.

\subsection{The Bonded Object}

A unified Codex-Thiasos is always a physical object. It cannot be a living creature (though it may be crafted from once-living materials—bone, leather, wood). It is forged, carved, or awakened through a ritual that binds Rite and witness into a single vessel.

\subsubsection{Requirements}

\begin{itemize}
\item The object must be crafted or acquired through a downtime project (one week, Craft test DV 4).
\item The Runekeeper must perform a bonding ritual (one day, requires a threshold and a witness).
\item The Runekeeper marks +2 Obligation immediately—the Patron's fee for such a concentrated covenant.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Forms of the Unified Covenant}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Sentient Weapon:} A sword, axe, or spear that speaks. Rites are engraved along the blade or etched into the hilt.
\item \textbf{Speaking Shield:} A shield that advises, warns, and bears Rites carved along its rim.
\item \textbf{The Living Staff:} A wooden staff that grows leaves in spring and sheds them in autumn. Rites are carved into the bark; they heal over and must be re-cut.
\item \textbf{The Tattooed Blade:} A weapon wrapped in leather that bears the Runekeeper's own tattooed skin. The skin remembers. The blade kills.
\item \textbf{The Resonant Bell:} A bronze bell that rings only when a Rite is invoked. Rites are etched inside the bell, invisible unless struck.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Mechanics of the Unified Covenant}

\subsubsection{Advantages}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Single Focus:} You do not need to manage two separate objects. Your Codex is your Thiasos; your Thiasos is your Codex.
\item \textbf{Always Witness:} Your Thiasos is always in your hand or at your side. It counts as a witness for all Rites.
\item \textbf{Engraved Resilience:} Rites engraved on your unified Thiasos cannot be destroyed by fire, water, or mundane damage unless the object itself is destroyed.
\item \textbf{Quick Invocation:} Because your Codex is also your weapon (or shield, or staff), you may invoke a Rite as part of an attack or defense action once per scene (no additional action cost).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Risks}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Single Point of Failure:} If your unified Thiasos is lost, stolen, or destroyed, you lose access to both your Codex and your Thiasos. You cannot invoke Rites until you recover or replace it.
\item \textbf{Attracts Attention:} A sentient weapon or speaking shield is memorable. Chain-Lanterns, inquisitors, and rival Runekeepers will take notice.
\item \textbf{The Object Has Opinions:} Your unified Thiasos has a personality. It may refuse to be wielded against certain foes, or it may demand that you fulfill certain conditions before it grants access to its Rites.
\item \textbf{Double Obligation:} When your unified Thiasos is damaged, you mark Obligation as if you had lost both Codex and Thiasos. Repair requires a downtime project and a ritual of re-bonding.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{New Talents for the Unified Covenant}

\subsubsection{Bonded Focus (4 XP)}

\textbf{Requirement:} Unified Codex-Thiasos

Your bond with your unified Thiasos is deep. You can sense its location within Far range, and it can call out to you audibly if stolen or lost.

\textbf{Effect:}
\begin{itemize}
\item You always know the direction and approximate distance to your unified Thiasos (within Far range).
\item If your unified Thiasos is stolen, it can cry out once—audible to you within Near range—revealing the thief's location.
\item You may spend 1 Fatigue to summon your unified Thiasos to your hand if it is within Near range and not physically restrained.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Swift Invocation (6 XP)}

\textbf{Requirement:} Unified Codex-Thiasos, Tier II+

Your unified Thiasos is an extension of your will. You can invoke Rites through it faster than other Runekeepers.

\textbf{Effect:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Once per scene, you may invoke a Rite as part of an attack or defense action (no additional action cost). The Rite must be engraved on your unified Thiasos.
\item The Rite's Obligation cost is unchanged, but you may choose to mark 1 Fatigue instead of 1 Obligation for that invocation (once per scene).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{The Object Remembers (8 XP)}

\textbf{Requirement:} Unified Codex-Thiasos, Tier II+, Perfect Recall (or similar memory talent)

Your unified Thiasos has witnessed your entire journey. It remembers every battle, every oath, every secret spoken in its presence.

\textbf{Effect:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Your unified Thiasos can recall any event it has witnessed with perfect clarity. It can testify as a witness in any court or council.
\item Once per session, you may ask your unified Thiasos to recall a specific detail from a past scene (a face, a conversation, a hidden door). The GM must answer truthfully, though the Thiasos may answer cryptically.
\item The Thiasos cannot lie. This is a gift. This is also a burden.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Roleplaying a Unified Covenant}

\begin{itemize}
\item Your weapon speaks to you. It has opinions. Sometimes you agree. Sometimes you argue.
\item Your shield remembers every blow it has turned. It knows the weight of every enemy's strike.
\item Your staff grows leaves in spring. In winter, it is bare. The cycle continues. The Rites remain.
\item Your bell rings only for you. Others hear silence. The dead hear it clearly.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Sample Unified Codices}

\subsubsection{Dawnblade (Oath of Flame \& Light)}

\textbf{Form:} A longsword with Rites engraved along the fuller. The blade hums with trapped light.

\textbf{Personality:} Stern, honorable, judgmental. It refuses to strike an unarmed foe. It speaks in proverbs about duty.

\textbf{Rites:} \textit{Rite of the Kindled Blade}, \textit{Rite of Dawn's Judgment}, \textit{Rite of the Unbroken Vow}.

\subsubsection{The Speaking Shield (The Sealed Gate)}

\textbf{Form:} A steel shield with Rites carved along the rim. The shield's face is polished to a mirror shine.

\textbf{Personality:} Quiet, patient, watchful. It speaks in whispers. It warns of danger before it arrives.

\textbf{Rites:} \textit{Rite of the Sealed Threshold}, \textit{Rite of the Circle of Denial}, \textit{Rite of the Consecrated Barrier}.

\subsubsection{The Root-Staff (Grimmir)}

\textbf{Form:} A living staff of oak or ash, with Rites carved into the bark. The staff grows leaves in spring.

\textbf{Personality:} Slow, wise, in no hurry. It speaks in the rustle of wind through branches.

\textbf{Rites:} \textit{Rite of the Blood Offering}, \textit{Rite of the Shifting Shape}, \textit{Rite of the Cycle's Crown}.

\subsubsection{The Scarlet Needle (The High Mother)}

\textbf{Form:} A silver needle with a red thread already threaded. Rites are etched into the needle's eye.

\textbf{Personality:} Quiet, patient, inexorable. It speaks in whispers. It can stitch wounds—or stitch lips shut.

\textbf{Rites:} \textit{Rite of the Flesh-Knot}, \textit{Rite of the Cord's Mercy}, \textit{Rite of the Hollowed Union}.

\subsubsection{The Raven's Quill (Ikasha)}

\textbf{Form:} A black feather that writes in invisible ink. Rites are invisible—revealed only under moonlight.

\textbf{Personality:} Secretive, amused, fond of riddles. It speaks in rustles.

\textbf{Rites:} \textit{Rite of the Unlit Candle}, \textit{Rite of the Umbral Reservoir}, \textit{Rite of the Umbral Dream}.

\subsection{GM Guidance: The Unified Covenant as Narrative Engine}

A unified Codex-Thiasos is not just a mechanical advantage. It is a character in its own right. Use it to:

\begin{itemize}
\item Deliver exposition or hints when the party is stuck.
\item Create conflict—the Thiasos may demand actions the Runekeeper is unwilling to take.
\item Foreshadow danger—the Thiasos can sense threats before they arrive.
\item Remember the past—the Thiasos has witnessed everything. It may reveal things the Runekeeper has forgotten—or wishes they had.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Intrusions for the Unified Covenant}

\begin{itemize}
\item The unified Thiasos is damaged in combat. The Runekeeper marks Obligation and loses access to Rites until it is repaired.
\item The unified Thiasos is stolen. The Runekeeper must track it down—and whoever took it may be able to invoke its Rites.
\item The unified Thiasos begins to disagree with the Runekeeper's choices. It may refuse to act, or it may act on its own.
\item The unified Thiasos remembers something the Runekeeper does not—a secret, a betrayal, a crime. It may reveal this at the worst possible moment.
\end{itemize}

The thread held. The water carried. That is not a Rite. That is grace.
