\subsection*{Codex Forms by Category}

\subsubsection*{Flesh Codex}
The Rites are inscribed on the Runekeeper's skin. Cannot be stolen. Always visible. Damage heals naturally but may fade or scar. Requires a needle, ink (or ash), and a steady hand to engrave.

\textbf{Examples by Location:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Hands:} Threshold Tattoo (Sealed Gate), Shadow Tattoo (Ikasha)
\item \textbf{Wrists:} Grey Cord (Pale Shepherd), Rot-Cord (Carrion-King), Threaded Vein (Inaea)
\item \textbf{Forearms:} Branded Bracer (Oath of Flame \& Light), Wergild Scar (Varnek Karn)
\item \textbf{Chest:} Censer Brand (Inquisitor Prime), Claw Necklace (Savage Heart)
\item \textbf{Back:} Memory Scar (Witness), Storm Scar (Xhak'Thul), Fractal Scar (Ninth)
\item \textbf{Face:} Shadow Tattoo (Ikasha), Veil Tattoo (Lethai), Sealed Lips (Silent Choir)
\item \textbf{Throat:} Pressure Scar (Khemesh)
\item \textbf{Soles of feet:} Road-Scar (Traveler)
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Stone Codex}
The Rites are carved, etched, or engraved onto a physical object. Durable. Can be lost or stolen. Can serve as a tool or weapon. Requires chisel, hammer, or stylus to engrave.

\textbf{Examples:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Gorget:} Engraved Gorget (Oath of Flame \& Light)
\item \textbf{Shield:} Shield of Precedents (Mykkiel)
\item \textbf{Gauntlet:} Gauntlet of Proscription (Inquisitor Prime)
\item \textbf{Mirror:} Silver Mirror (Witness)
\item \textbf{Key:} Gate-Key (Sealed Gate)
\item \textbf{Compass:} Waystone Compass (Traveler), Salt-Crusted Compass (Raéyn)
\item \textbf{Staff:} Carved Staff (Grimmir), Shepherd's Crook (Pale Shepherd)
\item \textbf{Drum:} Thunder Drum (Xhak'Thul)
\item \textbf{Stone:} Burial Stone (Varnek Karn)
\item \textbf{Slate:} Schematic-Slate (Clockwork Monad)
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Steel Codex}
The Rites are engraved on a weapon, tool, or sentient object. Can serve as both Codex and Thiasos. May be sentient. Requires a forge or fine tools to engrave.

\textbf{Examples:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Blade:} Dawnblade (Oath of Flame \& Light), The Fang (Savage Heart), The Shed Fang (Isoka)
\item \textbf{Gavel:} The Speaking Gavel (Mykkiel)
\item \textbf{Censer:} The Censer of Unforgiving Flame (Inquisitor Prime)
\item \textbf{Bell:} The Grey Bell (Oath of Mercy \& Grace, Pale Shepherd)
\item \textbf{Compass:} The Waystone Compass (Traveler), The Salt Compass (Raéyn)
\item \textbf{Drum:} The Thunder Drum (Xhak'Thul)
\item \textbf{Glass:} The Abyssal Glass (Khemesh)
\item \textbf{Quill:} The Raven's Quill (Ikasha)

\subsection*{Choosing a Codex Form}

When building a Runic Warrior, choose a Codex form appropriate to your Patron and cultural origin. Consider:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Visibility:} A Flesh Codex cannot be hidden without clothing. A Stone or Steel Codex can be concealed (in a satchel, under a cloak) but may be lost or stolen.
\item \textbf{Durability:} Flesh Codexes heal naturally. Stone and Steel Codexes can be repaired with Craft tests.
\item \textbf{Function:} A Steel Codex can serve as a weapon. A Stone Codex can serve as a shield or tool. A Flesh Codex is always present but offers no secondary function.
\item \textbf{Sentience:} A Steel Codex may be sentient (see optional rules). Sentient Codexes speak, remember, and witness—but they have opinions.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Cultural Variations}

The same Codex form may appear differently across cultures:

\begin{itemize}
\item An \textbf{Aeler} Flesh Codex might be geometric brands, precise and angular.
\item A \textbf{Ykrul} Flesh Codex might be scarification in the shape of wolf tracks or storm clouds.
\item A \textbf{Sekogo} Stone Codex might be a carved leopard tooth, not a shield.
\item A \textbf{Lethai} Steel Codex might be a silk-wrapped blade, not bare metal.
\end{itemize}

Work with your GM to design a Codex that reflects your character's people, Patron, and personal covenant.
