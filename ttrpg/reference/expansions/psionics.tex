\section*{Psionics: The Mind Ascendant}

In the shadowed courts and ancient ruins where Fate's Edge adventurers tread, power comes in many forms. Some bargain with Patrons, others weave elemental forces, and still others summon spirits from beyond. But there exists a subtler path—one that requires no oaths, no incantations, no material components. This is the path of Psionics, where the disciplined mind becomes the greatest tool of all.

Psionics represents the evolution of human potential, the unlocking of abilities that science claims impossible and magic struggles to explain. Where a warlock might call lightning from the sky, a psion shapes reality through the sheer force of thought. Where a mage might scry through mystical mirrors, a psion reaches across space with their consciousness alone.

But this power comes at a cost. The mind that bends reality to its will also bears the scars of that bending. Mental Fatigue becomes as real a threat as physical exhaustion, and the constant strain of psychic activity can fracture even the strongest psyche.

Based on the playtest experience, here are the key revisions I'd recommend for the Psionics module:

\section{Core Mechanics}

\subsection{Mental Fatigue Track}
Your \emph{Mental Fatigue} represents capacity for sustained psychic exertion.

\begin{center}
\textbf{Mental Fatigue $=$ Spirit} \quad (minimum 2)
\end{center}

Mark and clear Mental Fatigue as effects specify. When you \emph{cannot} pay a Mental Fatigue cost, choose:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Convert the unpaid point to \textbf{+2 Fatigue} (standard system), or
  \item Convert the unpaid point to \textbf{+1 Harm (Stress)}.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Overflow:} If you ever exceed your Mental Fatigue, immediately suffer \textbf{+1 Harm (Stress)} and set Mental Fatigue to your maximum (no further over-marking this scene).

\subsection{Psionics Skill}
\textbf{Psionics (0–5)} gates access to psionic Arts and sets control/efficiency. You cannot use psionic Arts without investing in this skill.

\subsection{Resolution and Attribute Keys}
Psionics uses \textbf{Attribute (per Art) $+$ Psionics vs.\ DV}. To encourage specialisation, each Art has a \emph{Key Attribute}. Using a non-key attribute for that Art imposes \textbf{+1 DV} (or $-1$ die, GM's choice).

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.1}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Art} & \textbf{Key Attribute} & \textbf{Alternate (with +1 DV)} \\
\midrule
Telekinesis & Spirit (force) & Wits (finesse/precision) \\
Telepathy & Wits & Presence (emotive broadcast) \\
Clairvoyance & Wits & Spirit (ritual trance) \\
Biofeedback & Spirit & Wits (clinical self-hack) \\
Astral Projection & Spirit & Wits (tethered skim) \\
Psychic Assault & Spirit (overwhelm) & Wits (needle, bypass) \\
Mind Shield & Wits (filter) & Spirit (soak) \\
Empathic Manipulation & Presence & Wits (cold read leverage) \\
Precognition & Spirit & Wits (tactical glimpses) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

DV is set by the GM (typical 2–5+) from scope, risk, and opposition.

\section{Psionic Consequence System}

Psionics interfaces with existing \textbf{Fatigue/Harm}. Use \emph{Mental Fatigue} as the psion-only resource; use \emph{Fatigue/Harm} for spillover consequences.

\subsection{Psionic Strain (simple thresholds)}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Mark Fatigue when:} DV~4+ effects, engaging 3+ foes with psionics in one exchange, or maintaining an effect across 2+ scenes.
  \item \textbf{At 2 Fatigue:} $-1$ die to psionic rolls.
  \item \textbf{At 4 Fatigue:} choose one:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Surge:} One psionic use this scene ignores \emph{Mental Fatigue} cost; then mark \textbf{+1 Harm} and generate \textbf{+1 SB}.
    \item \textbf{Efficiency:} Halve all \emph{Mental Fatigue} costs for the next scene (round up).
    \item \textbf{Reset:} Clear all Fatigue; you cannot use psionics for one scene and mark \textbf{+1 Harm}.
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\section{Psionic Enhancements}
\begin{description}
  \item[Psionic Surge (1/scene):] Spend \textbf{3 Mental Fatigue} to gain \textbf{+1 Effect} on a psionic action.
  \item[Mental Breakthrough:] At \textbf{4+ Fatigue}, you may Push a psionic effect \textbf{+1 step}; mark \textbf{+2 Harm} and generate \textbf{+2 SB}.
  \item[Psionic Meditation:] During any rest, recover \textbf{+1 Mental Fatigue} (in addition to normal recovery).
\end{description}

\section{Psionic Arts}

\subsection{Telekinesis}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Spirit $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Wits $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV} for fine manipulation). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action; sustain by fiction. \quad
\textbf{Range:} \textit{Near} (add \textbf{+1 DV} for \textit{Far}). \\
\textbf{Cost:} Activate \textbf{1 Mental Fatigue}; sustained lifts/holds \textbf{1 Mental Fatigue/scene}. \\
\textbf{SB:} Scales with mass/force:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Light (DV 2–3):} cups, tools, doors. \emph{No SB.}
  \item \textbf{Medium (DV 4):} furniture, a person’s mass, riding debris. \emph{+1 SB on Partial/Miss.}
  \item \textbf{Heavy (DV 5+):} carts, statues, boulders, multi-target shoves. \emph{+2 SB on Partial/Miss; +1 SB on Success.}
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Mechanical Benefits:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Hurl objects as weapons (Harm by fiction and force tier).
  \item Shape cover: lift slabs, hold doors, create transient \emph{Walls/Barriers}.
  \item Remote tasks: pull levers, pick up keys, disarm simple traps at range.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Limits/Notes:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Cannot \emph{directly} crush or twist living tissue; injury is \emph{environmental} (falling, debris, impact) at GM discretion.
  \item Complex, delicate work (lockpicks, surgery) typically requires the \textbf{Alternate} attribute (Wits) and adds \textbf{+1 DV}.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Examples:} Nudge a goblet off a shelf (DV~2); rip a door from hinges (DV~4); wrench a stone pillar sideways (DV~5).

% ------------------------------------

\subsection{Telepathy}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Wits $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Presence $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, emotive broadcast style). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action; sustain by fiction. \quad
\textbf{Range/Line:} \textit{Near}, LoS (\textbf{+1 DV} for \textit{Far}; \textbf{+1 DV} without LoS if you have a strong link). \\
\textbf{Targets:} One (add \textbf{+1 DV} per extra target this scene). \\
\textbf{Design Rule:} Telepathy never replaces social skills—it provides \emph{information and set-up}, not compliance.

\paragraph*{Depths \& SB}
Choose a depth before rolling:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Surface Thoughts (DV 2):} current focus. \emph{No SB} on any result \emph{the first time per target/scene}. Repeats: \textbf{+1 DV}; on Miss, \textbf{+1 SB}.
  \item \textbf{Emotional State (DV 3):} tones/urges. \emph{+1 SB} on Partial/Miss.
  \item \textbf{Deep Memories (DV 5):} episodic recall. \emph{+1 SB} on Success; \emph{+2 SB} on Partial/Miss.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph*{Outcomes}
\begin{description}
  \item[Success:] Gain the info; pick \emph{one} set-up and use it by the end of your next turn this scene:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Angle of Approach:} \(\,+1\) die to your next \emph{Insight/Sense Motive} vs.\ this target.
    \item \textbf{Read the Room:} \textbf{Position +1} on your next social roll vs.\ this target.
    \item \textbf{Pointed Question:} Ask one focused question they could presently answer; GM replies as impressions.
  \end{itemize}
  \item[Partial:] Blurred fragments; mark SB for depth.
  \item[Miss:] No useful read; mark SB for depth (if any); the target feels the brush.
\end{description}

\paragraph*{Defense \& Counterplay}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Unwilling Resist:} target rolls Spirit $+$ Resolve; on meet/beat, your read fails and you mark \textbf{+1 SB} (\textbf{+2 SB} if Deep). Margin \(2+\): they also intuit your intent.
  \item \textbf{Wards/Noise:} \texttt{[WARD]}s, mental static, or anti-psionic training impose \textbf{+1–2 DV} (training at least \textbf{+1 DV}).
  \item \textbf{Consent:} willing targets are \textbf{DV–1} and cause no SB.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Non-Substituting Benefits:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Silent Speech (willing only):} short thoughts in \textit{Near} (group size up to your \textbf{Wits}); no SB.
  \item \textbf{Sense Dissonance:} if you read \emph{Surface} this scene, \(\,+1\) die on \emph{Wits + Insight} to vet a lie; strong hit reveals \emph{which part} conflicts.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Notes:} Reading unwilling minds in a formal social scene risks fallout; if detected, \textbf{+1 SB} even on Success.

\subsection{Clairvoyance}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Wits $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Spirit $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, trance method). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action; ritual scry may take a scene. \quad
\textbf{Range:} Scene/locale by fiction; add \textbf{+1 DV} per range band beyond \textit{Near}. \\
\textbf{Cost:} DV 2–3 = \textbf{1 Mental Fatigue}; DV 4 = \textbf{2}; DV 5+ = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{SB:} \textit{Near} none • \textit{Distant} \textbf{+1 SB} on Partial/Miss • \textit{Complex Scry} \textbf{+2 SB} on Partial/Miss; \textbf{+1 SB} on Success.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Uses:} scout ahead, pierce \texttt{[VEIL]}, track signatures, read recent traces.
  \item \textbf{Notes:} Covert spying on private moments generates \textbf{+1 SB}. Each extra scene sustained adds \textbf{+1 SB}.
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------

\subsection{Biofeedback}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Spirit $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Wits $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, clinical technique). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action (Major work takes a focused scene). \\
\textbf{SB:} by intensity:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Minor (DV 2):} stabilize, close, purge shock. \emph{No SB.} Heal up to \textbf{Psionics} levels/day (cap 1 Harm per recipient/day).
  \item \textbf{Moderate (DV 3):} \emph{+1 SB} on Partial/Miss; heal up to \textbf{Psionics} (max 2/day).
  \item \textbf{Major (DV 4+):} \emph{+2 SB} on Partial/Miss; \emph{+1 SB} on Success; heal up to \textbf{Psionics} (max 1/day); mark \textbf{+1 Fatigue}.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Perks:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Convert \textbf{1 Harm} $\leftrightarrow$ \textbf{1 Mental Fatigue} (up to \textbf{Psionics}, max 3/day).
  \item Brief enhancement: \textbf{+1 die} to physical rolls for a few moments.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Limits/Notes:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Cannot heal Harm above your \textbf{Psionics} rating; active combat imposes \textbf{Position –1}.
  \item Major healing requires a quiet scene.
  \item Healing or enhancing \emph{others} requires consent and generates \textbf{+1 SB}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Astral Projection}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Spirit $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Wits $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, skim tether). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action to slip; travel by fiction. \quad
\textbf{Cost:} \textbf{1 Mental Fatigue/scene}. \\
\textbf{SB:} \textbf{+1} per band beyond \textit{Near}; \textbf{+1} per additional scene sustained.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Benefits:} pass barriers, scout hazards, converse across distance.
  \item \textbf{Vulnerabilities:} body is \textbf{Helpless}; non-physical contests accrue SB; astral Harm echoes to flesh; wards add \textbf{+2 DV}.
  \item \textbf{Risk:} extended ($>3$ scenes) projection courts separation (GM consequence); astral death = physical death.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psychic Assault}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Spirit $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Wits $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, needle-strike). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action. \quad
\textbf{Cost:} DV 2–3 = \textbf{1 Mental Fatigue}; DV 4 = \textbf{2}; DV 5+ = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{SB:} always \textbf{+1} (disruptive) plus DV 4 \textbf{+1} and DV 5+ \textbf{+2} more.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Benefits:} mental Harm; bypasses physical armor; target specific faculties (sight, balance, focus) by fiction.
  \item \textbf{Notes:} cannot be “willingly” received; high-Spirit foes may add \textbf{+1 DV}; use in sensitive locales may carry legal or social fallout.
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------

\subsection{Mind Shield}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Wits $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Spirit $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, soak-and-ground). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action (passive modes by fiction). \\
\textbf{SB:} Passive none; \emph{per effect blocked} \textbf{+1 SB}; \emph{Area} \textbf{+2 SB} and mark \textbf{+2 Fatigue}.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Benefits:} resist intrusion/telepathy; shelter allies; create dampened zones.
  \item \textbf{Paradox:} protecting \emph{others} costs \textbf{2$\times$ Mental Fatigue}.
  \item \textbf{Notes:} \emph{Area} protection covers all within \textit{Near} (friends and foes); cannot be maintained while unconscious.
\end{itemize}

% ------------------------------------

\subsection{Empathic Manipulation}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Presence $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Wits $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, cold-read leverage). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action; sustain by fiction. \\
\textbf{SB:} by intensity:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Subtle (DV 2–3):} nudge mood, steady hands. \emph{No SB.}
  \item \textbf{Moderate (DV 4):} calm rage, spark courage. \emph{+1 SB on Partial/Miss.}
  \item \textbf{Strong (DV 5+):} sway a crowd’s tenor. \emph{+2 SB on Partial/Miss; +1 SB on Success.}
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Benefits:} improve social \textbf{Position +1}; pacify beasts; inspire/demoralize groups. \\
\textbf{Risks/Notes:} You \emph{feel} what you project; same target not more than once per scene; attempts that oppose core values are \textbf{+2 DV}; affecting a crowd always generates \textbf{+1 SB}.

% ------------------------------------

\subsection{Precognition}
\textbf{Key Attribute:} Spirit $+$ Psionics \quad
\textbf{Alternate:} Wits $+$ Psionics (\textbf{+1 DV}, tactical glimpse). \\
\textbf{Action:} 1 action; deeper visions may take a scene. \\
\textbf{Cost:} DV 2–3 = \textbf{1 Mental Fatigue}; DV 4 = \textbf{2}; DV 5+ = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{SB (inherent):} Minor \textbf{+1} • Major (DV 4) \textbf{+2} • Detailed (DV 5+) \textbf{+3}.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Benefits:} advantage on future rolls (scope by GM), avoid named dangers, compare probable branches.
  \item \textbf{Paradox — Future Fixation:} until Downtime (or a defiant choice), lose \textbf{1 Boon/session}; actions that resist the shown path may be \textbf{Desperate} by GM call.
  \item \textbf{Notes:} No “pure randomness” predictions (e.g., dice, lots) without \textbf{+3 DV}; visions of death/calamity add \textbf{+1 SB}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Base Costs}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Activation:} 1 Mental Fatigue
    \item \textbf{Maintenance:} 1 Mental Fatigue per Scene/Significant Time
    \item \textbf{Skill Requirement:} Must invest XP in Psionics skill

\section*{Psionic Talents}

\subsection{Minor Talents (2-4 XP)}

\textbf{Mental Fortress (3 XP)} \\
Requirements: Psionics 2+, Spirit 3+ \\
Effect: +1 die to resist psychic effects; once per scene, convert incoming psychic Harm to Fatigue

\textbf{Thought Thief (4 XP)} \\
Requirements: Psionics 2+, Wits 3+, Telepathy \\
Effect: When reading surface thoughts, gain +1 die; once per scene, detect lies automatically

\textbf{Psychic Reservoir (4 XP)} \\
Requirements: Psionics 2+, Spirit 2+ \\
Effect: Increase Mental Fatigue track by +2; recover 1 additional Mental Fatigue during rest

\textbf{Revised Note:} All Minor Talents now have a prerequisite of at least 1 other psionic talent, encouraging specialization.

\subsection{Major Talents (6-8 XP)}

\textbf{Telekinetic Mastery (7 XP)} \\
Requirements: Psionics 3+, Spirit 3+, Telekinesis \\
Effect: +1 die to Telekinesis attacks; can lift objects one category heavier without increasing DV

\textbf{Mind Walker (8 XP)} \\
Requirements: Psionics 3+, Wits 3+, Astral Projection \\
Effect: Astral Projection requires no concentration; move at normal speed in astral form; reduce projection cost to 1/2 Mental Fatigue (round up)

\textbf{Psychic Vampire (8 XP)} \\
Requirements: Psionics 3+, Spirit 3+, Psychic Assault \\
Effect: When dealing psychic Harm, heal 1 Mental Fatigue; once per scene, drain 1 Mental Fatigue from target (requires successful Psychic Assault)

\subsection{Prestige Talents (12+ XP)}

\textbf{Master of Minds (12 XP)} \\
Requirements: Psionics 4+, Wits 4+, Telepathy, Empathic Manipulation \\
Effect: +2 dice to all telepathic effects; can communicate with entire group simultaneously; once per scene, impose emotional state on group (DV 4, mark 2 Fatigue)

\textbf{Mind Over Body (12 XP)} \\
Requirements:} Psionics 4+, Spirit 4+, Tier III+ \\[2pt]
Effect: Your Mental Fatigue track becomes \textbf{Spirit $+$ Wits} 
(\emph{min} 6, \emph{cap} 12). Once per scene, when you would suffer Mental Fatigue \emph{overflow}, you may instead convert each overflow point into \textbf{+1 Fatigue} (standard track), with no per-use limit. \\[2pt]
Paradox: Mind eclipses flesh. When reduced to 0 Harm, you may remain conscious at \textbf{Helpless} until your next turn; then mark \textbf{+2 Fatigue} and \textbf{+1 SB}. \\[2pt]

\subsection*{Reality Bender}
\label{talent:reality-bender}
\index{Talents!Reality Bender}\index{Psionics!Reality Bender}

\paragraph*{Type} Major Talent (15 XP)

\paragraph*{Requirements} \textbf{Psionics 5+}, \textbf{Spirit 4+}, \textbf{Telekinesis}, \textbf{Psychic Assault}, \textbf{Mind Shield}.

\paragraph*{Test} \emph{Wits + Psionics} vs.\ \textbf{DV} (per option below).

\paragraph*{Effect} Once per session, reshape local reality within \emph{Near} range. Choose one:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Minor Alteration (DV 3):} Change one obvious physical property (color, temperature, texture, sound) of a target within \emph{Close} range, or create a simple illusory effect. \emph{Lasts for the scene.}
  \item \textbf{Moderate Alteration (DV 4):} Alter the structural properties of a \emph{Medium} or smaller object (e.g., make glass flexible, metal brittle, water viscous) for \emph{one exchange}, or create a complex illusion that includes movement and sound.
  \item \textbf{Major Alteration (DV 5):} Temporarily defy a basic law of physics within \emph{Close} range for \emph{one exchange} (e.g., reverse gravity in a small area, stop time for a single object, make fire cold).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph*{Costs} Mark \textbf{1 Harm} and \textbf{1 Fatigue}; generate \textbf{1 SB}.

\paragraph*{Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Cannot create matter from nothing; you must transform or rephase existing phenomena.
  \item Cannot directly harm creatures (no direct damage; secondary environmental effects at the GM's discretion).
  \item Cannot duplicate other magic paths' signature effects exactly (adjacent expressions at the GM's discretion).
  \item Cannot affect unwilling targets' minds or souls.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph*{Examples} Reverse gravity in a small area; make stone flow like water; create a bridge of force; turn sound to color; make shadows solid.

\textbf{Revised Note:} Reality Bender now generates additional SB based on alteration severity: Minor (+1 SB), Moderate (+2 SB), Major (+3 SB). Use in populated areas generates additional 1 SB.

\section*{Anti-Psionic Effects}

Psionic characters are vulnerable to specific countermeasures that use existing mechanics:

\textbf{Psionic Dampening [WARD]} \\
DV = Psionics skill; Hit: impose -1 die to psionic rolls; Partial: -1 Effect

\textbf{Mental Null Zone} \\
Area effect that prevents psionic use; resisted by Spirit + Psionics

\textbf{Psychic Disruption [BANISH]} \\
Target a psionic effect; DV by fiction; Hit: end effect and mark 1 Fatigue

\textbf{Anti-Psionic Training} \\
Some NPCs get +1 die against psionic effects due to specialized training

\textbf{Revised Note:} Anti-psionic effects are more common in areas with strong magical or technological presence. Professional investigators and security personnel often have basic anti-psionic training.

\section*{Balance Adjustments Summary}

\subsection*{Telepathy}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Limited to once per target per scene for surface thoughts
    \item Increased DV for invasive effects (3-5)
    \item Unwilling targets get +2 DV bonus
    \item Social consequences for obvious use
    \item Legal implications in civilized areas
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Biofeedback}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Daily limits on healing (1-3 conversions per day based on severity)
    \item Major healing limited to 1 per day and marks Fatigue
    \item Requires concentration for serious healing
    \item Cannot be used freely in combat
    \item Consent required for use on others
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Psychic Fatigue}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Reset causes 1 Harm
    \item Surge now costs 3 Mental Fatigue instead of free
    \item Surge generates additional SB
    \item Integrates with existing Fatigue system
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{General}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Integrated with existing Fatigue/Harm system
    \item Uses Story Beats for consequences
    \item Added psionic-specific Talents for advancement
    \item Anti-psionic effects use existing tag system
    \item Maintains parity with other magic paths
    \item Clear talent prerequisite chains
    \item Scaled Fatigue costs for arts
    \item Flexible maintenance costs
    \item Legal and social consequences
\end{itemize}

\section*{Additional Psionic Complications}

\subsection*{Social Integration Issues}
Psionic characters may face prejudice or fear from non-psionic individuals. In some cultures, psionic abilities are viewed with suspicion or regulated by law.

\subsection*{Mental Scars}
Intense psionic use can leave lasting psychological effects. Characters may develop phobias, compulsions, or other mental conditions related to their abilities.

\subsection*{Psionic Signatures}
Powerful psionic use can leave detectable traces that skilled investigators or other psions can track. This creates opportunities for pursuit or identification.

\subsection*{Reality Bleed}
Extensive use of reality-altering abilities may cause minor inconsistencies in the local fabric of reality, creating unpredictable effects that generate Story Beats.

\section*{Psionic Investigation Procedures}

\subsection*{Evidence Handling}
Psionic evidence requires special handling and verification. Mental impressions must be corroborated with physical evidence in most legal systems.

\subsection*{Chain of Custody}
Just as physical evidence requires careful handling, mental evidence must maintain "psychic chain of custody" through proper documentation and witness verification.

\subsection*{Cross-Cultural Considerations}
Different cultures have varying attitudes toward psionic investigation techniques, affecting their admissibility and effectiveness in different jurisdictions.

\section*{Psionic Society}

\subsection{The Silent Orders}

Psionic practitioners rarely form large, public organizations. Instead, they gather in small, secretive conclaves known as Silent Orders. These groups share techniques, warn of dangers, and maintain the delicate balance between using their powers and being consumed by them.

\subsection{The Psionic Underground}

In many regions, psionic abilities are feared or outlawed. Practitioners must hide their talents or face persecution. This has led to the development of the Psionic Underground—a network of safe houses, coded signals, and false identities that allow psions to move freely while concealing their true nature.

\subsection{Psionic Artifacts}

Objects that have been exposed to strong psionic energies can retain psychic imprints. These artifacts might enhance psionic abilities, store mental energy, or even house fragments of consciousness. However, they often carry the mental scars of their previous users.

\section*{GM Guidance}

\subsection{Introducing Psionics}

Psionics should feel rare and mysterious. Not every NPC should have psychic abilities, and when they do, it should be notable. Consider making psionic practitioners:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Members of secretive orders
    \item Individuals who have undergone specific training or trauma
    \item People with unique genetic or spiritual heritage
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Balancing Psionic Power}

Psionics can be incredibly versatile. To maintain balance:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Emphasize the consequence tracks and SB generation
    \item Use Story Beats liberally for psychic activities
    \item Introduce psionic-specific threats (psychic predators, mental parasites)
    \item Remember that Mental Fatigue overflow leads to real Harm
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psionic Antagonists}

Enemies with psionic abilities present unique challenges:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item They can bypass physical defenses
    \item They might know the PCs' plans before they're enacted
    \item They can turn the PCs' emotions against them
    \item They leave no physical evidence of their crimes
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psionic Encounter Design}

When designing encounters for psionic characters:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Include psychic dampening fields (reduce Psionics dice)
    \item Feature enemies with Mind Shield or psychic immunity
    \item Create scenarios where psionic use attracts unwanted attention
    \item Design social encounters where telepathy reveals hidden agendas
\end{itemize}

\section*{Character Options}

\subsection{Psionic Talents}

Characters can invest XP in psionic-specific Talents:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Psionic Resilience (3 XP):} +1 to Mental Fatigue track
    \item \textbf{Focused Mind (4 XP):} Reduce SB generation from psionic use by 1 (minimum 1)
    \item \textbf{Psychic Synergy (5 XP):} When assisting another psion, both gain +1 die
    \item \textbf{Mental Fortress (6 XP):} Immune to non-magical psychic intrusion
    \item \textbf{Expanded Resonance (4 XP):} Mental Resonance clock increases to [6]
    \item \textbf{Echo Dampener (3 XP):} Reduce Echo Traces generation by 1 (minimum 1)
    \item \textbf{Contamination Control (4 XP):} Psychic Contamination clock increases to [8]
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psionic Complications}

Consider these Complications for psionic characters:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Uncontrolled Talent:} Your powers activate involuntarily under stress
    \item \textbf{Psychic Mark:} You're known to psionic hunters or organizations
    \item \textbf{Mental Parasite:} A fragment of another consciousness shares your mind
    \item \textbf{Empathic Overflow:} You constantly feel others' emotions, making social interaction difficult
    \item \textbf{Temporal Sensitivity:} You occasionally glimpse possible futures, generating Story Beats
\end{itemize}

\section*{Comparison to Other Paths}

\subsection{Psionics vs. Cantor's Path}
Both represent internalized power, but:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Psionics:} No Corruption Clock, instant effects, diverse Arts, consequence tracks
    \item \textbf{Cantor:} Corruption progression, delayed effects, mimics existing Rites, Push It mechanic
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psionics vs. Runekeeper}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Psionics:} Autonomous, Mental Fatigue resource, SB/consequence focus
    \item \textbf{Runekeeper:} Patron-dependent, Obligation resource, Push It for power
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psionics vs. Freeform Caster}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Psionics:} Defined Arts with unique mechanics, single roll activation, consequence tracks
    \item \textbf{Caster:} Elemental flexibility, Weave+Cast requirement, Backlash risk, freeform effects
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psionics vs. Invoker}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Psionics:} Instant effects, personal resource management, consequence tracks
    \item \textbf{Invoker:} Ritual preparation, Symbol maintenance, Crack the Seal for emergencies
\end{itemize}

\section*{Conclusion: The Mind's Edge}

Psionics in Fate's Edge represents the ultimate expression of human potential—and its greatest peril. The disciplined mind that can move mountains with thought can also be shattered by the weight of that responsibility. Every psionic practitioner walks a razor's edge between godlike power and complete mental collapse.

With streamlined consequence tracks and clear mechanical benefits for each Art, this final revision provides a balanced and playable psionic system. The focus on Story Beat generation and centralized consequence management maintains the flow of Fate's Edge while providing the unique mechanical identity psionics deserves.

This extension offers tools for players who want to explore the depths of mental discipline, and for GMs who wish to introduce subtle, mind-bending threats to their campaigns. Remember that psionics is not just another form of magic—it is the manipulation of reality itself through the power of consciousness.

Use these rules to tell stories where the greatest battles are fought in the space between thoughts, where the most dangerous weapons cannot be seen, and where the line between hero and monster is drawn in the architecture of the mind itself.

The future belongs to those who can shape it with their thoughts alone. Will you be among them?

\section{The Great Silence: Psionic History}

In the ancient past, before the rise of the Aeler holds and the establishment of the first Ykrul moots, the world experienced what scholars now call the Great Silence—a period when psionic abilities across all peoples were dramatically altered, suppressed, or transformed. This event, which lasted for what contemporary accounts describe as "three turnings of the great wheel" (approximately 200-300 years), fundamentally changed how minds could touch minds across the world.

\subsection{The Silence Event}

The Great Silence did not eliminate psionic abilities entirely, but rather \textit{compressed} them—making mental powers more difficult to access, more costly to maintain, and more dangerous to use. During this period, what had once been natural gifts became rare talents that required intense training and came with significant risks.

Archaeological evidence from this period shows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Abandoned psionic academies across multiple cultures
    \item Sealed mental archives that were too dangerous to access
    \item Artifacts designed to \textit{suppress} rather than enhance mental abilities
    \item Records of "mind-storms" where uncontrolled psionic energy caused physical and social chaos
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Resonance Scars}

Certain locations still bear the marks of the Great Silence, creating what scholars term "Resonance Scars"—areas where psionic abilities function differently due to the lingering effects of that ancient suppression:

\subsubsection{The Whispering Vaults (Aeler Holds)}
Deep within the oldest Aeler underways, certain chambers amplify mental fatigue by 2 segments but also grant +1 Effect to telepathic abilities. These spaces are both coveted and feared by Aeler psions, who must weigh the enhanced abilities against the increased mental cost.

\subsubsection{The Empty Circle (Violet Steppe)}
A circular depression in the heart of Ykrul territory where no psionic abilities function at all. Ykrul oral history claims this was once the site of the "Last Conclave"—where the greatest psions of their ancestors attempted to end the Silence but instead created a permanent null zone.

\subsubsection{The Singing Stones (Lethai Valewood)}
Ancient standing stones that resonate with psionic energy, allowing for enhanced telepathic communication across vast distances—but only when the stones themselves are "tuned" through proper context and ceremony.

\subsection{Awakening Cycles}

Since the end of the Great Silence, psionic abilities have slowly returned in what scholars recognize as distinct \textit{Awakening Cycles}. These periods of increased psionic activity last 50-75 years and are separated by longer periods of relative dormancy:

\begin{description}
    \item[First Awakening (Current Cycle, 150 years ago - present)] Marked by the gradual return of basic telepathic and telekinetic abilities, particularly among younger generations.
    
    \item[Second Awakening (400-325 years ago)] The period when precognition and biofeedback abilities first reappeared, leading to the establishment of many current psionic traditions.
    
    \item[Third Awakening (650-575 years ago)] The mysterious period when astral projection and empathic manipulation returned, though records from this time are fragmentary.
\end{description}

Each Awakening Cycle brings not just the return of abilities, but the transformation of how those abilities function. Modern psions often find that their powers work differently from historical accounts, suggesting that each cycle fundamentally reshapes the nature of psionic interaction.

\subsection{Cultural Memory and the Silence}

Different cultures retain distinct memories of the Great Silence, each reflecting their unique relationship with mental abilities:

\subsubsection{Aeler Records: The Accounting}
Aeler historical texts describe the Silence as a "misalignment of the mental ledgers"—where the balance between mind and world became corrupted. They maintain that the current Awakening Cycle requires careful "mental accounting" to prevent another collapse.

\subsubsection{Ykrul Oral Tradition: The Broken Paths}
Ykrul stories speak of the Silence as a time when "all routes between minds became blocked," making their Kon'reh-based approach to mental interaction impossible. They believe the current cycle represents a "reweaving" of those mental paths.

\subsubsection{Lethai Codices: The Lost Context}
Lethai records suggest that during the Silence, the context-saturated nature of their communication was temporarily severed, making their gift-based society impossible. They view the current Awakening as a "recontextualization" of mental abilities.

\subsection{Modern Implications}

The Great Silence has several ongoing effects on psionic practice in the present day:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Generational Differences:} Older psions often have different abilities and limitations compared to those born during the current Awakening Cycle.
    
    \item \textbf{Cultural Tensions:} Different peoples have varying levels of comfort with psionic abilities based on their historical relationship with the Silence.
    
    \item \textbf{Scholarly Interest:} The study of Resonance Scars and Awakening Cycles has become a major field of research, with implications for understanding the fundamental nature of consciousness itself.
    
    \item \textbf{Prophetic Concerns:} Some scholars worry that the current Awakening Cycle may be building toward another Silence event, creating tension between those who want to preserve psionic abilities and those who fear their consequences.
\end{enumerate}

The Great Silence serves as a reminder that psionic abilities are not static—they are part of a dynamic system that has shaped and been shaped by the history of the world. For psionic characters, this creates both opportunities and dangers, as they may be part of a new transformation in how minds can touch minds across the Fate's Edge setting.

\section{The Thought Trade: Psionic Economics}

In the bustling markets of Zakov's free ports and the ancient bazaars of Silkstrand, a new form of commerce has emerged that challenges traditional notions of value and exchange. The Thought Trade represents the economic system that has grown around psionic abilities, where mental services, experiences, and even raw consciousness itself have become commodities with measurable worth.

\subsection{Mental Labor Markets}

Just as physical labor has wages and specialized skills, psionic abilities have created entirely new categories of mental labor that command premium prices across all social strata:

\subsubsection{Memory Merchants}
Telepaths who specialize in memory storage and retrieval have become invaluable to merchants, scholars, and nobles who need to preserve sensitive information without physical records. The going rate for one hour of secure memory storage is equivalent to a skilled craftsman's daily wage, but the risk premium for storing particularly dangerous or controversial memories can increase the price tenfold.

\textbf{Market Integration:} In cities like Ecktoria and Thepyrgos, Memory Guilds have established standardized contracts that specify the exact nature of memories to be stored, the duration of storage, and the conditions under which memories can be retrieved. Breaking a memory storage contract is considered a serious offense, often resulting in both legal penalties and social ostracism that can last for generations.

\subsubsection{Calculation Circles}
Psions with enhanced mathematical abilities have formed Calculation Circles that serve as living computers for complex engineering, navigation, and financial projects. These psions can perform calculations that would take traditional mathematicians months in a matter of hours, but the mental strain often requires significant recovery time.

\textbf{Economic Impact:} The rise of Calculation Circles has dramatically reduced the time and cost of large-scale projects, from Aeler bridge construction to naval navigation for the Amaranthine Sea fleets. However, it has also created a dependency on psionic labor that some traditional craftsmen view as a threat to their livelihoods.

\subsubsection{Emotional Architects}
Empathic manipulators who specialize in mood and atmosphere control have found employment in everything from political rallies to theatrical performances. Their ability to subtly influence crowd emotions has made them highly sought after by politicians, merchants, and entertainers.


\textbf{Regulatory Response:} Several city-states have begun implementing "Mood Licensing" requirements for Emotional Architects, particularly when their services are used in political or commercial contexts. Violations can result in both financial penalties and temporary suspension of psionic practice licenses.

\subsection{Thought Currency Systems}

The most revolutionary aspect of the Thought Trade is the development of systems where mental experiences themselves function as currency:

\subsubsection{Experience Exchange}
In certain underground markets, particularly in Free Havens like Sable Key, psions can trade raw experiences—memories of adventure, intense emotions, or unique sensations—as direct equivalents to coin. A memory of a dragon flight might be worth fifty silver pieces, while the experience of true love might be valued at several gold coins.

\textbf{Market Dynamics:} The value of experiences fluctuates based on rarity, intensity, and cultural significance. Memories of historical events, first contact with supernatural entities, or particularly intense emotional experiences command premium prices. However, the market is also subject to fraud—false or artificially enhanced memories can be difficult to detect and have led to the development of specialized "Memory Authenticators."

\subsubsection{Skill Debt}
Psions can enter into "Skill Debt" arrangements where they agree to provide specific mental services over time in exchange for immediate payment or favors. This has created a complex system of mental credit and obligation that operates alongside traditional economic systems.

\textbf{Contract Law:} The legal frameworks around Skill Debt vary significantly between jurisdictions. In Aeler holds, such contracts are treated with the same seriousness as infrastructure agreements, with detailed terms and severe penalties for breach. In more chaotic environments like Free Havens, Skill Debt arrangements may be more informal but carry higher risks for both parties.

\subsection{Psionic Guilds and Economic Power}

The economic importance of psionic abilities has led to the formation of powerful guilds that control various aspects of the Thought Trade:

\subsubsection{The Memory Merchants' Consortium}
Operating primarily in the major port cities, this guild controls the legitimate market for memory storage and retrieval services. They maintain strict quality standards, detailed record-keeping systems, and significant political influence in cities where information security is paramount.

\textbf{Guild Benefits:} Members receive access to secure storage facilities, legal protection for their services, and networking opportunities with high-value clients. However, they must also adhere to strict confidentiality agreements and contribute a percentage of their earnings to guild funds.

\subsubsection{The Calculation Collective}
A loose network of mathematical psions who coordinate on large-scale projects that require multiple specialists. They've developed standardized protocols for collaborative mental computation and maintain a reputation for accuracy that commands premium rates.

\textbf{Collective Structure:} Unlike traditional guilds, the Calculation Collective operates more like a cooperative, with members sharing both risks and rewards. This structure has proven particularly effective for projects that require sustained mental effort from multiple participants.

\subsection{Economic Integration with Other Modules}

The Thought Trade creates natural connections with other aspects of the Fate's Edge economy:

\subsubsection{Caravan Integration}
Psionic couriers who can carry information mentally rather than physically have revolutionized long-distance communication. A telepath can carry the equivalent of an entire library across dangerous terrain without risk of physical loss, but the mental strain limits the duration and frequency of such services.

\textbf{Market Impact:} This has created new trade routes and commercial relationships, as merchants can now conduct business across vast distances with unprecedented speed and security. However, it has also made psionic couriers targets for kidnapping and exploitation.

\subsubsection{Political Intrigue Connections}
The ability to read minds and influence emotions has made psions both valuable assets and dangerous threats in political contexts. Political factions invest heavily in psionic protection (mental shielding) while also seeking to recruit or compromise opposing psions.

\textbf{Diplomatic Protocol:} Many courts have established formal protocols for psionic interactions, including designated "neutral mind spaces" where telepaths can operate without advantage to either side, and strict rules about when and how mental abilities can be used in negotiations.

\subsubsection{Black Banner Applications}
Military organizations have integrated psionic units into their command structures, creating new tactical possibilities and logistical challenges. Psionic reconnaissance, communication, and psychological warfare capabilities have become essential elements of modern military strategy.

\textbf{Military Economics:} The cost of training and maintaining psionic military units is extremely high, but the strategic advantages often justify the investment. This has created a new category of military expenditure and influenced the balance of power between different factions.

\subsection{Social and Cultural Implications}

The Thought Trade has created new social dynamics and cultural practices:

\subsubsection{The Gentry of the Mind}
A new social class has emerged consisting of wealthy individuals who have purchased extensive mental experiences, creating what some call "experiential aristocracy." These individuals often have more diverse and intense life experiences than those who rely solely on physical adventures or traditional education.

\textbf{Social Tension:} This has created friction with traditional nobility and wealthy merchants who view mental experiences as somehow less authentic than physical achievements. However, the practical advantages of extensive mental training and experience have made this tension less significant in practical terms.

\subsubsection{The Privacy Divide}
Society has split between those who have access to mental privacy protection (through anti-psionic training, magical wards, or technological means) and those who do not. This has created new forms of social stratification and economic inequality.

\textbf{Market Response:} Various industries have emerged to serve the privacy protection market, from specialized craftsmen who create anti-telepathic jewelry to legal specialists who navigate the complex regulations around mental privacy rights.

The Thought Trade represents one of the most significant economic innovations in the Fate's Edge setting, demonstrating how psionic abilities can create entirely new categories of value and exchange while also generating new forms of social complexity and economic tension. For players, this system provides numerous opportunities for adventure, from protecting valuable mental cargo to navigating the complex legal and ethical issues that arise when consciousness itself becomes a commodity.

\section{Psionic Investigation Procedures}

The unique capabilities of psionic characters have created new methodologies for investigation and detection that integrate seamlessly with Fate's Edge's existing investigative frameworks. These procedures transform how mysteries are solved while maintaining the core tension between information gathering and consequence management.

\subsection{Mental Detective Work}

Psionic investigators operate within the same framework as traditional investigators but with enhanced capabilities that come with their own risks and limitations. The key is that psionic abilities don't eliminate the need for traditional investigation skills—they amplify and complicate them.

\subsubsection{Memory Reading and Reconstruction}
Telepaths can access witness memories, but this process is far from simple information extraction:

\textbf{Memory Integrity Test:} When reading a witness's memory, the psion must make a Wits + Psionics vs. DV 3-5 (based on memory clarity, emotional trauma, and time elapsed). 
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Success:} Gain Clue +1 and can ask one specific question about the memory's context
    \item \textbf{Partial:} Gain Clue +1 but the memory is fragmented or confused with other events
    \item \textbf{Miss:} Create False Memory [4] - the witness now "remembers" something that isn't true
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Mental Fatigue Cost:} Each memory reading costs 1 Mental Fatigue, plus additional Fatigue equal to the witness's Spirit attribute (representing their mental resistance).

\subsubsection{Deception Detection}
Psionic abilities enhance but don't guarantee the detection of lies:

\textbf{Surface Thoughts Scan:} Wits + Psionics vs. DV 3 to read immediate emotional reactions during questioning.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Success:} Identify general emotional state (truth, deception, fear, confusion)
    \item \textbf{Partial:} Detect emotional inconsistency but cannot determine its source
    \item \textbf{Miss:} GM may spend SB to create False Confidence - the psion becomes convinced of truth when deception exists
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Deep Intent Reading:} Spirit + Psionics vs. DV 4-5 to probe underlying motivations and truthfulness.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Cost:} 2 Mental Fatigue plus target's Presence (if willing) or Spirit (if resistant)
    \item \textbf{Risk:} On miss, tick Psychic Backlash [4] - the target's mental resistance creates lasting strain
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Crime Scene Psionics}

Certain psionic abilities can extract information from crime scenes, but this practice is heavily regulated in most jurisdictions:

\subsubsection{Residual Impression Reading}
Very sensitive telepaths can sometimes read "echoes" left by intense emotional or traumatic events at crime scenes.

\textbf{Procedure:} Wits + Psionics vs. DV 4-6 (based on time elapsed, intensity of emotions, and environmental interference)
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Success:} Gain vivid impression of final moments, potentially identifying perpetrator or victim state
    \item \textbf{Partial:} Fragmented impressions that require interpretation and may be misleading
    \item \textbf{Miss:} GM spends SB to create Psychic Contamination [4] - the psion absorbs traumatic elements that affect future investigations
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Legal Implications:} In most civilized areas, residual impression reading requires:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Official authorization from investigative authorities
    \item Presence of legal witnesses during the procedure
    \item Documentation of all impressions in official records
    \item Potential Psychic Evidence [6] clock that tracks admissibility in court
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Psionic Evidence and Legal Complications}

The introduction of mental evidence has created significant legal and ethical challenges across different jurisdictions:

\subsubsection{The Admissibility Problem}
Most legal systems struggle with how to treat information obtained through psionic means:

\textbf{Aeler Courts:} Require dual testimony - both the psion's interpretation and traditional evidence must support conclusions. Mental evidence alone is inadmissible.

\textbf{Lethai Moots:} Accept mental evidence when proper context witnesses can verify the psion's reading, but require extensive documentation of the context keys used.

\textbf{Ykrul Bowl Proceedings:} Mental evidence is acceptable if obtained without deception and with the subject's implied consent (no explicit refusal).

\textbf{Free Haven Practice:} Generally more permissive but require the psion to testify about their methods and potential for error.

\subsubsection{Chain of Custody for Mental Evidence}
Just as physical evidence requires careful handling, mental evidence must maintain "psychic chain of custody":

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Initial Reading:} Documented with time, location, and witness presence
    \item \textbf{Storage:} Mental impressions must be either immediately recorded or maintained in the psion's memory with regular verification
    \item \textbf{Transfer:} If transferring evidence to another psion, both must witness the transfer and document any changes
    \item \textbf{Presentation:} In court, the psion must recreate the evidence from memory, with potential for Mental Fatigue costs
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Investigation Integration with Other Modules}

Psionic investigation procedures naturally connect with other Fate's Edge systems:

\subsubsection{Political Intrigue Integration}
In political investigations, psionic abilities create both opportunities and risks:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reading political opponents' true intentions
    \item Detecting treason or hidden loyalties
    \item Risk of political retaliation if discovered
    \item Potential for creating false evidence through Psychic Manipulation [4] clocks
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Horror Campaigns Enhancement}
Psionic investigation in horror settings amplifies both discovery and danger:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reading the memories of victims or witnesses increases Dread Clock segments
    \item Encountering alien or corrupted minds may cause Mental Corruption [4]
    \item Precognition abilities may reveal horrific truths that increase Reality Fracture Clock
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Caravan and Trade Investigation}
Psionic abilities are invaluable for detecting smuggling, fraud, and commercial espionage:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reading merchants' true intentions during negotiations
    \item Detecting hidden cargo or false manifests
    \item Risk of Merchant Guild retaliation for unauthorized mental intrusion
    \item Potential for creating Business Intelligence [6] assets through mental reconnaissance
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Professional Psionic Investigators}

Specialized investigators have emerged who focus primarily on psionic methods:

\subsubsection{The Memory Advocates (Guild)}
Operating primarily in legal systems that accept mental evidence, these investigators combine traditional detective work with psionic enhancement:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Services:} Witness memory recovery, deception detection, residual impression reading
    \item \textbf{Limitations:} Require legal authorization, maintain extensive documentation, face professional liability for false evidence
    \item \textbf{Costs:} 2-5 times normal investigation fees plus potential legal complications insurance
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{The Silent Seekers (Freelance)}
Independent investigators who operate in jurisdictions with less regulation:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Services:} Covert mental investigation, missing person location through memory tracing, corporate espionage detection
    \item \textbf{Risks:} Legal vulnerability, potential for Mental Burnout [6] from overuse, reputation-based economy
    \item \textbf{Network:} Maintain informal connections with law enforcement who use their services unofficially
\end{itemize}

These investigation procedures ensure that psionic characters remain meaningfully engaged in investigative scenarios while preserving the core tension and complexity that makes Fate's Edge investigations challenging and interesting. The integration maintains mechanical balance by ensuring that psionic advantages come with proportional costs, risks, and complications that create ongoing story opportunities rather than simple problem-solving.

\section{Racial Psionic Integration}

The development of psionic abilities has created unique relationships between different peoples and mental powers, with each culture's existing strengths and limitations shaping how they approach and utilize these extraordinary capabilities.

\subsection{Aeler: The Deep Accountants}

The Aeler's existing focus on precise measurement and resource management has profoundly influenced how they develop and utilize psionic abilities. For the Aeler, psionics represent another form of infrastructure that must be carefully maintained and accounted for.

\subsubsection{Breath Accounting and Mental Fatigue}
Aeler psions experience their mental strain through the same breath-debt system that governs all underground activity:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Each psionic use costs "breaths" that must be repaid through rest and proper ventilation
    \item Underground environments increase Mental Fatigue costs by 1 segment due to thin air
    \item Aeler holds maintain "breath banks" - communal rest chambers where psions can recover mental energy
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Deep Accounting Integration:} Aeler psions must maintain detailed ledgers of their mental activities, recording:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Mental Fatigue expenditure and recovery times
    \item Effectiveness of different abilities in various environments
    \item Cost-benefit analysis of psionic vs. traditional approaches
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Infrastructure Psionics}
Aeler psions often specialize in abilities that enhance their society's infrastructure:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Structural Sense:} Enhanced ability to perceive stress points and weak spots in buildings and constructions
    \item \textbf{Vent Reading:} Advanced understanding of air flow patterns that enhances telepathic range underground
    \item \textbf{Resonance Mapping:} Ability to sense the "health" of infrastructure systems through mental connection
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Cultural Integration:} Aeler psions are highly valued for maintaining underground settlements, but their "breath debt" makes them dependent on community support. This creates a symbiotic relationship where psions provide essential services while requiring significant community investment in their maintenance.

\subsection{Lethai: The Context Keepers}

The Lethai's context-saturated communication system has created a unique approach to psionics where mental abilities are as dependent on social and environmental context as their spoken language.

\subsubsection{Context-Saturated Telepathy}
Lethai psions must navigate the same context-saturated communication as their non-psionic kin:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reading minds without proper "context keys" (place, season, relationship) produces garbled or misleading information
    \item Mental communication requires the same ritualized preparation as spoken communication
    \item Different mental "dialects" exist between Lethai-al, Lethai-ar, and Lethai-thora communities
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Mark Integration:} Lethai psions often enhance their abilities through traditional Marks (tattoos, scars, resin-inlay) that serve as mental anchors:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Spider's Patience Mark grants enhanced concentration for telepathic eavesdropping
    \item Serpent's Breath Mark enhances ability to detect deception and hidden intentions
    \item Storm-Shadow Mark allows for enhanced precognition during weather events
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Gift-Based Psionic Development}
The Lethai's Gift division creates distinct psionic development paths:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Body-Gift Psions:} Enhanced physical psionic abilities (telekinesis, biofeedback) that align with physical excellence
    \item \textbf{Mind-Gift Psions:} Enhanced mental abilities (precognition, telepathy) that complement mental acuity
    \item \textbf{Bridge-Born Psions:} Rare individuals who can combine both types but face social skepticism
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Cultural Integration:} Lethai psions serve as living archives and context-mediators, but their knowledge becomes "cursed" when removed from proper context. This creates a natural limitation that prevents them from becoming overpowered while maintaining their cultural significance.

\subsection{Ykrul: The Weather Readers}

The Ykrul's mastery of Kon'reh (sacred geometry) and their relationship with open spaces has shaped their approach to psionics around pattern recognition and tactical awareness rather than direct mental manipulation.

\subsubsection{Kon'reh-Augmented Precognition}
Ykrul psions don't see the future directly—they read the "flow/weight/exit" patterns of probability through their existing geometric understanding:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Precognition is tied to route-finding and tactical awareness rather than personal fortune-telling
    \item Mental visions appear as geometric patterns that must be interpreted through Kon'reh principles
    \item Group precognition is more effective than individual - multiple psions can "map" probability spaces
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Weather Integration:} Ykrul psions have enhanced sensitivity to atmospheric conditions that affect their abilities:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Storm conditions enhance telepathic range but increase Mental Fatigue costs
    \item Clear weather allows for more precise precognitive readings
    \item Wind patterns can carry or disrupt mental communications over long distances
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Tactical Psionics}
Ykrul psions excel in abilities that support their nomadic, clan-based society:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Herd Sense:} Enhanced ability to perceive the emotional and physical state of groups
    \item \textbf{Route Reading:} Mental mapping that combines geographic knowledge with tactical awareness
    \item \textbf{Banner Communication:} Long-distance mental communication between clan members
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Cultural Integration:} Ykrul psions are invaluable scouts and tacticians, but their "sightings" must be interpreted through Kon'reh principles. This creates a collaborative relationship where psions provide raw data that clan leaders must interpret through their cultural wisdom system.

\subsection{Vilakari: The Information Brokers}

The Vilakari's expertise in information networks and their position as cultural intermediaries has led to the development of psionic abilities focused on information gathering and social manipulation.

\subsubsection{Networked Telepathy}
Vilakari psions excel at surface thoughts and emotional currents in crowded spaces:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Enhanced ability to read emotional undercurrents in marketplaces and social gatherings
    \item Difficulty with deep, private thoughts due to cultural emphasis on surface interactions
    \item Natural talent for detecting deception in commercial negotiations
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Thought-Markets:} Vilakari psions have developed "thought-markets" where information is traded through mental contact:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Information brokers who specialize in mental exchanges
    \item Standardized "thought-prices" for different types of information
    \item Reputation systems based on the accuracy and usefulness of mental information
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Commercial Psionics}
Vilakari psions often specialize in abilities that enhance their information-brokering activities:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Surface Reading:} Enhanced ability to read immediate thoughts and intentions in social situations
    \item \textbf{Memory Trading:} Ability to temporarily share specific memories for commercial transactions
    \item \textbf{Negotiation Sense:} Enhanced perception of the underlying dynamics in business dealings
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Cultural Integration:} Vilakari psions operate underground information networks, but their abilities make them vulnerable to mental "audits" by authorities. This creates a cat-and-mouse dynamic where psions must constantly balance the value of their abilities against the risks of detection.

\subsection{Cross-Cultural Psionic Development}

The interaction between different racial approaches to psionics has created unique opportunities and conflicts:

\subsubsection{Cultural Exchange Programs}
Some communities have established formal programs for cross-cultural psionic training:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Aeler academies that teach Lethai context integration
    \item Lethai moots that explore Ykrul tactical applications
    \item Ykrul clan gatherings that learn Vilakari information networks
    \item Vilakari brokerages that adopt Aeler accounting precision
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Hybrid Abilities}
Individuals of mixed heritage or extensive cross-cultural training may develop unique hybrid abilities:
\begin{itemize}
    \textbf{Aeler-Lethai:} Psions who combine precise mental accounting with context-sensitive communication
    \textbf{Ykrul-Vilakari:} Psions who merge tactical precognition with information brokering skills
    \textbf{Lethai-Ykrul:} Psions who blend context-based telepathy with geometric pattern recognition
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Social Dynamics:} Different cultures have varying levels of acceptance for psionic abilities, creating complex social landscapes where psions must navigate not just their mental abilities but also the cultural expectations and limitations of their communities. This integration ensures that psionic characters remain meaningfully connected to the setting's cultural diversity while providing unique mechanical and narrative opportunities that reflect their peoples' distinct worldviews and capabilities.
