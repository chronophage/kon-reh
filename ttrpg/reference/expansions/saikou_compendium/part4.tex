\part{The Bound}

\section{Demons and the Infernal}

I use the term \emph{demon} with reluctance. The entities that the Church of the Everflame calls demons are not a unified species. They are a \emph{category of convenience}---spirits that refuse to abide by the covenants of mortal society, that cannot be negotiated with in the language of debt and obligation.

\subsection{Classification by Behavior}

\begin{description}
  \item[Class A: The Tempter] Offers power, knowledge, revenge in exchange for a price. Bound by the letter of contracts. A contract witnessed by a Runekeeper of Mykkiel can be enforced.
  \item[Class B: The Ravager] Simple hunger, rage, destruction. Summoned by violence or mass death. Vulnerable to silver and fire, but the most effective banishment is \emph{overwhelming witness}.
  \item[Class C: The Possessor] Inhabits living hosts, gradually erasing the host's personality. Vulnerable to exorcism by representatives of the \emph{host's} faith.
  \item[Class D: The Parasite] Attaches to a single emotion (fear, grief, ambition) and grows stronger as the host experiences that emotion. Can be starved by rigorous emotional discipline.
\end{description}

\subsection{Investigation Notes}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Demon summoning is forbidden in every nation that has a functioning legal system, and for good reason. Every summoning is a hole in the world.
  \item Demon hunters require three things: silver, fire, and witness.
  \item I carry a censer that burns a compound of saltpeter, silver filings, and ironwood resin. The smoke reveals demonic possession: the host's shadow lags; the demon's shadow moves ahead.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{The Sentinel's Exorcism:} To exorcise a possessing demon, I invoke The Sentinel to draw a \emph{witnessed circle} around the possessed. The circle must contain three mirrors---one facing the host, one facing the demon (reflected in the host's eyes), and one facing the exorcist. I speak the demon's true name (learned through The Witness's revelation) three times. On the third utterance, the demon is forced into the central mirror. The mirror is then shattered, and The Sentinel seals each shard. The demon is not destroyed, but it is \emph{distributed}---too fragmented to act.

\textbf{Case Study: \emph{The Censor's Daughter}} (Ecktoria, 859). A young woman possessed by a Class C demon. The Everflame Inquisition was preparing to burn her. I conducted The Sentinel's exorcism. The demon---a Tempter---had been summoned by the girl's own father, a Censor who hoped to influence a trial. The father confessed. The demon was sealed. The girl survived. She now works as an archivist. She does not speak of those days.
