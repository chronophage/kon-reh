\section{Adventure Seeds}

\emph{Three scenarios drawn from Saikou Ira's unsolved cases. GMs are free to adapt them to their campaigns.}

\subsection{The Lamplighter's Echo}

\textbf{Setup:} In an Aeler mining town, the Echo of a lamplighter killed in a gas explosion has begun to manifest during the night shift. The Echo does not harm anyone, but its warning bell confuses the miners and has caused two accidents. The mine foreman wants to blast the tunnel where the lamplighter died. The lamplighter's widow begs the party to find another way.

\textbf{Complications:} The Echo is not the lamplighter's death---it is his final attempt to ring the warning bell. The bell itself was salvaged and now hangs in the town's chapel. The lamplighter's name has been forgotten; the widow is the only one who remembers.

\textbf{Resolution:} The party must retrieve the bell and ring it during the next manifestation, while the widow speaks her husband's name. The Echo will cease. If they blast the tunnel, the Echo transforms into a Poltergeist.

\textbf{Clocks:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Foreman's Patience [4] — when filled, the foreman blasts the tunnel.
  \item Echo's Desperation [6] — when filled, the Echo becomes a Poltergeist.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The Ghoul of the Sewers}

\textbf{Setup:} In Port Shed, a series of missing persons reports have been linked to the sewers beneath the spice warehouses. The party is hired by a Sidhi dock-master to investigate. The ghoul is a young woman who was cursed after drinking from a well contaminated by a broken Veiled Sister's seal. She does not want to kill; she is starving.

\textbf{Complications:} The woman's mother is still alive and has been leaving bowls of animal blood at the sewer entrance. The dock-master knows this and wants the party to \emph{deal with} the ghoul quietly. A rival smuggler has learned of the ghoul and plans to use it as a weapon.

\textbf{Resolution:} The party may kill the ghoul (straightforward but morally ambiguous), arrange a feeding schedule (requires negotiation and a reliable source of animal blood), or attempt to cure her with a journey to the Well of Ichor (a mini-campaign).

\textbf{Clocks:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Hunger [4] — when filled, the ghoul attacks indiscriminately.
  \item Smuggler's Scheme [6] — when filled, a rival faction captures the ghoul.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The Possessed Censor's Daughter}

\textbf{Setup:} In Ecktoria, a Censor's daughter is accused of witchcraft after her body begins to speak in languages she never learned. The Everflame Inquisition has arrested her and plans to burn her at the next Triumph. The Censor (the girl's father) secretly hired a summoner years ago to bind a Tempter demon to his family line; the demon has now passed to his daughter. He will not confess.

\textbf{Complications:} The demon's true name is recorded in a forbidden text held in the Censor's private archive. The archive is sealed with a ward that only The Sentinel can breach. The party must break into the archive, learn the name, and perform an exorcism before the Inquisition executes the girl.

\textbf{Resolution:} The party must choose between exposing the Censor (ruining his family but saving the girl), performing a secret exorcism (difficult, requires three witnesses and a safe location), or breaking the girl out of prison (leading to a fugitive arc).

\textbf{Clocks:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Inquisition's Schedule [6] — when filled, the girl is burned.
  \item Demon's Grip [8] — when filled, the demon permanently possesses the girl.
\end{itemize}
