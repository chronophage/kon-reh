\section{Field Procedures}

\emph{For the use of investigators, exorcists, and those who walk the edge.}

\subsection{The Silver Test}

Carry a small silver coin. When you suspect a supernatural presence, place the coin on your tongue. If the coin tastes of copper or blood, you are in the presence of a vampire, lycanthrope, or demon. If the coin remains tasteless, the threat is spiritual (ghost, poltergeist, Anchor) rather than physical.

\emph{Do not swallow the coin.}

\subsection{The Mirror Array (The Witness)}

To seal a room against possession or haunting:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Place a silvered mirror facing each doorway.
  \item Place a mirror facing each window.
  \item Place a single mirror in the center of the room, facing the ceiling.
\end{enumerate}
Clean the mirrors with salt water before placement. The central mirror serves as a \emph{focus}---any spirit that attempts to enter will see its own reflection and hesitate.

\subsection{The Traveler's Shortcut}

When you need to bypass a patrol, a locked door, or a stretch of dangerous road, hold a pinch of dust from a crossroads in your left hand and whisper: \emph{``The road that was not there, I walk it now.''} Throw the dust into the air. If The Traveler is listening, a path will open. It will not last long. Do not hesitate.

\subsection{Raéyn's Desperation}

If you are drowning or trapped beneath the waves, speak Raéyn's name three times and touch silver to salt water. She may grant you a single breath---enough to reach the surface or to strike one blow. This favor is never forgotten. She will ask for something in return, usually on a night when the tide is wrong and the wind is silent.

\subsection{The Sentinel's Ward (Exorcism)}

To exorcise a possessing demon:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Draw a circle of salt and iron filings around the possessed. The circle must have \emph{three} witnesses: one who loves the victim, one who fears the demon, and one who is neutral.
  \item Place three mirrors: one facing the victim, one facing the demon (reflected in the victim's eyes), and one facing the exorcist.
  \item Speak the demon's true name three times, learned through The Witness's revelation.
  \item On the third utterance, the demon is forced into the central mirror.
  \item Shatter the mirror with a silver hammer. The Sentinel seals each shard.
\end{enumerate}
\emph{The demon is not destroyed. It is distributed. It cannot act, but it may be reassembled by a skilled summoner. Dispose of the shards in separate bodies of water.}

\subsection{The Witness's Knot}

When interviewing a suspect who may be possessed or cursed, tie a red thread around your left wrist and the suspect's right wrist. Explain that the knot binds the truth. Speak your questions clearly. The suspect cannot lie while the knot holds---but the knot will tighten if the truth is painful. Cut the knot when the interview concludes. Do not reuse the thread.

\subsection{The Salt Line}

A continuous line of salt will bar most spirits and lesser cursed creatures. Use sea salt for Linn creatures, rock salt for Aeler entities, and salt blessed by any faith for general purposes. A single break---a finger's width---is enough for a determined spirit to cross.

\subsection{The Ninth Measure}

When you encounter a creature or spirit that seems to have no weakness, look for what is \emph{missing}. The ninth cup un-poured. The ninth bell un-rung. The ninth name unspoken. The ninth step uncounted. Fill the absence, and the creature will sometimes cease. \emph{I do not know why this works. I do not ask.}
