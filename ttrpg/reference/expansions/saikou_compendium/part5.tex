\part{The Thirteenth Chime}

\section{Conclusion: On the Necessity of Witness}

I have written this compendium not to frighten, but to \emph{prepare}. The creatures I have described are not myths. They are neighbors, patients, clients, and---sometimes---the faces we see in the mirror when we are alone at midnight.

The Witness has taught me that truth is not a weapon. It is a \emph{tool}. The truth reveals the weak point, the bargain, the memory that binds the spirit to its hunger.

I have killed. I have saved. I have wept over bodies I could not help and celebrated over curses I could not break.

The work continues.

If you are reading this, you are now a witness. The creatures I have described are real. They are hungry. They are waiting.

But so are we.

The ninth cup is not poured. The ninth bell is not rung. The threshold is held---by you, by me, by the quiet ones who tend the lamps and count the breaths and remember the names that others have forgotten.

Do not look away.

\begin{flushright}
Saikou Ira\\
Port Shed, Dhaharan Coast\\
The mirror is clean. The truth is waiting.
\end{flushright}
