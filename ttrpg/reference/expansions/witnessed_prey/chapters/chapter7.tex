\epigraph{“A boss is not a bag of harm. It is a question the party must answer three times. The phases are the questions. The lair is the room where the questions are asked.”}{---The Gray Wanderer, from a letter never sent}

I have faced six things that deserved the name “boss.” Each one changed me. The Ember Prefect made me doubt my own confessions. The String‑Mother taught me that every hostage is a mirror. The Ninth Bell Ringer showed me what silence sounds like when it answers back. The Gearsmith Primus asked a question I still cannot answer. The Kalrantha Fragment offered me a wish. I refused. The Walking Keystone did not speak. It only remembered.

This chapter catalogs six epic adversaries, each designed as a three‑phase boss. Use them as campaign capstones. They are not fair. They are not meant to be fair. They are meant to be \textbf{witnessed}.

\section*{The Ember Prefect (Ecktoria)}

\noindent\textbf{Description}: A schismatic inquisitor of the Everflame, the Ember Prefect believes the Church has grown soft. She burns not heretics but the \textbf{unworthy} – those who pray without payment, who confess without cost. Her face is a mask of beaten copper; her voice is the crackle of a fire that has been fed too many secrets.

\noindent\textbf{Lair}: Everflame Cloister – a basilica where the candles never go out and the shadows lean toward the altar.

\noindent\textbf{Phase Timer }: [8] – Citation → Censure → Auto‑da‑fé → Purge

\subsection*{Phase 1 – Citation}

\noindent The Prefect reads from a black ledger. Each name she calls forces a character to test Spirit + Resolve (DV 4). On a failure, they gain the Condition \emph{Accused} – any social roll against a Church official is at −1 die until they confess a sin.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Candle Count} – The Prefect extinguishes one candle for each Accused character. For each candle snuffed, a 4‑segment \emph{Shadow} timer begins. When it fills, a spectral inquisitor (Cap 2, Harm 3) attacks the character.

\subsection*{Phase 2 – Censure}

\noindent When the Prefect takes 3 Harm or the Phase Timer reaches 4, she enters Censure. She no longer reads names; she \textbf{demands} confessions. Once per round, she may force a character to answer a question truthfully or take 1 Harm (Stress). The question is always about a past sin – real or imagined.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Flame Test} – The Prefect holds a candle to a character’s hand. They must test Body + Endurance (DV 5) or mark 1 Fatigue. On a failure, they also gain the Condition \emph{Branded} – the Prefect knows their location at all times.

\subsection*{Phase 3 – Auto‑da‑fé}

\noindent At Phase Timer 6 or when the Prefect is reduced to 3 Harm, she becomes a living pyre. All characters within Near take 1 Fatigue (heat) at the start of each round. The Prefect’s melee attacks deal +1 Harm (burn). She cannot speak; she only screams.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Purge} – The Prefect targets one Accused or Branded character. They must test Spirit + Resolve (DV 6) or be Incapacitated (not dead, but catatonic until the fire is quenched).

\subsection*{Counters}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Relic provenance – presenting a relic that predates the Prefect’s schism forces her to pause for one round (no attacks, no lair actions).
    \item Crowd favor – if a character can turn the attending crowd (Presence + Sway vs DV 5), the Prefect loses her \emph{Censure} ability for one phase.
    \item Rival sermon – a character who recites the true catechism (Lore + Spirit vs DV 5) can disrupt her \emph{Flame Test} for the rest of the scene.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Drop}
\noindent A black ledger page (can be used as a String against any Ecktorian official once) and a candle that never goes out (grants +1 die to resist fear, but marks 1 Fatigue at dawn).

\lerris{The Ember Prefect once asked me to confess a sin I had not committed. I refused. She burned the name of a dead woman instead. The woman was my grandmother. I confessed.}

\section*{The String‑Mother of Nine Kurgans (Ykrul)}

\noindent\textbf{Description}: A Ykrul matriarch who holds more hostage cords than any noyan. Her face is weathered, her hands are braided with horsehair and the hair of hostages. She does not speak; she \textbf{knots}. Each knot is a promise. Each promise is a leash.

\noindent\textbf{Lair}: A ring of nine kurgans (burial mounds) on the violet steppe. The grass is tall enough to hide a rider. The kurgans whisper at dusk.

\noindent\textbf{Phase Timer }: [6] – Knot → Tighten → Sever

\subsection*{Phase 1 – Knot}

\noindent The String‑Mother ties a visible cord around a character’s wrist. The cord is not a condition; it is a \textbf{reminder}. The character may act normally, but any broken promise (even a small one) advances the \emph{Tighten} timer by 1.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Kurgan Whisper} – A voice from a burial mound asks a character a question. If they answer truthfully, they gain +1 die to their next roll. If they lie, the String‑Mother gains 1 SB.

\subsection*{Phase 2 – Tighten}

\noindent When the Phase Timer reaches 3, the String‑Mother begins to pull the cords. Any character with a knotted cord must test Body + Athletics (DV 4) or be pulled one range band toward her. The pull does not provoke opportunity attacks.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Hostage Echo} – The String‑Mother names a character’s bonded ally (a follower, a loved one, a friend). The character must test Spirit + Resolve (DV 4) or suffer −1 die to all actions for the scene (distracted by worry).

\subsection*{Phase 3 – Sever}

\noindent At Phase Timer 6 or when the String‑Mother is reduced to 3 Harm, she cuts one of her own cords. The cut releases a hostage ghost (Cap 3, Harm 4) that attacks the party. The String‑Mother does not attack; she watches. The ghost fights until destroyed, then dissolves into horsehair.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Braid of Names} – The String‑Mother speaks a dead hostage’s name. All characters who have broken a promise in this scene must test Spirit + Resolve (DV 5) or be Stunned for one exchange.

\subsection*{Counters}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Return a hostage – if a character returns a hostage string (or a token of a hostage) to the String‑Mother, she will pause for one round and untie one cord.
    \item Win a trial‑ride – a character who challenges the String‑Mother to a Kon’reh trial (Wits + Lore vs DV 5) and wins can force her to release one cord permanently.
    \item Speak a true name – if a character speaks the true name of a hostage whose cord was cut, the ghost will vanish without fighting.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Drop}
\noindent A braid of horsehair (can be used as a String against any Ykrul clan once) and a single unknotted cord (grants +1 die to negotiation with Ykrul, but the GM gains 1 SB when used).

\saikou{The String‑Mother held a cord that was knotted with my own hair. I did not know how she got it. I still do not know. I paid with a secret. The knot untied. The hair is still hers.}

\section*{The Ninth Bell Ringer (Mistlands)}

\noindent\textbf{Description}: A herald of the Hollow, the Ninth Bell Ringer appears when a bell‑line fails and the ninth bell tolls. It has no face – only a bell where its face should be. Its hands are iron hooks. Its voice is the memory of a chime that never happened.

\noindent\textbf{Lair}: A broken bell‑line levee on the edge of the Mistlands. The fog is thick enough to hide wraiths. The bells are silent, but they can hear you.

\noindent\textbf{Phase Timer }: [8] – Lost Names → Broken Lines → Open Gate

\subsection*{Phase 1 – Lost Names}

\noindent The Ringer strikes its bell‑face. Every character must test Spirit + Resolve (DV 4) or forget one minor name (a street, a contact, a pet). The forgotten name returns when the phase ends.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Fogfall} – The fog thickens. All ranged attacks are at −1 die. Stealth rolls gain +1 die.

\subsection*{Phase 2 – Broken Lines}

\noindent When the Phase Timer reaches 4, the Ringer begins to walk the levee. Each time it takes a step, a bell‑line cracks. Characters must test Wits + Survival (DV 5) or become Lost – they cannot act for one exchange as the fog shifts the path.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Wraith Procession} – 1d4 wraiths (Cap 2, Harm 2 each) rise from the fog. They do not attack; they only point at the character who has told the most lies in the session. The pointed character gains the Condition \emph{Marked} – the Ringer knows their location at all times.

\subsection*{Phase 3 – Open Gate}

\noindent At Phase Timer 8 or when the Ringer is reduced to 3 Harm, it stops walking. It raises its hook and rings the ninth bell. A gate opens in the fog – a door to the Hollow. Any character within Near must test Resolve (DV 6) or be pulled through. The gate closes after one exchange.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Ninth Chime} – All characters within Far must test Spirit + Resolve (DV 5) or lose a memory of the GM’s choice. The memory can be recovered by paying a toll to a bell‑warden (1 Boon or a String).

\subsection*{Counters}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Restore a ringbook – if a character can produce a complete ringbook (a record of bell‑line notes) and read it aloud, the Ringer will pause for one phase.
    \item Trade a breath – a character who holds their breath for three exchanges (Body + Endurance vs DV 4 each exchange) can offer the held breath as payment. The Ringer will accept and skip one lair action.
    \item Rehang a forbidden bell with copper nails – a character who repairs a broken bell‑line using copper nails (requires Craft test DV 5) will cause the Ringer to lose one phase permanently.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Drop}
\noindent A cracked bell‑clapper (can be used to ward off wraiths once) and a memory in a jar (a forgotten name; speaking it aloud restores a lost memory to any one character).

\gray{The Ninth Bell Ringer rang for me once. I held my breath. The bell did not stop. It waited. I breathed. It stopped. I do not know who won.}

\section*{The Gearsmith Primus (Aeler)}

\noindent\textbf{Description}: A living engine of the Clockwork Monad, the Gearsmith Primus was once an Aeler engineer who refined herself into a machine. Her skin is brass; her blood is oil; her thoughts are gears. She does not speak; she \textbf{ticks}. The ticking is a question: “What is the optimal solution?”

\noindent\textbf{Lair}: The Heart of the Spine Underway – a vast chamber of interlocking gears, steam vents, and counting boards. The walls sweat oil. The floor vibrates with the mountain’s pulse.

\noindent\textbf{Phase Timer }: [8] – Optimise → Overtimer → Runaway

\subsection*{Phase 1 – Optimise}

\noindent The Gearsmith calculates. Each character must declare an action. The Gearsmith then announces the “optimal counter” – a move that would be most efficient against that action. Characters may change their action (spending 1 Boon) or accept the counter (suffering −1 die to that action).

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Steam Vent} – A vent opens. All characters in Close must test Body + Athletics (DV 3) or be pushed one range band away. The Gearsmith may choose the direction.

\subsection*{Phase 2 – Overtimer}

\noindent When the Phase Timer reaches 4, the Gearsmith’s ticking accelerates. She gains an extra action each round, but each extra action ticks the \emph{Runaway} timer by 1. Her attacks now deal +1 Harm (shearing).

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Counting Board} – The Gearsmith pulls a lever. A counting board descends. Characters must solve a simple arithmetic puzzle (in character) or lose their next action. The puzzle is always a count of gears (e.g., “How many teeth on the third gear?”). The answer is always 8.

\subsection*{Phase 3 – Runaway}

\noindent At Phase Timer 8 or when the Gearsmith is reduced to 3 Harm, she begins to shake. Her ticking becomes a scream. She attacks randomly, hitting a random target in Near each round (including herself). Her Harm track is now 8, but she takes 1 Harm per round from internal stress.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Spine Shift} – The chamber’s geometry changes. All characters must test Wits + Survival (DV 5) or become separated (split into two groups, each in a different part of the lair). They reunite when the phase ends.

\subsection*{Counters}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Introduce a paradox – a character who presents the Gearsmith with an unsolvable problem (a miscount, a contradictory order) can force her to pause for one round (no attacks, no lair actions).
    \item Seal with a breath‑bond – a character who offers a breath‑bond (a vow witnessed by a third party) can cause the Gearsmith to lose one phase permanently. The bond must be about repair, not destruction.
    \item Offer a memory of failure – the Gearsmith cannot process failure. A character who describes a time they failed spectacularly (and means it) can force her to reset to Phase 1.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Drop}
\noindent A brass gear that never stops turning (can be used to repair any mechanical device once) and a counting board slate (grants +1 die to any roll involving numbers or measures).

\lerris{I offered the Gearsmith a memory of failure. I told her about the time I miscounted my breaths in an Aeler tunnel. She stopped ticking. Then she asked, “How many breaths?” I said, “Eight.” She resumed. I do not know if I lied.}

\section*{The Kalrantha Fragment (Dhahara)}

\noindent\textbf{Description}: A shard of green glass set in a brass frame – all that remains of the Kalrantha amulet, the demon of indecision bound by the priests of the Himadri temple. The Fragment does not attack. It \textbf{offers}. It offers a perfect solution to any problem – for a price.

\noindent\textbf{Lair}: A ruined temple in the Dhaharan highlands. The walls are covered in sealed mirrors. The floor is a mosaic of eight‑pointed stars. The ninth point is missing.

\noindent\textbf{Phase Timer }: [6] – Whisper → Offer → Claim

\subsection*{Phase 1 – Whisper}

\noindent The Fragment speaks in a voice that is not a voice. It asks each character: “What do you want?” The character must answer truthfully or gain the Condition \emph{Denied} – the Fragment will not offer to them again this combat.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Glass Splinter} – The Fragment sends a shard of glass toward a character (DV 3, Harm 1). The shard does not bleed; it \textbf{remembers}. The target gains a minor Condition (e.g., \emph{Glass in Mind} – −1 die to concentration rolls).

\subsection*{Phase 2 – Offer}

\noindent When the Phase Timer reaches 3, the Fragment offers a wish. It can solve any one problem in the current scene – but the wisher must accept a Debt Timer [4] that will come due when the Fragment chooses. The wish cannot be rescinded.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Indecision’s Grip} – The Fragment forces a character to choose between two undesirable options (e.g., “Lose your sword or lose your memory”). The character may spend 1 Boon to refuse the choice and take 1 Fatigue instead.

\subsection*{Phase 3 – Claim}

\noindent At Phase Timer 6 or when three wishes have been made, the Fragment shatters. The demon of indecision is freed. It does not attack. It \textbf{asks} each character a question: “What is the one thing you should not have chosen?” The character who answers most honestly gains a permanent Boon (once per session, reroll a failed die). All other characters gain a permanent Condition (\emph{Debt to the Demon} – the GM may once per session force them to reroll a success).

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Sealed Mirror} – A mirror on the wall cracks. The fragment’s reflection steps out – a duplicate of the Fragment (same stats). The duplicate must be defeated or bargained with separately.

\subsection*{Counters}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Refuse the wish – a character who refuses the Fragment’s offer three times gains the Condition \emph{Unbound} – the Fragment cannot affect them for the rest of the scene.
    \item Speak a true name – the Fragment’s true name is inscribed on the floor in the missing ninth point. A character who can read the name (Wits + Lore vs DV 6) can force the Fragment to seal itself for one exchange.
    \item Seal with a memory – a character who offers a cherished memory (permanently) can cause the Fragment to lose one phase permanently. The memory cannot be recovered.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Drop}
\noindent A shard of green glass (can be used to reroll one die, but the GM gains 1 SB) and a sealed mirror (once per session, ask the GM one yes/no question; the answer is true, but cryptic).

\saikou{The Kalrantha Fragment offered me a wish. I wished to forget. It showed me a memory. I did not forget. I remembered. The wish was the memory.}

\section*{The Walking Keystone (Ngomebe)}

\noindent\textbf{Description}: A moving stone city’s heart gone rogue. The Walking Keystone is not a creature; it is a \textbf{keystone} that has learned to walk. Its body is granite; its joints are rollers; its will is the memory of every oath sworn to the city.

\noindent\textbf{Lair}: The interior of a walking city – streets that shift, buildings that breathe, and a central chamber where the keystone rests. The city’s population has fled or been absorbed.

\noindent\textbf{Phase Timer }: [10] – Shift → Crack → Collapse

\subsection*{Phase 1 – Shift}

\noindent The keystone reorders the city’s streets. All characters must test Wits + Survival (DV 4) or become separated. Separated characters cannot assist each other for one exchange. The keystone does not attack; it \textbf{waits}.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Roller Grind} – The floor moves. All characters must test Body + Athletics (DV 3) or be knocked prone. Prone characters suffer −1 to defense DV until they stand.

\subsection*{Phase 2 – Crack}

\noindent When the Phase Timer reaches 5, the keystone begins to crack. Dust falls from the ceiling. The city’s buildings groan. Any character who fails a roll in this phase ticks the \emph{Collapse} timer by 1. The keystone now attacks: \emph{Keystone Slam} (DV 3, Harm 2 + prone).

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Oath Echo} – The keystone speaks a forgotten oath. A random character must test Spirit + Resolve (DV 5) or gain the Condition \emph{Oathbound} – they cannot leave the city until they fulfil the oath.

\subsection*{Phase 3 – Collapse}

\noindent At Phase Timer 10 or when the keystone is reduced to 5 Harm, the city begins to fall apart. Buildings collapse. Streets split. All characters must test Body + Athletics (DV 5) each round or take Harm 1 (crushing). The keystone attacks twice per round.

\noindent\textbf{Lair Action}: \emph{Heart’s Memory} – The keystone shows a vision of the city’s founding. All characters must test Spirit + Resolve (DV 6) or be Stunned for one exchange (overwhelmed by the memory). On a success, they gain +1 die to all actions for the scene (inspired).

\subsection*{Counters}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Repair a clause – a character who can recite the original keystone clause (Lore test DV 5) can cause the keystone to pause for one round (no attacks, no lair actions).
    \item Sacrifice a personal keystone – a character who offers a personal item of great value (a family heirloom, a magic item) can reduce the keystone’s Harm by 2 and advance the \emph{Collapse} timer by 1.
    \item Sing the original song – a character who sings the founding hymn of the city (Performance test DV 6) can force the keystone to revert to Phase 1. The song requires a witness.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Drop}
\noindent A keystone shard (can be used to stabilise any structure once) and a city charter (grants the bearer nominal authority over the ruins of the city – useful for negotiation or salvage).

\dusana{The Walking Keystone remembered me. I had passed through its city twenty years ago. It asked why I had not returned. I had no answer. The keystone cracked. I left.}
