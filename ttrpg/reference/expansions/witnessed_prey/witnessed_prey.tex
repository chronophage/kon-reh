\chapter*{A Note on Method and Madness}
\epigraph{``The truth does not kill the vampire; the silver does.''}{---Saikou Ira}

This compendium is not a catalog of trophies. It is a ledger of debts. Every creature herein owes something -- to a patron, to the Hollow, to a broken oath, or to the silence that follows a miscount. I have walked the stairs of Thepyrgos, the fog of the Mistlands, and the violet steppe; I have spoken with ghosts, bound spirits, and once, briefly, with a Trow who offered to sell me my own shadow. I offered another trade. I am still paying for that conversation.

You will find no challenge ratings here. You will find no tidy bags of experience. Instead, each entry offers a hunger, a witness, a weakness, and a timer. The rest is negotiation.

I have been aided -- or afflicted -- by three companions who insisted on scribbling in the margins. The Gray Wanderer, who has worn more faces than I have shirts; Saikou Ira, whose silver mirror never lies; and Dusana of the Raven Road, who pays her debts in stories. Their voices will appear unbidden. Ignore them at your peril.

If you are reading this, you are now a witness. The creatures within are real. They are hungry. They are waiting. But so are we.

---Lerris Fenwood\\
Thepyrgos, under the Ninth Bell (which did not ring)

%-------------------- Colophon --------------------%
\chapter*{Colophon}
\epigraph{``The ink is ash. The mirror is clean. The debt is never closed.''}{---Saikou Ira}

This book was written on paper that had been a cargo manifest, a love letter, and a bandage. The ink is lamp-black cut with salt water and the ash of a burnt confession. The binding is gray cord tied with nine knots. The ninth knot is not cut.

The Gray Wanderer contributed his usual cynicism and a recipe for tea that keeps the Hollow from your doorstep (mostly). Saikou Ira insisted on keeping a silver mirror beside the writing desk; it fogged exactly twice, and he will not say what he saw. Dusana sang a lark into the rafters; the bird stayed for three days and left a single feather, which I have pressed between these pages.

As for me, Lerris Fenwood, I have seen the faces of the Unmasked. I have read the true ledger of the Congregation. I have learned that the ninth name is never spoken because it belongs to the one who will speak it last. When that day comes, I hope this book serves as witness.

Until then, the road is south. The ink is fresh. The lamp is borrowed.

---Lerris Fenwood\\
Thepyrgos, in the hour before dawn
