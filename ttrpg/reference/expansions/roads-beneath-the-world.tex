\newcommand{\threeclockrule}{%
    \textbf{Three-Clock Rule} \\
    At any given time, maintain at most three active clocks in a scene. \\
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Merge Hunger/Consumption/Evolution into a single \textbf{Anomaly [6]} clock.
        \item For the tracks Air, Light, Noise, Attention: the GM selects three to be active per scene.
        \item Long-term clocks (Mask Integrity, Context Keys, Vengeance Weave, Venom Debt) are tracked between sessions (only one per scene max).
        \item Faction clocks are advanced at session end, not in real-time.
    \end{itemize}
}

\section*{Design Goals}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Lived Underground} Make depth a culture, not just a map---air, light, pressure, and custom matter as much as steel.
    \item \textbf{Courts, Not Caves} Replace "monster holes" with societies that negotiate: web-law tribunals, keystone charters, pressure guilds.
    \item \textbf{Simple, Scary Procedures} Survival tracks (Air/Light/Noise/Pressure) drive fiction without new math.
    \item \textbf{Seams to the Surface} Plug cleanly into Political Intrigue, Shadows \& Steel, Wilderness, Caravans, and Psionics.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Quickstart (2 minutes)}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Pick a Route Type} Keystone Road (Aeler), Pressure Way (Mazereth), Web-Law Lane (Lethai-ar), Drift-River (underwater/boat), or Raw Vein (wild).
    \item \textbf{Track Four Things} Air [6], Light [6], Noise [4], Pursuit/Attention [6] (choose drakes, patrols, or hazards).
    \item \textbf{Intent Dial (per leg)} Stealth / Speed / Survey / Communion (parley with place-spirits). It changes your default Position.
    \item \textbf{Strings to Carry} keystone courtesy, bell-wire, toll-chalk, web token, shed-skin writ, pressure map, reed charm.
    \item \textbf{Etiquette On Entry} Step on stone; speak under beam; ring once. (Grants Position +1 in first scene if observed.)
\end{enumerate}

\section{I. Peoples \& Polities Below}

\subsection{Lethai-ar --- Courts of Web \& Scale}
Wood-kin sworn to Inae (the Weaver) or Isoka (the Serpent Queen), ruling shadow-vales, web-bridges, and mirror chambers.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Doctrine} Order through context. Oaths are woven, not hammered; a clause can be as binding as a chain.
    \item \textbf{Marks} Animist skin-script that enhances sense/body---older ones can slip "out of season" and behave as Curses until recontexted.
    \item \textbf{War} Asymmetric. Light discipline, kill-lanes, denial of approach; they prefer deterrence to slaughter. Most peoples leave them alone.
    \item \textbf{Etiquette} Candle-right (never blow out another's light); Web-Law token gains DV −1 in courtly scenes; Shed-Skin rite (Isoka) forgives one past identity in exchange for a future favor.
    \item \textbf{Strings} Web-Token (enter parley anywhere their silk is anchored), Shed-Skin Writ (voids 1 coercive clause once), Hedge-Pass (move unseen on a marked ledge, once/leg).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Aeler --- Holds of Stone \& Ledger}
Mountain folk whose under-roads and keystone works keep nations standing. Underground to them is survival horror with accounting.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Orders}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Iron Avengers: feud-keepers; make blood ledgers balance.
        \item Spirit Shield Warriors: ancestor rites + corridor defense.
        \item True Masons: wandering repair crews (Acasia, Vhasia, Viterra).
        \item Edgewalkers: coin-hungry rogues in the seams of law.
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Politics} Collectivist, mercantile, pre-colonial soft power: your display equals what you"ve delivered to the common works. Many oppose human exploitation and live in foothills and cities.
    \item \textbf{Etiquette} Keystone Courtesy (touch stone, name the builder if known); Public Weights (declare said/meant).
    \item \textbf{Strings} Keystone Seal (close a passage behind you once), Display Writ (Position +1 when invoking public works), Stone-Oath Witness (turn a duel into arbitration).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Mazereth --- Pressure Guilds \& Root-Warrens}
Deep tunnelers (a subculture of the Aelinnel "People of Sums") who live by math and marrow: pressure shafts, root-veins, earthen acoustics.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Traits} Medium-small; +1 die underground; Pressure Adaptation. Gifts: Deep Earthen Sense, Tunnel Craft, Pressure Resistance, Pattern Reading.
    \item \textbf{Costs} Surface sickness; Lantern Sway (SB: bright light causes missteps).
    \item \textbf{Customs} Counting-breaths, toll-chalk tallies, bell-wire message lines.
    \item \textbf{Strings} Pressure Map (DV −1 to navigate collapses), Bell-Wire Tap (hear "ahead"), Tally-Chalk (prove dues paid at a junction).
\end{itemize}

\section{II. Geography \& Economies of Depth}

\subsection*{Road Types}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Keystone Roads (Aeler)} mortared galleries, air chimneys, counted lantern niches (repairable).
    \item \textbf{Pressure Ways (Mazereth)} breath-matched shafts, root-arches, drip ledges (readable).
    \item \textbf{Web-Law Lanes (Lethai-ar)} silk bridges, mirror turns, hush zones (negotiable).
    \item \textbf{Drift-Rivers} cold black water, rope ferries, bell-buoys (timed).
    \item \textbf{Raw Veins} ore cracks, crystal falls, gas pockets (hungry).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Trade \& Tithe}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Light Economy} Lantern oil, glow-silk, sun-scrip (surface days "bottled" in lenses).
    \item \textbf{Air Rights} Chimney shares, bellows rents, breath auctions during sieges.
    \item \textbf{Repair Years} Payment in labor to fix aqueducts, pillars, weirs; converts harm to civic credit.
\end{itemize}

\section{IV. Etiquette \& Law (Position wins)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Step on Stone} Don"t scuff sacred thresholds; grants Position +1 on first entry scene.
    \item \textbf{Speak Under Beam} Any negotiation under a single visible light gains Witness---convert one future social SB to a Price (repair, rite).
    \item \textbf{Ring Once} One bell to announce, two to plead, three for Bell Dawn (truce to bury, water, repair---break it and gain a portable Black Mark).
    \item \textbf{Two Ledgers (Said/Meant)} Required in Aeler holds and Lethai-ar courts; present both to gain DV −1 in disputes.
    \item \textbf{Mask Right (Lethai)} Respect a declared mask/persona; failure worsens Position in any social scene this leg.
\end{itemize}

\section{VIII. Example of Play (one page)}
\textbf{Setup:} PCs escort a treaty delegation from an Aeler hold to a Lethai-ar court through Mazereth pressure ways while a Deep Drake stirs. Tracks start Air 1/6, Light 1/6, Noise 0/4, Attention (Drake) 0/6. Intent: Survey.

\textbf{Scene 1 --- The Junction.} Keeper sets Pressure Way DV 3. Scout rolls Survey; strong hit: Air −1 (vented) and you find a bell-wire. SB spent: pocket gas → Noise +1 if you rush. They slow; Position remains Dominant.

\textbf{Scene 2 --- The Web Bridge.} Etiquette: Speak Under Beam; they hang a lamp and gain Witness. The envoy presents Two Ledgers (said/meant). DV −1 for the parley. A rival house tries to spring a Mask Right trap; Mirror-sharp PC honors the mask, flipping Position to Good.

\textbf{Scene 3 --- Drake Wake.} Attention ticks on an echo; the Deep Drake whispers the captain's childhood. Player marks Fatigue, keeps focus with Bell-Wire as third-party witness (negates first False Order). They trade a Repair Years pledge (fix three chimneys) to pass without a feud.

\textbf{Fallout:} Air 2/6, Light 2/6, Noise 1/4, Attention 3/6. They earned Keystone Courtesy (portcullis access) and Web-Token for the return---but owe chimneys.

\section{IX. Adventure Seeds}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Bell Dawn Broken} Someone used truce to plant drake-glass. Prove it before Articles turn to ash.
    \item \textbf{The Missing Chimneys} A hold suffocates; politics say sabotage, math says collapse. Find the third ledger.
    \item \textbf{Shed-Skin Bride} An Isoka rite frees a noble from a brutal identity; the "discarded" name becomes a curse in the mirrors.
    \item \textbf{The Quiet Charter} A town sleeps peacefully because thought-tax is paid to something below. Change the payer or change the price.
    \item \textbf{Pressure Festival} Mazereth races to re-balance a vein; bet strings, win breaths, make enemies.
\end{itemize}

\section{X. Running Depth at the Table (cheats)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Start every leg by naming a rule of the place (stone, web, pressure, water). Make rolls answer to it.
    \item Keep Air/Light/Noise/Attention visible; let players buy relief with Strings or etiquette, not just dice.
    \item Always offer one Diamond that could solve the scene if they"ll accept a future Price (repair, rite, or debt).
    \item Spend SB as environment first (collapse, sputter, echo) before monsters. The dark should be the first antagonist, the people the second, the beasts the third.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{The under realms are not a pit to be cleared but a commons to be negotiated. Where lights are counted and breaths are tallied, procedure is mercy---and every crossing is a promise that must hold under weight.}

\section{Appendix A: Runekeeper Build (Clockwork Monad)}
\textit{Clockwork Monad adherent • one-Patron Runekeeper • Thiasos-bound artificer who turns workshops into engines and contracts into devices}

\subsection*{Path \& Requirements (Rules-Accurate)}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{One-Patron Rule:} You are a \emph{Runekeeper} bound to a \emph{single} Patron (here: the Clockwork Monad).
  \item \textbf{Thiasos (Familiar) --- 2 XP:} A circle/retinue/emissary that grounds the pact in fiction and enables \textbf{Patron's Gift}.
  \item \textbf{Codex --- 4 XP:} Grants access to the Patron's \textbf{Rites} and \textbf{Obligation} system.
  \item \textbf{Patron's Gift (Imbuement):} With a Thiasos, once/scene as 1 action, imbue a held item with \textbf{+1 Weapon (Melee)} and \textbf{+1 Thematic Skill} (Monad: Engineering/Tinkering/Logistics as set by the table). \emph{Push It} to extend one additional scene (\(+1\) Obligation).
  \item \textbf{Obligation Capacity:} \(\textbf{Spirit + Presence}\). Track total Obligation to your Patron. If you exceed Capacity: mark Fatigue per segment above and you cannot Invoke again until you"re below Capacity.
  \item \textbf{Rites (Runekeeper Path):} Invoke a known Rite as 1 action; on completion, mark \textbf{+1 Obligation}. \emph{Push It} once/scene for amplified effect (\(+1\) Obligation). The Rite's Tier sets its DV floor.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Background (pick 2--3)}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Underway Apprentice (Aeler):} Beam-witness, breath-time, lift-safety. You learned that \emph{process saves lives}---until process began to want things.
  \item \textbf{Sum-Lodge Graduate (Aelinnel):} Proofs before proverbs; "reliability circle" internships; audits that left workshops \emph{better} and \emph{hungrier}.
  \item \textbf{Masker-Consultant:} Contracted "efficiency audits" for caravans and cities; your reports always work and always \emph{bind}.
  \item \textbf{Blue-Ledger Custodian:} Quarantine the hunger; use the engine to feed people, not feed on them.
  \item \textbf{Red-Tooth Finalist:} Every improvement is an offering. You accelerate the wake on purpose.
  \item \textbf{Conflicted Operator:} You carry a bell and a bad joke to break cadence when counts get too perfect.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Drives:} make the world legible; prove mercy can be engineered; leave a line straighter than you found it. \\
\textbf{Prices:} obsession; dissociation in repetition; sites that begin ticking after you "bless" them.

\subsection*{Build (Tier I; 32 XP)}
\textbf{Attributes:} Body 2 \quad Wits 4 \quad Spirit 3 \quad Presence 3 \\
\textbf{Skills:} Engineering 2, Tinkering 2, Lore (Rites/Codices) 2, Notice 2, Insight 1, Diplomacy 1, Deception 1, Stealth 1

\paragraph{Required Talents \& Bonds}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]

\subsection*{Clockwork Monad --- Sample Rites (Codex excerpts)}
\textit{DMs: Treat these as table-ready examples; keep DV $\geq$ Rite Tier. All mark +1 Obligation on completion; \emph{Push It} once/scene for a stronger or longer effect (+1 Obligation).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Consecrate Process} (Tier 3, DV 3): Define a \emph{method} for this scene/leg. Allies who follow it gain \textbf{+1 Effect} on relevant actions. Start a \textbf{Hunger/Evolution/Consumption [4]} clock tied to the asset/site you optimized.
  \item \textbf{Legibility Audit} (Tier 3, DV 3): Reveal load paths, failure modes, or hidden clauses. Clear 1 \emph{Obscurity} or grant \textbf{+1 Position} to the next "fix."
  \item \quad \textbf{Quarantine Loop} (Tier 4, DV 4): Box an anomaly into a marked area/tool. While up, allies gain \textbf{+1 Position} to work around it. Each scene under stress ticks \textbf{Containment [1]}.
  \item \textbf{Escrow Seal} (Tier 4, DV 4): Place an item/claim "under seal" under witness. Interference begins a \textbf{Breach [4]} clock; you owe an audit later.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Artificer Schematics \& Field Rigs (non-magical builds)}
\textit{These are gadgets, not Rites; they use core rules for gear/tags and simple rolls.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]

  \item \textbf{Ledger Latch (Device, Tag: \emph{Seal})}: Tamper-evident clasp; forces a \textbf{Breach [2]} mini-clock to open quietly.
  \item \textbf{Variance Whistle (Trick, Tag: \emph{Disrupt})}: Breaks cadence; clear a \emph{Loop Contagion} condition on a hit (1 use).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Strings \& Diamonds (start with 2 total)}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Optimization Grant (Diamond):} Waive a roll once when "doing it by the book," then start \textbf{Anomaly [1]} at that site later.
  \item \textbf{Brokered Audit (Diamond):} DV \(-1\) to negotiate with officials about process/work safety.
  \item \quad \textbf{Quiet Cycle (String):} Declare a 10-count lull on a floor/line; gain \textbf{+1 Position} to fix/stall.
  \item \textbf{Safe-Conduct Stamp (String):} Pass checkpoints as "inspectors" for one scene.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Typical Plays (fiction-first)}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Invoke: Consecrate Process} (Eng./Lore, DV 3): Establish the method; the crew rides \textbf{+1 Effect}; start \emph{Hunger} [4] on the site.
  \item \textbf{Loop Breaker} (Perf./Command or Tinkering, DV 3): Add variance (off-beat bell, step-swap). On a hit, clear \emph{Loop Contagion} and downgrade one Hunger tier; on partial, clear it but tick \emph{Containment [1]}.
  \item \textbf{Quarantine Loop} (Lore/Eng., DV 4): Sandbox the anomaly; allies get \textbf{+1 Position}; stress scenes tick \emph{Containment}.
  \item \textbf{Escrow Seal} (Diplomacy/Law, DV 3--4): Put it under seal; enemies must work \textbf{Breach} before touching.
  \item \textbf{Patron's Gift (Imbue)} (1 action): Turn a tool into a sanctified instrument of the method (\(+1\) Melee, \(+\) Thematic Skill) for the scene; \emph{Push It} to extend.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Personal Clock (optional)}
\textbf{Obsession [4]} --- whenever you choose "the perfect method" over "the merciful fix," tick 1. At 4: you must repeat a task or recite counts until a bell/joke/touch breaks cadence (or you resist).

\subsection*{SB Menu the Keeper can spend against you}
False Orders ("efficiency directive"); Gear-Tooth Shear (lose time/tools); Task Loop (repeat or Stress); Autonomous Spasm (a machine finishes the job \emph{wrong}).

\subsection*{Playstyle Dials}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Blue-Ledger Custodian:} Take \emph{Quarantine} early; favor "Bowl before Board" (fairness before optimization). Spend Diamonds on mercy clauses; accept slower wins.
  \item \textbf{Red-Tooth Finalist:} Lean on \emph{Consecrate}+\emph{Iteration-like} play; push cadence and accept growing \textbf{Hunger}. Big scenes, costly neighborhoods.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Advancement (pick any as milestones)}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Supply Architect (5 XP):} When you \emph{Consecrate} a supply chain, create \textbf{Surplus [2]} you can cash later; on cash, tick \textbf{Anomaly [1]} upstream.
  \item \textbf{Human-in-the-Loop (6 XP):} Once/scene, convert a machine failure into a human choice (ask a PC/NPC what they sacrifice to keep it humane).
  \item \quad \textbf{Masker Auditor (4 XP):} Under silk, DV \(-1\) to impose safety clauses; refusing them shifts \textbf{Reputation} against your foes.
  \item \textbf{Loop Immunity (8 XP):} You no longer tick \emph{Obsession} from ambient counting; only from deliberate over-optimization.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Example Scene}
The lift keeps going \emph{past} the last stop. You \textbf{Legibility Audit} (DV 3) to find the hidden loop (\(+1\) Position). You \textbf{Quarantine Loop} (DV 4) with chalk, cord, and a brace. Your Thiasos rings the variance whistle; the carriage halts mid-descent. Mark \emph{Containment [1]}. Offer a \textbf{Brokered Audit} to install a \textbf{human latch}---buying mercy into the method before the hunger learns stairs.

\subsection*{Why play this}
You are an \textbf{artificer of systems}. You save cities with checklists and turn factories into dungeons. Your tools are bells, ledgers, clamps---and the terrible beauty of a plan that works.

\section{Appendix B: Inaea \& Isoka Fanatics (Optional Module)}
\textit{This optional module explores fanatic variants of the Lethai-ar faiths. Use only if desired for your campaign.}

\subsection*{Inaea Fanatics --- The Family-State}
\textit{"All under one roof. All under one line."}
\paragraph{Civic Doctrine.}
Hospitality becomes law; \emph{Guest-Right} becomes jurisdiction. Neighborhoods are reorganized into \textbf{Households-of-Record} (block-roofs). Every door has a bell pattern; every conflict must occur \emph{under a line} with a registered witness. Travel requires \textbf{Line Permits} (who hosts you, who witnesses you).

\paragraph{Institutions \& Tools.}
Aunties of the Line (block wardens) • Bell-Wardens (signal police) • Mask-Mothers (context courts) • Household Books (who slept where) • Dew-Mirror Lattices (street-corner "mirrors" that log comings/goings).

\paragraph{Everyday Feel.}
Chimes everywhere; soft voices that demand your role; neighbors who "host" you for your own safety. People thank the state for keeping families intact while quietly living in fear of being declared \emph{Guestless}.

\paragraph{Mechanics.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Clocks:} Surveillance Grid [8], Household Compliance [6], Guestless List [4].
  \item \textbf{SB Menu (Keeper):} 
    1 SB --- A bell mis-rings; position worsens for the unhosted. 
    2 SB --- Auntie visit: papers, role, and host demanded. 
    3 SB --- Safe-conduct revoked; \emph{under-the-line} arrest. 
    4 SB --- District "under one roof" order: curfew lines drop.
  \item \textbf{Strings/Diamonds:} 
    \emph{Line-Pass} (bypass one checkpoint under escort), 
    \emph{Household of Record} (once per session, count as \emph{hosted}), 
    \emph{Witness Bell} (force parley, DV--1 if papers are clean).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{How to Resist.}
Variance in bells (Aveh shrines), two-ledgers (\emph{said/meant}) to expose coercive hospitality, Ykrul \emph{Bowl then Board} to reframe sanctuary as limited, seasonal rights instead of total jurisdiction.

\paragraph{Adventure Seeds.}
Smuggle a dissident \emph{off} the Household Books • Prove a "Guestless" family still has a host • Flip a Bell-Warden's lattice to record \emph{the watchers}.

\subsection*{Isoka Fanatics --- The Permanent Revolution}
\textit{"Decide by dawn or be decided."}
\paragraph{Civic Doctrine.}
Endless purification by choice. The city is ruled by \textbf{Dawn Committees} that settle everything with \emph{Fang of Decision}. Indecision is treason; nuance is counter-revolution. Members \emph{Shed-Skin} (role swaps, recantations) in public rites; yesterday's hero is today's saboteur.

\paragraph{Institutions \& Tools.}
Dawn Tribunals • Purity Heralds (announce tests) • Choice-Boards (public schedules of decisions) • Shedding Courts (compelled confessions) • Venom Truce Zones (violence-pauses for show trials).

\paragraph{Everyday Feel.}
Posters promising clean tomorrows; midnight runners posting new dawn questions; friends practicing "the hard truth" to survive their turn on the steps. Relief when a choice lands; dread of the next one.

\paragraph{Mechanics.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Clocks:} Decision Hegemony [6], Purity Ledger [4] (per PC/faction), Cadre Schism [6].
  \item \textbf{SB Menu (Keeper):} 
    1 SB --- Loyalty test now; speak a painful truth or lose Position. 
    2 SB --- Cell split: an ally denounces a nuance. 
    3 SB --- Purge wave: lose an Asset \emph{or} accept a branded geas. 
    4 SB --- Summary Dawn: a tribunal seizes jurisdiction over the current scene.
  \item \textbf{Strings/Diamonds:} 
    \emph{Revolutionary Mandate} (override one standing order once, then tick Purity), 
    \emph{Truce Seal} (bind a dawn parley that \emph{must} end in a choice), 
    \emph{Scale Draught} (resist fear in a tribunal scene).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{How to Resist.}
Flood the witness stand (Gravel Seraphs, multiple contexts) • Convert "decision" into \emph{exits} via Kon"reh---split tolls, timebox rights • Force \emph{Bowl} (fairness) before \emph{Fang} (finality) in public framing.

\paragraph{Running the Tone}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Inaea Fanatics = Creeping Intimacy as Control.} Bells, Aunties, hospitality audits, "for your safety." Make every courtesy a checkpoint.
  \item \textbf{Isoka Fanatics = Purity as Motion.} Decisions as spectacle, slogans change weekly, cadres splinter mid-scene. Make every victory threaten to become yesterday's heresy.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Cross-Pressure}
When both wings contest a city: the Family-State demands \emph{roles under a line}; the Revolution demands \emph{choices at dawn}. PCs can weaponize the clash: force Inaea to sign seasonal exits (limits surveillance), force Isoka to accept Bowl-first hearings (limits purges).

\subsection*{Table Dials}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Severity (0--3):} number of active clocks per district from each wing.
  \item \textbf{Visibility:} bells and posters obvious (street fear) vs. subtle (only papers and whispers).
  \item \textbf{Mercy Valve:} once/session any PC can spend 2 Boons to insert a humane clause into a line or a dawn decision.
\end{itemize}
