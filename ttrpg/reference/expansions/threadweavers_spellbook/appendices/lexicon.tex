\chapter{A Short Lexicon of Thepyrgos Slang for Casters}

Every trade develops its own language. Free casting is no exception. The following terms are current among the Threadweavers of Thepyrgos—some borrowed from the University, some from the gutter, and a few that I invented myself.

\section{Terms for the Caster}

\begin{description}
    \item[Threadweaver] A free caster who shapes the raw Weave without patron or writ. Slightly derogatory when used by Runekeepers. A badge of honor when used by us.
    \item[Gutter- mage] A Threadweaver who learned on the streets, not in a classroom. Often more skilled. Never more careful.
    \item[The Unlicensed] What the Synod calls us. We call ourselves \emph{the unburdened}.
    \item[Receipt] Backlash. A minor receipt is a singed sleeve. A major receipt is a scar. A catastrophic receipt is a crater.
    \item[The Grammar] The TAGS system. \emph{``Study your Grammar.''} \emph{``His Grammar is terrible.''}
\end{description}

\section{Terms for Spells and Casting}

\begin{description}
    \item[Cantrip] A one- tag spell. \emph{``That's just a cantrip. Anyone can light a candle.''}
    \item[Heavy Spell] A spell with three or more tags. \emph{``He's throwing heavy spells now. Watch out for the receipt.''}
    \item[Folding] Casting a dangerous tag, especially [FOLD] or [LEAP]. \emph{``She folded out of the locked room.''}
    \item[The Surcharge] The additional Difficulty for dangerous tags. \emph{``The surcharge on [GATE] is worth it if you're running from a mob.''}
    \item[Witness] A third party who observes a casting. The Weave pays attention to witnesses. \emph{``Always cast with a witness. The Weave lies less.''}
\end{description}

\section{Terms for Failure and Backlash}

\begin{description}
    \item[The Weave's Humour] A catastrophic failure. \emph{``I rolled a miss. The Weave's humour was on full display.''}
    \item[Singed] Minor backlash. \emph{``I'm singed, but I'll live.''}
    \item[Interest] A debt incurred by repeated failures. \emph{``I've paid too much interest on that spell. I'm not casting it again.''}
    \item[The Ledger] A caster's personal record of failures. \emph{``Check the ledger before you try that. I've seen it go wrong.''}
\end{description}

\section{Terms for the Weave Itself}

\begin{description}
    \item[The Old Conversation] The relationship between caster and Weave. \emph{``She has a good Old Conversation going. The Weave listens to her.''}
    \item[The Receipt Book] The Weave's own record of every spell ever cast. Apocryphal. Believed in by those who have cast dangerous tags.
    \item[The Surcharge King] A joking name for any tag that adds DV +2. \emph{``[LEAP] is the Surcharge King. [TRANSFORM] is his queen.''}
\end{description}

\section{Conversational Examples}

\begin{verbatim}
``How was the examination?''
``Singed. I attempted a heavy spell without a witness.''
``The Weave's humour?''
``A minor receipt. My sleeve will recover.''

``Why didn't you fold out of the ambush?''
``The surcharge was too high. I was already carrying interest from a previous miss.''

``She's a natural.''
``She's a gutter- mage. Same thing.''
\end{verbatim}

\begin{description}
    \item[The Old Conversation] The relationship between caster and Weave. \emph{``She has a good Old Conversation going. The Weave listens to her.''}
    \item[The Receipt Book] The Weave's own record of every spell ever cast. Apocryphal. Believed in by those who have cast dangerous tags.
    \item[The Surcharge King] A joking name for any tag that adds DV +2. \emph{``[LEAP] is the Surcharge King. [TRANSFORM] is his queen.''}
    \item[Burn] To cast a spell recklessly, without regard for backlash. \emph{``He burned a [GATE] with no witness. The Weave's humour was not kind.''}
    \item[Cold Casting] A spell performed with no witnesses, relying solely on the caster's precision. \emph{``Cold casting [VEIL] is fine. Cold casting [REWIND] is suicide.''}
    \item[The Unwritten Line] The gap between what the caster intends and what the Weave hears. \emph{``Every miss teaches you something about the Unwritten Line.''}
\end{description}

\section{A Dialog from the Amber Gate}

Two Threadweavers meet after an examination.

\begin{verbatim}
``How did you fare?''
``Singed. I attempted a heavy [FOLD] without a witness.''
``The Weave's humour?''
``A minor receipt. My sleeve will recover. You?''
``Cold cast [SCRY] to find the exit. Clean success. No receipt.''
``Show-off.''
``The Weave prefers precision. You know that.''
``The Weave also prefers courage. Singed is not failed.''
``True. Buy me a drink and I'll show you my ledger.''
\end{verbatim}

Language shapes practice. Learn the slang. It will help you sound like you know what you are doing—even when you do not.
