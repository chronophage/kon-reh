\chapter{Talents of the Threadweaver}

\section{Old Talents (From the Core)}

These talents appear in the core rules. I list them here for completeness, with commentary in my own voice. A talent is not a spell. It is a habit the Weave has learned to expect from you.

\subsection{Spellcraft (6 XP)}

The foundational talent. Without it, you cannot shape the raw Weave. With it, you are a free caster—unlicensed, unbound, and unforgettable. The Synod disapproves. The Weave does not.

\subsection{Ritual Mastery (6 XP)}

You have learned to compress time. A spell that would require a full minute of focus now requires half that. The Weave is not impatient; it is merely willing to negotiate.

\subsection{Elemental Focus (4 XP)}

Choose an element—Fire, Water, Earth, Air, Fate, Life, Luck, Death. When you cast a spell of that element, the Weave listens more attentively. You gain an additional die on the roll. I recommend Fire. It is honest.

\subsection{Stabilize Weave (8 XP)}

Once per session, you may cancel a single Story Beat generated by your own casting. The Weave does not forget; it merely postpones the receipt. Use this when the consequences would be catastrophic. Use it sparingly otherwise.

\subsection{Controlled Burn (4 XP)}

When you roll a one on a free casting roll, you may convert that Story Beat into a Boon for yourself. The GM does not gain that Beat. The Weave accepts this trade because you have demonstrated control. A useful talent for those who find themselves rolling many ones.

\subsection{Weave Resilience (4 XP)}

When you suffer Backlash from a free casting roll, reduce its severity by one step. Catastrophic becomes Major. Major becomes Moderate. Moderate becomes Minor. Minor becomes nothing. The Weave respects those who can take a hit and keep casting.

\subsection{Elemental Attunement (5 XP)}

Requires Elemental Focus. When you cast a spell using your chosen element, you may reroll one failed die (not a one) once per casting. Additionally, you gain a bonus die to Arcana rolls when identifying or analyzing that element. The Weave appreciates specialists.

\subsection{Backlash Siphon (6 XP)}

Requires Spirit 3+. When you would suffer Backlash from a free casting roll, you may instead mark one Obligation to a Patron of your choice (or gain one Corruption if you have no Patron). The Backlash is negated. The Patron's attention is drawn. The Weave does not care who pays, only that someone does.

\section{New Talents}

These talents are not found in the core rules. They emerged from my own practice and the practice of my more interesting students.

\subsection{Threadweaver's Patience (3 XP)}

You may delay the resolution of a spell for up to one exchange. During the delay, you maintain the tags in your mind but do not release them. The Weave holds its breath. If you are interrupted during the delay, the spell fails and you suffer Backlash as if you had rolled a miss. Useful for ambushes. Dangerous for the impatient.

\subsection{Signature Spell (5 XP)}

Choose a single spell you have cast successfully at least three times. When you cast that spell, you gain +1 die on the roll. Additionally, the first time you cast it in a scene, you may ignore the first Story Beat generated by ones. The Weave recognises repetition. It does not applaud, but it does not interrupt.

\subsection{Echo Recall (4 XP)}

Once per session, after a failed casting, you may immediately attempt the same spell again with a +1 die. The Weave permits a second draft. The Backlash for the second attempt is applied regardless of success or failure. I have used this talent twice. Both times, the second draft was worse than the first. Your experience may differ.

\subsection{Elemental Rebuke (6 XP)}

Requires Elemental Focus. When an enemy casts a spell of your chosen element within Near range, you may attempt to twist the Weave against them. Roll a contested Arcana check. On success, the enemy's spell resolves as you choose—redirected, weakened, or reflected. On failure, you suffer the Backlash instead. The Weave does not appreciate interference, but it respects audacity.

\subsection{Safe Harbour (4 XP)}

When you cast a [WARD] or [DEFEND] spell, you may extend its protection to one adjacent ally without increasing the DV. The Weave does not distinguish between your skin and theirs, provided you ask politely.

\subsection{Practiced Caster (2 XP)}

Reduce the DV of any cantrip (one- tag spell) by 1, to a minimum of 1. A candle that lights itself is not a miracle. It is practice.

\subsection{Catastrophe Insurance (8 XP)}

Once per campaign, when you would suffer a Catastrophic Backlash, you may instead convert it to a Major Backlash. The Weave accepts this substitution because you have paid in advance—with a memory, a name, or a promise. I have not used this talent. I keep it in my ledger in case.

\section{Talent Synergies}

Some talents work well together. A few work against each other. The Weave does not police your combinations, but it will charge interest on contradictions.

\subsection{The Stable Caster}

\emph{Controlled Burn + Weave Resilience + Practiced Caster.} You roll many ones. You convert them to Boons. You reduce the Backlash when you fail. You cast cantrips with trivial difficulty. You are not a spectacular caster. You are a \emph{reliable} one. The Weave appreciates reliability.

\subsection{The Elemental Specialist}

\emph{Elemental Focus + Elemental Attunement + Elemental Rebuke.} You have chosen an element and made it your own. Spells of that element are easier, more powerful, and occasionally turned back against your enemies. The Weave respects specialists. It also watches them more closely.

\subsection{The Desperate Gambler}

\emph{Backlash Siphon + Threadweaver's Patience + Catastrophe Insurance.} You cast dangerous spells with dangerous tags. You defer the cost to patrons, to memories, to the future. You delay resolution to achieve perfect timing. And when catastrophe looms, you have a final escape. This combination is not recommended. It is also very effective.

\subsection{The Silent Weaver}

\emph{Safe Harbour + Signature Spell (a veiling or silencing spell) + Echo Recall.} You are not a combat caster. You are a shadow. Your spells protect allies, hide movements, and erase evidence. When a veiling fails, you try again. The Weave does not notice you. That is the point.

\section{A Final Observation on Talents}

A talent is not a spell. It is a \emph{tendency}. The Weave notices tendencies. Over time, the Weave adjusts its expectations. A caster who always uses Controlled Burn will find that the Weave offers fewer ones to convert. A caster who never uses Backlash Siphon will find that the Weave offers more.

The Weave is not a machine. It is a conversation partner. It learns your habits. It adjusts its replies. A talent is the grammar of that adjustment.

Choose your talents as you choose your words: with intention, with economy, and with an ear for how the Weave will answer.

\textit{--- Lysandra}
