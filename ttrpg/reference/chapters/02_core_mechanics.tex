\chapter{Mechanics Reference}

\section{Core Mechanic: The Art of Consequence}\index{Core Mechanic}

All significant actions follow a three-step process:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Approach} --- The player describes both what their character wants and how they attempt it. This defines the primary Skill and clarifies the fiction.\index{Core Mechanic!Approach}
\item \textbf{Execution} --- Build a dice pool equal to Attribute + Skill and roll that many \dice{10}s. Each die of 6 or higher counts as a success. Each 1 rolled generates a Complication Point.\index{Core Mechanic!Execution}
\item \textbf{Outcome} --- The GM interprets total successes against the difficulty of the task. Complication Points are then spent to weave narrative setbacks, collateral costs, or escalating danger.\index{Core Mechanic!Outcome}
\end{enumerate}

\section{The Description Ladder}\index{Description Ladder}

The quality of the player's description affects the resilience of their roll against complication:

\begin{description}
\item[Basic Action] Roll the pool as-is. All 1s remain as Complication Points.\index{Description Ladder!Basic Action}
\item[Detailed Action] A clear, descriptive flourish allows the player to re-roll one die showing 1.\index{Description Ladder!Detailed Action}
\item[Intricate Action] A richly described, multi-sensory action allows the player to re-roll all dice showing 1, and add one positive narrative flourish to the scene if they succeed.\index{Description Ladder!Intricate Action}
\end{description}

\noindent\textbf{CP Note.} Re-rolling 1s does \emph{not} erase their CP; any new 1s on the re-roll add more CP.\index{Complication Points!Re-rolling}

\section{Outcome Matrix}\index{Outcome Matrix}

Let $S$ be successes ($\geq 6$) and $C$ be Complication Points (number of 1s rolled).

\begin{description}
\item[Clean Success] ($S \geq DV$ and $C = 0$) --- Deliver the intent crisply. Offer a small positional or information edge if description was Intricate.\index{Outcome Matrix!Clean Success}
\item[Success \& Cost] ($S \geq DV$ and $C > 0$) --- Grant the intent; spend/bank CP to add friction (noise, time lost, resource wear, new eyes on the scene).\index{Outcome Matrix!Success \& Cost}
\item[Partial] ($0 < S < DV$) --- Progress with a fork: Get it but... (time/position/gear cost) or Leave it and... (take safety, new intel).\index{Outcome Matrix!Partial}
\item[Miss] ($S = 0$) --- No progress. Cash some CP now or bank for a coming beat. Award a boon to the player.\index{Outcome Matrix!Miss}
\end{description}

\section{Complication Point Spend Menu}\index{Complication Points!Spend Menu}

\subsection{Universal CP Options}\index{Complication Points!Universal Options}

\begin{description}
\item[1 CP] Minor pressure: noise, trace, +1 Supply segment.\index{Complication Points!1 CP}
\item[2 CP] Moderate setback: alarm raised, lose position/cover, lesser foe or lock.\index{Complication Points!2 CP}
\item[3 CP] Serious trouble: reinforcements, key gear breaks, rail tick.\index{Complication Points!3 CP}
\item[4+ CP] Major turn: trap springs, authority arrives, scene shifts.\index{Complication Points!4+ CP}
\end{description}

\subsection{Combat}\index{Complication Points!Combat Options}

\begin{description}
\item[1 CP] lose footing (next defense --1d).\index{Complication Points!Combat!1 CP}
\item[2 CP] weapon or battlefield shifts (fireline, cave-in, cavalry arrives).\index{Complication Points!Combat!2 CP}
\item[3 CP] pinned, disarmed, or separated.\index{Complication Points!Combat!3 CP}
\end{description}

\subsection{Stealth \& Intrusion}\index{Complication Points!Stealth Options}

\begin{description}
\item[1 CP] footstep/squeak; shadow seen.\index{Complication Points!Stealth!1 CP}
\item[2 CP] patrol changes; lock resists (extra test).\index{Complication Points!Stealth!2 CP}
\item[3 CP] partial alarm (initiated).\index{Complication Points!Stealth!3 CP}
\item[4 CP] full alarm and lockdown protocol.\index{Complication Points!Stealth!4 CP}
\end{description}

\subsection{Social}\index{Complication Points!Social Options}

\begin{description}
\item[1 CP] rumor cost or faux pas (future --1d with this contact).\index{Complication Points!Social!1 CP}
\item[2 CP] a concession is now required (gift, favor).\index{Complication Points!Social!2 CP}
\item[3 CP] rival interjects with leverage.\index{Complication Points!Social!3 CP}
\item[4 CP] patron turns, audience hostile; by Pillar (Examples) or oath invoked.\index{Complication Points!Social!4 CP}
\end{description}

\subsection{Travel \& Survival}\index{Complication Points!Travel Options}

\begin{description}
\item[1 CP] lose time; minor injury; weather turns.\index{Complication Points!Travel!1 CP}
\item[2 CP] Supply +1 segment; mount lamed.\index{Complication Points!Travel!2 CP}
\item[3 CP] wrong valley or blocked pass; Fatigue 1 to all.\index{Complication Points!Travel!3 CP}
\item[4 CP] storm, rockslide, flood---scene rewritten.\index{Complication Points!Travel!4 CP}
\end{description}

\subsection{Arcana \& Ritual}\index{Complication Points!Arcana Options}

\begin{description}
\item[1 CP] prickle of backlash; sensory bleed.\index{Complication Points!Arcana!1 CP}
\item[2 CP] unintended side-effect (cold from fire, echoes draw attention).\index{Complication Points!Arcana!2 CP}
\item[3 CP] residue anchors a foe/hex.\index{Complication Points!Arcana!3 CP}
\item[4 CP] backlash condition or manifestation; ritual mark persists.\index{Complication Points!Arcana!4 CP}
\end{description}

\paragraph{High-Tier CP Sinks.}\index{Complication Points!High-Tier Sinks}
For 3–6+ CP spends that move the world (reputation cascades, faction instability, resonance, prophecy), see the stand-alone \emph{High CP Sinks} handout. A good default: at end of leg, \textbf{3 CP → tick 1 Front}.

\section{Boon Economy}\index{Boons}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Holding cap: You can hold at most 5 Boons.\index{Boons!Holding Cap}
    \item Conversion: Once per session, in downtime, you may convert 2 Boons → 1 XP (max 2 XP via conversion per session).\index{Boons!Conversion}
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}

\item +2 XP bonus to one player for a signature moment of the arc\index{Experience Points!Signature Moments}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Complication Dividend}\index{Experience Points!Complication Dividend}

    \item Face Card: +1 XP\index{Experience Points!Face Cards}

\subsection{Spending}\index{Experience Points!Spending}

\begin{description}
\item[Attributes] Cost = new rating $\times$ 3. Downtime = new rating in days.\index{Experience Points!Attributes}
\item[Skills] Cost = new level $\times$ 2. Downtime = new level in days.\index{Experience Points!Skills}
\item[On-Screen Followers] Cost = Cap$^2$. Downtime = 1--3 days to recruit and brief.\index{Experience Points!Followers}
\item[Off-Screen Assets] Minor (4 XP, 1 day), Standard (8 XP, 1 week), Major (12 XP, 1 month).\index{Experience Points!Assets}
\end{description}

\section{Rush Rule}\index{Advancement!Rush Rule}
A player may skip downtime, but the GM creates a Haste clock of four segments. If the clock fills, the new ability or asset carries flaws or narrative complications.

\section{Tiers of Reputation}\index{Reputation Tiers}

\begin{description}
\item[Tier I -- Rookie] (0--40 XP): Local reputation; prestige locked.\index{Reputation Tiers!Rookie}
\item[Tier II -- Seasoned] (41--90): Regional notice; prestige abilities may be unlocked.\index{Reputation Tiers!Seasoned}
\item[Tier III -- Veteran] (91--150): National influence; second follower slot suggested.\index{Reputation Tiers!Veteran}
\item[Tier IV -- Paragon] (151--220): Movers and shakers; rivals emerge to challenge.\index{Reputation Tiers!Paragon}
\item[Tier V -- Mythic] (221+): Legendary status; kingdoms and cults respond.\index{Reputation Tiers!Mythic}
\end{description}
