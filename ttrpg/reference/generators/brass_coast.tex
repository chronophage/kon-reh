\section{The Brass Coast --- ``The Shore of Silver and Spice''}
\label{chap:brasscoast}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Every tariff is a story. Every story has a price. The green flame remembers.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Admiralty Port-Master (Elite):}  
  ``The tariff is the law. The law is the tariff. I do not care if your goods are stolen, blessed, or weeping. I care that you pay. The Admiralty does not rule this coast. The ledger rules. I am merely its pen.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Sidhi Freedman (Commoner):}  
  ``My grandmother was born in Galanina. My mother was born in a slave ship. I was born in a lighthouse. The Admiralty calls me a subject. The Covenant calls me a freedman. I call myself a Sidhi of the Brass Coast. We are far from home, but the tide remembers.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Mercantile noir, post‑colonial tension, ghost‑lit coast.

\textbf{Starting Location:} The pepper auction floor, where a hundred merchants clap their bids and a whalebone hammer cracks. A Sidhi port-master pulls you aside: ``The Admiralty has doubled the tariff on Sidhi goods. The lighthouse keepers are talking of a strike. And there is a green lantern ship in the harbour — the Drowned Captain's. The dead are offering passage. I need you to find out why.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The Brass Coast is Dhahara's southern rim: a humid, feverish coastline of palm groves, salt pans, and crowded port cities. The Kahfagian Admiralty administers it as a ``client province,'' but the Sidhi run the real trade, and the local Dhaharan rajas chafe under foreign tariffs. The air smells of pepper, cinnamon, and the low tide of a thousand ships. Here, a man can buy anything — a ship, a spice lot, a freedman's seal — and sell anything except his own shadow. The Admiralty's Maritime Court settles disputes with a judge's gavel and a marine's cutlass. The Sidhi lighthouse courts offer an alternative: justice by green flame, witnessed by the drowned. The ropeways that carry spice baskets from ship to warehouse are braided with Sidhi hair — a reminder that the dead still pull their weight.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The heat pressing down like a damp cloth. The constant noise of haggling in a dozen languages. The way the sea is the same colour as the sky, so the horizon disappears.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never board a ship that has a green lantern at the mast. It means the captain is under a death vow — and you are the payment.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Weight of the Tariff}

Power here is measured in spice lots, tariff exemptions, and the allegiance of the lighthouse keepers. The Admiralty holds the legal authority, but the Sidhi hold the ropes — literally. The ropeways that move cargo are Sidhi-built and Sidhi-maintained; a Sidhi strike could halt all trade in a day. The native Dhaharan rajas of the inland hills resent the Admiralty's taxes and the Sidhi's influence, but they need the ports to sell their iron and tea. The Covenant has a Maritime Court in every major port, but the Admiralty often ignores its rulings. The Brass Coast is not a colony; it is an argument.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:brasscoast-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 → 4, 6–10 → 6, J/Q/K → 8, A → 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (charters, lenses, seals) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{coast-omens}—green flames that burn blue, salt that tastes sweet, a drowned face in a still puddle.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:brasscoast-mechanics}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Sidhi Diaspora Network:} Sidhi characters gain +1 die on social rolls with other Sidhi, but –1 die on social rolls with Admiralty officials (who view them with suspicion). Once per session, a Sidhi PC can declare a “lighthouse contact” in any port — a cousin, a freedman, a former shipmate who will offer safe harbour or a warning.
\item \textbf{The Tariff Clock [6]:} Represents the Admiralty's pressure on local trade. Each time the party bribes an official, smuggles goods, or is caught without papers, tick the clock. At 3 segments: all goods cost +1 Supply. At 6: a general audit is declared — all ships are searched, and the party gains a “Wanted by Customs” tag (social DV +1 in any Admiralty-controlled port).
\item \textbf{Green Flame Justice:} Sidhi lighthouse courts operate on the principle of “witnessed truth.” Any party member may request a green flame judgment instead of a conventional trial. Roll Presence + Lore (DV 4). On success, the judge rules in your favour; on a miss, the green flame shows a hidden crime you committed, and the penalty is doubled.
\item \textbf{Ropeway Leverage:} The ropeways are the arteries of the coast. A character who controls a ropeway (by owning a share, bribing a foreman, or holding a Sidhi charter) may impose a “ropeway toll” on any cargo moving through that district: +1 Supply to the party or –1 Supply to a rival. Using this leverage ticks the Tariff Clock by 1.
\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Green Keeper} — The Witness. *The flame that shows the truth, even when it burns.*
    \item \textbf{The Hook} — Maelstraeus. *The price of a second chance; the interest on a freedman's soul.*
    \item \textbf{The Tide Father} — Khemesh. *The pressure beneath the keel; the debt that the sea collects.*
    \item \textbf{The Lantern Bearer} — The Traveler. *The light that guides lost ships — and lost souls.*
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Sidhi Freedmen and the Breath-less (Ashaan):} The Sidhi of the Brass Coast have family who were taken as slaves to Ashaan. A freedman's seal can be used to smuggle a Breath-less agent out of Galanina. Captain Idris has done this nine times. The tenth may be his last.
  \item \textbf{Spice Trade and the Fhara (Way of Silk):} Pepper from the Brass Coast is a major commodity on the Fhara caravan routes. A Fhara well-judge may accept a Sidhi lighthouse charter as proof of credit. The charter must be witnessed by a living flame.
  \item \textbf{The Undercliff and the Kalrantha Amulet (Dhahara):} The smuggler's cove is a known transit point for artifacts stolen from the Himadri temple. The Sea King's ninth wife claims to have seen the amulet pass through. She also claims the amulet spoke to her. It asked for a drink of water.
  \item \textbf{The Drowned Priest and the Hollow (Eastern Realms):} The ghost who walks the shore at low tide is said to be a Debt-Walker who lost his way. Aelaerem leave offerings of salt to keep him from following them home. The salt must be from the pans of the Silent Widows. Any other salt will attract him.
\end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Cut the Tariff (Presence + Sway):} On a hit, freeze range for one exchange; if a lighthouse charter is in your possession, +1 die.
\item \textbf{Weather the Raid (Resolve + Spirit):} On a hit in \emph{Admiral's Press Gang}, you pick who is taken; on a miss, the GM does.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
On the Brass Coast, power flows through tariffs, lighthouse charters, and the weight of the green flame. The Admiralty holds the legal authority, but the Sidhi control the ropes and the courts. The native rajas of the inland hills resent both, but they need the ports. The Covenant's Black Bishop is the only neutral arbiter — and he is not as neutral as he seems.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power on the Brass Coast is measured in spice lots, tariff exemptions, and the loyalty of the lighthouse keepers. Patronage often takes the form of lighthouse charters, freedman's seals, or a Black Bishop's ruling — each tied to a specific port, ship, or court. To emphasize the Sidhi minority presence:
\begin{itemize}
\item Make Sidhi NPCs scarce but influential. A Sidhi port-master may be the only person who can issue a freedman's seal.
\item Let the Admiralty's tariffs be a recurring obstacle — players must find Sidhi smugglers or bribe officials to avoid ruinous taxes.
\item Use the tension between the Maritime Court and the lighthouse courts as a source of adventures: a disputed verdict, a bribed judge, a ghost that testifies.
\end{itemize}
On the Brass Coast, the sea forgets nothing — and the green flame remembers every face.
\end{tcolorbox}
