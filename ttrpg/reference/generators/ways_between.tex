\section{The Ways Between --- ``Spiritways \& Veilways''}
\label{chap:ways-between}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{The Wayfinder (Elite):}  
  ``I exist only in the moment of choice, the breath between one path and another. To guide here is to understand that every way leads somewhere, but only the wise know which somethings are worth the journey.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Lost Pilgrim (Commoner):}  
  ``The paths here don't care where you think you're going – they care where you need to be. Best walk softly and listen to what the waystones whisper when you pass. I've been walking for... I forget how long. But the road hasn't lost me yet.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Between Death and Dreams lies a country of crossings. Roads remember promises; milestones remember names you never spoke. The Ways Between do not measure miles—they measure choices. Here, a wrong turn might lead to yesterday, or to a version of yourself who made a different decision. The mist does not hide landmarks; it hides destinations. Every threshold demands a toll, and the currency is always something you did not know you had: a memory, a fear, a forgotten debt. Walk softly, pay what the path asks, and mind which version of you arrives.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The air tastes of copper and old keys. Your footsteps echo half a beat too late. The waystones are warm—and they pulse like hearts.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never answer a question asked by a reflection. The answer will bind you to the path you did not choose.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Road That Remembers}

Here, the road is not a thing—it is a witness. It remembers every traveler who has walked it, every promise made at a crossroads, every lie told to a milestone. The Ways Between do not forgive, but they do not forget either. They offer passage in exchange for truth. The longer you walk, the more the path learns about you. By the time you reach your destination, you may find that the destination knows you better than you know yourself. Some travelers never leave. They become waystones, their faces pressed into moss, their names still warm on the lips of the next pilgrim.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A crossroad shrine of black glass stones, where travelers meet their reflections coming the other way. The shrine is silent except for the soft hum of the stones. A Backwards Pilgrim stands at the crossroads, facing the direction you came from. “The Thirteenth Stone speaks,” they say. “But the path chooses who arrives. What did you bury on the road here? The stones can smell it.”

\paragraph*{(Path/Crossing/Threshold)} Mist‑shrouded ford where water runs uphill; footsteps echo backwards; bone‑lit corridor where shadows walk the walls.

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:ways-between-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Mist-Ford} --- Water runs uphill; footsteps echo backwards through time.
  \hfill \textit{The ford shows you wading through your own past. Do not look down at the water.}
\item \textbf{Bone Corridor} --- Shadows walk the walls but never touch the floor; light as memory.
  \hfill \textit{The bones are not from any creature you know. They shift when you blink.}
\item \textbf{Threshold Arch} --- Shows a reflection as someone you might have been; the past as a mirror.
  \hfill \textit{The reflection has a scar you never got. It asks how you avoided it.}
\item \textbf{Spiral Path} --- Ascends but returns to the same stone marker; the journey as meditation.
  \hfill \textit{Each loop, the stone moves closer to the center. Or you do.}
\item \textbf{Promise Bridge} --- Cross with a vow and it remembers your debt; an oath as a toll.
  \hfill \textit{The bridge's planks are old letters. Read one, and you may owe the writer.}
\item \textbf{Crossroads} --- All four paths lead to the same destination, but different tomorrows.
  \hfill \textit{The center stone is warm. A coin placed there will vanish—or be accepted.}
\item \textbf{Stone Circle} --- Marks where a village used to be; the houses are now constellations.
  \hfill \textit{The stars are too close. You can touch them. They crumble like ash.}
\item \textbf{Memory Tunnel} --- Hear the thoughts of those who passed here; the past as an echo.
  \hfill \textit{The thoughts are in a language you almost understand. One word is your name.}
\item \textbf{Fossil Stairs} --- Each step is an eye that watches your ascent; the climb as scrutiny.
  \hfill \textit{The eyes belong to those who fell here. They blink in sequence.}
\item[J] \textbf{Waystation} --- The keeper trades in unfinished conversations; words as currency.
  \hfill \textit{The keeper has no face. It wears the faces of those who paused too long.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Junction} --- Paths physically collide; you must choose which reality to follow.
  \hfill \textit{The collision sounds like breaking glass. The glass reforms into a door.}
\item[K] \textbf{Long Mile} --- The road stretches differently for each traveler; distance as perception.
  \hfill \textit{You are the only one walking. The others are already at the end.}
\item[A] \textbf{Thirteenth Stone} --- Where the path reveals its true destination; truth as revelation.
  \hfill \textit{The stone whispers. The whisper is a promise you made to yourself and broke.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Wayfarer/Spirit/Dream-walker)} Lost pilgrim who thinks they're going home but forgot where; toll‑taker accepting payment in forgotten memories.

\section*{Hearts --- Travelers \& Guides}
\label{sec:ways-between-people}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Lost Pilgrim} --- Thinks they're going home but forgot where; destination as a memory.
  \hfill \textit{Their pack is full of stones. Each stone is a forgotten promise.}
\item \textbf{Toll-Taker} --- Accepts payment in memories you didn't know you had.
  \hfill \textit{Their scales weigh truth and echo. A balanced scale means you pass—for now.}
\item \textbf{Wayward Guide} --- Directions technically correct, morally questionable.
  \hfill \textit{They point with a broken compass. The needle spins, but the path is true.}
\item \textbf{Dream-Merchant} --- Carries a sack of nightmares and one perfect dream for sale.
  \hfill \textit{The perfect dream is their own. They are selling it because they cannot wake up.}
\item \textbf{Child-Ghost} --- Knows shortcuts but charges in riddles; wisdom as a puzzle.
  \hfill \textit{The riddles have no answer—only a choice. Choose wrong, and you become a child.}
\item \textbf{Wounded Walker} --- Bleeding metaphor; their pain grows thorns along the path.
  \hfill \textit{The thorns have faces. Each face is someone they failed to save.}
\item \textbf{Map-Merchant} --- Selling maps to places that don't exist yet; the future as a commodity.
  \hfill \textit{The maps change as you watch. The destination is always one page ahead.}
\item \textbf{Guide-Dog} --- Made of shadow and starlight; follows those who walk with purpose.
  \hfill \textit{It never barks. It whines when you are about to make a mistake.}
\item \textbf{Backwards Pilgrim} --- Moving through time; their steps erase what just happened.
  \hfill \textit{They are walking toward their birth. Every step, they forget something.}
\item[J] \textbf{Soul-Ferryman} --- Carries the luggage of the living; a burden as a service.
  \hfill \textit{The luggage is full of things you never said. The ferryman knows them all.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Road's Child} --- Born where paths crossed, raised by waymarks; navigation as instinct.
  \hfill \textit{They have no navel. They were never born—they simply began.}
\item[K] \textbf{Dead-Road Keeper} --- Ensures proper passage for those who should not walk.
  \hfill \textit{Their lantern burns a cold blue. The flame does not flicker.}
\item[A] \textbf{The Wayfinder} --- Exists only in the moment a choice is made; a decision as an entity.
  \hfill \textit{They are every guide you ever needed. They are also every road you refused.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Veil‑Thin/Dream‑Bleed/Wayward)} Path loops back to show your funeral preparations; reality thins—you see the dreams of sleeping travelers.

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:ways-between-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Funeral Loop} --- The path shows your funeral preparations; death as a destination.
  \hfill \textit{The mourners are people you have not met yet. They seem to know you well.}
\item \textbf{Reality Thins} --- You see the dreams of sleeping travelers; the boundary as a veil.
  \hfill \textit{A dreamer mutters your name. They are dreaming of a choice you will make tomorrow.}
\item \textbf{Waymark Lies} --- Points the wrong way; trust leads to places that never were.
  \hfill \textit{The waymark has a crack. Through the crack, you see a path you should have taken.}
\item \textbf{Dream-Bleed} --- Waking memories become someone else's nightmares.
  \hfill \textit{You remember a childhood fear that was not yours. It still terrifies you.}
\item \textbf{Toll Demanded} --- Currency you did not know you carried; payment as a revelation.
  \hfill \textit{The toll is a secret you told a dead person. They are now standing behind you.}
\item \textbf{Path Split} --- Each version of you remembers differently; identity as a choice.
  \hfill \textit{One you is smiling. One you is weeping. One you is already walking away.}
\item \textbf{Gravity Shifts} --- Emotional weight grows physically heavy; a burden as physics.
  \hfill \textit{Your pack feels heavier. Inside is a regret you forgot you carried.}
\item \textbf{Time-Sickness} --- You arrive before you left; paradox shadows follow.
  \hfill \textit{Your past self waves at you. Do not wave back.}
\item \textbf{Truth Payment} --- The road remembers your lies and demands truth as payment.
  \hfill \textit{The lie is carved on a stone. The stone is warm. It knows your temperature.}
\item[J] \textbf{Crossroads Judgment} --- The path you chose judges the ones you did not; decision as karma.
  \hfill \textit{A ghost of the road not taken asks why. You have one sentence to answer.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Memory-Thief} --- A rest stop keeper pays in counterfeit recollections.
  \hfill \textit{The memory is almost right. The color of the sky is wrong.}
\item[K] \textbf{Forbidden Path} --- Opens only for those already lost; loss as a key.
  \hfill \textit{The path smells of old tears. You taste salt on your lips.}
\item[A] \textbf{Convergence} --- All travelers arrive together, none recall how; arrival as a mystery.
  \hfill \textit{You recognize everyone. You have never seen any of them before.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(True Name/Safe Passage/Waywisdom)} Waymark that always points toward your next important choice; a token of passage that the road recognizes as belonging.

\section*{Diamonds --- Rewards/Leverage}
\label{sec:ways-between-rewards}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Choice Compass} --- Always points toward your next important choice.
  \hfill \textit{The needle is a splinter of the Thirteenth Stone. It hums when you are near a decision.}
\item \textbf{Passage Token} --- The road recognizes you as one who belongs.
  \hfill \textit{The token is a braid of your own hair. You do not remember cutting it.}
\item \textbf{Dream-Catcher} --- Filters nightmares from your rest; sleep as a sanctuary.
  \hfill \textit{The catcher is woven from old promises. It smells of lavender and regret.}
\item \textbf{Truth-Compass} --- Points to what you most need to know, not what you want.
  \hfill \textit{The compass has no needle. The housing spins until you speak a truth.}
\item \textbf{Memory-Anchor} --- Keeps you from losing yourself on deeper paths.
  \hfill \textit{The anchor is a river stone. Your name is carved on it in a language you have never seen.}
\item \textbf{Safe Haven} --- A guaranteed rest stop without complication.
  \hfill \textit{The haven is a dry arch. The arch is a doorframe with no door.}
\item \textbf{Guide-Light} --- Burns the color of your truest intention; purpose as a beacon.
  \hfill \textit{The light is blue. Blue means you are honest. Red means you are not.}
\item \textbf{Path-Cutter} --- Cuts distance through metaphor, not space.
  \hfill \textit{The cutter is a pair of shears. Cut a memory, and the road between here and there vanishes.}
\item \textbf{Debt-Clearing} --- The road forgives one obligation you thought you owed.
  \hfill \textit{The obligation was to a dead person. They are no longer dead on this path.}
\item[J] \textbf{Crossroads Boon} --- Choose among three paths, each exactly where you need to be.
  \hfill \textit{The paths are labeled: Regret, Forgive, Forget. Choose carefully.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Way-Wisdom} --- Understand the language of paths and signs for one journey.
  \hfill \textit{The waystones whisper. They are complaining about the last traveler. It was you.}
\item[K] \textbf{Grace Passage} --- Walk safely through any dangerous crossing for one night.
  \hfill \textit{The grace is a bell. Ring it, and the road will pretend not to see you.}
\item[A] \textbf{Road's Name} --- Call the path by its true name and command its nature.
  \hfill \textit{The name is a single syllable. Speaking it will change the road—and you.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:ways-between-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (true names/safe passages/waywisdom) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{dream‑road} motifs—reflections that lie, paths that judge, destinations that choose the traveler.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Dream-Logic Navigation:} Any Ace introduces a metaphysical requirement (a sacrifice, a taboo, an exchange) that must be satisfied to pass. Name it at the table; the road will enforce it.
\item \textbf{Reflection Points:} Face cards (J, Q, K) show alternate selves or unlived choices. Treat them as temporary NPCs or scene tags that can help, hinder, or tempt.
\item \textbf{Memory Currency:} Diamonds may be traded for knowledge or safe passage as if they were memories. Describe the memory paid; the path (or its keeper) pays in kind.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Bitter Root:} Daughters of the Cord know the Ways Between as the Cord's shadow. A lost Hollowed wanders here, its thread trailing into the mist. Follow it, and you may find a Sister who cut herself free.
  \item \textbf{The Threshold Compendium:} Saikou Ira's silver mirror shows the Ways Between as a tangled knot of unfulfilled obligations. He has walked three paths here. He will not speak of the fourth.
  \item \textbf{Malachai, the Chained Angel:} A silver coin that never spends lies at every crossroads. Pick it up, and the road will offer you a shortcut—but the coin will reappear in your pocket at the next junction, heavier.
  \item \textbf{The Grey Wanderer's Grimoire:} Psions call the Ways Between “the Stair.” They climb it in their dreams, trading memories for glimpses of paths not yet walked. Some never wake.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} On any path in the Ways Between, the ninth step you take in a straight line will land on a threshold you did not see. The wise take eight steps, then pause to look around.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Ways Between Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Stones:} Crumble a crust at the base of a waystone. Ignore the first \emph{Waymark Lies} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Path:} Tie a single knot in a piece of string and hang it from a milestone. Cancel \emph{Path Split} or take –1 die on your next navigation roll (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Crossroads:} Whisper a dead traveler's name into the center stone. Gain +1 Effect when bargaining with a Toll‑Taker this scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Wayfinder.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In the Ways Between, power flows through understanding the metaphysical nature of paths, the ability to navigate dream‑logic, and the careful management of obligations and truths. The true authorities are those who know the True Names of paths, can mediate between alternate versions of travelers, and understand the currency of memory and choice. Power here is not about commanding others, but about understanding the fundamental nature of journey and destination.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in the Ways Between revolves around guidance, safe passage, and the ability to navigate metaphysical requirements. Rewards often take the form of waymarks, tokens, and knowledge that can be leveraged into greater understanding. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, compasses, lights) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival guides issue conflicting directions, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the crossroads, waystations, and thresholds as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of path‑logic determines success.
\end{itemize}
In the Ways Between, your understanding is your power, and your power determines whether you walk or are walked.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Path‑Moves \& Procedures (Optional Depth)}
\label{sec:ways-between-procedures}

\paragraph{Weighing of Steps (Travel Move)}
When the company enters a liminal path (Mist‑Ford, Bone Corridor, etc.), choose one:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Pay the Toll:} Each PC names a small truth or a fond memory (GM: 1 SB banked, +1 Position on navigation).
  \item \textbf{Refuse the Toll:} +1d on Speed, but start a \textbf{Way‑Debt} [4] clock.
  \item \textbf{Bargain the Toll:} Trade a \emph{Diamond} reward as if it were a memory (see Memory Exchange below); cancel one imminent \emph{Club} card in this scene.
\end{itemize}
When \textbf{Way‑Debt} fills, trigger \emph{Crossroads Judgment} at the next junction.

\paragraph{Reflection Duel (Social/Moral Test)}
When confronted by an alternate self or \emph{Reflection Point}, state what that self wants from you and roll your best social action.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Success:} Gain a \emph{Guide‑Light} tag for the scene and clear 1 tick from \textbf{Paths Decide}.
  \item \textbf{Mixed:} Trade places for one exchange or take a \textbf{Condition: Disoriented}.
  \item \textbf{Miss:} The reflection walks away with one of your \emph{Diamonds} (GM picks the most narratively apt).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Memory Offered} & \textbf{Buys You} & \textbf{Side Effect} \\

\medskip
\textbf{Exchange Rule:} If you pay the same \emph{kind} of memory twice in a journey, start \textbf{Hollowing} [4]. When it fills, you gain the \emph{Backwards Pilgrim} tag until you reclaim what was lost.

\subsection*{Way‑Keys \& Taboos (d6)}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Speak Last}: Only the final word said at a gate is true; others are “practice.” First speakers take –1d on their next test.
\item \textbf{Step Odd}: Crossings require an odd number of steps; even counts loop you back one scene.
\item \textbf{Name Nothing}: True names wake \emph{Name‑Theft}; use titles or lose a \emph{Diamond}.
\item \textbf{Wet Iron}: A drop of water on iron cancels one glamour once per scene.
\item \textbf{Back Gift}: Leave something behind to go forward; refuse and tick \textbf{Way‑Debt}.
\item \textbf{Answer Bent}: Answer a waystone's question with a metaphor, not a fact, or trigger \emph{Truth Payment}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Extensions (Plug-in)}
\label{sec:ways-between-extensions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Choice Dial (Resolve $\leftrightarrow$ Drift):} Track the party's footing between firm intent and wandering.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Resolve High:} +1 Position on \emph{Path Split} tests; the first \emph{Dream‑Bleed} each session hits harder (start at 2).
    \item \emph{Drift High:} +1 Effect when bargaining with way‑entities; the first \emph{Truth Payment} each session costs a cherished \emph{detail}.
    \item Center the Dial by naming aloud the next choice \emph{and} why you might refuse it; clear 1 tick from \textbf{Way‑Debt}.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Counter‑Seal (once/scene, if justified):} Break a single metaphysical instruction (taboo, key, or omen) by presenting a memory in symbol form (a token, a letter, a sketch). Gain +1d on that crossing; the GM banks 1 SB as a \emph{Marked Consequence} to spend when reflections return.

  \item \textbf{Start‑of‑Scene Murmurs (1d6):}
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \emph{Second Sunrise:} light arrives twice; your first missed navigation roll becomes a mixed success.
    \item \emph{Name Draft:} the air steals a syllable; your next lie sounds true once.
    \item \emph{Echo Debt:} your footprints owe a step—skip a square or tick \textbf{Way‑Debt} +1.
    \item \emph{Pale Bell:} time hiccups; the first \emph{Club} can be delayed until the end of the scene.
    \item \emph{Traveler's Chorus:} distant voices agree with you; +1d on appeals to path entities.
    \item \emph{Hollow Weather:} rain that does not wet; conditions look dire but impose no penalties this scene.
  \end{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{Plug‑in SB Conversions (Ways Between Flavor):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item 1 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Footprint Vote}: your tracks choose a different direction; increase DV by 1 on any roll that relies on following a trail.
    \item 2 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Echo of Refusal}: a choice you avoided manifests as a minor obstacle (a locked gate, a forgotten toll).
