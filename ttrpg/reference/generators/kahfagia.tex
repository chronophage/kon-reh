\section{Kahfagia --- ``Pilot's Mirror''}
\label{chap:kahfagia}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Admiral of Lantern-Law (Elite):}  
  ``Every mirror is a blade, every beacon a promise. The sea obeys when we move the lights --- and woe to the fool who rows against them.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Dockside Factor (Commoner):}  
  ``The pilots see farther than kings. A gesture in lantern-code can open ports, or close them. Keep your debts paid, and your lamps trimmed.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
In Kahfagia, the sea is a contract. Every channel is a clause, every beacon a signature. The Admiralty of Lantern-Law does not rule with armies---they rule with light. Shift a beacon, and a harbor closes. Dim a mirror, and a fleet founders. Foreign powers curse the ``pilot's monopoly,'' but they still pay the toll. Because the alternative is sailing blind.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The constant chime of bell-buoys. The smell of salt, tar, and burnt oil from a thousand lanterns. The way everyone---admiral to dockhand---glances at the tide clock before speaking.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never argue with a Mirror-Keeper about where the channel lies. If they say the safe water moved, it moved. Your chart is already kindling.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Lantern's Vigil}

Power is visible here---literally. Every night, a thousand lamps burn along the channels, each a statement of control. To dim a light is to declare bankruptcy. To move a beacon is to declare war. Commodores sit in their mirror‑towers, watching reflections, while tide‑clocks tick down to the next auction, the next inspection, the next quiet murder in a fog‑shrouded creek.

\textbf{Starting Location:} The Lantern Pier admiralty chapel, where votive ships hang from the ceiling and brine drips from the icon of a drowned saint. A sea‑notary unlocks a salt‑stained reliquary and produces a chart with gilt edges. ``Fetch the contraband bell from the corsair galleass \emph{Santa Dolorosa} before moonrise---or the harbor names you accomplices.''

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:kahfagia-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (papers/rights/priority) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{sea \& signal} motifs---beacons that migrate, tides that remember, law that flows like water.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Pilot's Mirror:} Kahfagia's strength is not fleet but monopoly on light and lane. To post a beacon is to redraw the map.
\item \textbf{Oligarchic Rule:} Families rule by shares in ports and auctions; politics is brokerage in ledgers and lanterns.

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Saint Alvaro of the Lantern (The Traveler):} The Admiralty's mirror‑towers double as wayshrines for the Saint of Lost Channels. Sailors who light a candle before a voyage and whisper a destination to the mirror claim the tide will not turn against them before they reach it. The Mirror‑Keepers charge a fee for this service—payable in salt, not silver.
  \item \textbf{Saint Lucia of the Undying Flame (Oath of Flame \& Light):} The Lantern Pier chapel keeps a single flame that has burned for three hundred years. It is said that any oath sworn over that flame cannot be broken—but the breaker will see their own reflection in every wave until they drown. Privateers pay handsomely to have their letters of marque consecrated there.
  \item \textbf{The Tide‑Sworn Compact (The Admiral of Sealed Tides):} A small but influential order of notaries based in Kassamira Port. They adjudicate disputes over beacon rights, cargo claims, and pilotage fees. Their judgments are always delivered in triplicate, and they accept no bribes—only binding oaths. Those who lie before a Covenant judge find that their next three voyages are plagued by contrary winds.
  \item \textbf{The Hospitallers of the Salted Veil:} A hospice on Deadman's Quay is run by grey‑robed healers of the Order. They ask no payment, but they never forget a face. It is said that if you accept their care, you owe a favour to the sea itself—and the sea always collects. Smugglers leave small offerings of fresh water on the pier to stay in the Order's good graces.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any lantern‑lit scene, the ninth lamp lit in a room reveals a threshold. The wise stop at eight. Those who light the ninth are said to see a figure standing on the water outside the window—waiting.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Kahfagia Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Wake:} Toss a crust over your shoulder before entering a new channel. Ignore the first \emph{Smothering Fog} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Turn the Lantern:} Spin the binnacle lamp once counter‑clockwise. Cancel \emph{Boom Slam} or take --1 die on your next pilot check (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Deep:} Whisper a true name into a lead line. Gain +1 Effect against corsairs for the scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Tide Court.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Kahfagia, power flows through control of the sea lanes themselves. The maritime oligarchs have built their empire not through conquest of territory but through mastery of navigation. Every beacon, every channel marker, every mirror‑light is both a navigational aid and a legal instrument. To control the lights is to control the sea.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Kahfagian patronage revolves around access---to channels, to cargo, to legal protection. Rewards often take the form of pilotage tokens, safe‑conducts, or salvage rights that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (beacons, writs, lantern codes) that can be challenged, moved, or voided.
\item Let rival admirals issue conflicting safe‑conducts, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the Admiralty courts and spice auctions as arenas for social contests, where legal maneuvering determines success.
\end{itemize}
In Kahfagia, your route is your rank, and your rank determines your right to passage.
\end{tcolorbox}
