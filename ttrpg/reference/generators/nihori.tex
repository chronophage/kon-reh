\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Duel at dawn. Honor the storm. The ninth wave never breaks.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Storm Emperor's Ghost (Elite):}  
  ``I ruled the waves before the Hollow learned to walk. My empire sank because I spoke a name I should not have. Do not speak my name. Do not ask what it was. The sea remembers. That is enough.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Wanderer (Commoner):}  
  ``I carry a broken sword and a grudge. The sword was my father's. The grudge is mine. The man who killed him died nine years ago. I still walk the isles, looking for his ghost. The ghost is looking for me too.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Storm−tossed honor duels, kami politics, oni cults, volcanic doom, drowned ghosts, yokai in the ash.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A bamboo grove on a clifftop, where two duelists face each other at dawn. Ash from the volcano dusts their shoulders. A Shrine Maiden rings a copper bell: ``The Shogun's envoy demands the duel stop. The daimyo of the southern isle says it will not. The storm−spirit is watching. Choose a side −−− or the ninth wave will choose for you.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme & Atmosphere}]
Nihori is a chain of volcanic islands, shrouded in mist and lashed by storms −−− a shattered shogunate where the old empire sank beneath the waves and the \textbf{Lethai−al} guard the drowned kingdom's memory. The people are wanderers, duelists, shrine maidens, and oni−touched outcasts. They worship the storm spirits (kami of wind, wave, and crater) and fear the ninth wave. The Hollow breathes in the salt foam; Oath−Keepers ride the tide. The Shogun sits in a half−ruined castle, his authority challenged by three rival daimyo who sharpen their blades and their treaties. \textbf{Oni cults} whisper in the ash wastes, offering forgotten bargains. \textbf{Y{\=u}rei} (vengeful ghosts) wail from sunken ships. \textbf{Kappa} drag children from tidal pools. \textbf{Tengu} mock travelers from the high crags. The Storm Emperor's ghost does not rule −−− but his silence haunts every island. The ninth taboo is absolute: never speak the name of the drowned capital. The sea will hear you, and it will ask why you called.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of salt and volcanic ash. The way every traveler carries a sword, even the tea−sellers. The constant sound of bells on shrine gates −−− ringing in the wind. And the silence when a daimyo's retinue passes. The distant wail of a \textit{funay{\=u}rei} (a drowned sailor's ghost) calling for a boat that never comes.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never draw steel in a shrine. The kami will witness. They may not forgive.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Weight of the Wave and the Edge of the Blade}

Power here is measured in duels won, storms survived, and the number of clans that bow to your banner. The Shogun rules from Castle Kurotani, but his writ fades as the islands stretch into the mist. Three daimyo vie for supremacy: the \textbf{Northern Crane} (old blood, slow to anger, quicker to negotiate), the \textbf{Southern Serpent} (pragmatic, pirates in his pay, a smile like a cracked bell), and the \textbf{Western Oni} (rumored to have made a pact with a Crater Oni, his eyes glow faintly red in the dark). Between them, the \textbf{Shrine Maidens} keep the peace −−− not through steel, but through storm−calling and the threat of the ninth wave. A duel is not merely a fight; it is a negotiation sanctified by kami. First blood decides the truth. The loser yields a story. The winner returns a cup of sake −−− and a prayer at the nearest shrine.

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
łabel{sec:nihori−features}

\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Duel Law:} Disputes in Nihori are settled by duel to first blood. The loser yields a story; the winner returns a cup of sake. A duel can be witnessed by anyone; the witness's word is law. Refusing a duel is an admission of guilt. In the shogunate courts, a duel verdict can overturn a written agreement.
ℹtem \textbf{Storm Season Timer [8]:} Tracks the approach of the monsoon. When it fills, a typhoon strikes −−− all travel becomes Desperate for 2 legs. The timer ticks each day during storm season. The Shrine Maidens can delay it by one day for a suitable offering (a tide−charm, a memory, or a promise).
ℹtem \textbf{Kami Favor (Reputation Track):} Instead of debt, track \textbf{Kami Favor [6]}. Gain favor by respecting shrines, winning duels honorably, and appeasing storm−spirits. Spend favor to ask a kami for a boon (see Kami Favor Boons table). At 0 favor, shrines are hostile −−− all social rolls at shrines are Desperate.
ℹtem \textbf{Oni Corruption Track [6]:} Represents the influence of the Oni−Eyed's cult and the Crater Oni. Tick when a character makes a bargain with an oni, drinks oni−brewed sake, or speaks the forbidden name. At 3 segments: the character's shadow grows horns. At 6: the character becomes oni−touched −−− they gain $+1$ die to intimidation but suffer $−1$ die to all social rolls with shrine maidens and can never enter a shrine without being attacked.
ℹtem \textbf{Shogunate Faction Timers:} Track the three daimyo's schemes:
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Southern Serpent's Ambition [6]} −−− advances when she bribes officials, builds her fleet, or sponsors pirate raids. When full, she declares open war on the Shogun.
    ℹtem \textbf{Northern Crane's Patience [6]} −−− retreats when he is humiliated or his allies defect. When it reaches 0, he abandons the Shogun's peace and marches south.
    ℹtem \textbf{Oni−Eyed's Ascension [8]} −−− advances each time a character accepts an oni bargain or the party fails to stop a cult ritual. When full, the Crater Oni manifests in the material world.
  \end{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Yokai and the Old Exchange:} Unlike simple monsters, yokai in Nihori treat with travelers. They may offer bargains, demand tolls, or curse those who break taboos. Use the Yokai Encounters table to introduce them, and track any promises made to a yokai as a \textit{Yokai Promise} timer [4]. When it fills, the yokai returns to call it in.
ℹtem \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{The Storm Emperor's Ghost} −−− Khemesh + The Witness. \textit{The pressure of a forbidden name, the memory of a drowned kingdom.}
    ℹtem \textbf{The Crater Oni} −−− Malachai. \textit{Cursed gifts, bargains that leave scars, power that transforms you from the inside.}
    ℹtem \textbf{The Ninth Wave} −−− Ra{\'e}yn. \textit{The storm that does not break, the tide that remembers every sailor.}
    ℹtem \textbf{The Bell of Drowned Shrines} −−− The Sealed Gate. \textit{The boundary between the living and the dead, guarded by bells and salt.}
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross−Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem \textbf{The Drowned Capital and the Kalrantha Amulet (Dhahara):} The sunken city is said to contain a temple that holds a fragment of the demon Varash. The Veiled Sisters want it. The Oni−Eyed wants to bargain with it. The Shrine Maidens want it to stay drowned.
  ℹtem \textbf{Tide−Charms and the Tulkani (Way of Silk):} Tulkani story−tellers sometimes carry Lethai−al tide−charms as proof of a witnessed drowning. A charm from Nihori is worth twice as much −−− and attracts twice as much Hollow attention.
  ℹtem \textbf{Storm−Spirits and the Veiled Sisters (Ashaan):} The Veiled Sisters have tried to bind storm−spirits for use in their fleet. The Lethai−al have foiled nine attempts. The tenth attempt is being funded by the Southern Serpent's pirate gold.
  ℹtem \textbf{Oni Bargains and the Chain (Bitter Root):} Daughters of the Cord refuse to treat with oni −−− the price is always paid in souls, and the Cord cannot collect from a damned spirit. An oni−touched Daughter is a contradiction; one may be hiding in the ash wastes, trying to untie her cord from a Crater Oni's bargain.
  ℹtem \textbf{The Ninth Wave and the Hollow (Eastern Realms):} Aelaerem hedge−keepers say that the ninth wave is the Hollow's breath. When it crashes, the Hollow exhales. Those who hear it must speak a true name or lose one of their own.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage & Power}]
In Nihori, power flows through duels, storms, the favor of kami, and the dangerous bargains of oni. The Shogun is a figurehead; the three daimyo are the real players. A clan chief's authority rests on how many duels he has won and how many shrines he has funded. A shrine maiden's on how many storms she has predicted and how many oni she has banished. Yokai watch from the margins, ready to lend aid or call in a promise.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Use the \textbf{Storm Season Timer } as a visible threat. Let \textbf{Duel Law} be a way to resolve conflicts without combat −−− or a way to escalate them when honor is insulted. Make \textbf{Tide−Charms} valuable but dangerous −−− each use may attract the Hollow or the attention of an oni. Track the three daimyo's \textbf{Faction Timers} as a campaign front; the party's actions can accelerate or delay civil war. Use \textbf{Kami Favor} as a measure of standing −−− players must choose which shrines to support and which kami to appease. The \textbf{Storm−Spirit Negotiation} and \textbf{Oni Cult Whispers} tables add depth to supernatural encounters, while \textbf{Duel Complications} keep combat fresh and honor−bound. Introduce \textbf{Yokai Encounters} to make the wilderness feel alive and capricious, and offer \textbf{Haunted Blades} to characters willing to accept a curse for power.
\end{tcolorbox}
