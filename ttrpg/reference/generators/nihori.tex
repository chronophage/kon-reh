\section{Nihori --- ``The Isles of the Dawn Spirit''}
\label{chap:nihori}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Duel at dawn. Honour the storm. The ninth wave never breaks.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Storm Emperor's Ghost (Elite):}  
  ``I ruled the waves before the Hollow learned to walk. My empire sank because I spoke a name I should not have. Do not speak my name. Do not ask what it was. The sea remembers. That is enough.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Wanderer (Commoner):}  
  ``I carry a broken sword and a grudge. The sword was my father's. The grudge is mine. The man who killed him died nine years ago. I still walk the isles, looking for his ghost. The ghost is looking for me too.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Storm-tossed honour duels, kami politics, oni cults, volcanic doom.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A bamboo grove on a clifftop, where two duelists face each other at dawn. Ash from the volcano dusts their shoulders. A Shrine Maiden rings a copper bell: ``The Shogun's envoy demands the duel stop. The daimyo of the southern isle says it will not. The storm-spirit is watching. Choose a side — or the ninth wave will choose for you.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Nihori is a chain of volcanic islands, shrouded in mist and lashed by storms — a shattered shogunate where the old empire sank beneath the waves and the Lethai-al guard the drowned kingdom's memory. The people are wanderers, duelists, shrine maidens, and oni-touched outcasts. They worship the storm spirits (kami of wind, wave, and crater) and fear the ninth wave. The Hollow breathes in the salt foam; Debt-Walkers ride the tide. The Shogun sits in a half-ruined castle, his authority challenged by three rival daimyo who sharpen their blades and their treaties. Oni cults whisper in the ash wastes, offering forgotten bargains. The Storm Emperor's ghost does not rule — but his silence haunts every island. The ninth taboo is absolute: never speak the name of the drowned capital. The sea will hear you, and it will ask why you called.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of salt and volcanic ash. The way every traveller carries a sword, even the tea-sellers. The constant sound of bells on shrine gates — ringing in the wind. And the silence when a daimyo's retinue passes.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never draw steel in a shrine. The kami will witness. They may not forgive.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Weight of the Wave and the Edge of the Blade}

Power here is measured in duels won, storms survived, and the number of clans that bow to your banner. The Shogun rules from Castle Kurotani, but his writ fades as the islands stretch into the mist. Three daimyo vie for supremacy: the Northern Crane (old blood, slow to anger, quicker to negotiate), the Southern Serpent (pragmatic, pirates in his pay, a smile like a cracked bell), and the Western Oni (rumoured to have made a pact with a Crater Oni, his eyes glow faintly red in the dark). Between them, the Shrine Maidens keep the peace — not through steel, but through storm-calling and the threat of the ninth wave. A duel is not merely a fight; it is a negotiation sanctified by kami. First blood decides the truth. The loser owes a story. The winner owes a cup of sake — and a prayer at the nearest shrine.

\subsection*{Storm-Spirit Negotiation (d6 Outcomes)}
\label{sec:nihori-storm-spirit}

When the party attempts to bargain with a storm kami (at a shrine, during a typhoon, or at the Ninth Wave's Rock), roll on this table to determine the outcome. The GM sets the initial DV based on the offering (a tide-charm, a story, or a cup of sake).

\begin{longtable}{|p{2cm}|p{10cm}|}
\hline
\rowcolor{gray!20}
\textbf{Outcome} & \textbf{Result} \\
\hline
\textbf{Critical Success} & The spirit grants a \textit{Storm-Charm} (ignore one weather penalty on any future voyage). The kami may also whisper a warning about the next oni cult activity. \\
\hline
\textbf{Success} & The spirit calms the storm for the party only. Other ships in the area are not protected. Gain +1 \textit{Kami Favour}. \\
\hline
\textbf{Partial} & The storm abates, but the spirit demands a tide-charm as payment. Gain a Debt Clock [4]. If you cannot pay, the storm returns in 1d4 hours. \\
\hline
\textbf{Failure} & The spirit is offended. All sailing rolls are Desperate for the next leg. The kami may send a \textit{kappa} or \textit{funayūrei} (drowned ghost) to harass the party. \\
\hline
\textbf{Critical Failure} & The ninth wave rises. Test Body + Athletics (DV 5) or be swept overboard. Lose 1 Supply and gain the \textit{Marked by the Storm} condition (weather hazards are one step worse for the next journey). \\
\hline
\end{longtable}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:nihori-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits. Highest rank sets main Clock.
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (tokens, bells, charms) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{sea-omens}—a wave that breaks twice, a bell that rings underwater, a name that echoes from the foam.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:nihori-features}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Duel Law:} Disputes in Nihori are settled by duel to first blood. The loser owes a story; the winner owes a cup of sake. A duel can be witnessed by anyone; the witness's word is law. Refusing a duel is an admission of guilt. In the shogunate courts, a duel verdict can overturn a written contract.
\item \textbf{Storm Season Clock [8]:} Tracks the approach of the monsoon. When it fills, a typhoon strikes — all travel becomes Desperate for 2 legs. The clock ticks each day during storm season. The Shrine Maidens can delay it by one day for a suitable offering (a tide-charm, a memory, or a promise).
\item \textbf{Kami Favour (Reputation Track):} Instead of debt, track \textbf{Kami Favour [6]}. Gain favour by respecting shrines, winning duels honourably, and appeasing storm-spirits. Spend favour to ask a kami for a boon (see Kami Favour Boons table). At 0 favour, shrines are hostile — all social rolls at shrines are Desperate.
\item \textbf{Oni Corruption Track [6]:} Represents the influence of the Oni-Eyed's cult and the Crater Oni. Tick when a character makes a bargain with an oni, drinks oni-brewed sake, or speaks the forbidden name. At 3 segments: the character's shadow grows horns. At 6: the character becomes oni-touched — they gain +1 die to intimidation but suffer –1 die to all social rolls with shrine maidens and can never enter a shrine without being attacked.
\item \textbf{Shogunate Faction Clocks:} Track the three daimyo's schemes:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Southern Serpent's Ambition [6]} — advances when she bribes officials, builds her fleet, or sponsors pirate raids. When full, she declares open war on the Shogun.
    \item \textbf{Northern Crane's Patience [6]} — retreats when he is humiliated or his allies defect. When it reaches 0, he abandons the Shogun's peace and marches south.
    \item \textbf{Oni-Eyed's Ascension [8]} — advances each time a character accepts an oni bargain or the party fails to stop a cult ritual. When full, the Crater Oni manifests in the material world.
  \end{itemize}
\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Storm Emperor's Ghost} — Khemesh + The Witness. *The pressure of a forbidden name, the memory of a drowned kingdom.*
    \item \textbf{The Crater Oni} — Malachai. *Cursed gifts, bargains that leave scars, power that transforms you from the inside.*
    \item \textbf{The Ninth Wave} — Raéyn. *The storm that does not break, the tide that remembers every sailor.*
    \item \textbf{The Bell of Drowned Shrines} — The Sealed Gate. *The boundary between the living and the dead, guarded by bells and salt.*
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Drowned Capital and the Kalrantha Amulet (Dhahara):} The sunken city is said to contain a temple that holds a fragment of the demon Varash. The Veiled Sisters want it. The Oni-Eyed wants to bargain with it. The Shrine Maidens want it to stay drowned.
  \item \textbf{Tide-Charms and the Tulkani (Way of Silk):} Tulkani story-tellers sometimes carry Lethai-al tide-charms as proof of a witnessed drowning. A charm from Nihori is worth twice as much — and attracts twice as much Hollow attention.
  \item \textbf{Storm-Spirits and the Veiled Sisters (Ashaan):} The Veiled Sisters have tried to bind storm-spirits for use in their fleet. The Lethai-al have foiled nine attempts. The tenth attempt is being funded by the Southern Serpent's pirate gold.
  \item \textbf{Oni Bargains and the Chain (Bitter Root):} Daughters of the Cord refuse to treat with oni — the interest is always paid in souls, and the Cord cannot collect from a damned spirit. An oni-touched Daughter is a contradiction; one may be hiding in the ash wastes, trying to untie her cord from a Crater Oni's bargain.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth Wave and the Hollow (Eastern Realms):} Aelaerem hedge-keepers say that the ninth wave is the Hollow's breath. When it crashes, the Hollow exhales. Those who hear it must speak a true name or lose one of their own.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Nihori, power flows through duels, storms, the favour of kami, and the dangerous bargains of oni. The Shogun is a figurehead; the three daimyo are the real players. A clan chief's authority rests on how many duels he has won and how many shrines he has funded. A shrine maiden's on how many storms she has predicted and how many oni she has banished.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Use the \textbf{Storm Season Clock} as a visible threat. Let \textbf{Duel Law} be a way to resolve conflicts without combat — or a way to escalate them when honour is insulted. Make \textbf{Tide-Charms} valuable but dangerous — each use may attract the Hollow or the attention of an oni. Track the three daimyo's \textbf{Faction Clocks} as a campaign front; the party's actions can accelerate or delay civil war. Use \textbf{Kami Favour} as a reputation currency — players must choose which shrines to support and which kami to appease. The \textbf{Storm-Spirit Negotiation} and \textbf{Oni Cult Whispers} tables add depth to supernatural encounters, while \textbf{Duel Complications} keep combat fresh and honour-bound.
\end{tcolorbox}
