\section{Linn --- ``Skerries \& Storm-Oaths''}
\label{chap:linn}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{High Jarl (Elite):}  
  ``The sea keeps better counsel than any scribe, and the storm remembers every oath sworn upon its winds. To rule these shores is to dance between their moods and never miss a step.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Net-Wife (Commoner):}  
  ``The water speaks to those who know how to listen – the way it moves tells you if the fish are running, if the weather's turning, or if something's coming that wasn't invited to the feast.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
North of the Aberderrin Sea, where fjords split the coast and skerries break the tide, dwell the Linn. They are traders and raiders in equal breath, their longships crowding the whale‑road and river mouths. They descend the Ylolka like an axe‑blade and cut into southern shores, or slip through the Thin Strip where the Valewood dares not touch salt. Yet even these storm‑oath people dread the Mistlands and the Valewood, whose fog and trees have no need of ships or harbors to claim their due.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of tar and salt fish. The creak of oars from unseen boats. The way everyone—jarl to thrall—touches a knotted cord before speaking of the sea.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never name a drowned person aloud while underway. The sea collects names, and if it hears you speaking one it doesn't recognize, it may reach up to correct the ledger.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Skerry Stormcall}

Power here is spoken, not written. A jarl's authority rests on the weight of his oath‑chest and the sharpness of his skald's tongue. Longships are not vessels; they are promises carved in timber. Every voyage is a gamble against tide, reef, and the attention of things that sleep beneath the waves. The Linn do not fear death—they fear dying without a witness, swallowed by fog where no verse will remember their name.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A longship house lined with storm‑charms and whale‑bone runes, salt wind clawing at the doors. A Net‑Wife binds a storm‑token into a PC's palm. “The Whale‑Road sings,” she says. “But the Mistlands whistle back. Bring us three keel‑lengths of ironwood from the Valewood edge, or the tide will take our boats in the spring.”

\paragraph*{(Fjord/Skerry/Hall)} Kelp‑skerry gut: a narrow channel where oars scrape weed and rock; tide‑shed of longship houses with tar smoke and gulls for sentries.

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:linn-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Kelp-Skerry Gut} --- Narrow channel where oars scrape weed and rock.
  \hfill \textit{Kelp strands like dead men's hair; row silently or the reef listens.}
\item \textbf{Tide-Shed} --- Longship houses; tar smoke and gulls for sentries.
  \hfill \textit{Each shed holds a keel's ghost; old crews sleep under the floor planks.}
\item \textbf{Wave-Gate Reef} --- Just off a raiding beach; only locals know the cut.
  \hfill \textit{One wrong turn and the wave breaks over you—twice.}
\item \textbf{Runestone Causey} --- Across tidal flat; names half lost to barnacle.
  \hfill \textit{Touch the stone with your left hand. If it's warm, the ancestors approve.}
\item \textbf{Herring-Stairs} --- Drying racks above cliff path; knives flicker like fish.
  \hfill \textit{Workers sing a counting song—eight verses, never nine.}
\item \textbf{Winter Hall} --- Turf walls, smoke‑eye roof, benches crowded with shields.
  \hfill \textit{The fire never goes out. Feed it a story each night or it goes hungry.}
\item \textbf{Mistlands Maze} --- Reed‑channels braid and vanish; navigation by instinct.
  \hfill \textit{The reeds whisper names you've forgotten. Do not answer.}
\item \textbf{Boomed Harbor} --- Iron rings in whale‑bone posts; controlled access.
  \hfill \textit{The chain was forged from a broken oath. It rattles when liars approach.}
\item \textbf{Aberderrin Headland} --- Current rips past wreck timbers in the kelp.
  \hfill \textit{On foggy nights, the drowned signal with lanterns. Never follow.}
\item[J] \textbf{Dolmis Waystation} --- Low isle with fresh water and old debts.
  \hfill \textit{The well is lined with copper. A single dropped coin returns as a blessing.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Thing-Holm} --- Flat islet ringed with stones for speaking and swearing.
  \hfill \textit{The stones have ears; oaths spoken here echo for three tides.}
\item[K] \textbf{High Jarl's Seat} --- Above fjord, beacon tower glaring out to sea.
  \hfill \textit{The beacon oil is mixed with whale fat. It burns green when a storm is close.}
\item[A] \textbf{Whale-Road Horizon} --- Open water where storms are born and names are made.
  \hfill \textit{Here, the sky meets the sea. Here, the dead meet the living.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Jarl/Oar‑Crew/Keeper)} Net‑wife who reads the sea like a ledger and the sky like a mood; steersman whose tiller‑hand never shakes.

\section*{Hearts --- People \& Factions}
\label{sec:linn-people}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Net-Wife} --- Reads sea like ledger, sky like mood; weather‑witch in all but name.
  \hfill \textit{She ties a knot for every storm she's survived. Her belt has thirty.}
\item \textbf{Steersman} --- Tiller‑hand never shakes, even when keel does.
  \hfill \textit{His left ear is scarred—he listened too long to a siren's promise.}
\item \textbf{Shipwright} --- Pitch on palms, schedule in teeth; knows every plank's story.
  \hfill \textit{He names each timber after a dead relative. The keel is his grandmother.}
\item \textbf{Oar-Master} --- Sets beat that decides if home is today or never.
  \hfill \textit{He drums on a hollowed skull. The rhythm is a prayer to the sea.}
\item \textbf{Mist-Pilot} --- Ghosts boats through marsh lanes; reed‑runner with secret paths.
  \hfill \textit{He never blinks in fog. Some say he traded his blink for safe passage.}
\item \textbf{Skald} --- Verses buy silence, start fights, end them; law in rhyme.
  \hfill \textit{Her tongue is forked—she can praise and curse in the same breath.}
\item \textbf{Shield-Band} --- Cousins fine with coin, better with cattle.
  \hfill \textit{They sharpen their swords on runestones. The sparks tell fortunes.}
\item \textbf{Foster-Son} --- Southern coast birth; speaks both laws and subtext.
  \hfill \textit{He wears two brooches—one iron, one bone. Neither is his true allegiance.}
\item \textbf{Thing-Speaker} --- Makes war into lawsuit, lawsuit into lunch.
  \hfill \textit{He carries a water‑clock. When it runs out, so does your patience.}
\item[J] \textbf{Sea-Queen's Hand} --- Envoy with seal‑bag and sword‑belt; queen's will made manifest.
  \hfill \textit{He never draws his blade. The threat is enough.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Sea-Queen} --- Crown of hammered silver, eyes for weather and law.
  \hfill \textit{She lost an eye to a storm—or to a spy. She won't say which.}
\item[K] \textbf{High Jarl} --- First among raids, last to boast; paramount over strands.
  \hfill \textit{His beard is braided with silver from every ship he's taken.}
\item[A] \textbf{Volva of Mist} --- Oath‑reader whose nod stills surf; seer of tides and truth.
  \hfill \textit{She speaks once a year. The rest of the time, she listens to the fog.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Weather/Reef/Feud)} Black squall drops from clear band; oars or prayers—pick one; fogfall makes friend and reef trade places.

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:linn-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Black Squall} --- Drops from clear band; oars or prayers—pick one.
  \hfill \textit{The wind screams a name. Listen carefully—it might be yours.}
\item \textbf{Fogfall} --- Sound travels, sight does not; friend and reef trade places.
  \hfill \textit{You hear oars. Then you don't. Then they're behind you.}
\item \textbf{Boom Lift} --- Harbor chain rises; schedules drown in harbor mouth.
  \hfill \textit{The chain's links are inscribed with names of debtors. Each one rattles differently.}
\item \textbf{Levy Clash} --- Viterra's wardens arrive with polite spears.
  \hfill \textit{They carry a writ stamped with the queen's seal. The seal is still wet.}
\item \textbf{Keel-Rot Rumor} --- Crews beach mid‑voyage; trust in timber fails.
  \hfill \textit{The rot smells sweet—too sweet. Someone poisoned the tar.}
\item \textbf{Feud Token} --- Raid becomes wedding... or funeral; oath‑debt called due.
  \hfill \textit{A bronze ring arrives on an arrow. The meaning is clear.}
\item \textbf{Oath Matured} --- Verse sworn last winter comes due today.
  \hfill \textit{The skald clears her throat. The room goes cold.}
\item \textbf{Mistlands Miscount} --- Channels ``move,'' cargo vanishes without witnesses.
  \hfill \textit{The fog lifts to reveal a different shore. Your charts are now lies.}
\item \textbf{Aberderrin Race} --- Contrary currents split flotilla; half late, half lost.
  \hfill \textit{The water churns like a dying man. Something beneath is feeding.}
\item[J] \textbf{Southron Fire-Pots} --- Night‑run turns to daylight; surprise ashore.
  \hfill \textit{Pots shatter on the deck. The fire burns blue—alchemical.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Thing Injunction} --- No sailing till grievance heard (hours? days?).
  \hfill \textit{The Thing‑Speaker smiles. He has all the time in the world.}
\item[K] \textbf{General Muster} --- Horns on every headland; all bays become spears.
  \hfill \textit{The war fleet assembles. Your ship is the smallest.}
\item[A] \textbf{Ground-Sea} --- Long, blind swell rolls under; steering lies.
  \hfill \textit{The swell has a rhythm—two short, one long. A signal from below.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Right/Token/Escort)} Harbor‑green mark—dock without levy in named strand; oar‑share claims two benches on fast longship.

  \hfill \textit{The barrels are sealed with your mark. Break one to test quality.}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:linn-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (rights/tokens/escorts) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{sea‑omens}—horns on wind, white horses on swell, verse unfinished until landfall.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Storm-Oaths:} Every raid or voyage sealed by oath to sea and storm. Break one, invite the sea's judgment.
\item \textbf{Skald's Burden:} Verses are law, history, curse. A poor rhyme can damn a crew as surely as a reef.
\item \textbf{Mist-Dread:} The bravest Linn will not enter the Direwood's breath or the Valewood's shade. Even the Sea‑Queen pales at the Ninth Bell of the Mistlands.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Frost Ledger:} The Linn tell tales of draugr who rise from unpaid weregild. A High Jarl's ghost now haunts the Thing‑Holm, demanding a blood‑price paid in stories.
  \item \textbf{The Bitter Root:} Daughters of the Chain run a hospice on a skerry. They take no payment—but they take names. A renegade Sister hides among the herring‑stairs.
  \item \textbf{Malachai, the Chained Angel:} The Sea‑Queen's court whispers of a cursed coin that grants fair winds—for a year, then collects. The coin is in the hands of a rival jarl.
  \item \textbf{The Grey Wanderer's Grimoire:} Linn psions are called ``Tide‑Readers.'' They taste salt on their tongues before a squall. The Admiralty of Kahfagia pays well for their services—and disposes of those who see too far.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any longhouse, the ninth horn blown at a feast summons the Hollow. The wise stop at eight.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Linn Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Wake:} Toss a crust over the gunwale before a voyage. Ignore the first \emph{Black Squall} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Oar:} Tie a single knot in your oar's leather grip. Cancel \emph{Keel-Rot Rumor} or take –1 die on your next rowing check (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Deep:} Whisper a dead enemy's name into the tide. Gain +1 Effect against his kin for the scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Sea‑Queen.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
Among the Linn, power flows through oaths, kinship, and the spoken word. Jarls maintain authority through successful raids and wise judgments, while skalds wield influence through their ability to immortalize deeds in verse—or damn names through satire. The Sea‑Queen holds sway over the coastlands, but her rule depends on the respect of the High Jarl and the Thing‑speakers who interpret ancient laws.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Linn society is fluid and performance‑based. Patronage often takes the form of foster‑bonds, oar‑shares, or raid‑truces—each binding the recipient to service and reputation. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, banners, verses) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival jarls issue conflicting claims, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the Thing‑holm and harbor gatherings as arenas for social contests, where oaths are sworn and broken.
\end{itemize}
In Linn society, your word is your bond, and your reputation is your greatest treasure.
\end{tcolorbox}
