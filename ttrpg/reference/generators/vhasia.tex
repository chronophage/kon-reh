\section{Vhasia --- ``The Fractured Sun''}
\label{chap:vhasia}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Cardinal-Regent of the Broken Sun (Elite):}  
  ``The sun may be fractured, but our authority is absolute within our domains. Every coin minted here carries our blessing, and every law written bears our seal – the rest is just politics.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Road Warden (Commoner):}  
  ``I've served under three different banners on this same stretch of road, and the only constant is that someone always wants to collect tolls. My horn calls honest warning – best heed it. The Fenwoods burned Lence, and now every duke with a coin purse thinks he's king.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Vhasia is a realm of broken coronations and split loyalties. Once a proud province of the Utaran empire, the unified Sun‑court shattered like its heraldic sunburst when the Fenwoods of Viterra and their allies sacked the capital of Lence and cast down the old Blood line. Now rival dukes, duchesses, abbesses, and parlements claim the fractured light. Everywhere, signs of fracture: chipped sun‑discs on milestones, worn tabards bearing half a crown, coins minted in rival cities with different suns. In Vhasia, legitimacy is as fragile as a mirror, and every oath risks breaking along the fault lines of politics, faith, and fortune.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of old smoke and fresh ink. The way every noble's seal has a hairline crack. The constant, nervous laughter of courtiers who remember whose head rolled last season.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never carry a coin that bears only one sunburst. The realm has three mints, and the wrong metal on the wrong road is treason.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Crowns, Courts, and Companies}

Here, the past is a weapon and the future is a debt. The Fenwoods came from Viterra with fire and iron, broke the Sun‑king's army at the Ford of Ashes, and burned Lence until the mirrors melted. They left the throne empty—not destroyed, but open. Now every duke with a long purse and a short conscience backs a claimant. The Queen‑Mother weaves alliances from her riverside keep. The Two Crowns—rival dukes crowned in rival cathedrals—march their levies through fields that can feed neither. Routiers sell their swords by the season, and parlement clerks drown justice in procedure. Vhasia is not a nation at war. It is a nation that forgot how to be at peace.

\textbf{Starting Location:} The Pont‑du‑Tithe, its golden arches catching fire in the sunset as pilgrims chant from both banks. A Duchy Constable in splendid armor guards the toll gate. ``The Chevauchée burns,'' he says, gesturing to distant smoke. ``Two Crowns march to meet it. Whose seal do you carry? Speak quickly—the levy waits for no one.''

\paragraph*{(Château/Cathedral/Forest)} Wayside shrine with pilgrims tacking petitions to a scorched sun‑disc; vine‑terraced clos above a millstream with napping watchmen.

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:vhasia-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Wayside Shrine} --- Pilgrims tack petitions to a scorched sun‑disc; each parchment curls in different winds.
  \hfill \textit{The petitions are never answered. The shrine is a mailbox for the dead.}
\item \textbf{Vine-Terrace} --- Clos above a millstream; watchmen nap, halberds traded for bottles.
  \hfill \textit{The best vintage is called ``Crownbreaker.'' It tastes like smoke and regret.}
\item \textbf{Bastide Square} --- Neat as a canray board; merchants pay homage to different “hours” of the Sun.
  \hfill \textit{The square has a gallows. It rotates to face whichever claimant lost the last duel.}
\item \textbf{Royal Forest} --- Antler posts mark laws no king remembers; poachers still hang.
  \hfill \textit{The forest's deer have grown bold. They wear crowns of shed antlers.}
\item \textbf{Pont-du-Tithe} --- Toll‑bridge flanked by chapels and crossbows; chains blessed by rival bishops.
  \hfill \textit{The chains were forged from melted sunbursts. They ring in different keys.}
\item \textbf{Great Fair} --- Outside a chartered town; counting sheds ring like bells, rumors louder still.
  \hfill \textit{The fair has a Court of Miracles where counterfeiters sell to informers.}
\item \textbf{Salt Pans} --- Wind‑pumps on an estuary; gulls own the law, smugglers own the night.
  \hfill \textit{The salt is grey. It tastes of old tears and older debts.}
\item \textbf{Siege-Château} --- Hoardings patched, cistern low, banners cut into ragged suns.
  \hfill \textit{The banner's ragged edges are from a Fenwood sword. The owner still limps.}
\item \textbf{Cathedral Yard} --- Cranes creak, stones fly, scaffolds bristle with rival masons' marks.
  \hfill \textit{The mason's marks are clan signs. A scratched‑out mark means a dead foreman.}
\item[J] \textbf{Parlement Hall} --- Hung with faded tapestries; sunbursts spliced and re‑stitched by claimants.
  \hfill \textit{The tapestries show battles that never happened—and some that shouldn't have.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Queen's Causeway} --- Mile‑long oath paved in quarried stone; half‑sunk each spring.
  \hfill \textit{The sunken half is a graveyard for vows. The stones are slippery with old lies.}
\item[K] \textbf{Sun Palace (Lence)} --- Shuttered mirrors, brambled gardens; courtiers playing at kingship.
  \hfill \textit{The mirrors were covered after the sack. No one wants to see what they show now.}
\item[A] \textbf{King's High Road} --- Mile‑stones chipped of crowns; yet every army still marches it.
  \hfill \textit{The road is paved with the bones of tax collectors. They are not missed.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Lord/Court/Company)} Road warden with dented badge and honest horn; vintner‑guild syndic counting saints' days as credit terms.

\section*{Hearts --- People \& Factions}
\label{sec:vhasia-people}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Road Warden} --- Dented badge, honest horn; ignored by both crowns but knows the roads.
  \hfill \textit{His horn is cracked. It still sounds—but the note is always half a step flat.}
\item \textbf{Vintner Syndic} --- Counts saints' days as credit terms, vintages as treaties; wine as diplomacy.
  \hfill \textit{He keeps a bottle of Crownbreaker in every cellar. He knows which vintage each duke prefers.}
\item \textbf{Abbess-Chatelaine} --- Rules a fortress‑convent with iron accounts and sharper scripture.
  \hfill \textit{She has never drawn a blade. The scaffold has, on her orders.}
\item \textbf{Routier Captain} --- Scarred and mercenary; prefers ransoms to loyalty; coin as conscience.
  \hfill \textit{He switches banners as often as his shirt. His loyalty is to the paychest—and his mother.}
\item \textbf{Duchy Constable} --- Splendid armor, empty coffers, pride swollen as a banner; glory over gold.
  \hfill \textit{His armor was his grandfather's. It still has the dent from a Fenwood axe.}
\item \textbf{Parlement Clerk} --- Drowns foes in procedure, fishes them out for fealty; law as a weapon.
  \hfill \textit{His quill is a dagger. He sharpens it every morning.}
\item \textbf{Salt-Farmer} --- Gabelle lessee with cudgels, writs, and a river‑chart no crown has; tax as tyranny.
  \hfill \textit{His ledger is written in salt. One drop of water and the debts vanish—along with the evidence.}
\item \textbf{Trouvère} --- A scandalous lay about the last Sun‑king brings knives to tavern doors.
  \hfill \textit{He sings the truth. That is why he sleeps in a different inn every night.}
\item \textbf{Marshal-Exile} --- The Sun‑court's hammer; now wielded by whoever pays in silver.
  \hfill \textit{He lost the Battle of Ashes. He still sees it in his dreams—and his nightmares.}
\item[J] \textbf{Heretic Perfect} --- Preaches peace while tucking hidden daggers into parchment margins.
  \hfill \textit{His sermon is a code. The psalms tell you which bridge to cross—and which to burn.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Queen-Mother} --- Riverside keep; weaves alliances as if they were dowries.
  \hfill \textit{She has outlived three husbands and two sons. She is not mourning.}
\item[K] \textbf{Two Crowns} --- Rival dukes, each “crowned” in rival cathedrals, neither whole.
  \hfill \textit{One wears a crown of iron. The other wears a crown of words. Both rust.}
\item[A] \textbf{Last Dauphin} --- A rumor with a birthmark; armies march for whispers alone.
  \hfill \textit{He may be a baker's son. The armies don't care. They need a sun to follow.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Chevauchée/Law/Church)} Chevauchée burning loyalty oaths as well as barns; interdict falls on a county; bells silenced, trade stalls.

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:vhasia-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Chevauchée} --- Raiders burn loyalty oaths as well as barns; smoke curls like scripture.
  \hfill \textit{The raiders wear no colors. Their horses answer to whistles—and grudges.}
\item \textbf{Interdict} --- Falls on a county; bells silenced, trade stalls, heretics prosper.
  \hfill \textit{The bells were melted for shot three wars ago. The silence is old.}
\item \textbf{Forest Law} --- Venison becomes treason; poachers hang from antler posts.
  \hfill \textit{The antler posts are carved with the names of the hanged. They whisper at dusk.}
\item \textbf{River Spate} --- Ferries chained, bridges drowned, pilgrims stranded mid‑prayer.
  \hfill \textit{The water is the color of old gold. It washed away a mint last spring.}
\item \textbf{Coin Debasement} --- Three mints, three suns, no trust; soldiers demand silver alone.
  \hfill \textit{The silver is from the same mine—but the stamp is a prayer, not a promise.}
\item \textbf{Parlement Divided} --- A siege laid on parchment before walls; war by paperwork.
  \hfill \textit{The clerks duel with citations. The loser pays the winner's ink bill.}
\item \textbf{Company Mutiny} --- Unless arrears are paid—in blood or coin; mercenaries as a time bomb.
  \hfill \textit{The mutineers have a list. Your name is on it—or will be by nightfall.}
\item \textbf{Relic Dispute} --- Two abbeys claim the same saint's bones; mobs answer with stones.
  \hfill \textit{The bones are actually from a cow. The saints don't mind. They like the attention.}
\item \textbf{Harvest Blight} --- Bread locked behind pikes; bakers guarded as lords.
  \hfill \textit{The blight has a name. It is the name of a duke who raised the levy too late.}
\item[J] \textbf{Tournament “Accident”} --- Hides an assassination; your favor is now evidence.
  \hfill \textit{The lance was filed with a prayer. The prayer was answered—but not for the target.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Royalist Restoration} --- Sunburst badges painted overnight on doors and graves.
  \hfill \textit{The paint is the color of dried blood. The badges face the wrong direction.}
\item[K] \textbf{Feudal Levy} --- Lords demand levies in days, not months, with three days' bread.
  \hfill \textit{The bread is hardtack from the last Fenwood siege. It still smells of smoke.}
\item[A] \textbf{Winter Campaign} --- Roads turn to mud, hooves to ice, armies to ruin.
  \hfill \textit{The snow is grey with ash. The Pass of Ashes has a sister pass now.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Charter/Patent/Right)} Safe‑conduct sealed by a duke or abbey; honored only once, on one road; burgess charter for a market ward.

\section*{Diamonds --- Rewards/Leverage}
\label{sec:vhasia-rewards}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Safe-Conduct} --- Sealed by a duke or abbey; honored only once, on one road.
  \hfill \textit{The seal is cracked. The road is watched. Use it before the seal breaks.}
\item \textbf{Burgess Charter} --- A market ward; walls, watch, and revenue at your call.
  \hfill \textit{The charter is a bell. Ring it, and the town answers—or it doesn't.}
\item \textbf{Bridge Farm} --- Tolls at Pont‑du‑Tithe for a season, if you can hold it.
  \hfill \textit{The tollkeepers are loyal to the last coin. Yours or theirs.}
\item \textbf{Paréage Charter} --- Shared rule with a monastery—half justice, half rent, no peace.
  \hfill \textit{The monks pray for you. They also pray for your enemies. Balance.}
\item \textbf{Wardship} --- Over a minor heir; lands obey you “for now.”
  \hfill \textit{The heir has a knife. He knows how to use it. He learned from watching you.}
\item \textbf{Gabelle Lease} --- Salt‑tax rights; lucrative, hated, and dangerous.
  \hfill \textit{The lease is a death warrant written in profit. Cash it wisely.}
\item \textbf{Letters Patent} --- Raise a free‑company under your banner; an army as a business.
  \hfill \textit{The letters are signed in blood—yours or the notary's. Both are valid.}
\item \textbf{Remission} --- Of feudal dues for a village; they'll march when called.
  \hfill \textit{The village has a long memory. They remember the last lord who promised mercy.}
\item \textbf{Low-Justice} --- Grant (stocks, fines, gallows) in a troublesome hamlet.
  \hfill \textit{The gallows rope is new. The notches are from the last hamlet.}
\item[J] \textbf{Confiscation Writ} --- Against a royalist estate—if you can seize it first.
  \hfill \textit{The writ is a race. The other claimant rides a faster horse.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Queen's Audience} --- Private with the Queen‑Mother; a whisper becomes policy.
  \hfill \textit{The audience is a chess game. She always wins—but she lets you take a pawn.}
\item[K] \textbf{Sunburst Warrant} --- Of the fallen court—most still bow if shown boldly.
  \hfill \textit{The warrant is a ghost. It has no power—only the power of belief.}
\item[A] \textbf{General Pardon} --- Temporary: rebels absolved if they bend the knee.
  \hfill \textit{The pardon is a spring. It will close when the season turns.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:vhasia-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (charters/patents/rights) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{fractured sun} motifs—light that splinters, oaths that crack like glass.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Fractured Seals:} When brandishing a writ, badge, or charter tied to a Sun‑court claimant, step \textbf{Position} up one step for the scene's next interaction at a gate, court, or checkpoint. Each use advances a \textbf{Legitimacy} [4] clock linked to the claimant; when it fills, rivals challenge the authority on sight.
\item \textbf{Parlement Games:} If a scene touches petitions, guild disputes, or feudal appeals, the first \textbf{Diamond} played becomes a \emph{policy lever}: treat it as two Diamonds toward swaying a decision or rewriting a custom. Afterward, start or advance a \textbf{Whispered Precedent} clock; when it fills, backlash spreads as other courts cite the case against you.
\item \textbf{Gabelle Knife-Edge:} At bridges, fairs, or salt depots, convert \textbf{Diamonds} tied to tolls, leases, or safe‑conducts into immediate passage or tariff relief for the company (no roll). Each conversion ticks a \textbf{Resentment} [4] clock for the locale; when it completes, expect boycotts, ambush rumors, or an audit by rival officials.
\item \textbf{Viterran Holdings:} Much of northern Vhasia is held by Viterran dukes, abbots, and condottieri. Any travel seed through Vhasia may be overclaimed by Viterran authority. Safe‑Conducts are fragile—Viterra treats them as favors, not guarantees. When this occurs, the GM may pull an additional \textbf{Club} from the Viterra deck (if available) or introduce a \textbf{Royal Incursion} complication.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Bitter Root:} Daughters of the Chain run a hospice in the burned quarter of Lence. They ask no payment—but they cut threads. A renegade Sister hides among the salt‑farmers, trying to untie her cord from a Fenwood death warrant.
  \item \textbf{The Threshold Compendium:} The Parlement Hall's tapestries are Class II Anchors—the woven sunbursts remember the dead clerks. Saikou Ira would trade his silver mirror for a single thread.
  \item \textbf{Malachai, the Chained Angel:} A silver coin that never spends circulates among the routiers. Take it, and your company's paychest never empties—but your name will appear on every mutiny list.
  \item \textbf{The Grey Wanderer's Grimoire:} Vhasian psions are called “Crown‑Readers.” They hear the weight of legitimacy in a claimant's voice. The Queen‑Mother employs two—and has never let them meet.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Vhasian scene, the ninth signature on a petition invalidates all others. The wise sign eighth and leave.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Vhasian Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Sun:} Crumble a crust onto a cracked sun‑disc. Ignore the first \emph{Chevauchée} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Seal:} Tie a single knot in a charter's ribbon. Cancel \emph{Parlement Divided} or take –1 die on your next legal argument (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Salt:} Whisper a dead claimant's name into a salt pan. Gain +1 Effect when collecting debts from their faction this scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Queen‑Mother.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Vhasia, power flows through the remnants of imperial authority, fractured legitimacy, and the careful manipulation of legal and religious institutions. Former Utaran provincial structures still provide a framework for governance, but competing claimants to the Sun‑court have created a complex web of overlapping jurisdictions and conflicting loyalties. The true power lies with those who can navigate this legal maze and maintain the appearance of legitimate authority.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Vhasia revolves around legitimacy, legal documents, and the ability to command respect from institutions that no longer have a clear central authority. Rewards often take the form of charters, writs, and official positions that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (seals, badges, writs) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival claimants issue conflicting authorities, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the parlements, cathedrals, and châteaux as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success.
\end{itemize}
In Vhasia, your papers are your power, and your power determines whether you rule or are ruled.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Extensions (Plug-in)}
\label{sec:vhasia-extensions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Legitimacy Dial (Claimant $\leftrightarrow$ Neutrality):} Track the party's public stance.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Claimant High:} +1 Position when invoking seals, warrants, or feudal rights; first parley with a rival claimant starts one step worse.
    \item \emph{Neutrality High:} +1 Effect when brokering truces or smuggling between factions; first petition to a court or cathedral starts one step worse.
    \item Center the dial by paying wergild to both sides or hosting a neutral moot.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Parlement Mini‑Game (legal duel):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Lay three counters: \textbf{Precedent}, \textbf{Equity}, \textbf{Force}.  
    \item Argue a point (roll Lore+Diplomacy vs DV 3). On a hit, move one counter to your side. On a miss, the clerk gains \emph{Citation Edge}.  
    \item Win two counters to secure a temporary \textbf{Parlement Ruling} (treat as a Diamond for one scene).  
    \item \emph{Spider's Interjection (2 SB):} flip \textbf{Precedent}, but start \emph{Whispered Precedent} [2].
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Chevauchée Pressure (raid clock):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Two opposed clocks: \emph{Outrun the Fire} vs \emph{Scorched Earth} (size by highest rank in seed).
    \item Each leg, choose \emph{High Road} (fast, exposed), \emph{Bypass} (slow, hidden), or \emph{Ford} (risky, shortcut).  
    \item Advantage: Bypass$\rightarrow$High Road (cover), Ford$\rightarrow$Bypass (speed), High Road$\rightarrow$Ford (navigation).  
    \item Spend a \textbf{Watch-Code} (from Three‑Fires Ridge) to shift one segment from \emph{Scorched Earth} to \emph{Outrun}.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Favor of the Sun (prestige track):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Earn by resolving disputes between claimants without bloodshed, or by returning stolen relics.  
    \item At 3 Favor, treat one \textbf{Safe-Conduct} or \textbf{Sunburst Warrant} as unquestioned for a scene.  
    \item Each use after the first advances \emph{Legitimacy Challenge} [4] against that claimant.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Start‑of‑Scene Omens (1d6):}
  \begin{enumerate}
