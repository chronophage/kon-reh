\section{Vhasia — ``The Fractured Sun''}
\label{chap:vhasia}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Cardinal-Regent of the Broken Sun (Elite):}  
  ``The sun may be fractured, but our authority is absolute within our domains. Every coin minted here carries our blessing, and every law written bears our seal – the rest is just politics.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Road Warden (Commoner):}  
  ``I've served under three different banners on this same stretch of road, and the only constant is that someone always wants to collect tolls. My horn calls honest warning – best heed it. The Fenwoods burned Lence, and now every duke with a coin purse thinks he's king.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Vhasia is a realm of broken coronations and split loyalties. Once a proud province of the Utaran empire, the unified Sun‑court shattered like its heraldic sunburst when the Fenwoods of Viterra and their allies sacked the capital of Lence and cast down the old Blood line. Now rival dukes, duchesses, abbesses, and parlements claim the fractured light. Everywhere, signs of fracture: chipped sun‑discs on milestones, worn tabards bearing half a crown, coins minted in rival cities with different suns. In Vhasia, legitimacy is as fragile as a mirror, and every oath risks breaking along the fault lines of politics, faith, and fortune.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of old smoke and fresh ink. The way every noble's seal has a hairline crack. The constant, nervous laughter of courtiers who remember whose head rolled last season.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never carry a coin that bears only one sunburst. The realm has three mints, and the wrong metal on the wrong road is treason.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Crowns, Courts, and Companies}

Here, the past is a weapon and the future is a debt. The Fenwoods came from Viterra with fire and iron, broke the Sun‑king's army at the Ford of Ashes, and burned Lence until the mirrors melted. They left the throne empty—not destroyed, but open. Now every duke with a long purse and a short conscience backs a claimant. The Queen‑Mother weaves alliances from her riverside keep. The Two Crowns—rival dukes crowned in rival cathedrals—march their levies through fields that can feed neither. Routiers sell their swords by the season, and parlement clerks drown justice in procedure. Vhasia is not a nation at war. It is a nation that forgot how to be at peace.

\textbf{Starting Location:} The Pont‑du‑Tithe, its golden arches catching fire in the sunset as pilgrims chant from both banks. A Duchy Constable in splendid armor guards the toll gate. ``The Chevauchee burns,'' he says, gesturing to distant smoke. ``Two Crowns march to meet it. Whose seal do you carry? Speak quickly—the levy waits for no one.''

\section*{Spades — Places}
\label{sec:vhasia-places}
\paragraph*{(Château/Cathedral/Forest)} Wayside shrine with pilgrims tacking petitions to a scorched sun‑disc; vine‑terraced clos above a millstream with napping watchmen.
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Wayside Shrine} --- Pilgrims tack petitions to a scorched sun‑disc; each parchment curls in different winds.
  \hfill \textit{The petitions are never answered. The shrine is a mailbox for the dead.}
\item \textbf{Vine-Terrace} --- Clos above a millstream; watchmen nap, halberds traded for bottles.
  \hfill \textit{The best vintage is called ``Crownbreaker.'' It tastes like smoke and regret.}
\item \textbf{Bastide Square} --- Neat as a checkerboard; merchants pay homage to different ``hours'' of the Sun.
  \hfill \textit{The square has a gallows. It rotates to face whichever claimant lost the last duel.}
\item \textbf{Royal Forest} --- Antler posts mark laws no king remembers; poachers still hang.
  \hfill \textit{The forest's deer have grown bold. They wear crowns of shed antlers.}
\item \textbf{Pont-du-Tithe} --- Toll‑bridge flanked by chapels and crossbows; chains blessed by rival bishops.
  \hfill \textit{The chains were forged from melted sunbursts. They ring in different keys.}
\item \textbf{Great Fair} --- Outside a chartered town; counting sheds ring like bells, rumors louder still.
  \hfill \textit{The fair has a Court of Miracles where counterfeiters sell to informers.}
\item \textbf{Salt Pans} --- Wind‑pumps on an estuary; gulls own the law, smugglers own the night.
  \hfill \textit{The salt is grey. It tastes of old tears and older debts.}
\item \textbf{Siege-Château} --- Hoardings patched, cistern low, banners cut into ragged suns.
  \hfill \textit{The banner's ragged edges are from a Fenwood sword. The owner still limps.}
\item \textbf{Cathedral Yard} --- Cranes creak, stones fly, scaffolds bristle with rival masons' marks.
  \hfill \textit{The mason's marks are clan signs. A scratched‑out mark means a dead foreman.}
\item[J] \textbf{Parlement Hall} --- Hung with faded tapestries; sunbursts spliced and re‑stitched by claimants.
  \hfill \textit{The tapestries show battles that never happened—and some that shouldn't have.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Queen's Causeway} --- Mile‑long oath paved in quarried stone; half‑sunk each spring.
  \hfill \textit{The sunken half is a graveyard for vows. The stones are slippery with old lies.}
\item[K] \textbf{Sun Palace (Lence)} --- Shuttered mirrors, brambled gardens; courtiers playing at kingship.
  \hfill \textit{The mirrors were covered after the sack. No one wants to see what they show now.}
\item[A] \textbf{King's High Road} --- Milestones chipped of crowns; yet every army still marches it.
  \hfill \textit{The road is paved with the bones of tax collectors. They are not missed.}
\end{enumerate}

% ====== ADDITIONAL FEATURES ======
\subsection*{Additional Features}

\paragraph{Fractured Seals:}
When brandishing a writ, badge, or charter tied to a Sun‑court claimant, step Position up one step for the scene's next interaction at a gate, court, or checkpoint. Each use advances a \textbf{Legitimacy [4]} clock linked to the claimant; when it fills, rivals challenge the authority on sight.

\paragraph{Parlement Games:}
If a scene touches petitions, guild disputes, or feudal appeals, the first Diamond played becomes a policy lever: treat it as two Diamonds toward swaying a decision or rewriting a custom. Afterward, start or advance a \textbf{Whispered Precedent} clock; when it fills, backlash spreads as other courts cite the case against you.

\paragraph{Gabelle Knife-Edge:}
At bridges, fairs, or salt depots, convert Diamonds tied to tolls, leases, or safe‑conducts into immediate passage or tariff relief for the company (no roll). Each conversion ticks a \textbf{Resentment [4]} clock for the locale; when it completes, expect boycotts, ambush rumors, or an audit by rival officials.

\paragraph{Vhasian Chivalry \& the Round of Five Virtues:}
The knights of the western cantons (Ethor, Adria) follow the Round of Five Virtues: \textbf{Courage, Courtesy, Mercy, Prowess, Truth}. In a formal joust or a legal duel, the GM may award \emph{Victory by Virtue} rather than by blood: the combatant with the higher Virtue score wins the dispute, even if they lost the physical exchange. This is the law of the tilt—and the law of the court.

\paragraph{The Armor Closes / The Song Escapes (Chivalric Clocks):}
The Cantor Alain, bearer of the Cracked Ring, sings a warning at every tourney:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Armor Closes [6]} – advances when knights choose protection over mercy, when gorgets are buckled tight, when chivalry becomes siege mentality. When full, the cantons close their borders and the raids become wars.
  \item \textbf{Song Escapes [8]} – advances when a Cantor sings the truth, when mercy is shown, when armour is unbuckled. When full, a moment of genuine connection occurs—though it may be fatal.
\end{itemize}
In Rouen, Alain still sings the \emph{Planctus Armatus} each spring, and the congregation weeps for Sir Corin, who unbuckled his gorget to hear a thief's voice and was undone by the mercy he offered.

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Seint Michel, the Judge of the Scar (Mykkiel):} The Order of the Gilded Scale maintains the \emph{Chivalric Ledger}—a record of every oath sworn and broken in the cantons. A page torn from this ledger can serve as a single‑use Claim String (gain +1 Position in any court). The Order’s justiciars are feared and respected; they never unbuckle their gorgets, and they have never lost a case. The Queen‑Mother keeps a copy in her riverside keep, wrapped in a grey shroud.
  \item \textbf{Seint Isabeau, Who Slumbers Amongst the Lilies (Ikasha):} In the Ethorian marches, knights whisper prayers to Seint Isabeau when they long to set aside their steel. Her shrine is a hawthorn grove where armour must be left at the gate. Pilgrims who enter bare‑skinned and speak a true regret find that their next wound heals in a single night—but they dream of a woman in white who asks, “Will you remember how to be touched?” Those who return to the grove a second time are never seen again.
  \item \textbf{Seint Evroul of the Cairn (Mor'iraath):} In the high passes of the Ethorian marches, a rusted knight in an unopened helm haunts the old barrows. He is the patron of those who carry a blood‑debt and cannot rest. To call upon him, one must pour a cup of salt water at a cairn and name the wrong done to your house. He will witness one blow; if it lands, the foe is \emph{Marked by the Cairn}—any spirit may locate them for a year. If it misses, the blow turns inward. The Marshal‑Exile carries a scar from such a bargain; he does not speak of it.
  \item \textbf{The Order of the Sunken Cup (The Hospice of the Broken Blade):} A grey‑robed hospice in the ruins of Lence treats the wounded of both crowns. They ask no payment, but they record every name in a ledger that never dries. The Restorationists suspect they are spies for the Queen‑Mother; the Two Crowns accuse them of erasing debts. Sister Oria, the hospice’s custodian, is also the keeper of the Lantern‑Rites House on the Belworth—neutral ground where even claimants must leave their blades at the door.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Vhasian court, the ninth citation of precedent voids all previous. The wise stop at eight and settle. Those who cite the ninth are said to hear a faint laughter from the marble walls—and their cases are never heard again.
\end{itemize}

% ====== PATRONAGE & POWER ======
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Vhasia, power flows through the remnants of imperial authority, fractured legitimacy, and the careful manipulation of legal and religious institutions. Former Utaran provincial structures still provide a framework for governance, but competing claimants to the Sun‑court have created a complex web of overlapping jurisdictions and conflicting loyalties. The true power lies with those who can navigate this legal maze and maintain the appearance of legitimate authority.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Vhasia revolves around legitimacy, legal documents, and the ability to command respect from institutions that no longer have a clear central authority. Rewards often take the form of charters, writs, and official positions that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (seals, badges, writs) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival claimants issue conflicting authorities, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the parlements, cathedrals, and châteaux as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success.
\end{itemize}
In Vhasia, your papers are your power, and your power determines whether you rule or are ruled.
\end{tcolorbox}

% ====== CAMPAIGN CLOCKS ======
\subsection*{The Vacant Throne \& Campaign Clocks}
\label{sec:vhasia-campaign}
For long‑form political campaigns:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Vacant Throne Clock [10]:} Advances when a claimant gains a major advantage (battle, noble defection, foreign alliance). Retreats when the party brokers a truce or exposes a weakness. When full, a claimant takes Lence and declares themselves king.
  \item \textbf{Armor Closes [6]:} Advances when knights choose protection over mercy, when gorgets are buckled tight, when chivalry becomes siege mentality. When full, the cantons close their borders and the raids become wars.
  \item \textbf{Song Escapes [8]:} Advances when a Cantor sings the truth, when mercy is shown, when armour is unbuckled. When full, a moment of genuine connection occurs—though it may be fatal.
  \item \textbf{Claimant's Coup (per claimant):} A 6‑clock that advances when that claimant wins a Parlement vote, a joust, or a skirmish. When full, they march on Lence.
\end{itemize}

% ====== EXTENSIONS (PLUG‑IN) ======
\subsection*{Extensions (Plug‑in)}
\label{sec:vhasia-extensions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Legitimacy Dial (Claimant $\leftrightarrow$ Neutrality):} Track the party's public stance.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Claimant High:} +1 Position when invoking seals, warrants, or feudal rights; first parley with a rival claimant starts one step worse.
    \item \emph{Neutrality High:} +1 Effect when brokering truces or smuggling between factions; first petition to a court or cathedral starts one step worse.
    \item Center the dial by paying weight to both sides or hosting a neutral moot.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Parlement Mini‑Game (legal duel):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Lay three counters: \emph{Precedent}, \emph{Equity}, \emph{Force}.
    \item Argue a point (roll Lore + Sway vs. DV 3). On a hit, move one counter to your side. On a miss, the clerk gains \emph{Citation Edge}.
    \item Win two counters to secure a temporary \emph{Parlement Ruling} (treat as a Diamond for one scene).
    \item \emph{Spider's Interjection (2 SB):} flip Precedent, but start \emph{Whispered Precedent} [2].
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Chevauchée Pressure (raid clock):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Two opposed clocks: \emph{Outrun the Fire} vs \emph{Scorched Earth} (size by highest rank in seed).
    \item Each leg, choose \emph{High Road} (fast, exposed), \emph{Bypass} (slow, hidden), or \emph{Ford} (risky, shortcut).
    \item Advantage: Bypass $\rightarrow$ High Road (cover), Ford $\rightarrow$ Bypass (speed), High Road $\rightarrow$ Ford (navigation).
    \item Spend a \emph{Watch-Code} (from Three‑Fires Ridge) to shift one segment from Scorched Earth to Outrun.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Favor of the Sun (prestige track):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Earn by resolving disputes between claimants without bloodshed, or by returning stolen relics.
    \item At 3 Favor, treat one \emph{Safe-Conduct} or \emph{Sunburst Warrant} as unquestioned for a scene.
    \item Each use after the first advances \emph{Legitimacy Challenge} [4] against that claimant.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Start‑of‑Scene Omens (1d6):}
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \emph{Cracked Bell}: first legal action gains +1d; first boast draws \emph{Parlement Divided}.
    \item \emph{Smoke on the Ridge}: Chevauchée advances once; shortcuts open.
    \item \emph{Three Suns}: three claimants claim the same road; pick a side or start \emph{Coin Debasement}.
    \item \emph{Queen's Dove}: a message arrives; +1d on the next \emph{Queen's Audience} attempt.
    \item \emph{Salt Wind}: \emph{Gabelle Lease} counts double value today; smugglers' ears open.
    \item \emph{Routier Campfire}: mercenaries are hireable for one scene at half price—but they'll ask questions later.
  \end{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{Plug‑in SB Conversions (Vhasia Flavor):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item 1 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Seal Crack}: a writ loses half its value until re‑witnessed.
    \item 2 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Citation War}: increase DV by 1 unless you produce a precedent (spend a \Diamond{} or recall a past ruling).
    \item 3+ SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Crown Fracture}: the claimant's legitimacy splits; halve a consequence by accepting a rival's seal on your paper (start \emph{Legitimacy Challenge}).
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Reputation Echoes:}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Court-Favored}: once/session, downgrade \emph{Parlement Divided} to a minor delay; the next document you present is scrutinized (+1 DV).
    \item \emph{Chevauchée-Tested}: +1 Position when evading raids; supply lines run 1 segment longer.
    \item \emph{Neutral Broker}: +1d with both claimants in a truce scene; any betrayal earns \emph{Resentment} [2] from both sides.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Cross-Region Hooks (quick integration):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Thepyrgos Synod Leak}: a \emph{Synod Indulgence} can suspend a Vhasian interdict—but starts \emph{Whispered Precedent} in Thepyrgos.
    \item \emph{Ubral Feud Echo}: paying wergild to a Vhasian claimant instead of a clan starts \emph{Feud Rekindled} along the border roads.
    \item \emph{Kahfagia Salt War}: cashing a \emph{Gabelle Lease} to outbid corsair lanes adds \emph{Market Knives} [4] in Silkstrand or on the coastal quays.
    \item \emph{Valewood Relic Interest}: a relic from a Vhasian cathedral may be claimed by fae courts; a Diamond spent on relic control may instead draw \emph{Court Tithe}.
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
