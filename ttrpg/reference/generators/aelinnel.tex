\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Count the steps, weigh the words, and never trust a single telling.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Lady of Thorns (Fae Sovereign):}  
  ``The law here flows like water over stone ‚ąí‚ąí it finds its own path, and woe to those who try to dam it with paper and pretense. The earth itself will testify against the unjust. But the earth also lies ‚ąí‚ąí politely, and only when the moon is full.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Charcoal‚ąíburner (Commoner):}  
  ``Our kilns tell us more than your books ever could. When the smoke curls left, the tide's with you. When it splits in three, best stay by your own hearth till it burns straight again. And never trust a court that speaks in only one voice ‚ąí‚ąí the silence is always the second witness.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Dark faerie tale, moonlit groves, polite iron, and the uncanny weight of a promise unsaid.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A tide‚ąíshed at dawn, fog curling over anchored barges, gulls crying omens over the river's black glass. A gnomish reeve offers passage on a sealed barge: ``The Green Gate opens at neap tide. What we trade there, the Dolmis will not name. And if you hear someone counting your footsteps, do not turn around ‚ąí‚ąí they are only making sure you still have them.''

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Stone, Bough, and Bright Things}]
The Aelinnel do not build cities. They \emph{grow} them ‚ąí‚ąí from granite and hawthorn, from tide‚ąípolished bone and the shadow of a laugh. Their halls thread through sea‚ąícliffs and root‚ąígalleries, their roads are counted by antler‚ąíposts that move at night, and their doors open only when you have forgotten why you came. Here, promises have \emph{weight} ‚ąí‚ąí not the weight of ink on parchment, but the weight of a stone dropped into a still pool. The ripples go on forever.

To the north, the Valewood breathes; to the south, the downs of Amedell where the Aelaerem tie red threads to keep the Hollow at bay. The Green Gate sometimes opens onto halfling barrows ‚ąí‚ąí a fact neither people speaks of aloud, because speaking aloud gives the gate permission to move.

No single crown rules the gnomish lands. Dozens of courts, seasonal pacts, and garden societies weave a shifting lattice of loyalty. Some Aelinnel keep the old fae treaties, speaking in two voices and never carrying naked iron; others barter in the Green Market with memories instead of coin, treating courtesy as a kind of lockpicking. Still others vanish into the deep woods, listening to stag‚ąíroads where the trees whisper gossip about the living. The gnomish heart is divided ‚ąí‚ąí and that division is the only reason the fae haven't eaten them whole.

Craft and courtesy govern every traffic between mortal keeps and the courts under the hill. Gnomish stone‚ąísingers hum bridges into alignment, their melodies keeping the Green Gate at bay. Forester‚ąíwardens hammer copper nails into thresholds because iron, they say, \emph{startles the truth}. In markets under living roofs, a sentence can be folded like a napkin, and a memory can be wrapped for travel ‚ąí‚ąí but never opened before the second sunrise. Paths sometimes shorten for those who keep good count, and lengthen for those who sneer at patterns. The fae are never rude ‚ąí‚ąí but they are never, ever \emph{safe}.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Field Procedures (Use At the Table)}
\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Two‚ąíVoice Talk:} When parley stalls, the GM asks: ``What was said? What was meant?'' If players state both, cancel the first social SB this scene; if they refuse, the next bargain demands collateral (memory/name). The collateral is always returned ‚ąí‚ąí but never in the same condition.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Count or Be Counted:} At any fae‚ąítouched crossing, a player who audibly counts in a fitting sequence (steps, heartbeats, breaths) shifts \emph{Position} one step safer for that crossing. Boasting or mocking numbers adds a free ``Geas Catches'' SB. The geas is always about a door. The door is always behind you.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Copper Courtesy Check:} Producing copper/brass tools in a ritual space removes the first \emph{Fae Offence} tag this scene; drawing iron instead escalates the next Complication by +1 Cap. The escalation is always polite. The result is never.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage & Power}]
Among the Aelinnel, power flows through courtesy, oath, and the hidden geometry of the land. A Stone Prince's authority rests on indexed fae treaties; a Green Knight's on the trials he has witnessed. There is no single crown, only the shared understanding that a broken promise cuts deeper than a sword ‚ąí‚ąí and that a promise kept is a door that opens both ways.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Aelinnel society is measured in hazel tokens, oath‚ąíbeads, and the precision of a courtesy. Patronage often takes the form of guest‚ąíboughs, tide‚ąípath keys, or the Green Knight's escort ‚ąí‚ąí each tied to a specific court, grove, or obligation. To emphasise the cultural mechanics:
\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem Use \textbf{Two‚ąíVoice Courtesy} to reward players who pay attention to the unspoken. The unspoken is where the fae live.
‚ĄĻtem Enforce \textbf{Copper over Iron} ‚ąí‚ąí carrying iron is not forbidden, but it is a statement. The statement is always heard. The hearing is always remembered.
‚ĄĻtem Let \textbf{The Unsaid Clause} be a source of future complications; the fae love what is not said. They will fill the silence with their own words ‚ąí‚ąí and their words have weight.
