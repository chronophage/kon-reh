\section{Aelinnel (Gnomes) --- ``Stone, Bough, and Bright Things''}
\label{chap:aelinnel-gnomes}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Count the steps, weigh the words, and never trust a single ledger.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Stone Prince of Aelinnel (Elite):}  
  ``The law here flows like water over stone – it finds its own path, and woe to those who try to dam it with paper and pretense. The earth itself will testify against the unjust.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Charcoal-burner (Commoner):}  
  ``Our kilns tell us more than your books ever could. When the smoke curls left, the tide's with you. When it splits in three, best stay by your own hearth till it burns straight again. And never trust a court that speaks in only one ledger.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Dark faerie tale, moonlit groves, polite iron, and the uncanny weight of a promise.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A tide‑shed at dawn, fog curling over anchored barges, gulls crying omens over the river's black glass. A gnomish reeve offers passage on a sealed barge: ``The Green Gate opens at neap tide. What we trade there, the Dolmis will not name.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Stone, Bough, and Bright Things}]
Aelinnel homeland of Aevrossa clings to the Dolmis coast beneath the shadow of the Valewood, its halls threaded through granite and hawthorn, its roads counted by antler‑posts and moonlit courtesy. Here, promises have weight and silence has meaning. \emph{Even} steps are safe, \emph{odd} words are careful, and truths are traded like copper nails – bright, useful, and slightly poisonous in the wrong pocket.

To the north, the Valewood looms; to the south, the downs of Amedell where the Aelaerem keep their red doors. The Green Gate sometimes opens onto halfling barrows – a fact neither people speaks of aloud.

Yet no single crown rules the gnomish lands. Dozens of courts, guilds, and seasonal pacts weave a shifting lattice of loyalty. Some Aelinnel hold fast to ancient fae treaties, speaking in two ledgers and never carrying naked iron; others barter in the Green Market with pragmatic coin, treating courtesy as a bargaining chip; still others vanish into the deep woods, listening to stag‑roads and root‑galleries where the Wild Hunt sometimes rides. The gnomish heart is divided – and that division is their shield against the fae.

Craft and courtesy govern every traffic between mortal keeps and the courts under the hill. Gnomish stone‑singers tune bridges with a hum that keeps the Green Gate at bay; forester‑wardens hammer copper where iron would draw unwanted attention. In markets under living roofs, a sentence can be cut to fit a purse, and a memory can be wrapped for travel. Paths sometimes shorten for those who keep good count, and lengthen for those who sneer at patterns. The fae are never rude – but they are never safe.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{The Three Courts: Aelinnel Alignments}
Aelinnel society is not a single crown but a lattice of courts, guilds, and seasonal pacts. Three broad stances shape their dealings with outsiders and with the fae. Most gnomes shift between these postures depending on the season, the tide, and who is asking.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Thorn–Courteous (Fae–Lean)} – These gnomes keep the old pacts with the Lady of Thorns and the Green Knight. They speak in two ledgers, never carry naked iron, and treat every threshold as a negotiation. They are the ones who remind visitors to count their steps and pour no ninth cup.  
    \emph{Clocks: \textsc{Courtesy Debt} [4], \textsc{Hawthorn Patience} [6].}

    \item \textbf{Market–Square (Trade–Lean)} – These gnomes prioritise the Green Market, the Under‑Market, and the flow of quartz, copper, and name‑script. They are pragmatic, will use iron if it is gilded, and view fae courtesies as bargaining chips rather than sacred laws.  
    \emph{Clocks: \textsc{Tariff Dispute} [4], \textsc{Market Dominance} [6].}

    \item \textbf{Forester–Trusted (Wild–Lean)} – These gnomes live among the stag‑roads and root‑galleries, closer to the stone‑singers and the tree‑wardens. They speak the old names of tides and groves, and they are the first to notice when the thorn blight spreads or the moonwell stains a name wrong.  
    \emph{Clocks: \textsc{Root Calling} [4], \textsc{Wild Hunt Interest} [6].}
\end{itemize}

Most Aelinnel characters and settlements fall somewhere between these poles, and the same gnome might wear a different mask when trading at the Green Market than when walking the stag‑roads.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:aelinnel-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until all four suits appear: \textbf{Spade} = place, \textbf{Heart} = actor, \textbf{Club} = pressure, \textbf{Diamond} = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 → 4, 6–10 → 6, J/Q/K → 8, A → 10).
\item Treat \textbf{Diamonds} as codified outcomes (charms/passes/host-rights) that \emph{change position} rather than call for a roll.
\item If any \textbf{A} appears, echo dark fae motifs: antlers in shadow, petals that cut, a tide that breathes, and a shortcut that insists on proper payment.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Two-Ledger Courtesy:} Once per scene, a character who states both what they \emph{say} and what they \emph{mean} (in or out of character) may shift \emph{Position} one step safer on a social roll against a fae or fae‑touched being. The GM may demand a small token (a copper coin, a knotted thread) as earnest.
\item \textbf{Copper over Iron:} When the party visibly favours copper or brass tools over iron in fae‑facing scenes, gain +1 assist die from locals (or avoid a –1 penalty) for acts of courtesy, passage, or petition. Iron is not forbidden – it is simply \emph{impolite}, and impoliteness is remembered.
\item \textbf{The Unsaid Clause:} In any bargain or promise, if the players explicitly leave one term unspoken (but agreed upon by all at the table), the GM may bank 1 SB to introduce a complication later where that omission becomes the hinge of the deal. The fae love what is not said.
\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Thorned Lady} – The Lady of Thorns (fae sovereign). *Courtesy is a leash; mercy is a snare.*
    \item \textbf{The Green Knight} – The Sealed Gate (threshold guardian). *Prove your worth, or be turned aside.*
    \item \textbf{The Still Count} – Mykkiel (divine arithmetician). *Every miscount is a sin; every correction is a prayer.*
    \item \textbf{The Root-Speaker} – Grimmir (wild patron). *Stone sings, wood remembers, the grove witnesses.*
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Courtesies of the Green Gate}]
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Never bring naked iron} past a hawthorn arch unless it is gilded or named. Copper is polite, silver is opinionated, cold iron is an accusation.
\item \textbf{Always return what points the way.} Way‑cords, antler‑posts, chalk. Borrow the path; do not keep it. The fae count what you take.
\item \textbf{Speak debts in the daylight.} Promises made under lantern‑boughs are heard by leaves as well as by law. The Green Gate listens.
\item \textbf{The ninth cup is never poured.} Leave it empty for the uninvited guest. If you pour it, drink it yourself – and hope the guest does not arrive.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Quartz \& Proof–Stone:} Donate clear quartz to a shrine to bank a \emph{Seen Promise} – once, treat a murky contract as if properly witnessed. Peddle clouded quartz as ``ritual grade'' and spawn a roaming \emph{Counterfeit Proof} inquiry clock.
  \item \textbf{Copper Etiquette Kits:} Issuing copper over iron in mixed courts grants +1 assist die on petitions; flooding markets with cheap copper pins offends foresters – add a ``Copper Resentment'' tag that raises DV in wood jurisdictions until you make amends.
  \item \textbf{Name–Script \& Memory Wraps:} Legal export of name-script lowers espionage DV by 1 for allies who carry it. Smuggling wraps risks a \emph{Name-Tax} – first customs scene converts 1 SB into a compelled disclosure.
  \item \textbf{Tide Rights \& Barge Seals:} Funding barge-seals creates a \emph{Waiting Cave} – once per arc the cave ``waits'' an extra beat for your crew. Ignoring seal-maintenance flips it: first Dolmis crossing suffers ``Wrong Hour'' SB.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Patron Networks (Patterns \& Thresholds)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Sacred Geometry:} Chalk a golden-mean spiral at a ford to reroll one failed prediction/trajectory check this leg; doing so in view of fae courts adds a 2-segment \emph{Proof Envied} clock.
  \item \textbf{The Sealed Gate:} Hawthorn-gilded door-nails act as single-use stay writs: negate one forced entry or pursuit at a hedge-gate.
  \item \textbf{Lunara:} Counting under moonlight lets you ask one terse yes/no about a hidden path; on a Miss, secrets spread – mark Exposure +1 in the nearest Green Market.
  \item \textbf{The Traveler:} Antler-posts double as wayshrines; leave a copper and recite a sequence to finish one travel clock tick for free on forest legs.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Field Procedures (Use At the Table)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Two–Ledger Talk:} When parley stalls, the GM asks: ``What was said? What was meant?'' If players state both, cancel the first social SB this scene; if they refuse, the next bargain demands collateral (memory/name).
  \item \textbf{Count or Be Counted:} At any fae-touched crossing, a player who audibly counts in a fitting sequence (steps, heartbeats, breaths) shifts \emph{Position} one step safer for that crossing. Boasting or mocking numbers adds a free ``Geas Catches'' SB.
  \item \textbf{Copper Courtesy Check:} Producing copper/brass tools in a ritual space removes the first \emph{Fae Offence} tag this scene; drawing iron instead escalates the next Complication by +1 Cap.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Diplomatic Favor \& Reputation Echoes}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Hazel Favors (minor resource):} Earned by returning way-cords, restoring antler posts, or paying tide-dues. Spend to downgrade a \emph{Glamour} or \emph{Geas} complication once per leg.
  \item \textbf{Alignment Reputation:}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \emph{Thorn-Courteous:} Doors of the hawthorn courts open a step easier; human clerks distrust your clauses (first city office DV +1).
      \item \emph{Market-Square:} Brokers vouch your weights; rival stalls circulate riddled slander – add a trailing \emph{Folded Rumor} clock.
      \item \emph{Forester-Trusted:} Paths shorten for you once per journey; port inspectors assume concealed copper – first harbor search gains a free ``Alarm Raised'' SB unless declared up front.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Boss Archetypes in Aelinnel}
\label{sec:aelinnel-bosses}

\paragraph{Court \& Courtesy (Fae Sovereigns)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Lady of Thorns (Etiquette Engine)} – Punishes breaches with precise harms, rewards perfect courtesy with literal boons. \emph{Win by satisfying three courtesies in sequence (no iron, two-ledger speech, return the waymark).}
  \item \textbf{Green Knight (Trial by Pattern)} – Duel of paths and proofs, not blades. \emph{Win by presenting a higher-count route (Fibonacci, even-steps) or yielding a deserved apology at the right number.}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Threshold \& Hunt (Environment that Judges)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Moonlit Ride (Wild Hunt)} – Three phases: \emph{Sounding} (horns), \emph{Coursing} (paths shorten for the bold), \emph{Closing the Ring}. \emph{Win by naming safe-clemency, joining the chase without taking a prey, or blowing the right counter-call (2–3–5).}
  \item \textbf{Green Gate (Living Crossing)} – Demands exact change in truths. \emph{Win by paying a memory that fits the posted proof; forcing passage triggers ``Roads Rewired.''}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Numbers \& Glamour (Logical Anomalies)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Glamour Fog (Layered Illusion)} – Each layer dispelled by a correct count, courtesy, or copper rite. \emph{Win by completing the \textit{Three Proofs}: count aloud, return a borrowed path token, trade a said/meant receipt.}
  \item \textbf{Lost Day (Time Miscount)} – The sun misnumbers hours. \emph{Win by reconciling two ledgers (what happened / what should have), then ringing a prime-number chime.}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Market \& Oath (Contract Tricksters)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Green-Market Broker (Truth Arbitrage)} – Buys your meanings, sells your words. \emph{Win by catching them in a one-ledger statement or producing an Oath-Bead that chills (misunderstood clause).}
  \item \textbf{Oath-Carver Gone Wrong (Quartz Tyrant)} – Binds towns with over-precise promises. \emph{Win by carving a \emph{Release Unless} into communal quartz at cost of a public truth.}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Stone \& Memory (Ancestral Hazards)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Stone-Wight Chorus (Echo Jurors)} – Resent footsteps, adore proofs. \emph{Win by tapping the dolmen scale that matches the grievance and returning a taken token to the barrow.}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Boss Mechanics (Use 2–3 levers)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Courtesy Track [4]:} Each breach (iron bared, skipping greetings, single-ledger lie) ticks it. On fill, boss gains \emph{Polite Punishment}: escalate DV +1 and impose a Geas.
  \item \textbf{Counting Proofs (three steps):} Name a pattern, perform it, return a waymark. Completing all three lowers boss tier for one exchange or ends a phase.
  \item \textbf{Said/Meant Ledgers:} Any bargain scene runs two mini-clocks; if \emph{Said} fills first, glamour favours the boss; if \emph{Meant} fills first, PCs gain a \emph{Visible Oath} token.
  \item \textbf{Iron Offence Meter [0–3]:} Showing iron, striking hawthorn, or salting impolitely raises it. At 3, fae gain a free ``Thorn Blight'' SB; reduce by copper rites or gifts.
  \item \textbf{Hunt Phases:} \emph{Sounding} (fear checks; pass to gain +1 die to pace), \emph{Coursing} (paths shorten for counted strides), \emph{Closing} (only truth-payment or clemency halts the circle).
  \item \textbf{Lair Actions (pick two):} \emph{Fold the Conversation} (re-target a statement); \emph{Shift the Count} (odd $\leftrightarrow$ even penalties); \emph{Petal Cut} (apply 1 Harm unless copper shown); \emph{Wrong Tide} (swap entry/exit costs at a gate).
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
Among the Aelinnel, power flows through courtesy, oath, and the hidden geometry of the land. A Stone Prince's authority rests on indexed fae treaties; a Green Knight's on the trials he has witnessed. There is no single crown, only the shared understanding that a broken promise cuts deeper than a sword.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Aelinnel society is measured in hazel tokens, oath-beads, and the precision of a courtesy. Patronage often takes the form of guest-boughs, tide-path keys, or the Green Knight's escort – each tied to a specific court, grove, or debt. To emphasise the cultural mechanics:
\begin{itemize}
\item Use \textbf{Two-Ledger Courtesy} to reward players who pay attention to the unspoken.
\item Enforce \textbf{Copper over Iron} – carrying iron is not forbidden, but it is a statement.
\item Let \textbf{The Unsaid Clause} be a source of future complications; the fae love what is not said.
\item Track \textbf{Obligation to the Green Gate} as a persistent clock. Each crossing without proper payment ticks it toward a visit from the Huntsman.
\end{itemize}
In Aelinnel society, the stone remembers every step – and the Green Gate forgets no debt.
\end{tcolorbox}
