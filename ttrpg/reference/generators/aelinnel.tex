\section{Regional Guide: Aelinnel}
\label{chap:aelinnel-gnomes}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Count the steps, weigh the words, and never trust a single telling.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Lady of Thorns (Fae Sovereign):}  
  ``The law here flows like water over stone -- it finds its own path, and woe to those who try to dam it with paper and pretense. The earth itself will testify against the unjust. But the earth also lies -- politely, and only when the moon is full.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Charcoal-burner (Commoner):}  
  ``Our kilns tell us more than your books ever could. When the smoke curls left, the tide's with you. When it splits in three, best stay by your own hearth till it burns straight again. And never trust a court that speaks in only one voice -- the silence is always the second witness.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Dark faerie tale, moonlit groves, polite iron, and the uncanny weight of a promise unsaid.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A tide-shed at dawn, fog curling over anchored barges, gulls crying omens over the river's black glass. A gnomish reeve offers passage on a sealed barge: ``The Green Gate opens at neap tide. What we trade there, the Dolmis will not name. And if you hear someone counting your footsteps, do not turn around -- they are only making sure you still have them.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Stone, Bough, and Bright Things}]
The Aelinnel do not build cities. They \emph{grow} them -- from granite and hawthorn, from tide-polished bone and the shadow of a laugh. Their halls thread through sea-cliffs and root-galleries, their roads are counted by antler-posts that move at night, and their doors open only when you have forgotten why you came. Here, promises have \emph{weight} -- not the weight of ink on parchment, but the weight of a stone dropped into a still pool. The ripples go on forever.

To the north, the Valewood breathes; to the south, the downs of Amedell where the Aelaerem tie red threads to keep the Hollow at bay. The Green Gate sometimes opens onto halfling barrows -- a fact neither people speaks of aloud, because speaking aloud gives the gate permission to move.

No single crown rules the gnomish lands. Dozens of courts, seasonal pacts, and garden societies weave a shifting lattice of loyalty. Some Aelinnel keep the old fae treaties, speaking in two voices and never carrying naked iron; others barter in the Green Market with memories instead of coin, treating courtesy as a kind of lockpicking. Still others vanish into the deep woods, listening to stag-roads where the trees whisper gossip about the living. The gnomish heart is divided -- and that division is the only reason the fae haven't eaten them whole.

Craft and courtesy govern every traffic between mortal keeps and the courts under the hill. Gnomish stone-singers hum bridges into alignment, their melodies keeping the Green Gate at bay. Forester-wardens hammer copper nails into thresholds because iron, they say, \emph{startles the truth}. In markets under living roofs, a sentence can be folded like a napkin, and a memory can be wrapped for travel -- but never opened before the second sunrise. Paths sometimes shorten for those who keep good count, and lengthen for those who sneer at patterns. The fae are never rude -- but they are never, ever \emph{safe}.
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Tide-rift steps} -- Cut into black rock; limpets and old votive nails keep count for those who forget. The ninth step is underwater -- and it still counts. \texttt{[THRESHOLD]}
\item \textbf{Moonwell basin} -- A granite bowl; coins turn green in a week, names in a season, and memories in a year. The water never reflects the same face twice. \texttt{[MAGIC]}
\item \textbf{Dolmen stair} -- Up a ridge; each capstone rings a different interval if you tap it true. The ninth stone is silent -- it only rings when someone lies. \texttt{[MUSIC]}
\item \textbf{Charcoal coppice} -- Under witch-hazel; a neat ash-heap hides a door that opens on even knocks only. Knock an odd number, and the door will open -- but not where you left it. \texttt{[SECRET]}
\item \textbf{Basalt organ cliffs} -- Sea-caves breathe like slow bellows; the wind prefers prime numbers. When it plays a ninth, the cliffs hum back. \texttt{[NATURE]}
\item \textbf{Stag Road} -- Game-trail marked by antler-posts; hooves know it best, maps accept it grudgingly. The posts move at night -- not to confuse, but to keep the road honest. \texttt{[TRAVEL]}
\item \textbf{Quartz spring} -- Sand shines like ground stars; drinkers tell the same story with better structure. The ninth sip tastes of copper -- and a promise you haven't made yet. \texttt{[HEALING]}
\item \textbf{Elf-causey} -- Pale flags through a marsh; visible at dawn and dusk and whenever someone is counting aloud. Count to nine, and the flags turn to water. \texttt{[FAE]}
\item \textbf{Root gallery} -- Under an oak hill; lanterns hang from living bark -- two lit, one listening. The listening lantern is for the Hollow. It never lights. \texttt{[UNDERGROUND]}
\item[J] \textbf{Barrow gallery} -- Stone chimes; a cold draft from below plays scales no throat can sing. The ninth note is always a sigh. \texttt{[DEATH]}
\item[Q] \textbf{Thorn Court ring} -- Hawthorns trained into arches; petals fall like knife-points and settle into proofs. Step into the centre, and the thorns will ask you a riddle. Answer wrong, and you become a hedge. \texttt{[FAE]} \texttt{[DANGER]}
\item[K] \textbf{Hall of Aelinnel} -- A timber keep threaded between standing stones; floors level themselves for guests. The ninth stone in the floor is always warm -- it remembers the last person who lied. \texttt{[CIVILISATION]}
\item[A] \textbf{The Green Gate} -- In a sea-arch; at certain tides it opens to a forest not on any map, and expects exact change. It does not accept coin. It accepts \emph{explanations}. \texttt{[GATE]}

\section*{Diamonds --- Treasures \& Discoveries (charms, passes, host-rights, exceptions)}
\label{sec:aelinnel-rewards}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Hazel token} -- Step through a hedge uncut, once; the scratch you did not get will \emph{remember} you kindly. The token is a nut. Crack it open, and the hedge will part -- but it will also ask a question. \texttt{[PROTECTION]}
\item \textbf{Salt license} -- Carry and use ward-salt where it's discouraged; doors frown and open anyway. The license is a pinch of salt in a silk pouch. The silk was a promise. \texttt{[UTILITY]}
\item \textbf{Dolmen carving-right} -- Inscribe a name or claim on a speaking stone; arguments quiet while you carve. The stone will remember your chisel's touch. It will also remember what you were thinking. \texttt{[AUTHORITY]}
\item \textbf{Guest-bough} -- From the Thorn Court; one night's safe table among thorns; knives behave. The bough is a twig with three thorns. Break one thorn for each courtesy you ask. When all three are broken, the table becomes a door. \texttt{[HOSPITALITY]}
\item \textbf{Quarry allotment} -- Of clear quartz; subtle doors open to its bearer if asked in the right tense. The right tense is not past or future -- it is \emph{conditional}. \texttt{[ACCESS]}
\item \textbf{Green Market voucher} -- Buy a truth at face value (today only); tomorrow costs interest. The interest is always a memory -- and the voucher chooses which one. \texttt{[COMMERCE]}
\item \textbf{Oath-bead} -- A spoken promise warms the bead when kept, chills when not, glows if misunderstood. A glowing bead is not a broken promise -- it is a promise that has learned to speak for itself. \texttt{[OATH]}
\item \textbf{Tide-path key} -- Lawful crossing of a named sea-cave at neap; the cave will wait. The key is a piece of dried seaweed. It will dissolve after one use -- but the cave will remember your face. \texttt{[TRAVEL]}
\item \textbf{Forest truce} -- Hunt, cut, or travel through a warded copse without offence; the birds file no reports. The truce is a single leaf. Carry it in your mouth, and the forest will not see you -- but the Hollow will. \texttt{[PERMISSION]}
\item[J] \textbf{Green Knight escort} -- From ridge to gate; watchers bow, paths part, insults lodge elsewhere. The Knight does not speak during the escort. The silence is the escort. \texttt{[PROTECTION]}
\item[Q] \textbf{Private audience} -- With the Lady of Thorns; a whisper exits as policy with petals attached. The petals are sharp. Do not handle them carelessly -- they cut what they agree with. \texttt{[AUTHORITY]}
\item[K] \textbf{Stone Prince's seal} -- Levies defer, foresters guide, scribes stop arguing and fetch tea. The seal is a pebble. It only works when you are not looking at it. \texttt{[AUTHORITY]}
\item[A] \textbf{Wild Hunt clemency} -- Ride under the horn for one night; no hound will take you, no obligation will sleep. The clemency is a silver bell. Ring it, and the Hunt will pass you by -- but the Hollow will ride behind you until dawn. \texttt{[PROTECTION]}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Field Procedures (Use At the Table)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Two-Voice Talk:} When parley stalls, the GM asks: ``What was said? What was meant?'' If players state both, cancel the first social SB this scene; if they refuse, the next bargain demands collateral (memory/name). The collateral is always returned -- but never in the same condition.
\item \textbf{Count or Be Counted:} At any fae-touched crossing, a player who audibly counts in a fitting sequence (steps, heartbeats, breaths) shifts \emph{Position} one step safer for that crossing. Boasting or mocking numbers adds a free ``Geas Catches'' SB. The geas is always about a door. The door is always behind you.
\item \textbf{Copper Courtesy Check:} Producing copper/brass tools in a ritual space removes the first \emph{Fae Offence} tag this scene; drawing iron instead escalates the next Complication by +1 Cap. The escalation is always polite. The result is never.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Boss Archetypes in Aelinnel}
\label{sec:aelinnel-bosses}

\paragraph{Court \& Courtesy (Fae Sovereigns)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Lady of Thorns (Etiquette Engine)} -- Punishes breaches with precise harms, rewards perfect courtesy with literal boons. \emph{Win by satisfying three courtesies in sequence (no iron, two-voice speech, return the waymark).} The third courtesy is always a riddle. The riddle is always about a door.
\item \textbf{Green Knight (Trial by Pattern)} -- Duel of paths and proofs, not blades. \emph{Win by presenting a higher-count route (Fibonacci, even-steps) or yielding a deserved apology at the right number.} The right number is never nine. The right number is always eight -- and a pause.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Threshold \& Hunt (Environment that Judges)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Moonlit Ride (Wild Hunt)} -- Three phases: \emph{Sounding} (horns), \emph{Coursing} (paths shorten for the bold), \emph{Closing the Ring}. \emph{Win by naming safe-clemency, joining the chase without taking a prey, or blowing the right counter-call (2--3--5).} The counter-call must be a question. The question must be about the Hollow. The Hollow does not answer -- but it listens.
\item \textbf{Green Gate (Living Crossing)} -- Demands exact change in truths. \emph{Win by paying a memory that fits the posted proof; forcing passage triggers ``Roads Rewired.''} The posted proof is always incomplete. The missing part is the ninth word.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Numbers \& Glamour (Logical Anomalies)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Glamour Fog (Layered Illusion)} -- Each layer dispelled by a correct count, courtesy, or copper rite. \emph{Win by completing the \textit{Three Proofs}: count aloud, return a borrowed path token, trade a said/meant receipt.} The third proof is always a promise. The promise is always to a shadow.
\item \textbf{Lost Day (Time Miscount)} -- The sun miscounts hours. \emph{Win by reconciling two ledgers (what happened / what should have), then ringing a prime-number chime.} The prime number is always 2, 3, 5, or 7 -- never 9. 9 is not a number in Aelinnel. 9 is a \emph{suggestion}.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Market \& Oath (Contract Tricksters)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Green-Market Broker (Truth Arbitrage)} -- Buys your meanings, sells your words. \emph{Win by catching them in a one-voice statement or producing an Oath-Bead that chills (misunderstood clause).} The misunderstood clause is always the one you left unsaid. The broker knew you would.
\item \textbf{Oath-Carver Gone Wrong (Quartz Tyrant)} -- Binds towns with over-precise promises. \emph{Win by carving a \emph{Release Unless} into communal quartz at cost of a public truth.} The truth must be one you have never told. The quartz will remember it. The quartz will also share it -- politely, and only with the Hollow.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Stone \& Memory (Ancestral Hazards)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Stone-Wight Chorus (Echo Jurors)} -- Resent footsteps, adore proofs. \emph{Win by tapping the dolmen scale that matches the grievance and returning a taken token to the barrow.} The token is always a memory. The memory is always someone else's. Return it carefully -- the dead are particular about provenance.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
Among the Aelinnel, power flows through courtesy, oath, and the hidden geometry of the land. A Stone Prince's authority rests on indexed fae treaties; a Green Knight's on the trials he has witnessed. There is no single crown, only the shared understanding that a broken promise cuts deeper than a sword -- and that a promise kept is a door that opens both ways.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Aelinnel society is measured in hazel tokens, oath-beads, and the precision of a courtesy. Patronage often takes the form of guest-boughs, tide-path keys, or the Green Knight's escort -- each tied to a specific court, grove, or obligation. To emphasise the cultural mechanics:
\begin{itemize}
\item Use \textbf{Two-Voice Courtesy} to reward players who pay attention to the unspoken. The unspoken is where the fae live.
\item Enforce \textbf{Copper over Iron} -- carrying iron is not forbidden, but it is a statement. The statement is always heard. The hearing is always remembered.
\item Let \textbf{The Unsaid Clause} be a source of future complications; the fae love what is not said. They will fill the silence with their own words -- and their words have weight.
\item Track \textbf{Obligation to the Green Gate} as a persistent timer. Each crossing without proper payment ticks it toward a visit from the Huntsman. The Huntsman is always polite. The Huntsman is always hungry.
\end{itemize}
In Aelinnel society, the stone remembers every step -- and the Green Gate forgets no promise. The ninth step is not a step. The ninth step is a door. The door is always open. You just have to remember how to knock.
