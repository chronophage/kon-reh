\section{Theona --- ``Three Greens, No Ninth''}
\label{chap:theona}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{The Green Host rides for those who count to nine. Viterra counts to nine.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Matron of Wells (Elite):}  
  ``The wells here hold more than water -- they hold the dreams and prayers of generations. To govern is to tend these sacred spaces where the living commune with what came before. The Viterran sheriffs call it superstition. The Green Host calls it trespass.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Peat-Cutter (Commoner):}  
  ``The land here remembers everything, especially what you don't say. Skip the ninth step, forget the ninth name, and the green earth takes note. Best keep your omissions honest. The Viterran scribes write everything down. That's why they'll never understand.'' 
\end{quote}

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The isles of Theona rise green and jagged from the Dolmis, their basalt cliffs wrapped in mists and moorland song. Nominally a duchy sworn to Viterra, in truth the Three Greens heed no crown but their own moots, no law but their taboos. The greatest of these is the silence of Nine: no bell tolls a ninth, no bridge counts that span, no oath dares its syllable. It is said that those who break the ban invite the Green Host -- the \textit{Aos S\'i}, the fair folk who were here before the first Viterran longship. The Viterran Viterrans call it superstition. The Theona call it survival.

The Viterran crown claims the isles by right of conquest and papal bull. Its sheriffs impose common law, its bishops build stone churches, its undertakers plant English towns on confiscated lands. But the old ways persist: Brehon judges still speak judgment at the Moot Hill, and the Green Host still rides on the ninth night. To break a taboo is to invite not only the fae but the Viterran inquisitor. Both collect debts. Only one accepts coin.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of wet peat and salt. The silence that follows every eighth bell‐ring. The hawthorn trees at every crossroads -- the \textit{fairy trees} that no local will touch, and that Viterran surveyors learn to avoid after the first one disappears.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never pour the ninth cup at a feast. Never speak the ninth name in a bargain. Never step where a step is missing. The Green Host rides for those who complete what should remain incomplete. The Viterran sheriff will hang you for the same offence -- but the Host takes your name, not your neck.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Three Greens, No Ninth}

Here, peace is a ritual, and every word sharpens the blade that seals it. Theona is claimed by Viterra but ruled by moots, well‑keepers, and the old faith. The Viterran sheriffs demand taxes in silver; the Brehon judges settle disputes in cattle. The Viterran bishops burn offerings to the White Flame; the well-keepers leave butter at hawthorn groves. Every hedge is a boundary, every bridge a negotiation, every ninth step a door the Green Host might open.

Linn longships cut through the fog for raiding; Ashaani slavers hunt the coves in stolen coracles; pirates from Zakov and the Vilikari shore trade stolen goods for kelp and salt. Sidhi sailors and Aelinnel factors run cargo up the tide‑stairs, and Aelaerem hedge‑wives barter red thread for hawthorn passes. Through it all, the Ninth Taboo threads every handshake, every toast, every whispered prayer. To break it is to invite the Green Host---and the Host does not bargain. To break Viterran law is to invite the sheriff's noose. The Theona have learned to break neither -- and to bend both.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A Moot Hill under rain, where vows are carved into stone and knives gleam beside the altar. A Bride‑Peacemaker draws a blade between two bloodlines. ``The ninth vow was never spoken,'' she says. ``But the Green Host remembers. Bring us the head of the slaver who burned the eastern strand, and the feud will end. Fail, and your name will join the uncounted. Or the sheriff will take you for vagrancy -- the crown has declared our moots unlawful assemblies.''

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:theona-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Tide-Stairs} --- Basalt steps into cove; everyone skips the ``missing'' rung.
  \hfill \textit{The missing step is still there---you just can't see it. Step where it should be, or don't step at all. The Viterran engineers measured the stairs. They counted nine. They are no longer here.}
\item \textbf{Saint's Well} --- Under hawthorn hung with ribbons and secrets; wishes tied in cloth.
  \hfill \textit{The ribbons are knotted in eights. The ninth knot is never tied---only imagined. The crown calls it idolatry. The locals call it irrigation.}
\item \textbf{Fog-Wick Tower} --- Burning peat; bell plays eight notes, never nine.
  \hfill \textit{The bell‑ringer wears a mask. Underneath, he has no mouth---only a whisper. The sheriff has tried to arrest him three times. Each time, the tower was empty.}
\item \textbf{Black Bog Causeway} --- White stones like teeth; will‑lights test your stride.
  \hfill \textit{The stones were set by a king who drowned. He counts each footstep from below. The Viterran surveyors replaced the stones with wooden posts. The posts burned.}
\item \textbf{Cliff Ringfort} --- Over kelp‑nets; storm‑ladders creak like hymns.
  \hfill \textit{The ladders are hauled up at dusk. If you're left below, you sleep with the tide. The crown calls it a "fortified dwelling without licence." The fort has no licence. It has never needed one.}
\item \textbf{Barrow Field} --- Standing stones; birds hush at noon, the earth remembers.
  \hfill \textit{The stones have ears. Do not speak a secret you cannot afford to lose. The Viterran antiquary who tried to excavate one now tends the barrow as a shrine. He does not remember why.}
\item \textbf{Sea-Cave Harp} --- Tide‑ropes sing under headland; echoes bargain for safe passage.
  \hfill \textit{The harp has no player. It plays itself when the drowned want to speak. The crown has posted a notice: "Unauthorized musical performances prohibited." The notices wash away.}
\item \textbf{Uncounted Bridge} --- Nine‑arch causeway; locals tally ``eight‑and‑one.''
  \hfill \textit{The ninth arch is there---but the locals will deny it. Walk it at your own risk. The Viterran cartographer marked it on his map. His map now shows a bridge to nowhere.}
\item \textbf{Ogham Grove} --- Carved pillars around an absent ninth post; runes speak in silence.
  \hfill \textit{The missing post was removed by a king who broke a vow. The runes still curse his line. The Viterran bishop ordered the grove destroyed. The axes broke.}
\item[J] \textbf{Coracle Harbor} --- Jammed in a notch; upturned boats roof the sheds.
  \hfill \textit{The coracles are made of hide and guilt. Each one has carried a secret. The harbormaster pays no tax. The crown cannot find him.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Green Moot Hill} --- Three Greens' court; benches of turf, salt wind judges.
  \hfill \textit{The wind never stops. It carries the votes of the dead. The Viterran sheriff declared the moot an "illegal assembly." The wind knocked his hat off. He has not returned.}
\item[K] \textbf{High Hall} --- Whale‑rib beams; storm banners sleep, kings are chosen.
  \hfill \textit{The kings are chosen by lot---a knotted cord pulled from a pot. The cord has eight knots. The crown's appointed governor was given the ninth knot. The knot came undone.}
\item[A] \textbf{Lookout Cliff} --- One vantage where all three isles stand in a single gaze.
  \hfill \textit{From here, you can see the Green Host riding on the horizon---if it wants you to. You can also see the Viterran garrison on the far isle. They do not wave.}
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Green Neighbor} --- Polite emissary from under the hill; borrowed boots, strange customs.

\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Peacemaker} --- Walks between bloodlines with a silver knife; peace in marriage.

  \hfill \textit{The word you cannot speak sits on your tongue like a stone. The Viterran merchant who said "nine" in a contract now sells only in eights. His customers have not noticed.}
\item \textbf{Salt-Rot} --- Smokehouse fails; fish spoil, tempers rise like the tide.
  \hfill \textit{The salt was cut with grave dust. Someone wanted the winter to be hard. The Viterran garrison's supplies rotted. They bought from the locals. The price was a promise.}
\item \textbf{Procession Clash} --- Saint‑day vs. hawthorn taboo; shutters slam, peace fractures.
  \hfill \textit{The saint's banner and the green branch cannot touch. When they do, blood follows. The Viterran bishop ordered the hawthorn removed. The tree grew back overnight.}
\item \textbf{Wave-Count} --- Eighth swell, Nameless follows; boats tremble, sailors pray.
  \hfill \textit{The ninth swell never comes. But something rides the eighth. The Viterran customs cutter that chased a smuggling boat did not see the eighth swell. It is at the bottom.}
\item \textbf{Ringfort Wakes} --- A bone‑judge is ``consulted''; now you're named in the old law.
  \hfill \textit{The bone‑judge's skull is filled with peat smoke. It speaks in riddles---and judgments. The Viterran magistrate who ignored a bone‑judge's ruling now has no memory of the case.}
\item \textbf{Ninth Law} --- Something vital cannot be counted, measured, or named.
  \hfill \textit{You try to count your crew. The number keeps changing. The Viterran census taker who tried to count the islanders now lives in a cave. He counts seagulls.}
\item \textbf{Net-Surge} --- Harbor stairs; kelp wraps ankles and alibis like truth.
  \hfill \textit{The kelp moves against the current. Someone underneath is pulling. The Viterran harbormaster who tried to dredge the kelp is now missing. The kelp is thicker.}
\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Theft} --- At Moot---peace flips to feud; you're caught mid‑ritual.
  \hfill \textit{The stolen bride's hand is cold. She has been dead for a year. The Viterran sheriff who tried to stop the theft now wears a wedding ring. He does not remember the wedding.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Mainland Return} --- Exile with papers; elders arrested ``for progress.''
  \hfill \textit{The papers are stamped with a Viterran seal. The seal is still warm---and wrong. The elder who was arrested now works in the governor's garden. He does not speak. The plants are very healthy.}
\item[K] \textbf{Green Host Rides} --- Hooves like drums, cattle scatter, sentries sleep.
  \hfill \textit{The riders have no faces---only masks of woven hawthorn. The Viterran garrison that saw the Host now stands watch at noon. They will not explain why.}
\item[A] \textbf{Great Fog} --- Routes loop, bells lie, every promise takes the long way.
  \hfill \textit{The fog is old. It remembers promises made to the dead. The Viterran governor who swore to "pacify" the isles is still lost in it. His dispatches arrive late. They make no sense.}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:theona-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (blessings/tokens/rights) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item The ``No Ninth'' custom: whenever a 9 appears in your seed, add an omission to the scene (a missing step, an unsaid name, an unseen guest). If the 9 is a Diamond, you may break the taboo once---but someone will come to collect later.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{green and count} motifs---hawthorn that whispers, bells that miscount, promises that take the long way home.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:theona-mechanics}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Silent Count:} Players and GMs are encouraged to omit a detail (a name, a step, a tally) when the Ninth appears, echoing Theona's taboo. This is how the islanders evade Viterran law: they simply do not say what the law requires. The law cannot count what is not spoken.
\item \textbf{Green Host's Omen:} Any dream sequence or omen may be framed as the ride of the Green Host---hoofbeats across the sky, wind through the moors, sea spray tasting of salt and blood. The Viterran garrison has learned to ignore the omens. The garrison is understrength.
\item \textbf{Bride-Peace Feasts:} Weddings, truces, and funerals blur; sharing a feast can end a feud for a season---or doom a guest if the ninth cup is poured. The Viterran crown has outlawed these feasts. The feasts continue. The crown's officers attend. They never pour the ninth cup.
\item \textbf{The Viterran Plantation Clock [8]:} Tracks the Viterran crown's efforts to colonise the isles. Advances when the party assists the crown (provides evidence, enforces a writ, pays a tax) or when a Viterran sheriff successfully imposes common law. Retreats when the party supports a moot, hides a fugitive, or exposes a crown official's corruption. When full, a new plantation is declared: a Viterran town is built, lands are confiscated, and a new sheriff is appointed. The Green Host's attention ticks up.
\item \textbf{Brehon Law vs. Common Law:} In any legal dispute, the party may choose to invoke Brehon law (custom, precedent, witness) or Viterran common law (statute, seal, written evidence). Brehon law grants +1 die if the party can produce a respected elder or a witnessed oral history; Viterran law grants +1 die if the party has a writ or a seal. Choosing one over the other shifts the Viterran Plantation clock by 1 segment toward or away from the crown.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Saint Bríd of the Unquenched Well (Syncretic):} A local saint venerated by both the Temple of Light and the old well‑keepers. Her shrine is a well hung with red ribbons; pilgrims who tie an eighth knot and leave a silver coin may ask one question of the Green Host—but the answer comes in a dream, and the dream always costs a memory. The Viterran bishop has declared her a heretic three times. Each time, the declaration was found burned. The well still flows.
  \item \textbf{Saint Colmán of the Eighth Bell (Oath of Mercy and Grace):} A missionary who tried to convert the isles by ringing a ninth bell. The Green Host took his tongue, and he spent the rest of his life speaking in eights. His hospice still stands near the Fog‑Wick Tower; it heals the fog‑sick, but every patient leaves unable to count past eight. The Viterran crown has tried to close the hospice. The hospice's patients now include the governor's son.
  \item \textbf{The Dullahan (The Headless Horseman):} A fae omen that rides when a Viterran sheriff is about to die. It carries its own head under its arm, and it calls the sheriff's name. Those who hear it are dead before dawn. The current sheriff has heard it three times. He is still alive. He is very afraid.
  \item \textbf{The Banshee (Bean Sidhe):} A spirit who keens when a pure-blooded Theona chieftain dies. The Viterran crown has tried to suppress her songs as "noise pollution." The keening continues. The chieftains die. The crown's record-keepers have given up counting.
  \item \textbf{The Morrigan (The Phantom Queen):} A war goddess who appears as a crow before battle. She does not choose sides. She chooses the dead. The Viterran soldiers who see a crow before a skirmish often desert. The local fighters see a crow and sharpen their blades. The Morrigan is amused.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Theona scene, the ninth person to speak in a room draws the Green Host's attention. The wise hold their tongues until someone else breaks the count. Those who speak ninth are said to see a pale rider on the horizon the next night—and the rider is always one name closer. The Viterran governor who spoke ninth now has a portrait of himself that ages instead of him. He has not looked at it in a year.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Theona Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Bog:} Toss a crust into the mist before crossing a causeway. Ignore the first \emph{Ground-Mist} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Eighth:} Tie a single knot in a ribbon before a moot. Cancel \emph{Spoken Geas} or take --1 die on your next oath (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Well:} Whisper a dead enemy's name into Saint's Well. Gain +1 Effect against his kin for the scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Matron.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Theona, power flows through ancient custom, sacred spaces, and the careful observance of taboo. The Three Greens maintain their independence through a web of moots, well‑keepers, and geas‑keepers who ensure the old laws are honored. The King and Matron rule by consensus and tradition, while the unseen Lady Beneath influences events through dreams and whispers. Against them stands the Viterran crown — a Viterran power of sheriffs, bishops, and undertakers who seek to replace Brehon law with common law, hawthorn with church stone, and the Green Host with the White Flame.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Theona revolves around blessings, exemptions, and the delicate balance of keeping or breaking taboos. Rewards often take the form of tokens, rights, or divine favor that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize the Viterran‑Irish conflict:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (ribbons, tokens, blessings) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided by crown officials.
\item Let the Viterran crown and the Green Host issue conflicting exemptions — players must choose whose favor matters more, and whose curse is worse.
\item Use the moots, wells, and causeways as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of Brehon law (and its deliberate omissions) determines success against Viterran common law.
\item The Viterran Plantation Clock is a visible campaign front. Players can accelerate or delay it, but once it fills, the isles change forever — new towns, new sheriffs, new taboos.
\end{itemize}
In Theona, your word is your weave, and your weave determines whether you walk the green path or invite the Host — or the sheriff's noose.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Thematic SB Spend Table}
\label{sec:theona-sb}

\subsection*{Minor Complications (1 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Exposure:} Your actions draw unwanted attention from \textbf{moot-keepers, well-guardians, or Viterran sheriffs}.
\item \textbf{Noise:} Sounds of your actions alert nearby \textbf{coracle crews, ringfort sentries, or crown patrols}.
\item \textbf{Trace:} Evidence of your passage marks your route for \textbf{trackers, green neighbors, or crown informers}.
\item \textbf{Delay:} A brief but meaningful setback costs you \textbf{time or favorable tide}.
\item \textbf{Supply Strain:} Mark +1 segment on a relevant \textbf{resource clock}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Moderate Setbacks (2 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Alarm Raised:} \textbf{Wick-warden, moot official, or Viterran bailiff} becomes aware and begins responding.
\item \textbf{Position Lost:} You lose advantageous ground/cover/stealth due to \textbf{mist shift, taboo breach, or a sheriff's roadblock}.
\item \textbf{Foe Appears:} A \textbf{feuding house member, bone-judge, or crown soldier} arrives on scene.
\item \textbf{Gear Trouble:} A piece of equipment becomes \textbf{Compromised/Neglected}.
\item \textbf{Lock/Barrier:} A simple obstacle now requires a test to overcome.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Serious Trouble (3 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Reinforcements:} Additional \textbf{coracle crews, green neighbors, moot enforcers, or crown militia} arrive.
\item \textbf{Key Gear Breaks:} A crucial tool/weapon becomes temporarily unusable.
\item \textbf{Major Twist:} The situation fundamentally changes - \textbf{taboo broken/green host rides/feud declared/crown warrant issued}.
\item \textbf{Rail Tick:} Advance a relevant campaign/front clock by 1 segment.
\item \textbf{Condition Applied:} Mark \textbf{Fatigue 1/Harm 1/Condition} appropriate to fiction.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Major Turns (4+ SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Trap Springs:} A prepared danger activates with full effect.
\item \textbf{Authority Arrival:} \textbf{King, Matron, Lady Beneath, or Viterran Governor} intervenes.
\item \textbf{Scene Shift:} The environment changes dramatically - \textbf{fog rises/bells ring/causeway shifts/crown forces arrive}.
\item \textbf{Patron Omen:} Divine/arcane forces take notice - \textbf{omen appears/blessing lost/curse manifests}.
\item \textbf{Narrative Pivot:} The story takes an unexpected turn that reframes objectives.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Region-Specific SB Options}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Theona (Silent Count):} Steps disappear mid‑climb, names vanish from lips, the ninth item in a list goes unmentioned.
\item \textbf{Theona (Green Host):} Hoofbeats echo without a horse, mist takes familiar shapes, dreams predict the next action.
\item \textbf{Theona (Viterran Law):} A writ is declared void, a sheriff's seal cracks, a witness remembers the ninth name.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Pursuit Ladder (Near / Mid / Far)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Advance/Withdraw (Wits + Stealth):} On a hit, shift one band. On a strong hit, also impose \emph{Fog Bank}.
\item \textbf{Cut the Writ (Presence + Sway):} On a hit, freeze range for one exchange; if a moot token is in your possession, +1 die.
\item \textbf{Weather the Host (Resolve + Spirit):} On a hit in \emph{Green Host Rides}, you pick who is named; on a miss, the GM does.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Boss Seeds (three‑phase foes)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Unsung Bell (forbidden note given will)} --- \emph{Phases}: Hum in Mist $\rightarrow$ Echo Under Well‑stone $\rightarrow$ Command of Fog. \emph{Cracks}: bind its clapper with eight ribbons; let a rival speak the ninth and pay; drown the echo at the Sea‑Cave Harp.
\item \textbf{Bride-Thief of the Green Host (dream raider)} --- \emph{Phases}: Invitation in Sleep $\rightarrow$ Procession Across Moor $\rightarrow$ Feast Without Ninth Cup. \emph{Cracks}: cut the dream‑veil with silver; sing satire at the Green Moot Hill; trade a Bride‑Charter.
\item \textbf{The Sheriff's Reckoning (Viterran enforcer)} --- \emph{Phases}: Plantation Decree $\rightarrow$ Common Law Assize $\rightarrow$ The Ninth Hanging. \emph{Cracks}: produce a witnessed Brehon judgment older than the crown's claim; pay a wergild in cattle, not coin; let the Green Host take his name.
\item \textbf{The Missing Step (living omission)} --- \emph{Phases}: Vanished Witness $\rightarrow$ Unread Clause $\rightarrow$ Unwalkable Bridge. \emph{Cracks}: invite a Taboo‑Witness to count aloud; spill nine drops into Saint's Well but \emph{name only eight}; let the Lady Beneath keep a memory.
\end{itemize}
