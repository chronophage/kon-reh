\subsection*{Quick Reference}

\end{quote}

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The isles of Theona rise green and jagged from the Dolmis, their basalt cliffs wrapped in mists and moorland song. Nominally a duchy sworn to Viterra, in truth the Three Greens heed no crown but their own moots, no law but their taboos. The greatest of these is the \textbf{silence of Nine}: no bell tolls a ninth, no bridge counts that span, no oath dares its syllable. It is said that those who break the ban invite the \textbf{Green Host}---the \textit{Aos S\'{\i}}, the elder folk who were here before the first Viterran longship. 

The Viterran crown claims the isles by right of conquest and papal bull. Its sheriffs impose common law, its bishops raise stone steeples. But the \textbf{Church of the Everflame} watches from Whiterick Abbey. The Abbess schemes to preserve power by pitting Viterra, the native factions, and raiders against each other, seeking to purge the ``pagan'' taboos and impose imperial orthodoxy. To break a taboo is to invite not only the fae but the inquisitor. The sheriff leaves a noose. The Host leaves a hollow in the mist where your name used to be. The Abbess leaves a brand that burns until confession.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of wet peat and salt. The silence that follows every eighth bell-ring. The hawthorn and blackthorn trees at every crossroads---the \textit{fairy thorns} that no local will touch, and that Viterran surveyors learn to skirt after the first one disappears.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never pour the ninth cup at a feast. Never speak the ninth name in a bargain. Never step where a step is missing. The Green Host rides for those who complete what should remain incomplete. The Viterran sheriff will hang you for the same offense---but the Host takes your name, not your neck. The Everflame Inquisitor will burn you for the silence itself.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Three Greens, No Ninth}

Here, peace is a ritual, and every word sharpens the blade that seals it. Theona is claimed by Viterra but ruled by moots, well-keepers, and the old faith. The Viterran sheriffs count heads for the tax-roll; the Brehon judges measure truth by the witness of cattle and the memory of old words. The Viterran bishops raise hymns to the White Flame; the well-keepers leave milk and butter at the thorn crosses. The Everflame Inquisitors count sins in the silence between prayers. Every hedge is a boundary, every bridge a negotiation, every ninth step a door the Green Host might open.

Linn dragon-prows cut through the fog for raiding; Ashaani slavers hunt the coves in stolen coracles; pirates from Zakov and the Vilikari shore trade stolen goods for kelp and salt. Sidhi sailors and \textbf{Aelinnel} factors run cargo up the tide-stairs, and \textbf{Aelaerem} hedge-wives barter red thread for hawthorn passes. To the west, across the narrow sound, the Viterran crown sharpens its taxes and its nooses. Through it all, the Ninth Taboo threads every handshake, every toast, every whispered prayer. To break it is to invite the Green Host---and the Host does not relent. To break Viterran law is to invite the sheriff's noose. To speak the silence is to invite the Everflame's brand. The Theona have learned to break neither---and to bend all three.

\noindent\textbf{Starting Location:} A Moot Hill under rain, where vows are carved into standing stone and blackthorn knives gleam beside the ogham pillar. A Bride-Peacemaker draws a blade between two bloodlines. ``The ninth vow was never spoken,'' she says. ``But the Green Host remembers. Bring us the head of the slaver who burned the eastern strand, and the feud will end. Fail, and your name will join the uncounted. Or the sheriff will take you for vagrancy---the crown has declared our moots unlawful assemblies. Or the Abbess will call it heresy.''

\subsection*{The Almanac of Ordinary Days (Theona)}

\noindent\textit{A few glimpses of life on the Three Greens, as told by a peat-cutter to a visiting merchant.}

\begin{quote}
``What do we eat for breakfast? Oatcakes and heather ale---the oats grown on terraced hillsides, the heather harvested from the high moors. The ale is brewed in secret, by women who have forgotten more recipes than the men will ever learn. The men don't ask. The men are wise.

``What does a child learn before they can walk? To count to eight. Theona children are taught numbers on their fingers---eight fingers, two thumbs. The thumbs are not counted. The thumbs are for pointing. Pointing is for warnings. Warnings are for the ninth thing.

``First thing you notice about a stranger? Their shadow. Theona is a land of mists and twilight. A stranger whose shadow is too short has been touched by the Host. Too long? Running from them. No shadow? Already taken. Don't speak to them. Don't meet their eyes. Don't offer them salt. The Everflame priests say shadows are lies. They burn the shadow to save the soul.

``Most common crime? Breaking the ninth taboo---but that's not a crime, it's an invitation. The Host rides for those who pour the ninth cup, speak the ninth name, or take the ninth step. The Host doesn't arrest. It \textit{takes}. The taken are never found. Their empty cups remain on the table. The cups are warm for three days. The Inquisitors say the cups are evidence. They keep them in glass jars.

``What does no one talk about but everyone knows? The Green Host aren't ghosts. They're living Theonans who broke the taboo and were taken. They ride with the King in Green, who was once a chieftain of the northern isle. He broke the ninth vow. He wasn't taken. He was crowned. His crown is woven from blackthorn. His throne is a barrow. His court is the mist. The Abbess says he is a demon. She has offered gold for his head. The gold sits in the bog.

``Where do the dead go? To the barrows. Buried in long, low mounds that line the eastern cliffs. Planted with gorse and broom. The gorse blooms yellow in spring---yellow for mourning. The broom is for memory. The dead aren't mourned. They're remembered. Remembering is enough. The Everflame demands graves with names. The names are washed away by rain.

``How do you apologize for a serious wrong? You walk the Ninth Mile---a stretch of road that circles the central isle. Nine miles long. Walk it at night, alone, with no light. If you reach the end by dawn, you're forgiven. If you don't, the Host will find you. It's always looking. It's always waiting. The Inquisitors have tried to pave the Ninth Mile. The stones sink.

``Most important festival? The Feast of the Eighth Cup. Autumn equinox. Every household pours eight cups of mead---one for each living generation. The ninth cup is poured onto the ground. The ground drinks. The ancestors listen. They don't answer. The Abbess sends her clerics to spill the cups. They spill them on their own feet.

``The secret that could destroy a powerful person? The Three-Isles King is a changeling. The real king drowned in a peat bog while hunting, back in 798 AR. The changeling was placed by the Lady Beneath as part of an old pact---peace between the isles. The peace has held. The changeling has aged. His reflection doesn't. The Abbess knows. She waits for the reflection to fail.''
\end{quote}

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:theona-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Tide-Stairs} --- Basalt steps into cove; everyone skips the ``missing'' rung.
  \hfill \textit{The missing step is still there---you just can't see it. Step where it should be, or don't step at all. The first Viterran engineer counted nine rungs aloud. He is still counting, somewhere in the fog. The Abbess tried to fill the gap with stone. The stone sank.}
\item \textbf{Saint's Well} --- Under hawthorn hung with ribbons and secrets; wishes tied in cloth.
  \hfill \textit{The ribbons are knotted in eights. The ninth knot is never tied---only imagined. The crown calls it idolatry. The Abbess calls it witchcraft. The water tastes of iron and old tongues.}
\item \textbf{Fog-Wick Tower} --- Burning peat; bell plays eight notes, never nine.
  \hfill \textit{The bell-ringer wears a mask. Underneath, he has no mouth---only a whisper. The sheriff has tried to arrest him three times. Each time, the tower was empty. The Inquisitor tried to silence the bell. The bell rang in his head.}
\item \textbf{Black Bog Causeway} --- White stones like teeth; will-lights test your stride.
  \hfill \textit{The stones were set by a king who drowned. He counts each footstep from below. The Viterran surveyors replaced the stones with wooden posts. The posts burned. The Abbess claims the bog is consecrated ground. It isn't.}
\item \textbf{Cliff Ringfort} --- Over kelp-nets; storm-ladders creak like hymns.
  \hfill \textit{The ladders are hauled up at dusk. If you're left below, you sleep with the tide. The crown calls it a ``fortified dwelling without license.'' The fort has no license. It has never needed one. The Everflame wants to build a chapel there. The chapel slides into the sea.}
\item \textbf{Barrow Field} --- Standing stones; birds hush at noon, the earth remembers.
  \hfill \textit{The stones have ears. Do not speak a secret you cannot bear to lose. The gorse hums with bees that should not be awake. The Viterran antiquary who tried to excavate one now tends the barrow as a shrine. He does not remember why. The Abbess tried to exorcise the field. The exorcism wrote itself on the stones.}
\item \textbf{Sea-Cave Harp} --- Tide-ropes sing under headland; echoes bargain for safe passage.
  \hfill \textit{The harp has no player. It plays itself when the drowned want to speak. The crown has posted a notice: ``Unauthorized musical performances prohibited.'' The notices wash away. The Abbess says the music is a siren song. She listens anyway.}
\item \textbf{Uncounted Bridge} --- Nine-arch causeway; locals tally ``eight-and-one.''
  \hfill \textit{The ninth arch is there---but the locals will deny it. Walk it at your own risk. The Viterran cartographer marked it on his map. His map now shows a bridge to nowhere. The Abbess tried to bless the ninth arch. The blessing curdled.}
\item \textbf{Ogham Grove} --- Carved pillars around an absent ninth post; runes speak in silence.
  \hfill \textit{The missing post was a king's vow carved in heartwood. The runes still curse his line. The Viterran bishop ordered the grove destroyed. The axes broke. The Abbess ordered it burned. The fire turned green.}
\item[J] \textbf{Coracle Harbor} --- Jammed in a notch; upturned boats roof the sheds.
  \hfill \textit{The coracles are made of hide and guilt. Each one has carried a secret. The harbormaster pays no tax. The crown cannot find him. The Abbess cannot buy him.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Green Moot Hill} --- Three Greens' court; benches of turf, salt wind judges.
  \hfill \textit{The wind never stops. It carries the votes of the dead. The Viterran sheriff declared the moot an ``illegal assembly.'' The wind knocked his hat off. He has not returned. The Abbess sent a clerk to record the votes. The clerk recorded his own confession.}
\item[K] \textbf{High Hall} --- Whale-rib beams; storm banners sleep, kings are chosen.
  \hfill \textit{The kings are chosen by lot---a knotted cord pulled from a pot. The cord has eight knots. The crown's appointed governor was given the ninth knot. The knot came undone. The Abbess offered a golden cord. It strangled the bearer.}
\item[A] \textbf{Lookout Cliff} --- One vantage where all three isles stand in a single gaze.
  \hfill \textit{From here, you can see the Green Host riding on the horizon---if it wants you to. You can also see the Viterran garrison on the far isle. They do not wave. You can see Whiterick Abbey's smoke. It is always green.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Hearts --- People \& Factions}
\label{sec:theona-people}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Peat-Cutter} --- Steady barrow, steadier gossip; knows every hidden path.
  \hfill \textit{Her barrow has a false bottom. She hides keepsakes there---and the tax collector when he comes. He is always grateful. She hides Inquisitors too. They are less grateful.}
\item \textbf{Well-Keeper} --- Ties ribbons, unknots feuds; wishes and wisdom in equal measure.
  \hfill \textit{She never ties a ninth ribbon. The ninth wish is always her own. She has wished for the sheriff's departure every day for seven years. He is still here. She is patient. She has wished for the Abbess's silence. The silence is growing.}
\item \textbf{Kelp-Netter} --- Reads rip lines like currents; tide as teacher.
  \hfill \textit{His net has a torn patch. He sewed it with thread from a hanged man's shirt. The hanged man was a horse thief. The net has never torn again. The thread glows in the presence of Everflame steel.}
\item \textbf{Harp-Satirist} --- A few bars can end a career or a war; music as a weapon.
  \hfill \textit{Her harp is strung with catgut and grudges. Each string is a name. She has a string for the sheriff. She has not played it yet. She is waiting. She has a new string for the Abbess. It hums when the censer smokes.}
\item \textbf{Wick-Warden} --- Tends fog-lamps, counts in eights; guardian of the silent count.
  \hfill \textit{He counts on his fingers---thumb, finger, finger, finger---and stops. The ninth is for the fog. He taught the Viterran lighthouse keeper to count. The keeper lost his job. He taught the Abbess's novice. The novice lost his tongue.}
\item \textbf{Taboo-Witness} --- Geas-keeper who knows which words ruin deals.
  \hfill \textit{She carries a bell with no clapper. She rings it with her tongue. The Viterran magistrate demanded she testify. She rang the bell. He withdrew the charge. The Inquisitor demanded she confess. She rang the bell. He confessed for her.}
\item \textbf{Island Abbot} --- Saint's bell, tolerant smile; faith in the old ways.
  \hfill \textit{His bell rings eight times. The ninth ring is the silence that follows. The Viterran bishop sent a letter of censure. The abbot used it to light his fire. The Abbess sent a letter of conversion. The abbot used it to wrap his cheese.}
\item \textbf{Green Neighbor} --- Polite emissary from under the hill; borrowed boots, strange customs.
  \hfill \textit{He never steps on a crack. He says the earth has teeth. The Viterran tax assessor stepped on a crack. He now has a limp. The Abbess's envoy stepped on a crack. He now preaches a new gospel of dirt.}
\item \textbf{Coracle-Captain} --- Eight-boat flotilla; swift as rumor, sure as the tide.
  \hfill \textit{Her boats have no names. She says naming a vessel invites the Green Host to count it. The Viterran harbormaster tried to register one. He could not find it. It was right in front of him. The Abbess tried to buy one. The gold turned to kelp.}
\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Peacemaker} --- Walks between bloodlines with a silver knife; peace in marriage.
  \hfill \textit{Her knife has a wooden blade. The families cut their palms with wood---blood without iron. The Viterran magistrate declared the ritual ``superstitious.'' The knife is now his letter opener. The Abbess declared it ``unconsecrated.'' The blade cuts her robes.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Matron of Wells} --- Queen-mother in truth if not in name; wisdom in water.
  \hfill \textit{She drinks only from the ninth well---the one no one admits exists. The Viterran governor demanded to see it. She showed him a dry hole. He believed her. The Abbess demanded to bless it. The water boiled her hand.}
\item[K] \textbf{Three-Isles King} --- Elected by the moots; whale-bone throne, weathered eyes.
  \hfill \textit{He lost his left hand to a Linn raid. The replacement is carved from hawthorn. He signs Viterran writs with the false hand. The signatures are legally binding. He is not. The Abbess claims he is a puppet. The strings are roots.}
\item[A] \textbf{Lady Beneath} --- Unseen queen who bargains in dreams; hawthorn's whisper.
  \hfill \textit{She has no face. Those who see her forget their own names for a year. The Viterran surveyor who saw her now calls himself ``John.'' He does not remember his wife's face. The Abbess claims she is a demon. The demons agree.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:theona-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Ground-Mist} --- Erases tracks; horns travel, edges do not; navigation by sound alone.
  \hfill \textit{The mist has a voice. It whispers your name---but only once. The Viterran patrol that entered the mist returned speaking only in questions. The Everflame patrol returned speaking in confessions.}
\item \textbf{Bog-Lights} --- Lure teamster off the causeway; recovery costs time and pride.
  \hfill \textit{The lights are the souls of those who broke their geas. Follow them, and you'll never find your way back. The Viterran tax collector followed them. He now lives in the bog. He has no use for silver. The Inquisitor followed them. He found a shrine. He burned it. It grew back.}
\item \textbf{Spoken Geas} --- No ninth word in parley---or the oath curdles like milk.
  \hfill \textit{The word you cannot speak sits on your tongue like a stone. The Viterran merchant who said ``nine'' in a pact now sells only in eights. His customers have not noticed. The Abbess's priest said ``nine'' in a prayer. The prayer answered itself.}
\item \textbf{Salt-Rot} --- Smokehouse fails; fish spoil, tempers rise like the tide.
  \hfill \textit{The salt was cut with grave dust. Someone wanted the winter to be hard. The Viterran garrison's supplies rotted. They bought from the locals. The price was a promise. The Everflame pantry rotted. They blamed the Host. The Host blamed the Abbess.}
\item \textbf{Procession Clash} --- Saint-day vs.\ hawthorn taboo; shutters slam, peace fractures.
  \hfill \textit{The saint's banner and the green branch cannot touch. When they do, blood follows. The Viterran bishop ordered the hawthorn removed. The tree grew back overnight. The Abbess ordered the saint removed. The saint stayed. The Abbess left.}
\item \textbf{Wave-Count} --- Eighth swell, Nameless follows; boats tremble, sailors pray.
  \hfill \textit{The ninth swell never comes. But something rides the eighth. The Viterran customs cutter that chased a smuggling boat did not see the eighth swell. It is at the bottom. The Everflame ship saw it. It turned back.}
\item \textbf{Ringfort Wakes} --- A bone-judge is ``consulted''; now you're named in the old law.
  \hfill \textit{The bone-judge's skull is filled with peat smoke. It speaks in riddles---and judgments. The Viterran magistrate who ignored a bone-judge's ruling now has no memory of the case. The Inquisitor who ignored it now has no memory of his faith.}
\item \textbf{Ninth Law} --- Something vital cannot be counted, measured, or named.
  \hfill \textit{You try to count your crew. The number keeps changing. The Viterran census taker who tried to count the islanders now lives in a cave. He counts seagulls. The Abbess's scribe tried to count the sins. The ink ran out.}
\item \textbf{Net-Surge} --- Harbor stairs; kelp wraps ankles and alibis like truth.
  \hfill \textit{The kelp moves against the current. Someone underneath is pulling. The Viterran harbormaster who tried to dredge the kelp is now missing. The kelp is thicker. The Everflame divers found a bell. It rang underwater.}
\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Theft} --- At Moot---peace flips to feud; you're caught mid-ritual.
  \hfill \textit{The stolen bride's hand is cold. She has been dead for a year. The Viterran sheriff who tried to stop the theft now wears a wedding ring. He does not remember the wedding. The Abbess tried to sanctify the marriage. The ring turned to wood.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Mainland Return} --- Exile with papers; elders arrested ``for progress.''
  \hfill \textit{The papers are stamped with a Viterran seal. The seal is still warm---and wrong. The elder who was arrested now works in the governor's garden. He does not speak. The plants are very healthy. The Abbess offered him sanctuary. He is now her gardener. The plants are very healthy.}
\item[K] \textbf{Green Host Rides} --- Hooves like drums, cattle scatter, sentries sleep.
  \hfill \textit{The riders have no faces---only masks of woven hawthorn. The Viterran garrison that saw the Host now stands watch at noon. They will not explain why. The Everflame garrison that saw the Host now prays at noon. They pray to the Host.}
\item[A] \textbf{Great Fog} --- Routes loop, bells lie, every promise takes the long way home.
  \hfill \textit{The fog is old. It remembers vows spoken to the dead and the silence that answered. The Viterran governor who swore to ``pacify'' the isles is still lost in it. His dispatches arrive late. They make no sense. The Abbess's dispatches arrive early. They make too much sense.}
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Moot Token} --- Eight-knotted cord; skip any local queue or judgment.
  \hfill \textit{The cord is braided from horsehair and silence. Cut a knot to speak. The Viterran magistrate who tried to arrest a token-holder found his courtroom empty. The case was dismissed. The Inquisitor who tried to arrest one found his charges dismissed. By him.}
\item \textbf{Harbor-Green} --- Painted mark exempts your boat from the next levy; tide as ally.
  \hfill \textit{The mark is a circle with an eighth cut. The missing slice is for the Host. The Viterran customs officer who tried to board a marked boat now gathers shells on the beach. The Everflame officer who tried to board it now preaches to the shells.}
\item \textbf{Hawthorn Pass} --- Immunity from a single local taboo (for one scene).
  \hfill \textit{The pass is a hawthorn twig. Carry it in your mouth. Do not chew. The Viterran inquisitor who tried to burn a pass-bearer found his pyre would not light. The Abbess tried to bless the twig. It prickled her.}
\item \textbf{Bell-Right} --- Lawful ring at a fog-wick summons the watchers; law travels on sound.
  \hfill \textit{The bell has no clapper. You must ring it with your own breath. The Viterran constable who heard the bell now refuses to speak of it. His ears are scarred. The Abbess's novice who heard it now speaks only in riddles.}
\item \textbf{Coracle Share} --- Crew and oars pledged for one crossing; tide as a steed.
  \hfill \textit{The share is a hazel wand. Break it in half to claim your passage. The Viterran governor who tried to seize a coracle share found his hands would not close. The Abbess tried to buy the share. The wand turned to ash.}
\item \textbf{Ogham Ruling} --- Monk's reading settles a dispute in your favor; runes as law.
  \hfill \textit{The ruling is carved on a slate. The slate is damp. The runes may change by morning. The Viterran judge who ruled against an Ogham ruling now dreams in runes. He cannot read them. The Abbess's judge ruled against it. The slate cracked his gavel.}
\item \textbf{Unnumbered Right} --- Ignore the Ninth prohibition for one action (binds you to an answer later).
  \hfill \textit{The right is a silence. Use it, and the Green Host will ask for its name. The Viterran spy who used it now has no shadow. The Host has his name. The Abbess's spy who used it now has no reflection. The Flame has his soul.}
\item \textbf{Salt-Cure License} --- Process scarce fish despite ration edicts; preservation as power.
  \hfill \textit{The license is signed with a thumbprint. The thumb is not yours. The Viterran ration officer who refused a licensed boat now has no appetite. He eats only ash. The Abbess's officer who refused it now eats only salt. He is very thirsty.}
\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Charter} --- Two feuding houses sheath knives for a season; peace in marriage.
  \hfill \textit{The charter is written on a wedding veil. The bloodstains are from the first cut of peace. The Viterran crown's peace commissioners have tried to copy the charter. Their copies burn. The Abbess tried to consecrate it. The veil caught fire.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Private Moot} --- With Matron and King; whispers become policy.
  \hfill \textit{The moot is held in a fog hollow. What is spoken there is never written. The Viterran governor's spy who tried to listen now speaks only in fog. He is very popular with the locals. The Abbess's spy who tried to listen now speaks only in confessions. He is very popular with the Inquisitors.}
\item[K] \textbf{Whale-Escort} --- Eight-oar guards across the Dolmis reach; strength in numbers.
  \hfill \textit{The crew carries your name on a scrap of vellum. If the paper gets wet, you are alone. The Viterran warship that tried to intercept a whale-escorted vessel now has no name. The Everflame ship that tried to intercept it now has no crew.}
\item[A] \textbf{Green Favor} --- One uncanny exception to custom, spoken beneath hawthorn.
  \hfill \textit{The favor is a dream. When you wake, the hawthorn will remember---even if you don't. The Viterran governor who asked for a Green Favor now believes he is a king. The actual king is amused. The Abbess who asked for a Green Favor now believes she is a saint. The Host is amused.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:theona-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Timer (2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (blessings/tokens/rights) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item The ``No Ninth'' custom: whenever a 9 appears in your seed, add an omission to the scene (a missing step, an unsaid name, an unseen guest). If the 9 is a Diamond, you may break the taboo once---but someone will come to collect later.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{green and count} motifs---hawthorn that whispers, bells that miscount, promises that take the long way home.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:theona-mechanics}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Silent Count:} Players and GMs are encouraged to omit a detail (a name, a step, a tally) when the Ninth appears, echoing Theona's taboo. This is how the islanders evade Viterran law: they simply do not say what the law requires. The law cannot count what is not spoken. \textbf{Mechanical Effect:} If a player speaks the ninth name/step/item in a scene, tick the \textbf{Green Host Rising} timer by 1. If they omit it successfully, gain 1 Boon.
\item \textbf{Green Host's Omen:} Any dream sequence or omen may be framed as the ride of the Green Host---hoofbeats across the sky, wind through the moors, sea spray tasting of salt and blood. The Viterran garrison has learned to ignore the omens. The garrison is understrength. The Everflame Inquisitors document them as evidence of demonic activity.
\item \textbf{Bride-Peace Feasts:} Weddings, truces, and funerals blur; sharing a feast can end a feud for a season---or doom a guest if the ninth cup is poured. The Viterran crown has outlawed these feasts. The feasts continue. The crown's officers attend. They never pour the ninth cup. The Abbess sends clerics to bless the cups. The cups break.
\item \textbf{The Viterran Plantation Timer [8]:} Tracks the Viterran crown's efforts to colonize the isles. Advances when the party carries the king's salt (guides a patrol, enforces a writ, speaks against a moot) or when a Viterran sheriff successfully imposes common law. Retreats when the party supports a moot, hides a fugitive, or exposes a crown official's corruption. When full, a new plantation is declared: a Viterran town is built, lands are confiscated, and a new sheriff is appointed. The Green Host's shadow grows.
\item \textbf{The Everflame Orthodoxy Timer [6]:} Tracks the Abbess of Whiterick's influence. Advances when the party aids the Inquisition, purges ``heresy,'' or accepts Everflame gold. Retreats when the party exposes Everflame manipulation, protects native rites, or turns Everflame allies against the Abbess. When full, the Inquisition establishes a permanent tribunal; native rites become illegal under penalty of burning.
\item \textbf{Brehon Law vs.\ Common Law:} In any legal dispute, the party may choose to invoke Brehon law (custom, precedent, witness) or Viterran common law (statute, seal, written evidence). Brehon law grants +1 die if the party can produce a respected elder or a witnessed oral history; Viterran law grants +1 die if the party has a writ or a seal. Choosing one over the other shifts the Viterran Plantation timer by 1 segment toward or away from the crown. Choosing to ignore both (relying on Taboo) shifts the Green Host timer.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Saint Br\'{\i}d of the Unquenched Well (Syncretic):} A local saint venerated by both the Temple of Light and the old well-keepers. Her shrine is a well hung with red ribbons; pilgrims who tie an eighth knot and leave a silver coin may ask one question of the Green Host---but the answer comes in a dream, and the dream always takes a memory. The Viterran bishop has declared her a heretic three times. Each time, the declaration was found burned. The well still flows. The Abbess claims the well is a portal. She is right.
  \item \textbf{Saint Colm\'{a}n of the Eighth Bell (Oath of Mercy and Grace):} A missionary who tried to convert the isles by ringing a ninth bell. The Green Host took his tongue, and he spent the rest of his life speaking in eights. His hospice still stands near the Fog-Wick Tower; it heals the fog-sick, but every patient leaves unable to count past eight. The Viterran crown has tried to close the hospice. The hospice's patients now include the governor's son. The Abbess has tried to recruit him. He speaks only in eights.
  \item \textbf{The Dullahan (The Headless Horseman):} A fae omen that rides when a Viterran sheriff is about to die. It carries its own head under its arm, and it calls the sheriff's name. Those who hear it are dead before dawn. The current sheriff has heard it three times. He is still alive. He is very afraid. The Abbess has heard it once. She laughed. The head did not laugh.
  \item \textbf{The Banshee (Bean Sidhe):} A spirit who keens when a pure-blooded Theona chieftain dies. The Viterran crown has tried to suppress her songs as ``noise pollution.'' The keening continues. The chieftains die. The crown's record-keepers have learned to stop at eight. The Abbess's record-keepers stop at seven. They fear the eighth.
  \item \textbf{The Morrigan (The Phantom Queen):} A war goddess who appears as a crow before battle. She does not choose sides. She chooses the dead. The Viterran soldiers who see a crow before a skirmish often desert. The local fighters see a crow and sharpen their blades. The Morrigan is amused. The Abbess's soldiers see a crow and pray. The crow eats their prayers.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Theona scene, the ninth person to speak in a room draws the Green Host's attention. The wise hold their tongues until someone else breaks the count. Those who speak ninth are said to see a pale rider on the horizon the next night---and the rider is always one name closer. The Viterran governor who spoke ninth now has a portrait of himself that ages instead of him. He has not looked at it in a year. The Abbess who spoke ninth now has a portrait that does not age. She looks at it every day.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Theona Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Bog:} Toss a crust into the mist before crossing a causeway. Ignore the first \emph{Ground-Mist} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Eighth:} Tie a single knot in a ribbon before a moot. Cancel \emph{Spoken Geas} or take --1 die on your next oath (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Well:} Whisper a dead enemy's name into Saint's Well. Gain +1 Effect against his kin for the scene, but the Matron will remember your name. The Abbess will also remember.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Theona, power flows through ancient custom, sacred spaces, and the careful observance of taboo. The Three Greens maintain their independence through a web of moots, well-keepers, and geas-keepers who ensure the old laws are honored. The King and Matron rule by consensus and tradition, while the unseen Lady Beneath influences events through dreams and whispers. Against them stands the Viterran crown---a power of sheriffs, bishops, and undertakers who seek to replace Brehon law with common law, hawthorn with church stone, and the Green Host with the White Flame. And watching from Whiterick Abbey, the Everflame Abbess schemes to preserve power by pitting Viterra, the native factions, and raiders against each other.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Theona revolves around blessings, exemptions, and the delicate balance of keeping or breaking taboos. Rewards often take the form of tokens, rights, or divine favor that can be woven into greater standing. To emphasize the colonial conflict:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (ribbons, tokens, blessings) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided by crown officials.
\item Let the Viterran crown, the Green Host, and the Everflame Abbess grant conflicting protections---players must choose whose favor matters more, and whose curse is worse.
\item Use the moots, wells, and causeways as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of Brehon law (and its deliberate omissions) determines success against Viterran common law and Everflame orthodoxy.
\item The Viterran Plantation Timer and Everflame Orthodoxy Timer are visible campaign fronts. Players can accelerate or delay them, but once they fill, the isles change forever---new towns, new sheriffs, new taboos, new inquisitors.
\end{itemize}
In Theona, your word is your weave, and your weave determines whether you walk the green path or invite the Host---or the sheriff's noose---or the Abbess's brand.
\end{tcolorbox}

\paragraph{Minor Complications (1 SB)}
\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Delay:} A brief but meaningful setback costs you \textbf{time or favorable tide}.
\item \textbf{Supply Strain:} Mark +1 segment on a relevant \textbf{resource timer}.
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Foe Appears:} A \textbf{feuding house member, bone-judge, crown soldier, or Everflame penitent} arrives on scene.
\item \textbf{Gear Trouble:} A piece of equipment becomes \textbf{Compromised/Neglected}.
\item \textbf{Lock/Barrier:} A simple obstacle now requires a test to overcome.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Serious Trouble (3 SB)}
\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Major Twist:} The situation fundamentally changes---\textbf{taboo broken/green host rides/feud declared/crown warrant issued/Inquisition summons}.
\item \textbf{Rail Tick:} Advance a relevant campaign/front timer by 1 segment.

\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Authority Arrival:} \textbf{King, Matron, Lady Beneath, Viterran Governor, or Abbess of Whiterick} intervenes.
\item \textbf{Scene Shift:} The environment changes dramatically---\textbf{fog rises/bells ring/causeway shifts/crown forces arrive/Abbess's censer smokes}.
\item \textbf{Patron Omen:} Divine/arcane forces take notice---\textbf{omen appears/blessing lost/curse manifests/Host rides}.
