\section{Theona --- ``Three Greens, No Ninth''}
\label{chap:theona}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Matron of Wells (Elite):}  
  ``The wells here hold more than water – they hold the dreams and prayers of generations. To govern is to tend these sacred spaces where the living commune with what came before.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Peat-Cutter (Commoner):}  
  ``The land here remembers everything, especially what you don't say. Skip the ninth step, forget the ninth name, and the green earth takes note. Best keep your omissions honest.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The isles of Theona rise green and jagged from the Dolmis, their basalt cliffs wrapped in mists and moorland song. Nominally a duchy sworn to Viterra, in truth the Three Greens heed no crown but their own moots, no law but their taboos. The greatest of these is the silence of Nine: no bell tolls a ninth, no bridge counts that span, no oath dares its syllable. It is said that those who break the ban invite the Green Host, and that debts of nine can never be paid in coin.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of wet peat and salt. The silence that follows every eighth bell‐ring. The way locals count on their fingers—thumb, then fingers, then a long pause where the ninth should be.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never pour the ninth cup at a feast. Never speak the ninth name in a bargain. Never step where a step is missing. The Green Host rides for those who complete what should remain incomplete.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Three Greens, No Ninth}

Here, peace is a ritual, and every word sharpens the blade that seals it. Theona is claimed by Viterra but ruled by moots, well‑keepers, and the old faith. Linn longships cut through the fog for raiding; Ashaani slavers hunt the coves in stolen coracles; pirates from Zakov and the Vilikari shore trade stolen goods for kelp and salt. Sidhi sailors and Aelinnel factors run cargo up the tide‑stairs, and Aelaerem hedge‑wives barter red thread for hawthorn passes. Through it all, the Ninth Taboo threads every handshake, every toast, every whispered prayer. To break it is to invite the Green Host—and the Host does not bargain.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A Moot Hill under rain, where vows are carved into stone and knives gleam beside the altar. A Bride‑Peacemaker draws a blade between two bloodlines. “The ninth vow was never spoken,” she says. “But the Green Host remembers. Bring us the head of the slaver who burned the eastern strand, and the feud will end. Fail, and your name will join the uncounted.”

\paragraph*{(Ringfort/Well/Cliff)} Basalt tide‑stairs cut into cove; everyone steps past a “missing” rung; Saint's Well under hawthorn hung with ribbons.

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:theona-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Tide-Stairs} --- Basalt steps into cove; everyone skips the “missing” rung.
  \hfill \textit{The missing step is still there—you just can't see it. Step where it should be, or don't step at all.}
\item \textbf{Saint's Well} --- Under hawthorn hung with ribbons and secrets; wishes tied in cloth.
  \hfill \textit{The ribbons are knotted in eights. The ninth knot is never tied—only imagined.}
\item \textbf{Fog-Wick Tower} --- Burning peat; bell plays eight notes, never nine.
  \hfill \textit{The bell‑ringer wears a mask. Underneath, he has no mouth—only a whisper.}
\item \textbf{Black Bog Causeway} --- White stones like teeth; will‑lights test your stride.
  \hfill \textit{The stones were set by a king who drowned. He counts each footstep from below.}
\item \textbf{Cliff Ringfort} --- Over kelp‑nets; storm‑ladders creak like hymns.
  \hfill \textit{The ladders are hauled up at dusk. If you're left below, you sleep with the tide.}
\item \textbf{Barrow Field} --- Standing stones; birds hush at noon, the earth remembers.
  \hfill \textit{The stones have ears. Do not speak a secret you cannot afford to lose.}
\item \textbf{Sea-Cave Harp} --- Tide‑ropes sing under headland; echoes bargain for safe passage.
  \hfill \textit{The harp has no player. It plays itself when the drowned want to speak.}
\item \textbf{Uncounted Bridge} --- Nine‑arch causeway; locals tally “eight‑and‑one.”
  \hfill \textit{The ninth arch is there—but the locals will deny it. Walk it at your own risk.}
\item \textbf{Ogham Grove} --- Carved pillars around an absent ninth post; runes speak in silence.
  \hfill \textit{The missing post was removed by a king who broke a vow. The runes still curse his line.}
\item[J] \textbf{Coracle Harbor} --- Jammed in a notch; upturned boats roof the sheds.
  \hfill \textit{The coracles are made of hide and guilt. Each one has carried a secret.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Green Moot Hill} --- Three Greens' court; benches of turf, salt wind judges.
  \hfill \textit{The wind never stops. It carries the votes of the dead.}
\item[K] \textbf{High Hall} --- Whale‑rib beams; storm banners sleep, kings are chosen.
  \hfill \textit{The kings are chosen by lot—a knotted cord pulled from a pot. The cord has eight knots.}
\item[A] \textbf{Lookout Cliff} --- One vantage where all three isles stand in a single gaze.
  \hfill \textit{From here, you can see the Green Host riding on the horizon—if it wants you to.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Moot/Keeper/Court)} Peat‑cutter with steady barrow and steadier gossip; well‑keeper who ties ribbons and unknots feuds.

\section*{Hearts --- People \& Factions}
\label{sec:theona-people}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Peat-Cutter} --- Steady barrow, steadier gossip; knows every hidden path.
  \hfill \textit{Her barrow has a false bottom. She hides debts there—and bodies.}
\item \textbf{Well-Keeper} --- Ties ribbons, unknots feuds; wishes and wisdom in equal measure.
  \hfill \textit{She never ties a ninth ribbon. The ninth wish is always her own.}
\item \textbf{Kelp-Netter} --- Reads rip lines like ledgers; tide as teacher.
  \hfill \textit{His net has a torn patch. He sewed it with thread from a hanged man's shirt.}
\item \textbf{Harp-Satirist} --- A few bars can end a career or a war; music as a weapon.
  \hfill \textit{Her harp is strung with catgut and grudges. Each string is a name.}
\item \textbf{Wick-Warden} --- Tends fog‑lamps, counts in eights; guardian of the silent count.
  \hfill \textit{He counts on his fingers—thumb, finger, finger, finger—and stops. The ninth is for the fog.}
\item \textbf{Taboo-Witness} --- Geas‑keeper who knows which words ruin deals.
  \hfill \textit{She carries a bell with no clapper. She rings it with her tongue.}
\item \textbf{Island Abbot} --- Saint's bell, tolerant smile; faith in the old ways.
  \hfill \textit{His bell rings eight times. The ninth ring is the silence that follows.}
\item \textbf{Green Neighbor} --- Polite emissary from under the hill; borrowed boots, strange customs.
  \hfill \textit{He never steps on a crack. He says the earth has teeth.}
\item \textbf{Coracle-Captain} --- Eight‑boat flotilla; swift as rumor, sure as the tide.
  \hfill \textit{Her boats have no names. She says naming a vessel invites the Green Host to count it.}
\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Peacemaker} --- Walks between bloodlines with a silver knife; peace in marriage.
  \hfill \textit{Her knife has a wooden blade. The families cut their palms with wood—blood without iron.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Matron of Wells} --- Queen‑mother in truth if not in name; wisdom in water.
  \hfill \textit{She drinks only from the ninth well—the one no one admits exists.}
\item[K] \textbf{Three-Isles King} --- Elected by the moots; whale‑bone throne, weathered eyes.
  \hfill \textit{He lost his left hand to a Linn raid. The replacement is carved from hawthorn.}
\item[A] \textbf{Lady Beneath} --- Unseen queen who bargains in dreams; hawthorn's whisper.
  \hfill \textit{She has no face. Those who see her forget their own names for a year.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Fog/Feud/Taboo)} Ground‑mist erases tracks; horns travel, edges do not; bog‑lights lure teamster off the causeway.

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:theona-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Ground-Mist} --- Erases tracks; horns travel, edges do not; navigation by sound alone.
  \hfill \textit{The mist has a voice. It whispers your name—but only once.}
\item \textbf{Bog-Lights} --- Lure teamster off the causeway; recovery costs time and pride.
  \hfill \textit{The lights are the souls of debtors. Follow them, and you'll never pay your way out.}
\item \textbf{Spoken Geas} --- No ninth word in parley—or the deal curdles like milk.
  \hfill \textit{The word you cannot speak sits on your tongue like a stone.}
\item \textbf{Salt-Rot} --- Smokehouse fails; fish spoil, tempers rise like the tide.
  \hfill \textit{The salt was cut with grave dust. Someone wanted the winter to be hard.}
\item \textbf{Procession Clash} --- Saint‑day vs. hawthorn taboo; shutters slam, peace fractures.
  \hfill \textit{The saint's banner and the green branch cannot touch. When they do, blood follows.}
\item \textbf{Wave-Count} --- Eighth swell, Nameless follows; boats tremble, sailors pray.
  \hfill \textit{The ninth swell never comes. But something rides the eighth.}
\item \textbf{Ringfort Wakes} --- A bone‑judge is “consulted”; now you're named in the old law.
  \hfill \textit{The bone‑judge's skull is filled with peat smoke. It speaks in riddles—and judgments.}
\item \textbf{Ninth Law} --- Something vital cannot be counted, measured, or named.
  \hfill \textit{You try to count your crew. The number keeps changing.}
\item \textbf{Net-Surge} --- Harbor stairs; kelp wraps ankles and alibis like truth.
  \hfill \textit{The kelp moves against the current. Someone underneath is pulling.}
\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Theft} --- At Moot—peace flips to feud; you're caught mid‑ritual.
  \hfill \textit{The stolen bride's hand is cold. She has been dead for a year.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Mainland Return} --- Exile with papers; elders arrested “for progress.”
  \hfill \textit{The papers are stamped with a Viterran seal. The seal is still warm—and wrong.}
\item[K] \textbf{Green Host Rides} --- Hooves like drums, cattle scatter, sentries sleep.
  \hfill \textit{The riders have no faces—only masks of woven hawthorn.}
\item[A] \textbf{Great Fog} --- Routes loop, bells lie, every promise takes the long way.
  \hfill \textit{The fog is old. It remembers promises made to the dead.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Blessing/Token/Right)} Well‑blessing for a named ford crossing; moot token with eight‑knotted cord skips local queues.

\section*{Diamonds --- Rewards/Leverage}
\label{sec:theona-rewards}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Well-Blessing} --- Named ford crosses you safely, once; water remembers your need.
  \hfill \textit{The blessing is a ribbon. Tie it to your wrist. Do not tie a ninth knot.}
\item \textbf{Moot Token} --- Eight‑knotted cord; skip any local queue or judgment.
  \hfill \textit{The cord is braided from horsehair and silence. Cut a knot to speak.}
\item \textbf{Harbor-Green} --- Painted mark exempts your boat from the next levy; tide as ally.
  \hfill \textit{The mark is a circle with an eighth cut. The missing slice is the toll.}
\item \textbf{Hawthorn Pass} --- Immunity from a single local taboo (for one scene).
  \hfill \textit{The pass is a hawthorn twig. Carry it in your mouth. Do not chew.}
\item \textbf{Bell-Right} --- Lawful ring at a fog‑wick summons the watchers; law travels on sound.
  \hfill \textit{The bell has no clapper. You must ring it with your own breath.}
\item \textbf{Coracle Share} --- Crew and oars pledged for one crossing; tide as a steed.
  \hfill \textit{The share is a tally stick. Break it in half to claim your passage.}
\item \textbf{Ogham Ruling} --- Monk's reading settles a dispute in your favor; runes as law.
  \hfill \textit{The ruling is carved on a slate. The slate is damp. The runes may change by morning.}
\item \textbf{Unnumbered Right} --- Ignore the Ninth prohibition for one action (costs a favor later).
  \hfill \textit{The right is a silence. Use it, and the Green Host will ask for its name.}
\item \textbf{Salt-Cure License} --- Process scarce fish despite ration edicts; preservation as power.
  \hfill \textit{The license is signed with a thumbprint. The thumb is not yours.}
\item[J] \textbf{Bride-Charter} --- Two feuding houses sheath knives for a season; peace in marriage.
  \hfill \textit{The charter is written on a wedding veil. The bloodstains are from the dowry.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Private Moot} --- With Matron and King; whispers become policy.
  \hfill \textit{The moot is held in a fog hollow. What is spoken there is never written.}
\item[K] \textbf{Whale-Escort} --- Eight‑oar guards across the Dolmis reach; strength in numbers.
  \hfill \textit{The crew carries your name on a scrap of vellum. If the paper gets wet, you are alone.}
\item[A] \textbf{Green Favor} --- One uncanny exception to custom, spoken beneath hawthorn.
  \hfill \textit{The favor is a dream. When you wake, the hawthorn will remember—even if you don't.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:theona-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (blessings/tokens/rights) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item The ``No Ninth'' custom: whenever a 9 appears in your seed, add an omission to the scene (a missing step, an unsaid name, an unseen guest). If the 9 is a Diamond, you may break the taboo once—but someone will come to collect later.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{green and count} motifs—hawthorn that whispers, bells that miscount, promises that take the long way home.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Silent Count:} Players and GMs are encouraged to omit a detail (a name, a step, a tally) when the Ninth appears, echoing Theona's taboo.
\item \textbf{Green Host's Omen:} Any dream sequence or omen may be framed as the ride of the Green Host—hoofbeats across the sky, wind through the moors, sea spray tasting of salt and blood.
\item \textbf{Bride-Peace Feasts:} Weddings, truces, and funerals blur; sharing a feast can end a feud for a season—or doom a guest if the ninth cup is poured.
\item \textbf{Raider's Shadow:} Theona's coves are haunted by Linn longships, Ashaani slave barges, and Zakov pirates. Sidhi and Vilikari sailors crew the coracle fleets, while Aelinnel and Aelaerem traders bring red thread and counting stones from the far shore of the Dolmis.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Bitter Root:} Daughters of the Chain run a hospice near the Sea‑Cave Harp. They ask no payment—but they count breaths in eights. A renegade Sister hides among the peat‑cutters, trying to forget her own cord.
  \item \textbf{The Threshold Compendium:} The Ogham Grove is a Class II Anchor site—the missing ninth post holds a spirit that cannot be named. Saikou Ira would ring every bell but the ninth.
  \item \textbf{Malachai, the Chained Angel:} A silver coin that never spends circulates among the coracle crews. Take it, and the Green Host will not ride for you—until the ninth tide.
  \item \textbf{The Grey Wanderer's Grimoire:} Theona's “Taboo‑Witnesses” are natural psions. They read omissions in the same way others read thoughts.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Theona scene, the ninth person to speak in a room draws the Green Host's attention. The wise hold their tongues until someone else breaks the count.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Theona Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Bog:} Toss a crust into the mist before crossing a causeway. Ignore the first \emph{Ground-Mist} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Eighth:} Tie a single knot in a ribbon before a moot. Cancel \emph{Spoken Geas} or take –1 die on your next oath (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Well:} Whisper a dead enemy's name into Saint's Well. Gain +1 Effect against his kin for the scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Matron.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Theona, power flows through ancient custom, sacred spaces, and the careful observance of taboo. The Three Greens maintain their independence through a web of moots, well‑keepers, and geas‑keepers who ensure the old laws are honored. The King and Matron rule by consensus and tradition, while the unseen Lady Beneath influences events through dreams and whispers.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Theona revolves around blessings, exemptions, and the delicate balance of keeping or breaking taboos. Rewards often take the form of tokens, rights, or divine favor that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (ribbons, tokens, blessings) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival factions issue conflicting exemptions, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the moots, wells, and causeways as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of custom determines success.
\end{itemize}
In Theona, your word is your weave, and your weave determines whether you walk the green path or invite the Host.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Thematic SB Spend Table}

\subsection*{Minor Complications (1 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Exposure:} Your actions draw unwanted attention from \textbf{moot-keepers or well-guardians}.
\item \textbf{Noise:} Sounds of your actions alert nearby \textbf{coracle crews or ringfort sentries}.
\item \textbf{Trace:} Evidence of your passage marks your route for \textbf{trackers or green neighbors}.
\item \textbf{Delay:} A brief but meaningful setback costs you \textbf{time or favorable tide}.
\item \textbf{Supply Strain:} Mark +1 segment on a relevant \textbf{resource clock}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Moderate Setbacks (2 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Alarm Raised:} \textbf{Wick-warden or moot official} becomes aware and begins responding.
\item \textbf{Position Lost:} You lose advantageous ground/cover/stealth due to \textbf{mist shift or taboo breach}.
\item \textbf{Foe Appears:} A \textbf{feuding house member or bone-judge} arrives on scene.
\item \textbf{Gear Trouble:} A piece of equipment becomes \textbf{Compromised/Neglected}.
\item \textbf{Lock/Barrier:} A simple obstacle now requires a test to overcome.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Serious Trouble (3 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Reinforcements:} Additional \textbf{coracle crews, green neighbors, or moot enforcers} arrive.
\item \textbf{Key Gear Breaks:} A crucial tool/weapon becomes temporarily unusable.
\item \textbf{Major Twist:} The situation fundamentally changes - \textbf{taboo broken/green host rides/feud declared}.
\item \textbf{Rail Tick:} Advance a relevant campaign/front clock by 1 segment.
\item \textbf{Condition Applied:} Mark \textbf{Fatigue 1/Harm 1/Condition} appropriate to fiction.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Major Turns (4+ SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Trap Springs:} A prepared danger activates with full effect.
\item \textbf{Authority Arrival:} \textbf{King, Matron, or Lady Beneath} intervenes.
\item \textbf{Scene Shift:} The environment changes dramatically - \textbf{fog rises/bells ring/causeway shifts}.
\item \textbf{Patron Omen:} Divine/arcane forces take notice - \textbf{omen appears/blessing lost/curse manifests}.
\item \textbf{Narrative Pivot:} The story takes an unexpected turn that reframes objectives.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Region-Specific SB Options}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Theona (Silent Count):} Steps disappear mid‑climb, names vanish from lips, the ninth item in a list goes unmentioned.
\item \textbf{Theona (Green Host):} Hoofbeats echo without a horse, mist takes familiar shapes, dreams predict the next action.
\item \textbf{Theona (Taboo Law):} Words refuse to form, actions miscount, promises require exact phrasing.
\end{itemize}
