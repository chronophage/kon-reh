\label{chap:sidhi}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{The sea remembers. The lighthouse waits. We will return.}

\begin{quote}

  ``Galanina is not a place on a map. It is a promise that our ancestors made to the sea: we will remember, and we will return. The Covenant gave us courts, but the sea gave us memory. The Admiralty gives us tariffs. We give each other salt and stories.''
\end{quote}

\end{quote}

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The Sidhi are a people of the coast and the diaspora. Their ancestral homeland is Galanina, a rugged shore of limestone cliffs and ancient ports far to the west — a land of olive groves, whitewashed villages, and harbours that once held the ships of a dozen empires. The Ashaani magocracy enslaved them for generations, scattering families across the Amaranthine. Some were taken north to Oshiira, others east through the Hyaar passes, and many south to the slave markets of the Brass Coast. Those who survived the \textbf{Long Trail} — a forced migration spoken of only in whispers and lullabies — carried nothing but the memory of salt and the names of the dead.

Galanina broke free under the Covenant of Mykkiel, whose judges and notaries stamped the liberation with wax and witness. But freedom did not mean return. The Sidhi are scattered still: some returned to Galanina's lighthouse courts, some trade in Oshiiran ports, and many have found refuge on the Brass Coast of Dhahara — a sun-scorched shoreline of palm groves and spice auctions that resembles nothing of their lost homeland. They run ropeways, spice auctions, and Sidhi-run port guilds. They are fierce, proud, and never forget a face. Their speech is a creole of Ashaani and coastal tongues, their law is maritime, and their symbol is the lighthouse lamp — a green flame that shows the faces of the drowned, so that the dead may witness the living.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of salt and old parchment. The way every Sidhi touches a small brass key at their belt — the key to a house they may never see again. The green glow of lighthouse lamps along the shore.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never ask about ancestry. The Ashaani slavery raids and the Long Trail are fresh wounds. Asking which clan someone belongs to is asking how their grandparents were captured, sold, or died on the march.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Weight of Memory}

Power here is measured in lighthouse charters, freedman's seals, and the weight of witness. The Sidhi have no single king; their authority rests in the Covenant courts of Galanina and the port-masters' guilds of the diaspora. A Sidhi merchant's word is her bond, because every bond is witnessed by a lighthouse lamp and recorded in a water-resistant ledger. The Admiralty of Kahfagia administers the Brass Coast, but the Sidhi run the real trade — and they remember every raid, every broken treaty, every ancestor who did not return.

The Long Trail is not a tale told to children. It is a silence. A pause in conversation when someone mentions the eastern passes or the southern coast. An old woman who stops mid‑sentence, touches her brass key, and changes the subject. The Sidhi do not forget, but they have learned that memory is a kind of weight — and they have carried it far enough.

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:sidhi-mechanics}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Long Trail:} A forced migration that scattered the Sidhi across the Amaranthine. No Sidhi speaks of it directly, but every family has a Trail name — a place where an ancestor fell, a river where a child was born, a cave where they hid from slavers. A character who knows a Trail name may roll Wits + Lore (DV 3) to recall a forgotten route, a hidden spring, or a smuggler's cache. On a miss, the memory is too painful; gain 1 Fatigue and a Condition: \emph{Grieving}.
\item \textbf{Lighthouse Courts:} Sidhi legal disputes are settled in the lamp room of a lighthouse, with the green flame as witness. The judge is the keeper. The verdict is written in salt on a slate, then washed away by the tide. Only the flame remembers. A party may request a green flame judgment instead of a conventional trial. Roll Presence + Lore (DV 4). On success, the judge rules in your favour; on a miss, the green flame shows a hidden crime you committed, and the penalty is doubled.
\item \textbf{Freedman's Seal:} A brand or pendant that proves emancipation under the Covenant. A freedman cannot be re-enslaved in any Covenant signatory port. The seal is also a key — it opens the door to the Breath-less network. Once per session, a character with a freedman's seal may ask a Sidhi lighthouse keeper for one favour: safe harbour, a warning, or a message carried by green flame.
\item \textbf{The Ninth Well:} A sacred spring in Galanina where the first Covenant judge drowned a liar. The water is said to remember every false oath. Sidhi who need to prove their innocence drink from the well; liars vomit brine. A character who drinks from the Ninth Well (or carries its water) may reroll a failed social roll involving an oath — but on a second failure, they gain the Condition \emph{Brine-Tongued} (–1 die on all social rolls for one day).
\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Green Keeper} — The Witness. *The flame that shows the truth, even when it burns. Especially when it burns.*
    \item \textbf{The Hook} — Maelstraeus. *The price of a second chance; the interest on a freedman's soul. The interest is always paid in memories.*
    \item \textbf{The Tide Father} — Khemesh. *The pressure beneath the keel; the debt that the sea collects. The Sidhi paid it once. They will not pay again.*
    \item \textbf{The Lantern Bearer} — The Traveler. *The light that guides lost ships — and lost souls. The light that remembers the Trail.*
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Galanina and the Veiled Sisters (Ashaan):} The Sidhi diaspora exists because of Ashaani slavery. A Breath-less agent in the Brass Coast may be the same person who escaped Galanina as a child. Revenge is a slow debt. The Trail is a slow march. Both end at the sea.
  \item \textbf{Lighthouse Charters and the Fhara (Way of Silk):} Fhara water-clerks respect Sidhi lighthouse charters as proof of credit. A Sidhi merchant can borrow water rights against a charter; default means the lighthouse lamp goes out. The lamp has gone out three times. Each time, a Sidhi family vanished from the records.
  \item \textbf{Freedman's Seals and the Covenant (Mykkiel):} The Covenant recognises only one seal higher than a freedman's — the Seal of Mykkiel himself. A party carrying a freedman's seal can demand a hearing before any Covenant judge, anywhere. The judge must hear the case. The judge may also weep.
  \item \textbf{The Brass Coast and Dhaharan Independence:} The Sidhi of the Brass Coast are a major faction in Dhaharan politics. They pay tariffs to the Admiralty but run their own courts, their own ships, and their own spies. A Sidhi port-master may be the key to breaking the Admiralty's hold. The key is a freedman's seal. It is warm. It is waiting.
  \item \textbf{The Long Trail and the Hollow (Eastern Realms):} The Hollow is said to have followed the Sidhi during their migration, feeding on the grief of those who died unburied. Aelaerem hedge-keepers still find Trail markers — cairns of salt and bone — on the eastern passes. They leave offerings of bread and water. They do not speak of what they see.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
Among the Sidhi, power flows through witness, salt, and the green flame of the lighthouse. The Covenant courts provide law, but the true authority is memory — of the raids, of the Long Trail, of the ancestors who did not return. A Sidhi elder's word is law because she remembers when law was a whip.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Sidhi society is measured in charters, seals, and the trust of the lighthouse keeper. Patronage often takes the form of lighthouse rights, freedman's proofs, or archive keys — each tied to a specific port, a specific ancestor, a specific stop on the Trail. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to physical tokens (keys, seals, lenses) that can be lost, stolen, or voided by the Admiralty.
\item Let rival Sidhi port-masters issue conflicting charters, forcing players to choose whose lamp to trust.
\item Use the lighthouse courts, hidden coves, and freedman's squares as arenas for social contests, where a green flame is a witness and a bag of salt is a verdict.
\item Remember the Long Trail. Let it surface in NPC dialogue as an absence, a pause, a change of subject. The Sidhi do not speak of it, but they never forget.
\end{itemize}
In Sidhi society, the sea forgets nothing — and the lighthouse remembers every face.
\end{tcolorbox}
