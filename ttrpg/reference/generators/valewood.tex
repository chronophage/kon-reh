\section{Valewood --- ``Empire Under Leaves''}
\label{chap:valewood}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{The forest remembers. Speak truth, step lightly, and never eat the fruit of a tree with a face.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Hazel Queen (Elite):}  
  ``The forest here remembers empires that crumbled to dust, and its laws are older than your kingdoms. To walk these paths is to enter into covenant with powers that measure time in seasons, not years.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Pathweaver (Commoner):}  
  ``The woods here got a mind of their own -- they'll lead you where you need to go if you ask right, but they'll just as soon lose you if you're carrying ill intent. Best know which is which before you wander off the path.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Dark forest, fey remnant, imperial echo, folk horror.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A moss-covered amphitheater where roots crawl through marble benches and owls whisper verdicts to the wind. An Owl-Sister speaks in riddles: ``The Lethai-ar stir. The paths remember your true name. Speak it into the hollow of this stone, or the forest will learn it from your footsteps.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The Valewood is older than kingdoms, older than the maps that skirt its borders. Beneath its canopy lie the bones of a Lethai empire whose name is forgotten, its towers swallowed by vine and root, its star-roads cracked and phasing between worlds. The air hums with half-remembered songs of magic, and the ruins bleed their mysteries into root and stream. To walk the Valewood is to risk being claimed by it: as guest, prey, heir---or trespasser.

But the empire did not fall to war or plague alone. In their final centuries, the Lethai-thora --- the high elf archivists and sorcerers --- grew desperate to match the Astroegro Empire's arcane might. They turned to forbidden arts: flesh-shaping, soul-stitching, and the binding of patterns that should never have been woven. Deep beneath the Amber Ziggurat, in chambers sealed with runes that weep, they built the \textbf{Genesis Vats}. Here, elf and beast were combined, spirit and stone were fused, and the first beast-kin were born --- not as servants, but as weapons. The vats still hum. The failures, the half-finished, the screaming ones, were loosed into the forest when the laboratories collapsed. They bred true. They became the monsters that now stalk the star-roads: the redcaps, the thorn-walkers, the things with too many eyes.

The empire fell, yes. But something else came through the cracks. A scent of honey and rot. A laugh from a clearing that should not exist. The Lethai-thora sealed the deepest laboratories and burned their records. The few survivors spoke of a ``soft place'' in the world's fabric, a realm of endless twilight where the fae had always waited. They carved warnings into stone: \textit{Do not open the ninth gate. Do not sing the song that calls them.} The gates were sealed. The songs were forgotten. But the forest remembers. And in certain clearings, when the moon is wrong, the air smells of jasmine and grave-soil.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of wet moss and old stone. The way the light filters through leaves like stained glass. The silence that falls when you speak a name too loudly.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never eat the fruit of a tree that has a face. The forest does not forget those who take, and the first bite is always a promise.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Leaves, Oaths, and Echoes}

Here, the past is not past --- it phases in and out like moonlight on water. The Lethai-ar who remain are not conquerors but custodians, tending to laws written before the first human kingdom rose. The fae courts of Hazel and Alder play their endless games of courtesy and obligation, and the beast-kin speak in parliaments where every vote is a scent. Imperial shades march through walls that no longer exist, and the star-roads hum with geometries that twist the unwary into yesterday.

But the true horror lies beneath. The Lethai-thora's experiments left scars that never healed. The beast-kin are not a single people --- they are the descendants of those who survived the vats, their lineages mixed with wild creatures, their memories fragmented. Some still hear the whispers of the old laboratories, calling them to seek the sealed doors. Others have built a society of scent and scar, their laws a deliberate forgetting of what they once were. The monsters --- the true monsters, the ones with no parliament --- are the failed experiments: the redcaps, the thorn-walkers, the things that hunger for warmth. They are the empire's unfinished business.

And somewhere, in a place where the trees grow in impossible spirals, the soft place still breathes. The fae courts are its children. They do not speak of what lies beyond the ninth gate. They only smile, and their smiles have too many teeth.

\subsection*{The Ninth Taboo Manifestation}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=red!60!black,title=The Ninth Taboo]
In the Valewood, the ninth leaf to fall from any tree points to a hidden threshold. The wise catch it or pretend not to see. Those who follow the leaf's direction find a place that was not there a moment ago, and something old waits inside. The threshold may lead to a sealed laboratory, a fae court, or a clearing where the air smells of jasmine and grave-soil. It is always warm.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:valewood-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Sweet Wind} --- Nudges landmarks hedge-width---enough to matter; navigation as deception. `[WEATHER]` `[TRICK]`
  \hfill \textit{The wind smells of honey and lies. Do not trust your nose.}
\item \textbf{Path Reversal} --- Footprints vote to go elsewhere; direction as democracy. `[MAGIC]`
  \hfill \textit{Your tracks point the way you came---even when you haven't turned.}
\item \textbf{Ward-Trap} --- Ancient security treats you as yesterday's invader; the past as prison. `[TRAP]`
  \hfill \textit{The glyphs were set by a dead queen. She still enforces her grudges.}
\item \textbf{Oath-Magnet} --- Spoken promises stick and reshape plans; word as binding magic. `[OATH]`
  \hfill \textit{Every ``I will'' becomes a knot. Every ``I won't'' becomes a chain.}
  \begin{tcolorbox}[colback=red!3,title=Oath-Magnet Mechanics]
    When a character makes a promise in the Valewood, the GM creates a \textbf{Promise Clock [4]}. Each time the character acts against that promise (or fails to act in its favor), tick the timer. When it fills, the forest claims a piece of you: 1 Fatigue, a lost memory (erase a minor fact or face), or a minor geas (the GM chooses). Breaking a promise intentionally (not through failure) instantly ticks the timer twice.
  \end{tcolorbox}
\item \textbf{Geas Catch} --- A careless word triggers a task that names itself; speech as summoning. `[OATH]`
  \hfill \textit{You said ``if only.'' Now the forest has heard you.}
\item \textbf{City Phase} --- Streets rotate; the exit becomes a balcony; space as puzzle. `[DUNGEON]`
  \hfill \textit{The city breathes. You are in its lungs.}
\item \textbf{Name-Theft} --- Something learns your true name's edges; the tests begin. `[DEATH]` `[FEAR]`
  \hfill \textit{First, you forget a minor bond. Then a friend's face. Then your own.}
  \begin{tcolorbox}[colback=red!3,title=Name-Theft Mechanics]
    When \textbf{Name-Theft} occurs, the GM secretly records one piece of the target's identity (a memory, a bond, a talent name). The target suffers $-1$ die on any roll that would rely on that piece until it is restored by a quest or a bargain with the thief (often a fae or shade).
  \end{tcolorbox}
\item \textbf{Ring Claim} --- A circle names you host; feeding guests becomes law. `[SOCIAL]` `[COMMUNITY]`
  \hfill \textit{The mushrooms are listening. They will remember what you serve.}
\item \textbf{Mirror Rain} --- Leaf-drips replay choices you didn't make; the past as echo. `[MAGIC]`
  \hfill \textit{The rain shows you a path you refused. Now it offers it again---with weight.}
\item[J] \textbf{Redcaps Abroad} --- Hats wet, boots quick, courtesies thin; violence as hospitality. `[COMBAT]` `[FAE]`

\item[A] \textbf{Empire Wakes} --- A district aligns; doors open, guardians open farther. `[APOCALYPSE]`
  \hfill \textit{The shades have orders. You are the target. Beneath the city, a vat cracks.}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:valewood-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (charms/keys/passes) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item Empire Echo: when a J/Q/K is the highest rank in your seed, add one visible relic-logic (floating stairs, singing locks, patrol shades).
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{leaf and light} motifs---paths that remember, leaves that whisper, light that moves like water.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Thematic SB Spend Table}
\label{sec:valewood-sb}
\paragraph{Minor Complications (1 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Exposure:} Your actions draw attention from fae courtiers or beast-kin sentinels.
\item \textbf{Noise:} A rustle of leaves, a snapped twig, or a whispered word alerts nearby imperial shades.
\item \textbf{Trace:} Footprints on moss or disturbed lichen mark your passage for trackers.
\item \textbf{Delay:} A path reroutes, a bridge phases out, or a talking tree demands a riddle.
\item \textbf{Supply Strain:} Mark +1 segment on a relevant resource timer (food, light, memory).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Moderate Setbacks (2 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Alarm Raised:} A Green Neighbor or an Owl-Sister becomes aware and begins a hunt.
\item \textbf{Position Lost:} Glamour fog or a sudden star-road shift costs you advantageous ground.
\item \textbf{Foe Appears:} A redcap, a thorn-walker, or a patchwork horror arrives.
\item \textbf{Gear Trouble:} A way-cord tangles, a dew-mirror cracks, a city-shard goes dark.
\item \textbf{Oath Strain:} A promise you made in the forest begins to tighten (tick a Promise Clock).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Serious Trouble (3 SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Reinforcements:} Additional fae courtiers, beast-kin, or imperial shades arrive.
\item \textbf{Key Gear Breaks:} A star-road shard crumbles, a truce-bough wilts, a name-bead cools permanently.
\item \textbf{Major Twist:} A sealed door opens, a vat leaks, the Hazel Queen revokes a protection.
\item \textbf{Rail Tick:} Advance a relevant campaign/front timer by 1 segment (e.g., Forest Waking, Court Schism).
\item \textbf{Condition Applied:} Mark Fatigue 1, Harm 1, or a condition (Geas-Touched, Name-Threaded).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Major Turns (4+ SB)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Trap Springs:} A ward-trap activates, a star-road collapses, or a Genesis Vat failure emerges.
\item \textbf{Authority Arrival:} The Hazel Queen, the Alder King, or the Huntsman intervenes.
\item \textbf{Scene Shift:} The environment changes dramatically---a city phases in, a grove burns, the moon turns red.
\item \textbf{Patron Omen:} A fae omen appears (a singing stone, a weeping statue, a leaf that falls upward).
\item \textbf{Narrative Pivot:} The story reframes---the sealed door was a trap, the beast-kin are not the enemy, the soft place is already here.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Pursuit Ladder (Near / Mid / Far)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Advance/Withdraw (Wits + Survival):} On a hit, shift one band. On a strong hit, also impose \emph{Leaf Blind} (pursuers lose scent).
\item \textbf{Cut the Star-Road (Presence + Lore):} On a hit, freeze range for one exchange; if a way-cord is in your possession, +1 die.
\item \textbf{Weather the Glamour (Resolve + Spirit):} On a hit in \emph{Sweet Wind} or \emph{Name-Theft}, you pick who keeps their bearings; on a miss, the GM does.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Extensions (Plug-in)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Star-Road Phase Clock [6]:} Tracks how often ancient roads realign. Tick when the party lingers or uses magic. At 4, navigation becomes Desperate; at 6, the road leads to the Amber Ziggurat.
\item \textbf{Genesis Vat Leak Clock [8]:} Advances when the party disturbs sealed chambers or fails Spirit rolls near ruins. At 4, strange noises and whispers; at 8, a vat-born horror awakens (use Boss Seed).
\item \textbf{Favor of the Courts:} Earn Hazel Favor (by keeping promises) or Alder Favor (by collecting debts). Spend 3 favor to call a minor boon (safe passage, a single truthful answer from a fae).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Artifacts of the Valewood}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Leaf of the First Tree:} A fossilised leaf that never crumbles. Once per session, reroll a failed promise-related roll. After nine uses, it turns to dust and the Hollow gains a name.
\item \textbf{The Genesis Key:} A brass tuning fork that can open any sealed vat door, but each use ticks the Vat Leak timer by +1.
\item \textbf{Mirror of Unfinished Faces:} Shows not your reflection but the unfinished beast-kin you would have become. Use to intimidate fae (+1 die) but mark 1 Corruption.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Forest Claims:} Any oath, bargain, or gift exchanged binds with more weight. Once per session, a spoken promise becomes magically enforceable: breaking it creates 1d4 SB and echoes through scenes.
\item \textbf{Ruins Awaken:} If you linger at an ancient site, roll or draw to see which mechanism stirs. May grant guidance (lit star-road, a memory shown) or danger (patrol shades, collapsing glyphs).
\item \textbf{Court Interference:} Encounters with fae or beast-kin are never neutral. Once per session, the GM may convert a Diamond into a demand for hospitality, tribute, or alliance---shifting the expedition's tone.
\item \textbf{Genesis Vats Aftermath:} Any scene set in the deep ruins (Amber Ziggurat, Breathing City lower levels) has a chance to trigger a \textbf{Vat Leak} complication. Roll 1d6; on a 1--2, a failed experiment emerges (a redcap, a thorn-walker, or a patchwork horror). On a 3--4, a ghost of a Lethai-thora archivist appears and offers a bargain. On a 5--6, the party finds a sealed door. The door is warm.
\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Unseen Court} --- The Witness. \textit{A parliament of silent roots that remembers every broken promise.}
    \item \textbf{The Root-King} --- The Alder King (local aspect). \textit{Promise that grows like a vine, choked by its own leaves.}
    \item \textbf{The Star-Weaver} --- The Ninth. \textit{The missing thread in every star-road, the silence after the song.}
    \item \textbf{The Green Saint} --- Oath of Flame \& Light (syncretic). \textit{A martyr who fed her own name to the Hollow to seal a breach.}
    \item \textbf{The Vat-Keeper} --- The Clockwork Monad (apocalyptic aspect). \textit{The pressure to perfect, to optimize, to finish the work.}
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Forest Mood (d6 per scene)}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title=Forest Mood Table]
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Hazel's Favor} -- Paths open willingly. $+1$ die to navigation rolls this scene.
\item \textbf{Alder's Hunger} -- A claim is made. Someone in the scene must offer a memory or service to proceed.
\item \textbf{Empire Wakes} -- A patrol of imperial shades marches through. Avoid or leave something behind (1 Fatigue or a secret).
\item \textbf{Beast-kin Moot} -- Animals gather and watch. They will not attack unless provoked, but they remember faces.
\item \textbf{Sweet Wind} -- All navigation rolls suffer $-1$ die; landmarks shift subtly.
\item \textbf{Green Gate Breath} -- A threshold appears. Crossing it leads to a different part of the forest (or a different time). The air smells of jasmine and rot.
\end{enumerate}
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Star-Road Discovery (d6)}
When the party encounters a star-road shard or attempts to wake a segment, roll on this table for an additional feature:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Singing Stones} -- The flagstones hum a tune. Anyone who sings along gains $+1$ die on the next roll.
\item \textbf{Echo of an Emperor} -- A ghostly figure appears and asks a riddle. A correct answer grants a \textbf{City-Shard}.
\item \textbf{Fractured Timeline} -- The road shows a glimpse of the future (1d6 scenes ahead). The GM must answer one yes/no question truthfully.
\item \textbf{Ward-Trap} -- The segment is booby-trapped. Trigger a \textbf{Ward-Trap} complication (see Clubs).
\item \textbf{Shardwright's Cache} -- Hidden nearby is a \textbf{Shardwright's Favor} (Diamond) or a rare component (e.g., amber shard).
\item \textbf{The Ninth Step} -- The sequence has a hidden ninth stone. Stepping on it teleports the party to a \textbf{Valeheart Spire} or a court audience. The air tastes of honey.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Beast-kin Traits (for NPCs or PC ancestry)}
Beast-kin are not a single species but a loose alliance of animal-aspected beings --- the descendants of the Genesis Vats. Use these traits for quick NPCs or optional player characters.
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title=Beast-kin Traits]
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Scent Memory:} $+1$ die to track a creature you have smelled before.
  \item \textbf{Parliament Oath:} Once per session, you may invoke the beast-kin moot to ask a local animal one simple question (the GM answers truthfully).
  \item \textbf{Claw or Fang:} Unarmed strikes deal Harm 1 and count as Light weapons.
  \item \textbf{Vat Legacy:} You have a fragment of a forgotten memory --- a laboratory, a screaming face, a promise of perfection. Once per session, you may roll Spirit + Lore (DV 3) to recall a clue about a ruin or a monster's weakness. On a failure, the memory causes you 1 Fatigue.
  \item \textbf{Optional:} Choose a lineage:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textit{Stag:} $+1$ die to resist being moved or knocked prone.
    \item \textit{Lynx:} $+1$ die to stealth in forest terrain.
    \item \textit{Badger:} You may burrow through loose earth at half speed.
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Genesis Vats (The Clockwork Monad):} Deep beneath the Amber Ziggurat lie the empire's failed laboratories. Here, the Lethai ancients combined elf and beast, hoping to create the perfect guardian --- to match the Astroegro's engines of war. The vats still hum, and the beast-kin who survived do not speak of what they saw. The Shardwrights say the Monad's pressure is still optimizing the old designs, and that one day the vats will open again. When they do, the soft place will breathe deeper.
  \item \textbf{The Parliament of Claw and Fur (The Savage Heart):} The beast-kin are not savages. They hold a moot once every season, deep in a clearing that no star-road touches. Their laws are carved in scent and scar, and their judgments are final. The Savage Heart speaks through their eldest, but it does not command---it suggests. Outsiders who attend the moot must bring a true name of a foe and leave it on the speaking stone. Those who lie are given fur and four legs, and are hunted at the next moon. The beast-kin do not speak of the vats. The Savage Heart remembers.
  \item \textbf{Saint Oren of the Unfinished Star (The Ninth):} A Lethai-ar astronomer who calculated the exact angle at which the empire's star-roads would collapse. He was erased from the imperial records, but his ghost still walks the Ivory Observatory, tracing equations in starlight. Those who find him may ask one question about the forest's hidden geometry. The answer is always a single number, and that number binds you. He was trying to close the door. He failed.
  \item \textbf{The Gray Pilgrims (The Traveler):} A wandering order of Lethai-ar who maintain the old way-cords. They wear gray cloaks woven from mist and speak only in riddles. It is said that if you give a Gray Pilgrim a lock of your hair, they will knot it into your way-cord, and you will never be lost in the Valewood again---but you will also never find a place you truly wish to leave. They know the location of the sealed doors. They will not speak of it.
  \item \textbf{The Daughters of the Cord (The Bitter Root):} A renegade Sister hides among the moss-scribes of the Rooted Amphitheater, trying to untie her own cord from the Hazel Queen's design. She claims the queen's hospitality is a cage, and that the forest's memory is a trap. The Hazel Queen has not corrected her; she only smiles, and the roots grow tighter each night. The Sister whispers that the queen came through the door. No one knows if she means the door to the vats or the other door.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Valewood scene, the ninth leaf to fall from a tree will land pointing to a hidden threshold. The wise catch it---or pretend they didn't see. Those who follow the leaf's direction find a place that was not there a moment ago, and something old waits inside. The threshold may lead to a sealed laboratory, a fae court, or a clearing where the air smells of jasmine. It is always warm.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Valewood Superstitions (burn for $+1$ die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Roots:} Crumble a crust at the base of a standing stone. Ignore the first \emph{Sweet Wind} penalty. [SUPERSTITION]
  \item \textbf{Knot the Cord:} Tie a single knot in a way-cord. Cancel \emph{Oath-Magnet} or take $-1$ die on your next promise (your choice). [SUPERSTITION]
  \item \textbf{Name to the Cistern:} Whisper a dead enemy's name into the Moon-Cistern. Gain $+1$ Effect when evading fae this scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Alder King. [SUPERSTITION]
  \item \textbf{Honey to the Threshold:} Leave a drop of honey on a doorframe that should not exist. The door will not open for you, but it will not open behind you either. [SUPERSTITION]
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In the Valewood, power flows through ancient magic, forgotten oaths, and the delicate balance between guest and host. The Lethai-ar maintain influence through their knowledge of paths and ruins, while the fae courts wield power through hospitality and geas. Beast-kin serve as guardians of the deeper mysteries, and the echoes of the empire still patrol their eternal rounds, enforcing laws written in starlight and stone.

But beneath it all, the sealed doors wait. The fae courts are not the masters of the forest --- they are its children, born from the soft place that pressed through when the empire's experiments tore the fabric. The Lethai-thora who survived burned their records and sealed their guilt in vaults of amber and iron. The beast-kin carry the memory in their bones. The monsters are the ones who remember too clearly.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in the Valewood revolves around hospitality, knowledge, and the careful navigation of ancient laws. Rewards often take the form of charms, keys, or safe passage that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize the hidden horror:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, mirrors, cords) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided --- and that may carry a whiff of the vats.
\item Let rival courts issue conflicting protections, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more --- and which sealed door they are willing to approach.
\item Use the ruins, groves, and star-roads as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of ancient customs determines success --- and where a misstep may lead to a place that was sealed for a reason.
\item Introduce the \textbf{Genesis Vats Aftermath} as a recurring tension. The deeper the party goes, the more they risk waking something that should sleep.
\end{itemize}
In the Valewood, your word is your world, and your world determines whether you walk as a guest or a ghost. But the ghosts are not the only things that walk. Something came through the door. It is still here.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{The Lethai-al (Wood Elves)}
Beneath the courtly titles and the star-road records, the true wood elves endure. They call themselves the \textbf{Lethai-al}---those who remain when the tower falls. They do not walk the high roads; they mend them from below, speaking to roots instead of stars. Their elders are oaks, their children are saplings, and their grievances outlast stone.

Where the Lethai-thora sought perfection and the courts seek dominion, the Lethai-al seek only \textit{reciprocity}. They keep no scrolls and sign no contracts, yet nothing is given without expectation. A meal shared is a shield-pledge. A path shown is a future claimed. To them, the forest's question is not spoken but lived: \textit{What was taken, and what will be returned?} Outsiders rarely recognize the weight of these moments until the trees lean in.

The Lethai-al do not attend the parliaments of beast-kin or the audiences of Hazel. When they must speak with outsiders, they do so through intermediaries---moss-scribes, owl-sisters, or the rare gray pilgrim---so that the words are never truly theirs, and the obligation, if any, remains unrooted. They remember the sealed doors better than the archivists, for they were the ones who grew the thorns over them.
