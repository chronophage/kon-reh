\section{Mistlands --- ``Bells, Salt, and Breath''}
\label{chap:mistlands}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Legate of Mists (Elite):}  
  ``The bells here do more than mark time -- they weave the very fabric of reality, keeping the mist at bay and the dead in their proper places. Each chime is a thread in the pattern that holds the world together.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Salt-Monk (Commoner):}  
  ``Salt don't just preserve the fish -- it holds back the things that shouldn't walk. Every grain blessed is a prayer, and every prayer keeps the mist from remembering your name.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The Mistlands lie north of the Aelerian mountains, where the Aberderrin Sea laps against reed‑fens and drowned shores. To the west, the Direwood breathes; to the east, the salt tides of the Aberderrin. It is a Protectorate of the Aeler, but their iron passes only hold the southern roads---the land itself belongs to fog and bells. Every levee hums with ward‑notes, every ferry takes salt as tithe, and every shadow is measured against the Direwood's endless breath. The people here live by the weight of salt, the echo of bells, and the hope that their names will not be spoken by the mists.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The constant, distant chime of bells---never quite in sync. The taste of salt on your lips even inland. The way everyone holds their breath when crossing a bridge.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never answer a voice that calls your name from the fog. The mist collects names, and once it has yours, the ferryman will always know where to find you.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Bells and Breath}

Here, breath is currency and silence a sin. Every exhalation is counted by the ward‑bells, and every stolen inhalation feeds something that should not be. The Aeler Protectorate maintains order with ledgers and patrols, but the real law is older: salt lines drawn at dusk, iron nails hammered into threshold posts, and the unspoken knowledge that the ninth bell must never ring. The Mistlands do not forgive forgetfulness. The mist has a long memory, and it shares its grudges freely.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A Bell‑Line levee veiled in fog, where bell‑ropes sway though no wind stirs, and each ring tastes like a heartbeat. A Ferryman demands a name as toll. When the party hesitates, a Bell‑Warden steps forward. ``The Ninth Bell rings tonight. Will you answer---or flee?''

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:mistlands-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Reed-Fen Causey} --- Chalked ward‑runes on every milestone; safe path through the mist.
  \hfill \textit{Step only on the chalked stones. The mud between has no bottom.}
\item \textbf{Bell-Line Levee} --- Iron posts, eight bells per span; one bracket empty, one note forbidden.
  \hfill \textit{The empty bracket is for the bell that never rings. The rope is still there.}
\item \textbf{Ghost-Ferry Slip} --- Rope crosses black water; fares paid in salt and whispered names.
  \hfill \textit{The ferryman never blinks. Some say he traded his eyelids for the right to collect.}
\item \textbf{Pall Watch-Tower} --- Piles above mire; mirrors face west to the Direwood's breath.
  \hfill \textit{The mirrors are silvered on both sides. The wardens watch their own reflections as much as the trees.}
\item \textbf{Mist-Chapel} --- Stilted planks; votive lanterns gutter under damp rafters.
  \hfill \textit{The icon has no face. Worshippers say it lost its features to the fog, one by one.}
\item \textbf{Dead-Cut Canal} --- Old imperial ditch; patrolled by eel boats and law.
  \hfill \textit{The eels have eyes like men. Fishermen throw back any that blink.}
\item \textbf{Drowned Copse} --- Root‑tangles clutch tide like old hands; bones in the bark.
  \hfill \textit{The trees were planted on a mass grave. They grew twisted, hungry.}
\item \textbf{Fogmill Ridge} --- Wind vanes vanish into cloud; gears hiss like prayers to fog.
  \hfill \textit{The miller grinds salt, not grain. His customers pay in breath, not coin.}
\item \textbf{Salt-Pan Terraces} --- Patched with peat; ward‑salt dries under nets, watched by monks.
  \hfill \textit{The monks chant without ceasing. If they stop, the salt spoils---and so does the warding.}
\item[J] \textbf{Fort-Stair} --- Stacked timber and stone; flags swallowed by mist, guards by routine.
  \hfill \textit{The flags are changed daily. The ones that vanish are never spoken of again.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Witchlight Bridge} --- Over black runnel; ninth plank ``counted'' but unseen.
  \hfill \textit{The missing plank is there---you just can't see it. Step where it should be, or don't step at all.}
\item[K] \textbf{High-Mist Pass} --- Through Aelerians; cairns ringed in iron nails, bells in the stones.
  \hfill \textit{Each cairn holds a bell that rang once at the death of a warden. They ring again when mist takes another.}
\item[A] \textbf{Weeping Gate} --- Western palisade facing Direwood; bells wired to bones of trees.
  \hfill \textit{The gate weeps sap that smells of iron and old grief. Wardens collect it in silver cups.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Warden/Ferryman/Aeler)} Bell‑warden whose ringbook decides which notes keep which ghosts; oath‑ferryman collecting fares in salt and names.

  \hfill \textit{She carved out her own tongue. The trees still speak through her---in a voice not her own.}

  \hfill \textit{Their horses have no shadows. Some say they traded them for fog-sight.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Legate of Mists} --- Aeler governor; equal parts mercy and decree, bells at their command.
  \hfill \textit{She carries a bell that has never been rung. It will ring once, at the end of her reign.}
\item[K] \textbf{Lord Warden} --- Local strong hand sworn to Legate's seal; law walks the fens.
  \hfill \textit{He lost an eye to a wight. He wears a bell in the socket.}
\item[A] \textbf{Mist-Seer} --- Bargains breath for answers; candles shorten when she speaks.
  \hfill \textit{She has no shadow. The mist took it as a down payment.}

\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}

  \hfill \textit{Your footfalls vanish behind you. The mist is following, not rising.}

  \hfill \textit{The lights flicker in a pattern---two short, two long. They're signaling someone.}
\item \textbf{Ward-Salt Short} --- Pans go green; bells ring, nothing answers from the other side.
  \hfill \textit{The salt is grey, not white. Someone cut it with grave dust.}
\item \textbf{Wrong Bell} --- Cracked note opens a door the Legate can't close; mist takes the gap.
  \hfill \textit{The bell's clapper is a finger bone. The original owner wants it back.}
\item \textbf{Linn Raid} --- Slips reed‑maze; hearths douse, horns travel on the fog.
  \hfill \textit{The raiders wear bells on their belts---stolen from the last village.}
\item \textbf{Direwood Moan} --- At dusk; Weeping Gate ropes go taut by themselves.
  \hfill \textit{The moan has words. You don't want to understand them.}
\item \textbf{Valewood Wind} --- Crosses Aberderrin; voices sweet, landmarks false.
  \hfill \textit{The wind smells of honey and rot. Do not follow it.}

  \hfill \textit{The wraith carries a lantern. The light is blue---the color of drowned breath.}

  \hfill \textit{The ground exhales. Your name rises from it like smoke.}

  \hfill \textit{The seals are stamped with a bell. The wax is mixed with bone ash.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Rite-Purge} --- Inspectors call local customs heresy; tempers at a boil.
  \hfill \textit{The inspectors carry blank ledgers. They fill them with names of the accused.}
\item[K] \textbf{General Alarm} --- Pall horns sound; every lantern becomes law, every shadow suspect.
  \hfill \textit{The horns are made from the tusks of a sea beast. The beast's ghost is angry.}
\item[A] \textbf{Tide-Mist} --- Rolls from Aberderrin; spirits ride the white and won't be named.
  \hfill \textit{The spirits have no faces---only the faces of the drowned.}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:mistlands-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (passes/seals/tokens) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{breath and boundary}---bells answering across water, salt that won't dry, voices returning late.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Breath-Toll:} Many claim the mist remembers every breath. Travelers hold breath crossing bridges, fearing the mist will count them wrong.
\item \textbf{Salt as Promise:} A handful of salt is more than warding---it is a vow. Spill it on plank or stone to swear safety; break that vow, and invite the Direwood's claim.
\item \textbf{The Ninth Bell:} Legends say a ninth bell once rang on the Line. The note is not in any ringbook; no warden speaks of it. Some nights, though, it rings---and gates open where none should be.
\item \textbf{Payden's Port:} The only true city in the Mistlands, huddled on the western coast of the Aberderrin. Its wharves are built from salvaged longship timber, and its law is split between the Aeler Legate's seal and the old bell‑wardens' traditions. Grain ships leave from its harbour each autumn, carrying the Protectorate's tithe east---and returning with iron, engineers, and the occasional missionary.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Saint Kael of the Iron Bell (The Sealed Gate):} The bell‑wardens venerate a legendary warden who hammered a ninth bell into the Weeping Gate to seal a Direwood breach. The bell cracked, but the breach closed. His chisel is kept at Payden's Port; it is said to quench the mist for an hour if struck against an iron post. The Aeler Protectorate has tried to confiscate it twice; both attempts ended with the confiscators lost in fog.
  \item \textbf{Saint Anja of the White Pans (Oath of Mercy and Grace):} A salt‑monk who died blessing the pans during a tide‑mist. Her body never decayed, and her chapel now serves as a hospice for fog‑sick sailors. The Temple of Light sends offerings of fresh water each spring; the Everflame considers her a heretic. Pilgrims who pray at her shrine may ask one question of a drowned ancestor---but the answer is always a single word, and that word is a debt.
  \item \textbf{The Grey Hospice of the Unnamed:} Sister Darya runs a hospice near Payden's Port. She asks no payment, but she records every patient's name in a ledger that never dries. The Aeler clerks suspect she is erasing debt, not healing wounds. She has been investigated three times; each time, the investigator vanished into the fog and returned with no memory of why they came.
  \item \textbf{The Bell‑Ringer's Covenant (The Keeper of the Silent Seal):} Notary Voss is the Protectorate's appointed judge, charged with imposing Aeler law on local customs. He carries a water‑clock to measure the shifting of legal moods, but the mist ignores it. His rulings are always delivered in triplicate, and the bell‑wardens obey them—slowly. Those who lie before him find that their next passage through the Ghost‑Ferry Slip is denied; the ferryman refuses to name them.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Mistlands scene, the ninth person to speak in a room draws the mist's attention. The wise hold their tongues until someone else breaks the count. Those who speak ninth are said to see a pale figure standing at the foot of the bed that night---waiting to collect a breath.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Mistlands Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Fog:} Toss a crust into the mist before crossing a bridge. Ignore the first \emph{Ground-Mist} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Hold the Ninth Breath:} Cross a threshold while holding your breath. Cancel \emph{Witchlight Count} or take --1 die on your next action (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Salt:} Whisper a dead enemy's name into a salt pan. Gain +1 Effect against undead for the scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Legate.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In the Mistlands, power flows through ritual, warding, and the careful balance between the living and the dead. The Aeler Protectorate maintains nominal control through military presence and legal frameworks, but true authority lies with those who understand the bells, the salt, and the breath of the land itself. Local wardens, salt‑monks, and bell‑keepers hold sway over daily life, while the Legate of the Mists mediates between imperial law and ancient custom.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in the Mistlands revolves around protection---from mist, from spirits, from the encroaching forest. Rewards often take the form of tokens, seals, or safe passage that can be leveraged into greater security. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (salt, bells, lanterns) that can be challenged, depleted, or voided.
\item Let rival factions issue conflicting protections, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the levees, ferries, and watchtowers as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of the old ways determines success.
\end{itemize}
In the Mistlands, your safety is your standing, and your standing depends on who remembers your name.
\end{tcolorbox}
