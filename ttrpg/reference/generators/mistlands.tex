\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme & Atmosphere}]
The Mistlands lie north of the Aelerian mountains, where the Aberderrin Sea laps against reed‚ąífens and drowned shores. To the west, the Direwood breathes; to the east, the salt tides of the Aberderrin. It is a Protectorate of the Aeler, but their iron passes only hold the southern roads ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí the land itself belongs to fog and bells. Every levee hums with ward‚ąínotes, every ferry takes salt as tithe, and every shadow is measured against the Direwood's endless breath. The people here live by the weight of salt, the echo of bells, and the hope that their names will not be spoken by the mists.

The Aeler Protectorate maintains order with patrols, garrisons, and breath‚ąíbonds, but the real law is older: salt lines drawn at dusk, iron nails hammered into threshold posts, and the unspoken knowledge that the ninth bell must never ring. The Mistlands do not forgive forgetfulness. The mist has a long memory, and it shares its grudges freely.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The constant, distant chime of bells ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí never quite in sync. The taste of salt on your lips even inland. The way everyone holds their breath when crossing a bridge. The silence when a bell fails to ring.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never answer a voice that calls your name from the fog. The mist collects names, and once it has yours, the ferryman will always know where to find you.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Bells and Breath}

Here, breath is currency and silence a sin. Every exhalation is counted by the ward‚ąíbells, and every stolen inhalation feeds something that should not be. The Aeler Protectorate maintains order with patrols and garrisons, but the real law is older: salt lines drawn at dusk, iron nails hammered into threshold posts, and the unspoken knowledge that the ninth bell must never ring. The Mistlands do not forgive forgetfulness. The mist has a long memory, and it shares its grudges freely.

The tension between the Aeler governor (Legate Halvar) and the local bell‚ąíwardens is constant. The Aeler want standardized bell‚ąícodes, the salt‚ąítithe weighed true, and villages who keep the old pacts. The wardens want the old ways: counting in eights, never the ninth, and the right to refuse a name to the fog. Payden's Port, the only true city, is a battleground of writs and lanterns. Grain ships leave each autumn, carrying the Protectorate's due east and returning with iron, engineers, and the occasional missionary ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí who rarely stay.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage & Power}]
In the Mistlands, power flows through ritual, warding, and the careful balance between the living and the dead. The Aeler Protectorate maintains nominal control through military presence and legal frameworks, but true authority lies with those who understand the bells, the salt, and the breath of the land itself. Local wardens, salt‚ąímonks, and bell‚ąíkeepers hold sway over daily life, while the Legate of the Mists mediates between imperial law and ancient custom.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in the Mistlands revolves around protection ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí from mist, from spirits, from the encroaching forest. Rewards often take the form of tokens, seals, or safe passage that can be turned into greater shelter. To emphasize the folk‚ąíhorror feel:
\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem Tie rewards to visible symbols (salt, bells, lanterns) that can be challenged, depleted, or voided by the mist itself.
‚ĄĻtem Let rival factions (Protectorate vs. bell‚ąíwardens vs. Direwood refugees) issue conflicting protections, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
‚ĄĻtem Use the levees, ferries, and watchtowers as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of the old ways (counting breaths, naming spirits, offering salt) determines success.
\end{itemize}
In the Mistlands, your safety is your standing, and your standing depends on who remembers your name.
\end{tcolorbox}
