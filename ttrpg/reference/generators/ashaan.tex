\section{Ashaan --- ``The Veiled Throne''}
\label{chap:ashaan}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{The Veil is not a choice. It is survival. Speak in thirds – two options and a hidden third. The ninth word is always a knife.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Veiled Sister (Elite):}  
  ``The Veil is not a choice. It is survival. You will show your eyes and nothing more. Speak in thirds—offer two options and a hidden third. The ninth word is always a knife.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Breath-less Agent (Commoner):}  
  ``They say Ikasha sleeps. I say she is patient. The Sisters have ruled for generations; they believe the Veil is eternal. But the Veil is silk. Silk frays. And I am learning to bite through — one whisper at a time.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Grimdark spirit-horror, bureaucratic terror, whispered resistance.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A root cellar beneath a spice merchant's shop, lit by a smuggled candle. A Breath-less agent presses a folded paper into your hand: ``My brother argued with an Esoiti about a misweighed sack of cardamom. His name was erased from the rolls by morning. Help me find him — or help me remember his name.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Ashaan is a nation that died long ago. The Veiled Sisters keep its corpse walking through spirit-binding, terror, and the endless consumption of their own people. The capital, Galanina, is a city of black marble and brass cages. The Red Desert, which now covers half the country, was once fertile land; the Sisters' magic leeched the life from it. There is no recovery, only a slower dying. The Ashaani people are not inherently evil, but most are complicit—they must be to survive. A few resist in secret, forming cells of the Breath-less. The Sisters hunt them with spirit-hounds and slave-catchers.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of incense and rust. The way every doorway has a veil of brass chains. The silence when a Veiled Sister passes — even the spirits hold their breath.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never name the dead. The Sisters bind spirits to names. Speak a dead name aloud, and you may find yourself bound alongside them.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Weight of Damnation}

Power here is measured in spirits bound, names erased, and the silence of witnesses. The Veiled Sisters rule through terror, the Esoiti control every ledger and gate, and the Black Hand hunts without uniform. The Breath-less whisper in cellars, learning to read ledgers without leaving marks. Ashaan is not a place of open rebellion. It is a place of whispered warnings, vanished neighbours, and ledgers that rewrite themselves.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:ashaan-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 → 4, 6–10 → 6, J/Q/K → 8, A → 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (names, lamps, seals) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{veil-omens}—brass chains that rattle without wind, ledgers that open to the wrong page, a whisper that sounds like your own voice.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:ashaan-mechanics}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Veil:} The Sisters' control is maintained through spirit-binding and terror. The Veil is not just a political structure—it is a magical membrane that separates the living from the dead. When the Veil fails, spirits walk. Mechanically, the GM may spend 3 SB to trigger a \emph{Veil Failure} scene: all spirits become visible, bound spirits may break free, and social rolls against the Sisters gain +1 Position.
\item \textbf{Ikasha's Cult as Active Resistance:} She Who Sleeps is not a patron of open rebellion. Her followers meet in root cellars, learn to read ledgers without leaving marks, and whisper secrets under the cover of a cough. Her Rites—\textit{Touch the Umbral Veil}, \textit{Secret Keeper's Burden}—become tools of survival. A character who whispers Ikasha's name before sleeping gains +1 die to resist fear for the next day, but marks 1 \textbf{Exposure} (the Black Hand may notice).
\item \textbf{The Breath-less:} A secret network of escaped slaves, forbidden mages, and whisperers of Ikasha's name. They fight to tear down the system from within. Their symbol is a broken brass chain. A character who aids the Breath-less may earn a \textbf{Breath-less Cipher} (see Diamonds) and gain a contact, but each favour called in advances a \textbf{Black Hand Interest} clock.
\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Unbound Chain} – Ikasha. *She Who Sleeps; her dream is freedom.*
    \item \textbf{The Gilded Scale} – Mykkiel. *The Sisters' twisted version of divine law.*
    \item \textbf{The Drowned Prince} – Khemesh. *Pressure from the deep; the Red Desert was once his seabed.*
    \item \textbf{The Silvered Tongue} – Malachai. *Curses sold as blessings, whispered in the slave markets.*
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Kalrantha Amulet (Dhahara Connection):} The Red Desert was once fertile land, destroyed by an Ashaani prince who stole the Kalrantha amulet. The Sisters want the amulet to bind the demon again—or to use it for themselves. The Breath-less want to destroy it.
  \item \textbf{The Sidhi of the Brass Coast (Way of Silk):} The Sidhi are coastal cousins of the Ashaani who fled slavery generations ago. They maintain lighthouse courts and freedman's seals. They remember every raid. A Sidhi captain may be a Breath-less contact.
  \item \textbf{The Veiled Sisters and the Covenant (Mykkiel):} Mykkiel, the Judge of Galanina, is a direct antagonist to the Veiled Sisters. His followers are the secret courts of the Breath-less, trying the Sisters in absentia. A Covenant judge may offer sanctuary—or demand testimony.
  \item \textbf{The Hollow and the Ninth Name (Eastern Realms):} In Ashaan, the Hollow is the space left by an erased name. The Veiled Sisters feed the Hollow with forgotten debts. The Breath-less know that to restore a name is to weaken the Veil.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
Among the Ashaani, power flows through fear, spirit-binding, and the erasure of names. The Veiled Sisters hold ultimate authority, but the Esoiti control the ledgers, the Black Hand enforces silence, and the Breath-less whisper in cellars. A name can be a weapon or a shield.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Ashaani society is hidden and treacherous. Patronage often takes the form of true names, spirit-lamps, or erased ledger pages—each carrying the weight of damnation. To emphasise this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to secrets (names, whispers, visions) that can be betrayed, stolen, or used against the giver.
\item Let rival factions (Sisters vs. Esoiti vs. Breath-less) offer conflicting paths, forcing players to choose whose debt to incur.
\item Use the Brass Gate, the Ledger Hall, and the root cellars as arenas for social contests, where truth is a weapon and silence is a shield.
\item Track \textbf{Black Hand Interest} as a campaign clock. Each time the party aids the Breath-less or escapes a Sister, tick the clock. When it fills, a Black Hand squad arrives with a warrant for their erasure.
\end{itemize}
In Ashaani society, your name is your bond—and your bond can be erased.
\end{tcolorbox}
