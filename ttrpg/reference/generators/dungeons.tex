\section{Zakov --- ``Salt \& Serpent''}
\label{chap:zakov}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}

\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{The tide keeps a better tally than any scribe. The reef remembers every secret.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Salt Prince Lev (Elite):}  
  ``The tide here keeps better tallies than any scribe, and the reefs remember every secret whispered above their depths. Power flows like the currents --- those who understand them rule the waves. The Astroegro learned that. They are at the bottom now.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Dock-Rat (Commoner):}  
  ``The sewers and the sea both carry what folks want to forget, but they also remember everything. Best keep your business above water and your enemies guessing which dock you'll use next.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Lovecraftian dread meets pirate swagger; eldritch tide-horror, maritime noir, pressure as a living force.

\noindent\textbf{Starting Location:} A smuggler's gate built into the seawall, its iron mouth crusted with barnacles. The gatekeeper is a one-eyed woman who accepts payment in stories. Behind her, the Dregs Quarter stretches on stilts into the harbor, and the Salt Prince's flagship, the \textit{Red Wake}, rides at anchor off the Iron Pier. ``The levy rises tonight,'' she says, spitting into the water. ``Every ship pays, or every ship burns. You look like the burning type. Good. The Prince needs a reminder that the tide turns for everyone. And keep your eyes off the water at low tide. The Reaping doesn't like being watched.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Zakov is the pirate-city of the Dolmis coast, built on the drowned bones of an \textbf{Astroegro} port. Old stone ruins lie only fathoms down; at low tide their roofs break the surface like dead leviathans. The city's streets are piers, its courts are taverns, and its rulers are whoever the Syndicates fear most this season. The Salt Prince claims a throne of driftwood, but real power ebbs and flows with the tide --- and the tide remembers everything.

Beneath it all, the \textbf{Reaping}: a crystalline corruption seeping from a sealed vault. It does not kill; it \textit{harvests}. Dreams curdle into obsession; generosity rots into expectation. The Salt Prince keeps the source sealed beneath his palace, but the seal is cracking, and the crystal grows. Zakov sits at maritime crossroads: north to the Valewood, south to Theona, east to Aelinnel and Amedell. Its lifeblood is the smuggling lanes between Thepyrgos and Kahfagian patrols.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of brine and tar. The constant slap of water against stone. The way every tavern has a door that leads to a different dock. The faint harmonic hum from the deep, like a bell that never stops ringing.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never take a coin that tastes of brine. The Serpent's Mark is not a blessing --- it is a weight that drowns. And never look into a still pool at low tide. The Reaping looks back.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Using the Zakov Deck}
\label{sec:zakov-deck}

Draw until you have all four suits: \textbf{Spade} = place, \textbf{Heart} = actor, \textbf{Club} = pressure, \textbf{Diamond} = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).

\textbf{Diamonds} are codified outcomes (passes, contracts, goods) that change position rather than call for a roll.

If any \textbf{Ace} appears, echo \textbf{salt and serpent} motifs --- tides that remember, reefs that shift, the deep that listens, crystal that grows in the dark.

\subsection{Zakov Mechanics}
\label{sec:zakov-mechanics}

\subsubsection{Core Clocks}

\paragraph{Tide Clock [4]}
Scenes run on a four-segment cycle: \textbf{Low} $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Flood} $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Slack} $\rightarrow$ \textbf{Ebb}. Each advance shifts Position:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Low tide:} Hidden wrecks and bolt-holes are revealed. Stealth and infiltration gain +1 Effect.
  \item \textbf{Flood tide:} Streets drown; doors seal. Social and travel rolls suffer --1 die unless you have a boat.
  \item \textbf{Slack:} Negotiation and trade are at their safest. +1 die to social rolls.
  \item \textbf{Ebb:} Secrets are exposed; betrayals surface. The GM may bank 1 SB.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Reaping's Corrosion [8]}
Tracks the slow corruption of the party's souls by the crystalline entity beneath Zakov.

\textbf{Ticks when:}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item A character accepts a bargain from the Salt Prince or a Syndicate factor.
  \item A character uses a \textbf{Serpent's Mark} (Diamond A).
  \item A character fails a Resolve test while in Zakov.
  \item The party lingers in the Sunken Quarter or the Serpent's Spine for more than one scene.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{At 4 segments:} The character gains the \textit{Crystalline Whisper} condition (--1 die to resist fear; hears the Reaping's voice in quiet moments). Their shadow begins to shimmer faintly.

\textbf{At 8 segments:} The character becomes \textbf{Harvested}. Their shadow detaches and walks into the sea, and they owe the Reaping a name (a true name of someone they love, or their own). The GM may spend 2 SB to introduce a \textbf{Collector} (a crystalline spirit shaped like a drowned sailor) to demand payment.

\textbf{To clear the clock:} Offer the Reaping something it values more than a soul: a memory of a crime no one witnessed, a true name from the Astroegro era, or a shard of its own prison (requiring a heist into the Salt Prince's vault). Alternatively, perform a deep-dive ritual at the Serpent's Spine, cutting a growth from the reef and replacing it with a lock of hair from a willing volunteer.

\paragraph{Syndicate Weight (0--3)}
Every deal in Zakov leaves a mark. Track a single \textbf{Weight} value. At 3, the Syndicate calls it in: a betrayal, a levy, or an unwanted escort. Clear 1 Weight by paying a Diamond or sacrificing a useful contact.

\paragraph{Whispers Deep}
When characters openly boast, bargain, or betray, roll \textbf{1d6}: 1--3, whispers spread (the GM gains +1 SB this scene); 4--6, whispers favor the PCs (suspicion lowers by one step until the tide turns).

  \item \textbf{Patron Omen:} Divine or arcane forces manifest --- omen appears, blessing lost, curse manifests.

  \item \textbf{Syndicate Demand (3 SB):} Protection money increases; rival factions make conflicting demands; a contract is voided mid-deal.

\subsection{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\label{sec:zakov-lore}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{The Bitter Root:} Daughters of the Cord run a hospice in the Sunken Quarter. They ask no payment --- but they cut threads. A renegade Sister hides among the eel-cults, trying to untie her cord from the Salt Prince's tally. She claims the Reaping is the Cord's original sin. Her cord is made of crystal.
  \item \textbf{The Threshold Compendium:} The Serpent's Spine is a Class II Anchor site --- the bound spirit of a drowned admiral. Saikou Ira would trade his silver mirror for a single reef chart. The mirror shows the Reaping as a crystalline spider. He has not slept since. His reflection is missing.
  \item \textbf{The Salt-Bitten Angel (Malachai):} A silver coin that never spends circulates among the smugglers. Take it, and your cargo always passes inspection --- but the coin will taste of brine every time you touch it, and the Angel will ask for a favor before the next full moon. The message is always a single word: ``Soon.'' The coin grows warmer.
  \item \textbf{The Reckoner (Maelstraeus):} The Crimson Docks keep a tally signed in salt water; those who borrow from the Reckoner find that their interest compounds with every tide. A captain who defaults may wake to find their ship's name struck from all records, as if it had never sailed. The Reckoner's ink is crystalline. It writes itself. It also writes the names of the dead.
  \item \textbf{The Grey Wanderer's Grimoire:} Zakov psions are called ``Tide-Readers.'' They taste the future in the salt. The Pirate Queen employs two --- and has never trusted a forecast that didn't cost blood. The Reaping's presence makes their predictions unreliable. They taste crystal instead of salt. They have stopped predicting. They only listen.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Zakov scene, the ninth ship to enter the harbor in a tide will be claimed by the reef. The wise wait for the tenth --- or pay a name to the Kraken's Tongue. The reef is not a reef. It is a crystalline mouth. It has been feeding for centuries. It is still hungry.
\end{itemize}

% GM GUIDANCE
%-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Zakov, power flows through the control of smuggling routes, the management of syndicate weights, and the ability to navigate the complex web of pirate politics. The Salt Prince maintains nominal authority, but real power lies with those who control the tides, the reefs, and the flow of information through the city's taverns and black markets. The true authorities are those who can provide safe passage, protect cargoes, and mediate between competing syndicates.

\textbf{The Reaping changes everything.}  
Whoever controls the crystalline shard --- or understands its nature --- holds a weapon that could unmake the city or save it. The Salt Prince keeps the Reaping's location secret, but the Syndicates whisper that he is no longer its master. The Reaping has begun to act on its own. It reaches through the tide, through the dreams of the drowned, through the taste of brine in a smuggler's coin. Zakov is not ruled by the Prince. It is ruled by a hunger that has been patient for centuries. The Astroegro sealed it. The first Salt Prince bargained with it. Now the seal cracks, and the crystal grows.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Zakov revolves around smuggling rights, safe harbors, and the ability to provide protection from both legal and illegal authorities. Rewards often take the form of tokens, charters, and safe passage that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize the city's fluid power and the creeping horror of the Reaping:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, manifests, keys) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided --- and that may carry a trace of crystal.
  \item Let rival syndicates issue conflicting protections, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more --- and whose weights are safer.
  \item Use the docks, taverns, and smuggling routes as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of custom and connections determines success --- and where a character's shadow may betray them.
  \item Track \textbf{Reaping's Corrosion} as a campaign clock. Its advance is the city's slow death. The players can delay it, redirect it, or even embrace it --- but they cannot ignore it. The Reaping is patient. It has been waiting since the Astroegro fell.
\end{itemize}
In Zakov, your reputation is your wealth, and your wealth determines whether you rule the waves or feed the fishes. But beneath the waves, the Reaping feeds on reputation too. And it is very, very hungry.
\end{tcolorbox}
