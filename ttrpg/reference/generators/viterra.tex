\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme & Atmosphere}]
Viterra is a kingdom learning to stand alone after throwing off the Utaran yoke. Once a proud realm before the legions came, it never forgave the empire. Its hedge‚ąílaws and parish courts preserved a stubborn identity that parchment and wax could never erase. Now, under the young Warrior Queen Veralyn Everblood‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíward of the Fenwood dukes, veteran of the sack of Lence, and still Duchess of Aberiel in Vhasia‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíViterra is trying to remember what it means to rule itself.

But the crown is not secure. Veralyn took the throne only after the sudden death of her brother, King Baldwyn Brightstide, whose two‚ąíyear reign ended in a swift, mysterious illness. Rumors whisper it was Fenwood's doing, to clear the path for \emph{their} claim. His death was a wound the kingdom has not healed. The northern earls, descendants of old hill‚ąíkings, watched for a misstep that proved she was still Fenwood's instrument. Some of them secretly funded the fanatics who killed her father, Wyllmot, five years earlier‚ÄĒa betrayal she has just finished rooting out with steel and statute. The eastern barons, who expected greater spoils from the Lence campaign because she was the Fenwood duke's ward, now grumble that she has rewarded loyalty to the crown, not to the sword. Every spring, the Queen's justiciars ride west with warrants. Every autumn, Vhasian routiers raid the grain convoys. The border is not a line on a map. It is an argument. And the Queen intends to win it‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíeven if she must become the thing she swore to leave behind.

The realm's true sinews lie in writs, charters, and ancient rights that bind lord to tenant, crown to county, church to common. Yet there is no single, unified law. The Queen's law competes with the earls' law, the barons' law, the custom of the fens, the Ubral clan law of the high passes, and the Vhasian claims that bleed across the Belworth. A deed valid in Valora may be worthless in the Marches. A crown‚ąícourt judgment may be overturned by a hedge‚ąímoot at dusk. The very question \emph{which law is law} is settled not in courts, but in the mud of the Belworth, where writs are brandished like blades and blood soaks into the parchment.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of damp parchment laced with old iron. The clatter of writ‚ąírunners' boots on blood‚ąísoaked cobblestones. \textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never cross the Belworth without a charter recognized on both banks. Viterran law and Vhasian claim fight every crossing, and the river takes the losers‚ąí‚ąí‚ąísometimes still clutching a forged writ to their chest.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Queen's Long Shadow}

Here, the law is a weapon and the past is a ledger still open. Veralyn's throne is a fortress she built with borrowed steel and her own blood. The northern earls have been humbled‚ÄĒtheir levies disbanded, their charters rewritten‚ÄĒbut they watch from their keeps, waiting for a misstep. The eastern barons, who expected land and tariffs from the Lence campaign, now find themselves audited and outmaneuvered. They whisper that she fights like a Vhasian, rules like a Fenwood, and trusts no one. They are correct. Across the Belworth, the Vhasian claimants still style themselves the rightful kings of Vhasia, and they remember that Veralyn once rode with Braedyn Fenwood. The Queen's peace is a contract written in ink and witnessed by steel. Every spring, she rides west with her justiciars. Every autumn, the routiers test the borders. The hedge‚ąílaw holds‚ÄĒbarely.

\paragraph{Hedge‚ąíLaw Momentum}

\paragraph{River‚ąíRight Window}

\paragraph{Legal Duel Protocol}
When legal disputes cannot be resolved through argument, parties may challenge each other to a duel. The winner's position becomes legally binding. \paragraph{Theona Contest}
The Warrior Queen's authority over the Three Greens is nominal. Any attempt to enforce Viterran law in Theona triggers an \textbf{Isle Refusal} complication; local taboos may twist the law into knots.

\paragraph{The Succession's Shadow}
Any legal dispute touching on inheritance, legitimacy, or royal authority is automatically one step more volatile. The GM may spend 1 SB to introduce a witness, document, or rumor related to Baldwyn Brightstide's death or the northern earls' secret funding of King Wyllmot's murderers.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage & Power}]
In Viterra, power flows through the careful application of law‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíbut beneath every charter runs the threat of steel. The Warrior Queen maintains authority through a combination of military might and legal precision, but the throne is fragile. The old duchies and local officials wield influence through knowledge of hedge‚ąílaw and ancient custom. The true power lies not in the documents alone, but in the will to enforce them when the parchment fails.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Viterra revolves around legal documents, local rights, and the ability to command respect from institutions still finding their post‚ąíimperial identity. Rewards often take the form of writs, charters, and official positions that can be leveraged into greater influence‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíbut they can be challenged, stolen, or voided, especially if they bear the dead king's seal.
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  ‚ĄĻtem Tie rewards to visible symbols (seals, writs, charters) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
  ‚ĄĻtem Let rival duchies and Vhasian claimants issue conflicting authorities, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
  ‚ĄĻtem Use the courts, parishes, and muster grounds as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success.
  ‚ĄĻtem When legal conflicts escalate beyond words, transition naturally to physical confrontation with appropriate stakes.
  ‚ĄĻtem Introduce the \textbf{Succession's Shadow} as a recurring complication: old loyalties, forged documents, the ghost of Baldwyn Brightstide, and the secret funding of King Wyllmot's murder can upend any legal certainty.
\end{itemize}
In Viterra, your papers are your power. And your power determines whether you rule or are ruled. But when words fail, steel speaks‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíand the law says both are valid. The Queen's Peace is a contract. She has signed it in blood. Will you?
\end{tcolorbox}
