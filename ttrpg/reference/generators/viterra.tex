\section{Viterra --- ``The Hedge-Law Realm''}
\label{chap:viterra}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Warrior Queen (Elite):}  
  ``Every hedge here marks not just a boundary but a principle – the careful balance between order and freedom, justice and mercy. Our law grows like the hedges themselves, tended with precision and patience.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Fen Reeve (Commoner):}  
  ``The dikes hold back more than water – they hold back chaos. Every rod measured, every stone placed, keeps the flood from washing away not just our fields but our very way of life. And across the Belworth? The Vhasians still claim our hedges are theirs. Let them try.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Viterra breathes with the careful rhythm of precedent and petition. Once a proud kingdom before becoming a Utaran province, it is now reborn under a new Warrior Queen who rules by legal precision as much as military might. The realm's true sinews lie not in steel but in writs, charters, and the ancient rights that bind lord to tenant, crown to county, and church to common. Every hedge‑row, every parish stone, every ferry crossing carries the weight of custom older than memory.

But Viterra is also a kingdom of restless ambition. Northward, the Ubral highlands reject any crown but their own moots. Southward, the City of a Thousand Stairs—Thepyrgos—watches with scholarly scorn and synod edicts. Westward, across the Belworth River, lie the fractured cantons of Vhasia, where Viterran dukes hold ancient claims to estates and abbeys—claims that have fueled decades of raiding, ransom, and legal warfare. And in the Dolmis, the isles of Theona pledge nominal fealty but keep their own taboos, a constant tug between crown law and green custom. Viterra is not a nation at peace. It is a realm that wages law as war, and war as law.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of damp parchment and fresh ink. The clatter of writ‑runners' boots on cobblestones. The way every noble's hand rests on a seal ring—and every commoner's hand rests on a hatchet.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never cross the Belworth without a charter recognized on both banks. Viterran law and Vhasian claim fight every crossing, and the river takes the losers.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Hedge, Crown, and Claim}

Here, law is a blade. The Warrior Queen rules from Valora, her throne a bench of precedents. The old Fenwood dukes—whose ancestors sacked Lence and left the Vhasian Sun‑court fractured—still hold vast estates in the northern marches, and their claims across the Belworth keep the border smoking like a low fire. Every spring, the Queen's justiciars ride west with warrants and escorts, demanding homage from villages that have paid Vhasian tithes for a generation. Every autumn, Vhasian routiers raid the grain convoys, and the legal duels that follow are fought with steel and citation alike.

North, the Ubral clans watch from their cairns, ready to rekindle feuds if a Viterran levy crosses the passes. South, Thepyrgos sends clerks and spies, testing the edges of Viterran jurisdiction with every disputed toll. And in Theona, the Three Greens mumble old curses while Viterran bailiffs try to count their cattle—and always miscount by one.

\textbf{Starting Location:} The Hall of Dawning tilt‑yard at first light. Dawn‑knights drill in armor polished like mirrors. Beside them, advocates rehearse arguments with the same intensity. A Queen's Justiciar slides a parchment across an oaken rail. “Three charters, one road across the Belworth. The Vhasian claimant says the bridge is theirs. The local reeve says it's crown land. And the abbey says it's a pilgrimage, not a toll. Carry the Queen's writ, break the Quiet Tolls, and make them agree—by clause or by cut.”

\paragraph*{(Court/Law/Border)} Court gossip in Valora's law quarter; writ‑runners queue at dawn; border sheriffs argue over a hay‑cart's toll rights; dueling advocates prepare legal challenges like generals planning campaigns.

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:viterra-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Fen Causeway} --- Stile with toll‑rod and eel carts queueing; each reeve claims right to collect under a different charter. Dueling advocates argue cases on makeshift platforms.
  \hfill \textit{The causeway sinks an inch each year. The lawyers argue over whose fault that is.}
\item \textbf{Hedgerow Green} --- In the Dales; bows strung under apple trees where justices of the peace hold court beneath makeshift arbors. Court duels permitted for matters of honor.
  \hfill \textit{The apple tree is called ``the Hanging Judge.'' Its fruit tastes of iron.}
\item \textbf{Beacon Hill} --- Above the Highlands; fire‑grate still warm from last winter's levy call, signal tower with semaphore arms for legal dispatches and military alerts.
  \hfill \textit{The hill has three beacons. One for Ubral raids, one for Vhasian incursions, one for paperwork.}
\item \textbf{Belworth Stairs} --- Ferry ledgers gone damp with river‑mist; three manors claim toll rights, writs in three different hands. Legal challenges fought in boats while waiting.
  \hfill \textit{The stairs were repaired last year. The Vhasian side uses a different measurement.}
\item \textbf{Bloom Quarry} --- Old iron‑bloom turned drill yard; hammer echoes carry, forge‑reeve holds tally of crown contracts. Military contracts awarded through legal bidding wars.
  \hfill \textit{The forge‑reeve has never lost a contract. His opponents have a habit of finding better jobs elsewhere.}
\item \textbf{Parish Maze} --- Stone maze where three maps disagree; hedge‑priest's vote decides who pays tithe and who holds freehold. Legal disputes settled by navigating the maze correctly.
  \hfill \textit{The maze changes at night. The hedge‑priest says that's God's will.}
\item \textbf{Fairport Works} --- Tideworks at the Dolmis mouth; customs writs in three languages flutter, inspectors with magnifying glasses examine every manifest. Maritime law enforcement includes boarding actions.
  \hfill \textit{One language is Utaran, one is Linn, one is for the smugglers.}
\item \textbf{Law Quarter} --- Valora archives, oath‑rooms, Hall of Dawning where precedent is birthed in ink and sealed with wax. Adjacent training grounds for legal advocates.
  \hfill \textit{The archives have a locked wing. The key is a legal argument no one has won.}
\item \textbf{Counting Fields} --- Tarlington beside muster ground; wagons load, ledgers mis‑sum, auditors with quill‑pens and suspicious eyes. Military mustering follows strict legal procedures with armed oversight.
  \hfill \textit{The auditors carry swords. They say it's for counting cattle. No one asks further.}
\item[J] \textbf{Dike Crown} --- Ring of turf and timber; brotherhood bells warn of flood—and spies who move like water through the fen. Legal sanctuary for those fleeing court judgments.
  \hfill \textit{The sanctuary lasts for one tide. After that, the reeves can come—if they can find you.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Progress Camp} --- Queen's canvas palisade, banners stiff, whispering old courtiers plot advancement through legal maneuver and sanctioned duels.
  \hfill \textit{The camp moves every week. The lawsuits follow at a measured pace.}
\item[K] \textbf{Tilt-Yard} --- Hall of Dawning at first light; Dawn‑knights drill clean, loyalties in contracts signed with careful pen. Legal challenges often end in trial by combat.
  \hfill \textit{The sand is raked between duels. Sometimes the rake turns up old blood—or old oaths.}
\item[A] \textbf{Highway Stone} --- Queen's milestone above the Dolmis road; three sheriffs post seals over hers, each claiming different jurisdiction. Territory disputes resolved through armed arbitration.
  \hfill \textit{The stone has been broken and reglued seven times. Each crack is a forgotten compromise.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Reeve/Guild/Knight/Legal Advocate)} Fen reeve with tally‑rod speaking for dike guilds; river‑carter syndic moving grain faster than rumor; justices of the peace settling disputes with careful pen; master advocates who duel with words and swords.

\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Fen Reeve} --- Tally‑rod; speaks for dike guilds, remembers every flood‑year's debts and the charters that govern them. Authorized to call local levies for legal disputes.
  \hfill \textit{His tally‑rod has notches from three floods. The next notch will be from a Vhasian raid.}
\item \textbf{River Syndic} --- Moves grain faster than rumor; knows which writs matter and which customs officials can be… persuaded. Maintains armed merchant guards.
  \hfill \textit{His ledger has a watermark—a crown on one side, a sunburst on the other. He shows the right side to the right audience.}
\item \textbf{Parish Surveyor} --- Three maps, one opinion; hedge‑walks settle more disputes than courts, measures boundaries with careful chain. Expert witness in property disputes.
  \hfill \textit{His chain is exactly one rod long. The rod changes length depending on who is paying.}
\item \textbf{Dawn Quartermaster} --- Ledgers sharper than tongue; logistics before lances, counts every farthing spent in the Queen's name. Legal authority over military supplies.
  \hfill \textit{He once tracked a missing farthing to a Vhasian counting house. The farthing was returned—with interest.}
\item \textbf{Levy Serjeant} --- Dales bow calm, cider breath, memory keen for every loophole in the mustering laws. Commands armed civilians in legal enforcement actions.
  \hfill \textit{His bowstring is made from a cancelled writ. He says it shoots truer now.}
\item \textbf{Cleric-Pair} --- Light preacher vs. Everflame canon lawyer; debates decide tithe law, one for the spirit, one for the letter. Religious law carries weight in secular courts.
  \hfill \textit{They share a room at the inn. They have not spoken outside court in seven years.}
\item \textbf{Fairport Shipwright} --- Dolmis cousins; skiff carries more secrets than cargo, knows which manifests to forge and which to honor. Expert in maritime law and naval combat.
  \hfill \textit{Her skiff has no name. The customs clerks have given it three.}
\item \textbf{Fenwood Comptroller} --- Conjures wagons with a signature, silence with a seal, counts the Queen's coin and tracks every debt owed to the crown. Legal authority over taxation.
  \hfill \textit{His quill is a dagger. He has used it as both.}
\item \textbf{Queen's Justiciar} --- Law on the road, polite as a noose; bearing three kings' judgments and a purse full of pardons for the right price. Authorized to conduct trial by combat.
  \hfill \textit{She has never lost a legal duel. Her opponents have a tendency to fall down stairs.}
\item[J] \textbf{Border Routier} --- Reads ledgers as ambushes; serves two crowns, collects from both, knows which border posts take bribes in what coin. Expert in frontier law enforcement.
  \hfill \textit{His loyalty is to the highest bidder. The Queen and the Vhasian claimant are currently in a bidding war.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Warrior Queen} --- Crown on helm, sword at side, patient sums behind her gaze; rules by law's sharp edge and knows every precedent that supports her claim. Final arbiter in legal duels.
  \hfill \textit{Her crown has no jewels—only seals. Each seal is a conquered claim.}
\item[K] \textbf{Crown Council} --- Fenwood dukes, guild envoys, abbots; weighing grain against glory, charters against coin, precedent against practicality. Legal battles fought through proxy advocates.
  \hfill \textit{The council has a standing rule: no weapons within the chamber. The advocates have found seventeen loopholes.}
\item[A] \textbf{Master Advocate Halric} --- Silver‑tongued legal warrior who settles disputes through verbal duels that can end in physical combat. Keeper of legal combat traditions.
  \hfill \textit{He has argued cases in Vhasian, Thepyrgosian, and Ubral courts—and won all three using their own precedents.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:viterra-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Dike Breach} --- Black‑rain; the wrong reeve claims credit while wagons drown, insurance claims and liability writs fly like autumn leaves. Legal liability may require armed enforcement.
  \hfill \textit{The water is brown with peat and lies. It carries old deeds downstream.}
\item \textbf{Feast Clash} --- Light vs. Everflame processions collide; shutters slam, writs fly, justices of the peace called to settle which holy day takes precedence. Honor disputes may require legal duels.
  \hfill \textit{The processions take different routes now. The clash was not an accident.}
\item \textbf{Quiet Tolls} --- Sprout on the Queen's Highway; escorts sniff traps, sheriffs argue rights, customs officials demand duties no one knew existed. Territorial disputes resolved through armed arbitration.
  \hfill \textit{The toll posts have fresh paint. The paint is from the keep of a Vhasian claimant.}
\item \textbf{Audit Freeze} --- Counting‑house freezes cargo; Queen's seal vs. old writs, auditors with suspicious eyes and quill‑pens sharp as daggers. Legal challenges may escalate to physical confrontation.
  \hfill \textit{The auditors work by candlelight. The shadows on the walls look like gallows.}
\item \textbf{Border Snarl} --- Three courts arrest the same hay‑cart; whose hay is it? Which lord's charter takes precedence? Bribes flow like wine. Frontier justice often involves armed standoffs.
  \hfill \textit{The hay is from a field that was Vhasian last spring. Now it's Viterran. The field hasn't moved.}
\item \textbf{Isle Refusal} --- Theona withholds levy; the quays choke on rumor, ships idle while lawyers debate maritime law and ancient treaties. Naval blockades become legal enforcement actions.
  \hfill \textit{Theona's envoy carries a hawthorn branch. The Queen's justiciars will not touch it.}
\item \textbf{Delta Spat} --- Fairport vs. Marcott customs; barges stack three deep, merchants pick sides, inspectors with magnifying glasses examine every manifest. Trade disputes fought with merchant guards.
  \hfill \textit{The magnifying glasses reveal truth and also start fires. Both are useful.}
\item \textbf{Routier Arrears} --- A free‑company flips unless someone pays; your name on a ledger, a debt owed to men who know the price of blood. Legal debts enforced through hired swords.
  \hfill \textit{The routiers have a list. Your name is on it—or will be by sunset.}
\item \textbf{Salt Pinch} --- Import spikes, bakers barricade; the Queen's promise tastes like thin gruel, merchants hoard salt like gold, riots brew in back alleys. Economic warfare becomes physical conflict.
  \hfill \textit{The salt is grey. It was cut with ashes from a burned Vhasian customs post.}
\item[J] \textbf{Dawn Recall} --- Escort pulled to a flood‑struck parish; the writ argues with the river, justices demand inquiry into who neglected the dikes. Military enforcement of legal judgments.
  \hfill \textit{The escorts are back in a week. They bring new writs—and fewer men.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Aberielist Intrigue} --- Royalist seals in wrong hands; the Queen's patience thins, conspirators plot in counting houses, whispers of rebellion. Treason charges carry death penalty in legal duels.
  \hfill \textit{The seals are from a dead duke. The duke's ghost is said to approve.}
\item[K] \textbf{Levy Call-Up} --- Dalesmen mustered; your wagons seized “for the realm,” conscription writs signed with careful pen, families left to tend fields alone. Legal authority backed by armed force.
  \hfill \textit{The conscription writs have a clause: “void if Belworth freezes.” The river is still warm.}
\item[A] \textbf{Legal Duel Challenge} --- Master Advocate Halric issues a formal challenge; refuse and lose legal standing, accept and risk life and fortune in court‑yard combat. The ultimate legal weapon.
  \hfill \textit{The challenge is written on both sides of the parchment. There is no blank space to refuse.}
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Bribe/Seal/Writ/Duel Warrant)} Ferry priority at Belworth crossing; dike‑work allotment with brotherhood labor granted; market license sealed with proper wax; sanctioned duel to settle property dispute.

\section*{Diamonds --- Rewards/Leverage}
\label{sec:viterra-rewards}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Ferry Priority} --- Belworth crossing (once); a horn answers, though perhaps the wrong ferry; customs officials look the other way for the right coin. Legal right of way backed by armed escort.
  \hfill \textit{The priority token is a writ signed by both banks. The ink on one side is newer.}
\item \textbf{Dike Allotment} --- Brotherhood labor granted if three clerks are convinced and proper bribes distributed among the right hands. Work performed under legal contract with military oversight.
  \hfill \textit{The allotment includes a clause: ``as tides permit.'' The reeve decides what the tides permit.}
\item \textbf{Market License} --- Valora's square; a guild nod still needed, but the right seal on the right parchment opens many doors. Legal protection from merchant guild enforcement.
  \hfill \textit{The license is written on vellum from a Vhasian abbey. The irony is noted.}
\item \textbf{Dawn Escort} --- Four lances at dawn; loyalty waxes and wanes with pay, but their presence keeps lesser men from troubling you. Legal authority to enforce your writs.
  \hfill \textit{The lances are from the Fenwood armory. The Fenwood dukes have their own plans.}
\item \textbf{River Charter} --- Guaranteed haul on a grain artery—weather permitting and proper duties paid to the right officials. Legal right protected by river patrol.
  \hfill \textit{The charter is stamped with a seal that looks like a crown—or a sunburst. No one can agree.}
\item \textbf{Map Correction} --- A hedge moves, borders shift, lawsuits bloom; a new survey can make or break a man's fortune overnight. Legal survey backed by armed surveyors.
  \hfill \textit{The surveyors carry chains and swords. The swords are for when the chains disagree.}
\item \textbf{Customs Seal} --- Fairport for Dolmis cargo; honored today, disputed tomorrow, but tonight it clears the way for what needs moving. Legal immunity from customs enforcement.
  \hfill \textit{The seal is a wax crab. It pinches when pressed.}
\item \textbf{Thing Ruling} --- Sharp local bite, but appeals run swifter than oaths; justice swift but not always just, depending on which purse feeds the judge. Court decisions enforceable by local levy.
  \hfill \textit{The ruling is carved on an oar. Break the oar, and the ruling breaks with it.}
\item \textbf{Salt Allotment} --- Winter depot; the Queen's writ opens doors, old hands still count sacks, but the right connections ensure the best salt reaches the right tables. Legal monopoly backed by military force.
  \hfill \textit{The salt is from a pan worked by Ubral exiles. They still count in clan measure.}
\item[J] \textbf{Wardship} --- A minor fen‑keep; men‑at‑arms “for now,” a tutor has plans, but the right guardian can be found for the right price. Legal guardianship with military authority.
  \hfill \textit{The ward is a child. The child has a seal ring. The ring is older than the kingdom.}
\item[Q] \textbf{Queen's Audience} --- Private; her questions strike like thrusts, but her favors can elevate a man from nothing to something overnight. Royal decree carries immediate enforcement power.
  \hfill \textit{The audience lasts one hour. The paperwork lasts a generation.}
\item[K] \textbf{Ducal Warrant} --- Fenwood to enforce customs; old power bows, new power watches, but the parchment carries weight in any court. Legal authority backed by noble house military.
  \hfill \textit{The warrant is signed in blood—or ink that looks like blood. The difference matters at trial.}
\item[A] \textbf{Duel Sanction} --- Right to challenge in legal combat; the Queen's seal makes it official, Dawn‑knights serve as seconds, the winner takes all legally. Ultimate legal enforcement mechanism.
  \hfill \textit{The sanction is a coin. Heads you duel, tails your opponent chooses the weapon.}
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:viterra-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (writs/charters/priority) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{hedge‑law} motif—boundaries blur, charters contradict, precedent splinters, and the careful web of legal authority shows its cracks. Legal warfare escalates to physical conflict.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Hedge-Law Momentum:} When a valid \emph{writ, charter, or warrant} is presented in the proper parish, step \textbf{Position} up one category on social or logistical actions in that locale for the scene. The first contradiction starts a \textbf{Border-Lace} [4] clock; advance each time competing papers are invoked.
\item \textbf{Beacon \& Bell Chain:} If two adjacent \emph{beacons} or \emph{brotherhood bells} are under your influence, convert one \textbf{Club} draw into a travel delay for \emph{others} while the party proceeds with improved \textbf{Effect} for movement and logistics. The signal draws scrutiny; the GM may bank 1 SB tied to local authority.
\item \textbf{River-Right Window:} At a ferry, tidework, or customs stair, play any \textbf{Diamond} tied to crossings or seals to open a short \emph{window of lawful passage}: one convoy passes uninspected, or one cargo clears at priority. The window closes immediately if a storm, audit, or levy card is revealed.
\item \textbf{Legal Duel Protocol:} When legal disputes cannot be resolved through argument, parties may challenge each other to a duel. The winner's position becomes legally binding. Requires proper sanction and seconds.
\item \textbf{Vhasian Claims:} Any Viterran noble with land across the Belworth may invoke a \emph{Ducal Warrant} to attempt to collect rents or enforce judgments in Vhasia. Doing so starts a \textbf{Border Snarl} clock, as the Vhasian claimants are unlikely to cooperate.
\item \textbf{Theona Contest:} The Warrior Queen's authority over the Three Greens is nominal at best. Any attempt to enforce Viterran law in Theona triggers a \textbf{Isle Refusal} complication, and the local taboos may twist the law into knots.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Viterra, power flows through the careful application of law, the management of ancient rights, and the delicate balance between imperial legacy and reclaimed sovereignty. The Warrior Queen maintains authority through a combination of military might and legal precision, while the old duchies and local officials wield influence through their knowledge of traditional customs and hedge‑law. The true power lies with those who can navigate this complex legal landscape and maintain the appearance of legitimate authority while building genuine support.

But remember—in Viterra, law is warfare. Every document is a weapon, every courtroom a battlefield, and every advocate a soldier. The pen cuts as deep as any blade, and legal victory can be as decisive as military conquest.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Viterra revolves around legal documents, local rights, and the ability to command respect from institutions still finding their post‑imperial identity. Rewards often take the form of writs, charters, and official positions that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (seals, writs, charters) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival duchies and Vhasian claimants issue conflicting authorities, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the courts, parishes, and muster grounds as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success.
\item When legal conflicts escalate beyond words, transition naturally to physical confrontation with appropriate stakes.
\end{itemize}
In Viterra, your papers are your power, and your power determines whether you rule or are ruled. But when words fail, steel speaks—and the law says both are valid.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Legal Combat System (Summary)}
\label{sec:viterra-combat}
When legal disputes cannot be resolved through negotiation, parties may engage in formal legal duels. These combine verbal sparring with physical combat:

\textbf{Phase 1: Pleadings}
\begin{itemize}
\item Each advocate presents their case (Wits + Sway vs. DV 3)
\item Success grants +1 die to the next phase
\item Failure creates 1 SB that the opponent can spend
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Phase 2: Cross-Examination}
\begin{itemize}
\item Attack opponent's arguments (Wits + Insight vs. DV 3)
\item Success inflicts Fatigue 1 on the opponent
\item Critical success forces opponent to answer truthfully
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Phase 3: Trial by Combat (if unresolved)}
\begin{itemize}
\item Physical combat with legal stakes
\item Winner's position becomes legally binding
\item Loser suffers legal consequences plus physical harm
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Boss Seeds (three‑phase foes)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Violet Clerk (Master of Counter‑Seals)} — \emph{Phases}: Produce Earlier Patent $\rightarrow$ Split the Writ $\rightarrow$ Seal Fast. \emph{Cracks}: a \textbf{Queen's Audience} overrules his seal; a \textbf{Duel Sanction} forces personal combat.
\item \textbf{The Thorn Captain (Queen's Quiet Hand)} — \emph{Phases}: Queenspeace Zone $\rightarrow$ Duel Warrant $\rightarrow$ Swap the Field. \emph{Cracks}: a \textbf{Map Correction} reveals a loophole; a \textbf{Dike Allotment} lets you claim the field as “flood zone.”
\item \textbf{The Saffron Abbess (Keeper of Bell‑Law)} — \emph{Phases}: Wrong Hour $\rightarrow$ Bless or Damn $\rightarrow$ Mixed Tribunal. \emph{Cracks}: a \textbf{Queen's Justiciar} invokes crown supremacy; a \textbf{Border Routier} bribes her bell‑ringer.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Legendary Sites \& Trials}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Belworth Stairs Ordeal} — Cross the river without a charter. On success, you prove that possession is nine‑tenths of the law. On failure, the river takes your name—and your cargo.
\item \textbf{Hall of Dawning Vigil} — Spend a night in the tilt‑yard. At dawn, you recall a forgotten legal precedent. Gain a one‑use \emph{Precedent Edge} (+1 die in any court). The ghost of a dead duelist may challenge you.
\item \textbf{Hedge‑Moot at Dusk} — Settle a boundary dispute by walking the hedge. Each successful navigation test clears 1 segment from a \textbf{Border Snarl} clock. A miss plants the thorn of a future feud.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Extensions (Plug-in)}
\label{sec:viterra-extensions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Legal Dial (Crown $\leftrightarrow$ Local Custom):} Track the party's stance on authority.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Crown High:} +1 Position when invoking royal writs, charters, or the Queen's name; first appeal to local custom starts one step worse.
    \item \emph{Local High:} +1 Effect on hedge‑law, parish rights, or ancient precedent; first royal petition starts one step worse.
    \item Center the dial by hosting a mixed moot or surrendering a contested seal to a neutral third party.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Courtroom Mini‑Game (fast trial):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Lay three stones: \textbf{Precedent}, \textbf{Equity}, \textbf{Force}.  
    \item Argue a point (roll Lore+Diplomacy vs. DV 3). On a hit, move one stone to your side. On a miss, the opposing advocate gains \emph{Citation Edge}.  
    \item Win two stones to secure a temporary \textbf{Court Ruling} (treat as a Diamond for one scene).  
    \item \emph{Halric's Interjection (2 SB):} flip \textbf{Precedent}, but start \textbf{Whispered Precedent} [2] with the losing side.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Cross‑Border Tensions (fast pressure):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Two opposed clocks: \emph{Queen's Peace} vs \emph{Border Fire} (size by highest rank in the seed).
    \item Each leg, choose \emph{Viterran Road} (safe, slow), \emph{Vhasian Claim} (fast, risky), or \emph{Ubral Pass} (hidden, costly).  
    \item Advantage: Vhasian Claim$\rightarrow$Viterran Road (shortcuts), Ubral Pass$\rightarrow$Vhasian Claim (concealment), Viterran Road$\rightarrow$Ubral Pass (supplies).  
    \item Spend a \textbf{Ducal Warrant} to shift one segment from \emph{Border Fire} to \emph{Queen's Peace}.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Start‑of‑Scene Rumors (1d6):}
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \emph{Wrong Wax:} a rival's seals softened; +1d to forgeries this scene.
    \item \emph{Empty Beacon:} tower unmanned; travel faster, scrutiny harsher.
    \item \emph{Dawn‑Lane Cleared:} first convoy action gains +1 Position.
    \item \emph{Clerk's Wedding:} filings delayed; first petition ignores \emph{Audit Freeze}.
    \item \emph{Fen High Water:} ferries slow; river‑charters count double value.
    \item \emph{Mask at Progress:} someone uses your name at court; +1d to social opens, +1 SB banked by GM.
  \end{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{Plug‑in SB Conversions (Viterra Flavor):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item 1 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Hedge Shift}: a boundary moves; increase DV by 1 for navigation or legal rolls this scene.
    \item 2 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Citation War}: increase DV by 1 unless you produce a precedent (spend a \Diamond{} or recall a past ruling).
    \item 3+ SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Seal Crack}: a writ loses half its value; swap one held \Diamond{} for a lesser right until re‑witnessed.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Reputation Echoes:}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Court‑Favored:} once/session, downgrade \textbf{Audit Freeze} to a minor delay; the next document you present is scrutinized (+1 DV).
    \item \emph{Fen‑Tested:} +1 Position when resolving dike or flood crises; legal arguments about water rights start one step worse.
    \item \emph{Double‑Sealed:} +1d with both royal and local courts in a truce scene; any betrayal earns \textbf{Border Snarl} from both sides.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Cross‑Region Hooks (quick integration):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Ubral (Stone \& Oath):} A \textbf{Ducal Warrant} acts as a \emph{Guest‑Token} once at a clan hearth, but starts a \textbf{Feud Rekindled} tick if you collect tolls twice in a tenday.
    \item \emph{Thepyrgos (Synod Law):} A \textbf{Queen's Audience} can suspend a synod interdict—but starts \emph{Whispered Precedent} in Thepyrgos.
    \item \emph{Vhasia (Fractured Sun):} A \textbf{Duel Sanction} is recognized on the Pont‑du‑Tithe—but a rival abbey can turn it into \emph{Parlement Divided} on a Club draw.
    \item \emph{Theona (No Ninth):} \textbf{Ferry Priority} overrides a local taboo once; if the Ninth custom is in play, the writ “forgets” one clause on use (GM banks 1 SB).
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
