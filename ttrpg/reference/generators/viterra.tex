\section{Viterra --- ``The Hedge-Law Realm''}
\label{chap:viterra}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}

\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Every hedge is a boundary. Every boundary is a claim. Every claim is a blade.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Queen Veralyn Everblood (Elite):}  
  ``I was Fenwood's ward when I was sixteen. I took Lence's walls for him. I made the Vhasian Sun-court run red. Now I sit on a throne built of precedents---and I keep a dagger under my pillow. The hedge that is not defended is only a suggestion. The law that is not enforced is only a memory. I will have both remembered. But sometimes I wonder which hand shakes more when I seal a charter: the queen's, or the enforcer's.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Fen Reeve Cuthbert (Commoner):}  
  ``The dikes hold back more than water---they hold back the scream of Lence still echoing in the reeds. Every rod measured, every stone placed, keeps the flood from washing away not just our fields but the memory of what we were before the Fenwoods came. Across the Belworth, the Vhasians still claim our hedges are theirs. Let them try. We have lawyers who quote old charters. We have swords that remember Lence. The Queen's peace is a contract; she signs it each morning with a blade in her hand. I just hope the ink holds---and that it's not mine.''
\end{quote}

\noindent\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Post-imperial consolidation, legal warfare as blood sport, border adventurism, fragile crown haunted by fratricide.

\noindent\textbf{Starting Location:} The Hall of Dawning tilt-yard at first light. Dawn-knights drill in armor polished like mirrors---each dent a story from last week's legal duel. Beside them, advocates rehearse arguments with the same intensity, palms slick on pommels. A Queen's Justiciar slides a parchment across an oaken rail, her seal ring catching the dawn. ``Three charters, one road across the Belworth. The Vhasian claimant says the bridge is theirs. The local reeve says it's crown land. The abbey says it's a pilgrimage, not a toll. Carry the Queen's writ, break the Quiet Tolls, and make them agree---by clause or by cut. The Earls are watching, and the Queen has only just secured the throne. Fail, and you'll give her enemies the excuse they need: that she is still Fenwood's bloody handmaiden, not Viterra's queen.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Viterra is a kingdom learning to stand alone after throwing off the Utaran yoke. Once a proud realm before the legions came, it never forgave the empire. Its hedge-laws and parish courts preserved a stubborn identity that parchment and wax could never erase. Now, under the young Warrior Queen Veralyn Everblood---ward of the Fenwood dukes, veteran of the sack of Lence, and still Duchess of Aberiel in Vhasia---Viterra is trying to remember what it means to rule itself.

But the crown is not secure. Veralyn took the throne only after the sudden death of her brother, King Baldwyn Brightstide, whose two-year reign ended in a swift, mysterious illness. Rumors whisper it was Fenwood's doing, to clear the path for \emph{their} claim. His death was a wound the kingdom has not healed. The northern earls, descendants of old hill-kings, watched for a misstep that proved she was still Fenwood's instrument. Some of them secretly funded the fanatics who killed her father, Wyllmot, five years earlier—a betrayal she has just finished rooting out with steel and statute. The eastern barons, who expected greater spoils from the Lence campaign because she was the Fenwood duke's ward, now grumble that she has rewarded loyalty to the crown, not to the sword. Every spring, the Queen's justiciars ride west with warrants. Every autumn, Vhasian routiers raid the grain convoys. The border is not a line on a map. It is an argument. And the Queen intends to win it---even if she must become the thing she swore to leave behind.

The realm's true sinews lie in writs, charters, and ancient rights that bind lord to tenant, crown to county, church to common. Yet there is no single, unified law. The Queen's law competes with the earls' law, the barons' law, the custom of the fens, the Ubral clan law of the high passes, and the Vhasian claims that bleed across the Belworth. A deed valid in Valora may be worthless in the Marches. A crown-court judgment may be overturned by a hedge-moot at dusk. The very question \emph{which law is law} is settled not in courts, but in the mud of the Belworth, where writs are brandished like blades and blood soaks into the parchment.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of damp parchment laced with old iron. The clatter of writ-runners' boots on blood-soaked cobblestones. The way every noble's hand rests on a seal ring worn thin from constant gripping---and every commoner's hand rests on a hatchet notched with Vhasian blood.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never cross the Belworth without a charter recognized on both banks. Viterran law and Vhasian claim fight every crossing, and the river takes the losers---sometimes still clutching a forged writ to their chest.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Queen's Long Shadow}

Here, the law is a weapon and the past is a ledger still open. Veralyn's throne is a fortress she built with borrowed steel and her own blood. The northern earls have been humbled—their levies disbanded, their charters rewritten—but they watch from their keeps, waiting for a misstep. The eastern barons, who expected land and tariffs from the Lence campaign, now find themselves audited and outmaneuvered. They whisper that she fights like a Vhasian, rules like a Fenwood, and trusts no one. They are correct. Across the Belworth, the Vhasian claimants still style themselves the rightful kings of Vhasia, and they remember that Veralyn once rode with Braedyn Fenwood. The Queen's peace is a contract written in ink and witnessed by steel. Every spring, she rides west with her justiciars. Every autumn, the routiers test the borders. The hedge-law holds—barely.

\subsection*{The Ninth Taboo Manifestation}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=red!60!black,title=The Ninth Taboo]
In any Viterran court, the ninth citation of precedent voids all previous citations. The wise stop at eight and settle out of court. Those who cite the ninth are said to hear faint laughter from the marble walls—and their cases are never heard again. Some say the laughter is Baldwyn Brightstide's, still judging from the shadow of the throne.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Using the Viterra Deck}
\label{sec:viterra-deck}

Draw until you have all four suits: \textbf{Spade} = place, \textbf{Heart} = actor, \textbf{Club} = pressure, \textbf{Diamond} = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Timer : 2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4 segments, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6 segments, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8 segments, A $\rightarrow$ 10 segments.

\textbf{Diamonds} are codified outcomes (writs, charters, priority) that change position rather than call for a roll.

If any \textbf{Ace} appears, echo \textbf{hedge-law} motifs---boundaries blur like old scars, charters contradict like guilty consciences, precedent splinters like shaky oaths, and the careful web of legal authority shows its living cracks. Legal warfare escalates to physical conflict not as failure, but as the only language left when words run dry.

\item[K] \textbf{Ducal Warrant} --- Fenwood to enforce customs. The parchment carries weight in any court---and old power bows before new. `[AUTHORITY]`
  \hfill \textit{The warrant is signed in blood---or ink that looks like blood. The difference matters at trial.}
\item[A] \textbf{Duel Sanction} --- Right to challenge in legal combat; the Queen's seal makes it official. The winner takes all legally; the loser learns the cost of doubting the Queen's peace. `[LAW]` `[COMBAT]`
  \hfill \textit{The sanction is a coin. Heads you duel, tails your opponent chooses the weapon.}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Viterra Mechanics}
\label{sec:viterra-mechanics}

\subsubsection{Core Timers}

\paragraph{Border-Lace [4]}
Tracks competing legal claims in a locale. Starts when contradictory papers are invoked. \textbf{Ticks when:}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item A second (or third) valid writ is presented for the same right (+1).
  \item A player steps up Position using Hedge-Law Momentum (+1).
  \item A Vhasian or local authority disputes a crown document (+1).
\end{itemize}
\textbf{At 4:} The dispute escalates immediately to a riot, duel, or jurisdictional void. All social rolls in the locale suffer +1 DV until a ruling is imposed by force or decree.

\paragraph{Succession's Shadow [6]}
Tracks the erosion of Queen Veralyn's legitimacy from the ghosts of Baldwyn Brightstide's death and the lingering resentment of the northern earls and eastern barons. \textbf{Ticks when:}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item A document bearing Baldwyn's seal surfaces (+1).
  \item A northern earl or eastern baron publicly defies the crown (+1).
  \item A legal duel is lost by the Queen's champion (+1).
  \item The GM spends 2 SB to introduce a witness, forgery, or rumor tied to the crisis (+1).
\end{itemize}
\textbf{At 3:} Viterran officials and allies suffer --1 die to social rolls (they doubt the throne's stability). \textbf{At 6:} A serious claimant emerges—perhaps a northern earl with a forged charter, or a Vhasian pretender promising to restore the old border. The Queen must win a legal duel or face a general levy against her authority.

\paragraph{Queen's Peace [Front]}
Once per session, if the party resolves a cross-border dispute in the Queen's favor without bloodshed, advance this front. If they escalate violence or validate a Vhasian claim, tick \textbf{Succession's Shadow} instead.

\subsubsection{Status \& Environment}

\paragraph{Hedge-Law Momentum}
When a valid \emph{writ, charter, or warrant} is presented in the proper parish with dry ink and unbroken seal, step \textbf{Position} up one category on social or logistical actions in that locale for the scene. The first contradiction starts the \textbf{Border-Lace} [4] timer.

\paragraph{Beacon \& Bell Chain}
If two adjacent \emph{beacons} or \emph{brotherhood bells} are under your influence and lit for the correct reason, convert one \textbf{Club} draw into a travel delay for \emph{others} while the party proceeds with improved \textbf{Effect} for movement and logistics. The signal draws scrutiny; the GM may bank 1 SB tied to local authority.

\paragraph{River-Right Window}
At a ferry, tidework, or customs stair, play any \textbf{Diamond} tied to crossings or seals to open a short \emph{window of lawful passage}: one convoy passes uninspected, or one cargo clears at priority. The window closes immediately if a storm, audit, or levy card is revealed.

\paragraph{Legal Duel Protocol}
When legal disputes cannot be resolved through argument, parties may challenge each other to a duel. The winner's position becomes legally binding. Requires proper sanction and seconds (see \textbf{Duel Sanction} Diamond). If either party lacks a sanction, the duel is treated as assault under crown law.

\paragraph{Vhasian Claims}
Any Viterran noble with land across the Belworth may invoke a \emph{Ducal Warrant} to attempt to collect rents or enforce judgments in Vhasia. Doing so starts a \textbf{Border Snarl} complication (or ticks \textbf{Border-Lace}), as Vhasian claimants are unlikely to cooperate. The Queen's own Vhasian title (Duchess of Aberiel) adds a layer of irony—and suspicion.

\paragraph{Theona Contest}
The Warrior Queen's authority over the Three Greens is nominal. Any attempt to enforce Viterran law in Theona triggers an \textbf{Isle Refusal} complication; local taboos may twist the law into knots.

\paragraph{The Succession's Shadow}
Any legal dispute touching on inheritance, legitimacy, or royal authority is automatically one step more volatile. The GM may spend 1 SB to introduce a witness, document, or rumor related to Baldwyn Brightstide's death or the northern earls' secret funding of King Wyllmot's murderers.

\subsubsection{Procedures}

\paragraph{Courtroom Mini-Game (Fast Trial)}
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item Lay three stones: \textbf{Precedent}, \textbf{Equity}, \textbf{Force}.
  \item Argue a point (roll Wits + Sway vs.\ DV 3). On a hit, move one stone to your side. On a miss, the opposing advocate gains \emph{Citation Edge}.
  \item Win two stones to secure a temporary \textbf{Court Ruling} (treat as a Diamond for one scene).
  \item \emph{Halric's Interjection} (2 SB): flip \textbf{Precedent}, but start \textbf{Whispered Precedent} [2] with the losing side.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Cross-Border Tensions (Fast Pressure)}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Two opposed timers: \emph{Queen's Peace} vs.\ \emph{Border Fire} (size by highest rank in the seed).
  \item Each leg, choose \emph{Viterran Road} (safe, slow), \emph{Vhasian Claim} (fast, risky), or \textbf{Ubral Pass} (hidden, costly).
  \item Advantage: Vhasian Claim $\rightarrow$ Viterran Road (shortcuts), Ubral Pass $\rightarrow$ Vhasian Claim (concealment), Viterran Road $\rightarrow$ Ubral Pass (supplies).
  \item Spend a \textbf{Ducal Warrant} to shift one segment from \emph{Border Fire} to \emph{Queen's Peace}.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Start-of-Scene Rumors}
Roll \textbf{1d6} at the start of a Viterran scene:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Wrong Wax:} A rival's seals softened; +1d to forgeries this scene.
  \item \textbf{Empty Beacon:} Tower unmanned; travel faster, scrutiny harsher.
  \item \textbf{Dawn-Lane Cleared:} First convoy action gains +1 Position.
  \item \textbf{Clerk's Wedding:} Filings delayed; first petition ignores \emph{Audit Freeze}.
  \item \textbf{Fen High Water:} Ferries slow; river-charters count double value today.
  \item \textbf{Mask at Progress:} Someone uses your name at court; +1d to social opens, +1 SB banked by GM.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Hedge:} Crumble a crust at a parish boundary stone. Ignore the first \emph{Border Snarl} penalty. `[SUPERSTITION]`
  \item \textbf{Knot the Writ:} Tie a single knot in a charter's ribbon. Cancel \emph{Audit Freeze} or take --1 die on your next legal argument (your choice). `[SUPERSTITION]`
  \item \textbf{Name to the River:} Whisper a dead claimant's name into the Belworth. Gain +1 Effect when arguing against their heir's claim this scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Queen's Justiciar. `[SUPERSTITION]`
  \item \textbf{The Brightstide Candle:} Light a candle in memory of King Baldwyn before a difficult legal proceeding. On a roll that includes a 1, you may reroll that die once. If you still fail, the GM banks 1 SB. `[SUPERSTITION]`
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Strain Budget (SB) Spends}

\paragraph{Minor (1 SB)}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Exposure:} Crown officials or rival advocates take notice.
  \item \textbf{Noise:} Writ-runners or levy guards are alerted.
  \item \textbf{Trace:} Trackers or customs inspectors mark your route.
  \item \textbf{Delay:} Lose a favorable court date or legal window.
  \item \textbf{Supply Strain:} Mark +1 segment on a relevant resource timer.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Moderate (2 SB)}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Alarm Raised:} The Queen's Justiciar or a fen reeve responds.
  \item \textbf{Position Lost:} Legal challenge or river closure costs you advantage.
  \item \textbf{Foe Appears:} A rival claimant or border routier arrives.
  \item \textbf{Gear Trouble:} A key item becomes Compromised or Neglected.
  \item \textbf{Lock/Barrier:} A simple obstacle now requires a test to overcome.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Serious (3 SB)}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Reinforcements:} Dawn-knights, levy forces, or Vhasian routiers arrive.
  \item \textbf{Key Gear Breaks:} A crucial tool or weapon becomes temporarily unusable.
  \item \textbf{Major Twist:} Writ voided, duel declared, or levy called.
  \item \textbf{Rail Tick:} Advance a relevant campaign/front timer by 1 segment.
  \item \textbf{Condition Applied:} Mark Fatigue 1, Harm 1, or an appropriate Condition.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Major (4+ SB)}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Trap Springs:} A prepared danger activates with full effect.
  \item \textbf{Authority Arrival:} The Warrior Queen, Crown Council, or Master Advocate Halric intervenes.
  \item \textbf{Scene Shift:} Dike breaks, river floods, or court erupts.
  \item \textbf{Patron Omen:} Divine or arcane forces manifest---an omen appears, a blessing is lost, or a curse manifests.
  \item \textbf{Narrative Pivot:} The story reframes your objectives unexpectedly.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Region-Specific SB Conversions}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Hedge Shift (1 SB):} A boundary stone shifts; increase DV by 1 for navigation or legal rolls this scene.
  \item \textbf{Citation War (2 SB):} Increase DV by 1 unless you produce a precedent (spend a \Diamond{} or recall a past ruling).
  \item \textbf{Seal Crack (3+ SB):} A writ loses half its value until re-witnessed.
  \item \textbf{Succession's Whisper (2 SB, Viterra only):} A document or rumor tied to Baldwyn Brightstide's death surfaces. The GM must answer one yes/no question about royal legitimacy or introduce a related complication. Earl Morcar's involvement may also be hinted.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Boss Seeds (Three-Phase Foes)}

\paragraph{The Violet Clerk (Master of Counter-Seals)}
\emph{Phases:} Produce Earlier Patent $\rightarrow$ Split the Writ $\rightarrow$ Seal Fast.\\
\emph{Cracks:} A \textbf{Queen's Audience} overrules his seal; a \textbf{Duel Sanction} forces personal combat; a \textbf{Map Correction} voids his boundary claim.

\paragraph{The Thorn Captain (Queen's Quiet Hand)}
\emph{Phases:} Queenspeace Zone $\rightarrow$ Duel Warrant $\rightarrow$ Swap the Field.\\
\emph{Cracks:} A \textbf{Map Correction} reveals a loophole; a \textbf{Dike Allotment} lets you claim the field as ``flood zone''; a \textbf{Border Routier} testifies to the Queen's favor.

\paragraph{The Saffron Abbess (Keeper of Bell-Law)}
\emph{Phases:} Wrong Hour $\rightarrow$ Bless or Damn $\rightarrow$ Mixed Tribunal.\\
\emph{Cracks:} A \textbf{Queen's Justiciar} invokes crown supremacy; a \textbf{Border Routier} bribes her bell-ringer; a \textbf{Thing Ruling} from Theona forces her to recuse.

\paragraph{The Brightstide Claimant (Pretender)}
\emph{Phases:} Produce the Lost Charter $\rightarrow$ Rally the Northern Earls $\rightarrow$ March on Valora.\\
\emph{Cracks:} Find proof the charter is a forgery; convince Earl Morcar to stand with the Queen (or expose his role in Wyllmot's death); survive a legal duel with the claimant's champion.

\subsubsection{Legendary Sites \& Trials}

\paragraph{Belworth Stairs Ordeal}
Cross the river without a charter. On success, you prove possession is nine-tenths of the law. On failure, the river takes your name and cargo. (Wits + Survival vs.\ DV 5.)

\paragraph{Hall of Dawning Vigil}
Spend a night in the tilt-yard. At dawn, recall a forgotten precedent; gain a one-use \emph{Precedent Edge} (+1 die in any court). The ghost of a dead duelist may challenge you to a legal dream-duel. (Spirit + Lore vs.\ DV 4.)

\paragraph{Hedge-Moot at Dusk}
Settle a boundary dispute by walking the hedge. Each successful navigation test (Wits + Survival) clears 1 segment from a \textbf{Border Snarl} timer. A miss plants the thorn of a future feud (advance \textbf{Border-Lace} by 1).

\subsection{Lore Echoes}
\label{sec:viterra-lore}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{The Hedge-Seal (The Dike Covenant):} Covenant judges in the eastern parishes adjudicate river tolls and inheritance. They carry iron scales and a copy of the \emph{Hedge-Law Tables}---each page stained by a different flood. A ruling is binding in both Viterra and the Vhasian marches, but any party who lies before them forfeits their right to appeal. The Warrior Queen has granted them a charter; the Fenwood dukes consider them spies.
  \item \textbf{Saint Cuthbert of the Fen (The Traveler):} A legendary reeve who walked the flooded dikes during the Great Storm, lighting every beacon by hand. His shrines are stiles and bridge posts; travellers who touch the iron ring and speak a destination find the road holds firm. The fen folk leave offerings of eels at his cairns.
  \item \textbf{The Cog-Cults of the Northern Hills (The Timer work Monad):} Secret fraternities of millers and surveyors near the Ubral border believe law is a machine that can be perfected. They meet in abandoned watermills, measuring boundaries with brass chains and reciting the \emph{Canons of Proportion}. The Everflame calls them heretics; the Queen's justiciars ignore them.
  \item \textbf{The Salt Sworn of the Eastern Coast (The Pale Shepherd):} Along the Dolmis shore, a syncretic cult tends to the drowned. They build cairns of salt stone and leave no offerings---only the names of the lost, carved into driftwood. Temple of Light missions sent to suppress them have been found adrift with no crew and a single name carved into the mast: \emph{Oblivion}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Cross-Region Hooks}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Ubral (Stone \& Oath):} A \textbf{Ducal Warrant} acts as a \emph{Guest-Token} once at a clan hearth. Starts a \textbf{Feud Rekindled} tick if you collect tolls twice in a tenday.
  \item \textbf{Thepyrgos (Synod Law):} A \textbf{Queen's Audience} can suspend a synod interdict. Starts \emph{Whispered Precedent} in Thepyrgos.
  \item \textbf{Vhasia (Fractured Sun):} A \textbf{Duel Sanction} is recognized on the Pont-du-Tithe. A rival abbey can turn it into \emph{Parlement Divided} on a Club draw. The Queen's own Vhasian title (Duchess of Aberiel) may be used as leverage—or as a weapon against her.
  \item \textbf{Theona (No Ninth):} \textbf{Ferry Priority} overrides a local taboo once. If the Ninth custom is in play, the writ ``forgets'' one clause on use (GM banks 1 SB).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Conspiracy Flourishes}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Hedge-Moots at Dusk:} Parish elders ``trim hedges'' while trading sealed whispers.
  \item \textbf{Cipher Roses:} Folded writs stamped with thorn-petals; one petal missing marks a false clause.
  \item \textbf{Mirror Hours:} Beacons flash lawful signals---reversed for those who know the counter-code.
  \item \textbf{Eel-Letters:} Oiled parchment smuggled in eel-baskets; ink runs in water, truth does not.
  \item \textbf{Quiet Steel, Quieter Quills:} Dawn-knights duel at first light; advocates settle three duels before breakfast with ink alone.
  \item \textbf{Progress Masks:} Courtiers wear legal devices as masque motifs (seal, chain, beacon) to signal faction at a glance.
  \item \textbf{Bell Penance:} A single off-hour peal grants amnesty to those who confess a clause before the twelfth echo.
  \item \textbf{The Brightstide Prayer:} Whispering the dead king's name before a legal proceeding is said to turn the judge's heart---or curse the speaker. Some say Earl Morcar still whispers it each night.
\end{itemize}

% GM GUIDANCE
%-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Viterra, power flows through the careful application of law---but beneath every charter runs the threat of steel. The Warrior Queen maintains authority through a combination of military might and legal precision, but the throne is fragile. The old duchies and local officials wield influence through knowledge of hedge-law and ancient custom. The true power lies not in the documents alone, but in the will to enforce them when the parchment fails.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Viterra revolves around legal documents, local rights, and the ability to command respect from institutions still finding their post-imperial identity. Rewards often take the form of writs, charters, and official positions that can be leveraged into greater influence---but they can be challenged, stolen, or voided, especially if they bear the dead king's seal.
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Tie rewards to visible symbols (seals, writs, charters) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
  \item Let rival duchies and Vhasian claimants issue conflicting authorities, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
  \item Use the courts, parishes, and muster grounds as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success.
  \item When legal conflicts escalate beyond words, transition naturally to physical confrontation with appropriate stakes.
  \item Introduce the \textbf{Succession's Shadow} as a recurring complication: old loyalties, forged documents, the ghost of Baldwyn Brightstide, and the secret funding of King Wyllmot's murder can upend any legal certainty.
\end{itemize}
In Viterra, your papers are your power. And your power determines whether you rule or are ruled. But when words fail, steel speaks---and the law says both are valid. The Queen's Peace is a contract. She has signed it in blood. Will you?
\end{tcolorbox}
