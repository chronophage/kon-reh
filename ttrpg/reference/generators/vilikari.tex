\section{Vilikari --- ``Laurels \& Longhouses''}
\label{chap:vilikari}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Two laws, one people. The oak remembers; the marble writes.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Federate King (Elite):}  
  ``We are the bridge between empire and wilderness, the marriage of law and custom. Our strength lies not in choosing one path but in walking both with equal surety.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Hearth-Mother (Commoner):}  
  ``The old ways and new laws both have their place at our table. Serve the right mead to the right guest, honor the proper customs, and you'll find we're better hosts than most kings.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Early Holy Roman Empire on the steppe — legal dualism, hybrid identity, frontier pragmatism.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A longhouse quarter tucked into an old forum. Laurel garlands hang from broken columns. Smoke from a dozen hearths curls under cracked marble arches. A Hearth‑Mother offers a cup of small beer and a piece of oatcake. ``The Foedus Stone cracks,'' she says. ``The steppe listens. Which law will you speak when the Ykrul envoy arrives — oak or marble?''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
The Vilikari are the agrarian peoples straddling the frontiers between Ecktoria and Kahfagia, tillers and treaty‑makers who balance survival between empire, steppe, and sea. In a three‑way war with the Ykrul and the Black Banners, they live by the cleverness of their diplomacy, the leverage of their markets, and the shifting weight of oaths. Their halls are longhouses raised over ruined forums; their laws carved on both oak and marble; their loyalties braided like the braids of their war‑maidens' hair.

Unlike the Ykrul, who read the geometry of the steppe, the Vilikari read the geometry of \emph{jurisdiction}. They are the bulk of the non‑elite population in Ecktoria — the legionaries' descendants who never went home — and their blood runs through the villages of Acasia, where imperial tax records still list them as ``citizens of no fixed province.'' A Vilikari blacksmith in Ecktoria might swear by the marble bench; his cousin two valleys over in Acasia still ties knots for the oak. This is not contradiction. It is survival.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of woodsmoke and old parchment. The way everyone speaks two languages at once — switching mid‑sentence. The longhouse roofs that use broken imperial tiles as shingles.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never invoke only one law. The \emph{mallus} (oak‑ring) and the \emph{basilica} (marble bench) must both be honored. Choose one, and the other will find you.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Laurels and Longhouses}

Here, the past is not a ruin — it is a landlord. The old Utaran forums still echo with the footsteps of legionaries, but now those columns hold longhouse roofs. The Vilikari do not choose between empire and tribe; they marry them, awkwardly, imperfectly, and with frequent arguments. Their kings wear both a laurel wreath and a wolf‑tail cloak. Their courts meet twice: once under the oak for blood‑price, once on the marble bench for statute. No verdict is final until both have spoken. In the marches, a man can be a barbarian in the morning and a citizen by noon — if he knows which door to knock on.

On the coast, Vilikari fishing villages double as smugglers' coves, running fugitives, forbidden goods, and whispered messages between the powers of the Amaranthine. Their boats are fast, their pilots know every reef and patrol route, and their loyalty is to the cargo's survival, not the flag it flies.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:vilikari-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2--5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6--10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).

\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:vilikari-mechanics}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Two‑Law Balance:} Whenever a \emph{jurisdictional conflict} arises (mallus vs. basilica, oak vs. marble), players may step Position up by citing the Foedus Stone or an older precedent — but every use adds a tick to a \textbf{Two‑Law} [4] clock. When it fills, one side demands exclusive authority. The other side sharpens its knives.
\item \textbf{Frontier Diplomacy:} If a \emph{Heart} card involves a foreign envoy, a federate, or a mixed‑blood courtier, treat the first \textbf{Diamond} played in the scene as doubled leverage. Once per session, a deal struck reverberates — advance or start a \textbf{Border Tension} front.
\item \textbf{March Markets:} At any \emph{Staple Market, Ford, or Bridge}, Diamonds tied to rights or escorts may be cashed as currency in lieu of coin, one‑for‑one. Doing so creates a \textbf{Ledger Debt} that the GM may call in later through factions or raiders. The debt is never written. It is remembered.
\item \textbf{The Vilikari Diaspora (Ecktoria \& Acasia):} Vilikari characters in Ecktoria gain +1 die on Streetwise checks (they know the back alleys and the bribes), but –1 die on social rolls with Old Blood patricians (who see them as upstarts). In Acasia, they gain +1 die on Survival checks (their ancestors were farmers here) but –1 die on social rolls with the Curse‑touched (who remember imperial tax collectors). A Vilikari character may start with one ``family ledger'' — a minor asset that tracks a single unpaid favour across generations.
\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Two‑Faced King} — Mykkiel. *Justice that wears a laurel and a wolf's tail.*
    \item \textbf{The Hearth Judge} — The Witness. *The flame that watches both sides of the bargain.*
    \item \textbf{The Oak Father} — Grimmir. *The root that holds the law; the branch that signs the treaty.*
    \item \textbf{The Coin Saint} — Maelstraeus. *Interest on a handshake; the ledger that never sleeps.*
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross‑Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Smugglers' Covenant (The Sealed Gate):} Vilikari pilots who run the Kahfagian coast swear by a secret oath to the \emph{Unlatched Lock} — a threshold that never fully closes. They leave a copper nail at every hidden cove. A captain who breaks the covenant finds that their boat will not cross the tide line; the sea simply refuses to carry them. The Kahfagian Admiralty has tried to bribe the covenant's keeper; he accepted the coin and sank the next morning in calm water.
  \item \textbf{Saint Theodora of the Dual Writ (The Witness):} A march notary who uncovered a Fenwood spy in the Federate King's court. She forced the spy to sign a confession under both oak and marble, then burned the oak copy and kept the marble one. The spy vanished, but his signature appears on every broken treaty in Vilikari history. Her quill is kept in the Dux's Palace; it writes in lemon juice, and the truth it records cannot be erased by fire or water.
  \item \textbf{The Host of the Two Ledgers (The Longhouse Healers):} A hospice near the Foedus Stone is run by grey‑robed healers who ask no payment. They treat wounded raiders from both sides of the steppe, and the Ykrul and Ecktorian captains have agreed to a truce around its doors. The Federate King tolerates them because they also hold the rite of the \emph{Unbuckled Oath} — a ceremony where a knight may renounce a blood‑debt without losing honour, by placing their sword on the stone and speaking a single true regret.
  \item \textbf{The Ostrikari Cousins (The Savage Heart):} The eastern Vilikari, called Ostrikari by lowlanders, are no more savage than any frontier people. Their reputation comes from the bear cults of the upper Wolf Road, where initiates wear cured skins and speak in growls. The Ostrikari do not raid Vilikari villages; they trade furs for iron and send their young to serve as mercenaries in the Black Banners. A Vilikari proverb says: ``The Ostrikari sharpen their teeth on stone, not on kin.''
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Vilikari scene, the ninth witness called to a mallus court is always a ghost. The wise stop at eight and settle out of court. Those who call a ninth witness find that the ghost speaks the truth — but the truth is always the worst possible version, and the living witnesses remember it as their own.
\end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Patron Omen:} Divine/arcane forces take notice - \textbf{omen appears/blessing lost/curse manifests}.
\item \textbf{Narrative Pivot:} The story takes an unexpected turn that reframes objectives.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Region-Specific SB Options}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Vilikari (Two Laws):} Legal documents contradict each other, courts issue conflicting rulings, oaths bind to different authorities.
\item \textbf{Vilikari (Frontier Diplomacy):} Treaties expire without warning, foreign envoys demand additional concessions, mixed‑blood courtiers shift allegiances.
\item \textbf{Vilikari (Border Tensions):} Frontier posts close without notice, markets shift locations, safe‑conducts are revoked mid‑journey.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Extensions (Plug-in)}
\label{sec:vilikari-extensions}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Two‑Law Dial (Oak $\leftrightarrow$ Marble):} Track which legal tradition holds sway.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Oak High:} +1 Position when invoking mallus, blood‑price, or tribal custom; first basilica petition starts one step worse.
    \item \emph{Marble High:} +1 Effect when citing charters, patents, or Utaran precedent; first mallus oath starts one step worse.
    \item Center the dial by offering a wergild recognized by both sides or hosting a joint moot.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Treaty‑Feast Procedure (fast diplomacy):}
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Set the Table:} Name 2--3 \emph{terms} (what both sides want) and 1 \emph{taboo} (off‑limits).
    \item \textbf{First Cup (Position):} Roll a social test; success grants +1 die to bargain, failure grants the host 1 SB.
    \item \textbf{Bread \& Salt (Offer):} Put \emph{one} leverage on the table (Diamond or oath‑promise). Host matches with an obligation (escort, market access, or hostage).
    \item \textbf{Smoke (Complication):} The GM reveals a pressure tied to \emph{Old Grudges} or \emph{Two Laws}. Resolve it or accept a \emph{Rider Clause}.
    \item \textbf{Knot the Braid:} On success, create a \textbf{Feast‑Bond} [3] clock that protects both parties from betrayal in that matter while it has segments; on breach, the breaker immediately takes 1--2 SB and the clock flips to \textbf{Blood‑Owed} [3].
  \end{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{Frontier Market (quick trading):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item At any \emph{Staple Market} or \emph{Stone Ford}, players may convert a held \Diamond{} into a \emph{Market Edge} (+1 die to all buy/sell rolls for the scene).  
    \item Each conversion ticks a \textbf{Market Knives} [4] clock for that locale. When it fills, a rival factor challenges your right to trade — face a duel, a bribe demand, or a confiscation.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Start‑of‑Scene Omens (1d6):}
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \emph{Laurel Wilt:} first legal action gains +1d; first boast draws \textbf{Law Tangle}.
    \item \emph{Smoke on the Steppe:} \textbf{Raid Rumor} advances once; shortcuts open.
    \item \emph{Twin Bells:} both courts are in session; pick a side or start \textbf{Law Tangle}.
    \item \emph{Queen's Dove:} a message arrives; +1d on the next \textbf{Queen's Audience}.
    \item \emph{Grey Salt:} \textbf{Stipend Arrears} counts double value today; smugglers' ears open.
    \item \emph{Old Eagle:} an Utaran relic surfaces; \textbf{Utaran Patent} use is free this scene.
  \end{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{Plug‑in SB Conversions (Vilikari Flavor):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item 1 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Seal Crack}: a writ loses half its value until re‑witnessed.
    \item 2 SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Citation War}: increase DV by 1 unless you produce a precedent (spend a \Diamond{} or recall a past ruling).
    \item 3+ SB $\rightarrow$ \emph{Crown Fracture}: a treaty splits; halve a consequence by accepting a rival's seal on your paper (start \textbf{Two‑Law} clock).
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Reputation Echoes:}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Court‑Favored:} once/session, downgrade \textbf{Law Tangle} to a minor delay; the next document you present is scrutinized (+1 DV).
    \item \emph{Raid‑Tested:} +1 Position when evading Ykrul or Black Banners; supply lines run 1 segment longer.
    \item \emph{Neutral Broker:} +1d with both oak and marble in a treaty scene; any betrayal earns \textbf{Blood‑Owed} [2] from both sides.
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Cross‑Region Hooks (quick integration):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Kahfagia (Lantern‑Law):} A \textbf{Foedus Seal} may stand in for a \emph{Safe‑Conduct} along a beaconed lane once. Doing so starts \textbf{Port Clash} [2] in the next harbor touched.
    \item \emph{Ecktoria (Imperial Remnants):} An \textbf{Utaran Patent} counts as either \emph{Archive Hour} (scholars open doors) or \emph{Icon License} (display symbols) in Ecktorial sites — choose once per session.
    \item \emph{Aelerian Mountains (Pass \& Toll):} A \textbf{Pass-Ring} (from the Aeler expansion) grants priority at dwarf switchbacks; cashing it to bypass inspection spawns \textbf{Ledger Notice} [3].
    \item \emph{Direwood (Breath \& Bells):} Spending \textbf{Stipend Arrears} for levee labor converts one Direwood \textbf{Clubs} draw into a \emph{Ward-Salt} Diamond for a scene, then begins \textbf{Names Disputed} [3] with bell‑wardens.
    \item \emph{Acasia (Curse & Tithe):} A \textbf{Wergild Table} accepted by a Curse‑touched village also clears one \emph{Feud Spark} clock, but the Hollow gains +1 Attention.
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Vilikari society, power flows through the careful balance of multiple legal traditions, the management of frontier diplomacy, and the maintenance of complex oaths and treaties. The Federate King maintains authority through a combination of imperial forms and traditional customs, while local leaders wield influence through their knowledge of both Utaran law and tribal custom. The true power lies with those who can navigate this complex legal and diplomatic landscape while maintaining the loyalty of their people and the respect of their neighbors.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Vilikari society revolves around legal documents, diplomatic relationships, and the ability to command respect from institutions that operate under multiple legal systems. Rewards often take the form of treaties, charters, and official positions that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize the hybrid identity:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (seals, writs, treaties) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival legal systems issue conflicting authorities, forcing players to choose whose favour matters more.
\item Use the courts, markets, and frontier posts as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success.
\item Remember that Vilikari characters are at home in Ecktoria's counting‑houses and Acasia's hedgerow courts alike — but they are never fully trusted in either. That suspicion is their best tool.
\end{itemize}
In Vilikari society, your word is your bond, and your bond determines whether you rule or are ruled.
\end{tcolorbox}
