\section{Sihai --- ``The Central Kingdom''}
\label{chap:sihai}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{The Mandate is a loan. The ancestors are the creditors. The Hollow collects the interest.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Quiet Chancellor (Elite):}  
  ``The Mandate of Heaven is not a right. It is a loan. The emperor borrows legitimacy from the ancestors, from the land, from the Hollow itself. When the loan is due, the empire falls. I do not serve the emperor. I serve the ledger that tracks the interest.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Provincial Censor (Commoner):}  
  ``My brush is heavier than any sword. A single stroke can erase a name, a family, a dynasty. The emperor reads my reports and trembles. He knows that I have written nine reports this year. The ninth is about him.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Celestial bureaucracy, ghost-ledgers, ink-washed horror, examination hell.

\textbf{Starting Location:} An examination hall where ink-stained desks face a silent proctor. A Provincial Censor holds up a scroll: ``This candidate's family name has been erased. He does not know. You will tell him — or you will take his place in the ledger.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Sihai is the central kingdom, the seat of the Emperor and the Mandate of Heaven. Its canals carry grain from the south; its examination halls produce bureaucrats who govern by the brush. But beneath the mortal bureaucracy runs a deeper one: the Celestial Bureaucracy of gods, ghosts, and ancestors, where every official has a heavenly counterpart and every erased name becomes a hungry ghost. The Lethai-thora — the ancient archivists who taught the first emperors to write — still keep the hidden ledgers, the ones that record what the Censorate burns.

Power here is measured in ink, not iron. The Censorate watches everyone; the Salt Monopoly controls trade; the Kitchen God reports every household's secrets to the Jade Emperor at New Year. A well-written couplet can appease a ghost; a forged seal can summon a demon. The Hollow's ninth debt is the unpaid interest on the Mandate itself. Every dynasty borrows legitimacy; every silence is a debt.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of ink and canal water, but also incense and grave-soil. The way officials stamp every document with a brass seal — and the way the seal leaves a faint watermark that looks like a face. The constant ticking of water-clocks, but sometimes the ticking skips a beat, and the shadows pause to listen.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never speak of succession before naming the Emperor. The Censorate has ears in every tea house — and the ghosts have ears in every wall. A misplaced word can erase your name from the rolls, and from the memory of the ancestors.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Weight of the Brush}

Power here is measured in examination scores, salt licenses, and the thickness of a family's archive. The Emperor rules, but the Censorate audits, the Salt Monopoly finances, and the Lethai-thora remember what the Censorate burns. A provincial governor's authority rests on his examination rank; a merchant's on his salt license. The dead are not gone — they are merely demoted. The Hungry Ghost Festival is the only time the Celestial Bureaucracy accepts appeals. The Hollow watches from the missing ninth step, the unpoured ninth cup, the unsaid ninth name.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:sihai-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 → 4, 6–10 → 6, J/Q/K → 8, A → 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (passes, seals, tokens) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{ink-omens}—scrolls that rustle in still air, a lamp that burns green, a forgotten name whispered from a shelf, a ghost that asks for a pen.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Celestial Bureaucracy)}
\label{sec:sihai-features}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Mandate Clock [8] (Shared Campaign Clock):} Tracks the stability of the dynasty. The party inherits 2 ticks at the start of any adventure set in Sihai (the old debt). The clock ticks when the emperor's health fails, when a succession dispute arises, when a major corruption scandal is exposed, or when the party breaks an oath witnessed by an ancestor. When the clock reaches 4: the Censorate becomes more aggressive (all audits start at Desperate Position). When it reaches 8: the Mandate fails — the Hollow claims the throne, civil war erupts, and the party must choose a side or flee. Resetting the clock requires a \textbf{Ancestor Rite} at the Imperial Ancestral Temple (downtime, costs 1 Supply and a truthful confession) or a public execution of a corrupt official (costs 1 Boon and may create enemies).

\item \textbf{The Two Ledgers (Ancestral Names Mechanic):} Every character has two ``names'': their \textbf{Mortal Name} (what the Censorate knows) and their \textbf{Ancestral Name} (recorded in the Celestial Ledger, kept by the Lethai-thora). The Ancestral Name cannot be erased by mortal means. Discovering a character's Ancestral Name requires a \textbf{Research Clock (size 6)} in the Lethai-thora archives. Once known, the player may invoke the Ancestral Name once per session to gain +2 dice on any roll related to that ancestor's domain (e.g., a farmer's ancestor aids harvest, a soldier's ancestor aids battle). However, invoking the Ancestral Name creates an \textbf{Ancestral Debt (size 4 clock)}. When it fills, the ancestor demands a service — usually something the ancestor failed to do in life (e.g., ``Apologize to my widow'' or ``Find my stolen seal''). Refusing the demand marks 1 Corruption and adds 2 ticks to the Mandate Clock.

\item \textbf{Censorate Audit (Social Combat Mechanic):} The Censorate can audit any character at any time. An audit is a social combat with three phases:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Pleadings} (Presence + Sway vs. Censor's Lore). Success grants +1 die to the next phase.
    \item \textbf{Evidence} (Wits + Investigation vs. DV 4). Success reveals a hidden debt or a forged document; failure gives the Censor a piece of evidence against you.
    \item \textbf{Judgment} (Resolve + Lore vs. DV 5). Success: the character is cleared. Failure: the Censorate erases a name — the character loses one Bond (that person no longer remembers them) or one Asset (it is seized), and the Mandate Clock ticks +1.
  \end{enumerate}
  Players can avoid an audit by: \textbf{Bribing the Censor} (costs 1 Honey Jar or 1 Boon), \textbf{Presenting a forged document} (requires a Forgery Clock, size 4), or \textbf{Calling a ghost witness} (requires a Spirit + Lore test, DV 4; the ghost cannot lie, but it will ask for a favor in return – usually a promise that creates a new Ancestral Debt).

\item \textbf{Examination Hell (Dungeon Mechanic):} The Forbidden Garden contains a subterranean examination hall where the ghosts of failed candidates still write their answers. The party can enter to find a lost Ancestral Name – but the hall is alive.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item The hall has a \textbf{Stress Clock (size 8)}. Each failed roll, each lie spoken, each broken taboo ticks the clock.
    \item At 4: The hall begins to change. Doors lead to different corridors. Navigation becomes Desperate.
    \item At 8: The \textbf{Examiner Ghost} appears – a skeleton in scholar's robes. It asks one question. The party must answer in verse. If the verse is good, the ghost grants a boon (reveals one Ancestral Name). If poor, the ghost erases one of the party's names – that character is forgotten by the living for 1d4 days (lose all Bonds temporarily, cannot be recognised). The Stress Clock resets after the encounter.
  \end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Paper Soldiers and Hungry Ghosts (Minion Types):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Paper Soldiers (Tier I, Cap 2):} Origami constructs animated by Censorate magic. They have Harm 2, Body 2, Melee 1. They are Vulnerable to fire (double Harm) and to a successful Lore test (reveals their true name, causing them to crumple).
    \item \textbf{Hungry Ghosts (Tier II, Cap 3):} Spirits of those whose names were erased. They have Harm 3, Spirit 3, and the ability \textbf{Memory Drain} (touch, target forgets a minor detail – a face, a password, a skill point for one scene). They cannot cross a line of salt or a properly witnessed oath-circle.
  \end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Water Rights as Ancestral Currency:} In the canal system, water rights are traded like silver, but they are also offerings to the Dragon Kings of the Four Seas. A Canal Mother's key is worth more than a chest of coins — it can also summon a rain spirit (once per season, calm a storm or flood a district). Forging a water right angers the Dragon King; the forger's wells run dry for nine years (all Survival tests to find water suffer –2 dice).

\item \textbf{Lethai-thora Archivists of the First Seal:} The Lethai-thora are not merely librarians — they are the keepers of the original Mandate, written on tortoise shells and dragon bones. They do not serve the emperor; they serve the ledger that predates the empire. A Lethai-thora keeper can declare any document void by writing “void” in water over it. The water must be from the Well of Erased Names. Doing so costs 1 Obligation to the Lethai-thora and ticks the Mandate Clock +1 (the Censorate will investigate).

\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Jade Censor} — The Witness. *A magistrate who never blinks, who writes every truth in a ledger of jade.*
    \item \textbf{The Dragon Salt} — Maelstraeus. *The price of a monopoly; the interest on a nation's thirst.*
    \item \textbf{The Ninth Ancestor} — Khemesh. *The ghost of the first emperor, who still reviews petitions.*
    \item \textbf{The Brush That Walks} — The Traveler. *A calligraphy brush that guides lost officials through the underworld.*
    \item \textbf{The Kitchen's Ear} — The Sealed Gate. *The god who reports every secret to the Celestial Bureaucracy.*

\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Ninth Ledger and the Kalrantha Amulet (Dhahara):} The ninth ledger is said to contain the name of the prince who stole the amulet. The Veiled Sisters want to erase it. The Breath-less want to find it. The Lethai-thora have hidden it in a scroll that unrolls forever.
  \item \textbf{Silence-Tokens and the Covenant (Mykkiel):} The Covenant recognises Lethai-thora silence-tokens as valid requests for closed hearings. A silence token can pause any court proceeding for the duration of a single breath. The token is a strip of silk from a burial shroud.
  \item \textbf{Canal Mothers and the Fhara (Way of Silk):} Fhara well-judges and Sihai canal mothers use similar water-measuring systems. A canal mother's key can be used to negotiate a well contract — but the key must be dipped in the well first. The water will remember.
  \item \textbf{The Mandate and the Hollow (Eastern Realms):} The Hollow is the unpaid interest on the Mandate. Aelaerem hedge-keepers say that when a dynasty falls, the Hollow eats the ninth name. The name is always the emperor's secret childhood name.
\end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Name to the Well:} Whisper a dead censor's name into the Well of Erased Names. Gain +1 Effect when negotiating with the Censorate this scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Hollow. `[SUPERSTITION]`
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Sihai, power flows through ink, salt, and the weight of the Mandate. The Emperor rules, but the Censorate audits, the Salt Monopoly finances, the Lethai-thora remember, and the ancestors watch. A well-written couplet can appease a ghost; a forged seal can start a war. The Celestial Bureaucracy is always hiring.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Sihai is measured in examination scores, salt licenses, and the thickness of a family's archive. Patronage often takes the form of stamped passes, ledger copies, or silence-tokens — each tied to a specific office, canal, or debt. To emphasize the mythological flavour:
\begin{itemize}
\item Use the \textbf{Mandate Clock} as a visible campaign front. The party's actions can tick or reset it. Ancestor rites are the only safe way to reduce it.
\item Enforce \textbf{Censorate Audits} as a recurring threat — players must maintain clean papers or face erasure. Ghost witnesses can be summoned by either side.
\item Let \textbf{water rights} be a form of currency that can be stolen, forged, or contested — but angering the Dragon King has consequences.
\item Introduce \textbf{paper soldiers} (origami constructs) as low-tier enemies and \textbf{hungry ghosts} as mid-tier threats. The Censorate employs both.
\end{itemize}
In Sihai, the brush forgets nothing — and the Hollow remembers every erased name.
\end{tcolorbox}
