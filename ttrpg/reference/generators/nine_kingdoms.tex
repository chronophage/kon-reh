\section{Dhahara: The Elephant Kingdoms and the Iron Passes}
\label{chap:dhahara}

\textbf{Key Demographics: } Humans
\textbf{Capital City: } Chaunagar

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{The elephant remembers every path. The mountain counts every breath. The peacock watches the ninth throne.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Maharaja Suryavarman (Elite):}  
  ``The Peacock Throne is not a seat. It is a promise made by the first sun king: that the nine kingdoms would remain nine, never one, never fewer. I do not rule. I hold the pattern. A peacock's tail has nine eyes. Each eye watches a different kingdom. When one closes, the throne weeps.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Viceroy Thrain (Aeler Elite):}  
  ``The passes do not care for your lineage or your gods. They care for iron, tea, and the heft of a sworn oath. Aeler and Dhaharan have bled into the same stone here. The mountain accepts both.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Elephant Mahout (Commoner):}  
  ``My grandfather drove elephants for the Sun Kings. My father drove them for the Aeler. I drive them for the Viceroy. The elephants do not care who holds the howdah. They remember the old songs---the ones about the ninth kingdom that never was.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Elephant warfare, monsoon ritual, honor as ancestral dharma, high-altitude survival horror, industrial colonialism.

\textbf{Starting Location:} The Peacock Throne Hall in Ahimsa, where a golden peacock with ruby eyes watches the court. The Maharaja's chamberlain approaches: ``The Maharaja wishes to renew the Nine-Kingdom Pact. But the King of Vajrapur has sent a forged genealogy. The Queen of Mahishmati has refused to attend. And the ninth kingdom---the one that is always missing---has sent a messenger. The messenger is a ghost. Meanwhile, Viceroy Thrain has sent a breath-counter and a demand: the iron due must be met. Speak carefully. The peacock is watching.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere},breakable]
Between the high passes of the Aeler viceroyalty and the Brass Coast lies a fertile, contested lowland: the land of the Nine Kingdoms, the heart of Dhahara. Here, ancient dynasties still claim descent from the sun, and the Peacock Throne of Ahimsa casts its shadow over half a dozen rival courts. Rivers are borders; elephants are war machines; every harvest is a negotiation between kings who have never met but whose grandfathers swore blood oaths.

North, in the high passes, the Aeler viceroys rule on behalf of the distant High King. Here, the air is thin and cold, the rivers run white with snowmelt, and the passes are measured in breath-counts rather than miles. Iron mines scar the hillsides, worked by Dhaharan laborers and overseen by Aeler engineers. The Aeler have established a viceroyalty here---not conquest, but a long lease, renewed every nine years.

But both north and south share a common truth: the number ``nine'' is a wound---and a promise. There have never been nine kingdoms. The number is a ritual, a prayer that the Hollow will not notice that one of the nine is always missing. That missing kingdom changes. Sometimes it is a real kingdom that fell to plague or war; sometimes it is a kingdom that never was, a placeholder kept empty to satisfy the silence.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of jasmine and dust, and the ringing of iron hammers. The way every crossroads has a painted stone---a boundary marker, a warning, a prayer. The constant murmur of bazaars where haggling is a form of theater. The silence after a bell rings in the mountains---the silence is for the mountain to answer.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never count your breaths aloud in a mine. The mountain listens, and if it hears you reach the ninth, it will take one---a breath, a memory, a shadow. In the lowlands, never praise a king's elephant. The elephant is sacred; your praise will be taken as an insult to the king's own mount. Compliment the mahout.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: The Weight of the Elephant and the Gaze of the Hollow}

Power here is measured in river rights, elephant herds, the thickness of a dynasty's archive, and the number of breaths a miner can hold. In the lowlands, a king's authority rests on how many generations back to the sun---but also on how many of those generations kept their promises. The Peacock Throne of Ahimsa is the nominal center, but the other eight kings vie for influence, and the pattern shifts with every monsoon.

And somewhere, in the gap between the eighth and the ninth---between the eighth breath and the ninth breath, between the eighth throne and the ninth throne---the Hollow waits to ask what the missing kingdom promised.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:dhahara-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Timer (\(2\)--\(5 \rightarrow 4\), \(6\)--\(10 \rightarrow 6\), J/Q/K \(\rightarrow 8\), A \(\rightarrow 10\)).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (feathers, keys, seals, bells, ingots) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{Dhaharan omens}---peacock feathers shed without reason, a well that reflects a face not yours, a throne that creaks when empty, a whisper that counts to nine, iron that weeps rust, a pass that closes behind you, a bell that rings without a clapper.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features (Cultural Mechanics)}
\label{sec:dhahara-mechanics}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Ancestral Honor (Reputation Track)}: Each PC has an Ancestral Honor score from \(-3\) (Heavy Burden) to \(+3\) (Bearer of Light). Gain Burden by: breaking a sworn oath, failing to protect a family member, disrespecting a temple. Gain Honor by: keeping a difficult promise, restoring a forgotten genealogy, donating to a royal archive.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{+2 or +3 (Bearer of Light):} +1 Position in social encounters with nobles and priests. You may call in one minor family favor per session.
    \item \textbf{-2 or -3 (Heavy Burden):} Market prices double for you. You may be challenged to an honor-duel (combat or legal contest). If you refuse, you lose 1 permanent Reputation tier.
  \end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Elephant Community Share (Highland)}: Instead of individual ownership, elephant caravans operate on a share system. A character who helps the caravan gains a \textbf{Share Token}. Spend it to call on the caravan for a single favor: passage for a wagon, a day's fodder, or a warning about a rival. Share Tokens are reciprocity, not weight.

\item \textbf{Breath-Oaths as Leverage (Highland)}: Aeler engineers use breath-oaths to secure contracts. A character who holds a breath-oath can demand a renegotiation of tolls, quotas, or labor terms. Breaking a breath-oath does not incur a curse; it simply ends the trust, and the other party will never deal with you again.

\item \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names)}:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Peacock's Eye} --- The Witness. \textit{The ruby that sees all false genealogies.}
    \item \textbf{The Iron Queen} --- Mykkiel. \textit{The law that binds the nine kingdoms, even when kings forget.}
    \item \textbf{The Monsoon Lord} --- Khemesh. \textit{The pressure that brings the rain---and the flood that follows.}
    \item \textbf{The Ninth Throne} --- The Hollow. \textit{The empty chair. The unspoken promise. The kingdom that never was.}
    \item \textbf{The Silent Bell} --- The Sealed Gate (Highland). \textit{The threshold that asks only that you stop at eight.}
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Nine Kings' Tomb and the Kalrantha Amulet}: The tomb contains a map to the Himadri temple where the amulet was sealed. The map is tattooed on the inside of the Ninth King's ghost. You will need to ask politely. The ghost charges a memory for each word.
  \item \textbf{The Immortal Peacock and the Hollow}: The peacock is said to be the Hollow's original form---before it learned to hunger. Aelaerem hedge-keepers leave offerings of peacock feathers at the Hollow's gates. The ninth feather offered each year is returned, bloodied, to the Peacock Gardens.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth Seal Mine and the Kalrantha Amulet}: The demon sealed in the mine is a fragment of Varash the Unmaker. The Veiled Sisters want to free it. The Breath-less want to re-seal it.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power},breakable]
Among the Dhaharan, power flows through genealogy, elephant herds, the honor of the court, and the slow pull of ancestral promise---and in the high passes, through iron, tea, and the lease. The Peacock Throne is the center, but the other eight kingdoms are competitors. And the missing ninth is the question that never stops echoing.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in Dhahara is measured in iron, buffalo, the thickness of a family's scroll collection, the number of forgotten promises in its bloodline, and the number of breaths a miner can hold.

\begin{itemize}
\item Use the \textbf{Ancestral Honor Dial} as a visible track. High Honor opens doors; low Honor closes them---and may bring the Hollow's silent witnesses.
\item Track the \textbf{Ninth Kingdom Timer } as a slow-burn campaign timer. The party cannot stop it permanently---only delay it, redirect it, or prepare for the night when the missing kingdom returns.
\item In the high passes, use the \textbf{Lease Timer } to mirror the politics of the lowlands. The renewal of the lease is a recurring pressure point.
\item Emphasize that the Aeler are \textbf{not conquerors}---they are lessees, bound by treaty. Their engineers and viceroys are in Dhaharan territory on Dhaharan sufferance, renewed every nine years.
\end{itemize}
In Dhahara, the peacock remembers every eye, the well shows every death, the mountain takes every ninth breath, and the missing kingdom waits. Its question is never closed.
\end{tcolorbox}
