\section{Thepyrgos --- ``City of a Thousand Stairs''}
\label{chap:thepyrgos}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{The Archon (Elite):}  
  ``Every stair climbed is a word in the great conversation between earth and sky. This city is not built – it is grown, stone by stone, law by law, until the very architecture speaks wisdom.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Bell-Runner (Commoner):}  
  ``I know every step that creaks a warning and every landing that whispers gossip. The bells tell the city's mood better than any lord – three chimes means business, six means trouble, and nine means the hunters are coming.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Thepyrgos is both realm and city, a former Utaran province whose capital rises in tiers of towers, terraces, and endless stairs above the Dolmis shore. Once a military bastion, now a nation unto itself, it is famed for its universities, libraries, and debating synods, where precedent and philosophy weigh as much as pikes or coin. Outside the city, the countryside reflects a rugged coastal landscape of whitewashed villages, olive groves, and timber churches—home to the Hyro people, humans with an unusually high rate of arcane talent. This region is also the land of Canré, a Kon'reh variant that deliberately ignores its historical roots in favor of pure geometry.

The Chain‑Lantern order and other Witch‑Hunter Commissariats ruthlessly patrol the villages and hamlets, using bounty postings, candle‑smoke tests, and stair‑seizures to identify and kidnap children with arcane talent for the university's hidden colleges. The enigmatic Witness and the Ninth fight proxy wars via fraternal orders and occult mystery cults, their influence felt in the political maneuvering of the Synod and the mysterious disappearances that plague the countryside. Every stone stair carries the echo of old campaigns and civic quarrels, every bell carries law across the wind. Here, authority climbs as much as it rules: archons elected by the city's wards, synods decreeing rites and edicts, guilds policing ropes, cranes, and chains. In Thepyrgos, height is hierarchy, sound is sovereignty, and law is etched into every rung.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The endless chime of bells from different tiers, never quite in sync. The smell of brine and olive oil and candle wax. The way locals glance at the sky when a child coughs.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never let a bell‑ringer count your breaths. The Chain‑Lanterns pay well for names that ring false on the test stairs.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Stairs, Bells, and Smoke}

Here, every ascent is a plea and every descent a confession. Thepyrgos the city is a vertical labyrinth of stair‑rights, rope‑guilds, and synod edicts that shift with the tide. Thepyrgos the province is a haunted countryside of hidden talent and state‑sanctioned abduction. The Hyro keep their arcane children in root cellars and haylofts, praying the hunters take the neighbor's instead. The Chain‑Lanterns move at dawn, their brass censers swinging, their bells tuned to the frequency of latent magic. In the university's forbidden tiers, the Witness and the Ninth arm rival fraternities with citations and curses, and no one is sure which side the Archon secretly favors.

\textbf{Starting Location:} The Synod Hall at dusk. Gold‑glass mosaics glitter as a Matriarch reads a scroll aloud. “The Beacon Crown sees traitors in the smoke. A child's talent was sold to the Commissary last night. Find the informer—or the next stair seized will be yours.”

\paragraph*{(Tower/Stair/Sea‑wall)} Pilgrim's Stair zig‑zagging sea‑cliff to wicket gate; Tower Quarter with family keeps stitched by ladders and bridges.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:thepyrgos-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (keys/rites/papers) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{height and sound} motifs—bells that carry, wind that remembers, stairs that echo with purpose.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Bell-Law:} Bells are law made sound. Once per session, a Bellmark or tower bell summons the watch, a levy, or a decree. False rings stain a reputation; the Synod may inquire.
\item \textbf{Stair-Rights:} Every stair is owned, owed, or leased. A token bypasses barricades and tolls; climbing the wrong stair without leave sparks a riot or a duel.
\item \textbf{Synod Edicts:} Voice reshapes daily life. Once per session, a decree grants or strips rights: a chant forbidden, a symbol proscribed, a stair sealed. Rulings create leverage and risk.
\item \textbf{The Chain‑Lanterns:} The most feared of the Witch‑Hunter orders. Their captains carry brass lanterns that reveal latent magic through candle‑smoke tests. A Chain‑Lantern raid is heralded by a single, low bell—the death knell of innocence.
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Bitter Root:} Daughters of the Cord run a hospice in the lower tiers. They ask no payment—but they take names. A renegade Sister hides among the bell‑runners, trying to unbind her Hollowed from the Commissariat's ledger.
  \item \textbf{The Threshold Compendium:} The Library of Keys holds a Class II Anchor—a scholar who died mid‑citation. Saikou Ira would trade his silver mirror for a single shelf.
  \item \textbf{Malachai, the Chained Angel:} A cursed bell in the Tower Quarter rings once a year at midnight. Hear it, and you gain a year of luck—but the Chain‑Lanterns will know your face forever.
  \item \textbf{The Grey Wanderer's Grimoire:} Thepyrgos psions are called ``Stair‑Readers.'' They climb in darkness, tasting each riser's memory. The Commissariat employs three—and has never let them meet.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} In any Thepyrgos scene, the ninth bell rung in an hour opens a threshold. The wise stop at eight and cover their ears.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Thepyrgos Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bread to the Stair:} Toss a crust onto a rung before climbing. Ignore the first \emph{Tremor} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Rope:} Tie a single knot in a crane line. Cancel \emph{Rope Guild Interdict} or take –1 die on your next lift (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Smoke:} Whisper a hunter's name into the Beacon Crown's fire. Gain +1 Effect when evading the Chain‑Lanterns this scene, but mark \emph{Obligation} to the Synod.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Thepyrgos, power flows through elevation, education, and the precise application of law. The Archon maintains authority through a complex web of guilds, synods, and civic institutions that govern every aspect of vertical life. Social mobility is literally upward mobility, with stair‑rights, bellmarks, and archive access serving as both currency and status symbols.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Thepyrgos revolves around access—to towers, to knowledge, to the right to climb. Rewards often take the form of keys, passes, or legal exemptions that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize this:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, keys, papers) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival guilds issue conflicting rights, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the synods, towers, and staircases as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success.
\end{itemize}
In Thepyrgos, your ascent is your standing, and your standing determines whether you rise or fall.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Scenario Starters (Plug‑and‑Play)}
\paragraph{The Bright Child on the Exam Stair}
\emph{Hook:} A Bell‑Runner begs you to get a latent child to campus before the dawn sweep.  
\emph{Complications:} \textbf{Candle-Smoke Test}, \textbf{Commissary Sweep}, a jealous \textbf{Street Thaum}.  
\emph{Relief:} Trade a \emph{Latency Seal} for testimony; or ring a lawful \emph{Bellmark} to create a corridor—accept \emph{Censure Murmurs} [2].

\paragraph{The Black Ledger of Bounties}
\emph{Hook:} The College of Quiet wants proof that a Commissary inflates prices to fill quotas.  
\emph{Complications:} \textbf{Synod Summons}, \textbf{Smuggler's Ladder}, \textbf{Chain Jam}.  
\emph{Relief:} Publish via a \emph{Nomikon} colloquy (gain \emph{Synod Indulgence}); or sell it to the Archon (gain \emph{Archon's Clemency} but start \emph{Faculty Feud} [4]).

\paragraph{The Golden Key and the Silent Stair}
\emph{Hook:} A dying Archivist gives you a Golden Key. “It opens the stair the hunters fear.”  
\emph{Truth:} The stair leads to the Beacon Crown's secret basement—a prison for children who passed the smoke test.  
\emph{Relief:} Free the children and expose the Commissariat (gain \emph{Archon's Clemency} for the whole party, but start \emph{Exarch's Claim} at full); or seal the stair forever (gain \emph{Golden Key} to use elsewhere, but the children are lost to memory).
