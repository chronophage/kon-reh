\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme & Atmosphere}]
Thepyrgos is both realm and city, built on the bones of the drowned \textbf{Astroegro Empire}. The capital rises in tiers of towers, terraces, and endless stairs above the Dolmis shore‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíbut the stairs are not stone. They are \emph{fossilized vertebrae}, the spine of a leviathan that Khemesh dragged under when the last emperor broke a covenant older than the sea. Once a Utaran province, now a nation unto itself, Thepyrgos is famed for its universities, libraries, and debating synods, where precedent and philosophy weigh as much as pikes or coin.

The Astroegro were a human civilization of formidable magic, the ancient rivals of the \textbf{Valewood's Empire of Leaves}. Their ruins litter the hills and swamps south of the city, and their legacy haunts every threshold. When the Empire of Leaves fell, \textbf{Lethai‚ąíthora} refugees‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíhigh elves who had served as archivists and lore‚ąíkeepers‚ąí‚ąí‚ąífled across the Black River and were granted sanctuary in Thepyrgos. Generations later, their descendants still walk the city's tiers, their silver hair and ink‚ąístained fingers a quiet reminder of a pact older than the synod. Many now hold chairs at the university, tend the forbidden archives, or secretly serve the Witness or the Ninth.

Outside the city, the countryside is a rugged coastal landscape of limestone hills, olive groves, and whitewashed villages‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíbut the weather is warmer, wetter, and the valleys are choked with reed‚ąífilled marshes. This is the land of the \textbf{Hyro} people, the surviving descendants of the Astroegro. The Hyro carry the fracture of their ancestors in their blood. A child born in the hills might dream in geometries that do not exist. A farmer might hear the stones whispering in a language no living throat can shape. The \textbf{Chain‚ąíLanterns} and other Witch‚ąíHunter Commissariats ruthlessly patrol these villages, using posted warrants, candle‚ąísmoke tests, and stair‚ąíseizures to identify and kidnap children with arcane talent. The Hyro have learned to offer \emph{substitutes}‚ąí‚ąí‚ąía memory, a false name, a lock of hair‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíat crossroads, hoping the Hollow accepts the tribute.

In the university's forbidden tiers, the \textbf{Witness} and the \textbf{Ninth} fight a proxy war via fraternal orders and occult mystery cults. The Witness wants the \textbf{Astroegro Vaults} \emph{remembered} but sealed; the Ninth wants them \emph{opened}, to trigger a Great Unlearning that would erase the concept of record from the world. No provenance would remain. The Hollow would go unanswered. The Witness would have nothing to inscribe. The Ninth would become the only law: \emph{forgetting}. No one is sure which side the Archon secretly favors. And in the ruins of the Astroegro, something still ticks‚ąí‚ąí‚ąígears of black glass, locked doors that hum, and the occasional survivor with eyes like burning tar who speaks of a pressure from below.

\textbf{What you notice first:} The endless chime of bells from different tiers, never quite in sync. The smell of brine, olive oil, and candle wax. The way locals glance at the sky when a child coughs‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíand then at the nearest crossroads. The quiet grace of the Lethai‚ąíthora archivists, who never seem to blink.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never let a bell‚ąíringer count your breaths. The Chain‚ąíLanterns offer safe conduct to those who give names that ring false on the test stairs. But worse: never ask a question of a stair that has no end. The Ninth is listening, and it will answer.

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Stairs, Bells, and the Argument of Patrons}

Here, every ascent is a plea and every descent a confession. Thepyrgos the city is a vertical labyrinth of stair‚ąírights, rope‚ąíguilds, and synod edicts that shift with the tide. Thepyrgos the province is a haunted countryside of hidden talent and state‚ąísanctioned abduction. The Hyro keep their arcane children in root cellars and haylofts, praying the hunters take the neighbor's instead. The Chain‚ąíLanterns move at dawn, their brass censers swinging, their bells tuned to the frequency of latent magic. \subsection*{Geographic Integration}

Thepyrgos is landlocked on three sides by Viterra, separated by the Black River and the Belmont Marshes. The marshy lowlands breed strange fogs and rarer creatures, and the border is porous‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíViterran hedge‚ąícourts and Thepyrgos synods argue jurisdiction over every flooded farmstead. To the northeast, a short boat ride from Fairport (Viterra) across the Belworth estuary brings travelers to Marcott in Vhasia, making Thepyrgos a quiet but crucial link in the riverine trade between the fractured sun‚ąíkingdom and the imperial remnant. The city itself controls the \textbf{Astroegro Straits}‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíthe narrow sea passage between the Dolmis and the Amaranthine‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíand maintains a few small colonies on the western coast of Hyaar, where olive oil and wine are exchanged for eastern silks and spices. Those colonies are run by exiled scholars and retired Chain‚ąíLanterns, and they are said to be even more paranoid than the mother city.

In Thepyrgos, power flows through elevation, education, and the precise application of law‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíbut also through the hidden architecture of the Unfinished Stair. \textbf{For the GM:}

\begin{itemize}

‚ĄĻtem Let rival guilds and the two Patrons (Witness and Ninth) issue conflicting exemptions‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíplayers must choose whose favor matters more, and whose curse is worse.

‚ĄĻtem Introduce the Unfinished Stair timer as a campaign tension; every time the party uses a hidden stair, they advance the timer and bring the Ninth's attention closer.

In Thepyrgos, your ascent is your standing, and your standing determines whether you rise or fall‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíor whether you are simply \emph{forgotten}.
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage & Power}]
In Thepyrgos, power flows through elevation, education, and the precise application of law‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíbut also through the hidden architecture of the Unfinished Stair. The Archon maintains authority through a complex web of guilds, synods, and civic institutions, but the real power lies with whoever can climb the stairs that should not exist. Social mobility is literally upward mobility, with stair‚ąírights, bellmarks, and archive access serving as both currency and status symbols. The Lethai‚ąíthora, as the city's most respected archivists, hold a special kind of power: they know which shelves are watched, which keys are false, and which doors lead to the Ninth's vaults. Some whisper that the Archon is secretly a Lethai‚ąíthora puppet; others say the real ruler is the ghost of an Astroegro emperor, still counting stairs in the deep.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Thepyrgos revolves around access‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíto towers, to knowledge, to the right to climb, and to the hidden stairs. Rewards often take the form of keys, passes, or legal exemptions that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize the metaphysical war:
\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, keys, papers) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
‚ĄĻtem Let rival guilds and the two Patrons (Witness and Ninth) issue conflicting rights, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more, and whose curse is worse.
‚ĄĻtem Use the synods, towers, and staircases as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of law and custom determines success‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíbut where the Ninth's silence and the Witness's memory can tip the scales.
‚ĄĻtem Introduce the Unfinished Stair timer as a campaign tension; every time the party uses a hidden stair, they advance the timer and bring the Ninth's attention closer.
\end{itemize}
In Thepyrgos, your ascent is your standing, and your standing determines whether you rise or fall‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíor whether you are simply \emph{forgotten}.
