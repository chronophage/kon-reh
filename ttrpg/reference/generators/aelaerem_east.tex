\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{Count your steps. Tie your threads. The ninth cup is never poured.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Elder of the Woven Knot (Elite):}  
  ``The Hollow does not live in the dark. It lives in the space between the eighth and the ninth – the step you did not take, the word you did not say, the cup you did not fill. We tie red threads to remind the world where the boundary is. When the thread frays, the Hollow crosses.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Rice-Paddy Keeper (Commoner):}  
  ``My grandmother tied a red thread around this paddy nine generations ago. The water remembers her name. When the monsoon comes, the thread tightens. When the Hollow walks, the thread loosens. I do not water the rice. I water the thread.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Theme \& Atmosphere}

In the eastern valleys, the Aelaerem tend terraced rice paddies bound with red thread. Their villages nestle under wooded peaks, and every threshold is a negotiation with the unseen. Their magic is housekeeping writ large—thresholds groomed, names jarred like pickled plums, the Hollow fed with silence and the ninth cup left unpoured. When the Neighbors stir beneath the mountain cairns, the folk answer with lantern law and the careful geometry of guest-right.

Eastern Aelaerem share blood with their western kin but have adapted to monsoon rains, bamboo groves, and the proximity of Sihai and Nihori. They trade red thread for rice, stone fox charms for sake, and they have learned to count the Hollow's footsteps in the rhythm of falling water.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of rice porridge and old cedar. The way every terrace has a red thread tied at the water inlet. The silence of children when a stranger passes the village stone.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never refuse an offered cup. The Hollow counts refusals as invitations. Pour the ninth cup for the empty chair, and you may feed it – or feed it.

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Warm Hearths, Cold Thresholds}

Here, the land is not wilderness – it is a living weave of favors and omissions. The Aelaerem do not build walls; they build hedges, stiles, and the careful geometry of guest-right. A red door means bread, salt, and one safe night if you come honest. A cup-mark stone on the verge accepts butter as a prayer. The watch-geese know your step before you do, and the scarecrow turns to face the road when the Hollow is hungry. The Neighbors – the things that live under the barrows, in the uncounted ninth step, in the space between the candle and its shadow – are not enemies. They are neighbors. And neighbors, if treated politely, will stay on their side of the hedge.

\subsection*{The Hollow’s Attention (Campaign Timer)}
The Hollow is not a monster. It is a forgetfulness that lives in thresholds, miscounts, and broken guest-right. Track its attention with a visible timer:

\textbf{Hollow Attention [8]} – Advances when:
\begin{itemize}
\item A character refuses an offered cup (+1)
\item A promise is broken on a threshold (+1)
\item The ninth cup is poured (+2)
\item A guest-right is violated (GM spends 1 SB to tick +1)
\item A child points at an empty chair (the Hollow is already listening; +0, but the GM gains 1 SB)
\end{itemize}
At 4 segments: Lanterns flicker without wind. Dogs whine at nothing. The Hollow is watching.  
At 8 segments: The Hollow manifests. Roll on the \textbf{Hollow Walks} table. The party must offer a story, a memory, or a name to appease it.

\subsection*{Hollow Walks (d6)}
When the Hollow Attention timer reaches 8, roll 1d6 for the manifestation:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A pale figure stands at the edge of the circle. It asks for a name. Give a name (living or dead) and it leaves. Refuse, and it takes your shadow (–1 die on stealth for a week).
\item The ninth cup pours itself. Whoever drinks from it forgets their greatest joy (lose 1 XP, refundable by a quest).
\item A red thread appears tied around every PC’s wrist. It tightens when you lie. Cutting it costs 1 Corruption.
\item The geese fall silent. The Hollow is inside the circle. It demands a story that ends with a death. If the story is true, it leaves. If false, it takes a memory.
\item The Moot Oak bleeds sap. The Hollow speaks through it: ``One of you carries a promise unpaid. Settle it now.'' The one named must offer 1 Boon or 1 Supply. Refusal ticks the timer again immediately.
\item The empty chair is occupied by a figure in gray. It does not speak. It eats. If you serve it food, it leaves. If you ignore it, it eats your luck (all rolls are Desperate for the next scene).
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Spades --- Places}
\label{sec:eastern-aelaerem-places}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Terraced Rice Paddy} \hfill \textit{[HARVEST] [BOUNDARY]} \\
  Stepped field carved into a hillside, each level bordered by red thread. The thread is tied to wooden stakes. A broken stake means the Hollow has passed. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} To work the paddy, you must re-tie any loose thread (Wits+Craft DV 3). Failure ticks \textbf{Hollow Attention +1} as the boundary weakens.

\item \textbf{Red Thread Shrine} \hfill \textit{[RITUAL] [OMEN]} \\
  Small wooden shelter at a crossroads, hung with thousands of knotted cords. Each cord is a prayer, a warning, a promise. The oldest cord is black—its red has faded to the colour of dried blood. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Tying a new cord (Spirit+Lore DV 3) clears 1 Fatigue. Untying an old cord gives a vision of the person who tied it – the GM gains 1 SB.

\item \textbf{Mountain Cairn} \hfill \textit{[DEATH] [MEMORY]} \\
  Pile of river stones, each tied with a single faded thread. The stones are warm. They were placed by the dead before they died. The newest cairn has a loose thread – untie it, and the dead may speak. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Untying the thread allows one question of the deceased (Spirit+Lore DV 4). On failure, the ghost asks a question instead; refusing ticks \textbf{Hollow Attention +2}.

\item \textbf{Bamboo Grove} \hfill \textit{[OMEN] [FEAR]} \\
  Thicket where the stalks are carved with tally marks. Eight marks, a space, eight marks. The ninth mark is never carved. The bamboo bleeds sap when a promise is broken nearby. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} A character who breaks a promise within the grove ticks \textbf{Hollow Attention +1} and the sap stains their hands (–1 die on stealth for the scene).

\item \textbf{Stone Fox Shrine} \hfill \textit{[SPIRIT] [RIDDLE]} \\
  Weathered statue of a fox with a red cord around its neck. The cord is knotted in a pattern no living weaver can replicate. The fox’s eyes follow you. Offer a grain of rice; it may tell you a secret. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Offering 1 Supply lets you ask the fox one yes/no question. The answer is always a riddle. On a critical failure, the fox demands a story instead.

\item \textbf{Sake Brewery (Threshold)} \hfill \textit{[CRAFT] [SECRET]} \\
  Low wooden building where rice is fermented in clay jars sealed with red thread. The ninth jar is never opened. It contains the first batch, brewed when the village was founded. It is still bubbling. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Opening the ninth jar (Strength test DV 5) releases a spirit. The spirit offers a bargain: a year of good luck for a memory. Refusal ticks \textbf{Hollow Attention +2}.

\item \textbf{Well of the Ninth Stone} \hfill \textit{[WATER] [TOLL]} \\
  Stone-rimmed well with eight smooth stones around the edge. The ninth stone is missing. It was taken by the Hollow. If you place a new stone, the Hollow will leave an offering—a memory, a name, a tear. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Placing a stone (1 Supply) clears 1 segment of \textbf{Hollow Attention}. Taking the Hollow’s offering instead ticks \textbf{Hollow Attention +1} and gives the GM a String on you.

\item \textbf{Inn of the Unpoured Cup} \hfill \textit{[HOSPITALITY] [OMEN]} \\
  Traveller’s inn where the ninth seat is always empty. The seat is for the Hollow. Servers pour eight cups of tea. The ninth cup is left on the counter. It is always empty – and always warm. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Sitting in the ninth seat forces a Resolve test (DV 4). On failure, the Hollow visits your dreams; the GM gains 1 SB per PC.

\item \textbf{Bridge of the Missing Plank} \hfill \textit{[THRESHOLD] [DANGER]} \\
  Wooden bridge with eight planks. The ninth is missing—not broken, but never installed. The gap is exactly one foot wide. Step over it. Do not look down. The river below has no reflection. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Crossing requires a Body+Athletics test (DV 3). On a failure, you lose 1 Supply (dropped in the river). A critical failure draws the Hollow’s attention – tick \textbf{Hollow Attention +2}.

\item[J] \textbf{Cord-Maker’s Hut} \hfill \textit{[CRAFT] [MEMORY]} \\
  Small house where threads are spun from hemp, hair, and prayer. The spinner is blind. She has not seen the sun in forty years. She says the threads are her eyes now. The ninth spool is empty – reserved for the Hollow’s thread. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} She will weave a custom thread for 1 Supply and a lock of hair. The thread can be used as a one-time \textbf{Witness Cord} (enforce any oath).

\item[Q] \textbf{The Ninth Cairn} \hfill \textit{[DEATH] [TABOO]} \\
  Hidden mound behind the village, with no thread, no marker, no name. It is for promises that can never be kept. Do not touch it. If you must leave an offering, leave a single grain of rice – then walk away without looking back. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Touching the cairn forces a Resolve test (DV 5). On failure, you gain the Condition \textit{Debtor to the Ninth Cairn} (the Hollow will collect within a week). On success, the cairn whispers a forgotten name.

\item[K] \textbf{Elder’s Cord-House} \hfill \textit{[MEMORY] [AUTHORITY]} \\
  Longhouse where the village’s memory-cords are stored. Each cord is a generation. The oldest cord has nine knots, each from a different century. The ninth knot is loose. The Elder will not explain why. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} A character who unties the ninth knot sees a vision of the Hollow’s first visit. The GM must reveal a secret about the Hollow’s origin.

\item[A] \textbf{The Hollow’s First Thread (Myth)} \hfill \textit{[LEGEND] [ARTIFACT]} \\
  Red cord tied around a stone fox, hidden in a cave behind a waterfall. The cord has no knots – it is a single, endless loop. Touch it, and the Hollow will know your name. Untie it, and the Hollow will remember its own – and the world will change. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Untying the thread resets \textbf{Hollow Attention} to 0 but gives the GM 5 SB and a permanent String on the party. The Hollow becomes active in the region for the rest of the campaign.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Hearts --- People \& Factions}
\label{sec:eastern-aelaerem-people}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Rice-Paddy Keeper} \hfill \textit{[COMMUNITY] [WATER]} \\
  Weathered woman with red-stained hands and a straw hat. She knows which terrace to flood and which to drain by the way the thread tightens. She has never been wrong. The Hollow fears her – she once tied a thread around its finger. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} She lost a thread during a flood. If you retrieve it from the river, she will grant you a \textbf{Water Blessing} (once, ignore a drowning hazard). \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A day of labour in the paddies (Body+Athletics DV 3). Failure means you owe a story about water.

\item \textbf{Elder of the Woven Knot} \hfill \textit{[AUTHORITY] [DEATH]} \\
  Old man with no shadow. He sold it to the Hollow for a thread that would never break. He now ties cords for the dead. His fingers are raw bone. He does not feel pain. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} He knows the location of the Hollow’s First Thread. He will trade the secret for a promise – a promise you must tie in red thread. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A lock of hair from a person who has never broken an oath. He will not explain why.

\item \textbf{Blind Cord-Maker} \hfill \textit{[CRAFT] [TRUTH]} \\
  Woman who weaves threads by touch. She can feel a lie in the hemp – lies make the thread rough. She will not weave for liars. Her ninth spool is always empty, waiting for the Hollow’s order. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} She has a cord that can untie any promise. She will give it to someone who tells her a true secret about the Hollow. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A truthful confession of a small cruelty. She knots it into a cord.

\item \textbf{Stone Fox (Spirit)} \hfill \textit{[SPIRIT] [RIDDLE]} \\
  Living fox with red thread around its neck. It can speak, but only in riddles. It guards the mountain passes. If you feed it rice, it may let you pass. If you try to cut its thread, it will bite off your finger. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} It knows a shortcut through the passes. It will guide you if you answer three riddles. The riddles are always about thresholds. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A grain of rice from a harvest that failed. It will not say why.

\item \textbf{Wandering Thread-Seller} \hfill \textit{[TRADE] [MEMORY]} \\
  Traveller who carries a pack of knotted cords. Each cord contains a memory – a childhood face, a first kiss, a dead parent’s voice. She trades them for stories. She has nine cords left. She does not remember the ninth. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} She will sell you a cord for 1 Supply. Using it (cutting the knot) lets you experience that memory and gain +1 die on a related roll. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A story that she has not already tied. She will know if you lie.

\item \textbf{Hollow-Watched Child} \hfill \textit{[OMEN] [FEAR]} \\
  Child born with a red mark on their wrist – the Hollow’s touch. They can see the Hollow when it walks. Adults avoid them. The child knows which threads will break next. They never lie about it. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} They will warn you of the next \textbf{Hollow Attention} tick for free. If you give them a sweet (1 Boon), they will tell you which promise will break next. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A name. They will not say whose.

\item \textbf{Mountain Hermit} \hfill \textit{[WISDOM] [SILENCE]} \\
  Woman who lives alone in a cave, surrounded by knotted cords. She is tying a thread to keep the Hollow out of the valley. She has been tying for nine years. The thread is not yet long enough. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} She will teach you a \textbf{Thread-Ward} (once, ward a threshold against the Hollow for a night) in exchange for a day of silent meditation beside her. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A length of your own hair. She knots it into her thread. The Hollow will then know your scent.

\item \textbf{Sake Brewer} \hfill \textit{[TRADE] [SECRET]} \\
  Fat man with a brass bell on his belt. He brews rice wine in clay jars sealed with red thread. He never opens the ninth jar. He says it contains the first brew. He is lying – it contains the ashes of his daughter. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} He will trade a jar of his best sake (restores 1 Fatigue) for a story that makes him weep. He has not wept in nine years. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A name to carve on the ninth jar. He will not explain.

\item \textbf{Innkeeper} \hfill \textit{[HOSPITALITY] [WATCHFUL]} \\
  Woman who pours eight cups of tea for every guest. She never pours the ninth. If a guest pours it themselves, she throws them out. She has thrown out nine guests this year. The ninth guest is now the Hollow’s servant. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} She knows which travellers are marked by the Hollow. She will warn you for a cup of sake. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A promise that you will never pour the ninth cup in her inn. Break it, and she will set the geese on you.

\item[J] \textbf{Cairn-Watcher} \hfill \textit{[DEATH] [SILENCE]} \\
  Silent figure in grey, seen only at dusk. They tend the Ninth Cairn. They do not speak. They point. The direction they point is the direction the Hollow will walk tonight. If they point at you, run. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} They will not trade. They only warn. Giving them a coin (1 Boon) makes them point away from you – once. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} Nothing. They are not a person. They are a threshold.

\item[Q] \textbf{The First Thread’s Keeper (Ghost)} \hfill \textit{[LEGEND] [MEMORY]} \\
  Translucent figure in ragged clothes, sitting by the stone fox. She was the one who tied the first red thread. She does not remember why. She asks every traveller: ``What did I promise?'' No one knows the answer. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} If you give her a true answer, she will give you a fragment of the First Thread (see Diamonds). \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} A promise you have kept for nine years. She will knot it into her cord.

\item[K] \textbf{The Hollow’s Accountant (Oath-Keeper)} \hfill \textit{[DEATH] [JUSTICE]} \\
  Faceless figure in grey, carrying a ledger of red thread. It appears when a promise is broken. It does not speak – it writes a name in its book. That person will dream of the Hollow every night until they make amends. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} It cannot be bargained with. Only a true apology (a story that ends with the promise being kept) can erase the name. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} The name of the oath-breaker. It already knows.

\item[A] \textbf{The Unwound (Legend)} \hfill \textit{[PATRON] [ARTIFACT]} \\
  Figure made entirely of loose red thread, walking the mountain passes. It was once a person who cut every thread they ever tied. Now they are nothing but loose ends. They offer a deal: one unbreakable thread in exchange for all your memories. \\
  \textbf{What they carry:} They will give you a \textbf{Thread of Unbreaking} (see Diamonds) for a memory of your choice. The memory is gone forever. \\
  \textbf{What they ask:} Your name. They will not use it. They just want to know it.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Clubs --- Complications/Threats}
\label{sec:eastern-aelaerem-complications}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Thread Snaps} \hfill \textit{[DEATH] [BOUNDARY]} \\
  A boundary cord breaks. The Hollow crosses. For the next scene, all navigation rolls are Desperate – the path shifts. To repair, retie the thread with a promise. The promise must be spoken aloud. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Tick \textbf{Hollow Attention +1}. Repairing requires a Craft test (DV 4). On failure, the promise becomes a Burden – the GM gains a String on the speaker.

\item \textbf{Ninth Cup Poured (By Mistake)} \hfill \textit{[SOCIAL] [DEBT]} \\
  A stranger pours the ninth cup of sake. The room goes cold. A grey hand reaches from the shadow and takes the cup. The stranger now owes the Hollow a story. If they cannot tell one, they lose a memory. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} The stranger is a PC or an ally. They must tell a true story (Spirit+Performance DV 3) or lose 1 XP (refundable by a quest).

\item \textbf{Stone Fox’s Riddle} \hfill \textit{[RIDDLE] [THRESHOLD]} \\
  The fox blocks the pass. It asks: ``What is the number of the untying?'' The answer is not eight, not nine. It is silence. If you speak, the fox will not move for nine days. You must find another way. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Any character who speaks must test Spirit+Resolve (DV 4) or lose their voice for the scene. Silence is the only correct answer.

\item \textbf{Rice Paddy Floods} \hfill \textit{[DISASTER] [WATER]} \\
  The terrace wall collapses. The red thread loosens. The water is warm – not from the sun. Something is swimming in it. The villagers are afraid to look. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} The party must repair the wall (Craft test DV 5) or lose 1 Supply. On a failure, a water spirit emerges – treat as a Cap 2 adversary.

\item \textbf{Cord-Maker’s Refusal} \hfill \textit{[SOCIAL] [TRUTH]} \\
  The blind weaver refuses to make a thread for you. ``You carry a broken promise,'' she says. The broken promise is from your family’s past. You cannot leave until you name it. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} A character must confess a broken promise from their backstory (or invent one). The GM gains a String on that character. Refusal ticks \textbf{Hollow Attention +2}.

\item \textbf{Elder’s Shadow Returns} \hfill \textit{[FEAR] [DEATH]} \\
  The Elder sold his shadow years ago. Tonight, it returns. It knocks on the inn door. It asks: ``Is it time?'' No one knows what it means. If you answer wrong, it will take your shadow instead. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} A character who answers must test Spirit+Resolve (DV 5). On failure, they lose their shadow for a week (–1 die on stealth). On success, the shadow retreats and the Elder thanks you.

\item \textbf{Missing Stone} \hfill \textit{[WEIGHT] [TOLL]} \\
  The ninth stone at the well is gone. The Hollow has taken it. Without it, the well will dry. You must retrieve it from the Hollow – or offer a new stone carved with your own name. The Hollow will accept. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Offering a stone carved with your name clears \textbf{Hollow Attention} by 2 but gives the GM a permanent String. Retrieving the stone requires a quest into a barrow.

\item \textbf{Bridge Plank Appears} \hfill \textit{[THRESHOLD] [DANGER]} \\
  The missing ninth plank is suddenly there. It is warm to the touch. If you step on it, you will cross into the Hollow’s domain. The only way back is to tie a thread to the far side – but you have no thread. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Stepping on the plank transports the party to a nightmare version of the village. Escape requires a successful Spirit+Resolve test (DV 5) or offering a memory to the Hollow.

\item \textbf{Sake Jar Opens} \hfill \textit{[CURSE] [MEMORY]} \\
  The ninth jar is opened – by accident, by a child, by the wind. The sake inside is black. It smells of old grief. Anyone who drinks it forgets the face of someone they love. The jar cannot be resealed. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Drinking the sake gives +1 die to all rolls for one scene but erases one Bond (the loved one’s face). The bond can be restored by a quest.

\item[J] \textbf{Cairn Weeps Water} \hfill \textit{[OMEN] [DEATH]} \\
  The Ninth Cairn is wet. Water seeps from the stones. The water tastes of iron and salt – blood and tears. The Hollow is mourning. Someone is about to die. The cairn is weeping for them. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} The GM must name a character (PC or NPC) who will die within the next 24 hours unless the party intervenes. The water can be collected as a poison (Harm 1, ignores armour).

\item[Q] \textbf{Thread-Seller’s Last Cord} \hfill \textit{[MEMORY] [DEBT]} \\
  The wandering merchant offers you a cord for free. ``It contains a memory you have forgotten,'' she says. If you take it, you will remember. The memory is always painful. She cannot tell you why she is giving it away. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Taking the cord restores a lost memory (GM decides) but inflicts 1 Fatigue and gives the GM a String. The merchant vanishes after the exchange.

\item[K] \textbf{Hollow’s Ledger Page} \hfill \textit{[DEATH] [WEIGHT]} \\
  A page from the Hollow’s ledger is found on the road. Your name is written on it – twice. The second time is in red ink. That means the Hollow is coming for you. You have nine days to make amends. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} Start a \textbf{Hollow’s Claim [9]} timer. Each day, the player may attempt a Resolve test (DV 4) to delay. When the timer fills, the Hollow appears – treat as a Tier III adversary.

\item[A] \textbf{The First Thread Unties} \hfill \textit{[APOCALYPSE] [LEGEND]} \\
  The cord around the stone fox’s neck loosens. The fox speaks: ``The Hollow remembers its name.'' The ground trembles. The mountain passes close. The only way to re-tie the thread is to speak the Hollow’s true name – but no one knows it. \\
  \textbf{Mechanical hook:} The party must find the Hollow’s true name (a campaign arc) or the region becomes uninhabitable. Tick \textbf{Hollow Attention} to maximum – the Hollow now walks openly.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{(Threads/Stones/Cups)} Red-thread blessing (once, gain +1 Position on a stealth roll); cairn stone (delay the Hollow for one exchange); innkeeper’s cup (detect poison).

\section*{Diamonds --- Treasures \& Discoveries}
\label{sec:eastern-aelaerem-rewards}
\begin{enumerate}
\setcounter{enumi}{1}
\item \textbf{Red Thread (Knotted)} \hfill \textit{[TRAVEL] [ONCE]}  
  Simple cord with eight tight knots. Tie it around your wrist to gain +1 Position on a single Stealth or Survival roll. The thread frays after use. If it breaks, the Hollow will notice you.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} If the thread breaks while tied to you, tick \textbf{Hollow Attention +1}. The Hollow will watch your next camp.

\item \textbf{Cairn Stone} \hfill \textit{[PROTECTION] [ONCE]}  
  Smooth river stone from the Ninth Cairn. Carry it to make the Hollow hesitate for one exchange when it appears. The stone is warm. It cools after use. It cannot be used again.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} Using the stone alerts the Hollow to your presence. The GM gains 1 SB.

\item \textbf{Stone Fox’s Whisker} \hfill \textit{[DIVINATION] [ONCE]}  
  Single white hair from the fox. Whisper a question to it, and the fox will answer in a dream. The answer is always a riddle. The whisker burns to ash after use.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The fox will remember your face. The next time you meet, it will ask a riddle about a promise you broke.

\item \textbf{Brewer’s Ninth Seal} \hfill \textit{[STEALTH] [ONCE]}  
  Clay seal from the ninth sake jar. Press it onto any door or chest, and the lock will open – once. The seal then crumbles. The Hollow will know who opened it.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The Hollow will send a dream to the opener: a vision of the jar’s original owner. The GM gains 1 SB.

\item \textbf{Innkeeper’s Cup} \hfill \textit{[SOCIAL] [ONCE]}  
  Ceramic cup from the inn. Pour a drink into it, and the cup will tell you if the drink is poisoned. The cup’s glaze darkens if unsafe. The innkeeper will want it back – eventually.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The innkeeper will ask for a story about a death. If you cannot tell one, she takes the cup back immediately.

\item \textbf{Elder’s Memory-Cord} \hfill \textit{[KNOWLEDGE] [ONCE]}  
  Knotted cord containing a forgotten village secret. Untie a knot to learn one fact about the Hollow’s last visit. The cord is warm. It tightens if you lie about what you learn.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The Elder will ask for a secret in return. Refusal ticks \textbf{Hollow Attention +1}.

\item \textbf{Thread-Seller’s Story} \hfill \textit{[LEGEND] [ONCE]}  
  Small leather pouch containing a single knotted cord. The cord holds a true story about the Hollow. Speak it aloud to calm the Hollow for one night. The cord then dissolves.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The Hollow will listen to the story. The next time you break a promise, it will remind you of this story.

\item \textbf{Cord-Maker’s Spool} \hfill \textit{[CRAFT] [ONCE]}  
  Empty wooden spool. Bring it to the blind weaver, and she will tie a custom thread for you – one that will never break, for one specific purpose. She will ask for a lock of your hair as payment.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The thread is tied to your hair. If the purpose is not fulfilled within a year, the Hollow will claim the hair – and a memory.

\item \textbf{Rice Paddy Water} \hfill \textit{[WARD] [ONCE]}  
  Vial of water from a flooded terrace. Pour it onto a threshold to seal it against the Hollow for one hour. The water is warm. It smells of rice and old promises.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The Hollow will remember the scent of that threshold. The next time you cross it, the GM may spend 1 SB to have the Hollow appear.

\item[J] \textbf{Hollow’s Ledger Page (Blank)} \hfill \textit{[CURSE] [ONCE]}  
  Blank page from the Hollow’s book. Write a name on it, and that person will be marked – the Hollow will visit them within nine days. The page burns after writing. The Hollow will know your handwriting.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The Hollow will remember your handwriting. Any future promise you write may be scrutinized (the GM may ask for a Resolve test to avoid the Hollow’s attention).

\item[Q] \textbf{The First Thread’s Fragment} \hfill \textit{[LEGEND] [ONCE]}  
  Piece of the original red cord, found near the stone fox. Touch it to any threshold, and the Hollow cannot cross for one full day. The fragment then turns to dust. The fox will watch you use it.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The fox will ask you a riddle after use. If you cannot answer, the fragment’s protection is voided.

\item[K] \textbf{Stone Fox’s Promise} \hfill \textit{[TRAVEL] [ONCE]}  
  The fox owes you a favour. Once, you may call on it to guide you through the mountain passes. It will lead you safely – but it will ask a riddle in return. Answer wrong, and it will leave you lost.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} The fox will not protect you from the Hollow. If the Hollow appears during the journey, the fox will vanish.

\item[A] \textbf{The Unwound’s Thread} \hfill \textit{[ARTIFACT] [CURSE]}  
  Single red thread that never frays, never breaks, never loosens. It can tie any promise, seal any threshold, bind any oath. But it belongs to the Unwound. If you use it, they will come to collect – and they will ask for all your memories in exchange.  
  \textbf{Hollow cost:} Using the thread invokes the Unwound as a Patron. You gain +2 dice to all rolls involving promises, but each use erases a random memory (GM chooses). When all memories are gone, the Unwound consumes your identity.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:eastern-aelaerem-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = treasure. Highest rank sets the main Timer (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Treasures (Diamonds) are codified outcomes (threads, stones, cups, cords) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo thread-omens: a cord tightens without wind, a stone fox turns its head, a missing plank appears, the ninth cup pours itself. The Hollow is near.
\item Track the \textbf{Hollow Attention [8]} timer as a campaign-wide pressure. Use the \textbf{Hollow Walks} table when it reaches 8.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Rice & Thread:} Presenting a bowl of rice and a red thread at a threshold shifts your next social action one step safer while you remain a guest of that hearth. The rice must be from the host’s paddy.
\item \textbf{Mountain Courtesies:} When you keep local taboos (count ``eight-and-one,'' leave a grain of rice at the stone fox, never point at a cairn), cancel the first SB generated by rites or negotiations in that scene.
\item \textbf{Listen at the Cairn:} A quiet pause at a cairn yields a true omen – ask one short question about what the Hollow wants \emph{here and now}; the answer arrives as a sound, a smell, or a small animal’s behaviour.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Lore Echoes (Cross-Expansion Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Sealed Gate:} Some Aelaerem thresholds are marked with a nail from the Sealed Gate’s original shrines. A door with such a nail can be barred against any spirit for one night – but the nail must be removed before dawn, or the door will never open again.
  \item \textbf{The Savage Heart:} In the deepest hollows, the Hollow is said to wear a bear’s shape during the winter famine. Hedge-keepers leave raw meat at the boundary, not as an offering but as a treaty: ``Eat this, not our children.''
  \item \textbf{The Traveler:} Lantern-wardens sometimes find a gray pilgrim waiting at a stile at dusk. The pilgrim asks for directions to a village that no longer exists. Those who answer truthfully find that the next road they walk is half as long.
  \item \textbf{Ikasha, She Who Sleeps:} The Hollow is sometimes called Ikasha’s shadow – the part of her that did not fall asleep. Aelaerem children are taught never to whisper the name of the dreamer before bed, lest the Hollow mistake them for a messenger.
  \item \textbf{The Ninth:} On the ninth night of a feast, the empty chair is left for the Hollow. If a guest sits in it, the Hollow will take their place at the table. The feast will continue, but the guest will never be full again.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Eastern Aelaerem Superstitions (burn for +1 die once)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Rice to the Cairn:} Crumble a grain at the Ninth Cairn. Ignore the first \textit{Cairn Weeps Water} penalty.
  \item \textbf{Knot the Thread:} Tie a single knot in a red thread at a threshold. Cancel \textit{Cord-Maker’s Refusal} or take –1 die on your next oath (your choice).
  \item \textbf{Name to the Hollow:} Whisper a dead person’s name into an empty cup. Gain +1 Effect when invoking guest-right this scene, but mark \textit{Obligation} to the Pale Shepherd.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Boss Archetypes in Eastern Aelaerem}
\label{sec:eastern-aelaerem-bosses}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Scarecrow Regent (Omen/Threshold)} – A hedge-raised guardian wearing twelve charms and one wrong knot. \emph{Phases}: Stare of the Lane $\rightarrow$ Scare the Crows $\rightarrow$ Harvest of Faces. \emph{Cracks}: a \textbf{Guest-Loaf} offered to its post; a \textbf{Mummers’ License} to wear a mask it cannot read.
\item \textbf{The Hollow Bride (Otherworld/Hospitality)} – A Neighbor wearing a borrowed name and wedding veil. \emph{Phases}: Invitation Drifted $\rightarrow$ Feast of Shadows $\rightarrow$ Ninth Cup Poured. \emph{Cracks}: a \textbf{Shepherd’s Whistle} to call the dogs that guard the threshold; a \textbf{Lantern-Writ} to see her true face.
\item \textbf{Lantern Bailiff of the Moot (Justice/Order)} – Enforcer of hedge-law with warrant-nails and bell-keys. \emph{Phases}: Lantern Law $\rightarrow$ Midnight Moot $\rightarrow$ Binding Geas. \emph{Cracks}: a \textbf{Cider-Mark} to buy a night’s silence; a \textbf{Moot Oak bench} to appeal his ruling.
\item \textbf{Beekeeper of Winter Church (Sacred/Whisper)} – Keeper of cold honey and sleeping hymns. \emph{Phases}: Swarm Omen $\rightarrow$ Waxed Vow $\rightarrow$ Memory Tithe. \emph{Cracks}: a \textbf{Bee-Queen Share} to call her swarm to heel; a \textbf{Lantern} lit from a winter church candle.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Faction Entanglements}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Cairn-Watchers:} Honor cairn customs (leave a grain of rice, never point) or suffer +1 SB on each night march; bringing disputes to the Ninth Cairn reduces the next travel DV by 1.
  \item \textbf{Cord-Makers’ Guild:} Travel under a fresh red thread grants advantage crossing mountain passes; anger them (cut a thread without permission) and village gates close at dusk (Position –).
  \item \textbf{Rice-Paddy Wardens:} Donate rice or help repair terraces to earn an escort marker; ignore a flooded paddy and pay a ``water toll'' (first Survival roll each day suffers –1 die).
  \item \textbf{Stone Fox Circles:} Feast-days open mountain paths; snub an invitation and local guides double their fees until amends are made.
  \item \textbf{Neighbors (quiet powers):} Leave rice at the stone fox to cancel the first supernatural SB this leg; break taboo and the Hollow stirs (add a \emph{Hollow Attention} tick).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Cross-Regional Ties}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Sihai Influence:} Red-thread guest-right is recognised by some Sihai magistrates as proof of good faith. Reputation for keeping hearth-law grants +1 die to parley with Sihai clerks and merchants.
  \item \textbf{Nihori Sea-Lanes:} Beeswax, rice-brew, and red thread travel east to Nihori ports. Storm seasons bring Nihori traders inland – good silver, stricter contracts.
  \item \textbf{Ayokha Monsoon Caravans:} Caravans detour through Aelaerem valleys for safe night stops under thread-ward. Counterfeit thread tokens discovered here echo as distrust along Ayokha trade routes (Social DV +1 until cleared).
  \item \textbf{Valewood Fallout (Far West):} Moon-sap and dream pollen drift on certain winds: mountain cairns misalign, barrow-whispers grow bold. When \emph{Valewood Weather} is in effect, the first night omen is always true (good or ill).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Encounter Seeds (Quick Hooks)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{The Hollow Bride’s Invitation:} A wedding invitation arrives at the inn. The bride is not from any village on the map. The groom has not been seen in a week. The ninth chair at the feast is already occupied.
\item \textbf{The Scarecrow’s Harvest:} Crows are building a nest on the scarecrow’s shoulder. Inside the nest is a key. The key opens a door that should not exist.
\item \textbf{The Beekeeper’s Tithe:} The winter church’s bees have stopped producing honey. They are producing wax effigies of the PCs. The beekeeper wants to know why.
\item \textbf{The Missing Thread:} A boundary cord has been cut. The village is losing its shape – doors open to the wrong rooms, wells appear where there were none. The cord was cut by a child who wanted to see the Hollow.
\end{itemize}
