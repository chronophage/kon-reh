\textbf{Theme & Atmosphere}

In the eastern valleys, the Aelaerem tend terraced rice paddies bound with red thread. Their villages nestle under wooded peaks, and every threshold is a negotiation with the unseen. Their magic is housekeeping writ large‚Äîthresholds groomed, names jarred like pickled plums, the Hollow fed with silence and the ninth cup left unpoured. When the Neighbors stir beneath the mountain cairns, the folk answer with lantern law and the careful geometry of guest‚àíright.

Eastern Aelaerem share blood with their western kin but have adapted to monsoon rains, bamboo groves, and the proximity of Sihai and Nihori. They trade red thread for rice, stone fox charms for sake, and they have learned to count the Hollow's footsteps in the rhythm of falling water.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of rice porridge and old cedar. The way every terrace has a red thread tied at the water inlet. The silence of children when a stranger passes the village stone.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never refuse an offered cup. The Hollow counts refusals as invitations. Pour the ninth cup for the empty chair, and you may feed it ‚Äì or feed it.

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Warm Hearths, Cold Thresholds}

Here, the land is not wilderness ‚Äì it is a living weave of favors and omissions. The Aelaerem do not build walls; they build hedges, stiles, and the careful geometry of guest‚àíright. \subsection*{The Hollow‚Äôs Attention (Campaign Timer)}

\textbf{Hollow Attention [8]} ‚Äì Advances when:
\begin{itemize}
‚Ñπtem A character refuses an offered cup (+1)
‚Ñπtem A promise is broken on a threshold (+1)
‚Ñπtem The ninth cup is poured (+2)

At 8 segments: The Hollow manifests. Roll on the \textbf{Hollow Walks} table. The party must offer a story, a memory, or a name to appease it.
