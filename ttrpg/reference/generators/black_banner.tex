\section{Black Banners --- Condotta \& Crowns}
\label{chap:black-banners}

\subsection*{Quick Reference}
\textbf{Tagline:} \textit{A contract is only as good as the next payday.}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Banner-Captain (Elite):}  
  ``A contract is only as good as the next payday, and a grudge is only as sharp as the sword backing it. In this game, we sell steel by the season and vendettas by the year.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Veteran Sergeant (Commoner):}  
  ``I've seen three captains rise and fall in the time it takes to grow a beard, but the mud stays the same and the pay comes when it pleases. Best keep your own counsel and your blade sharp. The camp sings? You live. They stop singing? Even the best charter bleeds.''
\end{quote}

\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Mercenary realism, winter warfare, ledger-driven violence.

\textbf{System Type:} Portable overlay --- apply to any region (Acasia, Violette Steppe, Ecktorian borderlands). Not a fixed geography.

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Condotta \& Crowns}]
Ecktorian treasuries bankroll a perpetual border-war they claim to be \emph{containing}. In truth, the gold keeps the fire burning. The Black Banners are mercenary leagues --- ex-Utaran legionaries, second-sons, debt-dodgers, and exiles --- who sell steel to the highest bidder, today's ally becoming tomorrow's quarry. Between them move the Ykrul steppe-clans (swift, pragmatic, oath-canny) and the Vilikari hill tribes (crafty, patient, and proud). Ecktorian ministers call it ``pressure management.'' The companies call it a season.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of wet wool and old blood. The way every tent has a ledger pinned to its flap. The silence when the paychest is opened --- and the murmur when it's light.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never break a Bell Dawn. Three bells at sunrise means bury the dead, trade prisoners, fix the wells, no ambushes. Violating it is how legends end badly --- and how captains end hanging.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{The Theater of Banners}

Here, war is a marketplace. The Black Banners are not a single army but a squabbling league of freelance violence, bound by camp law, pay customs, and the shared knowledge that tomorrow's enemy might be today's employer. The real battles are fought in counting-tents over clauses and riders; the clash outside is just the audit. Loyalty is a coin that fluctuates with the market, and a captain's true skill lies in reading the political winds that blow across contested borders. Between the rigid honour codes of Ecktorian nobility and the fluid pragmatism of steppe clans and hill tribes, mercenaries navigate a dangerous middle ground where survival depends on knowing when to keep an oath and when to break one --- always at dawn, with a witness, and with surety paid.

\subsection*{Quickstart (Two-Minute Setup)}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Condotta Crossroads:} Put two conflicting charters on the table. Ask: \textit{Who do you disappoint first?}
    \item \textbf{Company Sheet:} Name, Colors, Captain. Add clocks: \textbf{Payday [4]}, \textbf{Supply [4]}, \textbf{Cohesion [6]}.
    \item \textbf{Pressure Clocks:} Pick two external threats (e.g., \textit{Skirmish Spiral [6]}, \textit{Camp Despair [6]}).
    \item \textbf{Allegiances in Flux:} At each scene start, roll 1d6 and apply the twist (see table).
    \item \textbf{Honor vs. Pragmatism Dial:} Slide based on choices; affects Position and logistics.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Company Mechanics (Visible Tracks)}

Every mercenary company (PC-led or NPC) tracks three clocks:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Payday [4]} – Missed pay, bad terms, seized chests. On fill: a squad deserts (lose 1 Supply) or a veteran sells secrets (GM gains 2 SB).
    \item \textbf{Supply [4]} – Weather, marches, sabotage. While Supply $\geq$ 3, all risky maneuvers start \textbf{Position –1} unless you bring a Diamond (scavenge, requisition).
    \item \textbf{Cohesion [6]} – Losses, betrayals, officer feuds. When Cohesion hits 1–2, the GM may convert the first 1 SB each scene into \textbf{Desertion, Brawl, or Refusal}.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Recovery:} A successful \textbf{Payline Drill} scene clears 1 Payday. A \textbf{Quartermaster Audit} clears 1 Supply. A \textbf{Rally \& Reform} roll (Presence+Command, DV 3) clears 1 Cohesion.

\subsection*{Allegiances in Flux (Scene Start, 1d6)}

At the start of every scene involving mercenaries, roll 1d6:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Condotta Flip} – Rival charter offers better terms. Accept: gain \textbf{Black Mark [1]} with old patron. Refuse: tick \textbf{Payday +1}.
    \item \textbf{Ykrul Counter-offer} – Accept an exit-rich but costly route: gain +1 Effect now, mark \textbf{Obligation [2]}. Refuse: tick \textbf{Payday +1}.
    \item \textbf{Vilikari Shortcut} – +1 Effect on this maneuver via night roads; GM gains 1 SB (hostile terrain later).
    \item \textbf{Ecktorian ``Correction''} – March under ``imperial guidance'': +1 Position this scene or take a \textbf{Black Mark} for defiance.
    \item \textbf{False Orders} – First command this scene suffers misinterpretation unless countered with a \textbf{Diamond} (cipher, cantor, witness).
    \item \textbf{Bannerless One} – A myth-wrapped figure clears 1 Payday – but starts \textbf{Price of Mercy [2]}. When it fills, she asks for a prisoner you'd rather keep.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Honor vs. Pragmatism Dial}

Place a two-step dial. Starting position: balanced (0).

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Honour} (left): +1 Position in parley/oathbound actions; logistics burn faster (tick \textbf{Supply} on a Partial where you'd normally be safe).
    \item \textbf{Pragmatism} (right): +1 Effect in ambush/sabotage; social rolls start one step worse Position when oaths/witnesses matter.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Centering Actions:} Pay arrears on-screen, return prisoners with witness, fulfill a broken writ at cost. Move the dial one step toward center.

\subsection*{GM Tools}

\paragraph{Field Procedures}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Colours \& Countersigns:} Declaring correct countersigns cancels the first \textbf{False Orders} SB each night; wrong signs worsen Position on first contact.
    \item \textbf{Payline Drill:} If a scene depicts orderly pay, clear 1 \textbf{Payday}. If pay is ``creative,'' clear it but mark \textbf{Black Mark} – future officers begin one step colder in parley.
    \item \textbf{Scavenge Doctrine:} After battles, gain 1 temporary Gear tag or advance \textbf{Supply} by 1; on a 1–2 on 1d6, spawn ``Honour vs. Pragmatism'' conflict immediately.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Boss Archetypes (Condensed)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Banner-Captain (Fixer General)} – Break chain-of-command clock [6] by flipping lieutenants, forging orders, or seizing the paychest.
    \item \textbf{Condotta Broker (Colour-Flipper)} – Expose a dual contract or force public arbitration under neutral bells to collapse their leverage track.
    \item \textbf{Quartermaster-Provost (Logistics Tyrant)} – Complete the \textit{Three Cuts} (road, ration, rumor) – any two stalls them; all three topple their supply engine.
    \item \textbf{Ykrul Raid-Mother (Oath-Weaver)} – Honour horse-rites to gain truce windows; break one braid in parley to end a phase.
    \item \textbf{Vilikari Hill-Fox (Ambush Architect)} – Clear \textit{Three High Stones} (overwatch, supply cache, signal cairn) to force even ground.
    \item \textbf{The Singing Wastes (Weapon-Host)} – Complete the \textit{Silencing Acts}: oil the choir (funeral honours), bury the standards, ring the wind-break bell – each lowers its Tier for one exchange.
\end{itemize}

\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In the Black Banners, power flows through contracts, paychests, and the weight of a reputation. A Banner-Captain's authority rests on the number of companies he commands; a Condotta Broker's on the thickness of his ledger. There is no loyalty, only the shared understanding that a broken contract costs more than a broken blade.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Power in the Black Banners is measured in payday clocks, broker's favours, and the length of a duel record. Patronage often takes the form of dual contracts, emergency requisitions, or the Bannerless Word – each tied to a specific company, broker, or debt. To emphasise the mercenary realism:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Use \textbf{Payday \& Supply Clocks} as visible pressure. Let players negotiate, raid, or pledge future services to clear ticks.
    \item Enforce \textbf{Allegiances in Flux} – every scene, loyalties may shift. The Keeper's SB pool grows when the party betrays a contract.
    \item Let \textbf{Honour vs. Pragmatism} be a dial the party can slide through their actions. Centre it by a costly act of mercy or a profitable betrayal.
    \item Track \textbf{Black Marks} as a persistent reputation score. Three Black Marks and no condotta broker will write a clean charter for you.
\end{itemize}
In the Black Banners, the camp sings when payday comes – and falls silent when the ledger is written in blood.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Final GM Mantra (Black Banners Edition)}

The camp sings when payday comes – and falls silent when the ledger is written in blood. Set \textbf{DV low}. Use \textbf{Allegiances in Flux} every scene. Let \textbf{Payday, Supply, and Cohesion} be visible. Spend \textbf{SB} as false orders, cut supply lines, or camp-fever. And remember: in the Black Banners, a broken contract costs more than a broken blade.
