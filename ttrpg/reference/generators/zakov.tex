\section{Zakov --- ``Salt \& Serpent''}
\label{chap:zakov}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Salt Prince (Elite):}  
  ``The tide here keeps better ledgers than any scribe, and the reefs remember every secret whispered above their depths. Power flows like the currents – those who understand them rule the waves.''
\end{quote}

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Dock-Rat (Commoner):}  
  ``The sewers and the sea both carry what folks want to forget, but they also remember everything. Best keep your business above water and your enemies guessing which dock you'll use next. The Salt Prince's crown floats, and the tide pulls for anyone with a sharp knife and a sharper tongue.''
\end{quote}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme \& Atmosphere}]
Zakov is the pirate‑city of the Dolmis coast, where tides trade secrets and knives settle debts. Its streets are piers, its courts are taverns, and its rulers are whoever the Syndicates fear most this season. The Salt Prince claims the throne, but the real power ebbs and flows with the tide—and the tide remembers everything. Here, every manifest is a lie, every harbor a doorstep to a dozen conspiracies, and every fog‑light a lure or a warning. The city breathes with the sea: at low tide, secrets exposed; at flood, the drowned walk the Sunken Quarter and knock on doors that should be locked. Zakov is not a place you visit. It is a place you survive—or rule.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of brine and tar. The constant slap of water against stone. The way every tavern has a door that leads to a different dock—and every dock has a name that changes with the tide.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never take a coin that tastes of brine. The Serpent's Mark is not a blessing. It is a debt that drowns.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Salt and Serpent}

Here, the sea is not a border—it is a landlord. Zakov rose from the wreckage of a sunken empire, and every stone is a salvage claim. The Salt Prince rules from a throne of driftwood and extortion, but the real lords are the Syndicate factors who own the docks, the eel‑cults that whisper from the sewers, and the fae courts that launder their influence through tide‑bargains. Ships arrive with goods no customs post will name, and depart with cargoes no manifest will show. The city's law is the Tide Clock: low water for killing, flood water for trading, slack for negotiation. Break the rhythm, and the city will break you.

\textbf{Starting Location:} A smuggler's gate built into the seawall, its iron mouth crusted with barnacles and secrets. The gatekeeper is a one‑eyed woman who accepts payment in stories. Behind her, the Dregs Quarter stretches on stilts into the harbor, and the Salt Prince's flagship, the \textit{Red Wake}, rides at anchor off the Iron Pier. “The levy rises tonight,” the gatekeeper says, spitting into the water. “Every ship pays, or every ship burns. You look like the burning type. Good. The Prince needs a reminder that the tide turns for everyone.”

\paragraph*{(Harbor/Bolt‑hole/Black Market)} Salt‑cracked wharf where tide brings back what law forgets; Bone‑yard beach with wrecks and corpses in kelp; Smuggler's Gate behind the Fish Market.

\section*{Quick use notes}
\label{sec:zakov-quick-use}
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw until you have all four suits: Spade = place, Heart = actor, Club = pressure, Diamond = leverage. Highest rank sets the main Clock (2–5 $\rightarrow$ 4, 6–10 $\rightarrow$ 6, J/Q/K $\rightarrow$ 8, A $\rightarrow$ 10).
\item Diamonds are codified outcomes (passes/contracts/goods) that change position rather than call for a roll.
\item If any A appears, echo \textbf{salt and serpent} motifs—tides that remember, reefs that shift, the deep that listens.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Tide Clock:} Scenes run on a four‑segment \textbf{Tide Clock}. Each advance shifts Position: \emph{Low tide} reveals hidden wrecks and bolt‑holes; \emph{Flood tide} drowns streets and seals doors. When the Clock turns, the Keeper may demand 1 SB tied to the waters.
\item \textbf{Syndicate Debt:} Every deal leaves a mark. Track a single \textbf{Debt} value (0–3). At 3, the Syndicate calls it in: a betrayal, a levy, an unwanted escort. Clear 1 Debt by paying a Diamond or sacrificing a useful contact.
\item \textbf{Whispers Deep:} Rumors carry teeth. When characters openly boast, bargain, or betray, roll 1d6: 1–3, Whispers spread, increasing SB generation; 4–6, Whispers favor the PCs, lowering suspicion until the tide turns.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage \& Power}]
In Zakov, power flows through the control of smuggling routes, the management of syndicate debts, and the ability to navigate the complex web of pirate politics. The Salt Prince maintains nominal authority, but real power lies with those who control the tides, the reefs, and the flow of information through the city's taverns and black markets. The true authorities are those who can provide safe passage, protect cargoes, and mediate between competing syndicates.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Zakov revolves around smuggling rights, safe harbors, and the ability to provide protection from both legal and illegal authorities. Rewards often take the form of tokens, charters, and safe passage that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize the city's fluid power:
\begin{itemize}
\item Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, manifests, keys) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided.
\item Let rival syndicates issue conflicting protections, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more.
\item Use the docks, taverns, and smuggling routes as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of custom and connections determines success.
\end{itemize}
In Zakov, your reputation is your wealth, and your wealth determines whether you rule the waves or feed the fishes.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{City Operating System (Fast Play)}

\paragraph{Tide-State (4)}
Track a \textbf{Tide Clock} (Low \textrightarrow{} Flood \textrightarrow{} Slack \textrightarrow{} Ebb).  
At each shift: 
\begin{itemize}
\item change \textbf{Position}: Low (+Infiltration), Flood (-Mobility), Slack (+Deals), Ebb (+Getaway).
\item Keeper may bank 1 SB tied to \emph{water, rot, or rumor}.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Heat \& Debt}
Track \textbf{Heat} (0--4) and \textbf{Debt} (0--3) citywide.
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Heat} rises on noisy jobs; at 4, a \emph{Syndicate Split} tick or \emph{Naval Blockade} rumor triggers.
\item \emph{Debt} rises when you stiff a faction; at 3, a creditor picks the next scene's complication.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{The Fae Leak}

\paragraph{Saltbound Rules}
Fae cannot \emph{enter} a circle salted with \textbf{tidewater salt}, but can \emph{bargain} across it. Breaking circle = 1 SB and a personal \emph{Mark}.

\paragraph{Eel Court}
Wharf-spirits (eel-hooded) adjudicate vows “where water hears.” Presenting a \textbf{Serpent’s Mark} lets you reroute a single clause in a contract once.

\paragraph{Drowned Names}
Names thrown from \emph{Bone-Yard Beach} return as \emph{Echo Debtors}. Cash one to erase 1 \textbf{Debt}—but the name occasionally speaks from your mouth.

\paragraph{Rope Court}
To settle a dispute without steel, each party knots a clause in one rope.  
Roll off; winner keeps their clause, loser’s knot is cut and their stake is forfeit.  
Spending \textbf{Serpent’s Mark} lets you retie one knot mid-judgment.
