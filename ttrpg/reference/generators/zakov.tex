\noindent\textbf{Genre / Mood:} Lovecraftian dread meets pirate swagger; eldritch tide−horror, maritime noir, pressure as a living force.

\noindent\textbf{Starting Location:} A smuggler's gate built into the seawall, its iron mouth crusted with barnacles and secrets. The gatekeeper is a one−eyed woman who accepts payment in stories. Behind her, the Dregs Quarter stretches on stilts into the harbor, and the Salt Prince's flagship, the \textit{Red Wake}, rides at anchor off the Iron Pier. ``The levy rises tonight,'' she says, spitting into the water. ``Every ship pays, or every ship burns. You look like the burning type. Good. The Prince needs a reminder that the tide turns for everyone. And keep your eyes off the water at low tide. The Reaping doesn't like being watched.''

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=blue!60!black,title={Theme & Atmosphere}]
Zakov is the pirate−city of the Dolmis coast, built on the drowned bones of an \textbf{Astroegro} port city that sank beneath the waves when that ancient empire fell. The old stone ruins — shattered columns, flooded plazas, sealed vaults — lie only a few fathoms down, and at low tide you can see their roofs breaking the surface like the spines of dead leviathans. The city's streets are built on the rubble, its piers driven into the wreckage, and its deepest cellars open into sunken chambers where the water is warm and still. The Astroegro are gone, but their secrets remain: crystalline shards that hum with a terrible patience, sealed doors that weep brine, and the slow, creeping corruption that the locals call the \textbf{Reaping}.

Zakov's streets are piers, its courts are taverns, and its rulers are whoever the Syndicates fear most this season. The Salt Prince claims the throne of driftwood and extortion, but the real power ebbs and flows with the tide — and the tide remembers everything. Here, every manifest is a lie, every harbor a doorstep to a dozen conspiracies, and every fog−light a lure or a warning. The city breathes with the sea: at low tide, secrets exposed; at flood, the drowned walk the Sunken Quarter and knock on doors that should be locked. Zakov is not a place you visit. It is a place you survive — or rule.

Beneath it all, the \textbf{Reaping}. Centuries ago, the first Salt Prince — then a common adventurer — dove into a submerged Astroegro vault off the coast. What he found there no one knows for certain. Some say it was a living crystal, a fragment of a god that fell into the sea. Others whisper it is the father of Khemesh, the first hunger. The oldest tales call it simply \textit{the Reaping}: a hunger that does not kill, but \textit{harvests}. The Reaping seeps into Zakov like groundwater, corrupting slowly, tainting every soul who stays too long. Dreams curdle into obsession. Generosity rots into expectation. The city's famous “everyone owes everyone” is not a culture; it is a symptom. The Salt Prince keeps the Reaping's source sealed beneath his palace, but the seal is cracking, and the crystal grows.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{What you notice first:} The smell of brine and tar. The constant slap of water against stone. The way every tavern has a door that leads to a different dock — and every dock has a name that changes with the tide. The faint, harmonic hum that comes from the deep, like a bell that never stops ringing.

\textbf{The one rule that kills newcomers:} Never take a coin that tastes of brine. The Serpent's Mark is not a blessing. It is a weight that drowns. And never look into a still pool at low tide. The Reaping looks back.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Regional Mood: Salt, Serpent, and the Crystalline Deep}

Here, the sea is not a border — it is a landlord. Zakov rose from the wreckage of a sunken Astroegro port, and every stone is a salvage claim. The Salt Prince rules from a throne of driftwood and extortion, but the real lords are the Syndicate factors who own the docks, the eel−cults that whisper from the sewers (descended from Astroegro cultists who worshipped the deep), and the fae courts that launder their influence through tide−bargains. Ships arrive with goods no customs post will name, and depart with cargoes no manifest will show. The city's law is the Tide Timer : low water for killing, flood water for trading, slack for negotiation. Break the rhythm, and the city will break you.

The Reaping is not a myth. It is a presence. It manifests as a crystalline growth that spreads through the submerged ruins, leaching color from stone and warmth from water. Those who stay in Zakov too long begin to dream of drowning; their shadows grow sharp and angular; their bargains feel heavier. The Reaping does not demand worship. It demands \textit{attention}. And it has been waiting for centuries.

\subsection*{The Reaping — A Campaign Timer }
łabel{sec:zakov−reaping}

The Reaping is not a monster to be fought. It is a pressure, a corruption, a slow unmaking of the soul. Track the party's entanglement with it using a \textbf{Reaping's Corrosion timer [8]}. Tick the timer when:
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem A character accepts a bargain from the Salt Prince or a Syndicate factor.
  ℹtem A character uses a \textbf{Serpent's Mark} (see Diamonds).
  ℹtem A character fails a Resolve test while in Zakov (drowning dreams, crystalline whispers).
  ℹtem The party lingers in the Sunken Quarter or the Serpent's Spine for more than a scene.
\end{itemize}
\noindent At \textbf{4 segments}: the character gains the \textit{Crystalline Whisper} condition (you hear the Reaping's voice in quiet moments; –1 die to resist fear). Their shadow begins to shimmer faintly. At \textbf{8 segments}: the character becomes \textbf{Harvested}. Their shadow detaches and walks into the sea, and they owe the Reaping a name (a true name of someone they love, or their own). They may still act, but they are marked. The GM may spend 2 SB to have a \textbf{Collector} — a crystalline spirit in the shape of a drowned sailor — appear and demand payment. To clear the timer, the party must:
\begin{itemize}
  ℹtem Offer the Reaping something it values more than a soul: a memory of a crime no one witnessed, a true name from the Astroegro era (found in the sunken vaults), or a shard of its own prison (a heist into the Salt Prince's vault).
  ℹtem Perform a deep−dive ritual at the Serpent's Spine, cutting a crystalline growth from the reef and replacing it with a lock of hair from a willing volunteer.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Geographic Integration}

Zakov sits at the cultural and maritime crossroads of the eastern Dolmis. To the north, the \textbf{Valewood}'s mist−shrouded coasts send driftwood and nightmares down the current; the Reaping's crystalline hum is said to be an echo of the forest's deepest hungers. To the south, the \textbf{Theona} isles regard Zakov with wary tolerance — the Ninth Taboo keeps the Green Host at bay, but Zakov's smugglers know which coves avoid the Host's riders. Across the Dolmis to the east, the gnomish \textbf{Aelinnel} of Aevrossa trade fine copper and stranger goods through Zakov's black market, their counting−songs an eerie counterpoint to the Reaping's harmonic pulse. The halfling \textbf{Aelaerem} of Amedell keep a wary distance, their red−thread wards flickering when the crystal grows strong.

The city's lifeblood is the narrow sea−lanes between the Dolmis and the Amaranthine. \textbf{Thepyrgos} controls the straits with its chain−towers and bell−warning systems, while \textbf{Kahfagian} patrols enforce tariffs on the open water. Zakov's pirates and smugglers must bribe, bluff, or blast past both to reach the rich southern waters. The \textbf{Linns} of the northern fjords sometimes portage their longships south from the Aberderrin into the Dolmis, trading amber and walrus ivory for Zakov's more questionable cargoes — and leaving behind storm−oaths that the local Syndicates are careful not to break.

\subsection*{A Commoner's View of Zakov}

\noindent\textit{A conversation overheard at the Crow's Roost, between an old net−mender and a newly arrived deckhand.}

\begin{quote}
``You ask what folks eat for breakfast? Salt fish and hard cheese, same as anywhere. The fish is dried on the docks, the cheese is smuggled from the mainland. The cheese is always stale. The fish is always fresh. Don't ask why. That's Zakov.

``The children? They learn to read the tide tables before they learn their letters. Not the water — the \textit{tally}. Who owes who, which dock's safe, which captain's about to be ''retired.'' A child who can't read the tide is a child who'll be caught by the flood. The flood ain't water. It's the Syndicate. They collect at high tide.

``First thing you notice about a stranger? Their coin−pouch. Fresh−minted coin means a merchant. Salt−crusted bits? Pirate. Empty pouch? Liar. Liars don't last long in Zakov. The sea takes 'em.

``Most common crime? Piracy — but that's a profession. The Syndicate licenses privateers. They pay a tithe. The tithe funds the harbor. The harbor protects the ships. The ships are protected from pirates. The pirates are the Syndicate. Beautiful, ain't it?

``No one talks about the Salt Prince, but everyone knows: he ain't a person. The title's held by a ring — a band of black coral passed from captain to captain for three centuries. The ring's cursed. The wearer can't sleep, can't die, can't leave Zakov. The current Prince has worn it for forty years. He hasn't slept in forty years. He hasn't smiled in forty years. He hasn't left the palace in forty years.

``Where do the dead go? To the Sunken Quarter — the drowned district beneath the harbor. Weighted with chains and dropped into the canals. The canals empty into the sea. The sea returns the bodies at low tide. The Syndicate collects 'em. Sells 'em to the Salt Prince's collectors. What do they use 'em for? No one asks. No one wants to know.

``The biggest festival? The Rising of the Tides. Summer solstice. The harbor fills with luminescent algae. Glows green. They call it the Kraken's Eye. The Kraken's said to rise on this night. Hasn't risen in living memory. The Kraken's patient. It's waiting.

``And the secret that could destroy a powerful person? The Pirate Queen is a spy. Planted by the Kahfagian Admiralty forty years ago. Her mission was to destabilize the Syndicate. She's \textit{become} the Syndicate. The Admiralty still sends her orders. She burns 'em. Drinks the ashes in her tea. That's Zakov for you.''
\end{quote}

\subsection*{Additional Features}
łabel{sec:zakov−features}

\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Tide Timer :} Scenes run on a four−segment \textbf{Tide Timer } (Low $→$ Flood $→$ Slack $→$ Ebb). Each advance shifts Position: \emph{Low tide} reveals hidden wrecks and bolt−holes; \emph{Flood tide} drowns streets and seals doors. When the Timer turns, the Keeper may demand 1 SB tied to the waters.
ℹtem \textbf{Reaping's Corrosion (Timer [8]):} Tracks the slow corruption of the party's souls. See §\ref{sec:zakov−reaping} for full mechanics.
ℹtem \textbf{Syndicate Weight:} Every deal leaves a mark. Track a single \textbf{Weight} value (0–3). At 3, the Syndicate calls it in: a betrayal, a levy, an unwanted escort. Clear 1 Weight by paying a Diamond or sacrificing a useful contact.
ℹtem \textbf{Whispers Deep:} Rumors carry teeth. When characters openly boast, bargain, or betray, roll 1d6: 1–3, Whispers spread, increasing SB generation; 4–6, Whispers favor the PCs, lowering suspicion until the tide turns.
ℹtem \textbf{Local Patrons (Syncretic Names):}

    ℹtem \textbf{The Kraken's Father} — Khemesh. \textit{The pressure beneath the keel; the weight the sea collects.}
    ℹtem \textbf{The Tide Father} — The Traveler. \textit{The current that carries you home — or to the reef.}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3,colframe=black!40!white,title={Patronage & Power}]
In Zakov, power flows through the control of smuggling routes, the management of syndicate weights, and the ability to navigate the complex web of pirate politics. The Salt Prince maintains nominal authority, but real power lies with those who control the tides, the reefs, and the flow of information through the city's taverns and black markets. The true authorities are those who can provide safe passage, protect cargoes, and mediate between competing syndicates.

\vspace{2mm}
\textbf{The Reaping changes everything.}  
Whoever controls the crystalline shard — or understands its nature — holds a weapon that could unmake the city or save it. The Salt Prince keeps the Reaping's location secret, but the Syndicates whisper that he is no longer its master. The Reaping has begun to act on its own. It reaches through the tide, through the dreams of the drowned, through the taste of brine in a smuggler's coin. Zakov is not ruled by the Prince. It is ruled by a hunger that has been patient for centuries. The Astroegro sealed it. The first Salt Prince bargained with it. Now the seal cracks, and the crystal grows.

\textbf{For the GM:}  
Patronage in Zakov revolves around smuggling rights, safe harbors, and the ability to provide protection from both legal and illegal authorities. Rewards often take the form of tokens, charters, and safe passage that can be leveraged into greater influence. To emphasize the city's fluid power and the creeping horror of the Reaping:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Tie rewards to visible symbols (tokens, manifests, keys) that can be challenged, stolen, or voided — and that may carry a trace of crystal.
ℹtem Let rival syndicates issue conflicting protections, forcing players to choose whose favor matters more — and whose weights are safer.
ℹtem Use the docks, taverns, and smuggling routes as arenas for social contests, where knowledge of custom and connections determines success — and where a character's shadow may betray them.
ℹtem Track \textbf{Reaping's Corrosion} as a campaign timer. Its advance is the city's slow death. The players can delay it, redirect it, or even embrace it — but they cannot ignore it. The Reaping is patient. It has been waiting since the Astroegro fell.
\end{itemize}
In Zakov, your reputation is your wealth, and your wealth determines whether you rule the waves or feed the fishes. But beneath the waves, the Reaping feeds on reputation too. And it is very, very hungry.
\end{tcolorbox}
