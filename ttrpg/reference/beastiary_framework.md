# Fate's Edge Beastiary Framework

## Core Beastiary Philosophy

In Fate's Edge, creatures are not just stat blocksâ€”they're narrative elements that serve the story. Each creature should:
- Advance the fiction through meaningful consequences
- Reflect the themes of their region/origin
- Create interesting tactical and social choices
- Generate Story Beats that enhance rather than punish player agency

## Beast Creation Template

### Basic Structure
```
[CREATURE NAME]
Tier: [1-5, corresponds to Cap rating]
Element: [Primary magical element]
Tags: [KEYWORD descriptors]
Threat Level: [Minor/Moderate/Severe/Critical]

Description: [2-3 sentences of flavor text]
Habitat & Behavior: [Where found, typical behavior patterns]
Lore Significance: [Cultural/mythic importance]

Combat Profile:
- Primary Attack: [DV, Effect, Special traits]
- Defenses: [Resistances, vulnerabilities, special defenses]
- Tactics: [Preferred combat approach]

Narrative Hooks:
- SB Spends: [Region-specific complications]
- Bonds/Relationships: [How they interact with factions/PCs]
- Rewards: [What can be gained from them]
```

## Sample Creatures by Region

### Acasia - Broken Marches

#### Thornback Wraith [Tier 2]
**Element:** Death/Shadow
**Tags:** Undead, Curse-Bearer, Territorial
**Threat Level:** Moderate

**Description:** Once travelers who died on Acasian roads, these translucent figures drag chains of thorns that never rust. They appear at crossroads where oaths were broken.

**Habitat & Behavior:** Haunt crossroads and abandoned bridges, especially where the Pale Causeway intersects old imperial roads. They're drawn to broken promises and unpaid debts.

**Lore Significance:** Embodiment of Acasia's curse-magic; their presence indicates areas where the land itself remembers betrayal.

**Combat Profile:**
- Primary Attack: Wits + Shadow DV 3, Effect: Harm 1 + Mark target with "Thorn Debt" (ongoing -1 die to social until resolved)
- Defenses: Immune to non-magical weapons, Vulnerable to honest oaths
- Tactics: Prefer to curse targets rather than deal direct harm; escalate to physical attacks only when disrespected

**Narrative Hooks:**
- SB Spends: Target's past oaths questioned publicly; crossroads become magically confusing; thorn-marked items appear in belongings
- Bonds/Relationships: Serve the Cursed Child of Silkstrand; despised by Hedge-witches
- Rewards: Thorn-seeds (can be used for curse-breaking rituals), knowledge of broken pacts

#### Blackwood Matriarch [Tier 4]
**Element:** Earth/Death
**Tags:** Fey, Territorial, Coven-Leader
**Threat Level:** Severe

**Description:** Ancient hag-like beings who rule the Blackwood territories, their skin bark-textured and eyes like amber sap. They speak in layered voices and weave living traps from thorn-vines.

**Habitat & Behavior:** Deep within charcoal-burned forests, commanding covens of 3-7 lesser fey. They're patient negotiators but merciless when crossed.

**Lore Significance:** Gatekeepers between civilized lands and fae territories; their favor allows safe passage through cursed woods.

**Combat Profile:**
- Primary Attack: Spirit + Nature DV 4, Effect: Harm 2 + Entangle (Position worsens by one step)
- Defenses: Regeneration (clear 1 Harm/round), Vulnerable to iron weapons
- Tactics: Control battlefield through terrain manipulation; summon 1-2 lesser fey allies per round

**Narrative Hooks:**
- SB Spends: Forest paths shift to trap the unwary; allies become suspicious of each other; thorn-vines grow through gear
- Bonds/Relationships: Rivalrous with Margravine's agents; trade with Mummers' companies
- Rewards: Blackwood amber (valuable trade good), fey-touched seeds, safe-conduct through cursed forests

### Aelaerem - Hearth & Hollow

#### Bell-Ward [Tier 1]
**Element:** Air/Fate
**Tags:** Construct, Guardian, Ritual-Bound
**Threat Level:** Minor

**Description:** Animated scarecrows with brass bells for joints, they patrol the edges of Aelaerem settlements. Their movements create haunting melodies that warn of approaching danger.

**Habitat & Behavior:** Boundary markers and perimeter patrols around villages. Generally non-hostile to those who respect local customs.

**Lore Significance:** Physical manifestation of Aelaerem hospitality law; their presence indicates areas under community protection.

**Combat Profile:**
- Primary Attack: Body + Guard DV 2, Effect: Harm 1 + Ring Alarm (summon 1d4 additional bell-wards)
- Defenses: Immune to fear, Vulnerable to silence effects
- Tactics: Prefer to warn rather than attack; work in groups of 3-5

**Narrative Hooks:**
- SB Spends: Bells ring false warnings; community becomes suspicious; hospitality is withdrawn
- Bonds/Relationships: Serve Hearth-Mothers; guided by Lantern-Wardens
- Rewards: Bell fragments (used in protection rituals), community favor tokens

### Aeler - Crowns & Under-Vaults

#### Deep Archive Sentinel [Tier 3]
**Element:** Earth/Fate
**Tags:** Construct, Guardian, Knowledge-Bound
**Threat Level:** Moderate

**Description:** Stone golems carved with runic equations, they guard the deepest vaults where ancient knowledge is stored. Their eyes glow with calculation as they assess threats to the accumulated wisdom.

**Habitat & Behavior:** Deep within vault-complexes, particularly around Measure Vaults and Reliquary Arcades. They're absolutely loyal to scholarly pursuits but hostile to thieves.

**Lore Significance:** Embodiment of Aeler obsession with precision and preservation of knowledge.

**Combat Profile:**
- Primary Attack: Body + Guard DV 3, Effect: Harm 2 + "Calculate Weakness" (next attack against target gains +1 Effect)
- Defenses: Immune to poison/mental effects, Vulnerable to incorrect measurements
- Tactics: Analyze opponents before attacking; call for backup from other constructs

**Narrative Hooks:**
- SB Spends: Vault security systems activate; measurements become impossible; knowledge becomes contradictory
- Bonds/Relationships: Serve Vault Wardens; respected by Oath-Keeper orders
- Rewards: Fragment of ancient calculations, access to restricted knowledge, measure-rod fragments

### Mistlands - Bells, Salt, and Breath

#### Pall-Hare [Tier 1]
**Element:** Air/Death
**Tags:** Undead, Omen-Bearer, Swift
**Threat Level:** Minor

**Description:** Ghostly white rabbits with bells for eyes, they appear on the edges of settlements during times of crisis. Seeing one running toward you is considered an omen of impending doom.

**Habitat & Behavior:** Appear at twilight near Pall Watch-towers and Bell-Line levees. They're not directly hostile but their presence heralds dangerous events.

**Lore Significance:** Messengers of the Mistlands' death-cult; their appearance predicts significant losses or changes.

**Combat Profile:**
- Primary Attack: Wits + Omen DV 2, Effect: Mark target with "Death's Attention" (ongoing SB generation)
- Defenses: Intangible, Can only be harmed by salt weapons
- Tactics: Prefer to flee rather than fight; appear in groups of 2-5 during major threats

**Narrative Hooks:**
- SB Spends: Death omens become literal; community panics; pall-bearers arrive prematurely
- Bonds/Relationships: Serve the Volva of Mist; feared by Bell-Wardens
- Rewards: Pall-bell fragments (used in death-warding), prophetic dreams, community preparedness

### Valewood - Empire Under Leaves

#### Echo-Legionary [Tier 3]
**Element:** Death/Fate
**Tags:** Undead, Military, Eternal
**Threat Level:** Moderate

**Description:** Ghostly soldiers in tattered imperial armor, they endlessly patrol the ruins of Lethai roads. Their marching feet make no sound, but their armor whispers of ancient campaigns.

**Habitat & Behavior:** Walk eternal patrol routes along Star-Roads and through Breathing Cities. They're mindlessly obedient to their forgotten orders.

**Lore Significance:** Remnants of the Lethai empire's military might; their presence indicates areas of strong imperial magic.

**Combat Profile:**
- Primary Attack: Body + Military DV 3, Effect: Harm 1 + "Phantom Formation" (gain +1 die when 3+ present)
- Defenses: Immune to fear/charm, Vulnerable to commands from imperial authority
- Tactics: Fight in military formations; call for reinforcements from nearby ruins

**Narrative Hooks:**
- SB Spends: Military discipline spreads to allies; imperial ghosts appear; patrol routes become dangerous
- Bonds/Relationships: Serve the Hazel Queen reluctantly; opposed by Antler-Hunters
- Rewards: Imperial coin (valuable to collectors), military knowledge, temporary military precision

### The Ways Between - Spiritways & Veilways

#### Threshold Warden [Tier 4]
**Element:** Fate/Threshold
**Tags:** Outsider, Guardian, Liminal
**Threat Level:** Severe

**Description:** Tall, faceless figures in robes that seem to be made of layered doorways. They carry keys that can open any threshold and close any path permanently.

**Habitat & Behavior:** Appear at crossroads, doorways, and any liminal space. They're neutral guardians who ensure the proper functioning of boundaries between worlds.

**Lore Significance:** Keepers of the fundamental structure that separates reality from the Ways Between.

**Combat Profile:**
- Primary Attack: Spirit + Gate DV 4, Effect: Harm 2 + "Lock Target" (Position worsens by two steps)
- Defenses: Can phase through attacks, Vulnerable to keys from their own realm
- Tactics: Prefer negotiation to combat; can banish opponents to random locations

**Narrative Hooks:**
- SB Spends: Doors/paths become one-way; boundaries seal unexpectedly; teleportation becomes dangerous
- Bonds/Relationships: Serve the Wayfinder; respected by Pathweavers
- Rewards: Way-keys (open/close any non-magical door), path-mark tokens, safe passage guarantees

## Beast Creation Guidelines

### Tier Ratings
- **Tier 1 (Cap 1):** Minor threats, easily handled by most characters
- **Tier 2 (Cap 2):** Standard encounters, require attention but not extreme tactics
- **Tier 3 (Cap 3):** Significant threats, can challenge veteran parties
- **Tier 4 (Cap 4):** Major threats, require coordinated efforts and planning
- **Tier 5 (Cap 5):** Epic threats, campaign-defining encounters

### Element Integration
Each creature should have a primary element that influences:
- Their appearance and abilities
- Their weaknesses and resistances
- The type of Story Beats they generate
- Their cultural significance

### Regional Flavor
Creatures should reflect their home region's themes:
- **Acasia:** Curses, feuds, broken promises
- **Aelaerem:** Hearth magic, hospitality, seasonal cycles
- **Aeler:** Precision, tradition, underground spaces
- **Mistlands:** Death, boundaries, salt and bells
- **Valewood:** Ancient empire, fae magic, transformation
- **Ways Between:** Thresholds, liminality, path magic

### Narrative Integration
Every creature should offer:
- Multiple ways to deal with them (combat, social, ritual)
- Story Beats that enhance rather than punish player creativity
- Rewards that tie into the broader campaign themes
- Relationships with factions and other creatures

This framework allows for creatures that serve the narrative while providing tactical variety and meaningful choices for players.
