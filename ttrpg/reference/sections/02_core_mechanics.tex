\chapter{Core Mechanics}\label{chap:core_mechanics}

\section{The Core Resolution Cycle}\label{sec:resolution_cycle}

When a player rolls dice, they are not simply trying to beat a number. They are engaging the world through risk, consequence, and discovery.

\subsection{Step‑by‑Step Resolution}\label{subsec:steps}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Declare Action \& Approach:} The player states their intent and the Attribute + Skill pair they will use.%
          \index{Resolution!declare action}
    \item \textbf{Set Difficulty Value (DV):}\index{Difficulty Value (DV)} The GM sets the DV based on narrative stakes, not simulationist difficulty.
    \item \textbf{Roll the Dice Pool:} Roll a number of d10s equal to Attribute + Skill (see \autoref{sec:dice_pool_cap} for the maximum).
    \item \textbf{Count Successes and Story Beats:}%
          \index{Resolution!count successes}
          \begin{itemize}
              \item Each die showing \textbf{6–10} counts as one success. A die showing \textbf{10} counts as \textbf{two successes}.
              \item Each die showing \textbf{1} generates one \textbf{Story Beat (SB)} for the GM.
          \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Check Against DV:} Compare the number of successes to the DV and apply the Outcome Matrix (\autoref{tab:outcome_matrix}).
    \item \textbf{Spend Story Beats:} The GM spends or banks SB as described in \autoref{sec:sb_spend_menu}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Difficulty Value (DV)}\label{subsec:difficulty_value}

DV measures the weight of the narrative stakes, not the intrinsic difficulty of a task.

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Default DV Ladder, colback=white!97!gray, colframe=black!80!gray, sharp corners]
\begin{center}
\begin{longtable}{c p{3.5in}}
\toprule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Typical Case} \\
\midrule
2 & Routine: clear intent, modest stakes, controlled environment \\
3 & Pressured: time limits, mild resistance, partial information \\
4 & Hard: hostile conditions, active opposition, precision required \\
5+ & Extreme: stacked constraints, dangerous failure, high drama \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

\noindent When in doubt, start at DV 3.

\subsection{Position \& Effect}\label{subsec:position_effect}

\begin{description}
    \item[Position (risk)]\index{Position} – \textbf{Dominant} (safe), \textbf{Controlled} (balanced), \textbf{Desperate} (high stakes).
    \item[Effect (reward)]\index{Effect} – \textbf{Limited} (small), \textbf{Standard} (expected), \textbf{Great} (big).
\end{description}
The GM sets both Position and Effect based on the player’s approach and the current fiction.

\subsection{Critical Success (10s)}\label{subsec:critical_success}

If the roll succeeds, each \textbf{10} on the dice adds additional narrative weight:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{One 10:} Strong success – improved Position or a minor advantage.
    \item \textbf{Two 10s:} Exceptional success – the player may choose two benefits (e.g., increased Effect and a free Boon).
    \item \textbf{Three 10s:} Legendary success – decisive victory; clear one segment from a relevant clock.
    \item \textbf{Four or more 10s:} Mythic success – reshape the scene; clear 1–2 clock segments.
\end{itemize}
Critical success effects apply only if the roll succeeds. \textbf{Ten (10) are never rerolled} by Position or other effects.

\subsection{Dice Pool Limit and Auto‑Successes}\label{sec:dice_pool_cap}

The maximum number of dice a character may roll for a single action is \textbf{10d10}. Any additional dice that would be added beyond this limit are converted into \textbf{automatic successes} (each extra die becomes one success, without being rolled and without generating Story Beats).

\subsection{Boon Economy}\label{subsec:boon_economy}\index{Boons}

\paragraph{Earning Boons}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Partial} ($0 <$ successes $<$ DV): Gain 1 Boon.
    \item \textbf{Miss} (successes $=0$): Gain 2 Boons (only on a meaningful roll that generates at least one Story Beat).
    \item \textbf{Bond‑driven action:} Once per bond per session, when you act meaningfully on a Mutual Bond and provide an Intricate Description, gain 1 Boon.
    \item \textbf{GM award:} For creative solutions, sacrifices, or strong narrative play.
\end{itemize}
\noindent Boons are not awarded for safe rehearsal, null‑risk probes, or repeated identical attempts in the same scene.

\paragraph{Spending Boons}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reroll a single die (after seeing the result; keep the second outcome).
    \item Activate an on‑screen Asset (1 Boon).
    \item Improve Position by one step before a roll.
    \item Power certain Rites or abilities as described in their text.
    \item Convert 2 Boons $\rightarrow$ 1 XP (once per session during downtime, max 2 XP per session via conversion).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Carryover Limit}
A character may hold a maximum of \textbf{5 Boons} at any time. At the end of each scene, reduce held Boons to a maximum of 2 (excess are lost).

\subsection{Story Beats (SB)}\label{subsec:story_beats}\index{Story Beats}

Every die that shows a \textbf{1} generates one Story Beat for the GM. \textbf{Rerolling a 1 does not erase its SB}; if the reroll also shows a 1, it generates an additional SB.

\section{Player‑Managed Modules}\label{sec:player_managed_modules}

Players are responsible for tracking their own character‑facing resources (Obligation, Corruption, Leash, Asset States). This reduces GM overhead and keeps the table moving.

\textbf{Module} & \textbf{Owner} & \textbf{Tick Triggers (examples)} \\
\midrule

Corruption Clock & Player & Cantor Push, performing a Resonant Rite, GM spending SB on occult actions. \\
Leash (Summoning) & Player & Spirit takes Harm, acts against its nature, crosses a \texttt{[WARD]}. \\
Asset State (Symbol) & Player & Maintenance neglect, \textit{Crack the Seal} $\rightarrow$ Compromised. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{tcolorbox}

\paragraph{Obligation Capacity}
\[
\text{Obligation Capacity} = \text{Spirit} + \text{Presence}
\]
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Over capacity:} Mark +1 Fatigue per segment over capacity.
    \item \textbf{Double capacity:} Clear all Fatigue, mark +1 Harm, and a \textbf{Patron Intrusion} occurs (the GM introduces a narrative demand).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Corruption Full (Cantor)}
Apply the last Patron’s thematic boon and burden (see Patron corruption tables). Then reset the Corruption Clock to zero.

\paragraph{Leash Full (Summoner)}
The spirit acts once according to its nature (the GM decides the action), then departs or turns hostile.

\paragraph{Symbol State (Invoker)}
A Symbol becomes \textsc{Compromised} (e.g., from \textit{Crack the Seal}). It can be repaired during downtime with a test (DV 3) or by spending 1 XP.

\section{Turn Economy}\label{sec:turn_economy}

Each character may take \textbf{1 Action and 1 Move} on their turn. Actions and Moves may be taken in any order; repeating the same Action is not allowed unless noted.

\begin{description}
    \item[Move] Traverse up to one range band (Close $\leftrightarrow$ Near $\leftrightarrow$ Far) or stand from prone.
    \item[Action] Options include: 
        \begin{itemize}
            \item \textbf{Attack} (melee or ranged).
            \item \textbf{Disengage} – then your Move does not provoke opportunity attacks; you cannot Attack this turn.
            \item \textbf{Charge} – full turn action: move two range bands and make one melee attack; costs 1 Fatigue; your next defense is Desperate; requires open ground.
            \item \textbf{Skirmish} – full turn action: move two bands away and make one ranged attack; costs 1 Fatigue; your next defense is Desperate; requires open ground.
            \item \textbf{Observe / Change Position} – gain +1 Position once per turn.
            \item \textbf{Setup} – grant an ally +1 Position or step up Effect on their next action.
            \item \textbf{Assist} – spend 1 Boon to give an ally +1 die (max +3 dice from all assists).
            \item \textbf{Defend}, \textbf{Channel/Weave}, \textbf{Cast Rite/Song}, \textbf{Interact}, etc.
        \end{itemize}
\end{description}
Talents such as \textit{Effortless Charge}, \textit{Effortless Skirmish}, and \textit{Relentless} modify these actions.

\subsection{Initiative}
No fixed initiative order. Turn order follows the fiction: the GM spotlights characters based on circumstances; ambushers act first.

\subsection{Opportunity Attacks}
If a character moves out of an enemy’s Close range without first using the \textbf{Disengage} action, that enemy may make one free melee attack against them. This attack is resolved at \textbf{Controlled Position} and does not consume the enemy’s action.

\section{Fatigue \& Harm}\label{sec:fatigue_harm}

\subsection{Fatigue Track}
A character’s Fatigue track size equals their \textbf{Body} attribute. Mark Fatigue for physical exertion, magical strain, or mental stress.

\begin{itemize}
    \item Each step of Fatigue \textbf{worsens Position} by one level: Dominant $\rightarrow$ Controlled $\rightarrow$ Desperate.
    \item If already Desperate, each additional Fatigue instead imposes a \textbf{–1 die penalty} on the character’s rolls.
    \item \textbf{Overflow:} When the Fatigue track fills, increase the character’s Harm by one level and clear all Fatigue.
\end{itemize}
\noindent \textbf{Recovery:} A short rest (Some Time) clears 1–2 Fatigue; a full night’s rest (Significant Time) clears all Fatigue.

\subsection{Harm Levels}\label{subsec:harm_levels}

\begin{longtable}{p{2cm} p{10cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Harm Level} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
Harm 1 & –1 die on actions related to the injury (GM’s discretion). \\
Harm 2 & –1 die on most actions until treated. \\
Harm 3 & Incapacitated or dying. \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\noindent \textbf{Recovery:} Harm requires medical or magical care (e.g., a Medicine roll, a healing Rite, or extended rest).

An action roll pairs an \textbf{Attribute} (Body, Wits, Spirit, or Presence) with a \textbf{Skill}. The GM sets Position and DV based on the fiction.

\subsection{Choosing the Right Combination}
The same fictional action can be resolved with different Attribute+Skill pairs depending on the character’s approach. Examples:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Climbing a wall:} Body+Athletics (raw strength), Wits+Athletics (finding handholds), Spirit+Athletics (sheer determination).
    \item \textbf{Persuading a guard:} Presence+Sway (charisma), Wits+Sway (logical argument), Spirit+Sway (moral conviction).
\end{itemize}

\section{Experience Points (XP)}\label{sec:xp}

\subsection{Awarding XP (per session)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Base participation: +2 XP.
    \item Major objectives completed: +2–4 XP.
    \item Discoveries (lore, secrets, locations): +1–2 XP.
    \item Hard choices with meaningful consequences: +1–2 XP.
    \item Story engagement and complications: +1–3 XP.
    \item Personal goals pursued: +1–2 XP.
\end{itemize}
\noindent At the end of a major story arc, award an additional \textbf{+8–12 XP} to all players.

\subsection{Spending XP}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Attribute increase:} New rating $\times$ 3 XP. Training time = new rating (days).
    \item \textbf{Skill increase:} New level $\times$ 2 XP. Training time = new level (days).
    \item \textbf{Talents:} See talent cost tiers (2–12+ XP).
    \item \textbf{Followers:} Cap$^2$ XP (Cap = 1–5).
    \item \textbf{Assets:} Minor (4 XP, 1 day), Standard (8 XP, 1 week), Major (12 XP, 1 month).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Rush Rule}
The player may skip the required downtime for an advancement by starting a \textbf{Haste [4] clock}. If the clock fills (through failed checks or narrative pressure), the new ability or asset gains a flaw or narrative hitch (GM’s discretion).

\subsection{Reputation Tiers}\label{subsec:tiers}
\begin{description}
    \item[Tier I – Novice (0–40 XP)] Local reputation; prestige abilities locked.
    \item[Tier II – Seasoned (41–90 XP)] Regional notice; some prestige abilities unlock.
    \item[Tier III – Veteran (91–150 XP)] National influence; second follower slot suggested.
    \item[Tier IV – Paragon (151–220 XP)] Continental reputation; rivals emerge.
    \item[Tier V – Mythic (221+ XP)] Legendary status; kingdoms and cults respond directly.
\end{description}

\section{Game Structure}\label{sec:game_structure}

\subsection{Narrative Time Scales}
\begin{description}
    \item[A Moment] A heartbeat, a single action or word.
    \item[Some Time] A few minutes – a skirmish, picking a lock, a short negotiation.
    \item[Significant Time] Hours – travel, a ritual, a siege, extended recovery.
    \item[Days] Large‑scale endeavours – marching across a region, training a militia, recovering from serious wounds.
\end{description}

\subsection{Game Units}
\begin{description}
    \item[Scene] The basic narrative unit; resolves a specific conflict or question.
    \item[Session] One game session (typically 3–6 hours), containing 2–4 major scenes.
    \item[Campaign] An entire story arc (6–20+ sessions) with major character development and lasting consequences.
\end{description}

\section{Assets \& Followers (Quick Reference)}\label{sec:assets_followers}

\subsection{Assets (Off‑Screen Resources)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Costs:} Minor (4 XP, 1 day), Standard (8 XP, 1 week), Major (12 XP, 1 month).
    \item \textbf{Free off‑screen use:} Once per session, the asset can provide its listed benefit without a roll.
    \item \textbf{Boon activation:} Spend 1 Boon to use the asset dramatically during an on‑screen scene.
    \item \textbf{Conditions:} Maintained $\rightarrow$ Neglected $\rightarrow$ Compromised.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Followers (On‑Screen Allies)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Cost:} Cap$^2$ XP (Cap = 1–5). Recruitment takes 1–3 days of downtime.
    \item \textbf{Assistance:} When helping a PC, the follower adds dice equal to $\min(\text{Cap}, \text{relevant skill})$. The total bonus from all sources (including multiple followers) is capped at +3 dice.
    \item \textbf{Independent action:} Once per scene (party‑wide), a follower may take a small independent action (scout, distract, fetch). This costs the follower 1 Exposure or 1 Harm.
    \item \textbf{Conditions:} Followers track Exposure (narrative pressure) and Harm (injury).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Upkeep (per downtime period)}
Each follower or asset requires upkeep once per downtime period. The player chooses one of the following methods:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Efficient (higher XP, less time):} Pay upkeep XP $= \max(1, \frac{\text{acquisition cost}}{3})$. This requires minimal narrative effort.
    \item \textbf{Intensive (lower XP, more time):} Pay 1 XP and spend a dedicated downtime action personally tending to the follower or asset.
\end{itemize}
\noindent \textbf{Failure to pay:} A follower becomes \textit{Wary} (then \textit{Seized} if already Wary); an asset becomes \textit{Neglected} (then \textit{Compromised} if already Neglected).
