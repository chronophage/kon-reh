\chapter{Core Mechanics}łabel{chap:coreₘechanics}

This chapter distils the essential rules of Fate’s Edge into a compact reference.
It assumes you have read the narrative‑first philosophy and are comfortable with
the basic flow of play. Here you will find the precise procedures for resolution,
advancement, and resource management that power the game.

\section{The Core Resolution Cycle}łabel{sec:resolution_cycle}

Every meaningful action follows the same simple loop. The GM states Position and
Difficulty, the player rolls, and the table interprets the result through the
fiction.

\subsection{Step‑by‑Step Resolution}łabel{subsec:steps}

\begin{enumerate}
    ℹtem \textbf{Declare action & approach.} The player states intent, the
        Attribute (Body, Wits, Spirit, Presence) and Skill they will use.
    ℹtem \textbf{Set Difficulty Value (DV).} The GM sets a target number of
        successes based on the narrative stakes (see \autoref{subsec:difficultyᵥalue}).
    ℹtem \textbf{Set Position.} The GM declares whether the action is
        Dominant, Controlled, or Desperate (see \autoref{subsec:positionₑffect}).
    ℹtem \textbf{Roll the dice pool.} Roll a number of d10s equal to
        Attribute + Skill.
    ℹtem \textbf{Count successes and Story Beats (SB).}
        \begin{itemize}
            ℹtem 6–9 = 1 success
            ℹtem 10 = 2 successes
            ℹtem 1 = 1 SB for the GM
        \end{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Compare successes to DV} using the Outcome Matrix
        (\autoref{tab:outcomeₘatrix}).
    ℹtem \textbf{Apply the outcome.} The GM may spend any SB generated to
        introduce complications (see \autoref{sec:sbₛpendₘenu}).
    ℹtem \textbf{Manage resources.} The player marks Boons, Fatigue, or
        Obligation as indicated by the outcome.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Difficulty Value (DV)}łabel{subsec:difficultyᵥalue}

The DV is the number of successes needed to achieve the character’s intent
without a cost. The GM sets it based on the difficulty of the task, not the
drama of the scene.

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Default DV Ladder, colback=white!97!gray, colframe=black!80!gray, sharp corners]
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c p{3.5in}}
⊤rule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Typical Case} \\
∣rule
2 & Routine: clear path, no pressure, almost guaranteed. \\
3 & Pressured: mild opposition, time limit, partial information. \\
4 & Hard: active resistance, precision required, serious consequences. \\
5+ & Extreme: stacked constraints, high drama, nearly impossible. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

\noindent \textbf{Default DV = 3.} Do not inflate DV to “challenge” players; use
Position, timers, and SB spends instead.

\subsection{Position and Effect}łabel{subsec:positionₑffect}

Position answers “How risky is this attempt?” Effect answers “How much do I
achieve on a success?” Both are set by the GM based on the fiction.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
⊤rule
\textbf{Position} & \textbf{Narrative Frame} & \textbf{Mechanical Effect} \\
∣rule
Dominant   & You press your advantage. & Re‑roll one failure. \\
Controlled & Balanced, normal risk.   & No re‑rolls. \\
Desperate  & You act under duress.    & Re‑roll one success (keep second). \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent \textbf{Effect:} Limited, Standard, or Great. It describes scope,
scale, or quality (e.g., disarming a trap: Limited = one person crosses,
Standard = two, Great = all four).

\paragraph{Fail Forward}
No roll should result in “nothing happens.” Every outcome changes the situation.
Even a Miss introduces a complication, advances a timer, or reveals a hidden danger.

\subsection{Critical Success (10s)}łabel{subsec:criticalₛuccess}

A die that shows 10 counts as two successes. If the overall roll succeeds, each
10 adds narrative weight (GM’s discretion):
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{One 10:} Strong success – improve Position by one step on your
        next action, or gain a minor advantage.
    ℹtem \textbf{Two 10s:} Exceptional – choose two benefits (e.g., increased
        Effect + a free Boon).
    ℹtem \textbf{Three 10s:} Legendary – decisive victory; clear 1 segment from
        a relevant timer.
    ℹtem \textbf{Four+ 10s:} Mythic – reshape the scene; clear 1–2 segments
        from a relevant timer.
\end{itemize}
\noindent Tens are never re‑rolled by Position effects.

\subsection{Dice Pool Limit and Auto‑Successes}łabel{subsec:diceₚoolₗimit}

The maximum number of dice rolled is 10. Any additional dice beyond 10 become
automatic successes (each extra die counts as one success, but no SB is
generated). This keeps high‑tier characters from rolling more than a handful of
physical dice while still rewarding their expertise.

\subsection{Assistance and Teamwork}łabel{subsec:assistance}

When multiple characters contribute to the same action:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Assistance:} One character is primary; up to three others add
        +1 die each by describing how they help.
    ℹtem \textbf{Total assist bonus capped at +3 dice.}
    ℹtem \textbf{Cooperation:} Several characters each roll; the group succeeds
        or fails based on combined results.
    ℹtem \textbf{Complementary:} Different skills cover different parts of a
        larger task.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Boons}łabel{subsec:boons}

Boons represent momentum and narrative leverage. You may hold up to 5.

\paragraph{Earning Boons}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Partial:} Gain 1 Boon.
    ℹtem \textbf{Miss:} Gain 2 Boons (on a meaningful roll).
    ℹtem \textbf{Bond‑driven action:} Once per bond per session, gain 1 Boon
        when you take a significant action that explicitly references your bond.
    ℹtem \textbf{GM award:} For creativity, sacrifice, or strong narrative play.
\end{itemize}
\noindent \textbf{Limit:} Max 2 Boons from failures per character per scene.
Repetition of the same approach does not award extra Boons.

\paragraph{Spending Boons (1 Boon)}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Reroll a single die after seeing the pool.
    ℹtem Activate an on‑screen Asset.
    ℹtem Improve Position by one step before a roll.
    ℹtem Power certain Rites or abilities.
    ℹtem Convert 2 Boons $→$ 1 XP (once per session during downtime,
        max 2 XP per session via conversion).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Carryover Limit} At the end of each scene, reduce held Boons to a
maximum of 2. Excess are lost.

\subsection{Story Beats (SB)}łabel{subsec:story_beats}

Every time a player rolls a 1, the GM gains one Story Beat. These are narrative
tools to introduce complications, escalate tension, or advance timers.

\paragraph{SB Spend Menu}łabel{sec:sbₛpendₘenu}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
⊤rule
\textbf{Cost} & \textbf{Effect} \\
∣rule
1 SB & Minor: noise, trace, tick a timer +1, worsen Position by one step. \\
2 SB & Moderate: alarm raised, lose advantage/cover, lesser foe appears. \\
3 SB & Serious: reinforcements, key gear breaks, major twist, advance a front timer +1. \\
4+ SB & Major: trap springs, authority arrives, scene shift, Patron intervenes. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{NPCs and Resources}łabel{subsec:npcᵣesources}

Nonplayer characters do not track Fatigue or Boons. If an ability would cause an
NPC to lose these resources, convert it into a narrative consequence:
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Instead of “loses 1 Boon”: “The target hesitates – they lose their next action.”
    ℹtem Instead of “marks 1 Fatigue”: “The target staggers – their next attack is at Desperate Position.”
    ℹtem Instead of “loses 1 Boon and marks 1 Fatigue”: “The target drops their weapon (disarmed) and stumbles back one range band.”
\end{itemize}

\section{Player‑Managed Modules}łabel{sec:playerₘanagedₘodules}

In Fate’s Edge, you are the primary manager of your character’s mechanical state.
This reduces GM overhead and keeps you engaged.

\paragraph{What you track:}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Obligation tracks (per Patron or Symbol)
    ℹtem Corruption timers (Cantors, Pact‑Whisperers)
    ℹtem Leash timers (summoned spirits)
    ℹtem Asset state (Flourishing, Stable, Strained, Collapsed)
    ℹtem Follower states (Loyalty: Faithful/Strained/Broken; Fitness: Ready/Hurt/Down)
    ℹtem Progression timers (Repertoire, Spirit Bond)
    ℹtem Boons, Favour, Leverage
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Your responsibilities:}
\begin{enumerate}
    ℹtem Mark immediately when a rule says “mark +X”.
    ℹtem Declare thresholds (“My Obligation is full!”).
    ℹtem Keep it visible (use trackers everyone can see).
    ℹtem Maintain ownership – you know your state best.
\end{enumerate}
The GM spot‑checks and provides consequences, but you drive your character’s
mechanical narrative.

\subsection{Obligation Capacity}łabel{subsec:obligation_capacity}

Obligation Capacity = Spirit + Presence. When you exceed capacity, mark 1 Fatigue
per extra segment. When you reach double capacity, clear all Fatigue, increase
Harm by 1, and the Patron intrudes (a demand, omen, or task).

\subsection{Corruption Timer (Cantors)}łabel{subsec:corruptionₜimer}

Timer size = Spirit. When it fills, gain the last Patron’s benefit and burden,
then reset the timer to your Tier (minimum 1). You are never completely free of
Corruption.

\subsection{Leash Timer (Summoners)}łabel{subsec:leashₜimer}

Capacity = Cap + Spirit. Tick when the spirit takes Harm, acts against its
nature, crosses a [WARD], or you split focus. Once per round, spend 1 Boon to
clear 1 tick. When the Leash fills, the spirit acts once to its nature, then
departs (or turns hostile).

\subsection{Asset and Follower States}łabel{subsec:asset_followerₛtates}

\paragraph{Asset Status (unified track)}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Flourishing:} +1 die on asset rolls, +1 Yield.
    ℹtem \textbf{Stable:} Normal operation.
    ℹtem \textbf{Strained:} –1 die on asset rolls, no Yield.
    ℹtem \textbf{Collapsed:} Non‑functional; requires quest or major downtime.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Follower Loyalty}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Faithful:} Normal assist, independent actions available.
    ℹtem \textbf{Strained:} Assist at –1 die; may refuse risky orders.
    ℹtem \textbf{Broken:} Follower leaves or becomes hostile.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Follower Fitness}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Ready:} Full function.
    ℹtem \textbf{Hurt:} Assist at –1 die; no independent actions.
    ℹtem \textbf{Down:} Incapacitated; requires rescue or medical care.
\end{itemize}
Change states when the fiction demands it; no numeric timers required.

\section{Turn Economy}łabel{sec:turnₑconomy}

Each character takes \textbf{1 Action and 1 Move} on their turn. Actions and
Moves may be taken in any order; repeating the same Action is not allowed unless
noted.

\subsection{Movement}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Move:} Shift one range band (Close $↔$ Near $↔$ Far) or stand from prone.
    ℹtem \textbf{Dash (Action):} Shift two bands; your next defense is Desperate.
    ℹtem \textbf{Disengage (Action):} Then your Move does not provoke
        opportunity attacks; you cannot Attack this turn.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Action Options}
\begin{description}
    ℹtem[Attack] Make a melee or ranged attack.
    ℹtem[Disengage] See above.
    ℹtem[Shove] Opposed Body + Athletics. Push target one band.
    ℹtem[Grapple] Opposed Body + Melee vs. Body + Athletics. Both Engaged;
        target suffers –1 die.
    ℹtem[Charge (full turn)] Move two bands toward an enemy + make one melee
        attack. Cost 1 Fatigue, next defense Desperate, requires open ground.
    ℹtem[Skirmish (full turn)] Move two bands away from an enemy + make one
        ranged attack. Cost 1 Fatigue, next defense Desperate, requires open
        ground.
    ℹtem[Observe / Change Position] Gain +1 Position once.
    ℹtem[Setup] Grant an ally +1 Position or step up Effect (requires a roll).
    ℹtem[Assist] Spend 1 Boon to give an ally +1 die (max +3 from all assists).
    ℹtem[Defend, Channel/Weave, Cast Rite/Song, Interact, etc.]
\end{description}

\subsection{Initiative}
There is no fixed initiative order. The GM frames spotlight order based on the
fiction. Ambushers act first; the spotlight may shift mid‑scene if narratively
appropriate.

\subsection{Opportunity Attacks}
Leaving an enemy’s Close range without using Disengage provokes one free melee
attack (Controlled Position, no action cost).

\section{Fatigue and Harm}łabel{sec:fatigueₕarm}

\subsection{Fatigue Track}
Your Fatigue track size equals your Body attribute. Mark Fatigue for physical
exertion, magical strain, stress, or harsh conditions.

\paragraph{Effects}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Each point of Fatigue worsens your Position by one step
        (Dominant $→$ Controlled $→$ Desperate).
    ℹtem If already Desperate, each additional Fatigue instead imposes a –1 die
        penalty on all rolls.
    ℹtem \textbf{Overflow:} When the track fills, increase Harm by one level and
        clear all Fatigue.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Recovery}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Short rest (one hour, quiet): remove 2 Fatigue.
    ℹtem Full night’s rest in safety: remove all Fatigue.
    ℹtem Fatigue cannot be removed while the party is at Dangerous or Empty
        Supply (see travel rules).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Harm Levels}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
⊤rule
\textbf{Harm Level} & \textbf{Effect} \\
∣rule
Harm 1 & –1 die on related actions. \\
Harm 2 & –1 die on most actions until treated. \\
Harm 3 & Incapacitated or dying. \\
⊥tomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Taking Harm and Fatigue – The Procedure}
When you are hit, follow these steps in order:

\begin{enumerate}
    ℹtem \textbf{Convert} incoming Harm to Fatigue using the Armor Conversion
        Table (see \autoref{tab:armor_conversion}). Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit,
        even if conversion would give 0.
    ℹtem \textbf{Apply remaining Harm} – if any Harm remains after conversion,
        increase your Harm level by that amount, and clear all existing Fatigue
        (the shock of a wound resets your exhaustion).
    ℹtem \textbf{Add the Fatigue} from step 1 to your Fatigue track. If this
        fills or exceeds your track, resolve Overflow (Harm +1, clear all Fatigue).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Alternative Attribute + Skill Pairs}
The same fictional action can often use different Attributes. For example:

\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Climbing:} Body + Athletics (strength) or Wits + Athletics
        (routefinding) or Spirit + Athletics (determination).
    ℹtem \textbf{Persuading a guard:} Presence + Sway (charm) or Wits + Sway
        (logic) or Spirit + Sway (conviction).
\end{itemize}
The GM has final say; creative alternatives are encouraged when they fit the
fiction.

\section{Experience Points (XP)}łabel{sec:xp}

XP is earned by engaging meaningfully with the world: overcoming challenges,
taking risks, making hard choices, and leaning into your character’s story.

\subsection{Awarding XP (per session)}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem Base participation: +2 XP.
    ℹtem Major objective: +2–4 XP.
    ℹtem Discoveries (lore, locations, secrets): +1–2 XP.
    ℹtem Hard choices (moral or strategic): +1–2 XP.
    ℹtem Story engagement (embracing complications): +1–3 XP.
    ℹtem Personal goals: +1–2 XP.
\end{itemize}
At the end of a major story arc, everyone gains an additional +8–12 XP.

\subsection{Spending XP}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Attribute increase:} new rating ×3 XP; downtime = new rating days.
    ℹtem \textbf{Skill increase:} new level ×2 XP; downtime = new level days.
    ℹtem \textbf{Talents:} 2 XP (minor) to 12+ XP (epic).
    ℹtem \textbf{Follower (Capability):} Cap² XP; downtime 1–3 days.
    ℹtem \textbf{Asset:}
        \begin{itemize}
            ℹtem Minor (4 XP, 1 day)
            ℹtem Standard (8 XP, 1 week)
            ℹtem Major (12 XP, 1 month)
        \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Rush Rule}
You may attempt to train faster. The GM starts a Risk Timer [4]. Each segment
you roll Spirit + Resolve (DV 3). When the timer fills, the new capability gains
a flaw (e.g., –1 die penalty, unreliability, hidden problem).

\subsection{Reputation Tiers}
\begin{description}
    ℹtem[Novice (0–40 XP)] Local reputation, prestige locked.
    ℹtem[Seasoned (41–90 XP)] Regional influence, some prestige unlock.
    ℹtem[Veteran (91–150 XP)] National reputation, second follower slot.
    ℹtem[Paragon (151–220 XP)] Continental influence, rivals emerge.
    ℹtem[Mythic (221+ XP)] Legendary, kingdoms react.
\end{description}

\section{Assets and Followers (Quick Reference)}łabel{sec:assets_followers}

\subsection{Assets (Off‑Screen)}
Cost: Minor (4 XP, 1d), Standard (8 XP, 1w), Major (12 XP, 1m).

\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Free off‑screen use:} once per session.
    ℹtem \textbf{Boon activation:} spend 1 Boon for on‑scene effect.
    ℹtem \textbf{Status:} Flourishing $→$ Stable $→$ Strained $→$ Collapsed.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Followers (On‑Screen)}
Cost: Cap² XP (e.g., Cap 3 = 9 XP), downtime 1–3 days.

\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Assistance:} add dice = min(Cap, relevant skill), max +3 from
        all sources.
    ℹtem \textbf{Independent action:} once per scene (party‑wide). Cost: worsen
        Loyalty or Fitness one step.
    ℹtem \textbf{Loyalty:} Faithful $→$ Strained $→$ Broken.
    ℹtem \textbf{Fitness:} Ready $→$ Hurt $→$ Down.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Upkeep (per downtime)}
\begin{itemize}
    ℹtem \textbf{Efficient:} XP = max(1, acquisition XP / 3). Minimal effort.
    ℹtem \textbf{Intensive:} 1 XP + a dedicated downtime action.
\end{itemize}
Failure to pay: Asset Status worsens one step; Follower Loyalty worsens one step.
