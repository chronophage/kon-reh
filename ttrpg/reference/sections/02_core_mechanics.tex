\chapter{Core Mechanics}\label{chap:core_mechanics}

\section{The Core Resolution Cycle}\label{sec:resolution_cycle}

When a player rolls, they are not simply trying to beat a number. They are engaging the world through risk, consequence, and discovery.

\subsection{Step‑by‑Step Resolution}\label{subsec:steps}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Declare Action \& Approach:} Player states intent and the Attribute + Skill pair.\index{Resolution!declare action}
    \item \textbf{Set Difficulty Value (DV):}\index{Difficulty Value (DV)} Based on narrative stakes, not just mechanics.
    \item \textbf{Roll Pool of d10s.}
    \item \textbf{Count:} \textbf{Successes (6+)} and \textbf{Story Beats (1s)}.\index{Resolution!count successes}
    \item \textbf{Check Against DV:} Apply the Outcome Matrix (\autoref{tab:outcome_matrix}). Note: \textbf{each 10 counts as two successes}.
    \item \textbf{Spend Story Beats:} GM spends or banks SB (\autoref{sec:sb_spend_menu}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Difficulty Value (DV)}\label{subsec:difficulty_value}

DV measures narrative weight, not simulationist difficulty.

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Default DV Ladder, colback=white!97!gray, colframe=black!80!gray, sharp corners]
\begin{center}
\begin{longtable}{c p{3.5in}}
\toprule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Typical Case} \\
\midrule
2 & Routine: clear intent, modest stakes, controlled environment \\
3 & Pressured: time limits, mild resistance, partial information \\
4 & Hard: hostile conditions, active opposition, precision required \\
5+ & Extreme: stacked constraints, dangerous failure, high drama \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

\noindent When in doubt, start at DV 3.

\subsection{Position \& Effect}\label{subsec:position_effect}

\begin{description}
    \item[Position (risk)]\index{Position} – \textbf{Dominant} (safe), \textbf{Controlled} (balanced), \textbf{Desperate} (high stakes).
    \item[Effect (reward)]\index{Effect} – \textbf{Limited} (small), \textbf{Standard} (expected), \textbf{Great} (big).
\end{description}
The GM sets both based on your approach and the fiction.

\subsection{Critical Success (10s)}\label{subsec:critical_success}

Each \textbf{10} on a success adds narrative weight:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{One 10:} Strong success – improved Position or minor advantage.
    \item \textbf{Two 10s:} Exceptional success – choose two benefits.
    \item \textbf{Three 10s:} Legendary success – decisive victory, clear 1 clock segment.
    \item \textbf{Four+ 10s:} Mythic success – reshape the scene, clear 1–2 clock segments.
\end{itemize}
Critical success effects apply only if the roll succeeds. 10s are \textbf{never} rerolled by Position or other effects.

\subsection{Boon Economy}\label{subsec:boon_economy}\index{Boons}

\paragraph{Earning Boons}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Partial (0 < successes < DV): gain 1 Boon.
    \item Miss (successes = 0): gain 2 Boons (on a meaningful roll).
    \item Bond‑driven action: once per bond per session, gain 1 Boon when acting on that bond with an Intricate Description.
    \item GM award: creativity, sacrifice, or strong narrative play.
\end{itemize}
\noindent No Boons for safe rehearsal, null‑risk probes, or repeated identical attempts in the same scene.

\paragraph{Spending Boons}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reroll a single die (after seeing the result).
    \item Activate an on‑screen Asset (1 Boon).
    \item Improve Position by one step before a roll.
    \item Power certain Rites or abilities.
    \item Convert 2 Boons $\rightarrow$ 1 XP (once per session during downtime, max 2 XP/session via conversion).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Carryover Limit}
At the end of each scene, reduce held Boons to a maximum of 2. Excess are lost.

\subsection{Story Beats (SB)}\label{subsec:story_beats}\index{Story Beats}

Every die that shows a \textbf{1} generates one Story Beat for the GM. Rerolling a 1 does \textbf{not} erase its SB; if the reroll also shows 1, it generates additional SB.

\section{Player‑Managed Modules}\label{sec:player_managed_modules}

Players are the primary stewards of their character‑facing trackers (Obligation, Corruption, Leash, Asset States). This reduces GM overhead and keeps the table moving.

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=What Players Track, colback=black!3, colframe=black!40!white]
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l l X}
\toprule
\textbf{Module} & \textbf{Owner} & \textbf{Tick Triggers (examples)} \\
\midrule
Obligation (per Patron) & Player & Invoking a Rite, Pushing, \textit{Crack the Seal}. \\
Corruption Clock & Player & Cantor Push, Resonant Rite, GM spends SB on occult actions. \\
Leash (Summoning) & Player & Spirit takes Harm, acts against nature, crosses a \texttt{[WARD]}. \\
Asset State (Symbol) & Player & Maintenance, \textit{Crack the Seal} $\rightarrow$ Compromised. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{tcolorbox}

\paragraph{Obligation Capacity}
\[
\text{Obligation Capacity} = \text{Spirit} + \text{Presence}
\]
\begin{itemize}
    \item Over capacity: mark +1 Fatigue per segment over.
    \item Double capacity: clear all Fatigue, +1 Harm, and a \textbf{Patron Intrusion} occurs.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Corruption Full (Cantor)}
Apply the last Patron’s theme boon/burden, then reset the clock.

\paragraph{Leash Full (Summoner)}
Spirit acts once to its nature, then departs (or turns hostile).

\paragraph{Symbol State (Invoker)}
\textit{Compromised} (e.g., from \textit{Crack the Seal}) – repair in downtime or pay 1 XP.

\section{Turn Economy}\label{sec:turn_economy}

\begin{description}
    \item[Move] Traverse up to one range band (Close $\leftrightarrow$ Near $\leftrightarrow$ Far) or stand from prone.
    \item[Action] Options include: Attack, Disengage, Charge\footnote{Full turn action: move two bands and melee attack; costs 1 Fatigue; next defense Desperate; requires open ground.}, Skirmish\footnote{Full turn action: move two bands away and ranged attack; costs 1 Fatigue; next defense Desperate; requires open ground.}, Observe/Change Position (+1 Position once per turn), Setup (grant ally +1 Position/Effect), Assist (spend 1 Boon for +1 die; max +3 dice from assists), Defend, Channel/Weave, Cast Rite/Song, Interact.
\end{description}
Talents such as \textit{Effortless Charge}, \textit{Effortless Skirmish}, and \textit{Relentless} modify these actions.

\subsection{Initiative}
No fixed initiative. Turn order follows the fiction: GM spotlights based on circumstances; ambushers act first.

\subsection{Opportunity Attacks}
Moving out of an enemy’s Close range without using the Disengage action provokes one free melee attack (Controlled Position).

\section{Fatigue \& Harm}\label{sec:fatigue_harm}

\subsection{Fatigue Track}
Fatigue track = \textbf{Body}. Mark Fatigue for exertion, magical strain, or stress.

\begin{itemize}
    \item Each Fatigue worsens Position one step: Dominant $\rightarrow$ Controlled $\rightarrow$ Desperate.
    \item If already Desperate, each Fatigue gives –1 die instead.
    \item Overflow: when Fatigue fills, increase Harm by 1 step and clear all Fatigue.
\end{itemize}
Recovery: short rest clears 1–2 Fatigue; full night’s rest clears all.

\subsection{Harm Levels}\label{subsec:harm_levels}

\begin{longtable}{p{2cm} p{10cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Harm Level} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
Harm 1 & –1 die on related actions. \\
Harm 2 & –1 die on most actions until treated. \\
Harm 3 & Incapacitated or dying. \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

Recovery: Harm requires medical/magical care.

\subsection{Fear Effects}\label{subsec:fear_effects}

When a character escalates on the Fear Track (Shaken $\rightarrow$ Frightened $\rightarrow$ Panicked), roll d10 or choose an effect:

\begin{center}
\begin{longtable}{c l}
\toprule
\textbf{d10} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
1 & Freeze (cannot act) \\
2 & Flee from the source of fear \\
3 & Drop held item \\
4 & Beg or bargain incoherently \\
5 & Hide \\
6 & Attack in panic (nearest target) \\
7 & Blunder into danger \\
8 & Obey a simple command from the fear‑causer \\
9 & Break down (useless until aided) \\
10 & Catatonia \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}
\end{center}

\subsection{Choosing the Right Combination}
The same fictional action can use different Attribute+Skill pairs. Examples:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Climbing a wall: Body+Athletics (strength), Wits+Athletics (route‑finding), Spirit+Athletics (determination).
    \item Persuading a guard: Presence+Sway (charm), Wits+Sway (logic), Spirit+Sway (conviction).
\end{itemize}

\section{Experience Points (XP)}\label{sec:xp}

\subsection{Awarding XP (per session)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Base participation: +2 XP.
    \item Major objectives: +2–4 XP.
    \item Discoveries: +1–2 XP.
    \item Hard choices: +1–2 XP.
    \item Story engagement: +1–3 XP.
    \item Personal goals: +1–2 XP.
\end{itemize}
\noindent Arc completion bonus: +8–12 XP.

\subsection{Spending XP}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Attribute increase: new rating $\times$ 3 XP.
    \item Skill increase: new level $\times$ 2 XP.
    \item Talents: see cost tiers (2–12+ XP).
    \item Followers: Cap$^2$ XP.
    \item Assets: Minor (4 XP), Standard (8 XP), Major (12 XP).
\end{itemize}
Training time (downtime) equals new rating (days) for Attributes/Skills.

\subsection{Rush Rule}
Skip required downtime by starting a \textbf{Haste} [4] clock. If it fills, the new ability has flaws or narrative hitches.

\subsection{Reputation Tiers}\label{subsec:tiers}
\begin{description}
    \item[Tier I – Novice (0–40 XP)] Local reputation.
    \item[Tier II – Seasoned (41–90 XP)] Regional notice.
    \item[Tier III – Veteran (91–150 XP)] National influence.
    \item[Tier IV – Paragon (151–220 XP)] Continental reputation.
    \item[Tier V – Mythic (221+ XP)] Legendary, world‑changing.
\end{description}

\section{Game Structure}\label{sec:game_structure}

\subsection{Narrative Time Scales}
\begin{description}
    \item[A Moment] A heartbeat, a single action.
    \item[Some Time] A few minutes (skirmish, lockpick, short negotiation).
    \item[Significant Time] Hours (travel, ritual, siege).
    \item[Days] Large‑scale endeavors (marches, training, recovery).
\end{description}

\subsection{Game Units}
\begin{description}
    \item[Scene] Basic narrative unit; resolves a specific conflict.
    \item[Session] One game session (3–6 hours).
    \item[Campaign] Entire story arc (6–20+ sessions).
\end{description}

\section{Assets \& Followers (Quick Reference)}\label{sec:assets_followers}

\subsection{Assets (Off‑Screen Resources)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Minor (4 XP, 1 day), Standard (8 XP, 1 week), Major (12 XP, 1 month).
    \item Free off‑screen use: once per session.
    \item Boon activation (1 Boon) for on‑screen dramatic effect.
    \item Conditions: Maintained $\rightarrow$ Neglected $\rightarrow$ Compromised.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Followers (On‑Screen Allies)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Cost = Cap$^2$ XP (Cap = 1–5).
    \item Assistance: add dice = min(Cap, relevant skill); max +3 dice total from all sources.
    \item Independent action (once per scene party‑wide): scout, distract, fetch – costs Exposure or Harm.
    \item Conditions: Exposure and Harm tracks.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Upkeep (per downtime period)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Efficient:} Upkeep XP = max(1, acquisition cost / 3), minimal effort.
    \item \textbf{Intensive:} 1 XP + dedicated downtime action.
\end{itemize}
Failure to pay: Asset becomes Neglected (then Compromised); Follower becomes Wary (then Seized).
