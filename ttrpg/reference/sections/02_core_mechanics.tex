\chapter{Core Mechanics}\label{chap:core_mechanics}

This chapter distils the essential rules of Fate’s Edge into a compact, look‑up
reference. It assumes you have already read the narrative‑first philosophy
(\autoref{chap:introduction}) and are comfortable with the basic flow of play.
Here you will find the precise procedures for resolution, advancement, and
resource management that power the game.

\section{The Core Resolution Cycle}\label{sec:resolution_cycle}

Every meaningful action follows the same simple loop. The GM states Position and
Difficulty, the player rolls, and the table interprets the result through the
lens of the fiction.

\subsection{Step‑by‑Step Resolution}\label{subsec:steps}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Declare action \& approach.} The player states intent, the
        Attribute (Body, Wits, Spirit, Presence) and Skill they will use.
    \item \textbf{Set Difficulty Value (DV).} The GM sets a target number of
        successes based on the narrative stakes (see \autoref{subsec:difficulty_value}).
    \item \textbf{Set Position.} The GM declares whether the action is
        Dominant, Controlled, or Desperate (see \autoref{subsec:position_effect}).
    \item \textbf{Roll the dice pool.} Roll a number of d10s equal to
        Attribute + Skill.
    \item \textbf{Count successes and Story Beats (SB).}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item 6–9 = 1 success
            \item 10 = 2 successes
            \item 1 = 1 SB for the GM
        \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Compare successes to DV} using the Outcome Matrix
        (\autoref{tab:outcome_matrix}).
    \item \textbf{Apply the outcome.} The GM may spend any SB generated to
        introduce complications (see \autoref{sec:sb_spend_menu}).
    \item \textbf{Manage resources.} The player marks Boons, Fatigue, or
        Obligation as indicated by the outcome.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Difficulty Value (DV)}\label{subsec:difficulty_value}

The DV is the number of successes needed to achieve the character’s intent
without a cost. The GM sets it based on the difficulty of the task, not the
drama of the scene.

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Default DV Ladder, colback=white!97!gray, colframe=black!80!gray, sharp corners]
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c p{3.5in}}
\toprule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Typical Case} \\
\midrule
2 & Routine: clear path, no pressure, almost guaranteed. \\
3 & Pressured: mild opposition, time limit, partial information. \\
4 & Hard: active resistance, precision required, serious consequences. \\
5+ & Extreme: stacked constraints, high drama, nearly impossible. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

\noindent \textbf{Default DV = 3.} Do not inflate DV to “challenge” players; use
Position, clocks, and SB spends instead.

\subsection{Position and Effect}\label{subsec:position_effect}

Position answers “How risky is this attempt?” Effect answers “How much do I
achieve on a success?” Both are set by the GM based on the fiction.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Position} & \textbf{Narrative Frame} & \textbf{Mechanical Effect} \\
\midrule
Dominant   & You press your advantage. & Re‑roll one failure. \\
Controlled & Balanced, normal risk.   & No re‑rolls. \\
Desperate  & You act under duress.    & Re‑roll one success (keep second). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent \textbf{Effect:} Limited, Standard, or Great. It describes scope,
scale, or quality (e.g., disarming a trap: Limited = one person crosses,
Standard = two, Great = all four).

\subsection{Critical Success (10s)}\label{subsec:critical_success}

A die that shows 10 counts as two successes. If the overall roll succeeds, each
10 adds narrative weight:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{One 10:} Strong success – improve Position by one step on your
        next action, or gain a minor advantage.
    \item \textbf{Two 10s:} Exceptional – choose two benefits (e.g., increased
        Effect + a free Boon).
    \item \textbf{Three 10s:} Legendary – decisive victory; clear 1 segment from
        a relevant clock.
    \item \textbf{Four+ 10s:} Mythic – reshape the scene; clear 1–2 segments
        from a relevant clock.
\end{itemize}

\noindent Tens are never re‑rolled by Position effects.

\subsection{Dice Pool Limit and Auto‑Successes}\label{subsec:dice_pool_limit}

The maximum number of dice rolled is 10. Any additional dice beyond 10 become
automatic successes (each extra die counts as one success, but no SB is
generated). This keeps high‑tier characters from rolling more than a handful of
physical dice while still rewarding their expertise.

\subsection{Boons}\label{subsec:boons}

Boons represent momentum and narrative leverage. You may hold up to 5.

\paragraph{Earning Boons}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Partial:} Gain 1 Boon.
    \item \textbf{Miss:} Gain 2 Boons (on a meaningful roll).
    \item \textbf{Bond‑driven action:} Once per bond per session, gain 1 Boon
        when you take a significant action that explicitly references your bond.
    \item \textbf{GM award:} For creativity, sacrifice, or strong narrative play.
\end{itemize}
\noindent \textbf{Limit:} Max 2 Boons from failures per character per scene.
Repetition of the same approach does not award extra Boons.

\paragraph{Spending Boons (1 Boon)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reroll a single die after seeing the pool.
    \item Activate an on‑screen Asset.
    \item Improve Position by one step before a roll.
    \item Power certain Rites or abilities.
    \item Convert 2 Boons $\rightarrow$ 1 XP (once per session during downtime,
        max 2 XP per session via conversion).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Carryover Limit} At the end of each scene, reduce held Boons to a
maximum of 2. Excess are lost.

\subsection{Story Beats (SB)}\label{subsec:story_beats}

Every time a player rolls a 1, the GM gains one Story Beat. These are narrative
tools to introduce complications, escalate tension, or advance clocks.

\paragraph{SB Spend Menu}\label{sec:sb_spend_menu}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{Cost} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
1 SB & Minor: noise, trace, tick a clock +1, worsen Position by one step. \\
2 SB & Moderate: alarm raised, lose advantage/cover, lesser foe appears. \\
3 SB & Serious: reinforcements, key gear breaks, major twist, advance a front clock +1. \\
4+ SB & Major: trap springs, authority arrives, scene shift, Patron intervenes. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Player‑Managed Modules}\label{sec:player_managed_modules}

In Fate’s Edge, you are the primary manager of your character’s mechanical state.
This reduces GM overhead and keeps you engaged.

\paragraph{What you track:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Obligation tracks (per Patron or Symbol)
    \item Corruption clocks (Cantors, Pact‑Whisperers)
    \item Leash clocks (summoned spirits)
    \item Asset state (Flourishing, Stable, Strained, Collapsed)
    \item Follower states (Loyalty: Faithful/Strained/Broken; Fitness: Ready/Hurt/Down)
    \item Progression clocks (Repertoire, Spirit Bond)
    \item Boons, Favour, Leverage
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Your responsibilities:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Mark immediately when a rule says “mark +X”.
    \item Declare thresholds (“My Obligation is full!”).
    \item Keep it visible (use trackers everyone can see).
    \item Maintain ownership – you know your state best.
\end{enumerate}

The GM spot‑checks and provides consequences, but you drive your character’s
mechanical narrative.

\subsection{Obligation Capacity}\label{subsec:obligation_capacity}

Obligation Capacity = Spirit + Presence. When you exceed capacity, mark 1 Fatigue
per extra segment. When you reach double capacity, clear all Fatigue, increase
Harm by 1, and the Patron intrudes (a demand, omen, or task).

\subsection{Corruption Clock (Cantors)}\label{subsec:corruption_clock}

Clock size = Spirit. When it fills, gain the last Patron’s benefit and burden,
then reset the clock to your Tier (minimum 1). You are never completely free of
Corruption.

\subsection{Leash Clock (Summoners)}\label{subsec:leash_clock}

Capacity = Cap + Spirit. Tick when the spirit takes Harm, acts against its
nature, crosses a [WARD], or you split focus. Once per round, spend 1 Boon to
clear 1 tick. When the Leash fills, the spirit acts once to its nature, then
departs (or turns hostile).

\subsection{Asset and Follower States}\label{subsec:asset_follower_states}

\paragraph{Asset Status (unified track)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Flourishing:} +1 die on asset rolls, +1 Yield.
    \item \textbf{Stable:} Normal operation.
    \item \textbf{Strained:} –1 die on asset rolls, no Yield.
    \item \textbf{Collapsed:} Non‑functional; requires quest or major downtime.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Follower Loyalty}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Faithful:} Normal assist, independent actions available.
    \item \textbf{Strained:} Assist at –1 die; may refuse risky orders.
    \item \textbf{Broken:} Follower leaves or becomes hostile.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Follower Fitness}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Ready:} Full function.
    \item \textbf{Hurt:} Assist at –1 die; no independent actions.
    \item \textbf{Down:} Incapacitated; requires rescue or medical care.
\end{itemize}

\noindent Change states when the fiction demands it; no numeric clocks required.

\section{Turn Economy}\label{sec:turn_economy}

Each character takes \textbf{1 Action and 1 Move} on their turn. Actions and
Moves may be taken in any order; repeating the same Action is not allowed unless
noted.

\subsection{Movement}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Move:} Shift one range band (Close $\leftrightarrow$ Near $\leftrightarrow$ Far) or stand from prone.
    \item \textbf{Dash (Action):} Shift two bands; your next defense is Desperate.
    \item \textbf{Disengage (Action):} Then your Move does not provoke
        opportunity attacks; you cannot Attack this turn.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Action Options}
\begin{description}
    \item[Attack] Make a melee or ranged attack.
    \item[Disengage] See above.
    \item[Shove] Opposed Body + Athletics. Push target one band.
    \item[Grapple] Opposed Body + Melee vs. Body + Athletics. Both Engaged;
        target suffers –1 die.
    \item[Charge (full turn)] Move two bands toward an enemy + make one melee
        attack. Cost 1 Fatigue, next defense Desperate, requires open ground.
    \item[Skirmish (full turn)] Move two bands away from an enemy + make one
        ranged attack. Cost 1 Fatigue, next defense Desperate, requires open
        ground.
    \item[Observe / Change Position] Gain +1 Position once.
    \item[Setup] Grant an ally +1 Position or step up Effect (requires a roll).
    \item[Assist] Spend 1 Boon to give an ally +1 die (max +3 from all assists).
    \item[Defend, Channel/Weave, Cast Rite/Song, Interact, etc.]
\end{description}

\subsection{Initiative}
There is no fixed initiative order. The GM frames spotlight order based on the
fiction. Ambushers act first; the spotlight may shift mid‑scene if narratively
appropriate.

\subsection{Opportunity Attacks}
Leaving an enemy’s Close range without using Disengage provokes one free melee
attack (Controlled Position, no action cost).

\section{Fatigue and Harm}\label{sec:fatigue_harm}

\subsection{Fatigue Track}
Your Fatigue track size equals your Body attribute. Mark Fatigue for physical
exertion, magical strain, stress, or harsh conditions.

\paragraph{Effects}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Each point of Fatigue worsens your Position by one step
        (Dominant $\rightarrow$ Controlled $\rightarrow$ Desperate).
    \item If already Desperate, each additional Fatigue instead imposes a –1 die
        penalty on all rolls.
    \item \textbf{Overflow:} When the track fills, increase Harm by one level and
        clear all Fatigue.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Recovery}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Short rest (one hour, quiet): remove 2 Fatigue.
    \item Full night’s rest in safety: remove all Fatigue.
    \item Fatigue cannot be removed while the party is at Dangerous or Empty
        Supply (see \autoref{sec:supply_clock}).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Harm Levels}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{Harm Level} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
Harm 1 & –1 die on related actions. \\
Harm 2 & –1 die on most actions until treated. \\
Harm 3 & Incapacitated or dying. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Taking Harm and Fatigue – The Procedure}
When you are hit, follow these steps in order:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Convert} incoming Harm to Fatigue using the Armor Conversion
        Table (see \autoref{tab:armor_conversion}). Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit,
        even if conversion would give 0.
    \item \textbf{Apply remaining Harm} – if any Harm remains after conversion,
        increase your Harm level by that amount, and clear all existing Fatigue
        (the shock of a wound resets your exhaustion).
    \item \textbf{Add the Fatigue} from step 1 to your Fatigue track. If this
        fills or exceeds your track, resolve Overflow (Harm +1, clear all Fatigue).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Armor Conversion Table}\label{tab:armor_conversion}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Armor Type} & \multicolumn{3}{c}{\textbf{Incoming Harm}} \\
\cmidrule(lr){2-4}
                   & Harm 1 & Harm 2 & Harm 3 \\
\midrule
Light  & Fatigue 1 & Harm 1 + Fatigue 1 & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Medium & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1           & Harm 2 + Fatigue 1 \\
Heavy  & Fatigue 1 & Fatigue 1           & Fatigue 2 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit.

\subsection{Fear Effects (Optional)}
When a Fear Track escalates (Shaken $\rightarrow$ Frightened $\rightarrow$ Panicked),
the GM may roll a d10 or choose an effect appropriate to the fiction.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
d10 & Effect \\
\midrule
1 & Freeze (cannot act). \\
2 & Flee. \\
3 & Drop held item. \\
4 & Beg or bargain. \\
5 & Hide. \\
6 & Panic attack (target nearest creature). \\
7 & Blunder into danger. \\
8 & Obey a simple command. \\
9 & Break down (useless until aided). \\
10 & Catatonia. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Skills}\label{sec:skills}

Your dice pool for any action is \textbf{Attribute + Skill}. The GM sets
Position and DV based on your approach.

\toprule

\midrule

\subsection{Alternative Attribute + Skill Pairs}
The same fictional action can often use different Attributes. For example:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Climbing:} Body + Athletics (strength) or Wits + Athletics
        (routefinding) or Spirit + Athletics (determination).
    \item \textbf{Persuading a guard:} Presence + Sway (charm) or Wits + Sway
        (logic) or Spirit + Sway (conviction).
\end{itemize}
The GM has final say; creative alternatives are encouraged when they fit the
fiction.

\section{Experience Points (XP)}\label{sec:xp}

XP is earned by engaging meaningfully with the world: overcoming challenges,
taking risks, making hard choices, and leaning into your character’s story.

\subsection{Awarding XP (per session)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Base participation: +2 XP.
    \item Major objective: +2–4 XP.
    \item Discoveries (lore, locations, secrets): +1–2 XP.
    \item Hard choices (moral or strategic): +1–2 XP.
    \item Story engagement (embracing complications): +1–3 XP.
    \item Personal goals: +1–2 XP.
\end{itemize}
At the end of a major story arc, everyone gains an additional +8–12 XP.

\subsection{Spending XP}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Attribute increase:} new rating ×3 XP; downtime = new rating days.
    \item \textbf{Skill increase:} new level ×2 XP; downtime = new level days.
    \item \textbf{Talents:} 2 XP (minor) to 12+ XP (epic).
    \item \textbf{Follower (Capability):} Cap² XP; downtime 1–3 days.
    \item \textbf{Asset:}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Minor (4 XP, 1 day)
            \item Standard (8 XP, 1 week)
            \item Major (12 XP, 1 month)
        \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Rush Rule}
You may attempt to train faster. The GM starts a Risk Clock [4]. Each segment
you roll Spirit + Resolve (DV 3). When the clock fills, the new capability gains
a flaw (e.g., –1 die penalty, unreliability, hidden problem).

\subsection{Reputation Tiers}
\begin{description}
    \item[Novice (0–40 XP)] Local reputation, prestige locked.
    \item[Seasoned (41–90 XP)] Regional influence, some prestige unlock.
    \item[Veteran (91–150 XP)] National reputation, second follower slot.
    \item[Paragon (151–220 XP)] Continental influence, rivals emerge.
    \item[Mythic (221+ XP)] Legendary, kingdoms react.
\end{description}

\section{Game Structure}\label{sec:game_structure}

\subsection{Narrative Time}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{A Moment:} a heartbeat, a single action.
    \item \textbf{Some Time:} a few minutes (skirmish, negotiation).
    \item \textbf{Significant Time:} hours (march, ritual, stakeout).
    \item \textbf{Days:} extended effort, travel, research.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Game Units}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Scene:} basic narrative unit, covers a specific conflict or
        question.
    \item \textbf{Session:} 3–6 hours, contains 2–4 major scenes.
    \item \textbf{Campaign:} entire story arc (6–20+ sessions).
\end{itemize}

\section{Assets and Followers (Quick Reference)}\label{sec:assets_followers}

\subsection{Assets (Off‑Screen)}
Cost: Minor (4 XP, 1d), Standard (8 XP, 1w), Major (12 XP, 1m).

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Free off‑screen use:} once per session.
    \item \textbf{Boon activation:} spend 1 Boon for on‑scene effect.
    \item \textbf{Status:} Flourishing $\rightarrow$ Stable $\rightarrow$ Strained $\rightarrow$ Collapsed.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Followers (On‑Screen)}
Cost: Cap² XP (e.g., Cap 3 = 9 XP), downtime 1–3 days.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Assistance:} add dice = min(Cap, relevant skill), max +3 from
        all sources.
    \item \textbf{Independent action:} once per scene (party‑wide). Cost: worsen
        Loyalty or Fitness one step.
    \item \textbf{Loyalty:} Faithful $\rightarrow$ Strained $\rightarrow$ Broken.
    \item \textbf{Fitness:} Ready $\rightarrow$ Hurt $\rightarrow$ Down.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Upkeep (per downtime)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Efficient:} XP = max(1, acquisition XP / 3). Minimal effort.
    \item \textbf{Intensive:} 1 XP + a dedicated downtime action.
\end{itemize}
Failure to pay: Asset Status worsens one step; Follower Loyalty worsens one step.

\section{Supply Clock}\label{sec:supply_clock}

The Supply Clock (0–3 segments) tracks the party’s access to essentials: food,
water, gear, and logistical support.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
\textbf{Segments} & \textbf{Narrative Effects} \\
\midrule
0 (Full) & Well‑provisioned; no penalty. \\
1 (Low)  & Minor complications (bland rations, damaged arrows). \\
2 (Dangerous) & Each character gains Fatigue 1. \\
3 (Empty) & Severe penalties (starvation, dehydration, equipment failure). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{When to advance the Supply Clock}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Extended travel through hostile lands without proper provisioning.
    \item GM spends 2+ SB on logistics failures or environmental hardships.
    \item The party chooses to travel light for speed or stealth.
    \item Failed Survival or Craft rolls related to hunting, foraging, or repair.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Clearing Supply segments}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reaching civilisation resets the clock to Full.
    \item A group Survival check (Wits + Survival, DV 2) in favourable
        conditions clears 1 segment.
    \item Downtime spent in relative safety removes 1 segment.
    \item Successful provisioning (a good hunt, a discovered cache) can reduce
        segments.
\end{itemize}
