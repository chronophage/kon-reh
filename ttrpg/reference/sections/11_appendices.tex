\section{Common Rules Questions}Ňāabel{sec:rules_questions}
‚ąądex{Rules questions}

\subsection{Core Mechanic Clarifications}Ňāabel{subsec:core_clarifications}
\paragraph{Q: Can players re‚ÄĎroll 1s to remove Story Beats?}
A: No. Re‚ÄĎrolling 1s does not remove SB already generated. If re‚ÄĎrolled dice show 1 again, they generate additional SB.

\paragraph{Q: When does a miss or partial success award Boons?}
A: Only when all three conditions are met: procedure followed, stakes stated, and consequence lands immediately.

\paragraph{Q: Can players assist each other on every action?}
A: Yes, but total assist dice are capped at +3 from all sources combined.

\subsection{Magic System Questions}Ňāabel{subsec:magic_questions}
\paragraph{Q: Can a character use multiple magic paths?}
A: Yes, but each path has its own tracking (Backlash, Obligation, Symbol states). Specialising is more efficient.

\paragraph{Q: How does Crack the Seal work for Invokers?}
A: Convert a ritual to instant casting by setting the Symbol to \emph{Compromised} and marking +2/+3 Obligation.

\subsection{Combat and Conflict Questions}Ňāabel{subsec:combat_questions}
\paragraph{Q: How does the Over‚ÄĎStack rule work?}
A: If the party \emph{enters} a scene with $‚Č• 3$ structural advantages, either start one challenge at +1 difficulty \emph{or} bank +1 SB.

\paragraph{Q: Can players spend Boons to improve Position?}
A: Yes. 1 Boon improves Position by 1 step for the current action.

    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Drive (‚ô•):} The villain's overarching scheme.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Complication (‚ô£):} Ongoing schemes/assets‚ąí‚ąí‚ąírival factions, crises, economic pressure.

\subsection{GM Procedure}
\begin{enumerate}
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Choose Archetype:} Solo, Mixed, or Mastermind.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Draw Four:} One per suit. Highest rank = Phase Timer size.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Interpret:} Combine Structure, Drive, Complication, Reward.

\subsection{Terrestrial Patrons}
Not all patrons are gods, demons, or cosmic forces. Mortals create power too: nobles, guilds, conspiracies, temples, syndicates, and commanders. A Terrestrial Patron represents an ongoing relationship with a powerful mortal faction.

\begin{description}
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{Why They Matter}] A terrestrial patron doesn"t grant magic. They grant \emph{leverage}: protection, resources, sanctuary, information, and political shifts.
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{Obligation (Terrestrial)}] Use the same Obligation track, but consequences are social, legal, or economic instead of supernatural. When you call on a Patron's influence, add +1 Obligation.
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{Getting a Patron}] Complete one of: a major job, a sworn Oath, legal/financial binding, blackmail or shared crime. Mark them on your sheet and write one sentence: "They want me because ___"
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{Perks}] Each Patron offers 2‚ąí‚ąí3 repeatable benefits (sanctuary, legal relief, black‚ÄĎmarket goods, elite followers, forged documents, military backing, rumours). Using a Perk never requires a roll.
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{Demands}] Terrestrial Patrons always want something back: silence, loyalty, a job, a name, a secret. Refusing raises Obligation by 1. Betrayal may have immediate consequences.
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{When Obligation Fills}] At 6 Obligation, the Patron acts. Choose one: you do a job you cannot refuse; you pay a severe price; they strike first. Reduce Obligation to 3 after the consequence lands.
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{Cutting Ties}] Lose all current perks, gain a new Rival faction, take a Curse, Bounty, or Scandal that follows you.
    ‚ĄĻtem[\textbf{Redemption or Favor}] A monumental service reduces Obligation by 2 and grants a permanent Favor: title, land, permanent access, unique asset.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Quick Example}
The Black Ledger smuggling syndicate gives sanctuary and illegal gear. Rellan calls on the Ledger for a smuggled border crossing. The GM rules it succeeds automatically, but adds +1 Obligation. Rellan now owes the Ledger. Later, the Ledger demands he silence a witness. If he refuses, Obligation rises again. If Obligation ever reaches 6, the Ledger collects: accounts frozen, bounty posted, or a rival informant sent after him.
