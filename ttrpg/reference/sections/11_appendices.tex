\subsection{Core Mechanic Quick Reference}
\label{subsec:core-mechanic-ref}
\index{Appendices!Core Mechanic}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Approach}: Player states intent and method (Attribute + Skill combination).\index{Core Mechanic!Approach}
\item \textbf{Execution}: Roll dice pool of d10s. Each 6+ is a success; each 1 generates 1 Story Beat.\index{Core Mechanic!Execution}
\item \textbf{Outcome}:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Basic} — Roll as-is; all 1s generate SB.
  \item \textbf{Detailed} — Re-roll one die showing 1.
  \item \textbf{Intricate} — Re-roll all 1s; add a positive flourish on success.
  \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Upkeep}\label{sec:upkeep-srd}
\index{Upkeep}\index{Downtime}\index{Followers}\index{Assets}

\textbf{Frequency.} Pay upkeep once per Downtime period.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Efficient (Higher XP, Less Time).} \emph{Cost:} Upkeep XP $= \max\big(1, \tfrac{\text{XP Acquisition Cost}}{3}\big)$. \emph{Time:} Minimal; delegation/check-in.
\item \textbf{Intensive (Lower XP, More Time).} \emph{Cost:} 1 XP. \emph{Time:} One dedicated Downtime action of significant personal attention.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Failure.} If upkeep is not paid this Downtime, the resource degrades:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Follower:} becomes \textbf{Wary} (or \textbf{Seized} if already Wary).
\item \emph{Asset:} becomes \textbf{Neglected} (or \textbf{Compromised} if already Neglected).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Notes.} Each follower/asset checks upkeep separately; a single Intensive action may cover a cohesive group if fiction supports it. Tie upkeep scenes to Patron themes for flavor, not discounts.

\subsection{Deck Types and Meanings}
\label{subsec:deck-types}
\index{Appendices!Deck Types}

\begin{description}
\item[\textbf{Travel Decks} (regional, 52-card)] Used for journey content and location-based adventures.\index{Decks!Travel}
\begin{itemize}
\item Spade $=$ Place/Location
\item Heart $=$ Actor/Faction
\item Club $=$ Pressure/Complication
\item Diamond $=$ Leverage/Reward
\end{itemize}

\item[\textbf{Deck of Consequences} (scene drama)] Used for immediate complications and narrative twists.\index{Decks!Consequences}
\begin{itemize}
\item Hearts $=$ Social/Emotional fallout
\item Spades $=$ Harm/Escalation
\item Clubs $=$ Material cost/Resource drain
\item Diamonds $=$ Magical/Spiritual disturbance
\end{itemize}
\end{description}

\textbf{Important:} Never mix suit meanings across decks. Travel deck suits differ from Consequences deck suits.

\subsection{Deck Usage Procedure}
\label{subsec:deck-procedure}
\index{Appendices!Deck Procedure}

After a roll generating Story Beats:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Direct Spend}: Translate SB into immediate consequences or clock ticks.
\item \textbf{Deck Draw}: Draw up to $\min(\text{SB},\,3)$ cards and synthesize a single twist.
\item Interpret cards based on suit meanings and highest rank.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Rank Severity Guide}
\label{subsec:rank-severity}
\index{Appendices!Rank Severity}

\begin{description}
\item[Ace--3] Minor inconvenience or flavor complication.\index{Decks!Ranks!Minor}
\item[4--6] Moderate setback with narrative impact.\index{Decks!Ranks!Moderate}
\item[7--9] Significant consequence altering the scene.\index{Decks!Ranks!Significant}
\item[10--King] Major fallout introducing new problems or lasting effects.\index{Decks!Ranks!Major}
\end{description}

\section{Common Rules Questions}
\label{sec:rules-questions}
\index{Appendices!Rules Questions}

\subsection{Core Mechanic Clarifications}
\label{subsec:core-clarifications}
\index{Appendices!Core Clarifications}

\textbf{Q: Can players re-roll 1s to remove Story Beats?}\\
A: No. Re-rolling 1s does not remove SB already generated. If re-rolled dice show 1 again, they generate additional SB.\index{Story Beats!Re-rolling}

\textbf{Q: When does a miss or partial success award Boons?}\\
A: Only when all three conditions are met: procedure followed, stakes stated, and consequence lands immediately.\index{Boons!Award Conditions}

\textbf{Q: Can players assist each other on every action?}\\
A: Yes, but total assist dice are capped at +3 from all sources combined.\index{Mechanics!Assist Maximum}

\subsection{Magic System Questions}
\label{subsec:magic-questions}
\index{Appendices!Magic Questions}

\textbf{Q: Can a character use multiple magic paths?}\\
A: Yes, but each path has its own tracking (Backlash, Obligation, Symbol states). Specializing is more efficient.\index{Magic!Mixing Paths}

\textbf{Q: How does Crack the Seal work for Invokers?}\\
A: Convert a ritual to instant casting by setting the Symbol to \emph{Compromised} and marking +2/+3 Obligation.\index{Invokers!Crack the Seal}

\textbf{Q: What happens when a Patron's Obligation clock fills?}\\
A: The GM resolves the debt in-fiction through service demands, omens, or narrative consequences.\index{Magic!Obligation Resolution}

\subsection{Combat and Conflict Questions}
\label{subsec:combat-questions}
\index{Appendices!Combat Questions}

\textbf{Q: How does the Over-Stack rule work?}\\
A: If the party enters a scene with $\geq$ 3 structural advantages, either start one challenge at +1 difficulty \emph{or} bank +1 SB.\index{Combat!Over-Stack}

\textbf{Q: Can players spend Boons to improve Position?}\\
A: Yes. 1 Boon improves Position by 1 step for the current action.\index{Combat!Position Improvement}

\textbf{Q: How does harm recovery work?}\\
A: Minor clears with rest; moderate requires medical treatment (DV 2); severe needs extended care (DV 3); critical requires major intervention.\index{Combat!Harm Recovery}

\subsection{Adventure Structure Template}
\label{subsec:adventure-template}
\index{Appendices!Adventure Template}

\textbf{Standard Three-Act Structure:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Introduction}: Establish situation; introduce key NPCs and locations.
\item \textbf{Development}: 2--3 challenges that advance the main objective.
\item \textbf{Climax}: Major confrontation or resolution point.
\item \textbf{Resolution}: Consequences and setup for future adventures.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Alternative Structures:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Hex Crawl}: Exploration-focused with multiple points of interest.
\item \textbf{Mystery}: Investigation-driven with clue accumulation.
\item \textbf{Siege}: Defense-focused with resource management.
\item \textbf{Journey}: Travel-based with episodic encounters.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Usage Notes}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{DV guidance:} 2 small/local, 3 scene-scale, 4 big swing, 5+ set-piece/ritual.
  \item \textbf{Duration defaults:} Buffs $\approx$ 3 beats; areas 1 beat. Sustaining costs 1 Fatigue/beat.
  \item \textbf{Costs:} “SB” = Story Beats generated/at risk; \emph{Obligation} applies to \textbf{Ritual} sources; some tags list sustain/asset ticks.
  \item \textbf{Counters:} A listed counter doesn’t auto-succeed—resolve with the appropriate action (often \texttt{COUNTER}, \texttt{DISPEL}, or fiction).
  \item \textbf{Gating:} “Gated By” lists \emph{typical} prerequisites—swap in specific Patrons, Arts, or Talents to taste.
\end{itemize}

\small
\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Rituals (SRD Format),colback=gray!4,colframe=black!60]
Each ritual lists: \textbf{Purpose}, \textbf{Prereqs} (minimum pools), \textbf{Time}, \textbf{Duration}, \textbf{Test} (typical DV), \textbf{Effect}, \textbf{Backlash (SB tiers)}.\\
\emph{Position:} Unless stated, assume \textbf{Controlled}. Consecrated/strong leverage may grant \textbf{Dominant}; hostile site/opposition may impose \textbf{Desperate}.\\
\emph{Criticals:} A roll of \textbf{10} counts as \emph{two successes}; does not auto-succeed if total $< \mathrm{DV}$.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Exorcism (Core)}
\textbf{Purpose:} Expel a possessing entity. \\
\textbf{Prereqs:} Spirit~3+, Lore~3+, Insight~2+. \quad \textbf{Team:} 1+ helpers (Lore/Spirit/Insight/Command/Presence). \\
\textbf{Time:} Significant Time (phased). \quad \textbf{Duration:} Encounter.\\
\textbf{Structure:} \emph{Contested clock} = $\max(4,\min(8, 2 + \text{Subject Tier}))$. Each \textbf{segment} resolves three beats:
\begin{enumerate}\item \textbf{Invocation:} Spirit+Lore vs DV (Tier).
\item \textbf{Confrontation:} Presence+Insight \emph{or} Command vs DV (Tier).
\item \textbf{Binding:} Wits + relevant (Tinker/Arcana/Rites) vs DV (Tier).\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Entity Resists/Acts:} On any beat where entity meets/exceeds total successes, it attempts one response (\textit{Whispers, Manifestation, Assault, Distortion}) at DV (Tier); on a fail to resist, \emph{advance the Exorcism clock +1 segment}.\\
\textbf{Effect (Success):} Three consecutive beats exceeding DV by $2+$ across segments expel the entity; subject stabilizes (Harm~2 care); participants mark Fatigue~1 per entity Tier. Mark 1~XP for major spiritual conflict.\\
\textbf{Failure:} Clock fills $\rightarrow$ exorcism fails; entity empowered (+1 die on future possessions), subject worsens (Harm +1 level or Corruption), all participants Fatigue~2; start \emph{Haunting [6]}.\\
\textbf{Backlash (SB):} \emph{1–2 SB} minor phenomena; \emph{3–4 SB} splintering influence (bank +2 SB later); \emph{5+ SB} possession flares (impose Desperate next beat).\\
\textbf{PC Possession (Ongoing):} If a PC remains possessed: bank \emph{Tier I–II: 1 SB}, \emph{III–IV: 2 SB}, \emph{V+: 3 SB} per session as intrusion fuel (player retains meaningful agency; resistance is always offered).

\section*{Cleansing Fire}
\textbf{Purpose:} Purify curses/contamination; weaken hauntings.\\
\textbf{Prereqs:} Spirit~4+, Arcana~3+, Lore~2+. \quad \textbf{Time:} Significant Time. \quad \textbf{Duration:} Immediate; site/object is clean for 1 month.\\
\textbf{Test:} Spirit+Lore (prep) then Presence+Arcana to enact, DV~3–4 (by strength).\\
\textbf{Effect:} Remove one curse/taint; beneficiaries gain +1 die to resist similar effects for a scene. If target is possessed, entity suffers $-1$ die this scene.\\
\textbf{Backlash:} \emph{1–2 SB} partial; returns at half strength next day. \emph{3–4 SB} agitation (+2 SB next encounter). \emph{5+ SB} inward scorch (caster Harm~1; minor fire-themed flaw).

\chapter{Disabling Traps and Wards}\label{chapter:disabling-wards}
\index{Wards}\index{Traps}\index{DV}\index{Position}\index{Clocks}\index{Story Beats}

\textbf{Traps} are mechanical obstacles (barriers, Harm, Fatigue/poison).  
\textbf{Wards} are the magical analog (sensors, bindings, anchored effects).  
Resolve like any obstacle: \textbf{state the fiction}, \textbf{set Position}, \textbf{assign DV}, \textbf{roll}, \textbf{spend SB}.

\section*{At the Table: Fast Structure}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{Step~\arabic*:}, leftmargin=2.1em]
\item \textbf{Name the Ward/Trap.} What it senses (heat, oath, Patron), what it does (alarm, bind, blast), and \emph{where} it anchors (sigil, keystone, idol, threshold).
\item \textbf{Reveal Two+ Handles.} Always surface multiple approaches in fiction:
  \begin{itemize}\item \textbf{Arcane Pattern} (\emph{Wits+Arcana})
  \item \textbf{Mechanism/Anchor} (\emph{Wits+Tinker})
  \item \textbf{Pathing/Timing} (\emph{Body+Agility/Move})
  \end{itemize}
  Tools, rare components, or Patron symbols count as handles.
\item \textbf{Set Position}, then \textbf{Set DV (from access/pressure)}:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Position:} \emph{Dominant} (one fail die re-roll), \emph{Controlled}, \emph{Desperate} (severe consequences).
  \item \textbf{Typical DV (do not bind to Position):}
    \begin{description}[leftmargin=1.6em]
    \item[DV~2] Clear access, right tools, time to work.
    \item[DV~3] Partial access, time pressure, mild interference.
    \item[DV~4–5+] Hostile field, active trigger, ancient/large scale.
    \end{description}
  \end{itemize}
\item \textbf{Declare Stakes.} Say what a Partial/Miss does. If relevant, put a \textbf{Ward Clock} and an \textbf{Alert Clock} on the table.
\end{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Body+Agility:} thread safe arc; pin plate; time the chime.

\item \textbf{Talents/Tools:} lockpicks, ward-ink, tuning forks, mirror-shards; aligned Patron sign may improve Position \emph{one step}.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Clocks that Matter}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item[\textsc{Ward Integrity} (4):] proximity to \emph{disarm or rupture}. Partial: +1. Miss: +2.
\item[\textsc{Alerted Attention} (4):] guardians/spirits/owners notice tampering.
\item[\textsc{Escalation} (4/6):] punitive effects ramp (more Harm/area/tags).
\end{description}

\section*{Outcome Palette}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Strong Hit:} Disabled or cleanly redirected. Optionally \emph{bank a tell} (you recognize this signature later).
\item \textbf{Partial:} Suppressed but unstable; pick one: tick \textsc{Alerted Attention}+1; apply a small elemental/Patron backlash; leave a trace that can be read later.
\item \textbf{Miss:} Ward holds or barks. GM may (a) advance \textsc{Ward Integrity} toward rupture, (b) trigger a reduced effect now, (c) spend SB for a hard complication.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Counter-Ward (Arcana focus; optional).}
On a success choose: \textbf{Suppress} (one scene) \emph{or} \textbf{Disarm} (permanent; pay rare material or mark +1 Obligation) \emph{or} \textbf{Invert} (first trigger redirects to a safe sink; start \textsc{Unintended Echo} 1/4).

\section*{Difficulty Dials (GM)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Scale:} Vast/ancient lattice: +1 DV or add a second clock.
\item \textbf{Haste:} Rushing worsens \emph{Position} one step.
\item \textbf{Right of Names:} Knowing the warder’s true name improves \emph{Position} one step \emph{or} grants +1d.
\item \textbf{Opposed Patron:} Using hostile symbols imposes –1d \emph{or} +1 DV (table call).
\end{itemize}

\section*{Fiction-First Hooks}
\begin{itemize}
\item Telegraph tells (ozone, cold breath, hair rising) before the roll.
\item On \textbf{Partial}, change the room: new cover lines, shimmered zones, boon-dead spots.
\item On \textbf{Miss}, offer a choice: eat a lesser effect now to avoid full trigger later, or press on and risk rupture.
\end{itemize}

\chapter{Poison as Story Beats}\label{sec:poison-sb}
\index{Poison}\index{Story Beats}\index{Fatigue}\index{Harm}\index{Clocks}\index{Conditions}

\textbf{Principle:} Poison should move the scene forward, not just drain health. It begins with \textbf{Fatigue}, escalates via \textbf{SB}, and resolves through fiction.

\section*{Core Model}
\begin{itemize}
\item Poison applies \textbf{Fatigue} first.  
\item GM may \textbf{spend SB} to escalate: Fatigue $\to$ Condition $\to$ Harm. Always narrate the change.  
\item Track with a \textsc{Poison Clock} (e.g. \emph{Neurotoxin Load} 1/4).  
\item Resist as normal; antidotes, masks, or purge rites improve Position or cancel ticks.  
\end{itemize}

\section*{Delivery Routes}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{>{\bfseries}l l X}
\toprule
Route & Base DV & Cues / Position \\
\midrule
Contact & 2 & Sweat, numbness; Controlled with gloves/tools. \\
Ingested & 3 & Bitter taste, oily film; Controlled if tested. \\
Inhaled & 3--4 & Perfume haze, dust motes; Desperate in tight spaces. \\
Injury/Venom & 3--4 & Fang strike, tainted blade; Controlled unless braced. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\section*{Outcomes}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item[\textbf{Strong Hit.}] Clear 1 tick and ignore the dose.  
\item[\textbf{Mixed.}] Mark \textbf{Fatigue +1} \emph{or} minor \textbf{Condition} (\textsc{Dizzy}, \textsc{Shaking}); GM may tick clock.  
\item[\textbf{Miss.}] Mark \textbf{Fatigue +1} and tick clock 1–2; GM may spend SB to escalate to Harm 1.  
\end{description}

\section*{GM SB Dials}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Pressure Valve:} Spend SB to delay impact, but advance clock.  
\item \textbf{Spike:} Spend SB to frontload with a Condition (\textsc{Blind}, \textsc{Cramping}).  
\item \textbf{Echo:} Bank SB to trigger a relapse later in play.  
\end{itemize}

\chapter{Boss Generator}

\section{Core Concept}

Bosses mirror player archetypes:  
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Solo Boss (The Brute):} A singular, overwhelming threat with unique mechanics.  
  \item \textbf{Mixed Boss (The Commander):} A strong figure who empowers and directs minions.  
  \item \textbf{Mastermind Boss (The System):} A schemer, institution, or environment that opposes players through assets and schemes.  
\end{itemize}

This chapter provides a deck-based tool to generate any of these archetypes.

\section{Deck Structure}

\subsection{Standard Deck (52 Cards)}

\textbf{Suits Define Core Aspects:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{♠ Spades (Structure):} Body, form, core mechanics.  
  \item \textbf{♥ Hearts (Drive):} Motivations, goals, psychology.  
  \item \textbf{♣ Clubs (Complication):} Hazards, minions, environmental twists.  
  \item \textbf{♦ Diamonds (Reward):} Loot, knowledge, or narrative shifts.  
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Ranks Define Scale/Intensity:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{2–5 (Minor):} Elite foe. 4-segment Phase Clock.  
  \item \textbf{6–10 (Standard):} Significant boss. 6-segment Phase Clock.  
  \item \textbf{J/Q/K (Major):} Set-piece villain. 8-segment Phase Clock.  
  \item \textbf{A (Pivotal):} Campaign-defining threat. 10-segment Phase Clock.  
\end{itemize}

\begin{examplebox}[Fatigue and Boons in Action]
\textbf{Situation}: Cael (Cantor) is channeling a ward during a pitched fight.  
He already has \textbf{1 Fatigue}, meaning his next roll will force him to re-roll one success.

\textbf{Roll}: Spirit 3 + Arcana 3 = 6d10 → \{6, 9, 1, 3, 2, 8\}  
Result: 3 successes, 1 SB. DV = 3 (meets requirement).  

\textbf{Fatigue Effect}: Because Cael has 1 Fatigue, he must re-roll one success die.  
He re-rolls the 6 → result = 4 (failure). New total = 2 successes → now a Partial.  

\textbf{Player Action}: Cael spends \textbf{1 Boon} to negate the Fatigue penalty.  
Instead of losing the success, the roll stands at 3 successes.  

\textbf{Outcome}: The ward stabilizes as a clean success. GM still gains 1 SB from the roll.  

\textbf{Lesson}: Fatigue pressures characters by threatening their rolls, but Boons let players 
push back—turning failure into agency without erasing the risk. 
\end{examplebox}

\section{Boss Archetypes}

\subsection{1. The Solo Boss (Brute)}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Focus:} Raw presence, unique mechanics, escalating phases.  
  \item \textbf{Structure (♠):} Defines resistances, body, and attack type.  
  \item \textbf{Drive (♥):} Shapes targeting and tactics.  
  \item \textbf{Complication (♣):} Ongoing hazard or condition (e.g., reality warp, poison cloud).  
  \item \textbf{Reward (♦):} Narrative payoff, artifact, or twist.  
\end{itemize}

\emph{Example: ♠A Titan, ♥J Vengeance, ♣10 Phased Attacks, ♦Q Command Obedience.}  
Interpretation: A colossal, vengeful giant who phases through attacks, defeated only to yield dominion over its kin.

\subsection{2. The Mixed Boss (Commander)}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Focus:} A dangerous leader plus followers. Defeating them requires managing both.  
  \item \textbf{Structure (♠):} Body plus a \emph{Minion Clock}. Followers act each round unless disrupted.  
  \item \textbf{Drive (♥):} Directs how the leader uses minions (e.g., sacrificial pawns, coordinated strikes).  
  \item \textbf{Complication (♣):} Minions escalate scene hazards (fire spreading, reinforcements).  
  \item \textbf{Reward (♦):} Control of followers, faction leverage, or treasure guarded by both.  
\end{itemize}

\emph{Example: ♠6 Swarm Core, ♥K Domination, ♣J Minion Control, ♦10 Faction Favor.}  
Interpretation: A commander directing a swarm. Defeating them secures the loyalty of a faction.

\subsection{3. The Mastermind Boss (Systemic)}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Focus:} A schemer, institution, or environment resolved through multiple scenes.  
  \item \textbf{Structure (♠):} Organization or environment. Represented by multiple linked clocks.  
  \item \textbf{Drive (♥):} The villain’s overarching scheme.  
  \item \textbf{Complication (♣):} Ongoing schemes/assets—rival factions, crises, economic pressure.  
  \item \textbf{Reward (♦):} Narrative leverage: reform, control, or expose the system.  
\end{itemize}

\emph{Example: ♠Q Hive Mind, ♥A Cosmic Hunger, ♣K Reality Distortion, ♦A Worldly Truth.}  
Interpretation: A cult network feeding a god-fragment, warping reality until the players dismantle the cult’s assets.

\section{GM Procedure}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Choose Archetype:} Solo, Mixed, or Mastermind.  
  \item \textbf{Draw Four:} One per suit. Highest rank = Phase Clock size.  
  \item \textbf{Interpret:} Combine Structure, Drive, Complication, Reward.  
  \item \textbf{Mechanize:}  
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Solo:} Pure mechanics and hazards.  
      \item \textbf{Mixed:} Add Minion Clock(s).  
      \item \textbf{Mastermind:} Use Scheme/Asset clocks, faction moves.  
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Phase & Escalate:} Break into 2–3 phases. Increase dice pools or trigger complications.  
  \item \textbf{Tie to Story Beats:} SB fuel Drives and Complications—rage, reinforcements, systemic backlash.  
\end{enumerate}
