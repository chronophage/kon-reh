\subsection{Core Mechanic Quick Reference}\label{subsec:core_mechanic_ref}
\index{Core resolution!quick reference}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Approach:} Player states intent and method (Attribute + Skill combination).\index{Core resolution!approach}
    \item \textbf{Execution:} Roll dice pool of d10s. Each 6+ is a success; each 1 generates 1 Story Beat.\index{Core resolution!execution}
    \item \textbf{Description Ladder:}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item \textbf{Basic} --- Roll as‑is; all 1s generate SB.
            \item \textbf{Detailed} --- Re‑roll one die showing 1.
            \item \textbf{Intricate} --- Re‑roll all 1s; add a positive flourish on success.
        \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Upkeep (Condensed)}\label{subsec:upkeep_srd}
\index{Upkeep}\index{Assets!upkeep}\index{Followers!upkeep}

\textbf{Frequency:} Once per Downtime period.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Efficient (Higher XP, Less Time):} Upkeep XP = \(\max\bigl(1, \frac{\text{XP Acquisition Cost}}{3}\bigr)\). Minimal effort (delegation/check‑in).
    \item \textbf{Intensive (Lower XP, More Time):} 1 XP + one dedicated Downtime action of significant personal attention.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Failure:} If unpaid, resource degrades:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Follower:} becomes \textbf{Wary} (or \textbf{Seized} if already Wary).
    \item \emph{Asset:} becomes \textbf{Neglected} (or \textbf{Compromised} if already Neglected).
\end{itemize}

\section{Deck Usage Reference}\label{sec:deck_reference}
\index{Decks!reference}

\subsection{Deck Types and Meanings}\label{subsec:deck_types}
\begin{description}
    \item[\textbf{Travel Decks} (regional, 52‑card)] Used for journey content and location‑based adventures.
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Spade $=$ Place/Location
            \item Heart $=$ Actor/Faction
            \item Club $=$ Pressure/Complication
            \item Diamond $=$ Leverage/Reward
        \end{itemize}
    \item[\textbf{Deck of Consequences} (scene drama)] Used for immediate complications and narrative twists.
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Hearts $=$ Social/Emotional fallout
            \item Spades $=$ Harm/Escalation
            \item Clubs $=$ Material cost/Resource drain
            \item Diamonds $=$ Magical/Spiritual disturbance
        \end{itemize}
\end{description}
\textbf{Important:} Never mix suit meanings across decks.

\subsection{Deck Usage Procedure}\label{subsec:deck_procedure}
After a roll generating Story Beats:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Direct Spend:} Translate SB into immediate consequences or timer ticks.
    \item \textbf{Deck Draw:} Draw up to \(\min(\text{SB},\,3)\) cards and synthesise a single twist.
    \item Interpret cards based on suit meanings and highest rank.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Rank Severity Guide}\label{subsec:rank_severity}
\begin{description}
    \item[Ace--3] Minor inconvenience or flavour complication.
    \item[4--6] Moderate setback with narrative impact.
    \item[7--9] Significant consequence altering the scene.
    \item[10--King] Major fallout introducing new problems or lasting effects.
\end{description}

\section{Common Rules Questions}\label{sec:rules_questions}
\index{Rules questions}

\subsection{Core Mechanic Clarifications}\label{subsec:core_clarifications}
\paragraph{Q: Can players re‑roll 1s to remove Story Beats?}
A: No. Re‑rolling 1s does not remove SB already generated. If re‑rolled dice show 1 again, they generate additional SB.

\paragraph{Q: When does a miss or partial success award Boons?}
A: Only when all three conditions are met: procedure followed, stakes stated, and consequence lands immediately.

\paragraph{Q: Can players assist each other on every action?}
A: Yes, but total assist dice are capped at +3 from all sources combined.

\subsection{Magic System Questions}\label{subsec:magic_questions}
\paragraph{Q: Can a character use multiple magic paths?}
A: Yes, but each path has its own tracking (Backlash, Obligation, Symbol states). Specialising is more efficient.

\paragraph{Q: How does Crack the Seal work for Invokers?}
A: Convert a ritual to instant casting by setting the Symbol to \emph{Compromised} and marking +2/+3 Obligation.

\subsection{Combat and Conflict Questions}\label{subsec:combat_questions}
\paragraph{Q: How does the Over‑Stack rule work?}
A: If the party \emph{enters} a scene with $\geq 3$ structural advantages, either start one challenge at +1 difficulty \emph{or} bank +1 SB.

\paragraph{Q: Can players spend Boons to improve Position?}
A: Yes. 1 Boon improves Position by 1 step for the current action.

\subsection{Condition \& Upkeep}
\begin{description}
    \item[\textbf{Neglected}] Weapons: --1 die until maintained. Armor: conversion reduced by 1 step (Light=0; Medium treats 2$\rightarrow$1F; Heavy treats 3$\rightarrow$2F).

\textit{Maintenance:} A Short Rest with tools removes \textbf{Neglected}. A scene of work or a Smith removes \textbf{Compromised}.

    \item \textbf{Aim:} +1 die \emph{or} +1 Effect next shot (lost if you Move or are disrupted).
    \item \textbf{Volley:} Spend extra ammo for +1 die (max +2).
    \item \textbf{Suppress:} Zone under fire; foes there act at --1 die or Limited Effect until they shift cover.
    \item \textbf{Overwatch:} Ready a Controlled shot on a clear trigger crossing your line.
\end{itemize}

\section{The Whispered Grimoire (Rituals: Condensed SRD)}\label{sec:rituals_condensed}
\index{Rituals!condensed}

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Rituals (SRD Format),colback=gray!4,colframe=black!60]
Each ritual lists: \textbf{Purpose}, \textbf{Prereqs} (minimum pools), \textbf{Time}, \textbf{Duration}, \textbf{Test} (typical DV), \textbf{Effect}, \textbf{Backlash (SB tiers)}.\\
\emph{Position:} Unless stated, assume \textbf{Controlled}. Consecrated/strong leverage may grant \textbf{Dominant}; hostile site/opposition may impose \textbf{Desperate}.\\
\emph{Criticals:} A roll of \textbf{10} counts as \emph{two successes}; does not auto‑succeed if total $< \mathrm{DV}$.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Exorcism (Core)}
\textbf{Purpose:} Expel a possessing entity. \\
\textbf{Prereqs:} Spirit 3+, Lore 3+, Insight 2+. \quad \textbf{Team:} 1+ helpers (Lore/Spirit/Insight/Command/Presence). \\
\textbf{Time:} Significant Time (phased). \quad \textbf{Duration:} Encounter.\\
\textbf{Structure:} \emph{Contested timer} = \(\max(4,\min(8, 2 + \text{Subject Tier}))\). Each \textbf{segment} resolves three beats:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Invocation:} Spirit+Lore vs DV (Tier).
    \item \textbf{Confrontation:} Presence+Insight \emph{or} Command vs DV (Tier).
    \item \textbf{Binding:} Wits + relevant (Tinker/Arcana/Rites) vs DV (Tier).
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Effect (Success):} Three consecutive beats exceeding DV by 2+ across segments expel the entity; subject stabilises (Harm 2 care); participants mark Fatigue 1 per entity Tier. Mark 1 XP for major spiritual conflict.\\
\textbf{Failure:} Timer fills $\rightarrow$ exorcism fails; entity empowered (+1 die on future possessions), subject worsens (Harm +1 level or Corruption), all participants Fatigue 2; start \emph{Haunting [6]}.\\
\textbf{Backlash (SB):} 1--2 SB minor phenomena; 3--4 SB splintering influence (bank +2 SB later); 5+ SB possession flares (impose Desperate next beat).

\subsection*{Cleansing Fire}
\textbf{Purpose:} Purify curses/contamination; weaken hauntings.\\
\textbf{Prereqs:} Spirit 4+, Arcana 3+, Lore 2+. \quad \textbf{Time:} Significant Time. \quad \textbf{Duration:} Immediate; site/object clean for 1 month.\\
\textbf{Test:} Spirit+Lore (prep) then Presence+Arcana to enact, DV 3--4 (by strength).\\
\textbf{Effect:} Remove one curse/taint; beneficiaries gain +1 die to resist similar effects for a scene. If target possessed, entity suffers --1 die this scene.\\
\textbf{Backlash:} 1--2 SB partial; returns at half strength next day. 3--4 SB agitation (+2 SB next encounter). 5+ SB inward scorch (caster Harm 1; minor fire‑themed flaw).

\subsection*{Bell of Truce}
\textbf{Purpose:} Compel parley before violence.\\
\textbf{Prereqs:} Presence 4+, Spirit 3+, Command 2+. \quad \textbf{Time:} Some Time. \quad \textbf{Duration:} Some Time (~30 min).\\

\section{Disabling Traps and Wards}\label{sec:disabling_wards}
\index{Traps}\index{Wards}

\textbf{Traps} are mechanical obstacles (barriers, Harm, Fatigue/poison). \textbf{Wards} are the magical analog (sensors, bindings, anchored effects). Resolve like any obstacle: state the fiction, set Position, assign DV, roll, spend SB.

\subsection*{At the Table: Fast Structure}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{Step~\arabic*:}, leftmargin=2.1em]
    \item \textbf{Name the Ward/Trap.} What it senses, what it does, and where it anchors.
    \item \textbf{Reveal Two+ Handles.} Always surface multiple approaches in fiction:
        \begin{itemize}
            \item \textbf{Arcane Pattern} (\emph{Wits+Arcana})
            \item \textbf{Mechanism/Anchor} (\emph{Wits+Tinker})
            \item \textbf{Pathing/Timing} (\emph{Body+Agility/Move})
        \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Set Position} then \textbf{Set DV (from access/pressure)}.
        \begin{itemize}
            \item \textbf{Position:} \emph{Dominant} (one fail die re‑roll), \emph{Controlled}, \emph{Desperate} (severe consequences).
            \item \textbf{Typical DV:} DV 2 (clear access, right tools), DV 3 (partial access, time pressure), DV 4--5+ (hostile field, active trigger).
        \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Declare Stakes.} If relevant, put a \textsc{Ward Timer } and an \textsc{Alert Timer } on the table.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Common Ward Types \& Anchors}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{>{\bfseries}l X X}
\toprule
Type & Trigger & Anchor Examples \\
\midrule
Sentry & Threshold, line‑of‑sight, cadence & Lintel sigil, eye‑gem, bell filament \\
Binding & Touch item, break seal, oath‑breach & Wax seal, chain knot, iron brand \\
Punitive & Trespass + timer, named foe & Idol tooth, ash glyph under rug, curse knot \\

\subsection*{Outcome Palette}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Strong Hit:} Disabled or cleanly redirected. Optionally \emph{bank a tell} (you recognise this signature later).
    \item \textbf{Partial:} Suppressed but unstable; pick one: tick \textsc{Alerted Attention}+1; apply a small elemental/Patron backlash; leave a trace that can be read later.
    \item \textbf{Miss:} Ward holds or barks. GM may (a) advance \textsc{Ward Integrity} toward rupture, (b) trigger a reduced effect now, (c) spend SB for a hard complication.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Counter‑Ward (Arcana focus; optional)}
On a success choose: \textbf{Suppress} (one scene) \emph{or} \textbf{Disarm} (permanent; pay rare material or mark +1 Obligation) \emph{or} \textbf{Invert} (first trigger redirects to a safe sink; start \textsc{Unintended Echo} 1/4).

\subsection*{Difficulty Dials (GM)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Scale:} Vast/ancient lattice: +1 DV or add a second timer.
    \item \textbf{Haste:} Rushing worsens \emph{Position} one step.
    \item \textbf{Right of Names:} Knowing the warder's true name improves \emph{Position} one step \emph{or} grants +1d.
    \item \textbf{Opposed Patron:} Using hostile symbols imposes --1d \emph{or} +1 DV (table call).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Worked Mini‑Example}
\emph{Vault Sentry (DV 3, \textbf{Desperate}).} Anchor: eye‑gem set in an idol. Crew uses \emph{Wits+Tinker} with ward‑ink and a mirror shard. \textbf{Partial:} \textsc{Ward Integrity}+1, \textsc{Alerted Attention}+1; beam is shunted for this scene. Later, a rival recognises the mirror signature---trace pays off.

\section{Poison as Story Beats}\label{sec:poison_sb}
\index{Poison}\index{Story Beats}

\textbf{Principle:} Poison should move the scene forward, not just drain health. It begins with \textbf{Fatigue}, escalates via \textbf{SB}, and resolves through fiction.

\subsection*{Core Model}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Poison applies \textbf{Fatigue} first.
    \item GM may \textbf{spend SB} to escalate: Fatigue $\to$ Condition $\to$ Harm. Always narrate the change.
    \item Track with a \textsc{Poison Timer } (e.g. \emph{Neurotoxin Load} 1/4).
    \item Resist as normal; antidotes, masks, or purge rites improve Position or cancel ticks.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Outcomes}

\end{description}

\section{Boss Generator}\label{sec:boss_generator}
\index{Bosses!generator}

Bosses mirror player archetypes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Solo Boss (The Brute):} A singular, overwhelming threat with unique mechanics.
    \item \textbf{Mixed Boss (The Commander):} A strong figure who empowers and directs minions.
    \item \textbf{Mastermind Boss (The System):} A schemer, institution, or environment that opposes players through assets and schemes.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Deck Structure}
\paragraph{Standard Deck (52 Cards)}
\textbf{Suits Define Core Aspects:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{♠ Spades (Structure):} Body, form, core mechanics.
    \item \textbf{♥ Hearts (Drive):} Motivations, goals, psychology.
    \item \textbf{♣ Clubs (Complication):} Hazards, minions, environmental twists.
    \item \textbf{♦ Diamonds (Reward):} Loot, knowledge, or narrative shifts.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Ranks Define Scale/Intensity:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{2--5 (Minor):} Elite foe. 4‑segment Phase Timer .
    \item \textbf{6--10 (Standard):} Significant boss. 6‑segment Phase Timer .
    \item \textbf{J/Q/K (Major):} Set‑piece villain. 8‑segment Phase Timer .
    \item \textbf{A (Pivotal):} Campaign‑defining threat. 10‑segment Phase Timer .
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Boss Archetypes}
\paragraph{1. The Solo Boss (Brute)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Focus:} Raw presence, unique mechanics, escalating phases.
    \item \textbf{Structure (♠):} Defines resistances, body, attack type.
    \item \textbf{Drive (♥):} Shapes targeting and tactics.
    \item \textbf{Complication (♣):} Ongoing hazard or condition.
    \item \textbf{Reward (♦):} Narrative payoff, artifact, or twist.
\end{itemize}
\emph{Example:} ♠A Titan, ♥J Vengeance, ♣10 Phased Attacks, ♦Q Command Obedience → a colossal vengeful giant who phases through attacks, defeated only to yield dominion over its kin.

\paragraph{2. The Mixed Boss (Commander)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Focus:} A dangerous leader plus followers. Defeating them requires managing both.
    \item \textbf{Structure (♠):} Body plus a \emph{Minion Timer }. Followers act each round unless disrupted.
    \item \textbf{Drive (♥):} Directs how the leader uses minions.
    \item \textbf{Complication (♣):} Minions escalate scene hazards.
    \item \textbf{Reward (♦):} Control of followers, faction leverage, or treasure guarded by both.
\end{itemize}
\emph{Example:} ♠6 Swarm Core, ♥K Domination, ♣J Minion Control, ♦10 Faction Favor → a commander directing a swarm; defeating them secures the loyalty of a faction.

\paragraph{3. The Mastermind Boss (Systemic)}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Focus:} A schemer, institution, or environment resolved through multiple scenes.
    \item \textbf{Structure (♠):} Organisation or environment. Represented by multiple linked timers.
    \item \textbf{Drive (♥):} The villain's overarching scheme.
    \item \textbf{Complication (♣):} Ongoing schemes/assets---rival factions, crises, economic pressure.
    \item \textbf{Reward (♦):} Narrative leverage: reform, control, or expose the system.
\end{itemize}
\emph{Example:} ♠Q Hive Mind, ♥A Cosmic Hunger, ♣K Reality Distortion, ♦A Worldly Truth → a cult network feeding a god‑fragment, warping reality until the players dismantle the cult's assets.

\subsection{GM Procedure}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Choose Archetype:} Solo, Mixed, or Mastermind.
    \item \textbf{Draw Four:} One per suit. Highest rank = Phase Timer size.
    \item \textbf{Interpret:} Combine Structure, Drive, Complication, Reward.
    \item \textbf{Mechanise:}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item \textbf{Solo:} Pure mechanics and hazards.
            \item \textbf{Mixed:} Add Minion Timer (s).
            \item \textbf{Mastermind:} Use Scheme/Asset timers, faction moves.
        \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Phase \& Escalate:} Break into 2--3 phases. Increase dice pools or trigger complications.
    \item \textbf{Tie to Story Beats:} SB fuel Drives and Complications---rage, reinforcements, systemic backlash.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Terrestrial Patrons}
Not all patrons are gods, demons, or cosmic forces. Mortals create power too: nobles, guilds, conspiracies, temples, syndicates, and commanders. A Terrestrial Patron represents an ongoing relationship with a powerful mortal faction.

\begin{description}
    \item[\textbf{Why They Matter}] A terrestrial patron doesn"t grant magic. They grant \emph{leverage}: protection, resources, sanctuary, information, and political shifts.
    \item[\textbf{Obligation (Terrestrial)}] Use the same Obligation track, but consequences are social, legal, or economic instead of supernatural. When you call on a Patron's influence, add +1 Obligation.
    \item[\textbf{Getting a Patron}] Complete one of: a major job, a sworn Oath, legal/financial binding, blackmail or shared crime. Mark them on your sheet and write one sentence: "They want me because \_\_\_"
    \item[\textbf{Perks}] Each Patron offers 2--3 repeatable benefits (sanctuary, legal relief, black‑market goods, elite followers, forged documents, military backing, rumours). Using a Perk never requires a roll.
    \item[\textbf{Demands}] Terrestrial Patrons always want something back: silence, loyalty, a job, a name, a secret. Refusing raises Obligation by 1. Betrayal may have immediate consequences.
    \item[\textbf{When Obligation Fills}] At 6 Obligation, the Patron acts. Choose one: you do a job you cannot refuse; you pay a severe price; they strike first. Reduce Obligation to 3 after the consequence lands.
    \item[\textbf{Cutting Ties}] Lose all current perks, gain a new Rival faction, take a Curse, Bounty, or Scandal that follows you.
    \item[\textbf{Redemption or Favor}] A monumental service reduces Obligation by 2 and grants a permanent Favor: title, land, permanent access, unique asset.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Quick Example}
The Black Ledger smuggling syndicate gives sanctuary and illegal gear. Rellan calls on the Ledger for a smuggled border crossing. The GM rules it succeeds automatically, but adds +1 Obligation. Rellan now owes the Ledger. Later, the Ledger demands he silence a witness. If he refuses, Obligation rises again. If Obligation ever reaches 6, the Ledger collects: accounts frozen, bounty posted, or a rival informant sent after him.

\subsection{Archetypal Adversaries}
\label{subsec:archetypal-adversaries}
Each has one thematic mechanic tied to existing rules. Use as recurring threats, scene‑setters, or mini‑bosses. Scale via Threat Level table.

\subsubsection{The Iron Patriarch / Matriarch}
\textit{Archetype:} Ruthless leader of a family, guild, or corporation. Values legacy and control above all.\\
\textit{Stats (Threat Level 2):} Body 2, Wits 3, Spirit 2, Presence 3 \quad | \quad \textit{Skills:} Sway 2, Command 1, Lore 1 (family/guild)\\
\textbf{Thematic Mechanic:} \textit{Legacy's Weight} \\
When the Patriarch/Matriarch would take Harm, they may immediately order a loyal subordinate (if present) to take the blow. Roll Wits + Command \textit{vs. DV 2}. On success, the subordinate takes 1 Harm instead (armour applies normally). On failure, the Patriarch takes the Harm but gains +1 die on their next action (resolve fuelled by desperation).\\
\textit{Flavor Hook:} “They don't fear death—they fear their life's work dying with them. Every blow you strike feels like it's aimed not at them, but at the empire they bled to build.”\\
\textit{Rules Tie-In:} Uses opposed rolls (\autoref{sec:when-to-roll}) and Harm (\autoref{sec:harm}).

\subsubsection{The Hollow Prophet}
\textit{Archetype:} Charismatic leader of a movement built on a lie (or partial truth). Believes their own deception.\\
\textit{Stats (Threat Level 2):} Body 1, Wits 2, Spirit 3, Presence 3 \quad | \quad \textit{Skills:} Sway 2, Lore 1 (doctrine), Deception 1\\
\textbf{Thematic Mechanic:} \textit{Faith's Fragility} \\
When the Hollow Prophet successfully uses Sway to convert a bystander or reinforce a follower's faith, they generate \textbf{1 extra Story Beat} (beyond any generated by 1s). However, if the Sway attempt \textbf{fails} (Miss), they gain a \textit{Despair} Condition: –1 die to all Spirit and Wits rolls until they successfully persuade someone or gain 1 Boon.\\
\textit{Flavor Hook:} “Their eyes glow with conviction as they speak—but when you catch their reflection in a dark window, you see only exhaustion and doubt. The lie sustains them... but how long until it cracks?”\\
\textit{Rules Tie-In:} Uses Story Beats (\autoref{sec:story-beats}) and Conditions (see \autoref{sec:harm-fatigue}).

\subsubsection{The Gearsmith}
\textit{Archetype:} Obsessed tinkerer, weaponsmith, or arcane engineer. Sees people as problems to solve.\\
\textit{Stats (Threat Level 2):} Body 2, Wits 3, Spirit 1, Presence 2 \quad | \quad \textit{Skills:} Craft 2, Mechanics 1, Brawl 1\\
\textbf{Thematic Mechanic:} \textit{Improvised Countermove (Cost: 1 Boon)} \\
When the Gearsmith is targeted by an action (attack, skill use, etc.), the GM may spend 1 Story Beat (or 1 Boon from the GM’s pool – see \autoref{sec:boons-gm}) to invent a reactive device on the spot. The device negates the incoming action and imposes one relevant Condition (e.g., \textit{Entangled}, \textit{Blinded}, \textit{Shaken}) on the attacker. The device is single‑use and narratively described (GM improvises based on the scene). This ability can be used at most once per round.\\
\textit{Flavor Hook:} “They don't dodge your sword—they're already calculating how to turn its momentum against you. That clang wasn't a block; it was the sound of a spring‑loading.”\\
\textit{Rules Tie-In:} Uses Story Beats (\autoref{sec:story-beats}) and Conditions (see \autoref{sec:harm-fatigue}). The GM may also spend Boons from the optional Threat Pool (\autoref{subsec:threat-pool}).

\subsubsection{The Iron-Bound Rival (PC Mirror)}
\textit{Archetype:} Former ally, rival adventurer, or ideological opponent who mirrors the PCs' path.\\
\textit{Stats:} Match the PC's highest two Attributes at their current rating. Match the PC's two highest Skills at their current rating. Other stats default to 1 or 0 as appropriate.\\
\textbf{Thematic Mechanic:} \textit{Anticipate Move} \\
When a PC declares an action targeting the Rival, the Rival gains \textbf{+1 die} to any defensive or opposing roll (e.g., dodging an attack, resisting Sway, countering a skill use). This bonus does not stack if multiple PCs act against the Rival in the same exchange.\\
\textit{Flavor Hook:} “They move not like a fighter, but like a shadow of your own choices—always one step ahead of where you \textit{think} you're going, but never where you \textit{are}.”\\
\textit{Rules Tie-In:} Uses opposed rolls (\autoref{sec:when-to-roll}) and mirrors PC progression (\autoref{sec:advancement}).

\subsubsection{The Weeping Martyr}
\textit{Archetype:} Someone who has lost everything and fights with suicidal abandon.\\
\textit{Stats (Threat Level 2):} Body 3, Wits 1, Spirit 3, Presence 1 \quad | \quad \textit{Skills:} Melee 2, Endurance 2, Intimidation (Presence+Brawl)\\
\textbf{Thematic Mechanic:} \textit{Blood Price} \\
When the Martyr takes Harm, they immediately gain +1 die on their next attack or physical action (pain fuels fury). Additionally, if they are reduced to Harm 3 (incapacitated), they may make one final attack as a free action before falling. This attack ignores all Position penalties and deals +1 Harm.\\
\textit{Flavor Hook:} “They don't even try to block your strike—they lean into it, grinning through bloody teeth. Every wound is another coin in a purse they'll never spend.”\\
\textit{Rules Tie-In:} Uses Harm levels (\autoref{sec:harm}) and the “roller‑coaster” effect (\autoref{sec:roller-coaster}).

\subsubsection{The Shadow Broker}
\textit{Archetype:} Information dealer, blackmailer, or spymaster who never fights fair.\\
\textit{Stats (Threat Level 2):} Body 1, Wits 4, Spirit 2, Presence 2 \quad | \quad \textit{Skills:} Subterfuge 3, Insight 2, Streetwise 2\\
\textbf{Thematic Mechanic:} \textit{I Know a Guy (Cost: 1 Story Beat)} \\
Once per scene, the Shadow Broker may spend 1 Story Beat to declare that they have prepared for the current situation. They produce a minor but useful asset (e.g., a hidden exit, a bribed guard, a forged document) that grants the party (or their own allies) +1 die or improves Position by one step on a single roll. The GM narrates the asset and its limitations.\\
\textit{Flavor Hook:} “You never see them draw a weapon. You only see the trap they set three scenes ago, the witness they paid off, the escape route you didn't know existed.”\\
\textit{Rules Tie-In:} Uses Story Beats (\autoref{sec:story-beats}) and Assets (\autoref{sec:assets-system}). The Broker rarely engages in direct combat; instead, they create advantages.

\subsection{Using These Templates in Play}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Generic foes}: Drop in instantly. Adjust Threat Level via Table~\ref{tab:threat-scaling}. No prep beyond naming them.
    \item \textbf{Archetypal foes}: Pick one matching your scene's theme. Note their one mechanic. Use the \textit{Flavor Hook} to guide narration.
    \item \textbf{Scaling}: If PCs are Tier III, treat Threat Level 2 as baseline. For major encounters, use Threat Level 3 or 4 archetypes.
    \item \textbf{No extra bookkeeping}: Mechanics reference existing rules—just apply them as written. The mechanic is the \textit{only} thing to remember beyond core stats.
\end{itemize}

\chapter{Talents and Special Abilities}
\label{ch:talents}
\index{talents}\index{special abilities}

Talents are the building blocks of character specialization. They represent learned techniques, supernatural gifts, or cultural inheritances. Each Talent costs XP, and its cost reflects its impact.

\section{Understanding Talents}
\label{sec:talents-understanding}
\index{talents!understanding}

Talents are purchased with \textbf{Experience Points (XP)} (see \ref{ch:advancement}) and provide special capabilities:
\begin{itemize}
\item They go beyond simple skill bonuses.
\item They often have specific \textbf{activation conditions}.
\item They may provide \textbf{narrative permissions} (you can try things others cannot).
\item They can define your character's \textbf{unique identity}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Talent Costs by Tier}
\label{sec:talent-costs}
\index{talents!costs}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Tier} & \textbf{Cost} & \textbf{Scope} \\
\midrule
Minor & 2--3 XP & Small situational bonus, once per scene. \\
Major & 4--6 XP & Strong upgrade, permanent effect in a niche. \\
Prestige & 7--10 XP & Campaign-defining, breaks fundamental limits. \\
Epic & 11+ XP & Legendary ability, shapes the story. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Activation Types.}
\label{sec:talent-activation}
\index{talents!activation}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Passive}: Always on; no action required.
\item \textbf{Active}: Requires an action or scene focus to use.
\item \textbf{Reactive}: Triggers automatically on a condition.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Limits and Economy.}
\label{sec:talent-limits}
Unless a talent says otherwise:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Per scene} uses refresh at scene end.
\item \textbf{Per session} uses refresh after downtime.
\item Some talents allow you to spend \textbf{Boons} (see \ref{ch:core-mechanics}) to push effects further.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=yellow!5!white,colframe=yellow!75!black,title=\textbf{Only One Active Talent?}]
\index{talents!active limit}
The rule ``only one talent can be active at a time'' applies only to \textbf{activated abilities} that require a decision or action.  
\textbf{Passive} talents (e.g., +1 die to perception) are always active and stack.  
\textbf{Active} talents that require an action or reaction cannot be used simultaneously -- you choose which one to activate when the trigger occurs.  
Talents that are ``once per scene'' reset at the end of the scene.
\end{tcolorbox}

\textit{Requires: Tier III, Spellcraft, Arcana 5, 2+ Focus Elements.}

\subsubsection*{Crack the Seal Mastery \textnormal{[RITUAL] [PRECISION]}}
\label{talent:crack-seal-mastery}

With 4+ Symbols, reduce all ritual casting times by 1 round. Once per scene, Crack the Seal on up to 3 Symbols as one action. Ignore No Symbol penalties. \\

\subsubsection*{Miststep Reflex \textnormal{[DEFENSE] [MOVEMENT]}}

\subsubsection*{Transcendent Harmony \textnormal{[LIFE] [WIND]}}

\subsubsection*{Deathblow \textnormal{[DEATH] [STRIKE]}}
\label{talent:deathblow}

This ability cannot be used against bosses or creatures with a Tier higher than your own (GM's discretion). \\

Additionally, whenever you generate a Story Beat from a 1, you may immediately spend 1 Boon to cancel that SB (the GM does not gain it). You cannot cancel more than 3 SB per scene this way. \\
\textit{Requires: Spellcraft, Resonance Anchor, Tier III.}

\section{Talent Synergies and Building Strategies}
\label{sec:talent-synergies}
\index{talents!synergies}\index{build strategies}

\subsection*{Synergy Examples}
\label{sec:synergy-examples}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Spellcraft + Elemental Focus + Elemental Mastery}: Reliable freeform casting with reduced backlash and epic control (see \ref{ch:magic}).
  \item \textbf{Backstab + Shadow Dance + Deathblow}: Stealth assassination chain.
  \item \textbf{Ritual Mastery + Efficient Invocation + Crack Specialist}: Fast, safe, low-cost rituals.
  \item \textbf{Defensive Survival + Elusive Dodge + Miststep Reflex}: High survivability in melee.
  \item \textbf{Iron Fist Technique + Flowing Spirit Technique + Untouchable Form}: Unstoppable monk durability.
  \item \textbf{Iron Fist Technique + Perfect Timing Technique + Inevitable Strike}: Monk counterattack master.
  \item \textbf{Effortless Charge + Relentless}: Charge over difficult terrain once per scene without Fatigue cost.
  \item \textbf{Stubborn Pursuer + Tactical Movement}: Stay glued to a disengaging foe while preserving your own movement options.
  \item \textbf{Free Caster Progression}: Controlled Burn $\rightarrow$ Spell Echo $\rightarrow$ Resonance Anchor $\rightarrow$ Weave Harmonist reduces SB generation and turns risk into personal resource.
  \item \textbf{Rogue Progression}: Light Fingers $\rightarrow$ Always Another Exit $\rightarrow$ Ghost Heist for the ultimate phantom thief.
  \item \textbf{Rogue Cantor Progression}: Crowd Veil + Shadow Song for the infiltrator who sings guards to sleep.
  \item \textbf{Mounted Combat Progression}: Mounted Archery $\rightarrow$ Horse Archer's Art for the steppe rider who rains arrows from a galloping horse.
  \item \textbf{Social Face Progression}: Smooth Talker + Trusted Reputation + Spymaster for the character who knows everyone and can call in favours across the city.
  \item \textbf{Follower/Asset Progression}: Efficient Recruiter + Loyalty Bond + Delegated Upkeep for the commander who builds a loyal network that runs itself.
  \item \textbf{Cantor Progression}: Projecting Voice $\rightarrow$ Captivating Performance $\rightarrow$ Master Cantor for the legendary performer who inspires armies.
  \item \textbf{Healer Progression}: Field Dressing $\rightarrow$ Triage for the combat medic who keeps allies fighting.
  \item \textbf{Ranger Progression}: Keen Tracker $\rightarrow$ Camouflage for the scout who moves unseen and never loses the trail.
  \item \textbf{Artificer Progression}: Master Tinker $\rightarrow$ Master Artificer for the crafter who builds anything, faster and cheaper.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{When to Take Talents (By Tier)}
\label{sec:when-to-take-talents}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Early Game (0--40 XP)}: Take 1--2 access talents (Spellcraft, Familiar) and 2--3 minor talents that support your core concept. For Free Casters, prioritize Controlled Burn and Weave Resilience. For Rogues, take Light Fingers and Silk Palms. For mounted archers, take Mounted Archery. For social characters, take Smooth Talker and Ear to the Ground. For healers, take Field Dressing. For rangers, take Keen Tracker. For crafters, take Master Tinker. For performers, take Projecting Voice.
  \item \textbf{Mid Game (41--90 XP)}: Add major talents (Codex, Patron's Symbol, Backstab, The Velvet Glove, Iron Fist Technique, Horse Archer's Art, Trusted Reputation, Efficient Recruiter, Triage, Camouflage, Master Artificer, Captivating Performance, etc.) and 2--3 synergistic power talents. Free Casters should take Elemental Attunement and Backlash Siphon.
  \item \textbf{Late Game (91+ XP)}: Acquire 1--2 prestige or epic talents that define your legacy. Free Casters should aim for Spell Echo, Resonance Anchor, and finally Weave Harmonist. Rogues should consider Shadow Dance, Deathblow, or Ghost Heist. Social characters should aim for Spymaster. Performers should aim for Master Cantor.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Example: The Shadow Assassin}
\label{sec:shadow-assassin-example}
A rogue who takes \textbf{Backstab} (\ref{talent:backstab}) at Tier I, \textbf{Shadow Dance} (\ref{talent:shadow-dance}) at Tier II, and \textbf{Deathblow} (\ref{talent:deathblow}) at Tier III can execute a devastating assassination chain:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Attack from stealth with Backstab (+1 Harm, ignore armor).
\item After a kill, use Shadow Dance to re-enter stealth.
\item Later, strike from Dominant Position with Deathblow (triple Harm).
\end{enumerate}
This combination requires careful positioning and Boon management, but is highly effective.

\subsection*{Example: The Silent Infiltrator}
\label{sec:silent-infiltrator-example}
A rogue who takes \textbf{The Unlocked Window} (\ref{talent:unlocked-window}) and \textbf{Crowd Veil} (\ref{talent:crowd-veil}) at Tier I, then \textbf{Always Another Exit} (\ref{talent:always-another-exit}) at Tier II, can bypass security, vanish into crowds, and escape any corner. Add \textbf{Shadow Song} (\ref{talent:shadow-song}) for the ability to lull guards and unlock spirit-locks with song.

\subsection*{Example: The Stable Free Caster}
\label{sec:stable-freecaster-example}
A Free Caster who takes \textbf{Controlled Burn} (\ref{talent:controlled-burn}) at Tier I, \textbf{Weave Resilience} (\ref{talent:weave-resilience}) at Tier I--II, \textbf{Spell Echo} (\ref{talent:spell-echo}) at Tier II, \textbf{Resonance Anchor} (\ref{talent:resonance-anchor}) at Tier III, and finally \textbf{Weave Harmonist} (\ref{talent:weave-harmonist}) at Tier IV can reduce the swinginess of free casting dramatically. The first 1 of each scene becomes player currency rather than GM fuel; backlash is softened; and once per session, the caster can completely ignore SB from a critical spell. This build remains more dangerous than a Runekeeper but avoids the ``wild mage'' volatility.

\subsection*{Example: The Unarmed Master}
\label{sec:unarmed-master-example}
A monk who takes \textbf{Disciplined Body} (\ref{talent:disciplined-body}) and \textbf{Open Hand Technique} (\ref{talent:open-hand-technique}) at Tier I, then \textbf{Iron Fist Technique} (\ref{talent:iron-fist-technique}) and \textbf{Flowing Spirit Technique} (\ref{talent:flowing-spirit-technique}) at Tier II, and finally \textbf{Untouchable Form} (\ref{talent:untouchable-form}) at Tier III becomes a durable, mobile unarmed fighter who converts harm to fatigue, shrugs off fear, and cannot be grappled.

\subsection*{Example: The Horse Archer}
\label{sec:horse-archer-example}
A Ykrul rider who takes \textbf{Mounted Archery} (\ref{talent:mounted-archery}) at Tier I and \textbf{Horse Archer's Art} (\ref{talent:horse-archers-art}) at Tier II can fire accurately from a galloping horse and use the Skirmish action to move twice as far while shooting. This build excels at hit-and-run tactics, wearing down enemies from a distance while staying out of reach.

\subsection*{Example: The Face of the Party}
\label{sec:face-example}
A social character who takes \textbf{Smooth Talker} (\ref{talent:smooth-talker}) and \textbf{Negotiation Stance} (\ref{talent:negotiation-stance}) at Tier I, then \textbf{Trusted Reputation} (\ref{talent:trusted-reputation}) and \textbf{Information Broker} (\ref{talent:information-broker}) at Tier II, and finally \textbf{Spymaster} (\ref{talent:spymaster}) at Tier III can navigate any court, gather intelligence from thin air, and call in favours from across the city. This build shines in political campaigns and urban intrigue.

\subsection*{Example: The Commander}
\label{sec:commander-example}
A leader who takes \textbf{Efficient Recruiter} (\ref{talent:efficient-recruiter}) at Tier I, \textbf{Loyalty Bond} (\ref{talent:loyalty-bond}) and \textbf{Delegated Upkeep} (\ref{talent:delegated-upkeep}) at Tier II, and builds a network of Cap 3+ followers can run a faction from the shadows. The followers manage Assets, handle upkeep, and remain fiercely loyal. This build is ideal for players who want to build a mercenary company, a spy network, or a criminal empire.

\subsection*{Example: The Legendary Performer}
\label{sec:cantor-example}
A performer who takes \textbf{Projecting Voice} (\ref{talent:projecting-voice}) at Tier I, \textbf{Captivating Performance} (\ref{talent:captivating-performance}) and \textbf{Voice of Reason} (\ref{talent:voice-of-reason}) at Tier II, and finally \textbf{Master Cantor} (\ref{talent:master-cantor}) at Tier III can calm a riot, inspire an army, or negotiate peace between warring factions. Their performances are the stuff of legend.

\subsection*{Example: The Combat Medic}
\label{sec:healer-example}
A healer who takes \textbf{Field Dressing} (\ref{talent:field-dressing}) at Tier I and \textbf{Triage} (\ref{talent:triage}) at Tier II can keep allies alive through the deadliest battles. Field Dressing prevents death from a killing blow; Triage mitigates penalties and restores energy. This build is essential for any party that expects heavy combat.

\subsection*{Example: The Master Artificer}
\label{sec:artificer-example}
A crafter who takes \textbf{Master Tinker} (\ref{talent:master-tinker}) at Tier I, then \textbf{Master Artificer} (\ref{talent:master-artificer}) and \textbf{Asset Crafter} (\ref{talent:asset-crafter}) at Tier II can build a workshop, forge magical items, and construct Assets at half the cost and time. This build is ideal for campaigns with significant downtime and base-building elements.

Work with your GM to create custom talents:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Base on story events.
  \item Use the cost tiers above (2--3 / 4--6 / 7--10 / 11+ XP).
  \item Define clear prerequisites, activation, and limits.

\section{Talent Respecification}
\label{sec:talent-respec}
\index{talents!respecification}
With GM approval, you may retrain talents during downtime (one downtime period). Provide a narrative justification. Typically limited to once per major story arc.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=purple!5!white,colframe=purple!75!black,title=\textbf{Talent Selection Quick Reference},fonttitle=\bfseries]
\textbf{Minor (2--3 XP)}: Small bonuses, once/scene tricks. \\
\textbf{Major (4--6 XP)}: Strong permanent upgrades, core path abilities. \\
\textbf{Prestige (7--10 XP)}: Campaign-shaping, breaks limits. \\
\textbf{Epic (11+ XP)}: Legendary, story-defining power. \\[2mm]
\textit{Remember: Passive talents stack; active talents require an action or reaction.}
\end{tcolorbox}
