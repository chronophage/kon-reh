\section{Design Philosophy}\label{sec:magic_philosophy}
\index{Magic!Design philosophy}

Magic in \textbf{Fate's Edge} is a dangerous negotiation with the fabric of reality. It is powerful and flexible---yet every attempt to shape it carries risk. Each die showing \textbf{1} generates \textbf{Story Beats (SB)}, which are prompts for twists and complications. The fiction leads; math follows.\index{Story Beats}\index{Backlash}

\section{The Four Paths of Magic}\label{sec:four_paths}
\index{Paths of Magic!Overview}

\subsection{Casting (Freeform Magic)}\label{subsec:freeform_casting}
\index{Paths of Magic!Free Caster}\index{Freeform magic}

Freeform casting represents raw, improvisational magic.

\begin{description}
    \item[Requirement] \textbf{Spellcraft} Talent (6 XP).\index{Talents!Spellcraft}
    \item[Mechanics] Two-step \emph{Weave \& Cast} coloured by the Eight Elements; fiction‑first targets and scope.
    \item[Risk] Each 1 generates SB; DV scales with scope; backlash is expressed by Element (or its opposite).\index{Backlash}
    \item[Limits] Heavy control effects (e.g., \texttt{[WARD]}, \texttt{[BANISH]}, \texttt{[UNWARD]}) require a printed source (Talent, \emph{Rite} text, or Spell result).\index{Tags}
\end{description}

\begin{tcolorbox}[title={Backlash Cheatsheet},colback=gray!5,colframe=black]
\textbf{Minor = wobble, Major = lurch.} Types: Position/Effect, Clock +1/2 or +1, Condition, Cost. Offer Major by (SB)+1. Earth/Fate binds; Fire/Life burns/grows; Air/Luck scatters/flips; Water/Obishaal pulls/opens.\par\smallskip
Place a mini version of Table~\ref{tab:backlash_8x2} on character sheets.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{Rites Users (Runekeepers)}\label{subsec:runekeepers}
\index{Paths of Magic!Runekeeper}\index{Rites}\index{Obligation}

Runekeepers bind themselves to structured \emph{Rites} from a single Patron.\index{Patrons}

\begin{description}
    \item[Requirement] \textbf{Thiasos (Familiar)} (2 XP) \emph{and} \textbf{Codex} (4 XP). Runekeepers are restricted to \textbf{one} Patron.\index{Thiasos}\index{Codex}
    \item[Mechanics] \textbf{Invoke} a known \emph{Rite} as \textbf{1 action}; on completion, mark \textbf{+1 Obligation} to that Patron. \emph{Push It} once/scene for amplified effect (\textbf{+1 Obligation}).\index{Obligation}
    \item[Patron's Gift (Imbuement)] With Thiasos, once/scene as \textbf{1 action} imbue a held item for the scene with \textbf{+1 Melee} and \textbf{+1 Thematic} (a fixed Skill set by the Patron). \emph{Push It} to extend one additional scene (\textbf{+1 Obligation}). A Codex is \emph{not} required for the Gift.\index{Imbuement}
\end{description}

\subsection{Rites Difficulty Value (Expanded)}\label{sec:rites_dv_expanded}
\index{Rites!Difficulty Value}\index{Difficulty Value (DV)!Rites}

The Difficulty Value (DV) to cast a Rite is:
\[
\text{DV} = \max\!\bigl(\text{Obligation Cost} - \text{Spirit},\; \text{Tier}\bigr)
\]

\begin{description}
    \item[Obligation Cost:] The Rite's listed cost in Obligation segments.
    \item[Spirit:] The caster's Spirit attribute. Each point reduces the effective weight of the Obligation.
    \item[Tier:] The Rite's intrinsic difficulty based on scope or potency. DV can never fall below this floor.
\end{description}

\paragraph{Worked Examples}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \emph{Novice:} Tier~1 Rite, Obligation Cost~2, Spirit~1 → DV = $\max(2-1,1)=1$.
    \item \emph{Mid‑level:} Tier~2 Rite, Obligation Cost~4, Spirit~2 → DV = $\max(4-2,2)=2$.
    \item \emph{Advanced:} Tier~3 Rite, Obligation Cost~7, Spirit~3 → DV = $\max(7-3,3)=4$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Obligation Capacity}\label{sec:obligation_capacity}
\index{Obligation!Capacity}

A character's \textbf{Obligation Capacity} equals \textbf{Spirit + Presence}. Track total Obligation segments across all Patrons (or Symbols, for Invokers).\index{Spirit}\index{Presence}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Exceeding Capacity:} For each segment above Capacity, mark 1 Fatigue. The character cannot Invoke Rites or perform rituals until Obligation is reduced below Capacity.\index{Fatigue}
    \item \textbf{Resolution:} Reduce Obligation through Downtime service, Patron tasks, ritual cleansing, or story resolution.\index{Downtime}
\end{itemize}
\textit{Example:} Spirit~2 + Presence~3 = Capacity 5. 6 segments → Fatigue 1; 7 segments → Fatigue 2; 10 segments → Harm 1; 11 segments → Harm 2.

\subsection{Invokers (Symbol Path)}\label{subsec:invokers}
\index{Paths of Magic!Invoker}\index{Symbols}

Invokers use consecrated \textbf{Symbols} as ritual anchors to access a Patron's \emph{Rites} without a full bond.

\begin{description}
    \item[Requirement] \textbf{Patron's Symbol} (4 XP) per Patron; one Symbol per Patron. No Thiasos or Codex required.\index{Symbols!Cost}
    \item[Ritual Invocation] Perform the \emph{Rite} as a \textbf{ritual}: \textbf{DV + 1 rounds} to complete. Completion always marks \textbf{+1 Obligation}.
    \item[Symbol Benefit] When the correct \textbf{Symbol} is openly displayed, reduce \textbf{DV by 1} (minimum 1) and reduce the Obligation cost by \textbf{1} (minimum 0).
    \item[No Symbol] Penalties: \textbf{+1 DV} (and +1 round to cast), \textbf{+1 Obligation} (in addition to base cost), and on Partial/Failure the GM gains \textbf{+1 extra SB}.
    \item[Crack the Seal] Resolve a known Rite instantly as \textbf{1 action} by setting the Symbol to \textsc{Compromised} and marking \textbf{+2 Obligation} (\textbf{+3} for High Rites). The GM may immediately spend 1 on‑theme SB. Restore the Symbol in Downtime (test DV 3 or fiction) or spend 1 XP.\index{Crack the Seal}
    \item[Limits] Symbols must be openly displayed. Carrying \textbf{4+} Symbols causes \textbf{+1 Obligation} on the first ritual each scene.
\end{description}

\subsubsection*{Borrowed Grace (Invoker Talent)}\label{talent:borrowed_grace}
\index{Talents!Invoker}\index{Imbuement!Lesser}

\textbf{Cost:} 1 Boon or 1 Fatigue, 1 action. \textbf{Effect (pick one):} +1 Melee \emph{or} +1 Thematic (a signature Skill). \textbf{Duration:} single action/attack only. \textbf{Requirement:} wield/display the relevant Patron's Symbol. \textbf{Obligation:} immediately mark +1 Obligation to that Patron. Cannot extend, stack, or Push.

\section{Summoning (Pact‑Whisperer Core)}\label{sec:summoning}
\index{Summoning}\index{Outsiders}\index{Leash}

Summoning is the art of calling and binding Outsiders (spirits, demons, elementals, etc.) for temporary aid.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Call} (1 Action): Spirit manifests at Near range. Choose a Spirit Template.
    \item \textbf{Bind}: Spend 1 Boon \emph{or} mark 1 Fatigue.
    \item \textbf{Leash Capacity}: Set equal to \textit{Cap} + \textit{Spirit} segments. (\textit{Cap} is the Outsider's tier: Cap~1 for Lesser, Cap~3 for Greater.)\index{Leash!Capacity}
    \item \textbf{Tick Leash} when any occur:\index{Leash!Ticks}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Spirit takes Harm.
            \item You command against its nature.
            \item You split focus (take another significant action while it acts).
            \item Rival contests control.
            \item It moves from Close to Far rapidly.
            \item It crosses a \texttt{[WARD]} (DV = Cap).
        \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Departure}: When the Leash fills, the spirit acts to its nature once, then departs (or turns hostile at GM discretion).
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Boon Finesse:} Once per round, spend 1 Boon to clear 1 tick from the current spirit's Leash (cannot be used after the Leash fills).\index{Boons!Boon Finesse}

\section{Rituals (Extended)}\label{sec:rituals_extended}
\index{Rituals}

Rituals are \emph{slow magic}: explicit intent, staged action, and negotiated risk. This section expands the SRD quick‑start with procedures, dials, and worked examples.

\subsection{Ritual Procedure}\label{subsec:ritual_procedure}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{Step \arabic*:}, leftmargin=2.2em]
    \item \textbf{State Intent.} What do you want? Clarify element/Realm if obvious.
    \item \textbf{Choose Scope.} Size, duration, range, and detail.
    \item \textbf{Lay Components.} Name \emph{two things}: (a) \textbf{Focus} (tool/site/Patron sign), (b) \textbf{Fuel} (herb, blood, pact). Decide which is consumed.
    \item \textbf{Set Time.} Default: Low 1 min / Med 5--10 min / High 15--20 min. More time improves position/effect; rushing worsens.
    \item \textbf{Call Risks.} Point to the element's Backlash and default SB trigger.
    \item \textbf{Roll and Resolve.} Apply position/effect and any clocks. Offer a \emph{push}: take +1 SB to step up result now.
    \item \textbf{Mark Costs.} Consume components, apply Conditions, or tick wear/concurrency per chassis.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Component Economy}\label{subsec:component_economy}
\index{Rituals!Components}

Components are levers, not taxes.

\begin{longtable}{>{\bfseries}l X X}
\caption{Components as Narrative Levers}\label{tab:ritual_components}\\
\toprule
\textbf{Type} & \textbf{Examples} & \textbf{Mechanical Nudge} \\
\midrule

\end{longtable}

\subsection{Teamwork and Aid}\label{subsec:ritual_teamwork}
\index{Rituals!Teamwork}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Hands \& Voices.} Each assistant names one component they contribute; either reduces cast time \emph{or} accepts up to 1 SB on the caster's behalf once per ritual.
    \item \textbf{Focus Chain.} Passing the Focus around grants advantage on the finishing action but risks \textsc{Distracted} if interrupted.
    \item \textbf{Distributed Load.} Splitting a High ritual into two coordinated Mediums avoids a Major backlash but creates two Minor hooks instead.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Clocks and Outcomes}\label{subsec:ritual_clocks}
\index{Rituals!Clocks}
Tie every consequential ritual to \textbf{named clocks} (e.g., \emph{Spreading Fire}, \emph{Inevitable Outcome}, \emph{Crossing Due}). Advancing or reducing clocks is often better than flat bonuses.

\subsection{Backlash Integration}\label{subsec:ritual_backlash}
Use the condensed table (\autoref{tab:backlash_8x2}). Calibrate by scene weight: exploratory scenes favor Minor; pivotal moments bait a Major via +1 SB.

\subsection{Chassis‑Specific Notes}\label{subsec:ritual_chassis}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Runekeeper:} Rites as \emph{accelerants} -- a published Rite may count as a Focus that upgrades position or halves time.
    \item \textbf{Invoker:} Symbols accumulate \emph{wear}; a maintenance rite can clear 1 wear mid‑scene on success or shift backlash from Major to Minor.
    \item \textbf{Summoner:} \emph{Gate} effects occupy concurrency slots. Disruption on broken terms: +1 SB and the entity acts on its last instruction.
    \item \textbf{Caster (Free):} Tags become explicit ritual steps (\emph{bind, veil, reveal}) -- chain two compatible tags once/scene for a synergy bump.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Worked Examples}\label{subsec:ritual_examples}
\paragraph{Quiet Veil (Team Infiltration)} \emph{Intent:} silence the group for one scene. \emph{Components:} ash (fuel, consumed), bell (focus, kept). \emph{Time:} 5 min. \emph{Risks:} Air/Luck Minor; offer Major to avoid dogs" scent. \emph{Roll:} mixed -- effect with cost. \emph{Outcome:} \textsc{Muted} condition until scene ends; Clock --1/2 on \emph{Patrol Pass}.

\paragraph{River's Memory (Investigation)} \emph{Intent:} view last night's ferry landing. \emph{Components:} bowl, token from dock. \emph{Time:} 10 min. \emph{Risk:} Water/Obishaal Minor. \emph{Roll:} strong -- clear image; token ruined. \emph{Outcome:} Clock --1 on \emph{Where did the courier go?}; whisper from the Ways foreshadows a revenant.

\paragraph{Fate‑Splice (Boss Rescue)} \emph{Intent:} move poison from prince to knight. \emph{Components:} paired names on vellum; vow. \emph{Time:} 15 min. \emph{Risk:} Fate/Earth. \emph{Roll:} weak -- Minor Backlash; \emph{Inevitable Outcome} +1/2. \emph{Push:} +1 SB to capture full consequence. \emph{Outcome:} knight bears poison; \textsc{Omen} marks both.

\section{Cantor's Path (Songs)}\label{sec:cantors_path}
\index{Paths of Magic!Cantor}\index{Songs}

Cantors perform Low Rites as Songs, trading Obligation for Corruption.

\begin{description}
    \item[Access] Talent: \textbf{Cantor's Path} (8 XP). Prereqs: Lore 1+, Performance 2+, Presence 2+.
    \item[Scope] \textbf{Low Rites as Songs} only.
    \item[Cast Test] \emph{Lore + Performance vs. DV} (typical DV 2--3).
    \item[Timing] Start with 1 Action; resolves at \emph{start of next turn} unless \emph{Pushed}.
    \item[Cost] Pay listed materials. \emph{No Obligation} on success.
    \item[Push] Resolve now; mark Fatigue 1; mark toward Corruption accumulation; trigger a GM Story Beat (Patron / social fallout).
\end{description}

\paragraph{Corruption Clock}\index{Corruption}
Track a \textbf{Corruption Clock} with segments equal to \textbf{Body}. Mark toward accumulation when: \emph{Push}, perform a \emph{Resonant Rite}, or the GM spends an SB tied to occult activity. On fill: apply the last Patron's benefit \& drawback; then reset to character's Tier minimum (minimum 1).

\paragraph{Corruption Accumulation Triggers}
Multiple triggers required for +1 segment:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{2 Push It uses} = +1
    \item \textbf{1 Push It + 1 Resonant Rite} = +1
    \item \textbf{3 GM SB spends} on occult activities = +1
    \item \textbf{1 High Cantor Standard Rite} = +1
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Repertoire (Optional)}\index{Repertoire}
Single \textbf{Repertoire Clock [6]}: 2 segments = base DV --1 (min 2); 4 segments = +1 die to Song rolls; 6 segments = one \emph{temporary} Standard Rite as Song (practice‑dependent).

\paragraph{Inspire Chorus}\label{subsec:inspire_chorus}
\index{Cantor!Inspire Chorus}
While actively singing a Song, the Cantor may \textbf{invoke Inspire Chorus}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Effect:} All allies within Near shift Position +1 for one exchange (cannot exceed Controlled).
    \item \textbf{Use:} Once per scene free. Additional uses mark toward Corruption accumulation.
    \item \textbf{Requirements:} Performance perceptible to recipients (line of hearing).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Cantor Talents}
\begin{description}
  \item[Resonant Performance (3 XP)] Gain +1 die when performing for an audience of 5+.

  \item[Song Weaver (4 XP)] Combine two compatible Songs for +1 Effect. Two Songs are compatible if they share a thematic tag (e.g., both affect healing, positioning, spirits) or if they are from the same Patron. The GM has final say. Sing both as a single action; resolve the Performance roll once; if successful, both effects occur with +1 Effect.

  \item[High Cantor (18 XP)] Learn Standard Rites as songs; resolve instantly (as described in \ref{sec:cantors_path}).

  \item[Steady Voice (2 XP)] \textbf{Passive.} When you Push a Song, you may choose to mark 1 additional Fatigue instead of marking a Corruption segment (once per scene). You cannot use this talent if your Corruption clock is already full. \textit{Requires: Cantor's Path.}

  \item[Patron's Dedication (4 XP)] \textbf{Prerequisite:} Cantor's Path. \\
  \textbf{Effect:} You bind your voice to a single Patron (choose one when you take this talent). Your Corruption clock is always keyed to that Patron -- it never resets to a different Patron's table when you bloom. Whenever you would gain Corruption from any source, you mark it on this same clock. \\
  \textbf{Downside (Rivalry):} Singing a Song (Low or Standard Rite) of any other Patron worsens your Position by one step (Dominant \(\rightarrow\) Controlled, Controlled \(\rightarrow\) Desperate). This penalty applies even if the song would otherwise be instant or free.
\end{description}

\section{Free Casting (TAGS System)}\label{sec:free_casting_tags}
\index{Freeform magic!TAGS}\index{Tags}

Some casters shape raw forces through shared arcane grammar known as \textbf{TAGS}. Construct a spell at the table using a short phrase of TAGS.

\subsection{Spell Structure}
\textbf{Intent} + \textbf{Target} + \textbf{Tags} = effect. Example: \textit{``I unleash Burning • Area • Force against the marauders.''}

\subsection{Base Difficulty Value (DV)}
\[
\text{DV} = 1 + \text{number of TAGS}
\]
Dangerous TAGS (Teleportation, Transformation, Domination) add +2 DV instead of +1.

\subsection{Casting Roll}
Roll \textbf{Wits + Arcana} (or similar). Success = spell works. Failure or any 1 = Backlash.

\subsection{Backlash for Free Casters}\index{Backlash!Free Caster}
Choose one:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Harm 2 (Arcane)
    \item +2 Fatigue
    \item Corruption +1
    \item Catastrophic side effect (GM describes)
\end{itemize}
If the spell includes a "Dangerous" TAG, Backlash triggers on \emph{mixed} results as well.

\section*{Guardrails}\label{sec:magic_guardrails}
\index{Magic!Guardrails}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Duration defaults:} Buffs $\approx$ 3 beats; areas 1 beat. Sustaining costs 1 Fatigue/beat.
    \item \textbf{Stacking:} Same‑source effects do not stack; take the best instance.
    \item \textbf{Assist cap:} +3 dice total from assists/buffs.
    \item \textbf{Over‑Stack:} Active magic can count as structural advantages for Over‑Stack.
    \item \textbf{Plausibility:} All effects must fit the fiction and established limits.
\end{itemize}
