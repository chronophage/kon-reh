‚ąądex{Aelaerem}‚ąądex{Aelinnel}‚ąądex{Small Folk}

The Aelaerem and Aelinnel are diminutive peoples attuned to liminal spaces and hidden ways. Their stature grants them agility and subtlety, though at the cost of bearing heavy arms or armor.

\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Restriction:} Cannot use \emph{Heavy Armor} or \emph{Heavy Weapons}.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Bonus:} Gain +1 \emph{Position} when Dodging or Resisting Knockback, and +1 die on \emph{Hide} or \emph{Evasion} rolls made while in cover.

\paragraph{Subtle Casting (Major Talent ‚ąí‚ąí 8 XP)}
\textit{Prerequisite:} Lore 3+, Performance 2+ \textbf{or} Runekeeper with Codex.

\noindent \textbf{Effect:} Make a \textbf{Performance + Lore} roll to quietly cast a spell, invoke a Rite, or sing a Cantos against DV (Tier).     ‚ĄĻtem Cannot be used for \textbf{Great} or \textbf{Extreme} Tier effects.

\paragraph{Backstab (Major Talent ‚ąí‚ąí 8 XP)}
\textit{Prerequisite:} Stealth 2+, Melee 2+, Light weapon.

\noindent \textbf{Effect:} When you attack an \textbf{Unaware} or \textbf{Engaged} foe from \textbf{Stealth}, deal \emph{+1 Harm} and ignore \emph{1 point of their Armor}.

\noindent \textbf{Definitions:}
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Unaware:} Target not aware of your presence or hostile intent.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Engaged:} Target is taking an \emph{Attack} action against another character, or casting a spell that targets another character.

\paragraph{Shadow Dance (Synergy Talent ‚ąí‚ąí 10 XP)}

\noindent \textbf{Effect:} After a successful \textbf{Backstab}, test \textbf{Stealth} vs. DV (Tier). On success, re‚ÄĎenter Stealth and either clear 1 Fatigue or improve Position +1. On failure, remain Exposed and mark 1 Fatigue. May only chain once per scene.

\paragraph{Deathblow (Capstone Talent ‚ąí‚ąí 12 XP)}
\textit{Prerequisite:} Shadow Dance, Stealth 4+, Melee/Ranged 3+.

\noindent \textbf{Effect:} Strike from Dominant Position or after re‚ÄĎentering Stealth, declare a Deathblow. On a hit, deal \emph{triple Harm}. If the attack incapacitates the target, attempt a free Stealth test to vanish. On a miss, become Exposed (Desperate Position and mark 1 Harm). Once per scene; may mark 1 Fatigue to attempt a second time.

\section{Optional Rule: Multi‚ÄĎCharacter Followers}Ňāabel{sec:multi_character_followers}
‚ąądex{Followers!Multi‚ąícharacter}‚ąądex{Optional rules}

\subsection{Overview}
A player may control multiple characters by linking them as mutual Followers. This option also covers absent players" characters when consent is given.

\subsection{Core Mechanics}
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Cap Tier +1:} A Follower may advance up to one Tier higher than their leader's current Tier.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Multi‚ÄĎCharacter Play:} A player may designate two PCs as Followers of each other. Only one is the \emph{active} PC at a time; the other functions as a Follower.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Absent PCs:} With consent, an absent player's PC may be run as a temporary Follower at Cap Tier +1.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Switching Active Characters}Ňāabel{subsec:switching‚āźctive}
‚ąądex{Followers!Switching}

\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Switch Cost:} When switching, the previously active PC immediately becomes a Follower of the new active PC at Cap Tier +1.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Condition Gate:} A PC cannot be switched \emph{to} if Compromised, Seized, or otherwise narratively unfit for active duty.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Narrative Justification:} The fiction must support the switch (returning from a journey, recovering from harm, taking point for a specific task).
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Strategic Choice:} Switching trades autonomy for support‚ąí‚ąí‚ąígaining a powerful Follower but losing a full PC.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Balance Notes}
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem Prevents abuse by making switching itself the cost.
    ‚ĄĻtem Maintains narrative logic ‚ąí‚ąí leadership shifts naturally reshape party structure.
    ‚ĄĻtem Creates strategic depth ‚ąí‚ąí switching is a meaningful tactical decision.
    ‚ĄĻtem Keeps all characters relevant, encouraging upkeep even when not active.
\end{itemize}
