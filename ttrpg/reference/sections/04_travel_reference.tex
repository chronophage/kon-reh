\chapter{Travel Reference}

\section{Introduction to Travel}
\label{sec:travel-intro}
\index{Travel}\index{Decks!Travel}\index{Deck-Based Generators}

Travel in \textbf{Fate's Edge} is not just movement between locations—it's an opportunity for adventure, discovery, and complication. The travel system uses deck-based generators to create meaningful journeys that advance the story while presenting challenges and opportunities. \index{Journeys}\index{Exploration}

\section{Core Travel Procedure}
\label{sec:core-travel}
\index{Travel!Core Procedure}\index{Procedures!Travel}

For each leg of a journey, follow this structured approach: \index{Legs of travel}

\subsection{Card Draw Sequence}
\label{subsec:card-draw}
\index{Travel!Card Draw}\index{Decks!Travel!Draw Sequence}

Draw 3–4 cards using the appropriate regional decks:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Spade} from the destination deck: Sets the scene (place and environment).\index{Travel!Spade}\index{Suits!Spade}\index{Places and terrain}
\item \textbf{Heart} from the destination deck: Introduces the local actor or faction.\index{Travel!Heart}\index{Suits!Heart}\index{Factions}
\item \textbf{Club} from the Wilds (general hazards) or destination (if strongly policed): Brings pressure and complications.\index{Travel!Club}\index{Suits!Club}\index{Hazards}
\item \textbf{Diamond} from the authority that controls the route: Represents papers, escorts, rights, or exceptions needed.\index{Travel!Diamond}\index{Suits!Diamond}\index{Permits and papers}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Travel Clock Setup}
\label{subsec:travel-clock}
\index{Travel!Clocks}\index{Clocks!Travel}

Set a travel clock based on the highest card rank drawn:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{2--5} $\Rightarrow$ 4-segment clock (short, straightforward journey).\index{Travel!Clocks!4-segment}\index{Clocks!4-segment}
\item \textbf{6--10} $\Rightarrow$ 6-segment clock (standard journey).\index{Travel!Clocks!6-segment}\index{Clocks!6-segment}
\item \textbf{J, Q, K} $\Rightarrow$ 8-segment clock (extended or complex journey).\index{Travel!Clocks!8-segment}\index{Clocks!8-segment}
\item \textbf{Ace} $\Rightarrow$ 10-segment clock (epic or highly dangerous journey).\index{Travel!Clocks!10-segment}\index{Clocks!10-segment}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Resolution and Consequences}
\label{subsec:travel-resolution}
\index{Travel!Consequences}\index{Consequences!Travel}

\begin{itemize}
\item On success: Advance to the next leg of the journey.\index{Success!Travel}
\item On failure: Mark delay, debt, or diversion and resolve consequences in the fiction.\index{Failure!Travel}
\item Each segment filled typically represents significant progress or a meaningful encounter.\index{Encounters!Travel}
\end{itemize}

\section{Travel Modes and Special Rules}
\label{sec:travel-modes}
\index{Travel!Mode Rules}\index{Modes of Travel}

\subsection{Sea Travel}
\label{subsec:sea-travel}
\index{Travel!Sea Legs}\index{Sea travel}\index{Maritime!Travel}

Sea journeys (Amaranthine/Dolmis/Aberderrin routes) follow special considerations:\index{Amaranthine}\index{Dolmis}\index{Aberderrin}
\begin{itemize}
\item If Theona or Valewood 9s appear in the seed, add an omission or taboo to the leg.\index{Travel!Taboos}\index{Theona}\index{Valewood}
\item Navigational challenges use Sailing or Survival skills with modified DVs.\index{Skills!Survival}\index{Skills!Sailing}\index{Difficulty Value (DV)}
\item Weather and sea conditions act as persistent complications.\index{Hazards!Weather}\index{Sea states}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Underground Passes (Aeler)}
\label{subsec:underways}
\index{Travel!Underways}\index{Aeler!Underways}\index{Underground routes}

Aeler's underground routes have unique mechanics:\index{Aeler}
\begin{itemize}
\item Any Ace can convert a surface route to an under-route.\index{Travel!Aces}\index{Aces}
\item Diamond cards from Aeler always codify outcomes—no roll required—for sealed doors, breath time, and escorted segments.\index{Aeler!Diamonds}\index{Permits and papers!Aeler}
\item Underground travel uses different skill sets (Engineering, Geology, Survival).\index{Skills!Engineering}\index{Skills!Geology}\index{Skills!Survival}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{River Travel}
\label{subsec:river-travel}
\index{Travel!Rivers}\index{River travel}

River journeys present distinct challenges:
\begin{itemize}
\item Ecktoria/Viterra rivers: Bridges, booms, and legal checkpoints.\index{Ecktoria}\index{Viterra}\index{Checkpoints}
\item Mistlands/Linn waters: Reed-mazes and bell-line navigation systems.\index{Mistlands}\index{Linn}\index{Bell-lines}
\item Current strength and river conditions affect travel pace and difficulty.\index{Currents}\index{Difficulty Value (DV)!River}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Frontier and Border Crossings}
\label{subsec:frontier-travel}
\index{Travel!Frontier Blends}\index{Borders}\index{Frontiers}

When traveling between regions with different laws or authorities:
\begin{itemize}
\item Draw two Diamond cards (one from each controlling authority).\index{Permits and papers!Dual authority}
\item Choose which authority's laws you will be judged by at the journey's end.\index{Jurisdiction}
\item Border crossings typically add +2 segments to the travel clock.\index{Travel!Law Boundaries}\index{Clocks!Travel!Modifiers}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Standard Travel Times}

\index{Travel!Time scales}
