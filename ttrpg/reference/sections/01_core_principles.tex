\section{Safety Tools}Ňāabel{sec:safety‚āúools}

Fate's Edge explores mature themes‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíconflict, loss, horror, betrayal. The table should always feel safe. Use these tools to keep the game enjoyable for everyone.

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Safety Tools: Lines, Veils & X‚ąíCard, colback=red!5!white, colframe=red!75!black, sharp corners]
\textbf{X‚ąíCard}‚ąądex{Safety Tools!X‚ąíCard} Any player (including the GM) can draw an "X" on a card or simply say "X" when a moment makes them uncomfortable. When the X‚ąíCard is shown, the described content is removed from the fiction‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíno questions, no explanations, no judgments. The scene is retconned or redirected immediately.

\textbf{Lines}‚ąądex{Safety Tools!Lines} A hard boundary: content that will not appear in the game at all. Examples: "No sexual violence," "No harm to children," "No body horror." Establish during Session Zero.

\textbf{Veils}‚ąądex{Safety Tools!Veils} A soft boundary: content that can happen "off screen" or fade to black. Example: "A romantic scene can happen, but fade to black before it becomes explicit."  
\end{tcolorbox}

Always keep an X‚ąíCard visible. When used, pause, rewind if needed, and move on. Do not interrogate the player. The GM may also use the X‚ąíCard for their own comfort.
