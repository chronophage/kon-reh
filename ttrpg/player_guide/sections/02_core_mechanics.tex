\chapter{Core Mechanics} \label{ch:core-mechanics}

\section*{The 90‑Second Play Loop}
\index{play loop}\index{GM loop}

At the heart of every scene is a simple, fast loop that keeps the story moving:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{GM sets the stage} – Describe the situation and ask “What do you do?”
  \item \textbf{Player declares intent and approach} – State what you want to achieve and how (Attribute + Skill).
  \item \textbf{GM sets Position and Difficulty Value (DV)} – Choose Dominant/Controlled/Desperate and a DV (2–5+).
  \item \textbf{Player rolls dice} – Count Successes (6+) and Story Beats (1s).
  \item \textbf{Apply outcome} – Compare Successes to DV (Clean Success / Success with Cost / Partial / Miss).
  \item \textbf{GM spends Story Beats (SB)} – Introduce complications, advance clocks, or escalate the scene.
  \item \textbf{Player marks resources} – Gain Boons (1 on Partial, 2 on Miss), track Fatigue/Harm, update Obligation, etc.
  \item \textbf{Pass the spotlight} – Next player acts, or GM frames a new beat.
\end{enumerate}

This loop should take about 90 seconds per player action. If it drags, simplify: focus on intent, one roll, and a clear consequence.

\section{Player‑Managed Modules}
\label{sec:player-managed}
\index{player trackers}\index{resources!player-managed}

In Fate's Edge, you are the primary manager of your character's mechanical state. This isn't just about convenience—it is a core design principle that reduces GM overhead and keeps you engaged.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3!white,colframe=black!40!white,title=You Track Your Own Story]
\textbf{What You Manage:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Obligation tracks (one per Patron or Symbol)
    \item Corruption clocks (Cantors, Pact-Whisperers)
    \item Leash clocks (summoned spirits)
    \item Asset states (Maintained, Neglected, Compromised)
    \item Follower conditions (Exposure, Harm)
    \item Progression clocks (Repertoire, Spirit Bond)
    \item Boons, Favor, Leverage
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Your Responsibilities:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Mark immediately} when a rule says “mark +X”.
    \item \textbf{Declare thresholds} (“My Obligation is full!”).
    \item \textbf{Keep it visible} (use trackers everyone can see).
    \item \textbf{Maintain ownership} – you know your state best.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{GM Support:} The GM spot-checks and provides consequences, but you drive your character's mechanical narrative.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Basic Dice Mechanics}

When you attempt a significant action, roll a pool of ten-sided dice (d10s). The size of your pool is:

\[
\text{Dice Pool} = \text{Attribute} + \text{Skill}
\]

\begin{description}
  \item[Attribute] (1–5) Broad traits like Strength, Wit, or Charm.
  \item[Skill] (0–5) Training in a specific area (e.g., Melee, Stealth, Arcana).
\end{description}

\subsection*{Reading the Dice}

Each die that rolls \textbf{6 or higher} counts as a \textbf{Success}.  
Each die that rolls a \textbf{1} generates a \textbf{Story Beat (SB)}.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lc}
\toprule
\textbf{Die Result} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
6--10 & +1 Success \\
1 & +1 Story Beat (SB) \\
2--5 & Nothing \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Example:} Lyra (Agility 3 + Stealth 2 = 5 dice) rolls: 6, 4, 3, 1, 6 → 2 Successes, 1 SB. The DV is 2 – she succeeds, but the GM has 1 SB to spend (perhaps a guard stirs).

\section{Difficulty Value (DV)}

The Game Master sets a \textbf{Difficulty Value} – the target number of Successes needed.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!5!white,colframe=gray!70!black,title=Difficulty Value (DV) Ladder]
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{cl}
\toprule
\textbf{DV} & \textbf{Situation} \\
\midrule
2 & Routine, no pressure \\
3 & Pressured, mild opposition \\
4 & Difficult, active resistance \\
5+ & Extreme, high stakes \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

\paragraph{Note:} DV 3 is most common. If the GM calls for a roll, something is at stake.

\section{The Outcome Matrix}

Compare your Successes to the DV to determine the outcome.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!5!white,colframe=gray!70!black,title=Outcome Matrix]
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{Outcome} & \textbf{What It Means} \\
\midrule
\textbf{Clean Success} (S ≥ DV) & You achieve your goal without complication. \\
\textbf{Success with Cost} (S ≥ DV, SB>0) & You succeed, but the GM spends SB to add a twist. \\
\textbf{Partial} (0 < S < DV) & You make progress, but the situation remains unresolved. \\
\textbf{Miss} (S = 0) & You fail, and the situation escalates. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

\paragraph{What a Partial Means.}
A Partial is not failure – it moves the story forward. Examples:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Improve your Position for the next attempt.
  \item Reduce the effective DV of the next roll.
  \item Succeed at reduced scale or duration.
  \item Reveal information or open a new path.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Critical Successes}

A roll of \textbf{10} counts as \textbf{two Successes}. If the roll succeeds, each 10 adds narrative weight.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{10s} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
1 & Strong success – improved Position, minor advantage. \\
2 & Exceptional success – choose two benefits. \\
3 & Legendary success – decisive victory, clear 1 clock segment. \\
4+ & Mythic success – reshape the scene, clear 1–2 clock segments. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent \textbf{Important:} 10s are never re-rolled by Position effects. Critical effects apply only if the roll succeeds.

\section{Boons}

Boons represent momentum and narrative leverage. You may hold up to \textbf{5 Boons}.

\subsection{Earning Boons}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Partial Success:} Gain 1 Boon
  \item \textbf{Miss:} Gain 2 Boons
  \item \textbf{Bond-Driven Action:} Once per bond per session, gain 1 Boon when an action meaningfully engages that bond.
  \item \textbf{GM Award:} For creativity, sacrifice, or strong narrative play.
\end{itemize}

\noindent \textbf{When not awarded:} Safe rehearsal, null-risk probes, or repeated identical attempts in the same scene do not generate Boons.

\subsection{Spending Boons}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Re-roll a single die (after seeing the result)
  \item Activate an on-screen Asset (1 Boon)
  \item Improve Position by 1 step before a roll
  \item Power certain Rites or abilities
  \item Convert to XP (2 Boons = 1 XP, once per session during downtime, max 2 XP per session)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Carryover Limit}
At the end of each scene, reduce held Boons to a maximum of \textbf{2}. Excess are lost – spend them or lose them.

\section{Position}

Every action takes place from a \textbf{Position} that reflects the character's advantage.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Position} & \textbf{Narrative Frame} & \textbf{Mechanical Effect} \\
\midrule
Dominant & You press your advantage & Re-roll one \textit{failure} \\
Controlled & Balanced norm & No re-rolls \\
Desperate & You act under duress & Re-roll one \textit{success} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Boon Interaction with Fatigue:} When Fatigue forces a success re-roll, spending 1 Boon negates \textit{one} such re-roll. You may spend Boons after seeing the result of a Fatigue re-roll.

\section{Story Beats (SB)}

Story Beats are narrative tools the GM spends to introduce twists. They keep the story alive with complications.

\subsection{SB Spend Examples}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{1 SB:} Minor complication – noise, trace, +1 Supply segment.
  \item \textbf{2 SB:} Moderate setback – alarm raised, lose cover, lesser foe appears.
  \item \textbf{3 SB:} Serious trouble – reinforcements, key gear breaks.
  \item \textbf{4+ SB:} Major turn – trap springs, authority arrives, scene shifts.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Player Advice:} Don't fear Story Beats – they are fuel for drama, not punishment.

\section{Harm and Fatigue}

\subsection{Fatigue Track}
Each character has a Fatigue track equal to \textbf{Body}. Mark Fatigue for exertion, magical strain, or stress.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Each Fatigue step worsens your Position: Dominant → Controlled → Desperate.
  \item If already Desperate, apply –1 die per Fatigue instead.
  \item \textbf{Overflow:} When the track fills, increase Harm by 1 step and clear all Fatigue.
  \item \textbf{Recovery:} Short rest clears 1–2 Fatigue; full night's rest clears all.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Harm Levels}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{Harm Level} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
Harm 1 & –1 die on related actions \\
Harm 2 & –1 die on most actions until treated \\
Harm 3 & Incapacitated or dying \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Recovery}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Fatigue:} Short rest (food/water) removes 2 Fatigue; full night removes all.
  \item \textbf{Harm:} Requires medical care and time – minor treatment downgrades Harm, proper care removes it.
\end{itemize}

\section{Initiative and Turn Order}

Fate's Edge does not use fixed initiative. Turn order follows the fiction:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Narrative Fiat:} The GM frames spotlight order based on circumstances.
  \item \textbf{Player Input:} Players may suggest acting when it makes sense.
  \item \textbf{Surprise:} Ambushers act first; targets respond after.
  \item \textbf{Flexibility:} Spotlight may shift mid-scene if fictionally appropriate.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Turn Economy (Quick Rules)}

Each character takes \textbf{1 Action and 1 Move} on their turn. Actions and Moves may be taken in any order; repeating the same Action is not allowed unless noted.

\paragraph{Move:}
Traverse up to normal movement.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Disengage:} Move without provoking; your next offensive action is Controlled.
  \item \textbf{Dash:} Move again this turn; your next defense is Desperate.
  \item \textbf{Stand:} Use your move to stand from prone.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Attack:}
Make melee or ranged attack versus DV set by the GM. Teamwork/Assist costs 1 Boon.

\paragraph{Observe / Change Position:}
Take a beat to read the field; gain +1 Position for one action this turn (e.g., Controlled → Dominant). Once per turn, cannot exceed Dominant.

\paragraph{Activate an Asset:} Use gear, symbol, or feature per its text. Items with \texttt{[Action]} consume an Action; \texttt{[Free]} do not.

\paragraph{Setup (Teamwork):} Create advantage for an ally. On success, grant their next action +1 Position or step up Effect.

\paragraph{Assist (Teamwork):} Spend 1 Boon to give an ally +1 die on their current roll; you share appropriate risk.

\paragraph{Defend / Protect:} Adopt a guarding stance. Choose a nearby ally; until your next turn you may intercept one hit on them and roll to resist it. On success, reduce/negate Harm; you take any fallout.

\paragraph{Channel / Weave:} Runekeeper/ritual flow – Channel (prime power) then Weave (shape/release). Interruption may worsen Position or cause failure.

\paragraph{Cast Rite / Song:} Perform a Rite/Song per its write-up. You may Push to accelerate at the cost of Fatigue/Corruption.

\paragraph{Interact:} Lift, pull, flip lever, shove foe, break object, apply poultice, reload, draw/stow, etc. GM sets DV/Effect.

\paragraph{Free Items:} Short shouts, dropping an item, quick glance. Longer actions require Observe or Interact.

\paragraph{Reactions (Out of Turn):} Protection may trigger when an ally is hit. Class/Asset reactions fire as written.

\paragraph{Position Caps:} Bonuses cannot raise Position above Dominant; penalties cannot drop below Desperate. Beyond these caps, adjust DV or Effect instead.

\section{Assistance}

Characters can help each other. One helper per action may provide assistance by spending 1 Boon, adding +1 die to the primary actor's roll. Maximum +3 dice from all assists.

\subsection{Defend Action (Optional Core Rule)}

Characters may explicitly \textbf{Defend} against incoming attacks.

\paragraph{Declare Defense:} On your turn, spend a Move or Standard action to enter a defensive stance. Until your next turn, you count as Defending.

\paragraph{Mechanical Effect:} When targeted by a harmful action, roll an appropriate Attribute + Skill pool (e.g., Body+Athletics to dodge, Spirit+Resolve to resist). Your Position improves by one step for this roll (if already Dominant, gain +1 die instead).

\paragraph{Resolution:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Success:} Avoid or fully negate the harm.
  \item \textbf{Partial:} Reduce severity by one tier (e.g., Harm 2 → 1).
  \item \textbf{Miss:} Full effect occurs, but your next Position against that attacker improves by one step.
\end{itemize}

\end{description}
\textit{Fix:} Short rest with tools removes Neglected. A scene of work or a smith removes Compromised.

\section{Ranged Options}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Aim:} +1 die or +1 Effect next shot (lost if you Move or are disrupted).
  \item \textbf{Volley:} Spend extra ammo for +1 die (max +2).
  \item \textbf{Suppress:} Zone under fire; foes there act at –1 die or Limited Effect until they shift cover.
  \item \textbf{Overwatch:} Ready a Controlled shot on a clear trigger crossing your line.
\end{itemize}

\section{Followers and Assets}

\subsection{Followers}
Followers are named NPCs with a personal relationship to your character. They:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Assist during scenes via Bonds and Assists.
  \item Act offscreen to gather information or manage tasks.
  \item Have motives and vulnerabilities – the GM may place them at risk.
\end{itemize}
Followers are not disposable tools. They can grow, change, or be lost.

\subsection{Assets}
Assets are durable resources or positions under your control – safehouses, networks, political offices, relics. They:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Create advantages that persist beyond a single scene.
  \item Resolve problems that dice alone cannot.
  \item Cost Boons to activate (the GM determines the impact).
\end{itemize}
Assets can be threatened, degraded, or lost. Overuse attracts attention.

\subsection{Followers vs. Assets}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{3cm}p{5cm}p{5cm}}
\toprule
 & \textbf{Followers} & \textbf{Assets} \\
\midrule
Nature & People & Resources/Structures \\
Primary Role & Personal support & Strategic leverage \\
Activation & Assists, Bonds & Boon expenditure \\
Risk & Emotional, narrative & Structural, political \\
Growth & Can become PCs & Can expand or collapse \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Design Principle:} Followers create \textit{depth}. Assets create \textit{reach}. High-tier play depends on managing both.

\section{Game Structure}

\subsection{Time Scales}
\begin{description}
  \item[Moment] A heartbeat, a single action.
  \item[Some Time] A few minutes, a short activity.
  \item[Significant Time] Hours, extended effort.
  \item[Days] Large-scale endeavors.
\end{description}

\subsection{Game Units}
\begin{description}
  \item[Scene] Basic narrative unit, covers a specific conflict.
  \item[Session] One game session (3–6 hours).
  \item[Campaign] Entire story arc.
\end{description}

\section{Action Resolution Steps}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Describe your intent and method.
  \item Build dice pool: Attribute + Skill (+ gear, assists).
  \item Roll d10s; count Successes (6+) and Story Beats (1s).
  \item Compare Successes to DV.
  \item Apply outcome from the Outcome Matrix.
  \item GM spends SB if applicable.
  \item Earn Boons for Partial or Miss.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!5!white,colframe=blue!75!black,title=Quick Reference,fonttitle=\bfseries]
\textbf{Dice Pool:} Attribute + Skill d10s \\
\textbf{Success:} 6+ on each die \\
\textbf{Story Beat:} 1 on any die gives SB to GM \\
\textbf{DV:} 2 (easy) to 5+ (extreme) \\
\textbf{Harm:} 3-level system with penalties \\
\textbf{Boons:} 2 on miss, 1 on partial
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Narrative Suggestions}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Collaborative Scene Framing:} Players may suggest scene elements (weather, NPC reactions) with GM approval.
  \item \textbf{Intent-Driven Resolution:} If success is reasonably assured, the GM may ask players to describe \textit{how} rather than roll.
  \item \textbf{Flashback Declarations:} Spend 1 Boon to declare a flashback establishing past preparation, and describe the scene.
  \item \textbf{Descriptive Assistance:} Players can assist by providing vivid descriptions, granting a +1 die to the primary actor.
  \item \textbf{Proactive Storytelling:} Players may suggest minor favorable details (a helpful NPC, a useful item on hand) subject to GM approval.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Small Folk of the Threshold}

The Aelaerem (halflings) and Aelinnel (gnomes) are diminutive peoples attuned to liminal spaces—doorways, hedge‑gaps, tide‑cut stairs, and the quiet places between hearth and hollow. While they share a small stature and a knack for slipping through cracks, their cultural instincts differ.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Restriction:} Cannot use Heavy Armor. May use any one‑handed or two‑handed weapon, but Heavy Weapons impose \textbf{–1 Position} on all actions while wielding them.
  \item \textbf{Bonus:} When wielding a \textbf{spear, quarterstaff, or light polearm} (any Medium or Light weapon with the \texttt{Reach} tag), treat the weapon as \textbf{one weight class lighter} for the purpose of attack modifiers. Additionally, once per scene when you make a melee attack with such a weapon, you may spend \textbf{1 Boon} to convert a \textit{Partial} success into a \textit{Clean Success} (representing a clever thrust that uses the enemy’s momentum against them).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Aelaerem — The Hearth’s Luck}
Aelaerem are survivors, homebodies, and masters of sudden fortune.

\textbf{Bonus — Once per scene}, you may improve your **Position by one step** (e.g., Desperate → Controlled, Controlled → Dominant) as a free action **before** rolling an action. This represents a flash of luck, a well-timed sidestep, or an unexpected opening.

\subsubsection*{Aelinnel — The Bright Trick}
Aelinnel are fae‑touched, mathematically clever, and never without a tool for the moment.
Choose **one** of the following abilities to represent their tricky nature.

\begin{description}
  \item[Option A: Short Step (Teleport)] \hfill \\
  \textbf{Bonus — Once per scene}, as a **Move action**, you may instantly teleport to any unobserved location you can see within **Near** range. You cannot pass through solid barriers. This is a fey‑like blink, not a physical jump.
  
  \item[Option B: Knack for the Right Thing (Handy Item)] \hfill \\
  \textbf{Bonus — Once per scene}, you may declare that you have a **small, mundane item** on your person that is useful in the current situation (e.g., a coil of rope, a lockpick, a candle, a chalk stick). This costs no Boon and requires no roll. The item must be plausible for your character to carry. (This is a free, once‑per‑scene version of the **Flashback Declaration** mechanic described in \S\ref{sec:narrative-suggestions}.)
\end{description}

\noindent Both peoples are often underestimated—a mistake that has left many a larger foe stumbling through a door that was never really shut.
