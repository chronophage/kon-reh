\section{1. The Guardian (Fighter / Paladin / Knight)}

‚ąądex{character concepts!guardian}

\textbf{Concept}: A protector who stands between danger and those they've sworn to defend. \emph{Steel in hand, vow in heart.} Whether a chivalrous knight, a grizzled mercenary, or a holy warrior of the Everflame, the Guardian makes others safe through sacrifice.

\textbf{Traditional Analogues}: Fighter, Paladin, Barbarian (defensive), Knight, Samurai, Legionary

\textbf{Cultural Affinities}: Vhasia (chivalric knight), Viterra (hedge‚ąíknight), Aeler (shield‚ąíwarden), Ubral (clan champion), Ecktoria (legionary), Oshiira (Vermilion Corps guardian)

‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Skills}: Melee, Athletics, Command, Endurance

\begin{itemize}

‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Tier III (91‚ąí‚ąí150 XP)}: Take \emph{Battlefield Mastery} or \emph{Mystic Bulwark}. Upgrade armor to Heavy if you have Body 4+. Start training a successor (Cap 3+ follower).

\paragraph{Tactical Advice}:
\begin{itemize}

‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Spend Boons on re‚ąírolling failed defense} rolls, not on offense. Your job is to survive.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Position matters more than damage.} A Dominant position lets you re‚ąíroll one failure ‚ąí‚ąí use shoves, terrain, and cover to stay in control.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{When you take Harm, remember it clears Fatigue.} Sometimes a small wound is worth the reset.

\end{itemize}

\section{Reskinning the Paths: Runekeeper and Invoker as Familiar Concepts}
Ňāabel{sec:reskinning‚ąípaths}
‚ąądex{reskinning}‚ąądex{Runekeeper!reskinning}‚ąądex{Invoker!reskinning}

The magic systems in \textit{Fate's Edge} are designed to be reimagined. Neither \textbf{Runekeeper} (Thiasos + Codex) nor \textbf{Invoker} (Patron's Symbol) is locked into a single narrative identity. Below are common reskins ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí use the same mechanics, change the fiction.

\subsection*{Runekeeper ‚ąí‚ąí The Devoted Agent}
A Runekeeper has sworn themselves to a single Patron, learning its Rites and bearing its Influence. This framework can represent:

\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Paladin / Crusader}: Sworn to the \textit{Oath of Flame & Light} or the \textit{Inquisitor Prime}. Rites become prayers, smites, and consecrations. The Codex is a prayer book or a vow‚ąíetched gauntlet.
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Warlock / Pact‚ąíBearer}: Bound to a distant, alien, or dangerous Patron (e.g., Khemesh, Malachai, the Ninth). Rites are granted, not learned. Influence is the steady pull of a bargain.
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Cleric / Priest}: Devoted to the Everflame, the Pale Shepherd, or other divine powers. Rites are liturgies. The Patron's Gift is a blessing or a relic.
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Dark Knight / Death Knight}: A fallen or morally ambiguous warrior who draws power from a grim Patron (Carrion‚ąíKing, Mor'iraath). Melee and Rites mix; Influence is a slow doom.
\end{itemize}

\noindent \textit{Mechanical core unchanged:} 1 action Rites, Influence track, Patron's Gift imbuement, Push It option.

\subsection*{Invoker ‚ąí‚ąí The Professional Occultist}
An Invoker does not fully commit to a single Patron. Instead, they carry \textbf{Symbols} (often tools, badges, or relics) of several Powers and perform rituals to invoke their Rites. This framework can represent:

\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Exorcist / Witch Hunter}: Carries symbols of several protective Patrons (The Sealed Gate, Oath of Flame, Gallow's Bell). Rites are wards, banishments, and purification rituals. \emph{Crack the Seal} is an emergency exorcism.
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Alienist / Scholar of the Unseen}: Studies forbidden Patrons (The Ninth, Khemesh, Mor'iraath) from a safe distance ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí ``safe'' being relative. Uses Symbol libraries to invoke their power without full devotion. Rituals are experimental and dangerous.
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Consulting Occultist / Paranormal Investigator}: Carries a briefcase or satchel of Symbols ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí a bell from the Mistlands, a sun‚ąíseal from Ecktoria, a hag's stone from Aelinnel. Hired to solve magical problems. Rituals are filed like work orders; \emph{Crack the Seal} is ``burning a favor.''
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Ritualist / Ceremonial Mage}: Believes that power lies in procedure, not faith. Each Symbol is a ``key'' ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí a geometric diagram, a calendar, a consecrated blade. Rites are slow, deliberate, and precise. The Invoker is a technician, not a worshipper.
\end{itemize}

\noindent \textit{Mechanical core unchanged:} Ritual time, Influence per invocation, Symbol display reduces cost, \emph{Crack the Seal} for instant effect.

\subsection*{Mixing and Blurring Lines}
Characters may also blend these frameworks ‚ąí‚ąí‚ąí a Runekeeper who occasionally carries a second Symbol for emergency rites, or an Invoker who one day pledges fully to a Patron and becomes a Runekeeper. The system does not penalize narrative growth; simply retrain Talents during downtime (see \ref{ch:talents}) when the fiction justifies it.

\noindent \textit{Remember:} The fiction leads. If you want to play a Paladin, describe your Runekeeper's Rites as prayers. If you want a Vodouisant or Hoodoo practitioner, reflavor Patron's Symbols as sacred bundles, veves, or spirit vessels. If you want a monster hunter, carry a satchel of monster‚ąíspecific Symbols (silver stake, iron bell, cold iron nail). The mechanics stay the same; the story breathes.
