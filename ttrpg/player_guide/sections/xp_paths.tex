\section{From Concept to Career}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąíconcept‚ąíto‚ąícareer}
‚ąądex{concept mapping}‚ąądex{career planning}

Before you spend a single XP, start with the story you want to tell.

\subsection*{Step 1: Concept Seed}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąíconcept‚ąíseed}

Begin with a simple statement of who your character is:
\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem ``Stoic bodyguard who will take the hit for anyone else.''
  ‚ĄĻtem ``Nervy courier who knows every back alley in the city.''
  ‚ĄĻtem ``Soft‚ąíspoken merchant who pulls strings behind the scenes.''
\end{itemize}

This is your \textbf{concept seed}. Everything else grows from here.

\subsection*{Step 2: Role in the Party}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąírole‚ąíparty}

Decide what you want to \emph{do at the table}:
\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem Take hits and lock down threats?
  ‚ĄĻtem Spot danger and solve practical problems?
  ‚ĄĻtem Talk through conflicts and manage social fallout?
  ‚ĄĻtem Create tools, magic, or plans that enable everyone else?
\end{itemize}

Your role helps you choose a \textbf{Primary Attribute} and 2‚ąí‚ąí3 \textbf{Key Skills}
(see \ref{ch:advancement}).

\subsection*{Step 3: Career Theme}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąícareer‚ąítheme}

Ask yourself:
\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem Is this a story of \emph{self‚ąímastery}? (training, discipline, pushing limits)
  ‚ĄĻtem A story of \emph{connections}? (alliances, obligations, webs of influence)
  ‚ĄĻtem A story of \emph{balance}? (learning to juggle many demands at once)
\end{itemize}

Your answer naturally points you toward one of the three \textbf{XP paths} in
this chapter.

\subsection*{Step 4: Long Arc in One Sentence}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąílong‚ąíarc}

Write a single sentence for where you imagine your character at high XP:
\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem ``I want to become the greatest duelist in the realm.''
  ‚ĄĻtem ``I want to map the unknown and bring others safely home.''
  ‚ĄĻtem ``I want to run the network everyone relies on but no one sees.''
\end{itemize}

You do not have to stick to this forever, but it gives you a \emph{direction}
for your early choices.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!5!white,colframe=blue!75!black,title=Character Career Checklist,fonttitle=\bfseries]
\textbf{Before spending XP, decide:}
\begin{enumerate}
  ‚ĄĻtem Your \textbf{concept seed} (one sentence).
  ‚ĄĻtem Your \textbf{role in the party} (what you do most).
  ‚ĄĻtem Your preferred \textbf{career theme}: self, balance, or influence.
  ‚ĄĻtem A rough \textbf{long arc} (where you want to end up).
\end{enumerate}
Start simple. You can always refine these as the campaign unfolds.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Three Career Paths}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąíthree‚ąípaths}
‚ąądex{advancement paths}‚ąądex{career paths}

There are three broad approaches to character development, each representing a
different \textbf{career philosophy}:

\begin{description}
‚ĄĻtem[Personal Path] Focus on personal mastery and self‚ąíimprovement (\ref{sec:xp‚ąípersonal‚ąípath}).
‚ĄĻtem[Balanced Path] Mix personal growth with resources and influence (\ref{sec:xp‚ąíbalanced‚ąípath}).
‚ĄĻtem[Influencer Path] Build networks, assets, and strategic power (\ref{sec:xp‚ąíinfluencer‚ąípath}).
\end{description}

These paths are not rigid classes. They are \emph{patterns} that help you
decide where most of your XP will go, especially in the early game.

\section{Player Archetypes}
Ňāabel{sec:player‚ąíarchetypes}
‚ąądex{player archetypes}‚ąądex{Solo}‚ąądex{Mixed}‚ąądex{Mastermind}

Not every player spends XP the same way. These three \textbf{player archetypes}
describe common patterns. Identify yours ‚ąí‚ąí it will help you choose a career
path and avoid frustration.

\subsection*{The Solo}
Ňāabel{sec:archetype‚ąísolo}

\textbf{Definition:} Invests XP primarily in Attributes and Skills. Minimal
followers, minimal holdings. All power is on the character sheet.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Typical XP Spread:} 70‚ąí‚ąí90% Self; 0‚ąí‚ąí10% On‚ąíscreen help; 0‚ąí‚ąí20% Off‚ąíscreen assets.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Strengths:} Consistent scene impact; few moving parts; resilient to
follower loss.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Risks:} Limited fiction reach between sessions; can stall when
problems demand logistics or networks.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Starting Focus:} Attributes 2‚ąí‚ąí3, Skills 1‚ąí‚ąí2, minimal assets.

\subsection*{The Mixed Player}
Ňāabel{sec:archetype‚ąímixed}

\textbf{Definition:} Splits XP between self‚ąígrowth and one or two meaningful
assets (a small follower or a reliable holding).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Typical XP Spread:} 50‚ąí‚ąí65% Self; 15‚ąí‚ąí‚ĖĎ On‚ąíscreen help; 15‚ąí‚ąí‚ĖĎ Off‚ąíscreen assets.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Strengths:} Versatile: credible in scenes and has a lever for special
problems.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Risks:} Upkeep pressure; helper can be targeted when the GM spends 2+ Story Beats.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Starting Focus:} Balanced approach with one minor asset or low‚ąícap follower.

\subsection*{The Mastermind}
Ňāabel{sec:archetype‚ąímastermind}

\textbf{Definition:} Prioritises followers / cadres / familiars and off‚ąíscreen
networks. The character sheet is the hub of a larger apparatus.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Typical XP Spread:} 25‚ąí‚ąí40% Self; 35‚ąí‚ąí55% On‚ąíscreen help; 20‚ąí‚ąí40% Off‚ąíscreen assets.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Strengths:} Scene control via assistance; strategic reach between
sessions; strong heist / social‚ąíplanning play.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Risks:} Dependency on assist lanes; followers can be endangered on 2+ SB
spends; upkeep pressure.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Starting Focus:} Significant investment in followers or major assets
from the beginning.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=cyan!5!white,colframe=cyan!75!black,title=Which Archetype Are You?,fonttitle=\bfseries]
Ask yourself:
\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem Do I want my character to be self‚ąísufficient and always ready? ‚Üí \textbf{Solo}
  ‚ĄĻtem Do I enjoy having a helpful sidekick or a home base, but not too much bookkeeping? ‚Üí \textbf{Mixed}
  ‚ĄĻtem Do I love managing networks, commanding troops, and solving problems through influence? ‚Üí \textbf{Mastermind}
\end{itemize}
There is no wrong answer ‚ąí‚ąí but knowing your style helps you build a character you will enjoy for many sessions.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Path 1: Personal Development}

The \textbf{Personal Path} centers your character's career on what \emph{they}

‚ĄĻtem 0‚ąí‚ąí10% Resources and assets

\section{Building for Group Synergy}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąísynergy}
‚ąądex{group synergy}

Your character's career path matters most in context with the rest of the
party. A well‚ąíconstructed party covers key roles without excessive overlap.

\subsection*{Key Roles to Consider}
\begin{description}
‚ĄĻtem[Combat] Frontline defence, skirmishing, or ranged fire support.
‚ĄĻtem[Social] Negotiation, intimidation, information gathering.
‚ĄĻtem[Exploration] Scouting, navigating, surviving hostile environments.
‚ĄĻtem[Magic] Free casting, rites, rituals, or summoning.

\end{description}

\subsection*{Complementary Paths}
\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Personal path} characters provide reliable combat and specialist solutions.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Balanced path} characters handle diverse, connective challenges.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Influencer path} characters create opportunities and resources for everyone.
\end{itemize}
Together, a mixed party can cover nearly every type of problem.

\subsection*{Redundant Paths}
\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem Multiple personal path characters may overlap in combat or role.
‚ĄĻtem Multiple influencer path characters may compete for spotlight and assets.
‚ĄĻtem Consider diversifying within similar paths: different weapon styles, different Patrons, different social approaches.
‚ĄĻtem Example: Two combatants ‚ąí‚ąí one a duelist (single‚ąítarget damage), one a shield‚ąífocused guardian (area protection).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Balancing Your Table}
If you notice a gap (e.g., no one has investment in social skills or no one has
a safehouse asset), consider adjusting your XP spending or discussing with the
group. A party that talks about roles early will have a smoother campaign.

\section{Progression Planning}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąíprogression}
‚ąądex{progression planning}

Think of your character's career in phases. These XP bands are approximate and

\subsection*{Early Game (0‚ąí‚ąí40 XP)}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąíearly‚ąígame}

\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem Reach attribute rating 3 in your primary area.
‚ĄĻtem Develop 2‚ąí‚ąí3 key skills to rating 2‚ąí‚ąí3.
‚ĄĻtem Acquire basic resources or one special ability (if your path uses them).
‚ĄĻtem Clarify your character's niche in the group.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{When to take a Follower vs. Asset vs. Talent (Early):}
\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Follower:} If you want on‚ąíscreen help (a scout, a bodyguard, an apprentice). Start with Cap 1‚ąí‚ąí2.
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Asset:} If you want an off‚ąíscreen advantage (a safehouse, a workshop, a contact network). Start with Minor Asset (4 XP).
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Talent:} If you want a signature move (combat technique, social edge, magic access). Minor talents (2‚ąí‚ąí3 XP) are great early.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Mid Game (41‚ąí‚ąí90 XP)}
Ňāabel{sec:xp‚ąímid‚ąígame}

\begin{itemize}
