\section{Design Principles}

Assets are written to:
\begin{itemize}
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Shift fiction} between scenes: open doors, quiet rumors, stock larders, calm crowds.

  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Stay narrative‚ąífirst}: prefer position, scale, and access over flat modifiers.
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}

  ‚ĄĻtem Stack identical tags‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíif two Assets would grant the same edge, take the best one. ‚ąądex{assets!stacking}

\paragraph{Narrative First, Mechanics Second}
When an Asset grants ``start \textbf{Dominant}'', it applies to social or informational rolls where positioning matters. For purely practical challenges (e.g., picking a lock, repairing a device), ``reduce DV by 1'' is the preferred phrasing. When in doubt, the GM chooses the form that best fits the fiction.

\paragraph{Narrative Prerequisites}

\begin{itemize}

\begin{enumerate}
  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Crisis or Power Vacuum} ‚ąí‚ąí The current leader dies, disappears, or is disgraced.

  ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Consolidation} ‚ąí‚ąí Defeat or co‚ÄĎopt rivals, bribe or intimidate dissenters, and secure the faction's resources.
\end{enumerate}
