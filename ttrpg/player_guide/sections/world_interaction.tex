\subsection*{The Ways Between}
Ňāabel{sec:ways‚ąíbetween}
‚ąądex{Ways Between}‚ąądex{Obishaal}

Beyond the physical map lie the Ways Between ‚Äď the dream‚ÄĎroads and spirit‚ÄĎpaths of Obishaal, realm of dreams and death. Travel here is measured not in miles but in choices. A wrong turn might lead to yesterday. A right turn might lead to a version of yourself who made a different decision and is still alive to regret it.

\paragraph{Entering the Ways:} Thresholds exist in thin places ‚Äď crossroads at midnight, bridges that appear only at low tide, caves behind waterfalls, or simply a mist that settles too thick and stays too long. A character may also enter through ritual, dream, or near‚ÄĎdeath experience.

\paragraph{Navigation in Obishaal:} Use the standard travel procedures, but the GM may replace the Spades (place) draw with a \textbf{Virtue Test}. The road judges. Acts of honesty, courage, mercy, or self‚ÄĎsacrifice advance a personal \textbf{Virtue Timer [3]}; acts of greed, cowardice, cruelty, or despair advance a \textbf{Vice Timer [3]}. When one fills, the character ascends (Virtue) to a higher layer or falls (Vice) to a lower one.

\paragraph{The Thirteenth Stone:} At the deepest layer, pressed into the silt of the oldest dream, lies the Thirteenth Stone ‚Äď warm, pulsing, and whispering. Here, Ikasha, She Who Sleeps, dreams of freedom. Her voice offers bargains. Her freedom would remake the world.

\section{Travel Framework}

\subsection*{Travel Process}
Ňāabel{sec:travel‚ąíprocess}
\begin{enumerate}
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Set the Leg:} Name origin and destination; start a Travel Timer (4‚ąí‚ąí10 segments based on difficulty).
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Draw Prompts:} Draw up to one card from each suit to establish terrain, people, pressures, and leverage.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Assign Roles:} Players take on travel roles (see below) to contribute actions.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Play the Leg:} Players take actions to advance the timer or mitigate complications. GM spends SB from rolls showing \textbf{1}s to introduce hazards.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Resolve:} When the timer fills, you arrive ‚Äď changed by the journey.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Travel Roles}
Ňāabel{sec:travel‚ąíroles}
\begin{description}
‚ĄĻtem[Guide] ‚ąądex{Travel!Guide} Sets course, reads signs. Rolls navigation (typically Wits + Survival); on success, tick the Travel Timer +1.
‚ĄĻtem[Scout] ‚ąądex{Travel!Scout} Moves ahead, first contact with hazards or ambushes. On success, the party cannot be surprised on the first encounter of the leg.
‚ĄĻtem[Quartermaster] ‚ąądex{Travel!Quartermaster} Manages supplies and encumbrance. On success, prevents one Supply drop or recovers 1 spent Supply.
‚ĄĻtem[Watch] ‚ąądex{Travel!Watch} Maintains vigilance. On success, the party gains +1 Position on the first defense roll of any ambush.

Each role action requires a roll (Attribute + appropriate skill, DV set by the GM). Failure may tick the Travel Timer backwards or introduce a complication.

\subsection*{Hospitality as a Social Mechanic}
Ňāabel{sec:hospitality}
‚ąądex{Social!hospitality}

In many cultures ‚Äď especially Aelaerem, Ykrul, Tulkani, Ubral, and Fhara ‚Äď hospitality is sacred. Accepting a cup, a meal, or a night's shelter creates a bond. Breaking hospitality is worse than breaking a sworn oath.

\paragraph{Hospitality Bond:} When you accept hospitality (food, drink, or shelter) from a host in a culture that honors guest‚ÄĎright, you and the host gain a mutual \textbf{Hospitality Bond [3]}. While the bond has segments, neither party may take hostile action against the other. The bond ticks when either party lies to the other, breaks a promise made under the host's roof, or acts to harm the other's interests. When it fills, the bond snaps ‚Äď and the violator gains a \textbf{Black Mark} in that culture (‚ąí1 die on all social rolls with that culture for one season).

\paragraph{Refusing Hospitality:} Refusing an offered cup, meal, or shelter is a grave insult in hospitality cultures. The GM may spend 1 SB to worsen Position by one step in all social interactions with that host and their community for the remainder of the visit.

\section{Summary}
Ňāabel{sec:world‚ąísummary}
‚ąądex{World interaction!summary}
The world has opinions. Movement is timers and color, position rises and sinks with weather and words, and every suit you draw speaks in a regional accent. Ask the land for a favor ‚Äď then pay it back on the road.

\paragraph{Remember:} Every interaction with the world is an opportunity. Use your assets, deploy your followers, and engage with the setting actively. The world responds to your choices, and every journey changes both you and the places you pass through. The road is not a ledger ‚Äď it is a conversation. Speak carefully. Listen closely. And always leave something behind for the next traveler.
