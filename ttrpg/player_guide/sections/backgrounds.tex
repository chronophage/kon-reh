% === Epigraph / Intro vignette ===
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!3!white,colframe=black!60!white,title={From the journal of a Thepyrgosi Witch Hunter},fonttitle=\bfseries,breakable]
They call it mercy when the Tower does it and cruelty when I do. The difference is a seal of wax and a hymn at the gate.

I was a bright spark once---could taste iron on my tongue when the air went thin before a working. The masters in Thepyrgos tested me, weighed me, and found me \emph{nearly} enough. ``Nearly'' is a door that never opens again.

So I learned the other doors.

I carry writs that make people look away. I carry cord and a promise I do not speak aloud. The University pays for talent delivered in time; the city pays for fires that never start. Between those accounts, I balance what I can.

You will say I go too far. Sometimes I do. Sometimes the street is a tinderbox and a wrong whisper is a spark. When the bells of Thepyrgos answer the river wind, I walk the colonnade to the bursar's door and do not look at the windows. The work is ugly. The work is necessary. The night does not care which of those is true---only that the lamps keep burning.
\end{tcolorbox}

Before your character took their first deliberate risk, they belonged somewhere. Backgrounds are the trail-dust on your boots, the school-ink on your fingers, the bell-pattern you still tap when frightened. They explain how you know what you know---and who still knows \emph{you}.

\section{Understanding Backgrounds}
\index{backgrounds}\index{character creation!backgrounds}

Backgrounds serve several important functions:
\begin{itemize}
\item Provide narrative context for abilities and choices
\item Explain how you gained skills and connections
\item Create ties to regions and factions (see \ref{ch:world-cultures})
\item Offer roleplaying hooks, responsibilities, and privileges
\item Seed reliable \emph{contacts}, \emph{customs}, and \emph{places} that matter on-screen
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Mechanical Hooks.}
\index{backgrounds!mechanics}
Choose \textbf{one background} at character creation. It grants:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Two Access Tags} (narrative permissions; see below)
\item \textbf{A Signature Contact} (named NPC; Cap~1 follower, no XP cost; grants +1d once per scene when their help is plausible)
\item \textbf{A Background Boon} (once per session, gain +1d or reduce DV by 1 for a task strongly tied to the background)
\item \textbf{An Obligation Timer [4]} (when filled, a favor is called in, a duty recalled, or a price comes due)
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Access Tags.}
\index{access tags}\index{backgrounds!access}
Tags describe \emph{where} and \emph{with whom} you have standing. Examples: \textit{Guild-Ledgered}, \textit{Court-Announced}, \textit{Veteran-of-the-Marches}, \textit{Bell-Kin}, \textit{Wind-Knot Tokens}, \textit{Charter-Stamped}. When a scene engages a tag, the GM may:
\begin{itemize}
\item Waive a trivial gate (no roll)
\item Lower Position risk by one step for first contact
\item Offer a \emph{Devil's Bargain} tied to that society's expectations
\end{itemize}

\section{Background Benefits and Limits}
\index{backgrounds!benefits}\index{backgrounds!limitations}

\subsection*{Typical Benefits}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Skill Access}: Strong narrative justification for certain tests
\item \textbf{Resource Access}: Borrowed tools, rooms, shrines, or records (scene-time instead of XP)
\item \textbf{Social Access}: Entry to circles your tags recognize
\item \textbf{Knowledge}: Clues, maps, or customs that lower DV by 1
\item \textbf{Reputation}: Advantage on first impressions in-tag
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Common Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Duties}: Responsibilities to house, guild, banner, or court
\item \textbf{Restrictions}: Codes, taboos, or public expectations
\item \textbf{Enemies}: Rival houses, crews, or schools
\item \textbf{Secrets}: Leverage others can pull
\item \textbf{Dependencies}: Loss of standing if you neglect ties
\end{itemize}

\section{Creating a Custom Background}
\index{backgrounds!creating}

\subsection*{Build Procedure (Player \& GM)}
Pick \textbf{2 Access Tags}, \textbf{1 Signature Contact}, \textbf{1 Boon}, and \textbf{1 Obligation Seed}. Name places and people.
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Origin}: Where were you taught/raised/forged?
\item \textbf{Training}: Which \emph{two} skills does this background naturally support?
\item \textbf{Experiences}: One memory that still changes how you act
\item \textbf{Relationships}: One ally, one rival; both want something
\item \textbf{Values}: What won't you let slide?
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Mechanical Considerations}
\index{backgrounds!mechanics mapping}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Boon Scope}: DV --1 or +1d once/session when strongly on-theme
\item \textbf{Contact Use}: +1d as assist; cannot take independent actions unless purchased as a \emph{Follower} (Cap\(^2\) XP)
\item \textbf{Assets}: Borrowing an institutional asset costs \emph{scene time} (counts as upkeep for that asset this downtime) but 0 XP
\item \textbf{Languages}: Choose one tongue linked to the background at \emph{Conversational} or better
\end{itemize}

\section{The Obligation Timer }
\label{sec:bg-obligation}
\index{backgrounds!obligations}

Each background includes an \textbf{Obligation Timer [4]}. It advances when:
\begin{itemize}
\item You fail a roll tied to your background (GM discretion)
\item You accept a favor from your signature contact
\item The GM spends 1 SB to represent a duty recalled
\item You ignore a responsibility linked to your access tags
\end{itemize}

When the timer fills, something is \emph{due}. The GM may choose or roll:
\begin{description}
\item[1--2] Minor favor, time, or tithe (1 Supply or a scene's work)
\item[3] Significant service, costly honesty, dangerous escort (a full scene)
\item[4] Defining ordeal: duel, audit, rite, or trial (potential Harm or permanent consequence)
\end{description}
Clear segments by honoring duties on-screen, sacrificing resources, or changing affiliation (which creates new timers).

\section{Background Combinations}
\index{backgrounds!combinations}

\paragraph{Sequential}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Veteran $\rightarrow$ City Watch}: Access shifts from \textit{Muster Papers} to \textit{Badge Rights}
\item \textbf{Scholar $\rightarrow$ Diplomat}: Keep libraries, gain court
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Parallel}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Noble Merchant}: Both \textit{Court-Announced} and \textit{Guild-Ledgered}; responsibilities multiply
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Contrasting}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Criminal Penitent}: Underworld ties vs. temple vows; great hooks, sharp bargains
\end{itemize}

\section{Using Backgrounds in Play}
\index{backgrounds!in play}

\subsection*{Player Applications}
\begin{itemize}
\item Name your places: the specific stall, shrine, or yard you know
\item Cash your Boon when you leverage that familiarity
\item Call on your contact to assist or to introduce a new lead
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{GM Applications}
\begin{itemize}
\item Offer choices where background allegiance matters
\item Turn Access Tags into \emph{gates} and \emph{keys}
\item Spend Story Beats to \emph{complicate the old ties}
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{GM SB Menu: Background Complications}
\index{complications!background}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{1 SB}: A rival claims your tag is invalid here; prove it or pay time
\item \textbf{2 SB}: Your contact is \emph{busy} or \emph{watched}; help will cost you exposure
\item \textbf{3 SB}: Obligation advances; an audit, rite, or muster arrives early
\item \textbf{4 SB}: An enemy weaponizes your background---publicly
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!5!white,colframe=blue!75!black,title=Background Quick Reference,fonttitle=\bfseries,breakable]
\textbf{At Creation}
\begin{itemize}
\item Choose: 2 \emph{Access Tags}, 1 \emph{Signature Contact} (Cap~1 assist), 1 \emph{Boon} (once/session), 1 \emph{Obligation Seed}.
\item Pick 1 language tied to the background at \emph{Conversational} or better.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{In Play}
\begin{itemize}
\item Invoke tags to open doors or shift Position.
\item Call your contact for +1d assist (no independent actions).
\item Track the \emph{Obligation Timer [4]}. Pay it on-screen.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Advancing}
\begin{itemize}
\item Upgrade contact to a \emph{Follower} by paying XP (Cap\(^2\)).
\item Add a second tag after a major arc or training.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}
