They call it mercy when the Tower does it and cruelty when I do. The difference is a seal of wax and a hymn at the gate.

I was a bright spark once—could taste iron on my tongue when the air went thin before a working. The masters in Thepyrgos tested me, weighed me, and found me \emph{nearly} enough. ``Nearly'' is a door that never opens again.

So I learned the other doors.

I carry writs that make people look away. I carry cord and a promise I do not speak aloud. The University pays for talent delivered in time; the city pays for fires that never start. Between those accounts, I balance what I can.

You will say I go too far. Sometimes I do. Sometimes the street is a tinderbox and a wrong whisper is a spark. When the bells of Thepyrgos answer the river wind, I walk the colonnade to the bursar's door and do not look at the windows. The work is ugly. The work is necessary. The night does not care which of those is true—only that the lamps keep burning.
\end{tcolorbox}

Before your character took their first deliberate risk, they belonged somewhere. Backgrounds are the trail−dust on your boots, the school−ink on your fingers, the bell−pattern you still tap when frightened. They explain how you know what you know—and who still knows \emph{you}.

\section{Understanding Backgrounds}
∈dex{backgrounds}∈dex{character creation!backgrounds}

Backgrounds serve several important functions:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Provide narrative context for abilities and choices
ℹtem Explain how you gained skills and connections
ℹtem Create ties to regions and factions (see \ref{ch:world−cultures})
ℹtem Offer roleplaying hooks, responsibilities, and privileges
ℹtem Seed reliable \emph{contacts}, \emph{customs}, and \emph{places} that matter on−screen
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Mechanical Hooks.}
∈dex{backgrounds!mechanics}
Choose \textbf{one background} at character creation. It grants:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Two Access Tags} (narrative permissions; see below)
ℹtem \textbf{A Signature Contact} (named NPC; Cap~1 follower, no XP cost; grants +1d once per scene when their help is plausible)
ℹtem \textbf{A Background Boon} (once per session, gain +1d or reduce DV by 1 for a task strongly tied to the background)
ℹtem \textbf{An Obligation Timer [4]} (when filled, a favor is called in, a duty recalled, or a price comes due)
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Access Tags.}
∈dex{access tags}∈dex{backgrounds!access}
Tags describe \emph{where} and \emph{with whom} you have standing. Examples: \textit{Guild−Ledgered}, \textit{Court−Announced}, \textit{Veteran−of−the−Marches}, \textit{Bell−Kin}, \textit{Wind−Knot Tokens}, \textit{Charter−Stamped}. When a scene engages a tag, the GM may:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Waive a trivial gate (no roll)
ℹtem Lower Position risk by one step for first contact
ℹtem Offer a \emph{Devil's Bargain} tied to that society's expectations
\end{itemize}

\section{Background Benefits and Limits}
∈dex{backgrounds!benefits}∈dex{backgrounds!limitations}

\subsection*{Typical Benefits}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Skill Access}: Strong narrative justification for certain tests
ℹtem \textbf{Resource Access}: Borrowed tools, rooms, shrines, or records (scene−time instead of XP)
ℹtem \textbf{Social Access}: Entry to circles your tags recognize
ℹtem \textbf{Knowledge}: Clues, maps, or customs that lower DV by 1
ℹtem \textbf{Reputation}: Advantage on first impressions in−tag
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Common Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Duties}: Responsibilities to house, guild, banner, or court
ℹtem \textbf{Restrictions}: Codes, taboos, or public expectations
ℹtem \textbf{Enemies}: Rival houses, crews, or schools
ℹtem \textbf{Secrets}: Leverage others can pull
ℹtem \textbf{Dependencies}: Loss of standing if you neglect ties
\end{itemize}

\section{Creating a Custom Background}
∈dex{backgrounds!creating}

\subsection*{Build Procedure (Player & GM)}
Pick \textbf{2 Access Tags}, \textbf{1 Signature Contact}, \textbf{1 Boon}, and \textbf{1 Obligation Seed}. Name places and people.
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Origin}: Where were you taught/raised/forged?
ℹtem \textbf{Training}: Which \emph{two} skills does this background naturally support?
ℹtem \textbf{Experiences}: One memory that still changes how you act
ℹtem \textbf{Relationships}: One ally, one rival; both want something
ℹtem \textbf{Values}: What won't you let slide?
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Mechanical Considerations}
∈dex{backgrounds!mechanics mapping}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{Boon Scope}: DV –1 or +1d once/session when strongly on−theme
ℹtem \textbf{Contact Use}: +1d as assist; cannot take independent actions unless purchased as a \emph{Follower} (Cap\(²\) XP)
ℹtem \textbf{Assets}: Borrowing an institutional asset costs \emph{scene time} (counts as upkeep for that asset this downtime) but 0 XP
ℹtem \textbf{Languages}: Choose one tongue linked to the background at \emph{Conversational} or better
\end{itemize}

\section{The Obligation Timer }
łabel{sec:bg−obligation}
∈dex{backgrounds!obligations}

Each background includes an \textbf{Obligation Timer [4]}. It advances when:
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem You fail a roll tied to your background (GM discretion)
ℹtem You accept a favor from your signature contact
ℹtem The GM spends 1 SB to represent a duty recalled
ℹtem You ignore a responsibility linked to your access tags
\end{itemize}

When the timer fills, something is \emph{due}. The GM may choose or roll:
\begin{description}
ℹtem[1−−2] Minor favor, time, or tithe (1 Supply or a scene's work)
ℹtem[3] Significant service, costly honesty, dangerous escort (a full scene)
ℹtem[4] Defining ordeal: duel, audit, rite, or trial (potential Harm or permanent consequence)
\end{description}
Clear segments by honoring duties on−screen, sacrificing resources, or changing affiliation (which creates new timers).

\subsection*{GM SB Menu: Background Complications}
∈dex{complications!background}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem \textbf{1 SB}: A rival claims your tag is invalid here; prove it or pay time
ℹtem \textbf{2 SB}: Your contact is \emph{busy} or \emph{watched}; help will cost you exposure
ℹtem \textbf{3 SB}: Obligation advances; an audit, rite, or muster arrives early
ℹtem \textbf{4 SB}: An enemy weaponizes your background—publicly
\end{itemize}

\textbf{At Creation}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Choose: 2 \emph{Access Tags}, 1 \emph{Signature Contact} (Cap~1 assist), 1 \emph{Boon} (once/session), 1 \emph{Obligation Seed}.
ℹtem Pick 1 language tied to the background at \emph{Conversational} or better.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{In Play}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Invoke tags to open doors or shift Position.
ℹtem Call your contact for +1d assist (no independent actions).
ℹtem Track the \emph{Obligation Timer [4]}. Pay it on−screen.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Advancing}
\begin{itemize}
ℹtem Upgrade contact to a \emph{Follower} by paying XP (Cap\(²\)).
ℹtem Add a second tag after a major arc or training.
\end{itemize}
