\subsection*{Key Differences}
\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Assets} are off‚ÄĎscreen resources: places, networks, titles, or enterprises.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Followers} are on‚ÄĎscreen allies who act during scenes.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Assets} change the situation \emph{before or around} a scene.
‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Followers} participate \emph{directly} in the moment.
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}
‚ĄĻtem They demand upkeep through XP, downtime, or narrative obligation.
‚ĄĻtem They can be targeted, compromised, or destroyed.
‚ĄĻtem Overuse draws attention from enemies, factions, and rivals.

\emph{Power creates gravity. What you rely on can be taken from you.}

\begin{itemize}

\paragraph{High‚ÄĎTier Use}

\end{itemize}
\emph{Assets are leverage, not immunity.}

\section{Terrestrial Patrons}

\subsection{What Are Terrestrial Patrons?}

\begin{itemize}

    ‚ĄĻtem Religious orders and temple hierarchies.
    ‚ĄĻtem Scholarly colleges and arcane societies.
\end{itemize}

Unlike supernatural Patrons (see \ref{ch:magic}), Terrestrial Patrons do not grant magic, but they offer \textbf{leverage}: protection, resources, sanctuary, information, and political influence.

\subsection{Gaining a Terrestrial Patron}
Ňāabel{sec:terrestrial‚ąígaining}

To gain a Terrestrial Patron, complete one of these significant actions:
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem Complete a major job or service for them.
    ‚ĄĻtem Swear a formal Oath of service.
    ‚ĄĻtem Enter into legal or financial binding.
    ‚ĄĻtem Share criminal secrets or be compromised by them.
    ‚ĄĻtem Perform a notable service that earns their favor.
\end{itemize}
Mark the Patron on your character sheet and write one sentence: \emph{``They want me because _______''}.

\subsection{Obligation System}

Terrestrial Patrons use the same Obligation track as supernatural Patrons (see \ref{sec:obligation‚ąícapacity}), but consequences are social, legal, or economic rather than mystical.

\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Calling in Favors}: When you request Patron influence, add +1 Obligation.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Capacity}: Spirit + Presence (same as supernatural Patrons).
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Overflow}: Each segment above capacity inflicts 1 Fatigue.
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Resolution}: Reduce through service, downtime actions, or fulfilling demands.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Patron Perks}

\textbf{Common Perks Include:}
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem Sanctuary in their territory or establishments.
    ‚ĄĻtem Legal relief or protection from certain authorities.
    ‚ĄĻtem Access to black market goods or specialized services.
    ‚ĄĻtem Elite followers or hirelings at reduced cost.
    ‚ĄĻtem Forged documents or identity protection.
    ‚ĄĻtem Information networks and rumor access.
    ‚ĄĻtem Trade advantages or exclusive contracts.
\end{itemize}
Using a perk never requires a roll‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíFate has already been paid. Each use comes with the ongoing cost of Obligation.

\subsection{Patron Demands}
Ňāabel{sec:terrestrial‚ąídemands}

Terrestrial Patrons always want something in return:
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem Silence about sensitive information.
    ‚ĄĻtem Loyalty over other conflicting interests.
    ‚ĄĻtem Completion of specific jobs or tasks.
    ‚ĄĻtem Delivery of valuable names or secrets.
    ‚ĄĻtem Political or social support for their causes.
    ‚ĄĻtem Elimination of rivals or threats to their interests.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Refusing a Demand} raises Obligation by 1 segment and may trigger immediate complications.

\begin{enumerate}
    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{Unavoidable Job}: You must complete a task you cannot refuse.

    ‚ĄĻtem \textbf{First Strike}: They act against you‚ąí‚ąí‚ąíreputation damage, warrants, bounty, blackmail.

\begin{itemize}

    ‚ĄĻtem Gain a new \textbf{Rival} faction that opposes your interests.
    ‚ĄĻtem Take a lasting \textbf{Complication} (Curse, Bounty, or Scandal) that follows you.
    ‚ĄĻtem May trigger immediate retaliation depending on the Patron's nature.

\section{Multi‚ÄĎCharacter Switching (Optional)}
Ňāabel{sec:multi‚ąícharacter‚ąíswitching}
‚ąądex{Echo Character}

You may choose to temporarily step away from your current character and play another, while your first character continues to act offscreen. This is called an \textbf{Echo Character}. It allows you to explore different parts of the story without permanently retiring anyone.

\subsection{How It Works}
When you switch, you declare:
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem Which character goes offscreen.
    ‚ĄĻtem Where they are going (fictional location).
    ‚ĄĻtem Why they are stepping out (story reason).
    ‚ĄĻtem How long you expect to be away (1‚ąí‚ąí3 sessions).
\end{itemize}
The offscreen character remains active in the fiction. During each session they are away, you complete a simple \textbf{Downtime Log}:
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem One \textbf{Resource Action} (how they maintain connections).
    ‚ĄĻtem One \textbf{Project Action} (a goal advanced).
    ‚ĄĻtem One \textbf{Event Response} (how they react to offscreen events).
\end{itemize}
The GM records the outcomes and may introduce a consequence.

\subsection{Returning to Play}
When you bring the character back:
\begin{itemize}
    ‚ĄĻtem You gain \textbf{2 Boons} for narrative re‚ÄĎentry.
    ‚ĄĻtem Roll \textbf{Wits + Spirit} (DV 3) to reestablish presence.
    ‚ĄĻtem The GM describes one consequence of your absence (e.g., an opportunity missed, a rival gained, a personal change).
\end{itemize}

For full mechanical details (Crisis Timers, Buddy System, etc.), see the GM Guide.

For groups that prefer strong narrative focus, consider these optional approaches.

\paragraph{Story‚ÄĎDriven Upkeep}
Instead of tracking XP costs for upkeep, the GM can introduce narrative complications that require attention. A neglected asset might attract unwanted attention, while a neglected follower might request a favor or special treatment.

\paragraph{Collaborative Management}
Describe how you maintain your assets and followers through roleplay rather than mechanical upkeep costs. A well‚ÄĎdescribed scene of tending a workshop or bonding with followers can fulfill maintenance requirements.

\paragraph{Flexible Condition Tracking}
Focus on the narrative implications of asset and follower conditions rather than strict penalties. A Strained asset might still function but with interesting complications, while a Collapsed asset might require creative solutions rather than just XP investment.
