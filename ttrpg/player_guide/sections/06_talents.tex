\chapter{Talents and Special Abilities}
\label{ch:talents}
\index{talents}\index{special abilities}

Talents are the building blocks of character specialization. They represent learned techniques, supernatural gifts, or cultural inheritances. Each Talent costs XP, and its cost reflects its impact.

\section{Understanding Talents}
\label{sec:talents-understanding}
\index{talents!understanding}

Talents are purchased with \textbf{Experience Points (XP)} (see \ref{ch:advancement}) and provide special capabilities:
\begin{itemize}
\item They go beyond simple skill bonuses.
\item They often have specific \textbf{activation conditions}.
\item They may provide \textbf{narrative permissions} (you can try things others cannot).
\item They can define your character's \textbf{unique identity}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Talent Costs by Tier}
\label{sec:talent-costs}
\index{talents!costs}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Tier} & \textbf{Cost} & \textbf{Scope} \\
\midrule
Minor & 2–3 XP & Small situational bonus, once per scene. \\
Major & 4–6 XP & Strong upgrade, permanent effect in a niche. \\
Prestige & 7–10 XP & Campaign-defining, breaks fundamental limits. \\
Epic & 11+ XP & Legendary ability, shapes the story. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Activation Types.}
\label{sec:talent-activation}
\index{talents!activation}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Passive}: Always on; no action required.
\item \textbf{Active}: Requires an action or scene focus to use.
\item \textbf{Reactive}: Triggers automatically on a condition.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Limits and Economy.}
\label{sec:talent-limits}
Unless a talent says otherwise:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Per scene} uses refresh at scene end.
\item \textbf{Per session} uses refresh after downtime.
\item Some talents allow you to spend \textbf{Boons} (see \ref{ch:core-mechanics}) to push effects further.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=yellow!5!white,colframe=yellow!75!black,title=\textbf{Only One Active Talent?}]
\index{talents!active limit}
The rule “only one talent can be active at a time” applies only to \textbf{activated abilities} that require a decision or action.  
\textbf{Passive} talents (e.g., +1 die to perception) are always active and stack.  
\textbf{Active} talents that require an action or reaction cannot be used simultaneously – you choose which one to activate when the trigger occurs.  
Talents that are “once per scene” reset at the end of the scene.
\end{tcolorbox}

\label{talent:backstab}
\textbf{Cost: 6 XP} \textbf{Active, once per scene.} When you attack an Unaware or Engaged foe from Stealth, deal +1 Harm and ignore 1 point of their Armor. On a miss, you are Exposed (Desperate Position or mark 1 Harm). \\

\subsubsection*{Elusive Dodge \textnormal{[DEFENSE] [EVADE]}}
\label{talent:elusive-dodge}
\textbf{Cost: 6 XP} \textbf{Reactive, once per exchange.} When targeted by a physical attack, you may immediately enter a Defend stance as a free reaction. Mark 1 Fatigue after use. \\

\subsubsection*{Berserker Rage \textnormal{[FURY] [POWER]}}

\subsubsection*{Cavalier \textnormal{[MOBILITY] [MARTIAL]}}
\label{talent:cavalier}
\textbf{Cost: 4 XP} \textbf{Passive while mounted.} Gain +2 dice to melee attacks when moving Far→Near, or +2 dice to ranged attacks when moving Near→Far. \\
\textit{Requires: War Mount Asset, Melee 2+ or Ranged 2+.}

\subsubsection*{Subtle Casting \textnormal{[HIDDEN] [CONTROL]}}
\label{talent:subtle-casting}
\textbf{Cost: 6 XP} \textbf{Active.} Make a Performance + Lore roll to quietly cast a spell, invoke a Rite, or sing a Cantos against DV (Tier). If successful, the casting does not generate Story Beats on the Channel or initial roll. Any SB generated are banked by the GM and applied later. Cannot be used for Great or Extreme Tier effects. \\
\textit{Requires: Lore 3+, Performance 2+ or Runekeeper with Codex.}

\subsubsection*{Effortless Charge \textnormal{[MOVEMENT] [MARTIAL]}}
\label{talent:effortless-charge}
\textbf{Cost: 4 XP} \textbf{Active, once per scene.} When you take the \textbf{Charge} action (see \ref{ch:core-mechanics}), you may ignore the Fatigue cost. All other costs and restrictions (Desperate defense, open ground requirement) still apply. \\
\textit{Requires: Body 3+, Melee 2+.}

\subsubsection*{Effortless Skirmish \textnormal{[MOVEMENT] [MARTIAL]}}
\label{talent:effortless-skirmish}
\textbf{Cost: 4 XP} \textbf{Active, once per scene.} When you take the \textbf{Skirmish} (or \textbf{Harring Shot}) action (see \ref{ch:core-mechanics}), you may ignore the Fatigue cost. All other costs and restrictions (Desperate defense, open ground requirement) still apply. \\
\textit{Requires: Wits 3+, Ranged 2+.}

\subsubsection*{Relentless \textnormal{[MOVEMENT] [TERRAIN]}}
\label{talent:relentless}
\textbf{Cost: 6 XP} \textbf{Passive.} You may take the \textbf{Charge} or \textbf{Skirmish} action over \textbf{difficult terrain} (rubble, dense forest, shallow water, steep slopes). The Fatigue cost (1) is \emph{not} waived unless you also have the relevant Effortless talent. \\
\textit{Requires: Body 3+, Athletics 2+.}

\subsubsection*{Stubborn Pursuer \textnormal{[MOVEMENT] [REACTION]}}
\label{talent:stubborn-pursuer}
\textbf{Cost: 6 XP} \textbf{Reactive.} When an opponent within Close range uses the \textbf{Disengage} action (or any movement intended to leave your engagement without provoking an opportunity attack), you may use your reaction to move with them, staying in Close range. This movement does not cost an action and does not provoke opportunity attacks. You move the same number of range bands as they do (e.g., Close → Near, or Near → Far), up to your normal movement limit for the round. \\
You cannot use this talent if you have already taken a Move action or Dash this round. Once per round. \\
\textit{Requires: Body 3+, Athletics 2+.}

\label{sec:synergy-examples}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Spellcraft + Elemental Focus + Elemental Mastery}: Reliable freeform casting with reduced backlash and epic control (see \ref{ch:magic}).
  \item \textbf{Backstab + Shadow Dance + Deathblow}: Stealth assassination chain.
  \item \textbf{Ritual Mastery + Efficient Invocation + Crack Specialist}: Fast, safe, low-cost rituals.
  \item \textbf{Defensive Survival + Elusive Dodge + Miststep Reflex}: High survivability in melee.
  \item \textbf{Iron Fist + Flowing Spirit + Untouchable Way}: Unstoppable monk durability.
  \item \textbf{Effortless Charge + Relentless}: Charge over difficult terrain once per scene without Fatigue cost.
  \item \textbf{Stubborn Pursuer + Tactical Movement}: Stay glued to a disengaging foe while preserving your own movement options.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Early Game (0–40 XP)}
\label{sec:early-game-talents}
Take 1–2 access talents (Spellcraft, Familiar) and 2–3 minor talents that support your core concept.

\subsection*{Mid Game (41–90 XP)}
\label{sec:mid-game-talents}
Add major talents (Codex, Patron’s Symbol, Backstab, etc.) and 2–3 synergistic power talents. Plan for prestige prerequisites.

\subsection*{Late Game (91+ XP)}
\label{sec:late-game-talents}
Acquire 1–2 prestige or epic talents that define your legacy. Focus on party-supporting or campaign-shaping abilities.

\subsection*{Example: The Shadow Assassin}
\label{sec:shadow-assassin-example}
A rogue who takes \textbf{Backstab} (\ref{talent:backstab}) at Tier I, \textbf{Shadow Dance} (\ref{talent:shadow-dance}) at Tier II, and \textbf{Deathblow} (\ref{talent:deathblow}) at Tier III can execute a devastating assassination chain:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Attack from stealth with Backstab (+1 Harm, ignore armor).
\item After a kill, use Shadow Dance to re-enter stealth.
\item Later, strike from Dominant Position with Deathblow (triple Harm).
\end{enumerate}
This combination requires careful positioning and Boon management, but is highly effective.

\section{Custom Talents}
\label{sec:custom-talents}
\index{talents!custom}
Work with your GM to create custom talents:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Base on story events.
  \item Use the cost tiers above (2–3 / 4–6 / 7–10 / 11+ XP).
  \item Define clear prerequisites, activation, and limits.
\end{itemize}

\section{Talent Respecification}
\label{sec:talent-respec}
\index{talents!respecification}
With GM approval, you may retrain talents during downtime (one downtime period). Provide a narrative justification. Typically limited to once per major story arc.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=purple!5!white,colframe=purple!75!black,title=Talent Selection Quick Reference,fonttitle=\bfseries]
\textbf{Minor (2–3 XP)}: Small bonuses, once/scene tricks. \\
\textbf{Major (4–6 XP)}: Strong permanent upgrades, core path abilities. \\
\textbf{Prestige (7–10 XP)}: Campaign-shaping, breaks limits. \\
\textbf{Epic (11+ XP)}: Legendary, story-defining power. \\[2mm]
\textit{Remember: Passive talents stack; active talents require an action or reaction.}
\end{tcolorbox}
