\subsection*{Resolution Index Card}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em,top=0.5em,bottom=0.5em]
\textbf{Declare Action → Build Dice Pool (Attribute + Skill) → Roll → Count Successes}

\medskip

0--1 Successes = \textbf{Miss} (GM gains SB, consequences occur)\\
2--3 Successes = \textbf{Partial} (success + complication)\\
4+ Successes = \textbf{Full Success} (clear win, no complication)

\smallskip

\textbf{Position} (Risk) + \textbf{Effect} (Impact) may shift outcomes.

\smallskip

\textbf{SB (Story Beats)} fuel abilities, magic, momentum, and narrative pushes.

\section*{Appendix Y: Magic Systems Reference}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix Y: Magic Systems Reference}

Magic in Fate's Edge is plural.  
Different traditions channel power through different metaphysical structures,
but all obey the same truth:

\begin{center}
\emph{Power has a cost, and magic shapes the one who wields it.}
\end{center}

This appendix provides quick–reference tools for players using magic–affiliated
systems, talismans, rites, and occult practices.

\bigskip

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Core Magical Principles}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Magic expresses \textbf{identity and belief}.
    \item Casting always creates \textbf{consequences somewhere}.
    \item Magic rewrites reality through \textbf{intention, structure, or sacrifice}.
    \item Excessive power erodes \textbf{choice} (see Iconic Ascension sidebar).
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Universal Casting Reference}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!5,colframe=gray!60]
\textbf{Cast Declaration Template:}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Intent} — What change do you seek?
    \item \textbf{Shape} — How does your tradition express it?
    \item \textbf{Cost} — What do you risk or expend?
    \item \textbf{Resolution} — Test; apply Position/Effect rules.
    \item \textbf{Backwash} — Mark Fatigue, SB pressure, taboo, instability, or bargain.
\end{enumerate}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Freeform Arcana (Wizardry)}
\textbf{Style:} Creative, rational, deliberate shaping.  
\textbf{Tools:} Glyphs, structure, metaphors, binding geometries.  
\textbf{Costs:} Fatigue, reality recoil, SB instability.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\textbf{Play Cues:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Diagram your spell or state its symbolism.
    \item Accept fatigue for precision.
    \item Big spells distort fate—SB spikes are normal.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Innate Arcana (Sorcery)}
\textbf{Style:} Emotional, instinctive, bloodline–linked, erratic.  
\textbf{Tools:} Breath, impulse, resonance surges.  
\textbf{Costs:} Fatigue, unpredictable backlash.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\textbf{Play Cues:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Treat magic like a reflex.
    \item Trigger surges in dramatic beats or crisis.
    \item Push your limits; expect consequences.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Invocation (Priest / Paladin / Medium)}
\textbf{Style:} Petition, prayer, pact, rite.  
\textbf{Tools:} Symbols, vows, places of power, obedience.
\textbf{Costs:} Taboo pressures, obligation clocks, moral binding.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\textbf{Play Cues:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Your magic is not your own — frame it as request.
    \item Mark obligation when you call for aid.
    \item Violating your ethos draws consequences.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Runic Consecration (Runekeeping)}
\textbf{Style:} Methods, seals, contracts, process sanctification.  
\textbf{Tools:} Ritual steps, codices, binding clauses.  
\textbf{Costs:} Hunger clocks, anomalies, escalation loops.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\textbf{Play Cues:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Magic is \emph{procedure}; name the method.
    \item Consecrate zones, tools, or roles.
    \item Expect the system to push back — loops and costs matter.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Shaping / Druidic Power}
\textbf{Style:} Resonance with place, element, life, cycle.  
\textbf{Tools:} Marks, offerings, seasonal rites.  
\textbf{Costs:} Environmental echo, territorial consequence.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\textbf{Play Cues:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Declare what the land wants.
    \item Your spells alter zones — tag the scene.
    \item Every shaping is a bargain with nature’s temperament.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Pact Binding (Warlock / Avatar)}
\textbf{Style:} Exchange, hunger, tilt, transformation.  
\textbf{Tools:} Tokens, signatures, reflection marks.  
\textbf{Costs:} Autonomy loss, compelled behavior, ascension risk.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\textbf{Play Cues:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Ask yourself: “What does my patron get?”
    \item Debt becomes plot.
    \item Your identity reshapes — reflect that in choices.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Summoning (Medium / Necromancer / Shaman)}
\textbf{Style:} Invocation of entities, binding, vassal action.  
\textbf{Tools:} Circles, bargains, lanterns, ancestral calls.  
\textbf{Costs:} Attention clocks, breach risk, backlash on failure.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=white,colframe=black,left=1em,right=1em]
\textbf{Play Cues:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Give the summoned a personality and motives.
    \item Their aid comes with demands.
    \item Tracking **Attention** is as important as tracking power.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Mutual Rules of Magical Cost}
\begin{center}
\textbf{No magic is free.}
\end{center}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|p{8cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{Cost Type} & \textbf{Examples in Play} \\
\hline
Fatigue & Exhaustion surge, shaking focus, body strain \\
SB Pressure & GM spikes complications or clocks \\
Taboo / Obligation & Commandments, rites, punishment for misuse \\
Attention / Backwash & Spirits notice; cosmic ledger ticks \\
Anomaly / Distortion & Places react, reality warps, rituals echo \\
Identity Erosion & Compelled acts, alignment tilt, Icon risk \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Cross–System Synergies}
\textbf{All magic systems can blend.}  
A Runekeeper may emulate a Cleric or a Druid;  
an Invoker may operate as a Paladin or Prophet;  
a Sorcerer may look like a Witch, Elementalist, or Psion.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!5,colframe=gray!60]
When combining systems:
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item Choose your \textbf{primary expression} (belief, intellect, instinct, ritual).
    \item Describe spells through that lens.
    \item Let costs reflect your system.
    \item Track consequences separately — mixing systems increases risk.
\end{enumerate}
\end{tcolorbox}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{GM Reminders for Magic}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Magic creates story — reward its use with consequences and momentum.
    \item Players should narrate method, not just effect.
    \item Big moments should change scenes, not just numbers.
    \item Magic should tempt the caster — echo their drives and flaws.
\end{itemize}

% ============================================================
\subsection*{Player Reminders for Magic}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Lean into cost — magic feels significant when it hurts.
    \item Make magic personal — describe what it looks and feels like.
    \item Tie powers to identity, belief, trauma, duty, or legacy.
    \item Let magic change your character over time.
\end{itemize}

\bigskip

\noindent
This appendix gives you a quick language and structure to express magic’s influence
without needing to reference full chapter rules mid–session.

\section{Pre-Made Spells with TAGS and DV Math}
\label{sec:premade-spells}
\index{magic!pre-made spells}

The following examples illustrate how TAGS, DV scaling, and spell intent interact within the casting system.

% ===========================
% Tier I
% ===========================
\subsection*{Tier I: Minor Utility (DV 2--3)}

\paragraph*{Simple Light (Air + Light)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[ILLUMINATE]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} Create a small sustained light (torch-brightness) for the scene. \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (ILLUMINATE) = \textbf{2}. \\
\textbf{Notes:} No backlash risk; lasts until dismissed.

\paragraph*{Mend Minor Wounds (Life + Mend)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[MEND]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} Close a small cut or bruise; reduce 1 Harm (Lesser). \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (MEND) = \textbf{2}. \\
\textbf{Notes:} Requires touch.

\paragraph*{Message on Wind (Air + Veil)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[WHISPER]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} Carry a short whispered message 100 feet on wind currents. \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (WHISPER) = \textbf{2}. \\
\textbf{Notes:} One-way communication; 30 seconds duration.

% ===========================
% Tier II
% ===========================
\subsection*{Tier II: Combat Utility (DV 3--4)}

\paragraph*{Blade Flame (Fire + Strike)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[BURNING][STRIKE]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} Coat a weapon in fire, adding +1 Harm and {[BURN]} to the next melee attack. \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (BURNING) + 1 (STRIKE) = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{Notes:} Lasts one exchange.

\paragraph*{Shield of Air (Air + Wall)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[WARD][DEFEND]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} Create a compressed air barrier; gain Position +1 on next defense. \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (WARD) + 1 (DEFEND) = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{Notes:} One use only.

\paragraph*{Fleet Step (Air + Leap)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[HASTE][MOVE]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} Double movement speed for one exchange. \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (HASTE) + 1 (MOVE) = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{Notes:} Requires open area.

% ===========================
% Tier III
% ===========================
\subsection*{Tier III: Area Control (DV 4--5)}

\paragraph*{Wall of Stone (Earth + Wall)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[BARRIER][FORTIFY]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} Summon a 10-foot stone wall (Near; Scene duration). \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (BARRIER) + 1 (FORTIFY) = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{Dangerous DV:} \textbf{4} (if blocking enemies in combat). \\
\textbf{Notes:} Provides cover; obstructs movement.

\paragraph*{Sleep's Gentle Touch (Water + Mind)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[DREAM][COMMAND]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} One target falls asleep (Scene duration; Resist: Spirit + Resolve DV 2). \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (DREAM) + 1 (COMMAND) = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{Dangerous DV:} \textbf{4} (combat use). \\
\textbf{Notes:} Harm wakes target.

\paragraph*{Flame Cloak (Fire + Area)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[BURNING][AREA]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} 6-foot radius of intense heat; enemies suffer --1 die to approach. \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (BURNING) + 1 (AREA) = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{Dangerous DV:} \textbf{4} (if allies are near). \\
\textbf{Notes:} Creates difficult terrain.

% ===========================
% Tier IV
% ===========================
\subsection*{Tier IV: Battlefield Shaping (DV 5--6)}

\paragraph*{Earth's Tremor (Earth + Force)}
\textbf{TAGS:} {[QUAKE][AREA]} \\
\textbf{Effect:} 20-foot tremor; enemies test Body + Athletics (DV 3) or fall prone. \\
\textbf{DV:} 1 (base) + 1 (QUAKE) + 1 (AREA) = \textbf{3}. \\
\textbf{Dangerous DV:} \textbf{5}. \\

\subsection*{Backlash Examples by Tier}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Tier I--II:} Minor fatigue, brief elemental effect (smoke, chill, static).
    \item \textbf{Tier III--IV:} Harm 1, temporary Condition, environmental hazard.
    \item \textbf{Tier V:} Harm 2, major Condition, significant environmental change.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Push Options}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{+1 Effect:} Increase radius, duration, or potency one step.
    \item \textbf{+1d:} Improve casting roll (cost: +1 Fatigue).
    \item \textbf{Instant:} Resolve immediately (cost: +1 Harm).
    \item \textbf{Sustain:} Maintain effect with concentration (+1 Fatigue/round).
\end{itemize}
