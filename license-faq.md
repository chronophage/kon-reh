## The Short Version

**The rules are open.** Build on them, hack them, share them.

**The world is mine.** The Amaranthine—its places, people, and stories—is the work of years. If you want to sell something set there, let's talk.

**Community content is free.** Share your adventures, characters, and hacks with the world. Just credit me and don't sell it.

**Commercial use requires permission.** Want to publish a paid supplement? Email me. I'm open to collaboration.

# Fate's Edge License FAQ

*For players, GMs, and community creators*

---

## For Players

### Q: Do I need to buy anything to play Fate's Edge?

**A:** No. The **Essentials Guide** is free and contains everything you need to create characters, run adventures, and play. It's a complete game.

### Q: What if I want more options?

**A:** The **Player's Guide** adds more talents, magic paths, character options, and advancement rules. It's a full expansion—but it's optional. The Essentials Guide is a complete game on its own.

### Q: Can I share the Essentials Guide with my friends?

**A:** Absolutely. It's licensed under CC BY-NC-SA, which means you can share it freely—as long as you don't sell it and you credit the creator.

### Q: Can I record or stream my Fate's Edge campaign?

**A:** Yes! We love seeing actual play content. No permission needed. Just credit the game and the creator.

### Q: Can I make fan art or fan fiction?

**A:** Yes. Non-commercial fan works are always welcome. Please credit the creator and include a disclaimer that your work is unofficial.

---

## For Game Masters

### Q: Can I create my own adventures for Fate's Edge?

**A:** Yes. You can create and share adventures freely. If you want to sell them, see the commercial use section below.

### Q: Can I share my house rules or system hacks?

**A:** Yes. The SRD is open for adaptation. Share your hacks with the community—we'd love to see them.

### Q: Can I use Fate's Edge mechanics in my own game?

**A:** Yes. The core mechanics (dice system, Position/Effect, Boons, Story Beats, TAGS system) are open under the SRD license. You can build your own game on top of them.

### Q: What if I want to create a custom setting for Fate's Edge?

**A:** Go for it! The SRD mechanics are open. Your custom setting is yours. You can share it freely. If you want to sell it, see the commercial use section.

### Q: Can I run Fate's Edge at conventions or events?

**A:** Yes. Running games at conventions, game stores, or private events requires no permission.

---

## For Community Creators (Free Content)

### Q: Can I create and share free content set in the Amaranthine?

**A:** Yes. Free adventures, NPCs, maps, and expansions are welcome. Please credit the creator and include a disclaimer that your work is unofficial.

### Q: Can I use named characters (Serafine, the Gray Wanderer, etc.) in my free content?

**A:** Yes, for non-commercial free content. Please credit the creator and include a disclaimer.

### Q: Can I use the Fate's Edge logo on my free content?

**A:** You may include a "Fate's Edge Compatible" or "A Fate's Edge Supplement" tagline, but you may not use the official logo without permission. Please include a disclaimer: "This product is not affiliated with or endorsed by the creator of Fate's Edge."

### Q: Can I share my free content on DriveThruRPG or itch.io?

**A:** Yes, as long as it's free. If you want to sell it, see the commercial use section.

---

## For Commercial Creators (Paid Content)

### Q: Can I sell a supplement that uses only the SRD mechanics?

**A:** Yes. The SRD is open for commercial use under CC BY-NC-SA. You can sell a supplement that uses the dice system, Position/Effect, Boons, Story Beats, and TAGS mechanics, as long as you don't use extended material (setting, characters, adventures, proprietary mechanics).

### Q: Can I sell a supplement set in the Amaranthine?

**A:** Not without permission. The Amaranthine—its regions, lore, characters, and adventures—is extended material and All Rights Reserved. If you want to sell something set there, you need a commercial license.

### Q: Can I sell a supplement that uses named characters (Serafine, Ikasha, etc.)?

**A:** No. Named characters and Patrons are extended material. You need permission for any commercial use.

### Q: Can I sell a supplement that uses the Fate's Edge logo or title?

**A:** Not without permission. The logo and title are trademarks. Commercial use requires a license.

### Q: How do I get permission?

**A:** Email **[PUBLISHER_EMAIL]** with:
- Your name and contact information
- A description of your project
- Which extended material you want to use
- How you plan to distribute and price it
- A sample or prototype (if available)

### Q: What are the terms?

**A:** Permission is granted on a project-by-project basis. A royalty may be negotiated. You must clearly credit Fate's Edge and the creator. You may not imply official endorsement.

### Q: How long does it take to get a response?

**A:** Expect a response within 2–4 weeks.

### Q: What kind of projects are you looking for?

**A:** Projects that respect the tone and spirit of Fate's Edge, are well-written and professionally presented, expand the world rather than just copying it, and show a willingness to collaborate.

## For Everyone

### Q: What's the difference between "SRD" and "extended material"?

**A:**

| SRD (Open) | Extended Material (Closed) |
|------------|----------------------------|
| Core dice mechanics | Setting lore (regions, cultures, history) |
| Position/Effect system | Named NPCs and Patrons |
| Boon and Story Beat economies | Published adventures and modules |
| TAGS magic system (core) | Proprietary magic expansions |
| Character creation framework | Artwork, maps, and design elements |
| NPC templates | Original prose and framing devices |

### Q: Can I translate Fate's Edge into another language?

**A:** The SRD can be translated freely under the CC BY-NC-SA license. Extended material (setting, adventures, characters) requires permission for translation.

### Q: Can I use Fate's Edge mechanics in a video game or app?

**A:** The SRD mechanics are open. You can build a digital adaptation using the core rules. If you want to use extended material (setting, characters, etc.), you need permission.

### Q: Who owns Fate's Edge?

**A:** Fate's Edge is the intellectual property of `Nicholas A. Gasper`. The SRD is open under CC BY-NC-SA. All extended material is All Rights Reserved.
