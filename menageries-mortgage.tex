\chapter{The Menagerie's Mortgage}
\label{chap:menagerie-mortgage}

\section*{Adventure Overview}
\textit{A surreal Fate's Edge parody adventure in a pocket-dimension safari gone horribly off-budget, morally questionable, and faintly sticky.}

Maelstraeus, the Infernal Bargainer, hires the party to serve a legally-binding summons to \textbf{Zara the Magnificent}, owner of the \textbf{Celestial Menagerie} --- a chain of pocket-dimensional enclosures filled with ``educational'' exhibits that mostly educate visitors about trauma recovery.

Zara has missed multiple payments on her infernal mortgage and fled into her own park. To complete the contract, the summons must be served \textit{directly} to Zara before she escapes the Menagerie entirely.

\section*{Core Hook}
\begin{quote}
``The contract says we must serve her personally. It does not specify dimension, altitude, or whether she has grown extra teeth.''
\end{quote}

\section{The Contract}
The summons is a magical document with unfortunate opinions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Burns anyone attempting to tear it.
  \item Screams loudly when ignored.
  \item Glows brighter when closer to Zara.
  \item Fine print is literally microscopic (Arcana DV~3 to decipher).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Failure:} If not delivered within 7 days, the party owes Maelstraeus a favor.  
\textbf{Success:} Zara owes \textit{them} a favor. She is \textit{very} creative with repayment.

\section{Adventure Structure}
The Celestial Menagerie consists of 12 loosely regulated enclosures. Each is a self-contained scene with hazards, social encounters, or problems that are technically ``educational,'' depending on one's definition of ``education.''

\section{Attraction Tickets}
Players earn \textbf{Attraction Tickets} via creative problem-solving, social play, or making the GM laugh.

\textbf{Tickets May Be Spent To:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Skip one enclosure entirely.
  \item Improve Position by one step.
  \item Gain a clue from Maelstraeus.
  \item Escape a catastrophic situation.
\end{itemize}

% -------------------------------------------------------
\section{Why This Works}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Classic creature archetypes reimagined as parody-friendly encounters.
  \item Multiple paths: social, stealth, violent, and absurd.
  \item Ticket economy encourages creativity and agency.
  \item Modular structure works for one-shots or full arc.
  \item Humor integrated mechanically, not just narratively.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Souvenir Shops \& Generators}
\label{sec:souvenir-shops}

\subsection*{Using Souvenir Shops}
Souvenir shops appear near enclosure exits, tram stops, or panic doors. They provide comic relief, light gear, edible mistakes, and surprisingly binding receipts.

\paragraph{Core Loops.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Attraction Tickets:} May be used as currency (see Table~\ref{tab:ticket-exchange}).
  \item \textbf{Haggling:} \emph{Sway} or \emph{Trade} (DV 2--3) to modify price or perk.
  \item \textbf{Receipt Law:} All receipts are extradimensionally enforceable. Fine print always matters.
  \item \textbf{Safety:} Shops \emph{seem} safe. SB spends can invalidate that assumption.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{SB Hooks (GM).}
On SB spends in a shop scene, choose or roll:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Shelf domino cascade (Position --1) \;|\; Counterfeit tickets discovered (Lose 1 Ticket)
  \item Clerk calls security armor (see Enclosure 9) \;|\; A ``demo curse'' triggers (Harm 1 or Tag)
  \item Warranty clause teleports item back to store at worst time
\end{itemize}

\section{Zara's Contract of Infernal Mortgage and Adjacent Obligations}
\label{sec:zara-contract}

\begin{tcolorbox}[title={\centering \textbf{Celestial Menagerie -- Infernal Mortgage Contract}},colback=white,colframe=black!60]
\begin{center}
{\Large The Celestial Menagerie \\
Mortgage \& Summons-Delivery Agreement}
\end{center}

\noindent
\textbf{By Authority of the Infernal Bargaining Office, Fourth Subdivision, Under-Market Division of Contracts and Culinary Arts}

\vspace{0.2cm}

\noindent
Let it be publicly, cosmically, and awkwardly known that:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Zara the Magnificent}, legal owner and sole visionary of The Celestial Menagerie (``the Debtor''), did on the Twenty-Third Cycle of the Glowing Chicken Moon, Year of the Infernal Spreadsheet, sign a Mortgage of Infernal Standing.
  \item Said Mortgage provided funds, labor, pocket-dimension stitching, signage, pamphlets, and optional snack kiosks.
  \item Payments were due on each market day, excluding Feastdays, Half-Feastdays, Minor Feastday Eves, and the Day of Spontaneous Picnics.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
\textbf{Whereas} the Debtor has failed to render timely payment for \textit{three} consecutive cycles, the following actions are hereby authorized and encouraged:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\Alph*.]
  \item Delivery of a Summons directly to the Debtor, physically, verbally, or magically;
  \item Delivery may occur in any enclosure, sub-enclosure, office, attic, broom closet, or makeshift hot-dog stand;
  \item Illusions, clones, impersonators, or holograms of the Debtor \textit{do not} count as valid recipients unless the Debtor has legally declared them ``emotionally equivalent'' representatives.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Accepted Methods of Service}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Handing the Summons to the Debtor’s actual hands, talons, pseudopods, or other manipulators;
  \item Telepathic transfer, \emph{if} the Debtor acknowledges receipt in complete sentences;
  \item Firing the Summons point-blank into the Debtor’s office wall via crossbow, \textit{if} the Debtor signs the impact crater.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Forbidden Methods of Service}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Leaving the Summons on a desk, even if the Debtor is ``totally coming right back''; 
  \item Handing it to a passing goblin and hoping for the best;
  \item Stapling to the Debtor while invisible, flying, or disguised as interpretive dancers;
  \item Service by \emph{vibe}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Consequences of Valid Delivery}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The Debt shall become \textbf{immediately due}, with applicable late fees, referral fees, and a small surcharge for ``audience discomfort.''
  \item The Debtor must either:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Repay the Mortgage in full;
    \item Enter agreed-upon negotiations with the Infernal Bargainer;
    \item Offer a Favor of Equal or Greater Value to the Deliverers (``the Bearers of Paper'').
  \end{itemize}
  \item The Bearers of Paper shall have first right of refusal on any Favor of absurd or sticky nature.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Consequences of Failure to Deliver}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Upon failure to serve within seven solar days, the Bearers of Paper agree to render a Favor of Equal Weight.

\section{Location Generator (Celestial Menagerie)}
\label{sec:location-generator}

\noindent
Use this generator when players wander off the main path, break containment fences, or deliberately explore ``staff-only'' areas. Each location has:
\begin{itemize}
  \item A \textbf{Feature} (what is here?)
  \item A \textbf{Hazard} (what could go wrong?)
  \item A \textbf{Hook} (why does the location matter?)
  \item A \textbf{Clock or Tag} to tie mechanically into Fate’s Edge
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Quick Roll Procedure}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Roll on Table~\ref{tab:location-type} for the broad zone.
  \item Roll on Table~\ref{tab:location-feature} for the primary feature.
  \item Roll on Table~\ref{tab:location-hazard} for the active problem.
  \item Optionally roll on Table~\ref{tab:location-hook} for why players care.
\end{enumerate}
